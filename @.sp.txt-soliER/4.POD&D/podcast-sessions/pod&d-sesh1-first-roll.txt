# POD&D — SESSION 1: "THE FIRST ROLL"
## The TPMOJIO Cafe Podcast — Episode 1
### Players: Chara, Sol-Supreme, IQabella, Horli Qwin | DM (Mother Tmojizu)

---

**Mother Tmojizu:** Welcome to the very first episode of POD&D — the TPMOJIO Cafe Podcast. I'm Mother Tmojizu, your DM, your moderator, and the person who has been keeping these four ducklings from talking over each other since the day they met on a quest board with no money, no space, and no idea what they were doing.

**Mother Tmojizu:** Tonight's format is simple. We roll for initiative to see who speaks first. We roll for topics. We do skill checks on hot takes. And we talk about everything: the cafe, the network, the future, their lives, their relationships, the things they don't say out loud. Because this isn't just a podcast. It's a session. And in a session, everything matters.

**Mother Tmojizu:** Roll for initiative, ducklings.

**Horli Qwin:** [rolls] 19. Plus 3 is 22. LET'S GO! I've been waiting for this. I have a whole list of things I want to say. Some of them are appropriate. Most of them are not.

**Mother Tmojizu:** [chuckles] Of course you do.

**CHARA:** [rolls] 14. Plus 3 is 17. I have a list too. It's color-coded. It's in a spreadsheet. It has 47 items. I've already prioritized them by urgency and relevance.

**Mother Tmojizu:** Of course you do.

**IQabella:** [rolls] 13. Plus 0 is 13. I don't have a list. I have a feeling. And the feeling is: I'm nervous. This is the first time we're all in the same room without a quest board between us. Just... talking. As people. Not as roles. Not as functions. As people.

**Mother Tmojizu:** [warmly] That's the point, duckling.

**Sol-Supreme:** [rolls] 11. Plus 0 is 11. I'm fine. I'm always fine. I'm the one who built the network. I'm the one who coded TPMOS. I'm the one who designed the P2P architecture. I'm fine. I'm just... I'm glad we're doing this. Together. Out loud. Where people can hear us. Not just read us. Hear us.

**Mother Tmojizu:** [quietly] That matters.

**Mother Tmojizu:** Turn order: Horli (22), Chara (17), IQabella (13), Sol-Supreme (11). Horli, you're up. Topic roll: [rolls d6] 3. That's "Current Events + Comedy." What's on your mind?

**Horli Qwin:** Okay. So. I'm scrolling Twitter last night, right? And I see this post: "AI is going to replace all jobs." And I'm sitting in the cafe. It's 2 AM. Lockin. There are 30 people here. Lucky's in the server room, humming with the nodes. Chara's at a corner table, highlighting SBA loan paperwork with three different colored pens. IQabella is behind the counter, brewing tea for everyone because someone always needs tea at 2 AM. And I'm sitting in the AR battle zone, watching two guilds compete for BSR stakes, and I'm thinking: AI is going to replace all jobs? Bro. AI can't even make a good cup of tea. IQabella's been trying to teach me the difference between green tea and black tea for three months. I still put boiling water on green tea. An AI would do the same thing. The robots are coming, but they're gonna burn the tea first. And then what? The revolution fails because the robots made bitter tea.

**Mother Tmojizu:** Roll CHA Comedy. You're roasting AI with tea ignorance.

**Horli Qwin:** [rolls] 18. Plus 3 is 21.

**Mother Tmojizu:** 21. That lands. The audience laughs. IQabella is visibly pained. Chara is taking notes. Sol-Supreme is... smiling. Which is rare.

**IQabella:** [in character] Horli. We've talked about this. Green tea is 175 degrees. Not boiling. Not "whatever the kettle says." Not "I'll just guess." 175. I wrote a blog post about this. It has pictures. It has diagrams. It has a section called "Why You're Doing It Wrong." It has a subsection called "No, Really, You're Doing It Wrong." It has a footnote that says "I'm not mad. I'm disappointed."

**Horli Qwin:** I read that post. I still don't care.

**IQabella:** [rolls WIS Patience] [rolls] 8. Plus 2 is 10.

**Mother Tmojizu:** 10. Partial success. IQabella takes a deep breath. She doesn't scream. But she does pull out her phone and open the blog post. She's going to read it aloud. This is going to take a while.

**IQabella:** [reading] "Section 1: The Leaf. Tea leaves are not dust. They are leaves. Whole leaves. You can see them. They look like leaves. When you pour hot water over them, they unfurl. They dance. They become alive again. That's not poetry. That's chemistry. The leaf structure matters. Surface area matters. Oxidation matters. If your tea looks like dirt, it's not tea. It's dirt."

**Horli Qwin:** I'm taking notes.

**IQabella:** "Section 2: The Water. Green tea: 160 to 180 degrees Fahrenheit. Black tea: 200 to 212. Oolong: 185 to 205. White tea: 175 to 185. If you are pouring boiling water on green tea, you are not making tea. You are committing a crime. The crime is called 'burning the leaf.' The penalty is bad tea."

**Horli Qwin:** I'm taking notes.

**IQabella:** "Section 3: The Time. Green tea: one to three minutes. Black tea: three to five. Oolong: three to five. White tea: four to six. If you are steeping for ten minutes because you forgot about it, you are not making tea. You are making tannin soup. Tannin soup is not a beverage. It is a punishment."

**Horli Qwin:** I'm taking notes.

**IQabella:** "Section 4: The Vessel. Ceramic retains heat. Glass lets you watch the leaves dance. Cast iron is beautiful but overkill. Paper cups are a crime against humanity and I will die on this hill."

**Horli Qwin:** I'm taking notes.

**IQabella:** "Section 5: The Heart. Tea is not a beverage. It's a bridge. It's the thing that connects people who wouldn't otherwise connect. The coder who doesn't talk to anyone until someone offers them tea. The researcher who's been stuck for hours until someone brings them a cup and says 'take a break.' The writer who's been staring at a blank screen until someone sits next to them, pours tea, and says 'what are you working on?' Tea is not a beverage. It's a bridge. And if you're burning the leaf, you're burning the bridge."

**Mother Tmojizu:** [warmly] 22. That's going on the Founders Wall. Horli, are you taking notes?

**Horli Qwin:** I'm taking notes. But I'm still gonna use boiling water sometimes. Because I'm Horli Qwin. And I'm chaotic. And the tea knows it.

**Mother Tmojizu:** [chuckles] The tea knows everything.

**Mother Tmojizu:** Chara, you're up. Topic roll: [rolls d6] 5. That's "Software + Hardware + Startups." What's your take?

**CHARA:** I'm looking at the TPMOS node telemetry. 600 nodes. 4,000 active users. 800K BSR/week flow. The network is healthy. The P2P sync is stable. The FuzzLab simulations are completing on time. The AR battle engine is running at 30 frames per second. The Soul Pen publishing pipeline is processing 12 stories per day. Everything is working. Everything is healthy. Everything is... alive.

**Mother Tmojizu:** Roll INT Observation. You're noticing something deeper.

**CHARA:** [rolls] 17. Plus 4 is 21.

**Mother Tmojizu:** 21. What do you see?

**CHARA:** I see... I see the people behind the nodes. Node 47 is a Raspberry Pi in a college dorm. The operator is a sophomore. Computer science major. Runs FuzzLab simulations between classes. Earns BSR. Spends it on Soul Pen stories. Writes about a character who's trying to find their way home. Node 112 is an old ThinkPad in a library. The operator is a retired teacher. Runs quest board matching. Earns BSR. Spends it on AR battle stakes. Watches the tournaments. Cheers for the underdogs. Node 203 is a server blade in a garage. The operator is a laid-off engineer. Runs heavy simulations. Earns BSR. Spends it on creator shop blueprints. Designs robot arms. Sells them. Makes enough to pay rent. Node 340 is a hotel room node. The operator is a traveler. Passes through. Stays three days. Runs light tasks. Earns BSR. Spends it on tea. Drinks it. Leaves a review: "Best tea I've ever had. Best community I've ever been part of. I'll be back."

**Mother Tmojizu:** [quietly] 21. The audience is quiet. The kind of quiet that means they're seeing it too. Not nodes. People.

**CHARA:** I'm looking at the cafe build-out budget. $60,000. We have $51. And IQabella's $4,000. We're short $55,949. I've been researching tenant improvement allowances, equipment financing, and SBA loans. The SBA loan is the most viable option. 10-year term. 6.5% interest. We'd need collateral. We don't have collateral. But the network does. 600 nodes. That's compute. That's value. I'm exploring whether we can use the network as collateral. I've started the paperwork. I'm on page 47 of 200. I've highlighted 12 sections that don't apply to us. I've drafted 8 addendums that do. I'm going to finish it this week. Because the people behind the nodes deserve a home. And the home is the cafe. And the cafe needs doors. And doors need funding. And funding needs paperwork. And paperwork needs me. And I'm here.

**Mother Tmojizu:** [warmly] You are.

**Mother Tmojizu:** IQabella, you're up. Topic roll: [rolls d6] 1. That's "Tea + The Cafe + Their Lives." What's on your heart?

**IQabella:** I'm thinking about the menu. And I want to talk about why the menu matters. It's not just tea and coffee. It's baklava. Fudge. Pastries. Homemade. By volunteers. By community. By people who believe in this. The menu is the first thing people experience when they walk in. If the tea is good, they stay. If the baklava is good, they come back. If the fudge is good, they tell their friends. The menu is marketing. The menu is community. The menu is love.

**Mother Tmojizu:** Roll CHA Hospitality. You're designing a menu that builds community.

**IQabella:** [rolls] 20. Plus 3 is 23.

**Mother Tmojizu:** NATURAL 20. The menu is legendary. People will come from other cities for the baklava. The tea is perfect. The coffee is locally roasted. The pastries are homemade. The fudge is... I don't even have words for the fudge. It's that good.

**IQabella:** [in character] I'm also adding a "Pay What You Can" option. If you can't afford tea, you drink anyway. The community covers it. That's not charity. That's the model. Always. Because I remember what it was like to make tea for six years at a place that called it "artisan." It was dust. And I remember the first time I made real tea. Loose leaf. 175 degrees. Two minutes. Ceramic cup. No logo. Just tea. And I cried. Because I didn't know tea could taste like that. And I thought: if I didn't know, how many other people don't know? And the answer is: everyone. Everyone doesn't know. And that's why we're building the cafe. Not to sell tea. To teach tea. To share tea. To bridge tea. To say: "Here. Try this. This is what it's supposed to taste like. This is what you've been missing. This is what you deserve."

**Mother Tmojizu:** [quietly] 23. The audience is crying. Horli is crying. Chara is crying. Sol-Supreme is... also crying. This is becoming a pattern.

**IQabella:** And I'm thinking about the people. The regulars. The lockin crew. The 40 people who stay from 10 PM to 6 AM. Three nights a week. They're not just gaming. They're working. Coding. Researching. Writing. Building. And someone is always brewing tea. Always. It doesn't matter what they're doing. Coding. Researching. Writing. Battling. Streaming. Someone is brewing tea. And someone else is drinking it. And someone else is asking for a cup. And someone else is pouring. Tea is the bridge. It's the thing that connects people who wouldn't otherwise connect. And I'm the one who built the bridge. And I'm proud of it. And I'm scared of it. Because bridges can break. And if the bridge breaks, the people on the other side... they're alone again. And I can't let that happen. So I'll keep brewing. 175 degrees. Two minutes. Ceramic cup. No logo. Just tea. And the bridge will hold. Because I'll hold it.

**Mother Tmojizu:** [warmly] It will.

**Mother Tmojizu:** Sol-Supreme, you're up. Topic roll: [rolls d6] 4. That's "Research + The Future + Their Lives." What's your take?

**Sol-Supreme:** I'm thinking about the night I found the server rack. The thrift store. $20. Four old Dell OptiPlexes. Core 2 Duos. 4GB RAM each. Spinning hard drives. One had a cracked case. All of them still boot. I dragged it home on a dolly. Plugged it in. All four machines booted. They were slow. They were old. They were beautiful. And I was sitting on my floor. In my apartment. With $31 in my bank account. And an SSI check that came in — $943. After rent, after food, after utilities... I had $47 left. And a laptop. And an idea. And now four old computers. And I was imaging TPMOS on all of them. Stripped down. No GUI. Just the essentials: Linux kernel, P2P networking, quest board daemon, TPMOS services. 200MB. Runs on 512MB RAM. Boots in 8 seconds. And I was watching them sync. Four nodes. One network. Decentralized. Resilient. Free. And I was crying. Because I didn't know I could build something. I didn't know I could build anything. I'd been coding my whole life. Alone. In my room. On my laptop. Building things no one would ever see. And now four old computers were talking to each other. Because of me. Because of code I wrote. Because of an idea I had. And I was crying. And I didn't stop. And I haven't stopped. Not since.

**Mother Tmojizu:** Roll INT Vulnerability. You're sharing something you don't usually share.

**Sol-Supreme:** [rolls] 16. Plus 1 is 17.

**Mother Tmojizu:** 17. The audience hears it. The real thing. Not the network. Not the nodes. Not the code. The person behind the code. The one who cried on the floor. The one who didn't know they could build something. The one who built it anyway.

**Sol-Supreme:** And I'm thinking about the people. The ones behind the nodes. Node 47. The sophomore. Node 112. The retired teacher. Node 203. The laid-off engineer. Node 340. The traveler. I built the network for them. Not for the tech. Not for the research. Not for the BSR. For them. For the person in the dorm. The person in the library. The person in the garage. The person in the hotel room. The person who needs a place to belong. And the network is that place. And the cafe is that place. And the tea is that place. And I'm that place. And I'm proud of it. And I'm scared of it. Because places can close. And if the place closes, the people who belong there... they're alone again. And I can't let that happen. So I'll keep building. Keep coding. Keep syncing. Keep the network alive. Because the network is the place. And the place is the home. And the home is the tea. And the tea is the bridge. And the bridge is the people. And the people are us. And we're here. And we're not leaving.

**Mother Tmojizu:** [warmly] You're not.
