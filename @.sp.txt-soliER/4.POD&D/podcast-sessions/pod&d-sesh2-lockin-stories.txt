**IQabella:** [rolls] 19. Plus 3 is 22. I made tea for six years at a place that called it "artisan." It was dust. And I wrote a blog post about it. "Why Your Tea Tastes Like Regret." 50,000 views in a month. People commented: "I've been making tea wrong my whole life." "I didn't know tea could taste like this." "Where can I get tea like this?" Six years of dreaming about something better. And I put it all in. Not as a loan. Not as an investment. As a contribution. As belief. And every night during lockin, when I'm brewing tea for 40 people who are coding and researching and writing and battling — I think: this is what belief tastes like. It tastes like green tea. 175 degrees. Two minutes. Ceramic cup. No logo. Just tea. And I think about the people. The regulars. The lockin crew. The 40 people who stay from 10 PM to 6 AM. Three nights a week. They're not just gaming. They're working. Coding. Researching. Writing. Building. And someone is always brewing tea. Always. It doesn't matter what they're doing. Coding. Researching. Writing. Battling. Streaming. Someone is brewing tea. And someone else is drinking it. And someone else is asking for a cup. And someone else is pouring. And I'm the one who pours. And I'm proud of it. And I'm scared of it. Because bridges can break. And if the bridge breaks, the people on the other side... they're alone again. And I can't let that happen. So I'll keep brewing. 175 degrees. Two minutes. Ceramic cup. No logo. Just tea. And the bridge will hold. Because I'll hold it. And because they hold it too. The lockin crew. The regulars. The ducklings. They hold the bridge with me. And that's what makes it strong. Not the tea. The people. Always the people.
