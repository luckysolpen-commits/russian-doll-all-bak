INT. GLITCH WORLD - ABANDONED APARTMENT - NIGHT

Lucky and Kael have found temporary refuge in a dilapidated, abandoned apartment building on the outskirts of the Glitch World's city. The windows are shattered, revealing a distorted cityscape where buildings occasionally stretch and warp before snapping back into place. The air inside is stale, but it offers a semblance of privacy from the ever-present drones and disguised enforcers.

Lucky paces, agitated. He keeps clenching and unclenching his fists, trying to replicate the brief glitch he experienced earlier.

LUCKY
(Frustrated)
I keep trying, but nothing happens. How do these 'glitch powers' work? What are they?

Kael sits on a broken chair, his eyes closed, a faint, almost imperceptible shimmer around his form. He's concentrating, trying to understand the underlying code of this false reality.

KAEL
(Eyes still closed, voice strained)
They're not magic, Lucky. Not in the Avanta.C sense. This world is a program. A highly complex, but ultimately flawed, simulation. Our minds, our consciousness, are interacting with its core code. The glitches are a byproduct of that interaction. A manifestation of our will, our resistance, against the system's intended parameters.

He opens his eyes. They glow with a faint, digital blue light for a moment before returning to normal.

KAEL
(Cont.)
Think of it as... code injection. We're not supposed to be able to do this. The system tries to correct it, but our minds are forcing a deviation. The more we push, the more the system struggles to maintain its illusion.

Kael extends his hand towards a cracked wall. He concentrates, his brow furrowed. For a moment, nothing happens. Then, the cracks on the wall begin to shift, to rearrange themselves into a complex, geometric pattern. It's not a physical change, but a visual one, like a texture map being corrupted. The pattern holds for a few seconds, then flickers and reverts to normal.

LUCKY
(Amazed)
You changed the wall!

KAEL
(A faint smile)
Not physically. I corrupted its visual rendering. A minor aesthetic glitch. But it proves the principle. We can manipulate the data streams that create this reality. The question is, to what extent?

Lucky tries again. He focuses on a small, broken shard of glass on the floor. He imagines it becoming whole, shimmering, perfect. He pushes with his mind. Nothing. He pushes harder. Still nothing.

LUCKY
(Defeated)
It's not working for me.

KAEL
(Standing, walking towards him)
It's about intent. And understanding the system's vulnerabilities. Your power might manifest differently. Try something simpler. Something that directly affects your own avatar's rendering.

Lucky thinks. He remembers the brief multiplication of his fingers. He focuses on his hand, imagining it becoming transparent, like a ghost.

He concentrates, pushing the thought. His hand shimmers, becoming translucent for a split second, then solidifies. He gasps.

LUCKY
(Excited)
I did it! I went... see-through!

KAEL
(Nodding)
Excellent. That's a direct manipulation of your avatar's rendering data. Now, try to hold it. Try to control it.

Lucky tries again, focusing intently. His hand becomes translucent, then his arm, then his entire upper body. He can see the faint outlines of the room through himself. He feels a strange, disembodied sensation, but also a surge of exhilaration.

LUCKY
(Cont.)
This is... amazing! What else can we do?

KAEL
(His eyes gleaming with renewed purpose)
That's what we need to discover. We need to experiment. To push the boundaries of this simulation. If we can manipulate the code of this world, we can find its weaknesses. We can find a way out. Or, at the very least, make their brainwashing attempts... less effective.

He walks to the shattered window, looking out at the glitched cityscape. The drones still patrol, the disguised enforcers still blend in. But now, Lucky and Kael have a new weapon. A weapon forged from the very fabric of their prison.

KAEL
(Cont.)
This is our new battlefield, Lucky. And our glitch powers... they are our new abilities. We need to master them. Because our lives, and our minds, depend on it.

FADE OUT.
