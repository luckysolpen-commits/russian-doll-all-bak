INT. GLITCH WORLD - DOWNTOWN STREETS - DAY

Lucky and Kael, now more attuned to the subtle distortions of the Glitch World, move through the downtown streets. The city still appears vibrant, but to their heightened senses, it's a tapestry of constant, unsettling anomalies. A building in the distance stretches impossibly tall, then shrinks back. A pedestrian's face momentarily pixelates into a mosaic of colors before resolving. The sound of a car horn warps into a distorted, digital shriek, then corrects itself.

They are actively searching now, not just observing. Kael, using his enhanced perception, points out subtle code overlays that flicker in his vision, invisible to the uninitiated.

KAEL
(Voice low)
See that shimmer around the fountain? It's a data loop. The system is constantly refreshing that particular asset. A sign of heavy processing. Perhaps a central node.

Lucky tries to focus, to see the code, but it's still beyond him. He relies on Kael's guidance. He's more focused on the inhabitants.

They pass a group of people laughing at a street performer. The performer's movements are fluid, but his shadow occasionally detaches and dances independently before snapping back. The audience claps, their hands briefly multiplying into a blur of phantom limbs.

LUCKY
(Whispering)
Are they all like this? Brainwashed?

KAEL
(Shaking his head)
Not all. Some are simply... unaware. They've adapted to the glitches, integrated them into their perception of reality. They don't question it. But others... others are different.

They turn into a quieter side street, lined with cafes. At one table, a woman sits alone, staring blankly at a cup of coffee. Her eyes are dull, her posture slumped. Her image flickers, not with a chaotic glitch, but with a slow, rhythmic fade, as if her very presence is being slowly erased.

LUCKY
(A pang of sympathy)
She's... gone, isn't she?

KAEL
(Grimly)
Her mind has been overwritten. Her identity suppressed. She's a shell. A warning.

Further down the street, they spot a man hunched over a holographic newspaper. He's muttering to himself, his fingers tracing patterns in the air. His movements are jerky, his eyes darting nervously. He's clearly agitated, and his avatar occasionally flickers with sharp, violent distortions, like a broken video feed.

KAEL
(Cont.)
But then there are those who resist. Those whose minds are fighting the overwrite. Their glitches are often more violent, more unpredictable. They're struggling.

They approach the man cautiously. As they get closer, they hear his muttering. It's a string of seemingly random numbers and symbols.

MAN
(Muttering)
...0101... loop... false... 77... the wall... it's not real... 0011...

Lucky realizes the man is speaking in binary, interspersed with fragmented thoughts. He's trying to make sense of the underlying code, but his mind is breaking under the strain.

LUCKY
(To the man, softly)
Are you okay?

The man's head snaps up. His eyes, though wild, hold a spark of desperate intelligence. He sees Lucky and Kael, not as normal citizens, but as fellow anomalies.

MAN
(Voice raspy, urgent)
They're watching! Always watching! The patterns... the loops... they're trying to erase us! You see it, don't you? The cracks in the world!

He points a trembling finger at a seemingly solid brick wall. To Lucky's eyes, it's just a wall. But to the man, and presumably to Kael, it's a shimmering, unstable facade.

KAEL
(To the man, calmly)
We see it. We're trying to understand it. What have you found?

The man's eyes dart around, paranoid. He leans in, whispering conspiratorially.

MAN
(Whispering)
The control. It's in the data streams. They feed us the illusion. But there are... anomalies. Gaps. If you can find the gaps, you can... you can break the loop. The old library. They hide the truth there. In the old data archives.

Suddenly, the man's eyes glaze over. His body stiffens, and his previous agitation vanishes. He smiles, a vacant, unsettling smile.

MAN
(Voice flat, devoid of emotion)
Beautiful day, isn't it? Nothing to see here. Just enjoying the peace.

A subtle, almost imperceptible WHIRRING sound emanates from his pocket. Lucky and Kael exchange a look. An enforcer, disguised as a civilian, has just activated a remote override. The man's resistance has been temporarily suppressed.

Kael quickly pulls Lucky away, blending back into the crowd. The encounter is a stark reminder of the constant threat, and the insidious nature of the Metal Mafia's control.

KAEL
(As they walk away, voice low)
He was close. Too close. The old library... it's a lead. They wouldn't suppress him like that if he wasn't onto something important.

Lucky looks back at the man, now calmly reading his newspaper, his face serene. The horror of it chills him to the bone. They are in a world where minds can be erased with a whisper, where reality is a weapon. But the man's fragmented words, his desperate struggle, have given them a new direction. The old library. The truth.

FADE OUT.
