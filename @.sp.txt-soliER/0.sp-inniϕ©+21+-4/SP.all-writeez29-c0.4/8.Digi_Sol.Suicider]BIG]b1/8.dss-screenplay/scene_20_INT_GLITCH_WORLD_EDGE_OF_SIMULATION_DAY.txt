INT. GLITCH WORLD - EDGE OF SIMULATION - DAY

WHITE. Then, a jarring, disorienting THUD. Lucky lands hard on what feels like solid ground, his body aching, his mind reeling from the chaotic transit. He gasps, sucking in air that smells faintly of ozone and something metallic.

He opens his eyes. The blinding white has given way to a landscape that is both familiar and utterly alien. They are on what appears to be a vast, desolate plain, stretching to a horizon that shimmers and distorts like heat haze. The sky above is a swirling vortex of muted colors – greys, purples, and deep blues – constantly shifting, revealing glimpses of raw, underlying code that flashes like lightning.

This is the edge of the simulation. A place where the Glitch World's illusion struggles to maintain itself, where the raw data of its construction is laid bare.

Anya is beside him, coughing, her face pale but resolute. Elara, still weak, is slumped against her, her glowing butterfly orb flickering faintly, as if struggling to maintain its light in this unstable environment.

ANYA
(Voice hoarse)
We made it. Just barely.

Elara stirs, her eyes fluttering open. She looks at the chaotic sky, then at the shimmering horizon.

ELARA
(Weakly)
This is... the buffer zone. Where the simulation ends, and the raw data begins. It's unstable. Dangerous. But... they can't control it here. Not fully.

Lucky looks back. Behind them, where the portal should have been, is nothing but a shimmering, unstable wall of static, occasionally revealing glimpses of the collapsing Core, a maelstrom of green and red light. The portal is gone. One way indeed.

LUCKY
(His voice filled with a mix of relief and concern)
Kael... he said it was a one-way portal. He's still back there.

Anya looks at the static wall, a grim expression on her face.

ANYA
He bought us time. A lot of time. He knew the risks. Kael's a survivor. If anyone can make it out of that mess, it's him.

Elara, despite her weakness, pushes herself up slightly. Her butterfly orb pulses, and a faint, shimmering map appears before them. It's not the detailed map of the city, but a schematic of the Glitch World's underlying architecture. The Core is a dark, shattered mass. And from it, thin, flickering lines extend, like broken nerves, leading to various points across the vast, unstable plain.

ELARA
(Cont.)
The Core is damaged. But not destroyed. It's trying to reroute. To rebuild. But its control is fractured. The brainwashing protocols... they're weakened. Many will be waking up. But they'll be disoriented. Vulnerable.

She points to several flickering blue dots on the map, scattered across the plain.

ELARA
(Cont.)
Those are... anomalies. Minds that are resisting. Minds that are waking up. They'll be drawn to the edges of the simulation, where the control is weakest. We need to find them. To guide them. Before the Metal Mafia can reassert control.

Suddenly, the ground beneath them trembles. In the distance, a massive, dark shadow moves across the shimmering horizon. It's a Metal Mafia Mothership, its sleek form distorted by the unstable reality, but unmistakably present. It's deploying units. Smaller, faster drones, their red optical sensors like angry pinpricks in the chaotic sky.

ANYA
(Drawing her energy pistol)
They're already here. They're trying to round up the awakened. To re-process them.

Lucky looks at the vast, desolate plain, then at the approaching drones. They are still outnumbered, still outgunned. But now, they have a purpose. A mission. To find the others. To build a resistance. To fight for the minds of those trapped in this digital nightmare.

LUCKY
(A new resolve in his voice)
Then let's go. We have to reach them first.

Elara nods, her butterfly orb glowing brighter, a beacon of hope in the chaotic landscape. Anya takes a defensive stance, her eyes scanning the horizon. The fight for the Glitch World has truly begun.

FADE OUT.
