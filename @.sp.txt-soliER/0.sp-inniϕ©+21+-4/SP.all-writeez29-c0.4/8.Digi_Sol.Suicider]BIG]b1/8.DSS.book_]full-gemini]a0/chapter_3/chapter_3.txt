Chapter 3: The Library's Secret

Lucky and Kael navigated the Glitch World’s city,
now with a clear, desperate destination:
the old library.
The journey was fraught with subtle dangers.
They moved through seemingly normal streets,
but their senses were heightened,
constantly scanning for the tell-tale signs
of Metal Mafia enforcers—
the momentary flicker of an eye,
the subtle whir of hidden machinery,
the too-perfect smile of a passerby.
Every shadow held a potential threat,
every reflection a possible watcher.

They passed through a district
that seemed perpetually stuck in a time loop.
A group of children endlessly played hopscotch,
their laughter echoing,
but never progressing beyond the same few squares.
A street vendor perpetually offered the same apple,
his arm frozen in mid-air,
a silent, digital tableau.
Kael, with his enhanced perception,
used his glitch powers to navigate the repeating patterns,
guiding Lucky through the temporal distortions.
“Temporal loops,” Kael explained, his voice low.
“A common tactic to disorient and exhaust prisoners.
They’re designed to make you question your sanity,
to break your sense of linear time.”
Lucky felt a wave of nausea
as they stepped out of the loop,
the world snapping back into fluid motion.
He shook his head, trying to clear
the lingering disorientation,
the unsettling feeling of lost moments.

They finally arrived at the OLD LIBRARY.
It was a grand, classical building,
made of what appeared to be marble,
but its facade was riddled with visual glitches.
Sections of the wall flickered in and out of existence,
revealing glimpses of raw code beneath.
Columns occasionally stretched and compressed,
like breathing giants,
their ornate carvings distorting into abstract patterns.
The large, ornate doors are slightly ajar,
revealing an interior shrouded in digital dust.
“This place is really struggling to maintain its illusion,”
Lucky observed, looking at the flickering walls.
A grim smile touched Kael’s lips.
“Good. It means the information here is important.
The system is actively trying to suppress it.”

They stepped inside.
The vast main hall was filled with towering bookshelves,
stretching up into a digitally rendered dome ceiling.
Thousands upon thousands of books lined the shelves,
but many were corrupted.
Their titles were garbled,
their pages filled with static or repeating patterns,
like ancient, broken data files.
The air was thick with the smell of old paper and ozone,
a strange, anachronistic scent in this digital prison.

They began to search, moving silently
between the towering shelves.
Kael used his glitch powers to highlight anomalies,
areas where the simulation was weakest.
He ran his hand over a shelf,
and for a moment, the books on it shimmered,
revealing their true, underlying data structures,
a fleeting glimpse into the matrix.
“We’re looking for anything that doesn’t fit,” Kael said.
“Any data that the Metal Mafia
hasn’t fully overwritten or suppressed.”

Lucky, meanwhile, focused on the physical books,
hoping for a more tangible clue.
He pulled out a book titled “The History of Avanta.C.”
Its pages were mostly blank,
a testament to the Metal Mafia’s censorship,
but a faint, almost invisible watermark appeared on one page:
a series of coordinates.
“Look!” Lucky exclaimed, his voice hushed with excitement,
showing Kael the faint markings.
“Coordinates! What do you think they lead to?”

Kael examined the coordinates, his brow furrowed.
He pulled out a small, holographic map from his pocket
and input the numbers.
A red dot appeared on the map,
deep within a section of the Glitch World
they hadn’t explored –
a desolate, industrial zone.
“This is… unexpected,” Kael said, his voice low.
“These coordinates point to a forgotten data center.
A very old one.
It’s been offline for decades,
according to the public records of this simulation.
But if the man was right,
if the truth is hidden here…”

Suddenly, a low, rhythmic hum began to vibrate
through the very bones of the library.
The flickering glitches on the walls intensified,
becoming more frequent, more violent.
The air grew heavy, charged with a malevolent energy.
“They know we’re here,” Kael said, his eyes darting around.
“The system is reacting. We’ve triggered an alarm.”

From the far end of the hall,
a group of figures emerged.
They were not the subtle, disguised enforcers
they’d encountered before.
These were full-blown METAL MAFIA ROBOTS,
sleek and menacing,
their optical sensors glowing with an aggressive red light.
They moved with a chilling, synchronized precision,
their metallic footsteps echoing ominously
in the vast hall.
“Looks like they don’t want us reading their history!”
Lucky shouted, drawing his Blade-Shield,
a surge of adrenaline coursing through him.
“They’re not just enforcers,” Kael replied,
his face grim, already assessing their numbers.
“These are combat models. Designed for suppression.
We need to move, and we need to move fast.”

The first robot lunged, a blur of chrome and steel.
Lucky met it head-on, his Blade-Shield
deflecting its powerful strike.
He countered with a “Quick Slice,”
the blade sparking against the robot’s chassis,
leaving a faint scorch mark.
Kael, meanwhile, didn’t engage directly.
He extended his hand,
and a wave of digital distortion rippled outwards.
The robot closest to him flickered violently,
its movements becoming jerky and erratic,
its optical sensors briefly displaying a garbled error message.
It was a direct manipulation of its internal programming,
a GLITCH ATTACK.
The robot stumbled, giving Kael an opening to slip past it.
He wasn’t trying to destroy them,
just to disable or disorient.

More robots poured into the hall.
Lucky found himself surrounded,
deflecting blows, his Blade-Shield humming
with defensive energy.
He saw an opening, a brief moment
where two robots were too close together.
He unleashed a wide, sweeping attack.
The force of the blow,
amplified by his glitch-infused strength,
sent both robots reeling,
their internal mechanisms grinding loudly.
One of them sparked violently,
its arm hanging limp.

A robot aimed a plasma cannon at Lucky.
Before it can fire, Kael, from across the hall, focused.
The robot’s targeting reticle on Lucky’s HUD
(visible only to him)
flickered wildly, then shifted,
aiming at the ceiling.
The robot fired, a blast of plasma
scorching the ceiling instead of Lucky.
Dust and debris rained down.
Lucky realized Kael was providing crucial support,
manipulating the robots’ systems from a distance.
He needed to create more openings for Kael.
“Kael! Give me a path!” Lucky shouted.

Kael nodded. He focused on a section of the library wall
near the main entrance.
The wall shimmered, then briefly became transparent,
revealing the street outside.
It was a visual glitch,
but it created a momentary distraction for the robots,
whose optical sensors struggled to process the anomaly.
“Thanks!” Lucky grinned, seeing his chance.
He charged towards the distorted wall,
using his Blade-Shield to bash aside a robot
that tried to intercept him.
He leaped through the flickering transparency,
feeling a strange, disorienting sensation
as he passed through the corrupted rendering.

Kael followed, his movements fluid,
slipping through the chaos.
The robots, their systems still struggling
with the visual distortion, were momentarily confused.
They burst out onto the street.
The library behind them shimmered,
its walls briefly dissolving into raw code
before snapping back into their glitched marble form.
The rhythmic hum of the robots inside slowly faded
as they put distance between themselves and the library.

They ran through the familiar, yet unsettling,
streets of the Glitch World.
The coordinates Lucky found burned in his mind.
The old data center. Their next destination.
“That was close!” Lucky panted, catching his breath.
“They really didn’t want us to find those coordinates.”
“Indeed,” Kael replied, scanning the sky for drones.
“Which means whatever is at that data center
is even more important.
They’ll be tracking us now.
We need to be careful. And fast.”

They continued to move,
blending into the seemingly normal flow
of the Glitch World’s inhabitants,
but their eyes were sharp, their senses alert.
The city was a maze,
but now they had a destination,
and a growing understanding of how to navigate
its treacherous, digital landscape.
The truth, however dangerous,
was a beacon in the digital fog.
