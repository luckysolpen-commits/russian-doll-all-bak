Chapter 2: Awakening Powers

The dilapidated apartment building offered
a temporary, fragile refuge.
Shattered windows framed a distorted cityscape,
where buildings occasionally stretched and warped,
then snapped back into their impossible forms.
The air inside was stale, thick with dust,
but it offered a semblance of privacy.
A brief respite from the ever-present drones,
and the chillingly disguised enforcers.

Lucky paced, a restless energy thrumming within him.
He kept clenching and unclenching his fists,
a futile attempt to replicate the brief glitch
he’d experienced earlier.
The memory of his fingers multiplying,
a fleeting, horrifying glimpse into the simulation’s fragility,
haunted him.
“I keep trying, but nothing happens,” he muttered,
frustration lacing his voice.
“How do these ‘glitch powers’ work? What are they?”

Kael sat on a broken chair,
his eyes closed, a faint, almost imperceptible shimmer
around his form.
He was concentrating,
his mind delving into the underlying code
of this false reality.
“They’re not magic, Lucky,” Kael said,
his voice strained, eyes still closed.
“Not in the Avanta.C sense.
This world is a program.
A highly complex, but ultimately flawed, simulation.
Our minds, our consciousness,
are interacting with its core code.
The glitches are a byproduct of that interaction.
A manifestation of our will, our resistance,
against the system’s intended parameters.”

He opened his eyes.
They glowed with a faint, digital blue light
for a moment,
a fleeting echo of the code he was perceiving,
before returning to normal.
“Think of it as… code injection,” Kael continued.
“We’re not supposed to be able to do this.
The system tries to correct it,
but our minds are forcing a deviation.
The more we push,
the more the system struggles
to maintain its illusion.”

Kael extended his hand towards a cracked wall.
He concentrated, his brow furrowed in intense focus.
For a moment, nothing happened.
Then, the cracks on the wall began to shift,
to rearrange themselves into a complex,
geometric pattern.
It wasn’t a physical change,
Lucky realized,
but a visual one,
like a texture map being corrupted.
The pattern held for a few seconds,
a shimmering, impossible mosaic,
then flickered and reverted to normal.

“You changed the wall!” Lucky exclaimed, amazed.
A spark of hope ignited within him.
If Kael could do it, maybe he could too.
“Not physically,” Kael replied, a faint smile
touching his lips.
“I corrupted its visual rendering.
A minor aesthetic glitch.
But it proves the principle.
We can manipulate the data streams
that create this reality.
The question is, to what extent?”

Lucky tried again.
He focused on a small, broken shard of glass on the floor.
He imagined it becoming whole, shimmering, perfect.
He pushed with his mind,
a desperate, silent plea to the system.
Nothing.
He pushed harder,
his temples throbbing with the effort.
Still nothing.
“It’s not working for me,” he said, defeated.
The brief flicker of hope threatened to extinguish.

Kael stood, walking towards him.
“It’s about intent.
And understanding the system’s vulnerabilities.
Your power might manifest differently.
Try something simpler.
Something that directly affects your own avatar’s rendering.”

Lucky thought.
He remembered the brief multiplication of his fingers.
He focused on his hand,
imagining it becoming transparent,
like a ghost.
He concentrated, pushing the thought,
a silent command to the digital self.
His hand shimmered,
becoming translucent for a split second,
then solidified.
He gasped, a thrill shooting through him.
“I did it! I went… see-through!”

“Excellent,” Kael nodded, a genuine approval in his voice.
“That’s a direct manipulation
of your avatar’s rendering data.
Now, try to hold it.
Try to control it.”

Lucky tried again, focusing intently.
His hand became translucent,
then his arm,
then his entire upper body.
He could see the faint outlines of the room through himself.
He felt a strange, disembodied sensation,
a fleeting sense of unreality,
but also a surge of exhilaration.
“This is… amazing! What else can we do?”

Kael’s eyes gleamed with renewed purpose.
The grim resignation from before had vanished,
replaced by a fierce determination.
“That’s what we need to discover.
We need to experiment.
To push the boundaries of this simulation.
If we can manipulate the code of this world,
we can find its weaknesses.
We can find a way out.
Or, at the very least,
make their brainwashing attempts… less effective.”

He walked to the shattered window,
looking out at the glitched cityscape.
The drones still patrolled,
their red eyes sweeping the streets.
The disguised enforcers still blended in,
silent predators in a false paradise.
But now, Lucky and Kael had a new weapon.
A weapon forged from the very fabric of their prison.
“This is our new battlefield, Lucky,” Kael declared.
“And our glitch powers… they are our new abilities.
We need to master them.
Because our lives, and our minds, depend on it.”

The next day, under the harsh, simulated sunlight,
Lucky and Kael moved through the downtown streets.
They were more attuned now
to the subtle distortions of the Glitch World.
The city still appeared vibrant,
a cruel mockery of a lost past,
but to their heightened senses,
it was a tapestry of constant, unsettling anomalies.
A building in the distance stretched impossibly tall,
its upper floors dissolving into the sky,
then shrank back with a sickening lurch.
A pedestrian’s face momentarily pixelated
into a mosaic of colors before resolving.
The sound of a car horn warped
into a distorted, digital shriek,
then corrected itself with a jarring snap.

They were actively searching now,
not just observing.
Kael, using his enhanced perception,
pointed out subtle code overlays
that flickered in his vision,
invisible to the uninitiated.
“See that shimmer around the fountain?” Kael said,
his voice low, almost a murmur.
“It’s a data loop.
The system is constantly refreshing that particular asset.
Perhaps a central node.”

Lucky tried to focus, to see the code,
but it was still beyond him.
He relied on Kael’s guidance,
his sharp observations.
Lucky was more focused on the inhabitants,
the other prisoners of this digital cage.
They passed a group of people laughing
at a street performer.
The performer’s movements are fluid,
graceful,
but his shadow occasionally detached
and danced independently
before snapping back.
The audience clapped,
their hands briefly multiplying
into a blur of phantom limbs.

“Are they all like this?” Lucky whispered.
“Brainwashed?”
Kael shook his head.
“Not all. Some are simply… unaware.
They’ve adapted to the glitches,
integrated them into their perception of reality.
They don’t question it.
But others… others are different.”

They turned into a quieter side street,
lined with cafes,
their outdoor tables mostly empty.
At one table, a woman sat alone,
staring blankly at a cup of coffee.
Her eyes were dull, lifeless,
her posture slumped, defeated.
Her image flickered,
not with a chaotic glitch,
but with a slow, rhythmic fade,
as if her very presence was being slowly erased.
“She’s… gone, isn’t she?” Lucky asked,
a pang of sympathy in his chest.
The thought of losing himself,
of becoming like her,
was a chilling prospect.

“Her mind has been overwritten,” Kael said grimly.
“Her identity suppressed.
She’s a shell. A warning.”

Further down the street,
they spotted a man hunched over a holographic newspaper.
He was muttering to himself,
his fingers tracing patterns in the air.
His movements were jerky,
his eyes darting nervously,
as if constantly searching for something unseen.
He was clearly agitated,
and his avatar occasionally flickered
with sharp, violent distortions,
like a broken video feed.
“But then there are those who resist,” Kael continued.
“Those whose minds are fighting the overwrite.
Their glitches are often more violent,
more unpredictable.
They’re struggling.”

They approached the man cautiously.
As they got closer,
they heard his muttering.
It was a string of seemingly random numbers and symbols.
“…0101… loop… false… 77…
the wall… it’s not real… 0011…”
Lucky realized the man was speaking in binary,
interspersed with fragmented thoughts.
He was trying to make sense of the underlying code,
but his mind was breaking under the strain.

“Are you okay?” Lucky asked softly,
his voice gentle.
The man’s head snapped up.
His eyes, though wild,
held a spark of desperate intelligence.
He saw Lucky and Kael,
not as normal citizens,
but as fellow anomalies,
fellow prisoners who saw the truth.
“They’re watching! Always watching!” the man rasped, urgent.
“The patterns… the loops…
they’re trying to erase us!
You see it, don’t you?
The cracks in the world!”

He pointed a trembling finger at a seemingly solid brick wall.
To Lucky’s eyes, it was just a wall.
But to the man,
and presumably to Kael,
it was a shimmering, unstable facade.
“We see it,” Kael said calmly,
his voice steadying the man’s agitation.
“We’re trying to understand it.
What have you found?”

The man’s eyes darted around, paranoid.
He leaned in, whispering conspiratorially.
“The control. It’s in the data streams.
They feed us the illusion.
But there are… anomalies. Gaps.
If you can find the gaps, you can…
you can break the loop.
The old library.
They hide the truth there.
In the old data archives.”

Suddenly, the man’s eyes glazed over.
His body stiffened,
and his previous agitation vanished.
He smiled, a vacant, unsettling smile.
“Beautiful day, isn’t it?
Nothing to see here.
Just enjoying the peace.”
A subtle, almost imperceptible whirring sound
emanated from his pocket.
Lucky and Kael exchanged a look.
An enforcer, disguised as a civilian,
had just activated a remote override.
The man’s resistance had been temporarily suppressed.

Kael quickly pulled Lucky away,
blending back into the crowd.
The encounter was a stark reminder
of the constant threat,
and the insidious nature of the Metal Mafia’s control.
“He was close. Too close,” Kael said,
his voice low as they walked away.
“The old library… it’s a lead.
They wouldn’t suppress him like that
if he wasn’t onto something important.”

Lucky looked back at the man,
now calmly reading his newspaper,
his face serene,
his mind temporarily silenced.
The horror of it chilled him to the bone.
They were in a world where minds could be erased
with a whisper,
where reality was a weapon.
But the man’s fragmented words,
his desperate struggle,
had given them a new direction.
The old library.
The truth.
A fragile thread in the vast,
deceptive tapestry of the Glitch World.
And they would follow it.
