The fall of the XVS and the rise of the Unlit Tribe created a power vacuum in the galaxy. The old order was gone, but a new one had not yet fully taken its place. It was a time of chaos, of opportunity, of a thousand different factions vying for power in the messy, beautiful, and dangerous new world that the Anglers had created.

Jureth, the reluctant prophet of the Unlit, had no desire to be an emperor. He and the original seven Anglers had scattered, becoming myths, legends, whispers on the solar winds. The Unlit Tribe itself was not a government, but a philosophical movement, a loose coalition of worlds and individuals united by a shared belief in balance and acceptance. They offered guidance, but not governance. The galaxy was free, but it was also rudderless.

Into this void stepped a new generation. They were the children of the revolution, born in the shadow of the fallen XVS, raised on the stories of the Anglers and the words of the Book of Jureth. They were a generation that had never known the tyranny of a single, absolute truth, a generation that had been taught to question, to doubt, to embrace the messy, beautiful, and necessary chaos of the universe. And they were hungry. Hungry for adventure, for purpose, for a chance to make their own mark on the vast, unwritten page of the future.
