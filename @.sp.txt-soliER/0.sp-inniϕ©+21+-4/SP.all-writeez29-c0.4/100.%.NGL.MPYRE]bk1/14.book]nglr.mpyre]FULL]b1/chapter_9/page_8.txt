Their first target was a small, insignificant moon that was a key hub in the Sol Pen’s vast, shadowy network. It was a place of secrets, of lies, of a thousand different deals made in the dark. It was a place that was about to have a very bad day.

The crew of the ‘Straylight’ did not attack the moon with weapons, but with a story. A story of a ghost ship, a phantom, a rumor on the solar winds. A story of a crew of misfits and outcasts who had stumbled upon a secret that was so vast, so dangerous, that it could tear the galaxy apart. A story that was just true enough to be believable, and just false enough to be deniable.

The story spread like a virus through the Sol Pen’s network, a seed of doubt in the perfect, orderly machine of their power. The puppet masters, for the first time in millennia, began to look over their shoulders. They began to question their allies, to doubt their own security. The perfect, silent, and utterly ruthless machine of their power was beginning to grind to a halt, its gears gummed up by the messy, beautiful, and necessary chaos of a single, well-told lie.
