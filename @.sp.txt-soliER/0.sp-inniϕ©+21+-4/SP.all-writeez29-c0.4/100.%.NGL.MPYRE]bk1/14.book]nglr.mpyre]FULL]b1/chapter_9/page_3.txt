The rest of the crew was a collection of strays and runaways. There was a disgraced XVS engineer, a brilliant but disgraced academic who had been kicked out of the university for her heretical ideas, a former thief with a heart of gold, and a silent, enigmatic alien of unknown origin who they had picked up on a forgotten moon. They were a mess, a beautiful, chaotic, and utterly dysfunctional family. And they were about to stumble into a conspiracy that would threaten to plunge the galaxy back into the darkness they had all fought so hard to escape.

Their journey began with a simple cargo run, a delivery of medical supplies to a remote outpost on the edge of the unlit void. It was a routine mission, a milk run. But when they arrived, they found the outpost deserted, its inhabitants vanished, the only clue a single, cryptic message scrawled on a wall: ‘The Sol Pen remember.’

The name was a ghost from a forgotten war, a whisper of a threat that the galaxy had long since believed to be vanquished. But the crew of the ‘Straylight’ knew that some ghosts do not rest easy. They knew that the past is never truly past. And they knew that their simple cargo run had just become something far more dangerous, far more important, and far more interesting.
