Vorith, his power consolidated, his authority absolute, declared war on the Unlit Tribe. He could not allow their heresy to spread. He could not allow the seeds of doubt they had sown to take root. He sent his fleet, his golden armada, to hunt them down, to silence their dangerous, seductive song of freedom.

The Unlit Tribe, outnumbered and outgunned, knew they could not win a conventional war. They were not soldiers, but philosophers, artists, explorers of the unlit void. They had to find a different way to fight. They had to use their enemy’s strength, his rigid, dogmatic belief in a single, absolute truth, against him.

They did not fight back with weapons, but with ideas. They broadcast Jureth’s writings, his ‘Book of Jureth,’ across the galaxy. They sang their songs of doubt, of struggle, of the messy, beautiful, and necessary imperfection of life. They became a virus in the system, a ghost in the machine, a quiet, persistent, and ultimately irresistible force for change.
