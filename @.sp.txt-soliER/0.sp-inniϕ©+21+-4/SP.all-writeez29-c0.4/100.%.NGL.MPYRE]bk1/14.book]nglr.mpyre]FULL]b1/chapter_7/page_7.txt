The newly formed ‘Unlit Tribe,’ a motley crew of disgraced heroes and disillusioned soldiers, found refuge in the unlikeliest of places: a rogue asteroid, a forgotten relic of a long-dead civilization, adrift in the unlit void between the galaxies. It was a harsh, unforgiving place, but it was their own. They were free from the prying eyes of the XVS, free to build a new society, a new way of life, based on the principles of balance, acceptance, and a radical, terrifying, and ultimately liberating freedom.

Jureth, their reluctant prophet, became their leader. He did not rule with an iron fist, but with a quiet, unassuming wisdom. He taught them not what to believe, but how to think. He encouraged them to question, to doubt, to explore the messy, beautiful, and necessary chaos of their own souls. He taught them that the unlit void was not something to be feared, but something to be embraced, a place of infinite possibility, of a quiet, peaceful, and ultimately more honest way of being.

The Unlit Tribe began to grow. They were joined by other outcasts, other heretics, other disillusioned souls who had grown tired of the rigid, dogmatic ways of the XVS. They were a small, but growing, island of sanity in a galaxy that was slowly, surely, tearing itself apart.
