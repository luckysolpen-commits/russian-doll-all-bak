Jureth’s sermon on the unlit path did not win the Anglers their freedom. Vorith, his face a mask of cold fury, declared them heretics and traitors. But the seeds of doubt had been sown. The tribunal was no longer a unified body. Jureth’s words had fractured the XVS, creating a schism that would tear the institution apart. The Anglers were found guilty, but their sentence was not death, but exile. They were to be stripped of their anglers, their connection to the XVS, and cast out into the unlit void they so admired.

The sentence was meant to be a humiliation, a final, crushing defeat. But the Anglers, their faith in the XVS shattered, saw it as a liberation. They were no longer bound by the rigid, dogmatic rules of an institution they no longer believed in. They were free to forge their own path, to explore the messy, beautiful, and necessary chaos of the universe on their own terms.

As they were being led away, a small, but significant, number of the XVS guards, their faces a mixture of fear and hope, laid down their weapons and joined them. They were the first converts to the unlit path, the first members of a new, burgeoning rebellion. The Anglers, the seven who had been chosen to save the multiverse, had become the leaders of a revolution.
