The royal guard were formidable opponents. They were no longer individuals, but extensions of the emperor’s will, their movements perfectly coordinated, their attacks relentless and precise. They fought not with weapons, but with pure, focused blasts of the golden energy, beams of raw power that could melt through the thickest armor. Hana was forced back, her pulse rifle useless against their shields. She switched to kinetic grenades, the concussive blasts staggering the guards, buying her precious seconds, but doing no real damage. They were invulnerable, untouchable, demigods in their own temple.

Jax was a whirlwind of motion, his blue energy sword a stark contrast to the golden onslaught. He was not trying to defeat the guards, but to bypass them, to create a path to the throne. He was a master of close-quarters combat, his movements a deadly dance of parries and dodges. But for every guard he evaded, two more would move to block his path. He was a rock being battered by an inexorable, golden tide. He could feel his strength waning, the sheer, oppressive power of the throne room draining his will, his energy, his very life force.

It was Kato who created the opening. He moved through the shadows like a phantom, unseen, unheard, a ghost in the machine of the emperor’s power. He had identified the flaw in their perfection. The guards were a hive mind, their strength derived from their connection to the emperor. But that connection was also a vulnerability. They were puppets on a string, and Kato was about to cut those strings. He emerged from the shadows behind two of the guards, his hands a blur as he placed small, specialized devices on the back of their necks.

The devices activated, emitting a high-frequency pulse that was calibrated to disrupt the golden energy. The two guards faltered, their movements becoming clumsy, their golden auras flickering. For the first time, a flicker of confusion, of individuality, appeared in their eyes. The hive mind had been momentarily severed. It was a tiny crack in the golden armor, but it was all Jax needed. He surged through the opening, his eyes fixed on the emperor, who watched from his throne with an expression of bored, divine amusement.
