The Anglers’ approach to Nexus was cautious, a stark contrast to their
previous, more direct assaults. They couldn’t simply fly in, guns blazing.
To attack the Nexus with conventional weapons would be like trying to
douse a star with a water pistol. It would be a futile,
suicidal gesture. They needed intelligence. They needed to understand the nature
of this new, golden ‘Power’ before they could even begin to formulate
a plan. This was a mission for a ghost, a spy, a silent
observer who could slip through the cracks of an empire built on
arrogance and ambition.

Kato was dispatched in a stealth ship, a vessel designed to
be invisible to both conventional sensors and psychic detection. His mission:
to infiltrate a Nexus trading outpost on a nearby moon, to observe
the corrupted species in their new, terrifying element, and to find
a weakness, a flaw in their golden armor. The rest of the
team waited on the ‘Stargazer,’ a tense, silent vigil on the
edge of the golden aura. The waiting was a unique form of
torture, a slow, creeping dread that was more unnerving than any
open battle.

Jureth, the young cartographer, found himself drawn to the observation deck,
his gaze fixed on the pulsating, golden sphere of Nexus. He saw
in its arrogant, overwhelming light the antithesis of his visions. His
dreams were of a quiet, peaceful darkness, a universe of subtle,
interconnected energies. Nexus was a roaring, boastful fire, a monument to
the ego, a declaration of dominance over the natural order. He
felt a profound, instinctual revulsion towards it. He began to write
down his thoughts, his visions, his burgeoning philosophy, not as a
scientific document, but as a form of personal catharsis, a way
to make sense of the growing schism in his own soul.

Days later, Kato’s report came through, a tight-beam transmission that was
nothing more than a whisper in the cosmic noise. His findings
were chilling. The Nexus had not just been corrupted by the
‘Power’; they had merged with it. They had become living conduits
of the golden energy, their bodies transformed, their minds consumed by
a singular, all-encompassing desire for more. They were no longer individuals,
but a hive mind, a collective consciousness dedicated to the acquisition
and consolidation of power. They were building a fleet, a golden
armada, with which they intended to spread their new gospel to
the rest of the galaxy.

But Kato had also found a weakness. The ‘Power’ was not
infinite. It was being drawn from a central source, a ‘throne’
on the surface of Nexus where a single, immensely powerful individual
sat, the focal point of the collective. This ‘emperor’ was the
heart of the hive mind, the source of their strength. But
he was also their single point of failure. If he could be
isolated, if the flow of power could be disrupted, even for
a moment, the entire golden empire could come crashing down. The
mission was clear. They had to assassinate an emperor. They had
to perform a heart transplant on a god. It was an impossible,
insane, and utterly necessary plan.
