The explosion was not one of fire and shrapnel, but of pure, silent energy. A wave of blackness erupted from the point of the siphon’s detonation, a perfect sphere of anti-energy that expanded outwards, consuming the golden light. It was not a destructive force, but a negating one. It did not shatter the crystal walls; it simply turned them dark. It did not kill the royal guard; it simply severed their connection to the emperor, their golden auras extinguishing like dying embers, leaving them as ordinary, confused Nexus, their transfigured forms collapsing back into their mortal shapes.

The wave of blackness washed over the emperor, and for a moment, he was simply gone, a hole in the universe. Then, as the blackness receded, he was there again, slumped on his throne, no longer a god, but a man. The golden light was gone, his ethereal form solidified, his eyes no longer burning with the light of a star, but filled with a dawning, horrified understanding of what he had lost. The hive mind was shattered. The golden empire had fallen.

The psychic shockwave of the emperor’s fall washed over the planet. The golden aura that had encased Nexus flickered, and then died, plunging the world into a sudden, shocking darkness. On the surface, a billion minds that had been one were suddenly, violently, returned to their individual consciousness. The silence of the hive mind was replaced by a cacophony of a billion terrified, confused voices. The world of Nexus, a world that had known only the unity of absolute power, was now a world of chaos, of fear, of a terrifying, unfamiliar freedom.

In the darkened throne room, Jax, Hana, and Kato, their bodies bruised and battered, their minds reeling from the psychic backlash, knew they had only seconds to escape. Kael, his voice a triumphant shout over the comms, locked onto their position. “I’ve got you!” he yelled. “Hold on!” The world dissolved into a torrent of light as the teleporter beam snatched them from the heart of the fallen empire, leaving behind a broken god on a darkened throne, and a world that would have to learn to live with the terrible, beautiful burden of being free.
