The Unlit Tribe grew, its ranks swelled by a diverse collection of beings from a thousand different worlds. They were a testament to the power of Jureth’s message, a living embodiment of a universe that was not a monolith, but a beautiful, chaotic, and constantly evolving mosaic.

Vorith, his power base crumbling, his ideology in ruins, launched a final, desperate gambit. He unleashed his ultimate weapon, a fleet of ships armed with a device that could not just destroy a world, but erase it from existence, leaving behind nothing but a cold, silent, and utterly empty void. It was the ultimate expression of his rigid, dogmatic belief in a single, absolute truth: if something did not conform to his reality, it should not exist at all.

The target of this new, terrible weapon was a small, insignificant moon that had become a haven for Unlit refugees. It was a test, a message to the galaxy: submit, or be erased. The Unlit Tribe, their hearts filled with a mixture of terror and resolve, knew they had to make a stand. They had to show the galaxy that there was another way, a way that was not based on the tyranny of a single, absolute truth, but on the messy, beautiful, and necessary chaos of a universe that was not a machine, but a living, breathing, and constantly evolving work of art.
