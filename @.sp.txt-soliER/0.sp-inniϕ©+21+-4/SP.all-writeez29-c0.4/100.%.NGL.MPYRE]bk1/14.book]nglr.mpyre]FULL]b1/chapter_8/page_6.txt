The aftermath of Vorith’s fall was not a celebration, but a quiet, sober moment of reflection. The galaxy had been saved, but it was a galaxy that was scarred, broken, and profoundly changed. The old ways were gone, the old institutions shattered. The future was a blank page, a vast, unlit void of infinite possibility.

The Unlit Tribe, their numbers now swelled by the disillusioned soldiers of Vorith’s fallen empire, found themselves at the center of this new, uncertain world. They were no longer a rebellion, but a government, a council of artists, and philosophers, and wanderers, tasked with the impossible, and beautiful, and necessary job of building a new world from the ashes of the old.

They did not create a new empire, a new set of rules, a new, single, absolute truth. They created a space for a thousand different truths, a thousand different ways of being. They created a galaxy that was not a monolith, but a beautiful, chaotic, and constantly evolving mosaic. They created a universe that was not a machine, but a work of art.
