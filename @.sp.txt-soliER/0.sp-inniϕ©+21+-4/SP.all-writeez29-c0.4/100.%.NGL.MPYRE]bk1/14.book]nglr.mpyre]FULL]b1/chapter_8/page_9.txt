But the universe is a vast and complex place, and the echoes of the past are not so easily silenced. The Sol Pen, the shadowy architects of the war, were not defeated, but had simply retreated into the unlit void, their motives as inscrutable as ever. They were a patient, ancient enemy, and they were not done with the galaxy.

The Unlit Tribe, for all their wisdom, were not a monolith. The seeds of dissent, of ambition, of the old, familiar lust for power, began to sprout in the fertile ground of their new, chaotic freedom. The galaxy was a messy, beautiful, and dangerous place, and the war for its soul was far from over.

But that is a story for another time. For now, let us leave them to their messy, beautiful, and necessary chaos. Let us leave them to their art. Let us leave them to their freedom.
