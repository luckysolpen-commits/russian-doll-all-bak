The journey through the portal was a disorienting experience, a whirlwind
of sensory overload that tested the limits of even the most
seasoned interdimensional travelers. Time and space seemed to lose all
meaning, replaced by a chaotic symphony of light and sound.
But the Anglers were no ordinary travelers. They were the XVS,
and they were trained to withstand the rigors of multiversal travel.
They held their positions, their minds focused on the mission that
lay ahead, their bodies braced against the turbulent currents of the
portal. And then, just as suddenly as it had begun, it
was over. The kaleidoscope of colors outside their viewscreen coalesced
into a single, breathtaking image: the planet Neutrino Prime.

It was a world of stunning beauty, a vibrant, bioluminescent sphere
that pulsed with a soft, ethereal light. Its oceans, which covered
the majority of its surface, were a swirling tapestry of iridescent
colors, a living, breathing work of art. The continents, what
little there were of them, were lush and verdant, their forests
of glowing flora casting an otherworldly glow on the surrounding
landscape. It was a paradise, a testament to the infinite
creativity of the multiverse. But as the 'Stargazer' drew closer,
the Anglers began to see the subtle signs of the corruption
that Vorith had warned them about. A faint, almost imperceptible
darkness seemed to cling to the planet, a shadowy veil that
dimmed its vibrant colors and cast a pall over its breathtaking
beauty.

"I'm picking up strange energy readings," Zara said, her voice tense.
"They're not like anything I've ever encountered before. It's like... a
void. A pocket of nothingness in the middle of all this
life." Dr. Luna, her eyes glued to her own console, nodded
in agreement. "I see it too," she said, her voice a
whisper of awe and trepidation. "It's a complete absence of
energy, a null-zone. It shouldn't be possible. It defies the
laws of physics." Kael, ever the pragmatist, zoomed in on
the source of the energy readings. "It seems to be emanating
from the largest continent," he said, his finger tracing a line
on the holographic map. "That's where we'll make our landing."

Jax nodded his approval. "Take us in, Kael," he said,
his voice calm and steady. "And be prepared for anything."
The 'Stargazer' descended through the planet's atmosphere, its hull shimmering
as it cut through the thick, humid air. The world below
them was even more beautiful up close, a symphony of light
and color that was almost overwhelming. But the closer they
got, the more they could feel the oppressive weight of the
'Darkness.' It was a palpable force, a suffocating presence that
seemed to drain the very life out of the air. The
ship landed in a clearing in the heart of a glowing
forest, its landing gear sinking into the soft, mossy ground.
For a moment, there was only silence, the Anglers taking in
the alien beauty of their surroundings. Then, the ship's ramp
lowered, and they stepped out onto the soil of Neutrino Prime,
the first outsiders to set foot on this world in centuries.
