A piercing shriek, a sound of pure agony and rage, erupted
from the Heartstone. It was the voice of the 'Darkness,' a
creature of shadow and hate, being torn from its host. The
black, shadowy tendrils that had wrapped themselves around the crystal
recoiled as if burned, dissolving into nothingness under the intense,
purifying light. The sickly green glow of the Heartstone flickered,
then died, replaced by a soft, warm, and healthy blue, the
color it was always meant to be. The light from the
Anglers' combined power intensified, a nova of pure energy that
expanded outwards from the center of the village.

The wave of light washed over the horde of corrupted creatures.
There was no explosion, no violence, only a silent, beautiful transformation.
The monstrous forms twisted and contorted, not in pain, but in
release. The 'Darkness' was burned away, leaving behind the creatures
as they were meant to be: the native fauna of Neutrino
Prime, confused and scared, but no longer slaves to a malevolent
will. They blinked in the sudden brightness, then turned and fled
back into the glowing forests from whence they came, their nightmare
finally over.

The light then washed over the Neutrinos, who were still standing
in their trance-like state. As the energy touched them, a collective
gasp rippled through the crowd. Their dull, listless skin began
to glow with its former vibrancy, their vacant eyes clearing, the
spark of intelligence and life returning. They looked at each other,
at the Anglers, at the purified Heartstone, a slow dawning of
understanding on their faces. The 'Darkness' was gone. They were
free.

As the last of the light faded, the seven Anglers collapsed
to their knees, the immense effort having drained them completely. The
circle was broken, their individual anglers dimming to a faint, tired
glow. They were exhausted, their bodies aching, but a sense of
triumphant relief washed over them. They had faced the 'Darkness'
and emerged victorious. They had saved a world. Lyren rushed
to their side, his face a mixture of awe and gratitude.
"You... you did it," he stammered, his voice thick with emotion.
"You saved us." Jax, breathing heavily, managed a weak smile.
"It's what we do," he said simply. The first battle was
won, but the war was far from over. This was just
the beginning of their journey, a single note in the grand,
cosmic symphony of the multiverse.
