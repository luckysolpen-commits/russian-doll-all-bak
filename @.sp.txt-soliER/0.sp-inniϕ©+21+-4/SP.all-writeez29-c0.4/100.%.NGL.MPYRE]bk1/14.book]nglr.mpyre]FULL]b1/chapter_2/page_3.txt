"This is worse than Neutrino Prime," Hana said, her voice a
hushed whisper as she stared at the chaotic scene on the
viewscreen. "At least there, the 'Darkness' was a subtle, creeping
threat. Here, it's a raging inferno." Jax nodded grimly. "The
Xerxes were always a volatile people," he said. "Their creativity
was matched only by their capacity for destruction. The 'Madness'
has simply amplified what was already there." He turned to
Kael. "Find us a safe place to land," he said.
"And be prepared for a hot reception."

Kael, his fingers a blur on the holographic controls, found a
relatively stable landing zone on the outskirts of a sprawling,
ruined city. The 'Stargazer' descended through the thick, polluted atmosphere,
its hull shimmering as it cut through the toxic clouds. The
air was thick with the stench of burning metal and ozone,
a far cry from the fragrant blossoms of Neutrino Prime. The
ship landed with a jolt, its landing gear sinking into
the cracked, scorched earth. The ramp lowered, and the Anglers
stepped out into the chaotic world of Xerxes.

The city was a testament to the 'Madness' that had consumed
its people. Towers of twisted metal and shattered glass clawed at
the sky, their once-gleaming facades now scarred and blackened. The
streets were littered with the wreckage of strange, fantastical vehicles,
the products of a brilliant but unhinged imagination. And everywhere,
there were the Xerxes, their bodies contorted in the throes of
a violent, self-destructive insanity. They fought each other with a
maniacal fury, their eyes burning with a wild, unholy light.

"This is not a battle we can win with force," Luna said,
her voice filled with a mixture of horror and pity. "They're
not soldiers, they're victims. We need to find the source of
this 'Madness' and neutralize it." But how could they find
the source of the 'Madness' in a world that was already
so consumed by chaos? It was like trying to find a
single drop of poison in a raging river. It was then
that Kato, the silent observer, stepped forward. "I can find
it," he said, his voice a low, confident whisper. "The
'Madness' is a psychic plague, and it leaves a trail. I
can follow it." It was a risky plan, but it was
the only one they had. Jax nodded his approval. "Go,"
he said. "And be careful." Kato vanished into the shadows,
a ghost in the madhouse, and the rest of the Anglers
prepared for a long and dangerous wait.
