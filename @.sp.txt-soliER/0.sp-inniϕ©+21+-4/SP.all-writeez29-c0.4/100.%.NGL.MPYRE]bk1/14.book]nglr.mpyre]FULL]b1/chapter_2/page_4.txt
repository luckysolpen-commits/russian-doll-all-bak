The hours crawled by, each one an eternity in the mad,
chaotic world of Xerxes. The Anglers held their position near
the 'Stargazer,' a small island of sanity in a sea of
madness. They didn't engage the crazed Xerxes directly, instead creating
a defensive perimeter with sonic barriers and kinetic shields. It was
a battle of attrition, a test of their endurance and their
resolve. The constant, high-pitched shrieks of the mad inventors, the
endless clang of metal on metal, the stench of ozone and
decay – it was a sensory assault designed to break even the
strongest of wills.

Jack, ever the pragmatist, worked on reinforcing their defenses, his
hands a blur of motion as he jury-rigged new barriers from
the wreckage that littered the streets. Hana, her pulse rifle always
at the ready, stood guard, her eyes scanning the chaotic cityscape
for any signs of a coordinated attack. She knew that while
the Xerxes were consumed by their individual madness, it would only
take a single, focused mind to unite them into a truly
unstoppable force. Zara, her angler pulsing with a soft, empathetic
light, tried to reach the minds of the Xerxes, to find
a spark of sanity in the raging inferno of their minds.
But it was like trying to whisper in a hurricane. Her
voice was lost in the storm.

Luna, her scientific curiosity overriding her fear, took samples of the
air, the soil, the very fabric of reality itself. She was
determined to find a rational explanation for the irrational chaos that
surrounded them. "The psychic energy here is off the charts,"
she muttered, her eyes glued to her tricorder. "It's like a
constant, high-frequency scream that is shattering their minds." Kael, the
strategist, watched the patterns of the chaos, his mind searching for
order in the disorder. He saw that the madness was not
entirely random. There was a rhythm to it, a pulse, a
terrible, beautiful, and deadly dance of self-destruction.

Jax, the stoic leader, stood at the center of it all,
a rock in the raging storm. He was the anchor that
held them all together, his calm presence a bulwark against the
encroaching madness. He knew that their greatest enemy was not
the crazed Xerxes, but the despair that threatened to consume
them. He would not let that happen. He would not let
his team fall. He was about to give the order to
fortify their position for a long siege when a flicker of
movement in the shadows caught his eye. It was Kato. He
had returned.
