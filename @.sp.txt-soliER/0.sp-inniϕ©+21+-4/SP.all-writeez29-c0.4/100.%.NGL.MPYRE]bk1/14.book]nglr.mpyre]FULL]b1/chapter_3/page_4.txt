Kato’s words sent a fresh wave of dread through the boarding party.
This was an enemy unlike any they had faced before. It didn’t
kill with claws or energy blasts; it seduced, it lured, it
captured the mind and left the body an empty, breathing shell.
On the ‘Stargazer,’ Zara’s antenna-like angler was vibrating with an intensity
that made her teeth ache. “He’s right,” she said, her voice
a strained whisper. “I can feel it now. It’s… beautiful. A
song of pure, perfect happiness. It promises everything you’ve ever wanted.
No pain, no fear, no loss. Just… peace.”

Her voice was filled with a dangerous longing. Kael, sitting beside
her, placed a hand on her arm. “Zara, fight it,” he
urged, his voice sharp. “It’s a lie. A siren song.” Zara
shook her head, clearing the psychic fog. “I know,” she said,
her voice trembling slightly. “But it’s so… convincing.” She understood now
how the crew had fallen victim so easily. The dream wasn’t
a nightmare; it was a paradise, a bespoke heaven for every
individual mind. To be torn from it would be like being
ripped from salvation.

“If we try to wake them by force, the psychic shock could
kill them,” Luna concluded, her tricorder confirming their fears. “Their minds
are too deeply integrated with the dream state. We can’t cut
the connection from this end. We have to go to the source.”
Jax’s voice was grim over the comms. “The broadcast from the
planet. That’s our target.” He made the decision with his usual
unflinching resolve. “Luna, Jack, stay with the crew. Monitor their
vitals. Do what you can to shield them from the broadcast’s
influence. Hana, you’re with me. We’re going down to the
planet.”

“Commander, I have to go,” Zara’s voice cut in, firm and
insistent. “This is a psychic battle. You need me on the
front line. I can navigate the dream-currents, I can shield your
minds, I can find the entity behind this.” Jax hesitated. He
knew she was right, but he also knew the immense risk.
She would be the most powerful asset, but also the most
vulnerable target. To send her into the heart of that siren
song was a gamble of the highest order.

“It’s not a request, Commander,” Zara said, her voice leaving no
room for argument. “It’s my duty.” Jax was silent for a
moment, the weight of command heavy on his shoulders. “Alright,” he
finally conceded. “Kael, get the shuttle ready. We’re taking a
trip into the clouds. Hana, you’re on defense. Protect Zara
at all costs. Kato, find the precise location of that broadcast.
We’re going to pay the piper a visit.” The plan was
set. They were leaving the silent ghost ship and descending into
the heart of the dream, a swirling, beautiful, and deadly world
of pure emotion.
