"Hold on!" Zara cried out, her voice a thin, strained wire of
sound. The psychic shield around the shuttle fractured, spiderwebs of dark
despair cracking its violet surface. The entity in the storm was
intelligent, and it had found their weaknesses. It wasn't just broadcasting
a general feeling; it was targeting them, delving into their minds
and weaponizing their own memories against them. Hana was trapped in
a waking nightmare, reliving the loss of her first command, the faces
of her fallen comrades accusing her in the swirling clouds. Jax
was on a desolate battlefield, the weight of a thousand impossible
decisions pressing down on him, the ghosts of his past whispering failures
in his ear.

But Jax was the Commander for a reason. His will was a
fortress, forged in the fires of a hundred campaigns. He fought
back, not with anger, but with a quiet, unyielding acceptance. "They're
gone, but not forgotten," he gritted out, the words a mantra
against the tide of grief. "We honor them by fighting on."
His own inner strength, the core of his leadership, pulsed outwards,
a steadying beacon in the psychic storm. Hana felt it, a
hand on her shoulder in the darkness, and she latched onto
it, pulling herself back from the brink. Her training kicked in,
her mind compartmentalizing the pain, her focus returning to the mission.

Zara felt the shift. Jax's resolve was a rock she could
anchor her shield to. She pushed back, not with force, but
with a different kind of emotion. She didn't try to block
the despair; she absorbed it, processed it, and broadcasted something
new. It was a feeling of shared struggle, of resilience, of
the quiet courage it takes to face loss and keep moving
forward. It was the heart of the Angler creed, the understanding
that even in the darkest void, there is light. The crushing
wave of despair faltered, confused by this unexpected response. The entity
only understood absolutes – pure joy, pure terror. This complex, bittersweet
emotion was an anomaly it couldn't process.

The psychic pressure lessened, and the dreamscape outside the shuttle stabilized,
the visions of grief receding back into the pastel clouds. "I can't
hold it off for long," Zara said, breathing heavily. "It's learning.
Adapting. We need to get to the source, now." Guided by
Kato's coordinates and Zara's empathic navigation, the 'Sandpiper' dove deeper
into the atmospheric sea, heading for the storm at the heart
of the dream. The clouds grew darker, the gentle pastel hues
replaced by angry shades of bruised purple and stormy grey. Lightning,
made not of electricity but of raw, chaotic emotion, arced between
the cloud banks.

They had arrived at the Heart of the Tempest. It was
a vast, swirling vortex, a hurricane of psychic energy. And in
its calm, silent eye, they saw it. It was not a
creature of flesh and blood, nor a machine of metal and
wire. It was a being of pure, concentrated thought, a shimmering,
chameleon-like entity that constantly shifted its form, mirroring the dreams and
nightmares of the captive Aetherians. One moment it was a radiant,
angelic figure offering salvation; the next, a terrifying, shadowy beast of
primordial fear. This was the dream-eater. The psychic vampire. The
Sol Pen's agent of chaos on Aether.
