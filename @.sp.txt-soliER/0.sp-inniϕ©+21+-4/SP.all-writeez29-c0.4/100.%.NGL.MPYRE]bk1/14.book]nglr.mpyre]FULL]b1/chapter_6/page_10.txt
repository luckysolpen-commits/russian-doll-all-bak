The aftermath of the Celestia mission was not a victory, but a revolution. The Anglers, their minds and souls forever changed by their journey, returned to the XVS central command not as soldiers, but as prophets. They spoke of a new way, a path of balance, of acceptance, of a radical, terrifying, and ultimately liberating freedom. Their words, backed by the quiet, unshakeable wisdom of the Celestials, shattered the beautiful, orderly, and utterly fragile world of the XVS.

Vorith, his power broken, his ideology in ruins, was swept away by the tide of revolution. The XVS, the rigid, dogmatic institution that had policed the multiverse for millennia, was dissolved, replaced by a new, more flexible, and ultimately more honest way of being. The war against the Sol Pen was over, not because they had been defeated, but because the very idea of a war had been rendered obsolete.

The Anglers, their mission complete, their purpose fulfilled, went their separate ways. They were no longer soldiers, but wanderers, philosophers, artists, each of them following their own, unique, and unlit path. They had saved the multiverse, not by fighting a war, but by ending one. They had not brought peace, but a new, more interesting, and ultimately more beautiful kind of chaos. The age of the Anglers was over. The age of the Unlit had begun.
