The chamber was a cavern deep beneath the surface of Archon, a place where the ‘Darkness’ was at its most concentrated. The Anglers stood in a circle, their faces pale and drawn in the faint, flickering light of their anglers. They had come to this place to die, or to be reborn. There was no in-between. On Jax’s command, they lowered their psychic shields, their minds open, vulnerable, an invitation to the storm.

The ‘Darkness’ descended upon them with a triumphant, hungry roar. It was a tidal wave of pure, undiluted terror, a symphony of every fear, every doubt, every insecurity they had ever known. Jax was a failure, a fraud, a commander who had led his team to their doom. Hana was a monster, a killer, her hands stained with the blood of a thousand innocent souls. Kael was a fool, his strategies a child’s game against an enemy that was a god. Zara was a ghost, her empathy a curse that had driven her mad. Luna was a heretic, her science a blasphemy against the true, dark nature of the universe. Jack was a broken toy, his machines a pathetic, useless defense against the coming storm. And Jureth… Jureth was a prophet of a false god, a fool who had led them all to this place of ultimate, eternal damnation.

The visions were so real, so powerful, that for a moment, they believed. Their circle faltered, their minds reeling from the psychic onslaught. But then, a single, quiet voice cut through the storm. It was Jureth. He was not fighting the fear, but embracing it. He was accepting the possibility of his own damnation, and in that acceptance, he was finding a strange, paradoxical freedom. “Let it in,” he whispered, his voice a calm, steady anchor in the raging storm. “Do not fight it. Do not fear it. Simply… be.”
