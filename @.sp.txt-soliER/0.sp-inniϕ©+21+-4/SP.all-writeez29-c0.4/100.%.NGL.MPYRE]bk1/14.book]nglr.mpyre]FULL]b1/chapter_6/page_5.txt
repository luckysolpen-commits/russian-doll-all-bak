The victory on Archon was a turning point. The Anglers had faced their deepest fears and had emerged victorious, not through force of arms, but through a profound, spiritual transformation. They had embraced the darkness, and in doing so, they had found a new, more powerful kind of light. But their victory had come at a cost. The XVS, the multiversal government they had sworn to protect, had been watching. And they were not pleased.

The Anglers’ unorthodox methods, their embrace of a philosophy that bordered on heresy, had not gone unnoticed. A new figure emerged from the shadows of the XVS command, a young, ambitious, and ruthlessly pragmatic officer named Vorith. He saw the Anglers not as heroes, but as a threat, a dangerous, unpredictable variable in a war that demanded order, discipline, and absolute, unquestioning obedience. He began to watch them, to monitor their movements, to build a case against them. The Anglers had defeated the ‘Darkness,’ but they had also made a powerful new enemy, an enemy who was far more dangerous than any monster, because he was one of them. The seeds of a new conflict, a conflict that would tear the XVS apart, had been sown.
