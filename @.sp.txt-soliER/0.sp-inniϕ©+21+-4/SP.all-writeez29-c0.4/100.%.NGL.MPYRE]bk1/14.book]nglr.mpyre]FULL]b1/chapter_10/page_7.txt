In the end, the decision was made not by a council, or a government, or a single, charismatic leader. It was made by a billion different individuals, on a thousand different worlds. It was made in the quiet, secret chambers of a billion different hearts.

The galaxy chose to fight. Not as a single, unified army, but as a beautiful, chaotic, and constantly evolving mosaic of resistance. They fought not with fleets and armies, but with art, and music, and stories, and a thousand different acts of quiet, persistent, and ultimately irresistible defiance. They fought not for a single, absolute truth, but for the right to believe in a thousand different truths. They fought not for victory, but for the simple, profound, and inalienable right to be messy, to be beautiful, to be free.
