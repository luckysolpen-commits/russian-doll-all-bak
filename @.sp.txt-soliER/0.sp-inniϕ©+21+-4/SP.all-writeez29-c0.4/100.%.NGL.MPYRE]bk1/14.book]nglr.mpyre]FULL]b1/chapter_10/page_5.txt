The idea was terrifying, and exhilarating. To declare war on the Sol Pen would be to unite them, to give them a common enemy, to turn their fractured, self-destructive civil war into a focused, unified campaign of galactic conquest. But it would also be to expose them, to reveal their secrets, to shatter the illusion of their power. It was a gamble of the highest order, a roll of the dice for the fate of the galaxy.

The crew of the ‘Straylight,’ their hearts filled with a mixture of fear and hope, made their decision. They would not hide in the shadows any longer. They would not fight a secret war. They would drag the Sol Pen into the light, and they would let the galaxy choose.

They sent a message to every corner of the galaxy, a message that was not a song, or a story, or a lie, but a simple, unadorned truth. They told the galaxy everything. They told them about the Sol Pen, about their secret war, about their manipulation of a thousand different worlds. They told them about the ‘Darkness,’ and the ‘Madness,’ and the ‘Dream,’ and the ‘Power,’ and the ‘Hope,’ and the ‘Control.’ They laid the secrets of the universe bare for all to see.
