INT. XERXES' DOMAIN - CENTRAL PLAZA - DAY

The central plaza of Xerxes’ Domain, once a scene of utter chaos, is now a picture of serene, if somewhat rigid, order. Xerxes move with purpose, their metallic forms gleaming under the still-overcast sky. The discordant hum has been replaced by a low, rhythmic thrum of efficient machinery.

The Anglers observe the restored Xerxes. Luna is taking final readings, a satisfied smile on her face.

LUNA
Cognitive functions are fully restored. Their network is clean. It’s remarkable how quickly they bounced back once the frequency was neutralized.

ZARA
(Watching a group of Xerxes meticulously tending to a holographic garden)
They’re so… precise. Almost too precise. Like they’re following a program.

Kael nods, his gaze distant, lost in thought.

KAEL
They are. The frequency didn’t just induce madness; it suppressed their individuality. Their natural inclination for… deviation. They’re efficient, yes, but also… predictable.

Jack kicks at a loose panel on a nearby structure. It reveals a hidden compartment filled with intricate, almost organic-looking circuitry.

JACK
Hey, check this out. This isn’t standard Xerxes tech. It’s… different. More fluid.

Hana examines the circuitry, her horn glowing with an investigative light.

HANA
It’s a listening device. And a transmitter. Embedded deep within their infrastructure. It’s been here for a long time.

Kato, who has been observing a group of Xerxes from a distance, approaches, holding a small, almost invisible data shard.

KATO
(Voice flat)
I found these embedded in their historical archives. Encrypted. But I managed to bypass the firewalls.

He projects a series of images onto a nearby wall. They show ancient Xerxes texts, depicting a long-standing conflict with a shadowy, almost ethereal entity. The symbol of the Sol Pen is prominent.

JAX
(Studying the images)
The Sol Pen. So they weren’t just broadcasting a frequency. They were… monitoring. Controlling.

LUNA
(Her eyes widening as she reads the translated text)
It says here… the Sol Pen has been in conflict with the Xerxes for centuries. A war of ideologies. The Sol Pen believes in absolute order, absolute control. They see individuality as a flaw, a source of chaos.

ZARA
(Horrified)
They were trying to perfect them? By stripping away their free will?

KAEL
(His voice grim)
And they used the Xerxes’ own technology against them. A subtle, insidious form of dominion. This isn’t just a local conflict, Commander. This is a pattern.

Jax looks from the images of the Sol Pen to the now-orderly Xerxes. He thinks back to the Neutrinos, their conflict with the sinqid-fishpeople. Two distinct worlds, two distinct problems, yet both seemingly manipulated by unseen forces.

JAX
(To the team, his voice low)
Neutrino Prime. The Darkness. Now the Xerxes. The Sol Pen. These aren’t isolated incidents. There’s a larger game at play. Someone, or something, is orchestrating these conflicts. Pushing worlds to the brink.

Hana tightens her grip on her spright-saber. Jack’s brow is furrowed in thought. Luna looks concerned. Zara’s vibrant horn dims slightly. Kato remains impassive, but his gaze is sharp.

JAX (CONT'D)
We need to understand who. And why. This mission just got a lot bigger.

FADE OUT.
