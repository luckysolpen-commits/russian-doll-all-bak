INT. XVS SHUTTLE - COMMON AREA - NIGHT

Weeks have passed since the encounter with the Archons’ Darkness. The Anglers are on a new, less urgent mission, but the psychological toll of the last one lingers. Jureth’s visions have intensified, no longer just fleeting glimpses but sustained, almost immersive experiences. His horn glows with a constant, vibrant, yet unsettling light.

Jureth sits in a quiet corner, his eyes distant, his horn projecting complex, swirling patterns of shadow and light onto the bulkhead. He murmurs to himself, words that sound like ancient poetry.

LUNA
(Observing Jureth, her scientific horn pulsing with a mix of concern and fascination)
His neural activity is off the charts. He’s processing information at an incredible rate. It’s almost as if he’s… downloading a new reality.

Kato, ever present but unseen, emerges from the shadows. His horn, usually hidden, now emits a faint, almost curious shimmer.

KATO
He is not downloading. He is remembering. The unlit void is not a new reality. It is the oldest.

Jureth suddenly looks up, his eyes clear and focused. He sees Luna and Kato.

JURETH
(Voice strong, resonant)
The Empire’s light… it is a filter. It shows us only what it deems safe. But beyond the light, there is a vastness. A truth that embraces both shadow and light. It is the source of true evolution. Not the controlled, sterile growth the Empire dictates, but a wild, untamed unfolding.

Luna’s scientific horn pulses with excitement. This isn't madness; it's a new paradigm.

LUNA
(To Jureth)
You’re articulating a unified theory. A philosophy that explains the Darkness, the Madness, the Dreams… and the Empire’s reaction to them.

JURETH
(Nodding)
They fear the unlit. They fear what they cannot control. But control… it is an illusion. True power lies in embracing the unknown. In allowing ourselves to be shaped by the void, not to conquer it.

Jack, who had been tinkering nearby, stops. He looks at Jureth, then at his own horn, its light a steady, practical pulse. He’s never thought of things this way.

JACK
So… all this time, we’ve been fighting shadows… that the Empire created?

Jureth looks at Jack, a sympathetic understanding in his eyes.

JURETH
They are not shadows. They are reflections. Of what the Empire denies.

Zara, drawn by the intensity of the conversation, approaches. Her horn, usually so expressive, is now still, absorbing Jureth’s words.

ZARA
(Softly)
If the Empire’s light is a filter… then what is our purpose? To break the filter?

JURETH
(A faint smile)
To see beyond it. To guide others to see. To embrace the unlit path.

He gestures to the swirling patterns his horn projects.

JURETH (CONT'D)
I must record this. The visions… they are fleeting. But the truth… it must endure.

He reaches for a data pad, his fingers hovering over the blank screen. He begins to type, slowly at first, then with increasing speed, his horn’s light flowing into the device, translating his visions into words. The title appears: "The Book of Jureth."

Luna, Kato, Jack, and Zara watch him, a silent understanding passing between them. A new seed has been planted. A new philosophy is taking root.

FADE OUT.
