INT. XVS SHUTTLE - COMMON AREA - NIGHT

The Anglers are gathered, a rare moment of downtime between missions. The victories on Neutrino Prime, Xerxes’ Domain, and Aethers’ Realm have brought a weary satisfaction, but also a growing unease. Jax and Kael review mission reports, Hana sharpens her spright-saber, Jack tinkers with a new gadget, and Zara quietly observes the stars.

Dr. Luna is engrossed in a data pad, but her attention is drawn to JURETH (20s), a young Angler, a junior member of the team, perhaps an apprentice to Luna or Jax. Jureth sits apart, his horn, usually a soft, inquisitive glow, now flickering erratically, almost painfully. His eyes are wide, unfocused, as if seeing something beyond the confines of the shuttle.

LUNA
(Softly, approaching Jureth)
Jureth? Are you alright? Your readings are… unstable.

Jureth flinches, as if startled from a deep trance. He looks at Luna, his eyes filled with a strange mix of fear and wonder.

JURETH
(Voice hushed, almost a whisper)
The unlit void… I see it. It’s not empty, Luna. It’s… full. Full of things the Empire doesn’t want us to see.

Luna’s scientific horn pulses with curiosity. She sits beside him.

LUNA
What do you mean, Jureth? What do you see?

JURETH
(His horn flickering more intensely, projecting faint, fleeting images into the air: swirling shadows, ancient symbols, glimpses of worlds without artificial light)
I see… a time before the lanterns. Before the Empire. A different kind of order. Not imposed, but… grown. And the Darkness on Neutrino Prime, the Madness on Xerxes, the Dreams on Aethers… they’re not just corruptions. They’re… echoes. Echoes of something the Empire tried to suppress.

Kato, who had been silently observing from the shadows, steps forward. His horn, usually hidden, now emits a faint, almost sympathetic shimmer.

KATO
(Voice low, devoid of emotion, yet with a hint of understanding)
The Empire’s narrative is a carefully constructed light. It illuminates what they wish us to see. And obscures what they do not.

Jureth looks at Kato, surprised that he understands. Luna’s scientific mind grapples with the implications.

LUNA
(To Kato)
Are you suggesting these are not isolated incidents? That they are… connected? Part of a larger pattern?

KATO
(Nodding almost imperceptibly)
The Sol Pen. The parasitic entity. The manipulative dreams. All tools. All designed to maintain a specific order. The Empire’s order.

Jax, who has been listening, approaches, his expression grim.

JAX
(To Jureth)
These visions… are they affecting your judgment? Your ability to perform your duties?

JURETH
(Looking at Jax, his gaze clear and resolute)
They are clarifying it, Commander. I see the purpose of our missions. But I also see the cost. And the… alternative.

Jax looks at Luna, then at Kato. The seeds of doubt, once subtle, are now openly acknowledged. The weight of their missions, once a burden, now feels like a complicity.

FADE OUT.
