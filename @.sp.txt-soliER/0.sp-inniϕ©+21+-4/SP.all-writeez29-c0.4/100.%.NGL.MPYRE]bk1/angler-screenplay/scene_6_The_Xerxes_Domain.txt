EXT. XERXES' DOMAIN - LANDING ZONE - DAY

The XVS shuttle descends onto a world of gleaming chrome and towering, angular structures that pierce a perpetually overcast sky. Unlike the organic vibrancy of Neutrino Prime, Xerxes’ Domain is a symphony of cold, hard lines and artificial light. Automated vehicles zip along elevated highways, but their movements are erratic, often colliding or swerving wildly.

Jax, Kael, Zara, Jack, Luna, Hana, and Kato disembark. The air crackles with static and a discordant hum of malfunctioning machinery.

ZARA
(Wincing, her horn flickering with discomfort)
Sensory overload. The ambient energy here is… fractured. Like a million thoughts screaming at once.

LUNA
(Scanning, her brow furrowed)
Technologically advanced, yes. But the data streams are corrupted. Logic loops, irrational algorithms… it’s a digital breakdown on a planetary scale.

They observe the XERXES, the inhabitants of this world. They are humanoid, clad in sleek, metallic suits, but their movements are jerky, their eyes wide and unfocused. Some babble incoherently, others engage in repetitive, meaningless tasks, while still others lash out violently at unseen threats.

JACK
(Watching a Xerxes repeatedly try to insert a square peg into a round hole)
"Madness," huh? Looks more like a system crash.

HANA
(Her hand on her spright-saber, scanning the chaotic environment)
Whatever it is, it’s contagious. Keep your guard up.

Jax turns to Kato, whose horn is almost imperceptible, blending into the chaotic energy of the world.

JAX
Kato. We need to understand the source of this. Can you get us some intel? Something beyond these surface observations.

Kato nods silently. His form seems to blur, and he melts into the chaotic flow of automated traffic, becoming just another fleeting shadow. His horn’s light, usually hidden, now pulses with an almost imperceptible, rapid rhythm, absorbing data.

ZARA
(Trying to communicate with a passing Xerxes, her horn emitting calming frequencies)
Hello? Can you understand me? What’s happening here?

The Xerxes merely stares through her, its eyes darting wildly, before it suddenly screams and runs headlong into a wall.

ZARA (CONT'D)
(Frustrated)
It’s worse than Neutrino Prime. At least they were just… empty. These ones are actively hostile to reality.

Kael observes the digital chaos, his strategic mind already working.

KAEL
This isn’t a biological corruption. It’s an informational one. A virus of the mind, perhaps. Or a deliberate manipulation.

Minutes later, Kato reappears, as silently as he vanished. He holds a small, glowing data chip.

KATO
(His voice low, devoid of emotion)
I accessed a central data hub. The "Madness" is not organic. It’s a frequency. A resonant pattern broadcast across their entire network. It’s designed to disrupt cognitive function. To induce… chaos.

LUNA
(Taking the chip, her eyes widening as she scans it)
A frequency? That’s… insidious. It bypasses all conventional defenses.

JAX
(His face grim)
Who would do this? And why?

Kato’s gaze sweeps the chaotic landscape, his horn’s light a cold, hard point.

KATO
The data points to a single origin. A hidden broadcast array. And a name… Sol Pen.

FADE OUT.
