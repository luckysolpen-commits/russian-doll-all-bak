# HORLI'S CHINESE TUTORAGE — SESSION 1
## "Street Chinese — The Basics You Won't Learn in School"

---

**Horli Qwin:** Yo. So. I'm Horli Qwin. Trap rapper, class clown, and apparently the person who needs to learn Chinese the most out of everyone in the Time Academy. Not because I'm bad at it — I'm actually pretty good — but because I need it for... specific purposes. Like talking to demon gangsters. And ordering food in the underworld. And rapping in Chinese. Which is my ultimate goal.

And I'm not doing this alone. I've got 中文老师 (zhōng wén lǎo shī — Chinese teacher) with me. She's patient, she's strict, and she has zero tolerance for my nonsense. Which is exactly what I need.

中文老师: 你好，Horli。今天我们开始学中文。 (nǐ hǎo, Horli. jīn tiān wǒ men kāi shǐ xué zhōng wén.) Hello, Horli. Today we start learning Chinese.

**Horli Qwin:** 你好，老师！ (nǐ hǎo, lǎo shī!) Hello, teacher! See? I already know something.

中文老师: 很好。 (hěn hǎo.) Very good. But today we're going beyond 你好. We're going to learn how to actually talk to people. On the street. In restaurants. In clubs. In... less respectable places.

**Horli Qwin:** You mean the demon underworld?

中文老师: I mean places where formal Chinese won't help you.

**Horli Qwin:** Same thing.

## PART 1: GREETINGS — FORMAL VS. INFORMAL VS. STREET

**中文老师:** Let's start with greetings. There are three levels.

**Formal:** 你好 (nǐ hǎo) — hello. Used with strangers, elders, authority figures. Safe. Polite. Boring.

**Informal:** 嘿 (hēi) — hey. Used with friends, peers, people your age. Casual. Friendly. What you'd say to someone at a club.

**Street:** 喂 (wèi) — hey! Used to get someone's attention. Can be rude if you say it wrong. Can be friendly if you say it right. Tone matters.

**Horli Qwin:** So 你好 is like "good evening, sir." 嘿 is like "yo." And 喂 is like "OI!"

中文老师: ...Yes. That's actually a perfect translation.

**Horli Qwin:** I got instincts.

中文老师: Now practice. Greet me three ways.

**Horli Qwin:** 你好，老师！ (nǐ hǎo, lǎo shī!) — formal. 嘿，老师！ (hēi, lǎo shī!) — informal. 喂！老师！ (wèi! lǎo shī!) — street.

中文老师: The last one was too aggressive. You're greeting me, not calling a taxi. Soften the 喂.

**Horli Qwin:** 喂～老师！ (wèi~ lǎo shī!) — like this?

中文老师: Better. Now you sound like a friendly street person instead of a hostile one.

---

## PART 2: SELF-INTRODUCTION — WHO ARE YOU?

**中文老师:** Now introduce yourself. In Chinese. Full sentences.

**Horli Qwin:** Okay. 我叫 Horli Qwin。 (wǒ jiào Horli Qwin.) My name is Horli Qwin. 我是 trap rapper。 (wǒ shì trap rapper.) I'm a trap rapper. 我来自时间学院。 (wǒ lái zì shí jiān xué yuàn.) I'm from the Time Academy.

中文老师: Good. Now add: what do you do? What do you like?

**Horli Qwin:** 我喜欢音乐和食物。 (wǒ xǐ huān yīn yuè hé shí wù.) I like music and food. 我不喜欢无聊的人。 (wǒ bù xǐ huān wú liáo de rén.) I don't like boring people. 我很会说话。 (wǒ hěn huì shuō huà.) I'm very good at talking.

中文老师: 你很会说话 is a bit arrogant.

**Horli Qwin:** But it's true.

中文老师: Say 我学中文很快。 (wǒ xué zhōng wén hěn kuài.) I learn Chinese fast.

**Horli Qwin:** 我学中文很快。 (wǒ xué zhōng wén hěn kuài.) See? I learn fast. And I'm gonna learn the street stuff too. The stuff they don't teach in school.

中文老师: ...We'll get there.

---

## PART 3: NUMBERS — MONEY, FOOD, ADDRESSES

**中文老师:** Numbers are essential. You need them for money, ordering food, giving addresses, and counting beats for your rap.

一 (yī) — one
二 (èr) — two
三 (sān) — three
四 (sì) — four
五 (wǔ) — five
六 (liù) — six
七 (qī) — seven
八 (bā) — eight
九 (jiǔ) — nine
十 (shí) — ten

**Horli Qwin:** Okay. 一 二 三 四 五 六 七 八 九 十。 (yī èr sān sì wǔ liù qī bā jiǔ shí.) Got it. What about bigger numbers?

中文老师: 十一 (shí yī) — eleven. 十二 (shí èr) — twelve. 二十 (èr shí) — twenty. 一百 (yī bǎi) — one hundred. 一千 (yī qiān) — one thousand. 一万 (yī wàn) — ten thousand.

**Horli Qwin:** 一万? That's a lot. How do I say "that's too expensive"?

中文老师: 太贵了。 (tài guì le.) Too expensive.

**Horli Qwin:** 太贵了！ (tài guì le!) I'm gonna use that in the demon underworld. Everything there is too expensive.

中文老师: How do you bargain?

**Horli Qwin:** 便宜一点吧？ (pián yi yī diǎn ba?) Make it a little cheaper?

中文老师: Perfect. Now say it like you mean it.

**Horli Qwin:** 便宜一点吧！ (pián yi yī diǎn ba!) Come on, give me a deal!

中文老师: You sound like a street vendor already.

---

## PART 4: CURSE WORDS (LITE) — THE ONES YOU NEED

**Horli Qwin:** Okay. I know why we're here. I need curse words. For the demon underworld. For self-defense. For... cultural immersion.

中文老师: I'm not teaching you curse words.

**Horli Qwin:** You already said 混蛋 and 笨蛋 in the master plan.

中文老师: ...Those are mild. 混蛋 (hùn dàn) means "bastard" or "jerk." 笨蛋 (bèn dàn) means "idiot" or "fool." They're not terrible. You can say them in front of your grandmother.

**Horli Qwin:** What about the ones I can't say in front of my grandmother?

中文老师: No.

**Horli Qwin:** 老师～ (lǎo shī~) Please?

中文老师: ...Fine. But only two. And you use them carefully.

傻逼 (shǎ bī) — dumbass. Very common. Very offensive. Don't say it to someone's face unless you're ready for a fight.

滚 (gǔn) — get lost. Literally "roll." It's what you say when you want someone to go away immediately.

**Horli Qwin:** 傻逼 (shǎ bī). 滚 (gǔn). Got it. What about the F-word equivalent?

中文老师: I said two.

**Horli Qwin:** I'm gonna find out eventually. Might as well be from you.

中文老师: 操 (cào). But I'm not explaining how to use it.

**Horli Qwin:** 操 (cào). Got it. I'll figure out the context.

中文老师: Please don't.

## PART 5: PRACTICE — INTRODUCING YOURSELF IN CHINESE

**中文老师:** Now put it all together. Introduce yourself in Chinese. Full paragraph. Use everything we've learned.

**Horli Qwin:** Okay. Here goes.

你好，老师！我叫 Horli Qwin。我是 trap rapper，来自时间学院。我喜欢音乐和食物。我不喜欢无聊的人。我学中文很快。我觉得中文很有意思。 (nǐ hǎo, lǎo shī! wǒ jiào Horli Qwin. wǒ shì trap rapper, lái zì shí jiān xué yuàn. wǒ xǐ huān yīn yuè hé shí wù. wǒ bù xǐ huān wú liáo de rén. wǒ xué zhōng wén hěn kuài. wǒ jué de zhōng wén hěn yǒu yì si.)

Hello, teacher! My name is Horli Qwin. I'm a trap rapper from the Time Academy. I like music and food. I don't like boring people. I learn Chinese fast. I think Chinese is very interesting.

中文老师: ...That was actually very good. Your tones need work — especially on 意思 (yì si). You said it like yì sǐ (meaning "die"). You said "Chinese is very die."

**Horli Qwin:** Chinese is very die. I mean — Chinese is very interesting. 有意思 (yǒu yì si).

中文老师: Better. And your 滚 was perfect. Very natural.

**Horli Qwin:** I've been telling people to get lost my whole life. I'm glad I can finally do it in Chinese.

中文老师: That's... not what I meant. But okay.

**中文老师:** Homework. Introduce yourself in Chinese. But make it a rap. Four bars. Use the vocabulary we learned today.

**Horli Qwin:** ...You're serious?

中文老师: Dead serious. If you're going to learn Chinese through rap, let's do it properly.

**Horli Qwin:** Okay. Let me think.

[beatboxing]

(nǐ yào shì shǎ bī, wǒ jiù shuō gǔn!)

中文老师: The first three bars were excellent. The last bar was... accurate but aggressive.

**Horli Qwin:** That's the point. It's a street rap.

**Horli Qwin:** I'll take "excessive but effective" as a compliment.

中文老师: It's not. But it's not not a compliment either.

**Horli Qwin:** That's the most Chinese thing you've ever said.
