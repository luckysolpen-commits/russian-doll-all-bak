# HORLI'S CHINESE TUTORAGE — SESSION 3
## "Martial Arts Chinese — Kung Fu Vocabulary"

---

**Horli Qwin:** Session three. Martial arts Chinese. This is where it gets fun. I'm learning how to talk kung fu — stances, strikes, kicks, and how to talk trash in a fight.

中文老师 looks resigned. She knows what's coming.

## PART 1: MARTIAL ARTS BASICS

**中文老师:** Let's start with the basic terms.

功夫 (gōng fu) — kung fu. Martial arts. Skill earned through hard work.
武术 (wǔ shù) — martial arts. More formal than 功夫.
太极 (tài jí) — tai chi. The slow, meditative martial art.
少林 (shào lín) — Shaolin. The famous temple. The origin of kung fu.

**Horli Qwin:** 功夫！武术！太极！少林！ (gōng fu! wǔ shù! tài jí! shào lín!) I know these! I've seen the movies!

中文老师: Good. Now the stances.

马步 (mǎ bù) — horse stance. Feet wide, knees bent, like riding a horse.
弓步 (gōng bù) — bow stance. One leg forward, bent. One leg back, straight.
虚步 (xū bù) — empty stance. Weight on the back leg, front leg light and ready.

**Horli Qwin:** 马步！ (mǎ bù!) Horse stance! I do this every morning. Well — I try to. My horse stance is more like a tired pony stance.

中文老师: Practice. 马步 gets stronger with time.

---

## PART 2: STRIKES — THE WEAPONS OF THE BODY

**中文老师:** Now the strikes. The body is a weapon. Every part of it.

拳 (quán) — fist. The basic strike.
掌 (zhǎng) — palm. An open-hand strike.
肘 (zhǒu) — elbow. Close-range devastating strike.
膝 (xī) — knee. Upward strike, very powerful.
指 (zhǐ) — finger. Precision strike to pressure points.

**Horli Qwin:** 拳！掌！肘！膝！指！ (quán! zhǎng! zhǒu! xī! zhǐ!) Fist, palm, elbow, knee, finger! I'm a one-Fuzzpet arsenal!

中文老师: Now the verbs.

打 (dǎ) — hit, strike.
击 (jī) — strike, attack. More formal than 打.
劈 (pī) — chop. A downward strike.
推 (tuī) — push. A palm strike that pushes instead of hits.
抓 (zhuā) — grab, claw. A raking strike.

**Horli Qwin:** 我打！我击！我劈！我推！我抓！ (wǒ dǎ! wǒ jī! wǒ pī! wǒ tuī! wǒ zhuā!) I hit! I strike! I chop! I push! I grab!

中文老师: You're very enthusiastic. Your tones are... adequate.

**Horli Qwin:** "Adequate" is Horli Qwin for "excellent."

中文老师: No, it isn't.

---

## PART 3: KICKS — THE LONG-RANGE WEAPONS

**中文老师:** Now kicks. The legs are longer than the arms. They reach further and hit harder.

踢 (tī) — kick. The basic verb.
腿 (tuǐ) — leg. The weapon itself.
扫堂腿 (sǎo táng tuǐ) — sweeping leg. A low kick that sweeps the opponent's legs.
侧踢 (cè tī) — side kick. A kick to the side.
后踢 (hòu tī) — back kick. A kick behind you, without looking.
飞踢 (fēi tī) — flying kick. Self-explanatory.

**Horli Qwin:** 飞踢！ (fēi tī!) Flying kick! That's my signature move! Well — it will be. I haven't mastered it yet. I mostly just fall with style.

中文老师: 飞踢 requires timing, power, and balance.

**Horli Qwin:** I have two of those three.

中文老师: Which two?

**Horli Qwin:** Style and enthusiasm.

---

## PART 4: FIGHT VOCABULARY — WINNING, LOSING, AND SURVIVING

**中文老师:** Now the vocabulary of combat.

战斗 (zhàn dòu) — fight, battle.
防守 (fáng shǒu) — defend, guard.
攻击 (gōng jī) — attack.
赢 (yíng) — win.
输 (shū) — lose.
跑 (pǎo) — run. (Sometimes the best martial art is running away.)
投降 (tóu xiáng) — surrender. (Also sometimes the best option.)

**Horli Qwin:** 我战斗！我防守！我攻击！我赢！ (wǒ zhàn dòu! wǒ fáng shǒu! wǒ gōng jī! wǒ yíng!) I fight! I defend! I attack! I win!

中文老师: What about 输? (shū?) Lose?

**Horli Qwin:** 我不输。 (wǒ bù shū.) I don't lose.

中文老师: Everyone loses sometimes.

**Horli Qwin:** 那我跑。 (nà wǒ pǎo.) Then I run.

中文老师: That's... actually very wise. 跑 is a valid martial arts strategy.

**Horli Qwin:** See? I'm a natural.

---

## PART 5: TRASH TALK — THE ART OF VERBAL COMBAT

**中文老师:** Now... the part I've been dreading. Trash talk.

**Horli Qwin:** YES. Teach me how to talk trash in Chinese.

中文老师: I'm only teaching you these for self-defense. If someone insults you in Chinese, you need to know how to respond.

你的功夫很弱。 (nǐ de gōng fu hěn ruò.) Your kung fu is weak.
你打不过我。 (nǐ dǎ bù guò wǒ.) You can't beat me.
你太慢了。 (nǐ tài màn le.) You're too slow.
回家吧。 (huí jiā ba.) Go home.

**Horli Qwin:** 你的功夫很弱！你打不过我！你太慢了！回家吧！ (nǐ de gōng fu hěn ruò! nǐ dǎ bù guò wǒ! nǐ tài màn le! huí jiā ba!) Your kung fu is weak! You can't beat me! You're too slow! Go home!

中文老师: ...You're enjoying this far too much.

**Horli Qwin:** This is the best day of my life.

中文老师: Now the responses. If someone says these to you:

是吗？ (shì ma?) Is that so? (The dismissive response.)
试试看。 (shì shi kàn.) Try and see. (The challenging response.)
你确定？ (nǐ què dìng?) Are you sure? (The confident response.)

**Horli Qwin:** 是吗？试试看。你确定？ (shì ma? shì shi kàn. nǐ què dìng?) Is that so? Try and see. Are you sure?

中文老师: Perfect. Now you can handle verbal combat in Chinese.

**Horli Qwin:** I'm going to use these on demon gangsters. They're going to be so confused.

中文老师: Please don't start fights in the demon underworld.

**Horli Qwin:** I'm not going to START them. I'm going to FINISH them.

## PART 6: COUNTING IN KUNG FU — FORM SEQUENCES

**中文老师:** Martial arts forms are counted in Chinese. Even in non-Chinese dojos. So you need to count.

一 (yī) — one
二 (èr) — two
三 (sān) — three
四 (sì) — four
五 (wǔ) — five
六 (liù) — six
七 (qī) — seven
八 (bā) — eight
九 (jiǔ) — nine
十 (shí) — ten

**Horli Qwin:** 一 二 三 四 五 六 七 八 九 十！ (yī èr sān sì wǔ liù qī bā jiǔ shí!) I know these from last session!

中文老师: Good. Now count a form sequence. 马步，一拳，二掌，三肘，四膝，五踢！ (mǎ bù, yī quán, èr zhǎng, sān zhǒu, sì xī, wǔ tī!)

**Horli Qwin:** 马步，一拳，二掌，三肘，四膝，五踢！ (mǎ bù, yī quán, èr zhǎng, sān zhǒu, sì xī, wǔ tī!) Horse stance, one fist, two palms, three elbows, four knees, five kicks!

中文老师: Your counting is perfect. Your form, however, is terrible.

**Horli Qwin:** I'm working on it.
