## PART 3: ASKING QUESTIONS

**中文老师:** Questions are essential. You need to ask them to learn.

什么 (shén me) — what.
为什么 (wèi shén me) — why.
怎么 (zěn me) — how.
哪里 (nǎ lǐ) — where.
谁 (shéi) — who.
什么时候 (shén me shí hòu) — when.

这是什么？ (zhè shì shén me?) What is this?
你为什么学中文？ (nǐ wèi shén me xué zhōng wén?) Why do you learn Chinese?
你怎么做火锅？ (nǐ zěn me zuò huǒ guō?) How do you make hot pot?
你去哪里？ (nǐ qù nǎ lǐ?) Where are you going?
谁是你的老师？ (shéi shì nǐ de lǎo shī?) Who is your teacher?
你什么时候说唱？ (nǐ shén me shí hòu shuō chàng?) When do you rap?

**Horli Qwin:** 这是什么？你为什么学中文？你怎么做火锅？你去哪里？谁是你的老师？你什么时候说唱？ (zhè shì shén me? nǐ wèi shén me xué zhōng wén? nǐ zěn me zuò huǒ guō? nǐ qù nǎ lǐ? shéi shì nǐ de lǎo shī? nǐ shén me shí hòu shuō chàng?)

中文老师: You're asking me all these questions?

## PART 4: STORYTELLING

**中文老师:** Now storytelling. This is the hardest skill. You need to connect sentences, use time words, and maintain a narrative.

有一天... (yǒu yī tiān...) One day...
然后... (rán hòu...) And then...
后来... (hòu lái...) Later...
最后... (zuì hòu...) Finally...
所以... (suǒ yǐ...) So...
但是... (dàn shì...) But...

**Horli Qwin:** Let me try. 有一天，我去了恶魔夜店。然后我遇到了魔王大哥。后来我们一起吃火锅。最后我给他说了我的说唱。所以他很喜欢。 (yǒu yī tiān, wǒ qù le è mó yè diàn. rán hòu wǒ yù dào le mó wáng dà gē. hòu lái wǒ men yī qǐ chī huǒ guō. zuì hòu wǒ gěi tā shuō le wǒ de shuō chàng. suǒ yǐ tā hěn xǐ huān.)

One day, I went to a demon nightclub. And then I met the demon king big brother. Later we ate hot pot together. Finally I performed my rap for him. So he liked it a lot.

中文老师: ...That was a complete story. Your grammar was correct. Your vocabulary was appropriate. And your story was... uniquely Horli.

**Horli Qwin:** Did the demon king really like my rap?

中文老师: I don't know. Did he?

中文老师: Of course he did.

## PART 4C: THE CALLBACK — CAPM, EFFICIENT PORTFOLIOS, AND THE HISTORY OF MARKETS

**Horli Qwin:** You know what else I've been thinking about? The history of markets. Not the math-heavy stuff — the stories. The human stories.

中文老师: I'm listening.

**Horli Qwin:** So CAPM — the Capital Asset Pricing Model. It's this formula that tells you how much return you should expect for the risk you're taking. The formula is: Expected Return = Risk-Free Rate + Beta × (Market Return - Risk-Free Rate). But you don't need the math to understand the idea. The idea is: higher risk = higher expected return. If you want more profit, you have to take more risk. There's no free lunch.

中文老师: That's... actually a very clear explanation.

**Horli Qwin:** I know. And here's the thing — my Fuzzpet empire is a portfolio. Each Fuzzpet is an asset. Some are high-risk (untrained Fuzzpets from the wild — they might be amazing or they might be useless). Some are low-risk (trained Fuzzpets from the Sanctuary — predictable, reliable). CAPM says I should diversify — hold both high-risk and low-risk assets. The high-risk ones give me upside. The low-risk ones give me stability. That's an efficient portfolio — maximum return for minimum risk.

中文老师: You're applying modern portfolio theory to Fuzzpet trading.

**Horli Qwin:** I'm applying it to EVERYTHING. My Fuzzpet Media company is a portfolio. My rap career is a portfolio. My demon underworld connections are a portfolio. Each one has a different risk-return profile. Rap is high-risk, high-return (I could be famous or I could be nobody). Fuzzpet trading is medium-risk, medium-return (steady profits but not explosive). Demon underworld connections are high-risk, infinite-return (if I play it right, I control the entire underworld economy).

中文老师: And bonds?

**Horli Qwin:** Bonds are the boring part of the portfolio. They're low-risk, low-return. You lend money to a government or a corporation, they pay you interest, you get your money back. It's safe. It's boring. But it's the foundation. Every good portfolio has bonds — the stable core that holds everything together while the risky assets do their thing.

My bonds are my relationship with 中文老师. You're my stable core. You keep me grounded. You make sure I don't do anything too stupid. You're the bond portion of my portfolio.

中文老师: ...That's the sweetest and most terrifying thing anyone has ever said to me.

**Horli Qwin:** I'm a romantic gangster.

中文老师: You're a gangster who romanticizes everything.

**Horli Qwin:** Same thing.

中文老师: No, it isn't.

**Horli Qwin:** 我觉得是一样的。 (wǒ jué de shì yī yàng de.) I think it's the same.

中文老师: Your grammar is correct. Your logic is not.

**Horli Qwin:** 但是我的投资组合是对的。 (dàn shì wǒ de tóu zī zǔ hé shì duì de.) But my portfolio is correct.

中文老师: ...I can't argue with that.
