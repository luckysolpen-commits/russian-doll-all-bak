## SESSION 1: "Street Chinese — The Basics You Won't Learn in School"
- 中文老师 introduces herself. Horli introduces herself (with a rap).
- Greetings — formal vs. informal vs. street
- 你好 (nǐ hǎo) vs. 嘿 (hēi) vs. 喂 (wèi) — when to use each
- Self-introduction — name, where you're from, what you do
- Horli: "I'm a trap rapper from the Time Academy." In Chinese.
- Numbers — counting money, ordering food, giving addresses
- Curse words lite — 混蛋 (hùn dàn, bastard), 笨蛋 (bèn dàn, idiot)
- 中文老师 is horrified but impressed.
- Homework: introduce yourself in Chinese. Horli raps it.

## SESSION 2: "Restaurant Chinese — Finding the Best Food in the Underworld"
- Ordering food — 我要... (wǒ yào..., I want...), 给我来... (gěi wǒ lái..., bring me...)
- Menu vocabulary — 面条 (miàn tiáo, noodles), 米饭 (mǐ fàn, rice), 火锅 (huǒ guō, hot pot)
- Spicy food — 辣 (là), 微辣 (wēi là, mild), 中辣 (zhōng là, medium), 特辣 (tè là, extra spicy)
- Horli's tangent: the best demon underworld restaurants she's heard about
- 中文老师 teaches: 好吃 (hǎo chī, delicious), 难吃 (nán chī, terrible), 太咸了 (tài xián le, too salty)
- Paying the bill — 买单 (mǎi dān), 多少钱? (duō shǎo qián?, how much?)
- Tipping — not a thing in China. Horli is confused.
- Curse words medium — 傻逼 (shǎ bī, dumbass), 滚 (gǔn, get lost)
- 中文老师: "I'm not teaching you these." Horli: "You already did."

## SESSION 3: "Martial Arts Chinese — Kung Fu Vocabulary"
- Martial arts basics — 功夫 (gōng fu), 武术 (wǔ shù), 太极 (tài jí)
- Stances — 马步 (mǎ bù, horse stance), 弓步 (gōng bù, bow stance)
- Strikes — 拳 (quán, fist), 掌 (zhǎng, palm), 肘 (zhǒu, elbow), 膝 (xī, knee)
- Kicks — 踢 (tī, kick), 腿 (tuǐ, leg), 扫堂腿 (sǎo táng tuǐ, sweeping leg)
- Horli's tangent: she wants to learn kung fu to fight demon gangsters (and also impress them)
- 中文老师 teaches: 打 (dǎ, hit), 防守 (fáng shǒu, defend), 赢 (yíng, win), 输 (shū, lose)
- Counting in kung fu — 一 (yī) through 十 (shí) for form sequences
- Curse words heavy — 操 (cào, f***), 他妈的 (tā mā de, f***ing)
- 中文老师: "That's enough for today." Horli: "But I need it for the rap!"

## SESSION 4: "Underworld Chinese — Gangster Slang and Demon Talk"
- 中文老师: "I can't believe I'm teaching this."
- Gangster hierarchy — 老大 (lǎo dà, boss), 小弟 (xiǎo dì, underling), 大哥 (dà gē, big brother)
- Money talk — 钱 (qián, money), 现金 (xiàn jīn, cash), 黑钱 (hēi qián, dirty money)
- Territory — 地盘 (dì pán, turf), 我的地盘 (wǒ de dì pán, my turf)
- Threats — 你死定了 (nǐ sǐ dìng le, you're dead), 别惹我 (bié rě wǒ, don't mess with me)
- Horli's tangent: she's been talking to demon gangsters and they're actually really nice (she's lying, they're terrifying, she loves it)
- 中文老师 teaches: 朋友 (péng yǒu, friend), 敌人 (dí rén, enemy), 合作 (hé zuò, cooperate)
- Demon-specific vocabulary — 恶魔 (è mó, demon), 妖怪 (yāo guài, monster), 鬼 (guǐ, ghost)
- Horli: "How do I say 'I respect your organization but I'm not joining' in Chinese?"
- 中文老师: "Why would you need that?" Horli: "...No reason."

## SESSION 5: "Club Chinese — Nightlife, Music, and Making Connections"
- Club vocabulary — 酒吧 (jiǔ bā, bar), 夜店 (yè diàn, nightclub), 音乐 (yīn yuè, music)
- Ordering drinks — 啤酒 (pí jiǔ, beer), 白酒 (bái jiǔ, baijiu), 鸡尾酒 (jī wěi jiǔ, cocktail)
- Getting someone's attention — 嘿! (hēi!), 喂! (wèi!), 美女 (měi nǚ, beautiful girl), 帅哥 (shuài gē, handsome guy)
- Horli's tangent: the best clubs in the demon underworld (she has a list)
- Dancing — 跳舞 (tiào wǔ, dance), 节奏 (jié zòu, rhythm), 节拍 (jié pāi, beat)
- Rapping in Chinese — 说唱 (shuō chàng, rap), 歌词 (gē cí, lyrics), 押韵 (yā yùn, rhyme)
- 中文老师 teaches Horli how to count beats in Chinese: 一拍 (yī pāi, one beat), 两拍 (liǎng pāi, two beats)
- Horli starts writing her first Chinese rap. 中文老师 is reluctantly impressed.

## SESSION 6: "Full Sentences — Conversational Chinese for Real Life"
- 中文老师: "Okay. No more single words. Full sentences only."
- Daily conversation — 你今天怎么样? (nǐ jīn tiān zěn me yàng?, how's your day?)
- Expressing opinions — 我觉得... (wǒ jué de..., I think...), 我认为... (wǒ rèn wéi..., I believe...)
- Agreeing and disagreeing — 对 (duì, correct), 不对 (bù duì, wrong), 不一定 (bù yī dìng, not necessarily)
- Horli's tangent: she's been having full conversations with demon gangsters and they think she's hilarious
- Asking questions — 什么 (shén me, what), 为什么 (wèi shén me, why), 怎么 (zěn me, how), 哪里 (nǎ lǐ, where)
- Storytelling — 有一天... (yǒu yī tiān..., one day...), 然后... (rán hòu..., and then...), 最后... (zuì hòu..., finally...)
- Horli tells 中文老师 a story about her encounter with a demon gangster boss. 中文老师 is concerned but also impressed by Horli's Chinese.
- 中文老师: "Your grammar is actually... not terrible." Horli: "I've been practicing!"
