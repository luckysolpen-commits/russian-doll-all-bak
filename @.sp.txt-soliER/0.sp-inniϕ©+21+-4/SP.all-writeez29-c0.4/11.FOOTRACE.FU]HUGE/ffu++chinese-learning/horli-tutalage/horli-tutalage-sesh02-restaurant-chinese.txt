# HORLI'S CHINESE TUTORAGE — SESSION 2
## "Restaurant Chinese — Finding the Best Food in the Underworld"

---

**Horli Qwin:** Session two. Restaurant Chinese. This is important because the demon underworld has the best food I've ever heard of. And I need to know how to order it, complain about it, and pay for it — all in Chinese.

中文老师 is here. She looks nervous. She should be.

中文老师: 今天我们去餐厅。 (jīn tiān wǒ men qù cān tīng.) Today we go to a restaurant.

**Horli Qwin:** 好！ (hǎo!) Good! Let's eat.

---

## PART 1: ORDERING FOOD — THE BASICS

**中文老师:** Let's start with the essential phrase: 我要... (wǒ yào...) — I want...

我要面条。 (wǒ yào miàn tiáo.) I want noodles.
我要米饭。 (wǒ yào mǐ fàn.) I want rice.
我要火锅。 (wǒ yào huǒ guō.) I want hot pot.

**Horli Qwin:** 我要火锅！ (wǒ yào huǒ guō!) I want hot pot! That's the one I need for the demon underworld. Hot pot is everything.

中文老师: Also: 给我来... (gěi wǒ lái...) — bring me... This is more natural in a restaurant.

给我来一碗面条。 (gěi wǒ lái yī wǎn miàn tiáo.) Bring me a bowl of noodles.
给我来两碗米饭。 (gěi wǒ lái liǎng wǎn mǐ fàn.) Bring me two bowls of rice.

**Horli Qwin:** 给我来三碗火锅！ (gěi wǒ lái sān wǎn huǒ guō!) Bring me three bowls of hot pot!

中文老师: You can't order three bowls of hot pot. Hot pot is one pot that you share.

**Horli Qwin:** I don't share.

中文老师: ...Okay. 给我来三个火锅。 (gěi wǒ lái sān ge huǒ guō.) Bring me three hot pots. But the waiter will judge you.

**Horli Qwin:** Let them judge. I'm a growing rapper.

---

## PART 2: SPICY LEVELS — SURVIVING THE DEMON UNDERWORLD

**中文老师:** In the demon underworld, the food is spicy. You need to know how to specify your spice level.

辣 (là) — spicy.
不辣 (bù là) — not spicy.
微辣 (wēi là) — mildly spicy.
中辣 (zhōng là) — medium spicy.
特辣 (tè là) — extra spicy.
变态辣 (biàn tài là) — pervert-level spicy. This is the highest level. It will destroy you.

**Horli Qwin:** 变态辣？ (biàn tài là?) Pervert-level spicy? Who came up with this?

中文老师: A demon chef, probably.

**Horli Qwin:** I'll take 变态辣. Always.

中文老师: You'll regret it.

**Horli Qwin:** 我要变态辣火锅！ (wǒ yào biàn tài là huǒ guō!) I want pervert-level spicy hot pot!

中文老师: ...The waiter is going to look at you with concern.

**Horli Qwin:** Good. I want them to know I'm serious.

---

## PART 3: FOOD REVIEWS — GOOD, BAD, AND TERRIBLE

**中文老师:** Now let's talk about how to describe food.

好吃 (hǎo chī) — delicious.
难吃 (nán chī) — terrible (literally "hard to eat").
太咸了 (tài xián le) — too salty.
太淡了 (tài dàn le) — too bland.
太辣了 (tài là le) — too spicy.
刚刚好 (gāng gāng hǎo) — just right.

**Horli Qwin:** 好吃！ (hǎo chī!) Delicious! 难吃！ (nán chī!) Terrible! 太辣了！ (tài là le!) Too spicy! 刚刚好！ (gāng gāng hǎo!) Just right!

中文老师: You're getting the tones wrong on 难吃. It's nán chī, not nán chǐ.

**Horli Qwin:** 难吃。 (nán chī.) Like this?

中文老师: Better.

**Horli Qwin:** So if a demon gangster serves me food and it's terrible, I say 难吃?

中文老师: If you want to start a fight, yes.

**Horli Qwin:** What if I want to be polite but honest?

中文老师: 不太好吃。 (bù tài hǎo chī.) Not very delicious. It's the polite way to say "this is bad."

**Horli Qwin:** 不太好吃。 (bù tài hǎo chī.) Got it. The diplomatic version of 难吃.

---

## PART 4: PAYING THE BILL — MONEY TALK

**中文老师:** When you're done eating, you need to pay.

买单。 (mǎi dān.) The bill. (Literally "buy the bill.")
多少钱？ (duō shǎo qián?) How much money?
太贵了。 (tài guì le.) Too expensive.
便宜一点吧？ (pián yi yī diǎn ba?) Make it a little cheaper?

**Horli Qwin:** 买单！多少钱？ (mǎi dān! duō shǎo qián?) The bill! How much?

中文老师: Good. Now the waiter says a price. You say 太贵了。 (tài guì le.) Too expensive.

**Horli Qwin:** 太贵了！便宜一点吧？ (tài guì le! pián yi yī diǎn ba?) Too expensive! Make it cheaper?

中文老师: Perfect. Now the waiter might give you a discount. Or they might not. In the demon underworld, they definitely won't.

**Horli Qwin:** What if I don't have enough money?

中文老师: 我没带钱。 (wǒ méi dài qián.) I didn't bring money. Or: 下次给。 (xià cì gěi.) I'll pay next time.

**Horli Qwin:** 下次给。 (xià cì gěi.) I'll pay next time. That's what I tell every demon gangster after a meal.

中文老师: And do they believe you?

**Horli Qwin:** No. But they let me go because they're afraid of my kung fu.

中文老师: ...You're learning Chinese for the wrong reasons.

**Horli Qwin:** I'm learning Chinese for ALL the reasons.

## PART 5: CURSE WORDS (MEDIUM) — THE ONES FOR RESTAURANTS

**中文老师:** I said I'd teach you two more. For restaurant use only.

**Horli Qwin:** I'm ready.

中文老师: 混蛋 (hùn dàn) — bastard/jerk. Use this when the waiter brings the wrong order.

**Horli Qwin:** 混蛋！你上错菜了！ (hùn dàn! nǐ shàng cuò cài le!) Bastard! You brought the wrong dish!

中文老师: ...That was very aggressive.

**Horli Qwin:** It's a demon underworld restaurant. Aggressive is the baseline.

中文老师: The second one: 滚 (gǔn) — get lost. Use this when someone is bothering you.

**Horli Qwin:** 滚！ (gǔn!) Get lost!

中文老师: You're enjoying this too much.

**Horli Qwin:** I've been waiting my whole life to say 滚 in a restaurant.

## PART 6: PRACTICE — FULL RESTAURANT CONVERSATION

**中文老师:** Now let's do a full restaurant conversation. I'll be the waiter. You be the customer.

**Horli Qwin:** Let's go.

中文老师 (as waiter): 你好，欢迎光临！ (nǐ hǎo, huān yíng guāng lín!) Hello, welcome!

**Horli Qwin:** 你好！给我来一个变态辣火锅！ (nǐ hǎo! gěi wǒ lái yī ge biàn tài là huǒ guō!) Hello! Bring me one pervert-level spicy hot pot!

中文老师 (as waiter): 好的，请稍等。 (hǎo de, qǐng shāo děng.) Okay, please wait a moment. ...你的火锅来了。 (nǐ de huǒ guō lái le.) Your hot pot is here.

**Horli Qwin:** 好吃！太好吃了！ (hǎo chī! tài hǎo chī le!) Delicious! So delicious! ...等等，太辣了！ (děng děng, tài là le...) Wait, too spicy! 水！给我来水！ (shuǐ! gěi wǒ lái shuǐ!) Water! Bring me water!

中文老师 (as waiter): 给你水。 (gěi nǐ shuǐ.) Here's your water.

**Horli Qwin:** 谢谢！买单！多少钱？ (xiè xie! mǎi dān! duō shǎo qián?) Thank you! Bill! How much?

中文老师 (as waiter): 一百块。 (yī bǎi kuài.) 100 yuan.

**Horli Qwin:** 太贵了！便宜一点吧？ (tài guì le! pián yi yī diǎn ba?) Too expensive! Make it cheaper?

中文老师 (as waiter): 九十块。 (jiǔ shí kuài.) 90 yuan.

**Horli Qwin:** 好吧。给你钱。 (hǎo ba. gěi nǐ qián.) Fine. Here's your money. 谢谢！再见！ (xiè xie! zài jiàn!) Thank you! Goodbye!

中文老师: ...That was actually a complete restaurant conversation. Your tones were mostly correct. Your vocabulary was appropriate. And your 变态辣 was committed.

**Horli Qwin:** I was born for this.

中文老师: You were born for chaos.

**Horli Qwin:** Same thing.
