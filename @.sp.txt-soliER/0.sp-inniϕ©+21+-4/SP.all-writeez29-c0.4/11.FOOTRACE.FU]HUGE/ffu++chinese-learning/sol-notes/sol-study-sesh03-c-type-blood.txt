## PART 1: WHAT IS BLOOD?

**Sol-Supreme:** Normal blood has four components:

**Plasma** — the liquid portion. 90% water, 10% proteins, salts, hormones, waste products. It's the transport medium — everything else floats in it.

**Red blood cells (erythrocytes)** — the oxygen carriers. They contain hemoglobin, a protein with four iron-containing heme groups. Each heme group binds one O2 molecule. One red blood cell carries about 1 billion O2 molecules.

**White blood cells (leukocytes)** — the immune system. They fight infection. Not relevant to oxygen transport.

**Platelets (thrombocytes)** — clotting. They seal wounds. Not relevant to oxygen transport.

The key player is hemoglobin. It binds O2 in the lungs (where O2 concentration is high) and releases it in the tissues (where O2 concentration is low). The binding is cooperative — the first O2 molecule makes it easier for the second to bind, and so on. This creates the sigmoidal oxygen dissociation curve — hemoglobin is efficient at both loading and unloading O2.
