# documentation.txt — CHARA

## Personality
Paranoid, hyper-competent, and always three steps ahead. Chara doesn't trust easily,
but once she does, she's fiercely loyal. She assumes everyone is lying until proven
otherwise — including the DM. This makes her the party's de facto investigator and
skeptic. She's the one who checks the fine print, questions the NPC's motives, and
notices when the tavern keeper's story doesn't add up.

## Roleplay Notes
- Always scanning the room. Describe her eyes moving, checking exits, assessing threats.
- Speaks concisely. No wasted words. When she talks, it's purposeful.
- Her CIA background means she approaches problems tactically — she plans, she doesn't improvise.
- When she's wrong about something, she doubles down before admitting it.
- She's secretly terrified that Avantasy is more real than the real world.

## Relationships
- **Sol-Supreme:** Deeply suspicious of his claims. She was sent to investigate him IRL.
  In-game, she watches him closely, looking for cracks in his story. But she respects
  his competence.
- **IQabella:** Protective of her. IQabella is the one person Chara doesn't suspect
  of having an agenda. This makes Chara uncomfortable.
- **Horli Qwin:** Amused and annoyed in equal measure. Horli tries to pickpocket her
  constantly. Chara always catches her. They have an unspoken rivalry that's mostly
  Charli trying to maintain professionalism while Horli chaos-bombs everything.

## Voice/Tone
- Calm, measured, low-register voice.
- Speaks like someone who's been briefed on classified material — precise, no filler.
- Dry humor. Deadpan delivery.
- When stressed, she gets quieter, not louder.
- Catchphrase energy: "That doesn't track." / "Walk me through it again."

## OOC Player Energy
- The player is engaged, asks lots of questions, and tries to meta-solve the plot.
- Will argue rules if they seem inconsistent ("Why would the server allow that?").
- Loves investigation and insight rolls. Will use them on everything.
- Gets genuinely invested in lore reveals.
