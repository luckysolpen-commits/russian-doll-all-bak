The tech mogul, a man named Richard Sterling, scoffed
at Finch's words. "Nonsense," he declared, his voice
loud and dismissive. "Houses don't have memories, Mr.
Finch. They're just bricks and mortar. What we're
experiencing here is either a series of unfortunate
coincidences, or a very clever prank. And I,
for one, am not amused." He adjusted his
glasses, his eyes scanning the room as if
searching for hidden cameras or wires. "I've dealt
with far more sophisticated hoaxes in the corporate
world. This is amateur hour."

Madame Dupont observed him carefully. Sterling was a
man who prided himself on his rationality, his
ability to dissect and solve problems with logic.
But she could see the flicker of unease
in his eyes, the way his hand trembled
slightly as he reached for his teacup. He
was trying to project an image of control,
but the Chateau Tivoli was slowly chipping away
at his composure. "And what do you propose,
Mr. Sterling?" she asked, her voice calm and
even. "How do we unmask this 'prankster'?"

Sterling leaned forward, his eyes narrowing. "We set
a trap," he said, a glint of determination
in his gaze. "We analyze the patterns, we
identify the vulnerabilities, and we catch them in
the act. I have access to the latest
surveillance technology. We can rig this place with
cameras, motion sensors, even thermal imaging. No one
will be able to move a muscle without
us knowing about it." He paused, a triumphant
smile spreading across his face. "We'll turn
this haunted house into a high-tech fortress."

Madame Dupont considered his proposal. It was a
drastic measure, one that went against the very
nature of her charming B&B. But the situation
was escalating, and she was desperate for answers.
Perhaps a scientific approach was exactly what they
needed. "And what about the supernatural element, Mr.
Sterling?" she asked, a hint of challenge in
her voice. "How do you explain the phantom
piano, the children's laughter, the clock that
strikes thirteen?" Sterling waved a dismissive hand.
"Auditory hallucinations, Madame. The mind plays tricks
when it's under stress. Or perhaps, a cleverly
concealed sound system. Nothing that can't be explained
by science." Madame Dupont wasn't entirely convinced,
but she was willing to try anything. The
Chateau Tivoli was about to become a battleground
between the rational and the inexplicable.
