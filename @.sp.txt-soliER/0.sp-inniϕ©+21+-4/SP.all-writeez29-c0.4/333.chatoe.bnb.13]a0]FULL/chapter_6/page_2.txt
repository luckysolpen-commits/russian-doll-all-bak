Madame Dupont, a woman of impeccable composure, found
her usual calm beginning to fray at the edges.
The Chateau Tivoli was her sanctuary, her life's
work, a place of charm and elegance. Now,
it felt as though the very walls were turning
against her, whispering secrets she couldn't comprehend. The
disappearances, the cryptic messages, the unsettling noises –
it was all too much. She decided that
passivity was no longer an option. She had to
take control, to unravel the mystery that was
threatening to consume her beloved B&B.

Her first step was to conduct a thorough search
of the house, from the dusty attic to
the wine cellar. She was looking for anything
out of place, any clue that might shed
light on the strange occurrences. She ran her
fingers along the spines of ancient books in
the library, tapped on walls searching for hidden
compartments, and peered into the shadows of every
nook and cranny. The house, however, held its
secrets close, revealing nothing. It was as if
it was toying with her, daring her to
uncover its dark heart.

She then turned her attention to the remaining guests.
The businessman and the religious woman had fled,
but the others remained, a curious collection of
souls bound together by the house's strange machinations.
There was the reclusive author, who seemed to
be observing the events with a detached, almost
clinical, interest. There was the tech mogul, who
scoffed at the idea of supernatural forces, yet
jumped at every creak and groan of the
house. And there was the famous actress, who
seemed to be enjoying the drama a little
too much, as if she were playing a
role in a real-life thriller.

Madame Dupont decided to engage them in conversation, hoping
to glean some information, some hint of what
was truly going on. She served them her
finest tea and pastries in the grand parlor,
creating an atmosphere of warmth and conviviality. But
beneath the surface of polite conversation, she could
feel the tension, the fear, the suspicion. Each
of them was hiding something, she was sure
of it. And she was determined to find
out what it was. The Chateau Tivoli was
her stage, and she was the director of
this strange, unsettling play. She would get to
the bottom of it, one way or another.
