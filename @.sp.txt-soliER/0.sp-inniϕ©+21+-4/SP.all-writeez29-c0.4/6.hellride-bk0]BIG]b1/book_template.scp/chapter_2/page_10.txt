Admiral Rostova stared at Lucky, her face a mask of disbelief.
"Are you insane?" she demanded, her voice rising.
"You want to board a transport ship in the middle of a battle?
That's a suicide mission!"

"Maybe," Lucky conceded, his gaze unwavering.
"But those bots are still coming. And our fighters are being overwhelmed.
If we can disable them from the inside, we can turn the tide."

Edge stepped forward, a glint of admiration in his cybernetic eye.
"He's right, Admiral," he said, a rare note of seriousness in his voice.
"It's a long shot, but it's the only one we've got."

Rostova looked from Lucky to Edge, then back to the main viewscreen,
where the battle raged, a chaotic ballet of destruction.
She was a woman of logic, of calculated risks.
But sometimes, logic had to yield to desperation.

"Very well," she said, her voice tight with reluctant acceptance.
"You'll have a shuttle. And a squad of my best marines.
But if you don't make it back, don't expect a rescue."

"Understood," Lucky said, a grim smile touching his lips.
He knew the risks. He had always known the risks.

"I'm coming with you," Edge declared, stepping forward.

Lucky raised an eyebrow.
"You sure about that? It's going to be messy."

"Messy is my middle name," Edge retorted, a predatory grin spreading across his face.
"Besides, someone needs to make sure you don't get yourself killed."

Lucky nodded. He wouldn't have it any other way.
Edge was a wild card, a dangerous ally.
But he was also fiercely loyal, in his own twisted way.
And he knew how to fight.

Within minutes, they were in the hangar bay,
suited up and ready to go.
The shuttle, a small, agile craft, was already prepped.
The marines, grim-faced and heavily armed, stood at attention.

"Remember your training, marines!" their sergeant barked.
"We're going in hot!"

Lucky looked at the shuttle's ramp, at the darkness beyond.
He felt a familiar surge of adrenaline, a thrill that always accompanied the edge of danger.
This was it.
Their last chance to stop the bots.
Their last chance to save Earth.

"Let's go," Lucky said, his voice firm.
He stepped onto the ramp, Edge at his side, and the marines following close behind.
The ramp hissed shut, sealing them inside the belly of the beast.

The shuttle launched, a tiny dart streaking into the heart of the battle.
It dodged laser fire, weaved through debris, a ghost in the maelstrom.
Lucky watched the viewscreen, his eyes fixed on the nearest transport ship.
It was massive, a hulking behemoth of metal, bristling with bots.

"Target acquired!" the pilot shouted.
"Brace for impact!"

The shuttle slammed into the transport's hull with a sickening crunch.
The impact alarms blared, and the marines braced themselves.
Lucky felt the jolt, the sudden deceleration.
They were in.

"Breaching charge set!" the sergeant yelled.

A muffled explosion rocked the shuttle, and a section of the transport's hull peeled away,
revealing a dark, cavernous interior.

"Go! Go! Go!" Lucky shouted.

He led the charge, blaster in hand, into the heart of the enemy.
The air inside the transport was cold and metallic, smelling of ozone and machine oil.
Rows upon rows of World Police Bots stood dormant, waiting to be deployed.
They were silent, menacing, an army of chrome and steel.

"Find the control core!" Lucky yelled over the comms.
"Disable these things!"

The fight for Earth had just moved to a new, more dangerous battlefield.
And Lucky, the outlaw, was leading the charge.
