The sight of Lucky was a punch to Edge’s gut.
He was thinner, harder, his eyes holding a weary wisdom that hadn’t been there before.
The last time Edge had seen him, they had been on opposite sides of a corporate war,
Lucky the idealistic freelancer, Edge the loyal company man.
Their friendship had shattered in a storm of betrayal and blaster fire.
Edge had walked away with a promotion and a hollow victory.
Lucky had walked away with a price on his head.

Now, here he was, a ghost walking into the heart of his own funeral.
The UN delegates murmured, a ripple of confusion and fear spreading through the chamber.
They knew who Lucky was. A notorious outlaw, a thorn in the side of corporate authority.
His presence here was a scandal, a violation of their sterile, ordered world.

"What is the meaning of this?" a portly official sputtered, his face turning a blotchy red.
"Arrest this man!"

But no one moved. The security guards, their hands hovering over their sidearms,
looked at Edge, then at Lucky, their faces a mixture of confusion and apprehension.
There was an aura of danger around the two men, a shared history that was a palpable force in the room.

"He's with me," Edge said, his voice a low growl that dared anyone to challenge him.
He walked towards Lucky, his heart a strange mixture of hope and dread.
He had hated this man, had hunted him, had wished him dead.
But now, in this new, terrifying world, the sight of him was a lifeline.

They stood face to face, the two old friends, the two bitter enemies.
The silence between them was heavy with unspoken words, with years of anger and regret.

"You look like hell, Edge," Lucky said, a ghost of his old, familiar smirk touching his lips.

"You're not exactly a vision of health yourself," Edge shot back, his own lips twitching into a reluctant smile.

The tension broke. The years of anger, of hatred, seemed to melt away,
replaced by a shared, weary understanding.
They were survivors. They were fighters.
And they were facing a common enemy.

"I heard you were starting a revolution," Edge said, his voice low.
"I thought you could use some help."

Lucky’s smile faded, his eyes turning serious.
"The UN won't help us," he said, his gaze sweeping across the frightened faces of the delegates.
"They're afraid. They're looking for a way to surrender, not a way to fight."

"I know," Edge said, his voice grim.
"But they have resources. Ships, weapons, intelligence.
We need what they have."

He turned to the delegates, his voice ringing with a newfound authority.
"The Architect is not just a threat to the fringe, to the outlaws.
He is a threat to all of you. To your power, to your comfortable lives.
He will not tolerate rivals. He will not tolerate dissent.
He will either convert you or crush you."

He let the words sink in, a cold, hard truth in the warm, stale air of the chamber.
He could see the fear in their eyes, but now, it was a different kind of fear.
It was the fear of a man who has just realized that the monster at the door is not going to go away.

"We have a plan," Edge said, his voice dropping to a conspiratorial whisper.
"A way to fight back. A way to win.
But we need your help."

He looked at Lucky, a silent question in his eyes.
Lucky nodded, a grim determination on his face.
They were a strange, unlikely alliance.
A gangster and an outlaw.
But they were the last, best hope of a world on the brink of annihilation.
And they were just getting started.
