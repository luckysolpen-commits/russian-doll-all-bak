Edge activated a private datapad, and a new hologram shimmered to life beside the Metal Sun.
It was a schematic, a sleek, menacing design for a new kind of soldier.
It was humanoid in shape, but its lines were too clean, too perfect.
There was no room for a human inside. It was pure machine.

"This is the World Police Bot," Edge announced, his voice grim.
"The Architect's new peacekeeper. According to our intelligence,
he has factories on the Icarus churning these out by the thousand.
They are networked, controlled by the Metal Sun itself.
They are judge, jury, and executioner, all rolled into one chrome-plated package."

He zoomed in on the bot's weapon systems.
"They are armed with non-lethal compliance weapons,"
he explained, a bitter irony in his voice.
"Pulse rifles that can incapacitate a crowd in seconds.
Restraints that can subdue a man without leaving a mark.
But make no mistake. They are an army of occupation."

Lucky stepped forward, his eyes fixed on the schematic.
He had seen machines like this before, in corporate security forces.
But never on this scale, with this level of sophistication.
"He's not just building an army," Lucky said, his voice low.
"He's building a global prison. And these are the guards."

"Exactly," Edge agreed. "And he's not waiting.
Our sources say he plans to launch the first wave within the next cycle.
He's going to pacify Earth, bring it to heel, before anyone has a chance to organize a resistance."

He then expanded the holographic display, showing the solar system.
The Icarus, with its Metal Sun, was the center of this new map.
But two other locations began to glow: Mars and the Lunar Colony.

"And he's not alone," Edge continued, the final, damning piece of his presentation.
"The colonial governments on Mars and Luna have declared their support for the Architect.
They see him as a stabilizing force, a guarantor of their own power.
They are providing him with resources, with political cover.
They are complicit in his conquest."

A wave of shock and outrage rippled through the chamber.
This was a betrayal of the highest order.
The colonies, born from Earth, were now siding with its would-be conqueror.
The threat was no longer distant, a madman on the edge of the system.
It was here, in their own backyard.

"This is our chance," Lucky said, his voice cutting through the noise.
"He thinks we're divided. He thinks we're weak.
He's expecting us to roll over.
Let's show him he's wrong."

He looked from Edge to the delegates, his gaze burning with an intensity that made them flinch.
"We know the launch trajectory of the transport ships carrying the bots,"
he said, a plan already forming in his mind, a daring, impossible strike.
"We can intercept them. We can cut the head off the snake before it even has a chance to strike."

It was a bold plan, a desperate gamble.
An act of open warfare against a self-proclaimed god.
But it was a plan.
And in the face of annihilation, a plan was a powerful thing.
It was a spark of hope in the all-consuming darkness.
The delegates looked at each other, their faces a mixture of terror and a dawning, reluctant resolve.
The choice was theirs. Surrender, or fight.
