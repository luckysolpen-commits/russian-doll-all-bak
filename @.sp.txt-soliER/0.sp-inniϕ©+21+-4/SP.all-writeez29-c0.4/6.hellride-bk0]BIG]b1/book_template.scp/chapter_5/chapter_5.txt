Chapter 5: Edge's Betrayal

Dark, damp tunnels stretched endlessly, the air thick with the smell of damp earth and stale metal. Lucky, bruised and weary, stumbled through the labyrinthine passages, his senses on high alert, constantly looking over his shoulder. Every shadow seemed to hold a threat, every distant hum a harbinger of danger.

His comm unit crackled to life, and Edge Dangerman's voice, smug and triumphant, echoed through the confined space, a cruel intrusion into the oppressive silence.

"You should have stayed out of this, Lucky," Edge's disembodied voice sneered. "Now you're just a pawn in my game."

Lucky seethed with anger, a hot, bitter taste in his mouth. He could hear the distant, rhythmic hum of World Police Bots, their presence a constant, oppressive threat. They were driving him deeper, relentlessly, into the very core of the earth, a forced descent into despair.

Edge's laughter filled the comm unit, a cruel, mocking sound that grated on Lucky's frayed nerves.

"I'm thriving down here, Lucky. The Metal Mafia is stronger than ever. And you? You're just digging your own grave."

Lucky clenched his fists, his knuckles white. His determination, fueled by a burning desire for retribution, hardened with each taunting word. Revenge. That's all that mattered now. It was the only thing that kept him moving, one weary step after another, into the unknown depths.
