Lucky moved through the subterranean labyrinth, his anger a burning coal in his gut.
Edge's taunts, his smug satisfaction, echoed in his mind, fueling his every step.
He was no longer just running; he was being driven, relentlessly, deeper into the earth.
Each turn, each descent, felt like a surrender, a concession to Edge's twisted game.

The tunnels grew narrower, the air thicker, the pressure more oppressive.
He passed through forgotten sections of old subway lines, their tracks rusted,
the trains themselves skeletal remains of a bygone era.
He saw the remnants of human life, hastily abandoned camps, crude drawings on the walls,
testaments to those who had come before him, those who had also sought refuge in the depths.

He found himself in a vast, echoing cavern, its ceiling lost in the oppressive darkness.
The only light came from the faint glow of phosphorescent moss clinging to the walls,
casting an eerie, green luminescence on the ancient rock formations.
He could hear the distant drip of water, a slow, maddening rhythm that seemed to mock his desperation.

He was alone, utterly alone, in this subterranean world.
The bots were still there, their metallic footsteps a constant, insidious reminder of his captivity.
But they were no longer actively pursuing him.
They were simply herding him, pushing him deeper, into the heart of the earth.
Edge's game.

He stumbled, his foot catching on a loose rock, and fell to his knees.
His body screamed in protest, every muscle aching, every joint protesting.
He was exhausted, bone-weary, his spirit frayed at the edges.
He wanted to give up, to just lie there and let the darkness consume him.
But Edge's face, his mocking smile, flashed in his mind.

No. Not yet.

He pushed himself up, his jaw set, his eyes burning with a cold, hard resolve.
He would not give Edge the satisfaction.
He would not break.

He thought of Astra, of her fierce spirit, her unwavering belief in freedom.
He thought of the world they had fought for, the world that was now being systematically dismantled.
He thought of the Architect, sitting on his throne, confident in his victory.
And a new kind of anger, cold and sharp, began to build within him.

This wasn't just about survival anymore.
It was about defiance. It was about retribution.
It was about showing them all that humanity, even when pushed to the brink,
would not surrender.

He began to move again, his footsteps echoing in the vast cavern.
He was no longer just running from Edge.
He was running towards something.
Towards a future where he could fight back.
Towards a future where he could make them pay.

He was driven deeper into the earth's core, fueled by anger.
An anger that was no longer just a hot, bitter taste in his mouth,
but a burning fire in his gut, a relentless engine that propelled him forward.
He would find a way.
He would survive.
And then, he would make them regret the day they ever crossed Lucky.
