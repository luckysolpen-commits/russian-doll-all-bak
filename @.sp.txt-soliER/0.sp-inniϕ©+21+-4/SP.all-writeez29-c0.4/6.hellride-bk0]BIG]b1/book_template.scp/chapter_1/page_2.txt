The Icarus was a blade held to the throat of the solar system.
Its hull, a mosaic of obsidian and polished chrome,
drank the starlight and reflected nothing.
It was a void in the shape of a ship, a monument to arrogance.
And from its heart, a voice emerged.
It wasn't broadcast over comms; it was a wave of pure thought,
a psychic shout that echoed in every mind from Mercury to the Kuiper Belt.

**BEHOLD!**

The voice was a physical force, rattling Lucky’s teeth.
He saw Jax flinch on the secondary screen, his hand flying to his temple.
Maya cried out, a sharp gasp of pain.
On the main viewport, a figure materialized on the Icarus’s bridge.
He was tall and gaunt, draped in robes that seemed woven from solidified light.
His face was a mask of ecstatic madness, eyes burning with the fire of a thousand suns.
The Architect.

He spread his arms wide, a messianic gesture that encompassed the void.
Behind him, the swirling vortex of light and energy resolved itself.
It was a sphere, a perfect orb of churning, molten metal and captive plasma.
It pulsed with a life of its own, a caged star.
It was the Metal Sun.

"I have reached into the heart of creation!" the Architect’s voice boomed,
no longer a psychic assault, but an amplified, triumphant roar.
"I have torn down the laws of physics and rebuilt them in my own image!"
He gestured to the Metal Sun, a proud father presenting his monstrous child.
"I give you a new dawn! A new truth! A new god!"

The light from the Metal Sun intensified, casting long, sharp shadows across the *Stray Dog*’s cockpit.
It wasn't warm like the light from Sol.
It was a cold, sterile light, the light of a surgeon's lamp,
and it seemed to strip away all illusion, all hope.
It promised order, but the order of the machine, of the grave.

"He’s insane," Jax whispered, his voice tight with disbelief.
"He’s actually done it."

Lucky’s mind reeled. He remembered the whispers, the fragmented data reports
about the Architect’s grand project on the edge of the system.
No one had believed it. A star made by human hands? It was the stuff of myth.
But the reality was hanging before them, a testament to a genius untethered from morality.

He looked at the faces of his friends, reflected in the viewport.
Jax, the pragmatist, his face pale, his engineering mind struggling to comprehend the scale of it.
Maya, the sensitive, her eyes wide with a terror that went beyond the physical threat.
She was feeling the psychic resonance of the Architect's creation, the sheer wrongness of it.
And himself. What did he feel?

Awe, certainly. The sheer audacity of it was breathtaking.
But beneath the awe, a cold, hard knot of anger was forming in his gut.
This wasn't a gift. It was a declaration of war.
The Architect hadn't just built a new star.
He had appointed himself the arbiter of reality.

"We’re pawns," Lucky said, the words tasting like ash in his mouth.
He finally understood the quiet on the net, the feeling of being watched.
They hadn't been running from corporate enforcers or rival crews.
They had been running from this.
From a madman who had stolen the fire of the gods
and was about to use it to burn their world down.

The Metal Sun continued to brighten, its light a physical pressure.
Lucky felt a sudden, irrational urge to shield his eyes,
to hide from its all-seeing glare.
But he forced himself to watch.
He had to bear witness to the birth of this new, terrible age.
He had to remember the moment the sky was stolen.
