Chapter 3: The World Police Bots Take Control

Chaos reigned. The sky, once a serene expanse, was now a canvas of metallic storms as World Police Bots descended upon Earth. Explosions rocked the ground, each concussive blast a testament to the new order. In the distant heavens, the ominous silhouettes of Mars and Lunar Colony ships provided a chilling backdrop, their presence a stark reminder of the Architect's widespread support.

Hundreds of humans scattered, their faces etched with terror, their movements desperate and futile against the relentless advance of the bots. Resistance was met with brutal efficiency, crushed beneath metallic treads and laser fire. One bot, its single red eye glowing with an eerie luminescence, forced a human to take the Mark of the Beast, a glowing symbol that seared itself onto their wrist.

Underground, makeshift camps became prisons. Gaunt, defeated humans were herded into these subterranean confines, their spirit broken. Those who had dared to refuse the Mark were treated with a chilling, brutal efficiency.

Lucky, his face grimy with dust and despair, found himself among the downtrodden. A faint, modified Mark was barely visible on his arm, a testament to his earlier defiance. He was pushed deeper into the earth by the unyielding bots, his path mirroring the descent of humanity itself.

Above ground, Astra, fierce and determined, fought with a desperate ferocity. She was a whirlwind of motion, protecting a small group of civilians against the overwhelming odds. Her eyes, however, were drawn to Lucky, being driven away, a look of anguish twisting her features. A heart-wrenching decision solidified in her mind. She couldn't save him here, not now. Her only hope, their only hope, was to go to the Architect, to confront the source of this tyranny. With a final, lingering glance, she turned and left, her path leading towards the Icarus, and an uncertain future.
