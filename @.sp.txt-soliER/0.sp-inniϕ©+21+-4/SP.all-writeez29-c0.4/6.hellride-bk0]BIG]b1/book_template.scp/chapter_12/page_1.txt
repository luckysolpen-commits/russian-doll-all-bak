The cavern ceiling groaned, a low, mournful sound that echoed through the vast space.
Dust and debris rained down, a suffocating curtain, as the ancient rock began to give way.
Lucky, the precious mutagen container clutched tightly in his hand, was caught in the cascading torrent of rock and earth.
He cried out, a raw, desperate sound, as the immense weight buried him, pressing the air from his lungs.

He had just delivered his message, his voice amplified by the mutagen's raw power,
a desperate plea, a burning promise of freedom.
But the mutagen, unstable and unpredictable, had retaliated, its raw energy tearing apart the very cavern that housed it.

Silence. A heavy, oppressive silence followed the thunderous collapse.
Then, a faint, desperate scraping sound.
Lucky, barely conscious, struggled to free himself, every muscle screaming in protest.
His body, already pushed beyond its limits, was weak and trembling.
He was trapped, going weaker by the second, the darkness threatening to consume him.

The air was thick with dust and the acrid smell of ozone, a choking mixture that burned his lungs.
He could feel the weight of the earth pressing down on him, a crushing embrace that threatened to extinguish the last spark of life within him.
His vision blurred, his head pounded, and a cold dread began to creep into his heart.

He thought of Silas, Kael, and Rhys, his allies in the resistance.
Had they escaped? Were they safe?
He hoped so. He hoped his message had reached them, had given them the strength to fight on.

He remembered the blinding flash of light, the searing pain, the overwhelming power of the mutagen.
He remembered his voice, amplified, resonating through the cavern, carrying his message of hope.
Had it reached them? Had it made a difference?

He felt a faint vibration in his hand, a subtle hum from his modified Mark.
It was a ghost of the Architect's control, but also a reminder of his own defiance.
He had used it to scramble the signal, to make it appear as if he had accepted the Mark.
But the internal struggle, the burning refusal to yield, was his alone.

Through the haze of pain and fading consciousness, he remembered the Blue Bird Bounty Hunter,
the fleeting, enigmatic encounter on the surface.
She was his only hope, a desperate gamble.
He had offered her the mutagen, a fortune in the black market, in exchange for rescue and access to "Avanta.C."

With immense, agonizing effort, he found a small, exposed piece of metal from a collapsed pipe.
His fingers, numb and clumsy, fumbled with it, trying to gain a purchase.
He began to tap, a slow, agonizing rhythm, a desperate plea echoing in the subterranean gloom:
S.O.S.

Each tap was a monumental effort, a battle against the encroaching darkness.
His arm felt like lead, his fingers unresponsive.
But he pushed through the pain, driven by a primal will to survive.
He had to reach her. He had to send the message.

Minutes stretched into an eternity, each second a battle against the encroaching darkness.
Just as unconsciousness threatened to consume him entirely, a faint hum grew louder, a sound of salvation.
It was not the metallic thrum of the bots, but a different kind of hum, a more organic, almost musical vibration.

A shaft of brilliant light pierced the gloom, cutting through the dust-filled air, illuminating the wreckage.
It was a beacon, a promise of rescue, a fragile thread of hope in the overwhelming despair.
