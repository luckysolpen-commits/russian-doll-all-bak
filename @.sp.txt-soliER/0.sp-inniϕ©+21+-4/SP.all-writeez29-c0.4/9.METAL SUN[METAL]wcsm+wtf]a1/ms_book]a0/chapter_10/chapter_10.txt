Chapter 10: Crash on Planet X, Soulpen Comms

An Unobtainium shipment, glowing with an ethereal, almost otherworldly light, was being loaded onto a transport vessel at the Metal Sun's dock. Lucky, Astra, and Gigishixmix oversaw the operation, their nerves stretched taut. "This feels too quiet," Lucky murmured to Astra, his gaze sweeping the surroundings. "Edge is going to make his move."

His words were barely out when the ground trembled violently. A colossal MECHA, the METAL MAN MECHA, burst through a wall, its metallic form a harbinger of chaos. Piloted by EDGE, its eyes glowed a malevolent red, and it unleashed a volley of devastating energy blasts. Metal Mafia thugs, armed and dangerous, swarmed the dock, engaging the Metal Sun's security forces. Chaos erupted, a symphony of explosions and shouts.

Edge, from within the cockpit of the Metal Man Mecha, targeted Lucky. "That Unobtainium is mine, Lucky!" his voice, distorted by the mecha's speakers, boomed across the dock. "And so is your life!" Lucky, still without his fully functional Unobtainium suit, was caught in the crossfire. He dodged a blast, the searing heat licking at his skin. Astra and Gigishixmix tried to cover him, but the sheer force of the attack was overwhelming.

"I have to go!" Lucky shouted to Astra over the din, his voice raw with desperation. "Get out of here!" He saw it then – a shimmering distortion in the air, a nascent WORM HOLE X, unstable and dangerous, but his only escape. It was the wormhole he had discovered, now manifesting due to the immense energy fluctuations from the battle.

With a desperate, adrenaline-fueled leap, Lucky dove towards the shimmering vortex, narrowly avoiding a crushing blow from the Metal Man Mecha. He disappeared into the wormhole just as Edge's massive fist slammed into the spot where he had stood moments before. Edge roared in frustration, his fury echoing across the embattled dock. The Metal Mafia continued their assault, but Lucky was gone.

Lucky's world became a blur of disorienting light and sound as he was propelled through the wormhole. He crashed onto a desolate, alien planet, the impact jarring his bones. His UNOBTAINIUM SUIT, still incomplete and now battered and dented, offered little protection, leaving him vulnerable in this hostile environment. The landscape was unforgiving: twisted metal trees, jagged rock formations, and a sky that burned with an eternal, sickly green fire. The air was thin and acrid, each breath a struggle.

He struggled to his feet, his head throbbing, a wave of nausea washing over him. He checked his SOULPEN, his vital communication device, but it was shattered, useless. A wave of despair, cold and heavy, washed over him. He was alone, stranded on an unknown world, with no way to contact Astra or Gem.

Suddenly, a faint, ethereal glow emanated from his wrist. It was the HALO, a mystical connection he shared with Astra, a bond that transcended physical distance. He focused, and her voice, distant but clear, echoed in his mind, a beacon in the desolate alien landscape. "Lucky? Are you there? What happened?"

"Astra..." he thought, his voice weak, telepathically reaching out. "I'm on some planet. My SoulPen is broken. I don't know where I am." Her response was immediate, filled with a fierce determination. "Don't worry, Lucky. I'll find you. Just... stay safe."

Lucky looked around the desolate landscape, the sickly green sky casting an eerie glow. Survival here would be a daunting challenge. He was alone, with limited resources, and the chilling realization struck him: the Metal Mafia was likely blocking the wormhole, preventing Astra from reaching him. His escape had come at a heavy price, leaving him isolated and facing an uncertain future on Planet X.
