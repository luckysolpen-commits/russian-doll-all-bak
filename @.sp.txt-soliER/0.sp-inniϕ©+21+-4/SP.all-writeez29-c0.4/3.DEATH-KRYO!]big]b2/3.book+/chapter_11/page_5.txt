**Chapter 12: The Deep Chase**

Chaos, a swirling vortex of shattered metal and disoriented bodies, consumed Sáoirse's command center. Gem, Spike, and Astra moved through the wreckage with practiced efficiency, securing the area. Disoriented Super-Soldiers, their augmented bodies now twitching and unresponsive, were being rounded up by New-Korea forces who had now entered the complex, their sleek armor a stark contrast to the ruined grandeur of the UN facility. The air was thick with the smell of ozone and burnt metal, a testament to the ferocity of the battle.

"Perimeter secured," Gem reported into her comms, her voice calm amidst the bedlam. "No other escape routes detected. Sáoirse is definitely in that sub." Her eyes scanned the debris, searching for any sign of a hidden passage, a secret exit, a last-ditch effort at escape.

Spike, his boot nudging a downed Super-Soldier's helmet, grunted. "Good riddance. But I have a bad feeling about this. Sáoirse doesn't run without a plan. She's too cunning, too ruthless. This is just a diversion." His instincts, honed by years of combat, rarely lied, and they were screaming now.

Astra, her fingers flying across a salvaged console, confirmed Spike's unease. "She's heading for the deep ocean. Beyond the continental shelf. Where the pressure is immense, and light doesn't penetrate. It's a graveyard down there. A place where even the most advanced technology struggles to survive." The words hung in the air, a chilling premonition of the horrors that awaited them, a place of no return.

**INT. PURSUIT SUBMARINE - CONTINUOUS**

Lucky, Dorchester, Ji-Hyun, and Soo-Jin were in a sleek, high-speed pursuit submarine, a marvel of New-Korea engineering. The interior was minimalist, dominated by a large panoramic viewscreen that showed the rapidly darkening depths, a swirling vortex of black and blue. The hum of the sub's powerful engines was a low thrum beneath their feet, a counterpoint to the frantic beat of their hearts. The air was thick with the scent of ozone and recycled oxygen, a sterile environment that belied the immense power contained within.

"She's fast," Dorchester muttered, her hands flying over the controls, her eyes fixed on the flickering blip that represented Sáoirse's escape pod. "Too fast for a standard escape pod. This sub must be heavily modified, custom-built for deep-sea evasion, for a desperate, last-ditch escape." Her voice was tight with concentration, every fiber of her being focused on the pursuit, on the hunt, on the capture.

Ji-Hyun, at a tactical console, reported, "Her energy signature is fluctuating. She's pushing her engines to their limit. She's desperate. She's burning out." The digital readouts confirmed the intensity of Sáoirse's flight, the raw power she was expending to escape, the desperate gamble she was taking.
