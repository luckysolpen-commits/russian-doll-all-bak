Lucky, his eyes glued to the viewscreen, a grim determination etched on his face, voiced a chilling thought, a premonition that sent a shiver down his spine. "She's not just running. She's leading us somewhere. She always has a purpose. Sáoirse is a strategist, a manipulator. Her actions are never random. This is a trap." His voice was low, filled with a dawning dread.

Soo-Jin, at a comms station, worked furiously, her fingers dancing across the holographic keyboard, a blur of motion. "I'm trying to hack into her sub's comms, but her encryption is military-grade. It's like trying to crack a diamond with a toothpick. It's impenetrable." The frustration in her voice was palpable, a rare display of emotion from the usually unflappable hacker.

As they descended deeper, the light outside faded, replaced by the eerie, almost blinding glow of the submarine's powerful headlights. Strange, bioluminescent creatures, their forms grotesque and alien, their eyes like pinpricks of light, drifted past the viewscreen, their movements slow and deliberate in the oppressive darkness. They were creatures of nightmare, living fossils from a forgotten age.

"Carc-Zillas," Lucky whispered, a thought striking him with the force of a physical blow, a sudden, horrifying realization. "She commanded them before. What if she's going to a place where she can control them on a massive scale? A breeding ground? A hive?" The idea was horrifying, a nightmare made real, a vision of a world consumed by the monstrous plague.

Dorchester's face was grim, her jaw set, her eyes narrowed. "The deep black. That's where the largest, most ancient Carcazod strains are rumored to reside. Untouched by human intervention. Until now." Her words were a confirmation of Lucky's worst fears, a chilling validation of his darkest suspicions. The deep black was a place of legend, a place of fear, a place where no human dared to venture.

"Energy spike!" Ji-Hyun exclaimed, pointing to a blip on her screen, her voice sharp with alarm. "Massive. Multiple signatures. Directly ahead!" The blip expanded, resolving into hundreds, then thousands, of glowing red eyes that appeared in the darkness. They belonged to Carc-Zillas, their monstrous forms emerging from the abyssal plains, their bodies a twisted mass of metal and flesh. They were larger, more grotesque than the one they had encountered in Sub-Korea, their forms more mutated, more terrifying. They were an army, a living, breathing tide of destruction.

"She's calling them," Lucky said, a chill running down his spine, a cold dread settling in his gut. "She's going to unleash them. She's going to use them to wipe out everything." Sáoirse's submarine, a tiny speck amidst the growing horde, weaved through the Carc-Zillas, seemingly unmolested. It was clear they were under her command, a terrifying extension of her will, her dark power.
