Inside, Sáoirse, elegant and composed even in the face of imminent invasion, sat at a large console, her back to the shattered entrance, her posture regal, her face a mask of serene confidence. She was surrounded by her personal guard – a dozen Augmented Super-Soldiers, their bodies rippling with enhanced musculature, their eyes glowing faintly with cybernetic implants, their weapons held at the ready. They were the pinnacle of UN military engineering, designed for one purpose: to protect Sáoirse at all costs, to be her living shield, her instruments of destruction.

Sáoirse turned slowly, a cold, amused smile playing on her lips, a hint of disdain in her eyes. "Well, well, well. Look what the tide dragged in. Dorchester. And her little friends. I knew you couldn't be trusted. I always knew you were a traitor." Her voice was a silken caress, yet it held an underlying current of venom, a predatory satisfaction, a promise of pain.

"And I knew you were a monster, Sáoirse," Dorchester retorted, her voice sharp and clear, cutting through the air like a blade. With a fluid motion, she drew a sleek, energy-bladed dagger, its edge humming with contained power, its light reflecting in her determined eyes. "Time to end this. Time to end your reign of terror." Her words were a declaration of war, a challenge thrown down.

The Super-Soldiers charged, a wave of enhanced muscle and gleaming armor, their movements synchronized, their faces grim. The command center erupted into a maelstrom of combat, a symphony of clashing metal, crackling energy, and the shouts of battle. The air filled with the scent of ozone and the clang of steel.

Lucky drew his Soul Pen Device. It pulsed with a vibrant blue light as he channeled his energy, firing concentrated blasts that sent Super-Soldiers reeling, their advanced armor smoking, their systems sparking. He moved with fluid grace, a whirlwind of precision strikes, his movements a blur of calculated aggression. He was not just fighting; he was dancing, a deadly ballet of light and shadow, a force of nature unleashed.

Ji-Hyun, a force of nature unleashed, conjured torrents of water, creating slippery surfaces that sent soldiers tumbling, their heavy boots unable to find purchase. She followed with bursts of fire, searing flames that forced them back, their armor sizzling, their screams echoing through the chamber. Her elemental control was absolute, a testament to her mastery of the Tear-It cards, a raw, untamed power.
