"It's a cipher," he stated, his voice gaining a newfound intensity, the weariness replaced by a spark of intellectual excitement. "A pirate's cipher. An old one. Pre-Exodus, I think. From the Golden Age of Oceanic Piracy, before the plagues drove us from the surface." He reached out, his finger tracing the worn, faint lines of the symbols, his mind rapidly, almost instinctively, translating the ancient, cryptic script. He muttered the words aloud as he deciphered them, each syllable a step closer to understanding, a key turning in a long-forgotten lock.

"Dorchester's... game... afoot." The words, when he spoke them, seemed to hang in the water, imbued with a strange, almost mystical significance. The name, Dorchester, echoed in the silent, watery tomb, a name whispered in hushed, fearful tones in the darkest corners of the deep.

Spike's eyes widened, a rare expression of surprise on his usually stoic face. "Dorchester? The pirate? I thought she was just a myth, a ghost story they tell rookie sailors to keep them in line."

"A myth?" Gem scoffed, a wry, almost amused smile playing on her lips. "More like a legend. A force of nature. Stories of her exploits, her daring raids, her impossible escapes, have been whispered in every dive bar and black market across the globe for centuries. But no one ever believed she was real. Not really. She was a ghost, a phantom, a convenient explanation for unexplained disappearances and lost cargo."

Lucky's expression was grim, the initial spark of intellectual curiosity extinguished by the cold, hard reality of the situation. "She's real," he said, his voice low and serious. "And if she's involved in this... then we're in a whole new level of trouble. This isn't just about the UN anymore. This is something else entirely." The cryptic message, a chilling, almost playful invitation to a deadly, high-stakes game, sent a shiver down his spine. This was no longer just about clearing his name, about escaping the UN's clutches. This was about survival, about navigating a treacherous, shadowy new landscape where the rules were unknown and the players were deadly, and legendary. The game, it seemed, had indeed begun.
