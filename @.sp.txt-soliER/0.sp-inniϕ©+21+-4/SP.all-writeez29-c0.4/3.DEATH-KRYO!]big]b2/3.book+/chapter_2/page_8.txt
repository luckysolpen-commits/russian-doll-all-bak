From the sidelines, a younger figure watched with wide, curious eyes, her presence a stark, almost jarring, contrast to the hardened, cynical atmosphere of the lair. Raven, Dorchester's younger sister, couldn't have been more than sixteen. Her bright blue hair, a vibrant, rebellious splash of color against the muted, utilitarian tones of the lair, framed a face that was alight with a mixture of wide-eyed curiosity and a barely contained, youthful excitement. She was a study in vibrant, untamed exuberance, a stark, almost poignant, contrast to her older sister's hardened, world-weary resolve.

"Lucky. I was wondering when you'd show up." Dorchester's voice was husky, confident, a low, seductive purr that commanded attention. It was a voice that could soothe, or threaten, with equal ease. "I trust you got my message. I was beginning to think you'd lost your nerve."

Lucky, still wary, his hand hovering near the Soul Pen device strapped to his wrist, kept his expression neutral, his guard up. "What do you want, Dorchester?" He wasn't about to be drawn into her games, her web of intrigue, without understanding the stakes.

"Straight to business. I like that." Dorchester's smirk widened, a flash of white teeth in the dim light. "I want to offer you a deal. A partnership. An opportunity for a little... creative problem-solving."

Gem, ever the protector, her hand resting on the hilt of her energy blade, stepped forward, her voice laced with a cold, sharp suspicion. "We're not interested in deals with pirates. We're not for sale."

Dorchester threw her head back and laughed, a rich, throaty, genuine sound that echoed through the cavernous space. "Oh, I think you'll be interested in this one," she said, her eyes gleaming with amusement. "You're being hunted. Framed for a murder you didn't commit. Your names are being dragged through the mud. I can help you find out who's behind it. I can help you clear your names. And, more importantly, I can help you get revenge."
