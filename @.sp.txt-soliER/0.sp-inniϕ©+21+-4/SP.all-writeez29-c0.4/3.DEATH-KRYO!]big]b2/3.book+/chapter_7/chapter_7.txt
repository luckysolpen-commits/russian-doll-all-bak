**Chapter 7: Forging Bonds**

The air in the Sub-Korea council chamber, usually thick with the solemnity of
ancient traditions, now crackled with a palpable anticipation. The New-Korea
General, a figure of imposing authority, stood before Lucky and his team, his
posture commanding, his gaze sweeping across their faces. The holographic
display in the center of the chamber, once a mere map, now pulsed with strategic
points, highlighting the vast, intricate dance of power and conflict unfolding
across the ocean's floor.

"The United Nations believes they can control the deep," the General began,
his voice resonating with a quiet power. "They believe their Carc-Zillas and
their naval might are invincible. They are wrong." His words were not a boast,
but a statement of grim fact, born from decades of observation and resistance.

The Mer-Elder, his sapphire scales gleaming softly in the chamber's ambient
light, added his voice, calm but firm. "For generations, our people have lived
in harmony with the ocean. We understand its currents, its depths, its power.
The UN seeks to dominate it, to exploit it. This cannot stand." His ancient
eyes, filled with the wisdom of countless tides, held a quiet resolve that
spoke volumes.

"We have developed a countermeasure," the General continued, a hint of pride
entering his voice. "A device capable of manipulating the very currents of the
ocean. With it, we can turn the tides against the UN, create maelstroms that
will swallow their fleets, and expose their hidden bases." He gestured towards
a sleek, crystalline device that rose from a pedestal in the center of the
table. It was about the size of a human torso, and it pulsed with a soft,
internal light, a silent testament to its immense power.

Lucky's eyes widened, a spark of scientific curiosity igniting within him.
"An oceanic current manipulator?" he breathed, a genuine awe in his voice.
"That's... revolutionary. The energy requirements alone would be astronomical."

"Our fusion reactors, powered by geothermal vents, provide more than enough,"
the General replied, a genuine smile touching his lips. "But the device
requires a specific frequency of sonar signals to activate and control. A
unique signature." He looked directly at Lucky, his gaze piercing. "Your
reputation, Lucky, as a master of sonic technology, precedes you. Your 'Soul
Pen' device, while designed for other purposes, operates on similar principles.
We believe you possess the unique insight and skill to interface with our
device, to truly unleash its potential."

Dorchester stepped forward, placing a hand on Lucky's shoulder, a subtle gesture
of support and encouragement. "This is it, Lucky. Our chance to hit them where
it hurts. To turn their own weapon against them."

Gem, ever cautious, voiced her concerns. "It's a huge risk. What if it's another
trap?" The memory of the Soul Pen's betrayal was still fresh.

"We just escaped one," Spike added, his arms crossed, his gaze unwavering.
"What makes this different?"

The Mer-Elder, with a gentle, reassuring tone, addressed their fears. "We offer
not a trap, but an alliance. Our survival, and the survival of the ocean,
depends on it. We seek not to dominate, but to protect." His words carried the
weight of ancient wisdom, a promise of genuine partnership.

Lucky looked from the pulsating device to the faces of the council, then to his
friends. The scale of the problem was immense, but the potential solution was
equally grand. "And what do you want in return?" he asked, his voice steady.
"Beyond just deploying this device."

The New-Korea General's smile widened slightly. "We want the UN's genocidal
plans to end. We want peace. And we want to ensure that such a threat can never
rise again. We believe your unique technological insights, combined with our
resources, could secure that future."

Lucky considered this. Dorchester gave him a subtle nod of encouragement. The
risks were undeniable, but the potential rewards, the chance to truly make a
difference, were too great to ignore. "Alright," he said, taking a deep breath.
"We'll consider your proposal. But we'll need to discuss the terms. And we'll
need guarantees."

"Excellent," the General replied, a genuine smile now gracing his features.
"Let us move to the negotiation chamber. We are prepared to be... flexible."

**INT. SUB-KOREA NEGOTIATION CHAMBER - DAY**

The negotiation chamber was a study in minimalist efficiency, a stark contrast
to the ornate council hall. Sleek, polished surfaces reflected the soft, ambient
light, and holographic displays projected data and agreements with silent
precision. Lucky, Dorchester, Gem, Spike, and Astra sat across from the New-
Korea General and a Mer-People Diplomat, the air thick with the unspoken weight
of their decisions. Raven, ever the quiet observer, sat in a corner, occasionally
tapping notes onto a tablet, her bright blue hair a splash of color in the
otherwise subdued room.

"Our proposal is simple: an alliance," the New-Korea General stated, projecting
a holographic document onto the table. "We provide the resources, the technology,
and the strategic support. You provide the unique expertise to operate the
current manipulator and, of course, your... unconventional methods."

Lucky leaned forward, his gaze direct. "And our terms are equally simple. We
deploy the device. We control its activation. And we retain a piece of the
technology. A full schematic, and access to its core programming. For research
purposes." It was a bold demand, a calculated risk, but he knew his worth.

The General and the Diplomat exchanged a glance, a silent communication passing
between them. The Mer-People Diplomat, his voice calm and measured, finally
spoke. "Such a powerful device... its secrets are closely guarded."

Dorchester, a wry smile playing on her lips, interjected. "And Lucky's secrets
are even more so. He's the only one who can make your toy sing. A fair exchange,
wouldn't you say?" Her words, though blunt, carried the undeniable ring of truth.

The General considered this, his gaze lingering on Lucky, then on the holographic
schematics of the device. Finally, he nodded slowly. "Agreed. A full schematic,
and access to the core programming. Provided the device is used solely for
defensive purposes against the UN's aggression." The concession was significant,
a testament to their desperation and their trust in Lucky's abilities.

"Understood," Lucky replied, a sense of relief washing over him. "And if anything
goes wrong, if this alliance proves to be a trap, or if your intentions shift...
we walk away. No questions asked, no repercussions." He wanted an escape clause,
a safeguard against further betrayal.

Spike, ever the protector, added his own condition. "And our people. The clone-
merpeople. They get full protection. No more experiments, no more provocations."

The Mer-People Diplomat's face softened, a genuine smile gracing his lips.
"That is our primary objective. Their safety is paramount." His sincerity was
unmistakable.

Gem, ever vigilant, turned to Astra. "Can you verify these schematics? Make sure
there are no hidden backdoors?"

"On it," Astra replied, already tapping on her comms, her fingers flying across
a holographic keyboard. "I'll need a secure connection to their network."

"Access granted," the New-Korea General stated, gesturing to a glowing port on
the table. "Our systems are open to you. We believe in transparency in this
alliance." The gesture was a powerful one, a sign of their commitment.

Lucky looked at Dorchester, who gave him a subtle nod, a silent affirmation.
He extended his hand across the table. "Then we have a deal." The General shook
his hand firmly, his grip strong and resolute. The Mer-People Diplomat also
shook his hand, a sense of hope shining in his ancient eyes.

"Excellent," the New-Korea General declared, his voice filled with renewed
purpose. "The deployment window is narrow. We must move quickly. Our intelligence
indicates a major UN naval exercise is scheduled for tomorrow. A perfect
opportunity."

Lucky's eyes gleamed with a determined glint. The game had changed. They were
no longer hunted, but hunters. "Then let's get to work."

**FADE OUT.**
