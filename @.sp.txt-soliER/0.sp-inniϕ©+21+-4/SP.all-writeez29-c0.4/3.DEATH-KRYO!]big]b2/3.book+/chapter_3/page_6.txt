The Sub-Korea Research Institute, a monolithic, brutalist structure of reinforced steel and darkened, impenetrable glass, loomed before them, a silent, imposing sentinel in the perpetual twilight of the deep. Under the cloak of the ocean's darkness, the team infiltrated the facility, moving with the practiced, silent stealth of seasoned operatives, a ghostly ballet of precision and efficiency. The constant, low hum of distant machinery was the only sound, a rhythmic, industrial pulse that seemed to echo the frantic, pounding beat of their own hearts.

The corridors, stark and utilitarian, stretched into the oppressive gloom, their polished, metallic surfaces reflecting the faint, eerie glow of the emergency lights, casting long, dancing, distorted shadows that seemed to writhe and twist with an unseen, malevolent menace. They moved with the silent, deadly grace of ghosts, a phantom strike team in the heart of the enemy's fortress.

Lucky, his fingers flying across holographic keypads, his mind a whirlwind of complex algorithms and decryption codes, bypassed electronic locks with an almost supernatural, effortless ease. His technical prowess, his ability to see the patterns in the chaos, was a stark, elegant contrast to the brute force often employed in such high-stakes infiltrations. Gem and Spike, their movements fluid and silent, a symphony of controlled violence, disabled security cameras with surgical, deadly precision, their bodies blending seamlessly, almost invisibly, with the deep, oppressive shadows. Astra, her bio-enhanced senses constantly scanning, a silent, vigilant guardian, alerted them to the approach of unseen patrols, her whispers a barely audible, ghostly warning through their comms. Raven, surprisingly, almost unnervingly, adept for her age, acted as a nimble, silent scout, her small, agile frame slipping through narrow, claustrophobic vents and forgotten, dusty maintenance passages, relaying vital, real-time information about enemy positions and patrol routes.

Their destination was the main laboratory, the same vast, cavernous hall they had glimpsed through the drone's fleeting, doomed camera feed. Even with the prior knowledge, the sheer, breathtaking scale of it was terrifying. Thousands upon thousands of sub-robots, in various stages of assembly, lined the vast, echoing hall. Some were mere skeletons of gleaming, polished metal, their inner workings exposed, others fully formed, their multi-jointed, insect-like limbs poised as if ready to spring to life at a moment's notice. The air was thick with the sharp, metallic tang of newly forged components and the faint, acrid, almost bitter scent of ozone. This wasn't just a research facility; it was a factory, a silent, relentless, terrifying engine of war.
