Raven's face lit up, a wide, infectious grin spreading across her features. She pulled a small, sleek, insect-like device from a hidden pouch on her belt. It was a sophisticated, state-of-the-art drone, no bigger than her palm, its miniature, silent propellers whirring to life with a barely audible hum. With a flick of her wrist, a gesture that was both casual and incredibly precise, she launched it. The drone, a tiny, almost invisible speck against the vast, imposing facade of the institute, zipped silently towards its target, its advanced camouflage technology making it all but undetectable to the naked eye. It fed a live, high-definition video stream to a shared, holographic display on Astra's datapad.

The drone, guided by Raven's nimble, dancing fingers on a holographic interface, slipped through an open, unguarded ventilation shaft, its camera providing a clear, albeit slightly shaky, view of the institute's interior. The scale of the operation was staggering, far beyond anything they had anticipated. Rows upon rows of workstations stretched into the distance, each manned by scientists in sterile, white lab coats. They were assembling and programming the sub-robots, their movements precise, efficient, and disturbingly robotic. Thousands of the spider-like machines, in various stages of completion, lined the vast, cavernous halls, a silent, waiting army in the making.

"They're building an army," Lucky breathed, the words a chilling, almost whispered realization. This wasn't just about corporate espionage or gaining a controlling interest in the city's economy. This was about something far more sinister, something that threatened the delicate, fragile balance of power in the entire underwater world.

Suddenly, the drone's camera whipped around, its view momentarily obscured by a blur of motion. A large, shadowy figure, far too massive to be human, was moving in the background, its form indistinct, partially hidden in the low, ambient light of the laboratory. It was glimpsed for only a fraction of a second, a fleeting, terrifying shadow that sent a jolt of cold, primal unease through the group.

"What was that?" Spike demanded, his voice tight with a sudden, sharp alarm.

Raven, her brow furrowed in concentration, her tongue sticking out slightly from the corner of her mouth, tried to maneuver the drone for a closer, clearer look. But before she could, a piercing, deafening alarm blared through the institute, a cacophony of sound that reverberated even through the drone's tiny, sensitive speakers. Red lights flashed, bathing the interior in an ominous, pulsating glow. A sudden, sharp crack echoed through the comms, and the video feed dissolved into a shower of static. The drone had been shot down, its mission, and its existence, abruptly, violently terminated.
