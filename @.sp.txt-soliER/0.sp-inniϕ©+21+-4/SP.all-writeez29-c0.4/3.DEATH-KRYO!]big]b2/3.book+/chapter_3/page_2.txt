Stepping into the city proper was like entering another world, another dimension. The streets, wide, sweeping avenues of polished obsidian and glowing coral, were bustling, teeming with a vibrant, chaotic tapestry of life. Mer-people, their scales shimmering with every color of the rainbow, glided effortlessly through the water, their powerful tails propelling them with an elegant, hypnotic grace. Humans, encased in sleek, personalized submersibles that looked like miniature, exotic fish, or walking in specialized, form-fitting atmospheric suits, mingled with them, a testament to the city's unique, and often fraught, coexistence. The air, or rather the water, filtered and recycled, carried the faint, clean scent of processed algae and something else, something indefinable, a scent of ambition, innovation, and a faint, underlying tang of corruption.

Lucky found himself mesmerized, his scientist's mind struggling to process the sheer scale and beauty of it all. After the desolate, ghostly ruins and the confined, claustrophobic spaces of Dorchester's lair, New Atlantis was a dazzling, overwhelming assault on the senses. He watched a group of children, a mix of human and mer-children, their laughter echoing through the water, playing a game of tag amidst the towering coral structures. It was a stark, poignant reminder of the world they were fighting to protect, a world that Sáoirse and her shadowy, unknown faction threatened to tear apart, to poison with their hatred and their lust for power.

"So, where do we start?" Astra asked, her calm, practical voice cutting through Lucky's momentary, wistful reverie. Her gaze, ever analytical, was already assessing the flow of traffic, the placement of the ubiquitous security checkpoints, the potential vulnerabilities in the city's infrastructure.

"We start by finding the source of the problem," Dorchester replied, her voice low and conspiratorial, a hunter's glint in her eye. "The faction that's pulling the strings. The ones who sent those agents after you, Lucky. The ones who are trying to start a war." She led them away from the glittering, ostentatious central districts, with their luxury boutiques and their five-star restaurants, towards a less glamorous, more industrial part of the city. Here, the beautiful, organic coral buildings gave way to structures of reinforced steel and dull, utilitarian composites. The water grew heavier, tinged with the metallic scent of heavy machinery and the faint, acrid tang of ozone.
