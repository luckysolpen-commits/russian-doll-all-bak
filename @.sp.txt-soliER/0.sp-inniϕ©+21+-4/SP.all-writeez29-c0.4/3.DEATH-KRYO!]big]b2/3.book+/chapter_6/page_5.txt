**Page 5**

The deployment vessel, once a symbol of their audacious plan, was now a twisted, mangled wreckage, consumed by the very anomaly it had sought to unleash. On the bridge, chaos reigned supreme. Alarms shrieked, their piercing cries echoing through the mangled metal, a symphony of impending doom. Lights flashed a frantic, blood-red, painting the scene in a macabre, terrifying glow. The crew, a blur of desperate motion, scrambled to stabilize the ship, to fight against the inevitable, but it was a futile effort. Outside the viewport, the massive energy anomaly from the malfunctioning device pulsed with a terrifying, growing intensity, a vortex of dark energy that threatened to swallow everything in its path, to consume the very fabric of reality.

"I can't shut it down!" Lucky's voice, raw with desperation, tore through the cacophony, a desperate plea against the rising tide of destruction. He was still at the console, his fingers flying across the unresponsive holographic interface, his mind racing, frantically searching for a solution, for a way to stop the unstoppable. "It's drawing power from everywhere! It's self-sustaining! It's a runaway reaction!" The device, once his tool, his creation, had become a monstrous, uncontrollable entity, a living, breathing black hole of pure, destructive power.

Dorchester, her face pale with a dawning horror, grabbed his arm, her grip like a vise, her nails digging into his flesh. "We have to get out of here! Now! This thing is going to consume everything! It's going to consume us all!" Her usual composure had shattered, replaced by a primal, animalistic fear, a raw terror that was contagious.

"All personnel, abandon ship! Repeat, abandon ship! The device is unstable! It's going to blow!" The New-Korea General's voice, distorted by static and fear, crackled over the comms, a desperate command that confirmed their worst fears. There was no saving the vessel, no containing the anomaly. Their grand plan had backfired spectacularly, catastrophically.

They scrambled towards an escape pod, their movements clumsy, desperate, the vessel groaning under the immense strain, its metal protesting with a tortured shriek, its very structure tearing itself apart. Just as they sealed the hatch, a massive wave of dark energy, a silent, consuming force, engulfed the deployment vessel, tearing it apart with an almost casual ease, reducing it to a cloud of shimmering debris. The escape pod was violently ejected, tumbling through the turbulent water, a tiny speck of hope in a sea of destruction, a fragile bubble against the overwhelming power of the unleashed anomaly.
