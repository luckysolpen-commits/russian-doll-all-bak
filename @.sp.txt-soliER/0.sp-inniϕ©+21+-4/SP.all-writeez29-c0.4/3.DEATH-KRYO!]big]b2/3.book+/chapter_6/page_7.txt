"Welcome," Ji-Hyun said, her voice soft, yet firm, a quiet authority in her tone. "You are safe here. We heard of your... incident. The device is contained, for now. But it was a close call. Too close for comfort." Her eyes held a deep understanding, a shared burden of knowledge.

"Too close," Lucky admitted, still haunted by the memory of the uncontrolled power, the raw, destructive force he had almost unleashed. "We almost destroyed everything we were trying to protect. The irony is almost unbearable." The bitter taste of failure, of unintended consequences, lingered in his mouth.

Dorchester, in a rare moment of vulnerability, sighed, a deep, weary exhalation. "I miscalculated. I didn't realize the UN had tampered with the device's core programming. They wanted it to fail, to create a disaster they could then 'solve', to justify their intervention." Her voice was low, filled with a quiet, simmering fury, a deep-seated resentment for the manipulation she had endured.

Soo-Jin, handing Lucky another steaming cup of tea, nodded sagely, her eyes twinkling with a mischievous glint. "Such is the way of the UN. They create the problems, then offer the solutions. A classic power play. But here, we have our own solutions. Our own way of fighting back." She gestured to a beautifully crafted wooden box on a nearby table. Inside, nestled on a bed of soft, crimson velvet, were dozens of intricately designed cards, each glowing with a faint, internal light, a soft, pulsating luminescence. These were Tear-It Cards.

"What are these?" Lucky asked, his scientific curiosity piqued, a familiar spark igniting in his eyes. He reached out, his fingers hovering over the glowing cards, a sense of wonder filling him.

Ji-Hyun explained, her voice filled with reverence, with a deep, abiding respect for the ancient power they held. "These are our heritage. Our power. The Tear-It Deck. They harness the energy of 'blood and souls'. Not in a macabre way, not through sacrifice, but through the resonance of intense human emotion and life force. Each card is attuned to a specific elemental force, a specific aspect of the human spirit. They are a conduit, a bridge between the physical and the spiritual."

Hye-Mi added gently, her voice soft and soothing, like a gentle breeze. "They are a conduit. A way to channel our inner strength, our will, into the world. To mend, to protect, to fight. To create, to destroy, to shape reality itself." Her words were a testament to the profound power contained within the seemingly simple cards.

Mi-Sun, ever the pragmatist, ever the warrior, grinned, a fierce, determined glint in her eyes. "And to kick some serious UN butt!" Her words, though blunt, were a welcome dose of reality, a reminder of the fight that still lay ahead.
