The New-Korea forces and the merpeople, equally stunned, equally bewildered, ceased their attacks. The vast, chaotic battlefield fell into an uneasy, almost silent, stalemate. The millions of Carc-Zillas drifted aimlessly, their red eyes, once burning with malice, now dull and lifeless, their monstrous forms slowly fading into the gloom, their purpose gone. "It's over?" Dorchester let out a bitter laugh, devoid of mirth, a harsh, cynical sound that cut through the silence. "Politics. The ultimate weapon. More effective than any Carc-Zilla. They had fought a desperate, bloody war, only for it to be ended by a stroke of a pen in a distant boardroom, by the machinations of unseen powers, by the cold, calculating hand of diplomacy.

**EXT. SURFACE - DAY (SEVERAL WEEKS LATER)**

The world had returned to an uneasy, anticlimactic peace. News channels were abuzz with analysis of the sudden truce, dissecting every nuance of the political maneuverings that had brought it about. Sáoirse's whereabouts were unknown, her public image carefully managed by the UN, who issued a statement about her 'retirement' due to 'health reasons.' The Carc-Zillas, their threat neutralized by the political machinations of global powers, had retreated to the deepest parts of the ocean, their monstrous forms once again relegated to the realm of myth and rumor, a forgotten nightmare, a silent threat lurking in the shadows.

Lucky, Gem, Spike, Astra, Dorchester, and Raven were back at the Korean-style villa, the sanctuary that had become their temporary home. The training dummies were gone, the Tear-It cards neatly stacked, their vibrant glow now subdued. The atmosphere was one of quiet reflection, almost disappointment. The adrenaline of battle had faded, replaced by a lingering sense of unfulfillment, a hollow victory, a sense of purpose lost.

"All that fighting... all that risk... for a treaty signed in a boardroom," Lucky mused, staring out at the vast, open ocean, its surface calm and deceptively peaceful. The contrast between the serene exterior and the turbulent depths beneath was a stark reminder of the hidden conflicts that still simmered, of the battles yet to be fought, of the endless struggle for power.
