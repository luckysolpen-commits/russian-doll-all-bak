**Chapter 12: The Deep Chase**

Chaos, a swirling vortex of shattered metal and disoriented bodies, consumed Sáoirse's command center. Gem, Spike, and Astra moved through the wreckage with practiced efficiency, securing the area. Disoriented Super-Soldiers, their augmented bodies now twitching and unresponsive, were being rounded up by New-Korea forces who had now entered the complex, their sleek armor a stark contrast to the ruined grandeur of the UN facility.

"Perimeter secured," Gem reported into her comms, her voice calm amidst the bedlam. "No other escape routes detected. Sáoirse is definitely in that sub." Her eyes scanned the debris, searching for any sign of a hidden passage, a secret exit.

Spike, his boot nudging a downed Super-Soldier's helmet, grunted. "Good riddance. But I have a bad feeling about this. Sáoirse doesn't run without a plan. She's too cunning, too ruthless." His instincts, honed by years of combat, rarely lied.

Astra, her fingers flying across a salvaged console, confirmed Spike's unease. "She's heading for the deep ocean. Beyond the continental shelf. Where the pressure is immense, and light doesn't penetrate. It's a graveyard down there." The words hung in the air, a chilling premonition of the horrors that awaited them, a place where even the most advanced technology struggled to survive.

**INT. PURSUIT SUBMARINE - CONTINUOUS**

Lucky, Dorchester, Ji-Hyun, and Soo-Jin were in a sleek, high-speed pursuit submarine, a marvel of New-Korea engineering. The interior was minimalist, dominated by a large panoramic viewscreen that showed the rapidly darkening depths. The hum of the sub's powerful engines was a low thrum beneath their feet, a counterpoint to the frantic beat of their hearts. The air was thick with the scent of ozone and recycled oxygen, a sterile environment that belied the immense power contained within.

"She's fast," Dorchester muttered, her hands flying over the controls, her eyes fixed on the flickering blip that represented Sáoirse's escape pod. "Too fast for a standard escape pod. This sub must be heavily modified, custom-built for deep-sea evasion." Her voice was tight with concentration, every fiber of her being focused on the pursuit, on the hunt.

Ji-Hyun, at a tactical console, reported, "Her energy signature is fluctuating. She's pushing her engines to their limit. She's desperate." The digital readouts confirmed the intensity of Sáoirse's flight, the raw power she was expending to escape.
