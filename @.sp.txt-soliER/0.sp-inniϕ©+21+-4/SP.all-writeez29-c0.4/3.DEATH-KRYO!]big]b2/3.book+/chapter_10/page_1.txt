**Chapter 10: A Deadly Plot**

**Page 1**

The Korean-style villa, usually a haven of tranquility, its traditional architecture a soothing balm to the soul, felt charged with a subtle, electric tension. The soft, almost imperceptible hum of the air conditioning was the only sound, a counterpoint to the rapid-fire clicks of holographic interfaces and the low murmur of voices. Lucky, Dorchester, Ji-Hyun, Soo-Jin, Hye-Mi, and Mi-Sun were gathered around a shimmering, three-dimensional map of the world, projected onto a polished obsidian table. Their focus, however, was narrowed to a single, ominous point: a heavily fortified UN complex nestled in the heart of Geneva, Switzerland. The air was thick with the scent of freshly brewed tea and the faint, metallic tang of advanced technology.

"Sáoirse's command center," Dorchester stated, her finger hovering over a section of the glowing, intricate complex, her voice low, almost a purr, but with an underlying steel that left no room for doubt. "Heavily guarded, yes, but not impenetrable. I've been feeding her false intelligence for weeks, making her believe I'm working on a top-secret project for her. She trusts me. Or, at least, she trusts my ability to deliver results." A wry, almost cynical smile touched her lips, a fleeting glimpse of the pirate beneath the polished facade.

Lucky, ever the pragmatist, ever the scientist, raised an eyebrow, a flicker of skepticism in his eyes. "She trusts a notorious pirate? A woman whose reputation precedes her in every black market and illicit port across the globe?" His skepticism was evident, a natural reaction to the audacious claim, to the sheer improbability of it.

Dorchester's smile widened, a glint of amusement, almost triumph, in her emerald eyes. "She trusts a mercenary who gets results. And I've been giving her results. Small, controlled leaks of information that make her look good, that subtly undermine her rivals within the UN, that solidify her position. It's a dangerous game, a high-stakes dance on the edge of a precipice, but I thrive on such risks. It's what I do." Her confidence was absolute, her self-assurance almost unnerving.

Ji-Hyun, her calm demeanor unwavering, her sharp, intelligent eyes missing nothing, voiced the obvious, unspoken question. "So, you're suggesting a direct assault? An assassination? That's a bold move, even for you, Dorchester." Her tone was neutral, professional, but her eyes betrayed a flicker of concern, a hint of the immense danger they were contemplating.
