**Chapter 12: The Deep Chase**

**Page 1**

Chaos, a swirling vortex of shattered metal and disoriented bodies, consumed Sáoirse's command center. The air was thick with the acrid scent of ozone and burnt metal, a testament to the ferocity of the battle that had just raged. Gem, Spike, and Astra moved through the wreckage with practiced efficiency, their movements fluid and precise, securing the area. Disoriented Super-Soldiers, their augmented bodies now twitching and unresponsive, lay scattered amidst the debris, their cybernetic implants sparking erratically. They were being rounded up by New-Korea forces who had now entered the complex, their sleek, dark armor a stark contrast to the ruined grandeur of the UN facility. The sounds of their heavy boots echoed through the shattered halls, a grim counterpoint to the wails of the wounded.

"Perimeter secured," Gem reported into her comms, her voice calm amidst the bedlam, her eyes scanning the debris, searching for any sign of a hidden passage, a secret exit, a last-ditch effort at escape. Her energy blade hummed softly in her hand, a silent promise of swift justice.

Spike, his boot nudging a downed Super-Soldier's helmet, grunted, a sound of grim satisfaction. "Good riddance. But I have a bad feeling about this. Sáoirse doesn't run without a plan. She's too cunning, too ruthless. This is just a diversion. A calculated retreat." His instincts, honed by years of brutal combat, rarely lied, and they were screaming now, a silent alarm bell ringing in his mind.

Astra, her fingers flying across a salvaged console, confirmed Spike's unease, her face grim. "She's heading for the deep ocean. Beyond the continental shelf. Where the pressure is immense, and light doesn't penetrate. It's a graveyard down there. A place where even the most advanced technology struggles to survive, where life itself is a constant struggle against the crushing darkness." The words hung in the air, a chilling premonition of the horrors that awaited them, a place of no return, a place where even hope struggled to breathe.

**INT. PURSUIT SUBMARINE - CONTINUOUS**

Lucky, Dorchester, Ji-Hyun, and Soo-Jin were in a sleek, high-speed pursuit submarine, a marvel of New-Korea engineering. The interior was minimalist, dominated by a large panoramic viewscreen that showed the rapidly darkening depths, a swirling vortex of black and blue, a descent into the unknown. The hum of the sub's powerful engines was a low thrum beneath their feet, a counterpoint to the frantic beat of their hearts. The air was thick with the scent of ozone and recycled oxygen, a sterile environment that belied the immense power contained within, the raw, untamed energy of the deep.
