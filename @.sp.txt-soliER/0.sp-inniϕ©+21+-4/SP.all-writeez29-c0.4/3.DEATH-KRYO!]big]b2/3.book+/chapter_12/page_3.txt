Dorchester's face was grim, her jaw set, her eyes narrowed, a cold, hard resolve etched into her features. "The deep black. That's where the largest, most ancient Carcazod strains are rumored to reside. Untouched by human intervention. Until now. A place of legend, a place of fear, a place where no human dares to venture." Her words were a confirmation of Lucky's worst fears, a chilling validation of his darkest suspicions. The deep black was a place of no return, a place where even hope struggled to breathe.

"Energy spike!" Ji-Hyun exclaimed, pointing to a blip on her screen, her voice sharp with alarm. "Massive. Multiple signatures. Directly ahead!" The blip expanded, resolving into hundreds, then thousands, of glowing red eyes that appeared in the darkness. They belonged to Carc-Zillas, their monstrous forms emerging from the abyssal plains, their bodies a twisted mass of metal and flesh. They were larger, more grotesque than the one they had encountered in Sub-Korea, their forms more mutated, more terrifying, more powerful. They were an army, a living, breathing tide of destruction, a nightmare made manifest.

"She's calling them," Lucky said, a chill running down his spine, a cold dread settling in his gut. "She's going to unleash them. She's going to use them to wipe out everything. To cleanse the deep of all life." Sáoirse's submarine, a tiny speck amidst the growing horde, weaved through the Carc-Zillas, seemingly unmolested. It was clear they were under her command, a terrifying extension of her will, her dark power, her twisted ambition.

"There are millions of them," Soo-Jin whispered, her voice tight with despair, her face pale, her eyes wide with horror. "We can't fight them all. It's suicide. We'll be overwhelmed." The sheer number of the creatures was overwhelming, a tide of nightmares, a sea of death.

"We don't have to," Dorchester retorted, her jaw set, her eyes burning with a fierce determination, a cold, calculating resolve. "We just have to get to Sáoirse. She's the key. Cut off the head, and the body will die. We take her down, and the horde will fall." The pursuit sub was suddenly buffeted by a massive Carc-Zilla, its grotesque face pressing against the viewscreen, its single, red eye staring into their souls, a silent, malevolent challenge. The sub's hull groaned under the immense pressure, the metal protesting with a tortured shriek, its very structure threatening to buckle, to implode.
