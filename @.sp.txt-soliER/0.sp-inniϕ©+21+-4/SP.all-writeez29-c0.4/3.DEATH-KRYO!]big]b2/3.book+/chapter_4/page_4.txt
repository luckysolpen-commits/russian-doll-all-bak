"We don't. Not yet," Dorchester replied, her eyes narrowed in thought, her mind a whirlwind of calculations and strategies. "First, we need to know what we're walking into. We need intelligence. We need to see inside the belly of the beast before we try to slay it. Raven, you're up." She turned to her younger sister, a mischievous, almost predatory, glint in her eye. Raven's face lit up, a wide, infectious grin spreading across her features. She pulled a small, sleek, insect-like device from a hidden pouch on her belt. It was a sophisticated, state-of-the-art drone, no bigger than her palm, its miniature, silent propellers whirring to life with a barely audible hum. With a flick of her wrist, a gesture that was both casual and incredibly precise, she launched it. The drone, a tiny, almost invisible speck against the vast, imposing facade of the institute, zipped silently towards its target, its advanced camouflage technology making it all but undetectable to the naked eye. It fed a live, high-definition video stream to a shared, holographic display on Astra's datapad.

The drone, guided by Raven's nimble, dancing fingers on a holographic interface, slipped through an open, unguarded ventilation shaft, its camera providing a clear, albeit slightly shaky, view of the institute's interior. The scale of the operation was staggering, far beyond anything they had anticipated. Rows upon rows of workstations stretched into the distance, each manned by scientists in sterile, white lab coats. They were assembling and programming the sub-robots, their movements precise, efficient, and disturbingly robotic. Thousands of the spider-like machines, in various stages of completion, lined the vast, cavernous halls, a silent, waiting army in the making.

"They're building an army," Lucky breathed, the words a chilling, almost whispered realization. This wasn't just about corporate espionage or gaining a controlling interest in the city's economy. This was about something far more sinister, something that threatened the delicate, fragile balance of power in the entire underwater world. It was a declaration of war, a silent, insidious war that had been brewing beneath the surface for years.

Suddenly, the drone's camera whipped around, its view momentarily obscured by a blur of motion. A large, shadowy figure, far too massive to be human, was moving in the background, its form indistinct, partially hidden in the low, ambient light of the laboratory. It was glimpsed for only a fraction of a second, a fleeting, terrifying shadow that sent a jolt of cold, primal unease through the group. It was something unnatural, something that shouldn't exist.
