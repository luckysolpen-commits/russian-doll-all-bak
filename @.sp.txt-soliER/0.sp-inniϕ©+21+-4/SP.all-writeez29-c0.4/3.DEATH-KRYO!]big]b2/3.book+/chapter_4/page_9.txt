"It's a Carcazod," Lucky whispered, his voice barely audible, a choked, strangled sound. "They weaponized it." The realization hit him with the force of a physical blow, knocking the air from his lungs. His cure, his triumph, his life's work, had been twisted, perverted, turned into this monstrosity. The UN hadn't just wanted his company; they had wanted his knowledge, his research, his genius, to create something far more terrifying, far more destructive, than the original plague. They had taken his gift to humanity and turned it into a curse.

Suddenly, with a jolt that sent a ripple of pure, unadulterated fear through the team, the creature's eyes snapped open. They glowed with a malevolent, pulsating red light, twin beacons of pure, unadulterated malice. An alarm, shrill and piercing, blared throughout the facility, its sound echoing off the cavernous walls, a siren of impending, inescapable doom. The air filled with the stench of fear and ozone.

"We've overstayed our welcome!" Dorchester yelled, her voice cutting through the cacophony, her hand already on her energy pistol, her eyes scanning the room for an escape route, her mind already calculating the odds of survival. Her usual bravado was replaced by a grim determination.

Carc-Zilla let out a deafening, soul-shattering roar, a sound that seemed to tear through the very fabric of the air, and lunged. Its massive claws, tipped with razor-sharp, diamond-hard blades, swiped through the space where they had just been standing. The team scattered, dodging its monstrous, lightning-fast attack, their movements a blur of desperate, panicked flight. The creature was incredibly, impossibly fast, its immense, bulky form belying its terrifying agility, and its power was immense, each swipe of its claws capable of tearing through reinforced steel as if it were paper. The ground trembled with its movements.

"We can't fight that thing!" Spike yelled, his voice strained with effort as he narrowly, miraculously avoided a crushing, bone-shattering blow from Carc-Zilla's massive, armored tail. He was a formidable fighter, but this was something else entirely.

"We don't have to! This way!" Dorchester shouted, already leading the way towards a maintenance tunnel, a narrow, dark opening in the wall that promised a slim, desperate chance at escape. But their path was blocked. A squad of Sub-Korea soldiers, clad in sleek, black, state-of-the-art armor, emerged from the shadows, their energy rifles raised. They opened fire, a barrage of searing, white-hot plasma bolts that illuminated the dark, narrow tunnel. The air crackled with energy, the smell of ozone and burnt metal filling their nostrils. It was a desperate, hopeless situation, a trap from which there seemed no escape.
