"What was that?" Spike demanded, his voice tight with a sudden, sharp alarm, his hand instinctively going to the hilt of his weapon. He was a man of action, and the unknown always made him uneasy.

Raven, her brow furrowed in concentration, her tongue sticking out slightly from the corner of her mouth, tried to maneuver the drone for a closer, clearer look. But before she could, a piercing, deafening alarm blared through the institute, a cacophony of sound that reverberated even through the drone's tiny, sensitive speakers. Red lights flashed, bathing the interior in an ominous, pulsating glow. A sudden, sharp crack echoed through the comms, and the video feed dissolved into a shower of static. The drone had been shot down, its mission, and its existence, abruptly, violently terminated. The silence that followed was almost as deafening as the alarm, a chilling testament to the efficiency of their unseen enemy.

Dorchester's lips curled into a grim, almost feral smile. "So, they have a pet," she said, her tone almost gleeful, a testament to her love of a good, old-fashioned, high-stakes challenge. "This just got more interesting. Much more interesting."

"We need to get inside," Lucky declared, his voice firm, resolute, all traces of his earlier, mesmerized wonder gone, replaced by a cold, hard determination. The shadowy figure, the sheer, terrifying scale of the sub-robot production, had ignited a new, burning resolve within him. "We need to find out what that thing is. We need to find out what they're planning. We need to stop them. Before it's too late."

Dorchester's smile widened, a predatory, dangerous glint in her eyes. "I thought you'd never ask," she purred, her voice a low, satisfied rumble. "Let's go hunting."
