The air in the Sub-Korea council chamber, usually thick with the solemnity of ancient traditions and the quiet hum of advanced technology, now crackled with a palpable, almost electric anticipation. The New-Korea General, a figure of imposing authority, his uniform crisp and adorned with countless medals, stood before Lucky and his team, his posture commanding, his gaze sweeping across their faces, assessing each one with a keen, intelligent eye. The holographic display in the center of the chamber, once a mere map, now pulsed with strategic points, glowing red and blue, highlighting the vast, intricate dance of power and conflict unfolding across the ocean's floor.

"The United Nations believes they can control the deep," the General began, his voice resonating with a quiet, unwavering power, a deep rumble that seemed to vibrate through the very water. "They believe their Carc-Zillas and their naval might are invincible. They are wrong. Terribly, fatally wrong." His words were not a boast, but a statement of grim, undeniable fact, born from decades of observation, resistance, and bitter experience.

The Mer-Elder, his sapphire scales gleaming softly in the chamber's ambient light, added his voice, calm but firm, his ancient eyes holding a deep, profound wisdom. "For generations, our people have lived in harmony with the ocean. We understand its currents, its depths, its power, its delicate balance. The UN seeks to dominate it, to exploit it, to bend it to their will. This cannot stand. We will not allow it." His words carried the weight of ancient wisdom, a promise of unwavering resolve.

"We have developed a countermeasure," the General continued, a hint of pride, almost a flicker of triumph, entering his voice. "A device capable of manipulating the very currents of the ocean. With it, we can turn the tides against the UN, create maelstroms that will swallow their fleets whole, and expose their hidden bases, their secret operations." He gestured towards a sleek, crystalline device that rose from a pedestal in the center of the table. It was about the size of a human torso, and it pulsed with a soft, internal light, a silent testament to its immense, untapped power. Its surface shimmered with intricate, almost organic patterns, a blend of natural beauty and advanced engineering.

Lucky's eyes widened, a spark of scientific curiosity, a familiar, comforting fire, igniting within him. "An oceanic current manipulator?" he breathed, a genuine awe in his voice. "That's... revolutionary. The energy requirements alone would be astronomical. How do you power such a thing?"
