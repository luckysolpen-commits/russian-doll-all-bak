Dorchester stepped forward, bowing respectfully, a gesture that surprised Lucky with its sincerity, a rare display of deference from the usually irreverent pirate. "Honored elders, General," she began, her voice clear and strong, resonating with a newfound gravitas. "I bring Lucky, the one the UN seeks to frame, and his companions. They have witnessed the UN's true intentions, their monstrous plans." Her words hung in the air, heavy with the weight of truth, a stark accusation against the global power.

The New-Korea General's eyes narrowed as he studied Lucky, a flicker of recognition, almost a spark of hope, in their depths. "We have heard of you, Lucky," he stated, his voice deep and resonant, like the hum of a powerful engine. "Your reputation precedes you. Your genius, your compassion, your unwavering dedication to humanity. And your recent... escape... confirms our suspicions about the UN's aggression, their insatiable hunger for power." His gaze was intense, assessing, but not hostile, a silent invitation to an alliance.

The Mer-Elder, with a gentle, almost imperceptible movement of his hand, silenced the murmuring that had begun to ripple through the chamber. His voice, a deep, resonant hum that seemed to vibrate through the water itself, spoke next. "The UN seeks to eradicate us, to pave the way for their dominion. They call us a threat, a mutation, an abomination. But we are simply... different. We are the ancient ones, the guardians of the deep. And we have allies, those who believe in peace, in coexistence." His gaze, ancient and unwavering, met Lucky's, a silent plea for understanding and cooperation, a desperate hope for a future where all sentient beings could live in harmony.

Dorchester turned to Lucky, her face serious, all traces of her usual mischievous glare gone, replaced by a grim, determined resolve. "I was once part of the UN's genetic engineering program," she confessed, her voice low, almost a whisper, a painful admission of a past she had tried to bury. "I saw the atrocities they planned, the genocidal intent behind their 'humanitarian' efforts. I saw the experiments, the suffering, the cold, calculating cruelty. I defected, and I've been working to expose them ever since. The merpeople are not a threat. They are a peaceful, advanced civilization. The UN wants to destroy them to seize their resources and eliminate any opposition to their global control." Her words were a revelation, a missing piece of the puzzle that finally explained Dorchester's motivations, her deep-seated hatred for the UN, her willingness to risk everything.
