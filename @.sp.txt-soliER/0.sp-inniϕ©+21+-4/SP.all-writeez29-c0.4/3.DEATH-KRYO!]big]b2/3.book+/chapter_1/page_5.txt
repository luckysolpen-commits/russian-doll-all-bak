**Page 5**

The UN agents remained unperturbed by the chaos they had unleashed. Their movements were economical, deliberate, their focus unwavering. Their heads, encased in those terrifying, featureless helmets, swiveled in perfect unison, their sensors, or perhaps their eyes, locked onto a single target. They ignored the fleeing, screaming masses, their purpose clear and chillingly specific.

One of the agents, moving with a deliberate, almost ritualistic slowness, raised a weapon. It wasn't a standard firearm. It was a sleek, futuristic piece of hardware, its barrel long and thin, designed for silent, efficient kills. A single, almost imperceptible *thwip* echoed through the sudden stillness, a sound so quiet it was almost swallowed by the screams of the crowd. It was the sound of a life being extinguished.

Queen Sophia's daughter, caught mid-twirl, her laughter still lingering in the air like a ghostly echo, stumbled. Her dance, so full of life and joy, came to an abrupt, clumsy halt. A look of profound shock, of utter disbelief, bloomed on her young, innocent face. Her hand went to her chest, her fingers brushing against the tiny, almost invisible dart embedded there. Her eyes, wide and questioning, found her mother's for a fleeting, heartbreaking moment. Then, with a soft, almost imperceptible sigh, she crumpled to the floor, a vibrant, beautiful life extinguished in an instant. She was dead before she even hit the ground.

The agents, their grim task seemingly complete, began to move again. Not towards the fleeing crowd, not towards the wailing, heartbroken Queen, but directly towards Lucky. One of them, the same one who had fired the fatal shot, extended a gloved finger, pointing directly at him through the gloom. The gesture was an accusation, a death sentence.

"Lucky is guilty," a synthesized voice, devoid of all emotion, boomed through the sudden silence. The voice was cold, metallic, inhuman. It cut through the chaos, the accusation hanging in the air, heavy and damning, a poison that spread through the room, infecting every heart with suspicion and fear.

As quickly and silently as they had appeared, the agents vanished, melting back into the shadows from which they had emerged, leaving behind only the chilling echo of their words and the shattered remnants of the celebration. The emergency lights, a harsh, sterile, unforgiving white, flickered on, illuminating the scene with a brutal, clinical clarity. The opulent nightclub, moments ago a beacon of joy and hope, was now a tableau of tragedy, chaos, and accusation.

Lucky stood frozen, a statue of disbelief, the synthesized voice still ringing in his ears. The eyes of every person remaining in the room, filled with a toxic mixture of fear, grief, and a dawning, terrible accusation, were fixed on him. He was no longer the hero, the savior. In the blink of an eye, he had become a suspect, a murderer. The weight of their gazes pressed down on him, a suffocating shroud of condemnation.
