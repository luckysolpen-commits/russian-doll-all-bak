Then, it happened. Without warning, without the slightest flicker of a prelude, the music died. The pulsating beat, the vibrant, life-affirming melody, vanished, ripped from the air with a brutal finality. The silence that crashed down in its place was a physical thing, a heavy, suffocating blanket that smothered the laughter and the chatter. It was a profound, jarring void, made all the more terrifying by the sudden absence of the rhythm that had united them all.

The lights, which had been bathing the club in a kaleidoscope of vibrant, shifting colors, flickered once, twice, then extinguished. The opulent space was plunged into a near-total darkness, a disorienting blackness that was absolute. The only illumination came from the faint, ghostly glow of the bioluminescent plants scattered throughout the club, their soft, ethereal light casting long, dancing shadows that seemed to writhe and twist with an unseen menace. The familiar, beautiful space was transformed into a nightmarish landscape of shifting shapes and unseen threats.

A collective gasp, a single, drawn-out exhalation of shock and disbelief, rippled through the crowd. It was a sound of a thousand people's joy turning to ice in their veins. The silence that followed was even heavier than before, pregnant with a rising, unspoken dread. Every eye strained, trying to pierce the oppressive gloom, trying to make sense of the sudden, terrifying shift in reality. The air grew cold, the earlier warmth replaced by a chilling premonition of violence.

From the deepest shadows at the far end of the club, five figures emerged. They didn't walk; they flowed, moving with an unnerving, synchronized precision that spoke of years of brutal training. They were clad in sleek, black tactical gear that seemed to absorb the faint light, making them appear as holes in the darkness. Their faces were completely obscured by featureless, matte-black helmets, their anonymity making them all the more terrifying. UN Counter-Force Agents. The very organization that had just acquired Lucky's company, the supposed guardians of this new world order, now moved with the chilling, silent efficiency of predators.

Panic, raw and primal, finally erupted. The crowd, which moments ago had been a unified mass of celebration, dissolved into a chaotic, screaming scramble. The carefully constructed illusion of safety shattered into a million pieces. Bodies collided in the darkness, desperate hands clawed at the air, and the air was filled with the sounds of terror and confusion. Each person was driven by the singular, animalistic instinct to flee, to escape the unseen threat that had so suddenly and violently descended upon them. The dream had become a nightmare.
