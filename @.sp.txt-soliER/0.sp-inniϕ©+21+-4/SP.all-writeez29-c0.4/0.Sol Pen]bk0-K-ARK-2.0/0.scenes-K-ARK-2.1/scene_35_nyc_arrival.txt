EXT. NEW YORK CITY OUTSKIRTS - DAY

SOUND of the courier ship landing softly, distant city noise, the hum of emergency vehicles

The Martian courier ship touches down on a stretch of abandoned highway on the outskirts of New York City. The landing is smooth, controlled — Gem's piloting, steady even after her infection.

Lucky, Astra, Gem, and Lauren step out. Their Light Exo Suits are active, their SolPens holstered, their Type C blood vials secured. They look like soldiers returning from a war that hasn't officially started.

Waiting for them is a delegation from the WORLD HEALTH ORGANIZATION (WHO). Three officials in hazmat suits, flanked by a small security detail. The lead official — DR. PATEL (50s, Indian, exhausted but sharp) — steps forward.

<center>DR. PATEL</center>
> Lucky Shepherd? We received your transmission from Mars orbit.

<center>LUCKY</center>
> (removing his helmet, his lavender mohawk springing free)
> That was fast. I didn't think anyone was still answering the phone.

<center>DR. PATEL</center>
> We're still functioning. Barely. The Clean Zones have their own operations, but the rest of us... we're improvising.

He gestures for them to follow. They walk to a mobile command center — a converted truck filled with monitors, medical equipment, and a holographic display of the city.

<center>ASTRA</center>
> What's the situation?

<center>DR. PATEL</center>
> The clowns control most of the city south of Central Park. They're not organized — they're chaotic. But the chaos is the weapon. It's creating conditions for the Carcazods to spread.

He activates the holographic display. New York appears, overlaid with data. Red zones everywhere.

<center>DR. PATEL</center>
> The Carcazods are in the infrastructure. Water treatment plants. Food distribution centers. Subway tunnels. They're not attacking — they're embedding. Growing. Waiting.

<center>GEM</center>
> How many?

<center>DR. PATEL</center>
> Thousands. Maybe tens of thousands. We can't track them all. They're too small, too patient, too... alien.

Lucky studies the display. His HALO HUD is cross-referencing the WHO data with his own SolPen intelligence. The picture is worse than he thought.

<center>LUCKY</center>
> The clowns are the distraction. The Carcazods are the invasion. We've been fighting the wrong enemy.

<center>DR. PATEL</center>
> What do we do?

<center>LUCKY</center>
> We fight both.

He looks at Astra. At Gem. At Lauren. They nod.

<center>LUCKY</center>
> Dr. Patel, I need access to everything. Your data, your facilities, your people. I have technology that can help — but I need to understand the full scope of the infiltration.

<center>DR. PATEL</center>
> You have it. We've been fighting in the dark. Maybe it's time we had some light.

Lucky activates his SolPen. The tip glows. The WHO team stares, awestruck.

<center>LUCKY</center>
> Let's get to work.

FADE OUT.
