INT. CLEAN ZONE "000010001000" - UNDERGROUND ACCESS TUNNEL - NIGHT

SOUND of dripping water, the hum of energy barriers above, careful footsteps

Lucky, Astra, Gem, and Lauren move through a maintenance tunnel beneath the Clean Zone. Lucky's SolPen provides light — a soft, focused beam that reveals walls of reinforced concrete and SolPen-constructed barriers.

<center>LUCKY</center>
> (whispering, his mohawk dark navy for stealth)
> The tunnel connects to the diamond mine's lower levels. Astra found the schematics in the WHO database. The mine was here before the Clean Zone — Lucky's future self built the zone AROUND it.

<center>ASTRA</center>
> (checking her HUD)
> Security is heavy on the upper levels. But the lower levels — the mine itself — are minimally guarded. He doesn't expect anyone to come from below.

<center>GEM</center>
> He's arrogant. Or he trusts himself.

<center>LUCKY</center>
> Same thing, apparently.

They move deeper. The tunnel opens into a vast underground space — the DIAMOND MINE. It's enormous, stretching for kilometers, its walls glittering with uncut diamonds. Conveyor systems, processing equipment, and storage facilities fill the space.

And everywhere — STASHES. Gold bars stacked in SolPen-constructed vaults. Weapons crates — advanced energy rifles, SolPen ammunition, Exo Suit components. Enough resources to equip a small nation.

<center>LAUREN</center>
> (staring)
> This is... this is an empire. Hidden underground while the world burns above.

<center>LUCKY</center>
> He's not just preparing for the Silent Shadow. He's preparing for EVERYTHING. Every possible future. Every possible threat.

They move through the mine, avoiding the occasional GUARD CLONE on patrol. Lucky's SolPen creates small distractions — a noise here, a light there — guiding them deeper.

They reach the central chamber. A massive office carved into the diamond rock. And there, at a desk made of polished diamond, sits FAKE LUCKY — Future Sol — reviewing holographic displays.

He's alone. No guards. No weapons. Just him and his plans.

<center>FAKE LUCKY</center>
> (without looking up)
> I knew you'd come this way. The underground. The unexpected. It's what I would do. What I DID do. When I was you.

Lucky steps into the light. His navy mohawk catches the diamond glow.

<center>LUCKY</center>
> Then you know why I'm here.

<center>FAKE LUCKY</center>
> (finally looking up)
> You want answers. You want to know why I manipulated you. Why I trained you. Why I abandoned you on Mars.

<center>LUCKY</center>
> I want to know if any of it was MY choice. Or if I was just following a script you wrote centuries ago.

Future Sol stands. He removes his mask — and Lucky sees his own face. Older. Harder. The face of someone who has lived through everything Lucky is about to face.

<center>FAKE LUCKY</center>
> Every choice you made was yours. I didn't control you. I created CONDITIONS. I brought you to the Moon. I gave you the SolPen. I showed you the threat. But every decision after that — every action, every alliance, every moment of courage and doubt — that was YOU.

<center>ASTRA</center>
> (from behind Lucky)
> Why the hoarding? The gold? The guns?

<center>FAKE LUCKY</center>
> Because the Carcazods are the first wave. The clowns are the second. The Silent Shadow is the third. And after that... there will be more. Humanity needs resources to survive what's coming. Gold for trade. Weapons for defense. Technology for creation. I'm not hoarding. I'm STOCKPILING. For the war that never ends.

<center>LUCKY</center>
> And Gem? The Gem inside?

<center>FAKE LUCKY</center>
> She chose to stay. She understood the plan. She saw the bigger picture.

The real Gem steps forward, her expression conflicted.

<center>GEM</center>
> I'm here now. With Lucky. Not you.

Future Sol nods. He expected this.

<center>FAKE LUCKY</center>
> I know. And I don't blame you. I was Lucky once too. Before I became Sol. Before I understood what was necessary.

He walks to the edge of the diamond chamber, looking out over the mine, the stashes, the empire he's built.

<center>FAKE LUCKY</center>
> I've lived this moment a thousand times in my head. Every version ends the same. You have to take my place, Lucky. You have to become Sol. You have to make the hard choices. The choices no one else can make.

<center>LUCKY</center>
> (quietly)
> What if I don't want to?

<center>FAKE LUCKY</center>
> Then humanity dies. The Carcazods win. The Silent Shadow consumes everything. And the universe forgets we ever existed.

Silence. The diamond mine glitters around them — beautiful, cold, indifferent.

Lucky looks at Astra. At Gem. At Lauren.

He looks at Future Sol — at himself, from the future, carrying the weight of everything that's coming.

<center>LUCKY</center>
> I'll make my own choices. Not yours. Not Sol's. Mine.

<center>FAKE LUCKY</center>
> (smiling)
> That's exactly what I said.

FADE OUT.
