INT. SSOL - RECREATION/LIVING QUARTERS - DAY

SOUND of low chatter, the clink of containers, a strange mix of military barracks and underground lounge

The recreation quarters of the SSOL are a sprawling, multi-level space carved into the ship's hull. Clone soldiers lounge on modular seating, some playing games with holographic pieces, others simply resting. The atmosphere is surprisingly casual — these are soldiers, but they're also a community.

Lucky enters, his gold chains catching the warm amber lighting. Today his mohawk is dyed a fiery crimson, matching the red silk vest he wears over his Exo Suit's chest plate. He carries himself with the swagger of someone who just realized he owns the room.

Astra follows, holding a small metallic vial that glows faintly red.

<center>ASTRA</center>
> Lucky. You need to see this.

She hands him the vial. Inside, a thick crimson liquid swirls on its own, as if alive.

<center>LUCKY</center>
> What am I looking at?

<center>ASTRA</center>
> Type C blood. I got a sample from the ventilation condensers. Sol was right — it's oxygen-infused at levels that should be toxic. But it's not just oxygen. There are nutrients, amino acids, trace elements. It's food, medicine, and a respiratory aid all in one.

A CLONE SOLDIER approaches, carrying a tray of identical vials. He offers one to Lucky with a practiced gesture.

<center>CLONE SOLDIER</center>
> Daily ration, sir. You'll feel the difference within minutes.

Lucky takes the vial, eyeing it suspiciously.

<center>LUCKY</center>
> I'm supposed to drink this?

<center>CLONE SOLDIER</center>
> Everyone does, sir. On Mars, it's how you breathe outside. How you work outside. Without it, the thin atmosphere would kill you in hours. With it, you can walk the surface for half a day. It's also how we pay for everything. One vial equals one credit. Ten vials equals a meal. A hundred vials equals a week's lodging. It's everything.

The Clone Soldier moves on, distributing vials to other clones in the room. Lucky watches as they crack the seals and drink without hesitation. Some savor it. Some gulp it. All of them seem energized by it.

Astra has already opened her vial and taken a measured sip. Her eyes widen.

<center>ASTRA</center>
> (breathing deeper)
> Oh. Oh, that's... I can feel it hitting my bloodstream. My heart rate just dropped but I'm more alert. This is incredible bioengineering.

<center>LUCKY</center>
> You just drink random alien blood products now?

<center>ASTRA</center>
> It's not alien, Lucky. It's synthetic. And if Sol's people can engineer this, imagine what else they can do.

Lucky looks at the vial in his hand. The crimson liquid seems to pulse with its own light. He cracks the seal. The smell is coppery, sweet, almost intoxicating.

He drinks.

The effect is immediate. A rush of clarity. His senses sharpen. The ambient noise of the room separates into distinct conversations, distinct sounds. He can hear the hum of the ship's systems through the walls. He can see the individual pixels in the holographic displays across the room.

<center>LUCKY</center>
> (exhaling)
> Okay. That's... that's something.

He moves through the room, observing the clones more carefully now. They're not just soldiers — they have a social structure. Some clones are clearly higher-ranking, wearing subtle insignia on their uniforms. Others are laborers, technicians, support staff. They interact with a familiarity that suggests years of shared existence.

A group of clones are gathered around a holographic table, studying what appears to be a map of the Martian surface. Lucky approaches.

<center>LUCKY</center>
> What am I looking at?

One of the clones — this one with a silver band around his collar — looks up.

<center>GUARD 1</center>
> Mars colony layout, sir. The rebel zones are marked in red. We're planning rotation schedules for the next deployment.

<center>LUCKY</center>
> Rebel zones? What kind of rebels?

<center>GUARD 1</center>
> Clone defectors, sir. Ones who've gone rogue. Some of them are... changing. Becoming something else. The Black Queen's influence spreads.

Lucky exchanges a look with Astra. This is new information.

<center>ASTRA</center>
> The Black Queen. Who is she?

The Silver-Band Clone hesitates, glancing around as if checking for eavesdroppers.

<center>GUARD 1</center>
> You'll learn soon enough, ma'am. Sol will brief you. But between us? She's not just a rebel leader. She's something older. Something that was here before Sol built this place.

As Lucky processes this, his eyes drift to the far corner of the recreation quarters. The lighting is dimmer there, shadows pooling around a structural column.

Something moves in those shadows.

Transparent. Shifting. A shape that's almost humanoid but wrong — elongated, angular, with a suggestion of scales or plates beneath the surface. It's there for less than a second, then it's gone, slipping into a seam in the wall that Lucky could have sworn was solid metal.

<center>LUCKY</center>
> (pointing)
> Did you see that?

Astra turns. The Silver-Band Clone turns. Other clones glance over.

Nothing. Just shadows and metal.

<center>ASTRA</center>
> See what?

Lucky stares at the spot. His enhanced senses from the Type C blood pick up something faint — a scent like ozone and old stone. Then even that fades.

<center>LUCKY</center>
> (quietly, to himself)
> Something's living on this ship that nobody's talking about.

<center>ASTRA</center>
> What?

<center>LUCKY</center>
> (forcing a smile, adjusting his crimson mohawk)
> I said this blood stuff is hitting different. Let's keep moving.

They move on, but Lucky glances back once more at the shadows.

The shadows don't glance back. But they seem to be watching.

FADE OUT.
