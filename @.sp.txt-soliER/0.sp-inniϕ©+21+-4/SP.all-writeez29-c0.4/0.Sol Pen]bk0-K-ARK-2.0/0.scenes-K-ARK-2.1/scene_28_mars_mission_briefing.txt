INT. SSOL - EN ROUTE TO MARS - DAY

SOUND of the SSOL's engines, a low, powerful hum

Lucky, Astra, and Gem are in the SSOL's briefing room. Lucky's mohawk is a tactical matte black today, but the gold chains still glint under the holographic light. He cracks a vial of Type C blood, shares a nod with Gem, and gets to work. A holographic map of the Martian colony hangs in the air between them. Gem, no longer hiding her role as a SOL agent, is all business.

<center>GEM</center>
> The Martian colony has been compromised by a rebel faction led by the Black Queen. Our objective is to neutralize her and restore order. The colonists are to be considered hostile.

<center>LUCKY</center>
> (Scoffs)
> Just like that? We're judge, jury, and executioner?

<center>GEM</center>
> We are the authority in this sector, Lucky. And the Black Queen is a direct threat to that authority.

Astra studies the holographic map, her expression grim.

<center>ASTRA</center>
> Their defenses are formidable. They've repurposed the mining equipment into makeshift weapons. A frontal assault would be suicide.

<center>LUCKY</center>
> So we don't do a frontal assault.

He steps up to the map, his mind already working, the SolPen in his hand. He begins to draw, sketching out a plan, a series of diversions and feints.

<center>LUCKY</center>
> We use the terrain to our advantage. We create a diversion here, draw their forces out, and then we hit them where they're weakest.

Gem watches, a flicker of admiration in her eyes. Lucky may be a reluctant soldier, but he's a brilliant strategist.

<center>GEM</center>
> A bold plan. But it might just work.

<center>LUCKY</center>
> It will work. Because they won't see it coming.

He looks at Astra, a silent question in his eyes. She nods, her faith in him unwavering.

<center>ASTRA</center>
> I'm with you, Lucky. Always.

Lucky turns back to the map, his resolve hardened. The doubts about Sol still linger, but for now, he has a mission. And he won't fail.

FADE OUT.
