INT. LUCKY'S RANCH - LATER

SOUND of a sleek, powerful car approaching, then silence

Hours later. The ranch house is quiet, but the tension from Loona's visit still hangs heavy in the air. Lucky and Astra are in the living room, trying to process the cryptic warning. Lucky has re-styled his mohawk — now standing tall again, a defiant burgundy spike against the weight of the evening. Gem is nowhere to be seen.

Suddenly, a sharp, authoritative KNOCK at the front door. Not a friendly knock. A demand.

Lucky and Astra exchange a wary glance. Lucky moves to the door, Astra close behind him.

He opens it to reveal AGENT THOMPSON (40s), impeccably dressed in a sharp suit, her expression unreadable, her eyes cold and calculating. Two equally stern-faced AGENTS stand behind her.

<center>THOMPSON</center>
> Lucky Shepherd, I presume?

<center>LUCKY</center>
> And you are?

<center>THOMPSON</center>
> Agent Thompson, CIA. We need to talk. Privately.

She glances pointedly at Astra.

<center>ASTRA</center>
> Anything you have to say to Lucky, you can say to me.

Thompson's gaze hardens.

<center>THOMPSON</center>
> I assure you, what I have to say is for Mr. Shepherd's ears only. Unless you'd prefer we discuss it in a more... public forum?

Her tone is a veiled threat. Lucky, sensing the gravity of the situation, nods to Astra.

<center>LUCKY</center>
> It's alright, Astra. I'll handle this.

Astra hesitates, then reluctantly retreats, but stays within earshot, her concern palpable.

Thompson steps inside, her eyes sweeping over the comfortable, unassuming living room, a hint of disdain in her gaze. She pulls out a tablet, swiping through files.

<center>THOMPSON</center>
> Let's not waste time, Mr. Shepherd. We know about your... extracurricular activities. The offshore accounts. The shell corporations. The unsanctioned tech development. Even the little incident in Monaco with the data heist. Impressive, really. For a civilian.

Lucky's face remains impassive, but a flicker of surprise crosses his eyes. She knows more than he thought.

<center>LUCKY</center>
> I don't know what you're talking about.

Thompson smiles, a humorless, predatory baring of teeth. She turns the tablet to him, displaying a series of damning documents and photographs – irrefutable evidence of his past.

<center>THOMPSON</center>
> Oh, I think you do. These are your files, Lucky. The ones you thought were buried deep. Lost forever. But nothing is truly lost to us.

Lucky's jaw tightens. He recognizes the files. The secrets he'd worked so hard to erase. The leverage she holds is immense.

<center>LUCKY</center>
> This is blackmail.

<center>THOMPSON</center>
> Call it what you will. We call it insurance. You're a very special individual, Lucky. And we believe you can be of great service to us. We have a mission that requires your unique skills and expertise.

She leans in, her voice dropping to a conspiratorial whisper.

<center>THOMPSON</center>
> A group of terrorists has gotten their hands on some... sensitive equipment. We need someone to infiltrate their organization and steal it back for us. Someone with your particular set of talents. Someone who can operate outside the usual channels.

Lucky stares at her, trapped. His peaceful life, his carefully constructed retirement, is shattered. He has no choice.

<center>LUCKY</center>
> What do you want me to do?

Thompson's smile widens, a chilling triumph.

<center>THOMPSON</center>
> Excellent. I knew you'd see reason. We'll provide you with the details. You leave tomorrow.

She turns, her agents already moving towards the door. Lucky watches them go, the weight of his past, and his forced future, settling heavily on his shoulders.

FADE OUT.
