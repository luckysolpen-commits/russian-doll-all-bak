INT. SSOL - BRIDGE/COMMAND CENTER - DAY

SOUND of a deep, resonant hum, the whisper of holographic interfaces, a sense of vast controlled power

The bridge of the SSOL is unlike anything Lucky has ever seen. It stretches across a space the size of a football field, yet feels intimate, alive. The walls are seamless, organic curves of dark metal that seem to breathe with a faint bioluminescent pulse. No buttons, no screens — just floating holographic displays that respond to thought.

LUCKY SHEPHERD strides in, his Light Exo Suit catching the ambient glow. He's wearing a deep purple silk shirt under the armor plating, gold chains draped across his chest, and his mohawk — today a vibrant electric blue — stands tall like a crown. Even in a crisis, Lucky dresses like a rich rapper who owns the galaxy.

ASTRA follows, her eyes wide, taking in every detail with scientific hunger.

SOL stands at the center of the bridge, his voice-modulating mask reflecting the holographic light. His thin exo suit shimmers with an almost liquid quality.

<center>SOL</center>
> Welcome to the nerve center of the SSOL. The Sovereign Ship of Orbital Light.

<center>LUCKY</center>
> (spinning slowly, taking it all in)
> This isn't a ship. This is a cathedral.

Sol gestures, and the holographic displays expand, filling the air with a three-dimensional map of the solar system. Planets rotate in miniature, their orbits traced in golden light.

<center>SOL</center>
> The SSOL is a mobile command center. A self-sustaining ecosystem. It generates its own atmosphere, recycles all matter at the molecular level, and draws energy from quantum vacuum fluctuations. It does not need to refuel. It does not need to land. It can remain operational for ten thousand years.

Astra approaches one of the holographic interfaces, her fingers passing through streams of data.

<center>ASTRA</center>
> The energy output alone... this should be impossible. Where does the power come from?

<center>SOL</center>
> The SolPen network. Every clone on this ship, every construct, every system — they are all connected. The collective consciousness of the clones doesn't just power the Pen. It powers everything.

Lucky stares at the holographic Earth, rotating slowly before him. He can see the continents, the clouds, the lights of cities on the dark side.

<center>LUCKY</center>
> You built all of this... in secret. While I was playing casino mogul, you were building a god-ship inside the Moon.

<center>SOL</center>
> While you were building an empire of information, I was building a fortress of necessity. Your work was not wasted, Lucky. The data networks you created, the surveillance infrastructure — they gave me eyes everywhere. I knew when the Carcazods would come. I knew when the clowns would rise. I knew when YOU would be ready.

Lucky's blue mohawk seems to catch the holographic light as he turns to face Sol directly.

<center>LUCKY</center>
> Ready for what, exactly?

Sol's masked face tilts. The holographic map shifts, zooming in on Mars. The red planet rotates, its surface dotted with hundreds of tiny lights — colonies, installations, something moving beneath the surface.

<center>SOL</center>
> Mars holds the key to everything. The colonies there are not what Earth believes them to be. They are a battleground. And you, Lucky Shepherd, are going to take the field.

Astra looks up from the holographic interface, her expression troubled.

<center>ASTRA</center>
> Sol... the life support systems on this ship. I'm reading something unusual in the atmospheric composition. There's a compound I don't recognize. It's in the air we're breathing right now.

<center>SOL</center>
> Type C blood. Vaporized, distributed through the ventilation system. You've been breathing it since you boarded.

<center>LUCKY</center>
> Type C what now?

<center>SOL</center>
> Oxygen-infused synthetic blood. It increases oxygen saturation in the bloodstream by four hundred percent. It allows humans to survive in vacuum for up to twelve minutes without a suit. It is also the currency, the nutrition, and the lifeblood of Mars. Everyone there drinks it. Everyone there depends on it. And soon, you will too.

Lucky looks at his hands, flexing his fingers. He feels different — sharper, more alert. He hadn't noticed until now, but the air tastes... metallic. Alive.

<center>LUCKY</center>
> You've been drugging us since we got here.

<center>SOL</center>
> Enhancing you. There is a difference.

A low CHIME echoes through the bridge. One of the holographic displays pulses red.

<center>SOL</center>
> (turning to the display)
> We are approaching the SSOL's primary docking orbit. Your advanced training begins now.

The ship shudders slightly — not from turbulence, but from something deeper. A shift in reality itself. Lucky feels it in his bones.

He looks out through the bridge's forward viewport. The Moon fills the view — cratered, ancient, hiding secrets beneath its surface.

And for just a fraction of a second, Lucky sees something in the reflection of the glass. Something transparent. Something reptilian. Standing in the shadows behind one of the bridge's support columns.

He turns. Nothing there.

<center>ASTRA</center>
> (noticing his reaction)
> What is it?

<center>LUCKY</center>
> (shaking his head)
> Nothing. Just... the light playing tricks.

But he's not sure he believes that.

FADE OUT.
