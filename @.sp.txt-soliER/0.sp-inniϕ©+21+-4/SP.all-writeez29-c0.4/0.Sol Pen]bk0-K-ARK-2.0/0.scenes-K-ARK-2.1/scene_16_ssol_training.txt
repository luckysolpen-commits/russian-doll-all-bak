INT. SSOL - TRAINING SIMULATION ROOM - DAY

SOUND of crackling energy, the snap of digital matter forming and dissolving, heavy breathing

The training simulation room is a vast, white void — a holographic canvas that can become anything. Today, it's set to a ruined cityscape: crumbling buildings, twisted metal, a sky choked with digital ash.

LUCKY stands at one end of the simulation, SolPen in hand. His mohawk is blazing gold today, matching the gold-plated SolPen grip he's customized himself. His Exo Suit is fully engaged, humming with power. He looks like a warrior-king who walked out of a music video.

SOL stands fifty meters away, his own SolPen raised. His mask reflects the ruined city around them. His exo suit is darker, more refined — the original template that Lucky's is based on.

LOONA TWILIGHT watches from an observation platform above, arms crossed, expression unreadable.

<center>SOL</center>
> Today you stop building objects, Lucky. Today you build strategy.

<center>LUCKY</center>
> (grinning, spinning the SolPen)
> I've been building strategy my whole life. You just gave me a better pen.

<center>SOL</center>
> We will see. The simulation will generate Carcazod threats. You will counter them. I will oppose you. This is not a test. This is a duel.

<center>LUCKY</center>
> A duel? You and me?

<center>SOL</center>
> You need to understand what you're fighting against. And you need to understand what I can do. Only then will you be ready for Mars.

Loona activates the simulation from above.

<center>LOONA</center>
> Simulation active. Carcazod wave one. Three... two... one... ENGAGE.

The ruined city SHUDDERS. From the digital ash, CARCAZODS materialize — grotesque, shifting forms of corrupted matter, part machine, part organism, all hunger. They move with unnatural speed, skittering across walls, dropping from buildings.

Lucky reacts instantly. His SolPen flashes. He draws a line in the air and a WALL OF LIGHT erupts, vaporizing the first wave. He pivots, creates a SWARM OF DRONES that dive into the Carcazod mass, exploding in bursts of white fire.

Sol doesn't move. He raises his SolPen and the Carcazods simply STOP — frozen in mid-leap, suspended in a field of digital stasis. Then he flicks his wrist and they UNRAVEL, their matter dissolving into harmless particles.

Lucky stares.

<center>LUCKY</center>
> Show-off.

<center>SOL</center>
> Your turn. Wave two.

More Carcazods. Bigger. These ones are ARMORED, their forms shifting between solid and liquid. Lucky's light wall shatters against them. He adapts — creates a GRAVITY WELL that pulls three of them into a crushing sphere of compressed force. They scream — a sound that shouldn't be possible from digital constructs.

Sol attacks from the flank. His SolPen creates a LANCE OF PURE VOID — a beam of nothingness that erases everything it touches. It's aimed past Lucky, obliterating a Carcazod that was about to flank him.

<center>LUCKY</center>
> I had that one!

<center>SOL</center>
> You had a half-second delay. In combat, that's death. Again.

The simulation intensifies. Now the Carcazods are COORDINATED — flanking, feinting, using the environment. Lucky is pushed back, creating shields, weapons, barriers in rapid succession. His gold mohawk is slick with sweat. His Exo Suit's HUD is flashing warnings.

He stops thinking and starts FEELING.

The SolPen becomes an extension of his will. He creates a MIRROR FIELD that reflects the Carcazods' own attacks back at them. He spawns a BRIDGE OF HARD LIGHT beneath his feet, launching himself into the air, raining digital fire from above. He's improvising, creating, DESTROYING — and it's beautiful.

Sol watches, and for the first time, there's something like pride in his posture.

Then Sol joins the fight properly.

What follows is a DANCE OF CREATION AND DESTRUCTION. Lucky and Sol move through the simulation like twin storms. Lucky creates — Sol unmakes. Lucky builds a fortress — Sol dissolves its foundations. Lucky spawns an army of constructs — Sol erases them with a gesture.

They're not fighting each other directly, but the competition is fierce. Each one pushing the other, testing limits, demonstrating mastery.

Loona watches from above, her expression shifting from analytical to something approaching awe.

The simulation reaches its peak. The ruined city is gone, replaced by a CHAOTIC STORM of digital matter — Lucky's creations and Sol's deletions colliding in a spectacular display of raw power.

Then Sol raises his hand. Everything STOPS.

The Carcazods freeze. The digital storm hangs suspended. The ruined city reassembles itself in reverse.

Silence.

Lucky is breathing hard, his SolPen still glowing in his hand. His gold mohawk is half-collapsed from the exertion. He's grinning.

<center>LUCKY</center>
> (panting)
> Okay. That was... that was something.

<center>SOL</center>
> You have raw talent, Lucky. Your creativity exceeds any clone operator I've trained. But you lack discipline. You create without considering consequence. Every construct costs energy. Every deletion has a ripple effect. You must learn to fight with precision, not just power.

<center>LUCKY</center>
> (straightening, adjusting his ruined mohawk)
> I'll take that as a compliment wrapped in a critique.

<center>SOL</center>
> Take it as survival advice. On Mars, the Carcazods are not simulations. They are real. And they will not pause when you need a moment to think.

Loona descends from the observation platform, clapping slowly.

<center>LOONA</center>
> I've been running these simulations for three years. I've never seen anyone push Sol like that. Not even the elite clones.

<center>LUCKY</center>
> (bowing slightly)
> I've been told I'm exceptional.

<center>LOONA</center>
> Don't let it go to your head. You still have weeks of training before you're ready for Mars.

<center>SOL</center>
> Less than you think. The situation is accelerating. We don't have the luxury of time.

Sol turns, his mask reflecting the frozen simulation around them.

<center>SOL</center>
> Rest. Refuel with Type C. Tomorrow we begin tactical strategy. And Lucky?

<center>LUCKY</center>
> Yeah?

<center>SOL</center>
> Your mohawk color is distracting. It makes you a target.

<center>LUCKY</center>
> (touching his gold mohawk protectively)
> My mohawk stays. It's who I am.

<center>SOL</center>
> Then learn to make it a weapon too.

Sol exits the simulation room. The frozen constructs dissolve into white nothingness.

Lucky stands alone in the white void, his SolPen still warm in his hand. He looks at it — really looks at it — and for the first time, he understands what it truly is.

Not a tool. Not a weapon. An extension of his will. His creativity. His soul.

He smiles.

<center>LUCKY</center>
> (to himself)
> Mars ain't ready for me.

FADE OUT.
