INT. MARTIAN COLONY - RED QUEEN'S CASTLE - DAY

SOUND of echoing footsteps in stone corridors, distant crowd noise, the hum of energy barriers

The RED QUEEN'S CASTLE is not a medieval structure — it's a repurposed Martian mining fortress, its walls carved from red rock and reinforced with stolen SolPen constructs. Banners hang from the ceilings — crimson and gold, bearing the Red Queen's sigil.

LUCKY is brought in under guard — but not as a prisoner. More like a guest who doesn't know he's a guest yet. His mohawk is a defiant gold today, his Exo Suit polished, his chains gleaming. He walks with the confidence of someone who knows he could escape if he wanted to.

The RED QUEEN awaits him in her throne room. She's tall, regal, with dark skin and eyes that miss nothing. Her exo-suit is ornate but functional — red and gold, matching her banners. She is not the Black Queen. She is something else entirely.

<center>RED QUEEN</center>
> Lucky Shepherd. The man Sol sent to conquer Mars. And yet here you are, walking into my castle like you own it.

<center>LUCKY</center>
> I don't own it. Yet. But I'm willing to negotiate.

The Red Queen laughs — a rich, genuine sound.

<center>RED QUEEN</center>
> I like you. Sol didn't tell me you had humor. He only said you had talent.

<center>LUCKY</center>
> Sol says a lot of things. How much of it is true?

<center>RED QUEEN</center>
> (leaning forward)
> Enough. But here's what you need to understand: I'm not your enemy. The Black Queen is. The clowns are. And if we don't work together, Mars falls, and Earth is next.

<center>LUCKY</center>
> The clowns. Sol mentioned them. Punk rock clone rebels.

<center>RED QUEEN</center>
> They're not just rebels. They're a corruption. Clone soldiers who've been infected — turning into something chaotic, violent, absurd. They paint their faces. They wear leather and spikes. They scream anarchy while the Carcazods feed on the chaos they create. And they're spreading.

Lucky processes this. His HALO HUD flickers with data — he's scanning the castle, the guards, the Red Queen herself. Everything checks out.

<center>LUCKY</center>
> So what's your play?

<center>RED QUEEN</center>
> (standing)
> First, you see what we're fighting for. Then we fight.

She gestures. Two of her guards escort Lucky — still willing, still curious — through the castle and out into a vast underground arena.

---

INT. MARTIAN COLONY - UNDERGROUND ARENA - CONTINUOUS

SOUND of a roaring crowd, bouncing balls, the squeak of sneakers on polished floor

The arena has been converted into a BASKETBALL COURT. Full regulation size, holographic backboards, energy-lined boundaries. The stands are PACKED — clones, Martians, imported humans, all cheering, all drinking Type C blood from vials.

On the court, two teams face off.

Team One: AFRICANS — humans imported from Earth, specifically from various African nations. They're tall, athletic, moving with natural grace. Their uniforms are green and gold.

Team Two: CLONES — identical soldiers, moving with military precision. Their uniforms are white and silver.

<center>LUCKY</center>
> (staring)
> Is this... a basketball game?

<center>RED QUEEN</center>
> (smiling)
> Mars has many needs. Entertainment is one of them. Type C blood gives everyone enhanced reflexes. The games are... spectacular.

The tip-off is a blur of motion. The Type C blood-enhanced players move faster than any Earth basketball game. The Africans use creativity, improvisation, flair. The clones use precision, coordination, efficiency.

It's breathtaking.

Lucky watches, mesmerized. The crowd is electric. Even Astra and Gem, who've joined him in the stands, are caught up in the energy.

<center>ASTRA</center>
> This is incredible. The athleticism... the Type C blood is giving them superhuman reflexes.

<center>GEM</center>
> (quietly)
> The clones are holding back. They could win easily. But they don't want to upset the Africans. There's politics here.

The game reaches its climax. The Africans are ahead by two. The clones have the ball. Their point guard drives — but then something changes.

The clone players STOP. Their movements become jerky. Their faces... SHIFT. Paint appears on their skin — white base, red smiles, dark eyes. Their uniforms tear, revealing leather and spikes underneath.

They're turning into CLOWNS. Right on the court.

The crowd SCREAMS. Panic erupts.

<center>RED QUEEN</center>
> (drawing a weapon)
> NOW! They're infected!

The punk rock clowns — former clone players — pull weapons from nowhere. Energy blades. Chaotic, unpredictable attacks. They're fast, enhanced by Type C blood and something darker.

Lucky doesn't hesitate. His SolPen is in his hand. He creates a SHIELD around the civilian stands, protecting the crowd.

The Red Queen's guards engage the clowns. It's brutal, efficient. But the clowns are unpredictable — they fight with chaos, with absurdity, with a kind of manic energy that's hard to counter.

Lucky joins the fight. His SolPen creates constructs — energy nets, gravity wells, containment fields. He's creative, adaptive, overwhelming.

Astra and Gem fight alongside him. Gem's combat skills are extraordinary — she moves like a trained assassin, each strike precise, each movement lethal.

The clown clones are subdued one by one. The last one — a towering figure with a mohawk that rivals Lucky's, dyed electric purple — lunges at Lucky with an energy blade.

Lucky sidesteps. Creates a containment cube around the clown. The clown screams, pounds the walls, but the cube holds.

Silence falls over the arena. The crowd is shaken but alive.

The Red Queen surveys the damage. Her expression is grim.

<center>RED QUEEN</center>
> This is what we're fighting. The corruption spreads through the clone population. They turn into clowns — violent, chaotic, connected to the Carcazods. And we have to stop them.

<center>LUCKY</center>
> (looking at the contained clown)
> They were soldiers. Now they're... this.

<center>RED QUEEN</center>
> There's no cure. Not yet. The only option is containment. And for the ones too far gone...

She doesn't finish the sentence. She doesn't need to.

---

INT. MARTIAN COLONY - COURT PLAZA - LATER

SOUND of marching boots, the hum of energy barriers, a crowd gathered in tense silence

The plaza has been cleared. The remaining CLOWN REBELS — two dozen of them, in their punk rock regalia of leather, spikes, and face paint — are lined up before an energy barrier. They're contained, defeated, but defiant.

The crowd watches in silence. This is not a celebration. This is justice. Or something close to it.

The Red Queen stands before the clowns. Lucky, Astra, and Gem stand beside her.

<center>RED QUEEN</center>
> (to the clowns)
> You were soldiers. You were protectors. The corruption took you. And now there's no going back.

One of the clowns — a female with electric blue face paint and a spiked collar — spits on the ground.

<center>CLOWN REBEL</center>
> Anarchy forever! The Carcazods will consume everything! Sol's empire will FALL!

Lucky steps forward. His gold mohawk catches the light.

<center>LUCKY</center>
> You're not anarchy. You're infected. There's a difference.

<center>CLOWN REBEL</center>
> (laughing maniacally)
> You think you're saving anyone, pretty boy? You're just delaying the inevitable!

The Red Queen raises her hand. Her guards take position.

<center>RED QUEEN</center>
> For Mars. For the uncorrupted. For the future.

The guards fire. Energy blasts. Clean. Quick. The clown rebels fall.

The crowd is silent. Some look away. Others watch with grim determination.

Lucky watches. He doesn't look away.

When it's over, the plaza is empty except for Lucky, Astra, Gem, and the Red Queen.

<center>ASTRA</center>
> (quietly)
> That was... necessary. But it doesn't feel like victory.

<center>RED QUEEN</center>
> It's not victory. It's triage. We cut out the infection so the body can survive. Until we find a cure.

<center>LUCKY</center>
> (turning to the Red Queen)
> We will find a cure. And we'll stop the Carcazods. Both of us. Together.

The Red Queen extends her hand. Lucky takes it.

<center>RED QUEEN</center>
> Together.

But as they stand there, Lucky's HALO HUD flickers with an incoming transmission. It's from the SSOL.

Or it WAS from the SSOL. The signal is degrading. Cutting out.

<center>LUCKY</center>
> (to himself)
> Sol... what are you doing?

The signal dies completely.

Silence from the SSOL.

Silence from Sol.

FADE OUT.
