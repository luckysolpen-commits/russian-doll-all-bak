INT. LUCKY'S RANCH - NIGHT

SOUND of crickets, distant coyote howl, the soft hum of greenhouse grow lights, the whinny of a horse in the distance

The ranch house is bathed in the soft glow of interior lights. Cozy, lived-in, a perfect blend of rustic and high-tech. A holographic display on the wall shows the status of the ranch systems: crop yields stable, greenhouse humidity optimal, horse vitals normal, humanoid robots in charging mode.

Lucky and Astra are in the living room. Lucky is sprawled on a leather couch, his burgundy mohawk slightly flattened from leaning back. His gold chains glint in the lamplight. He's reading a physical book — rare for a man who built a digital empire. Astra is at a desk, reviewing data from the day's crop experiments on a tablet.

<center>ASTRA</center>
> The new cannabis strain is ready for testing. I'm calling it "Shepherd's Dream." High CBD, low THC. Medicinal without the psychoactive effects.

<center>LUCKY</center>
> (without looking up from his book)
> You named a weed after me. I'm honored.

<center>ASTRA</center>
> It's not a weed. It's a genetically optimized medicinal plant with a complex cannabinoid profile.

<center>LUCKY</center>
> It's a weed. And I love it.

Astra smiles, shaking her head. Through the window, the marijuana greenhouse is visible in the distance — a glowing rectangle of green light in the darkness. The bioluminescent crop fields pulse faintly blue, like stars fallen to earth.

A HUMANOID ROBOT passes by the window, doing its nightly perimeter check. Its LED eyes sweep the grounds with methodical precision.

Suddenly, a soft THUD from the front porch.

Lucky and Astra exchange a look.

<center>LUCKY</center>
> What was that?

<center>ASTRA</center>
> Probably just a squirrel. Or a very large, clumsy owl.

But there's a hint of unease in her voice. Lucky rises, cautiously approaches the front door. He opens it slowly, peering out into the darkness.

On the porch, nestled against the doorframe, is a MYSTERIOUS PACKAGE. It's wrapped in plain brown paper, tied with twine, no discernible markings or address.

<center>LUCKY</center>
> Well, that's new.

Astra joins him, her curiosity piqued. She kneels, examining the package.

<center>ASTRA</center>
> No sender. No return address. And definitely not from Amazon.

GEM (30s), a seemingly friendly and unassuming individual, emerges from the kitchen, wiping her hands on a dish towel. She's been staying with Lucky and Astra for a few days, a mutual friend visiting.

<center>GEM</center>
> What's all the fuss about? Oh, a package! Are we expecting something?

She eyes the bundle with a hint of skepticism, a subtle, almost imperceptible shift in her demeanor.

<center>LUCKY</center>
> Not that I know of.

Just then, a figure emerges from the shadows at the edge of the porch. LOONA TWILIGHT (30s), striking and self-assured, steps into the faint light. She carries herself with an air of quiet power.

<center>LOONA</center>
> I believe that's for you, Lucky.

Lucky and Astra turn, surprised. Gem's eyes narrow almost imperceptibly.

<center>LUCKY</center>
> Loona? What are you doing here? And what is this?

Loona offers a small, enigmatic smile. She holds up a sleek, futuristic DEVICE. It glows with a soft, internal light.

<center>LOONA</center>
> A delivery. And a warning.

She presses a button on the device. The mysterious package on the porch begins to GLOW with an ethereal, pulsing light. The air around it seems to hum.

In the distance, the humanoid robot on perimeter patrol pauses. Its LED eyes flicker, as if confused by the energy signature. One of the horses whinnies nervously.

Lucky and Astra stare, a mixture of awe and apprehension on their faces. Gem watches Loona intently, a flicker of something unreadable in her eyes.

The peaceful ranch — with its horses, its robots, its genetically perfect crops — suddenly feels very small and very exposed.

FADE OUT.
