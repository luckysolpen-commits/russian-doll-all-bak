INT. NEW YORK CITY - VARIOUS LOCATIONS - DAY/NIGHT

SOUND of carnival music, laughter, screams, the cacophony of a city losing its mind

A MONTAGE of Clown World:

* TIMES SQUARE — The billboards have been repainted with clown faces. Giant, grinning, grotesque. Clowns perform in the streets — juggling fire, walking on stilts, making balloon animals that twist into shapes no balloon should form. The city is a canvas of color and chaos. A never-ending carnival.

* SUBWAY STATION — A group of punk rock clowns have taken over a platform. Their faces are painted white with red smiles, their leather jackets covered in spikes and patches. They play music — loud, discordant, aggressive. Commuters walk past, heads down, pretending not to see. In the shadows behind them, something TRANSPARENT shifts. A Carcazod, embedding itself in the tunnel wall.

* FOOD DISTRIBUTION CENTER — People line up for rations. The line is long, tense. A clown stands at the front, handing out food with a manic grin. His eyes are wrong — too dark, too deep. He's infected. The Carcazod is inside him, using him as a host. No one notices. No one wants to.

* WATER TREATMENT PLANT — Inside the facility, the water flows clear and clean. But in the pipes, in the filtration systems, in the chemical tanks — Carcazods. Tiny, invisible, patient. They're in the water. Not poisoning it. CHANGING it. Slowly. Incrementally. Preparing the population for something.

* CENTRAL PARK SOUTH — The boundary between clown-controlled territory and the last free zone. A wall of sandbags and barricades. Armed guards. Beyond the wall, the carnival continues. Music. Laughter. Screams. The guards don't look. They can't afford to.

* LUCKY'S COMMAND POST — A converted warehouse. Lucky, Astra, Gem, and Lauren are surrounded by holographic displays, WHO data, and SolPen constructs. Lucky's mohawk is a furious crimson — the color of war.

<center>LUCKY</center>
> (studying the displays)
> They're everywhere. The clowns are the visible threat — the chaos, the violence, the absurdity. But the Carcazods are the real enemy. They're in the water, the food, the infrastructure. They're not attacking. They're PATIENT.

<center>ASTRA</center>
> How do you fight something that's already inside everything?

<center>LUCKY</center>
> You don't fight it. You root it out. System by system. Infrastructure by infrastructure. It's going to take time.

<center>GEM</center>
> We don't have time. The Carcazods are accelerating. The clowns are creating more chaos, and the chaos feeds the Carcazod growth.

<center>LAUREN</center>
> Then we hit the clowns first. Cut off the chaos. Starve the Carcazods.

Lucky looks at the displays. At the city. At the carnival of madness.

<center>LUCKY</center>
> We negotiate first. If the clowns want something, maybe we can give it to them. Maybe we can buy time.

<center>ASTRA</center>
> Lucky. They're clowns. They don't want anything rational.

<center>LUCKY</center>
> Everyone wants something. Even clowns.

The montage ends with Lucky walking out of the command post, into the carnival. Into the chaos. Into the heart of Clown World.

The clowns see him coming. They smile.

He doesn't smile back.

FADE OUT.
