INT. MARTIAN COLONY - OBSERVATION TOWER - DAY

SOUND of Martian wind, the quiet hum of failing communications equipment, the vast silence of abandonment

The observation tower overlooks the Martian colony. The rebellion is quelled — for now. The Red Queen's forces hold the territory. The clown rebels have been executed. But the victory is hollow.

Lucky and Astra stand at the observation window, looking out at the red landscape. Lucky's gold mohawk is dull in the Martian light. He's been trying to reach the SSOL for hours.

Nothing.

<center>LUCKY</center>
> (tapping his comms)
> SSOL, this is Lucky Shepherd. Come in. Sol, respond.

Static. The kind of static that means no one is listening. Not that the signal is bad.

<center>ASTRA</center>
> How long has it been?

<center>LUCKY</center>
> Six hours since the last transmission. Six hours of nothing.

GEM enters. She's been checking the communication arrays. Her expression tells them everything before she speaks.

<center>GEM</center>
> The SSOL has left lunar orbit.

<center>LUCKY</center>
> (turning slowly)
> What?

<center>GEM</center>
> It's gone. Sol cut all communications. The SSOL departed the Moon approximately four hours ago. Its trajectory is... unknown. It's not heading here. It's not heading anywhere we can track.

<center>ASTRA</center>
> He left us.

<center>GEM</center>
> (carefully)
> His mission is done.

<center>LUCKY</center>
> (voice rising)
> His mission is DONE? He dropped us on Mars, told us to fight a war, and then LEFT? He cut comms? He's just... gone?

<center>GEM</center>
> Sol operates on a timeline we don't fully understand. His presence here was necessary to train you, to prepare you. Now you're ready. He doesn't need to stay.

<center>LUCKY</center>
> He abandoned us.

<center>GEM</center>
> (meeting his eyes)
> He did.

The honesty hits Lucky like a physical blow. He turns back to the window, to the red expanse of Mars. His gold mohawk catches the light — defiant, absurd, alone.

<center>ASTRA</center>
> (softly)
> What do we do now?

Lucky is quiet for a long time. Then:

<center>LUCKY</center>
> We survive. We fight. We figure out what Sol was really doing. And we make sure he doesn't get to walk away from this.

He turns to Gem.

<center>LUCKY</center>
> You knew. You knew he was going to leave.

<center>GEM</center>
> I suspected. Sol doesn't share his full plans with anyone. Not even me.

<center>LUCKY</center>
> Then you're not as valuable as you thought.

Gem flinches. Just slightly. But Lucky sees it.

<center>ASTRA</center>
> Lucky. We need to focus. Mars is secured, barely. The clowns are contained, for now. But Earth... Earth is still falling.

Lucky nods. The anger is still there, but it's being channeled now. Directed.

<center>LUCKY</center>
> We need a ship. A Martian vessel. Something that can get us to Earth.

<center>RED QUEEN</center>
(enters from the stairwell)
> I have one. A fast courier. Armed, shielded, Type C blood reserves for the journey. It's yours.

<center>LUCKY</center>
> (surprised)
> You're helping us?

<center>RED QUEEN</center>
> Mars is my home. Earth is yours. If Earth falls, Mars is next. We're in this together now.

Lucky looks at her. Nods. A real alliance, forged in blood and necessity.

<center>LUCKY</center>
> Thank you.

<center>RED QUEEN</center>
> Don't thank me. Save your world.

---

EXT. SPACE - EARTH ORBIT - DAYS LATER

The Martian courier ship drops out of faster-than-light travel. Earth fills the viewport — blue, green, beautiful.

But something is wrong.

Lucky, Astra, and Gem stand at the observation window. Their HALO HUDs are painting the picture in real-time.

<center>ASTRA</center>
> The Clean Zones are holding. But outside them...

The holographic display shows it. Red dots. Thousands of them. Spreading across continents.

<center>GEM</center>
> Carcazods. They've infiltrated Earth's major cities. New York. London. Tokyo. São Paulo. They're not attacking — they're HIDING. Waiting. Growing.

<center>LUCKY</center>
> The clowns created the chaos. The Carcazods used it to slip through. They're inside the infrastructure. The water systems. The food supply. The population centers.

<center>ASTRA</center>
> They're not just on Earth. They're IN Earth.

Lucky stares at the display. The scope of the infiltration is staggering. The Carcazods didn't invade. They infiltrated. Patiently. Quietly. While everyone was watching the clowns.

<center>LUCKY</center>
> Sol knew. He knew this would happen. That's why he trained me. That's why he brought me here. Not to save Mars. To save Earth.

<center>GEM</center>
> And then he left. Because his part was done.

<center>LUCKY</center>
> (quietly)
> His part is never done. Not while I'm breathing.

The Martian courier descends toward Earth. Toward New York. Toward the heart of the infiltration.

As they enter the atmosphere, Lucky's HALO HUD picks up something else. A signal. Faint. Encrypted.

It's from Sol.

One word:

"BEGIN."

Lucky looks at Astra. At Gem. At the Earth below.

<center>LUCKY</center>
> Let's go to work.

---

EXT. NEW YORK CITY - STREET LEVEL - NIGHT

The streets are a carnival of chaos. Clowns everywhere — laughing, screaming, performing acts of absurd violence. The city is a canvas of color and madness.

But in the shadows, between the neon and the noise, something else moves.

TRANSPARENT. Shifting. Reptilian.

The same beings Lucky glimpsed on the Moon. On the SSOL. Now here. On Earth.

They've been here all along.

Watching. Waiting.

And now, as Lucky's ship touches down on the outskirts of the city, they turn their invisible eyes toward him.

The real war has begun.

FADE TO BLACK.
