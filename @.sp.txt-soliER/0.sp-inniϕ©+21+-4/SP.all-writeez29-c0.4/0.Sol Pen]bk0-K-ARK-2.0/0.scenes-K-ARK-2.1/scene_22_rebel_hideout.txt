INT. MARTIAN COLONY - REBEL HIDEOUT/BARRACKS - NIGHT

SOUND of muffled rebel chatter, distant clanking of machinery

Lucky, Astra, and Gem move like shadows through the dimly lit corridors of a rebel barracks. Lucky's mohawk — now a matte black for stealth operations — is pressed flat under his Exo Suit hood, but the diamond chains still clink softly against his armor. He cracks open a vial of Type C blood, takes a quick hit, and caps it. The oxygen rush sharpens his senses instantly. The air is thick with the smell of stale rations and unwashed bodies. This is a makeshift hideout, carved out of a repurposed mining complex.

<center>GEM</center>
> (Whispering into comms)
> Scanners indicate a high concentration of rebel activity ahead. Barracks and a possible armory.

They press themselves against a grimy wall as two heavily armed REBEL GUARDS pass by, their conversation a low rumble. Lucky's HALO HUD translates their Martian dialect: "...new batch of 'evolved' coming online... Queen's impatient..."

<center>LUCKY</center>
> (Whispering back)
> "Evolved"? Sounds like more Carcazods. We need to find their manifest, or a data log.

They reach a reinforced door. Astra produces her universal bypass key. It glows faintly as she works, the intricate mechanisms of the lock whirring softly.

<center>ASTRA</center>
> Almost there. Their security protocols are surprisingly rudimentary. Or perhaps they're overconfident.

The door clicks open. They slip inside, revealing a large, cluttered barracks. Makeshift bunks line the walls, and crude weapons are stacked haphazardly. A few REBELS are asleep, their breathing heavy.

In the center of the room, a holographic display flickers, showing schematics of a new type of Carcazod – larger, with chitinous plating and glowing red eyes. It's a more advanced, more terrifying version than anything they've encountered.

<center>LUCKY</center>
> (Eyes wide)
> That's new. And not in a good way.

Gem moves to a nearby terminal, her fingers flying across the interface. She quickly downloads data logs.

<center>GEM</center>
> They're accelerating the mutation process. And these new variants... they're designed for close-quarters combat. More resilient.

Suddenly, a REBEL stirs, groaning in his sleep. Lucky freezes, his hand hovering over the SolPen. Astra subtly aims her energy weapon.

The rebel rolls over, muttering, and falls back into a deep sleep. Lucky lets out a slow breath.

<center>LUCKY</center>
> Too close.

<center>ASTRA</center>
> (Looking at the schematics)
> This is disturbing, Lucky. The Queen isn't just creating soldiers; she's perfecting a biological weapon.

Gem finishes her download.

<center>GEM</center>
> Got it. Their supply lines, troop movements, and a detailed breakdown of the new Carcazod variants. And... a schedule for the next "evolutionary" phase. It's happening sooner than Sol predicted.

A distant alarm blares, faint but unmistakable.

<center>LUCKY</center>
> We've been made. Let's move!

They exit the barracks, the muffled sounds of the hideout now replaced by the growing clamor of an awakened enemy.

FADE OUT.
