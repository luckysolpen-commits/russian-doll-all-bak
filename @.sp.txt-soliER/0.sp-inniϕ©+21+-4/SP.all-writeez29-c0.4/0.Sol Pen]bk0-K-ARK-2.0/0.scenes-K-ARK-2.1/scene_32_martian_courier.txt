INT. MARTIAN COURIER SHIP - SPACE - EN ROUTE TO EARTH

SOUND of the ship's engines, a higher-pitched whine than the SSOL, the quiet tension of a borrowed vessel

The Martian courier ship is nothing like the SSOL. It's smaller, rougher, built for speed rather than comfort. The interior is all exposed metal and utilitarian seating. Type C blood vials are strapped into holders on every wall — emergency rations for the journey.

LUCKY sits in the co-pilot's seat, his mohawk a deep space-black today. He's staring at the comms panel. Still nothing from Sol. Still silence.

ASTRA is in the rear, reviewing data on her tablet. The Type C blood reserves are adequate, the ship's systems are functional, but everything feels temporary. Borrowed. Impermanent.

GEM pilots the ship with practiced precision. Her hands move across the controls with a familiarity that suggests she's done this before — many times.

<center>LUCKY</center>
> (tapping the comms again)
> SSOL, this is Lucky Shepherd. Final attempt. Respond.

Static. The kind that means no one is home.

<center>ASTRA</center>
> He's not coming back, Lucky.

<center>LUCKY</center>
> I know. I just... I need to hear him say it. That he's done with us. That his mission is complete and we're on our own.

<center>GEM</center>
> (without turning)
> Sol doesn't do closure. He does objectives. His objective was to prepare you. You're prepared. The objective is complete.

<center>LUCKY</center>
> (bitter)
> Convenient how he defines completion.

<center>ASTRA</center>
> Focus on Earth. The Carcazods are infiltrating. The clowns are spreading chaos. That's what we're going to fight. Not Sol's abandonment.

Lucky leans back. Closes his eyes. The Type C blood in his system keeps his mind sharp even in the fatigue of space travel.

<center>LUCKY</center>
> What do we know about the situation on the ground?

Astra pulls up a holographic display. Earth rotates between them.

<center>ASTRA</center>
> The clowns have taken over most of South America. Mexico is falling. Texas is fortified but barely holding. The Clean Zones on Earth are sealed — only the elite inside. Everyone else is exposed.

<center>GEM</center>
> The Carcazods aren't attacking. They're infiltrating. Water systems. Food supply. Population centers. They're patient. They can wait.

<center>LUCKY</center>
> And the WHO? The governments?

<center>ASTRA</center>
> Fragmented. Panicking. The Clean Zones have their own governments now. The rest of the world is on its own.

Lucky stares at Earth. His home. The place he left behind. The place he swore he'd never return to.

He opens a vial of Type C blood. Drinks. The oxygen rush sharpens everything.

<center>LUCKY</center>
> We're going to New York. That's ground zero. If the Carcazods are anywhere, they're there.

<center>GEM</center>
> New York it is.

The courier ship accelerates through the void, carrying Lucky, Astra, and Gem toward Earth.

Toward the infiltration.

Toward the truth.

FADE OUT.
