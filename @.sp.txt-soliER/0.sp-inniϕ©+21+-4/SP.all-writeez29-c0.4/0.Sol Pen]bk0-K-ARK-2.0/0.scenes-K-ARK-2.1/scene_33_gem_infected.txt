INT. MARTIAN COURIER SHIP - MEDICAL BAY

SOUND of medical monitors beeping, the hiss of a bio-containment seal, labored breathing

The medical bay is cramped — a single bed, a few medical instruments, and a bio-containment unit that looks like it hasn't been used in years.

GEM is on the bed. Her skin is pale, her breathing shallow. Something is WRONG with her. Dark veins spider-web across her neck and face — the unmistakable sign of a CARCAZOD BIO-WEAPON infection.

LUCKY is at her side, his hands moving with practiced precision. His HALO HUD is displaying Gem's vitals, the infection's progression, and a treatment protocol that he's designing in real-time using the SolPen.

<center>ASTRA</center>
> (reading the HUD data)
> The infection is attacking her cardiovascular system. It's rewriting her DNA at the quantum level. Lucky, if we don't stop it—

<center>LUCKY</center>
> I know. I see it.

He extends the SolPen. Its tip glows with intense energy. He's not creating an object — he's creating a CURE. A nanobot swarm designed to target the Carcazod infection at the molecular level, identify the corrupted DNA sequences, and repair them.

It's the most delicate thing he's ever attempted.

<center>LUCKY</center>
> (murmuring to himself)
> Target the quantum corruption. Preserve the host DNA. Rebuild the cardiac tissue. Come on...

The SolPen hums. Tiny, invisible nanobots stream from its tip, entering Gem's bloodstream through a port Lucky has created in her arm.

Minutes pass. The monitors beep. Gem's breathing is shallow, irregular.

Then — a change. The dark veins begin to recede. Her breathing steadies. Her heart rate normalizes.

Lucky exhales. He didn't realize he was holding his breath.

<center>ASTRA</center>
> (watching the monitors)
> It's working. The infection is retreating. Her cardiac function is stabilizing.

Gem's eyes flutter open. She looks at Lucky — really looks at him — and something shifts in her expression. Something vulnerable.

<center>GEM</center>
> (weakly)
> You saved me.

<center>LUCKY</center>
> Don't make it a habit.

She reaches for his hand. He lets her take it.

<center>GEM</center>
> I was wrong about you, Lucky. I thought you were just Sol's tool. His weapon. But you're... more.

<center>LUCKY</center>
> (quietly)
> We all are.

She pulls his hand toward her. Their faces are close. The medical bay is quiet except for the monitors, steady now, rhythmic.

They kiss.

It's not passionate. It's not desperate. It's something quieter — recognition. Two people who've been through something impossible and found each other on the other side.

When they pull apart, Gem's color is returning. The infection is gone.

<center>ASTRA</center>
> (from the doorway, dry)
> I'll just... be in the cockpit. Making sure we don't crash into the Atlantic.

Astra exits. Lucky and Gem share a smile.

<center>LUCKY</center>
> Rest. We'll be in New York soon.

<center>GEM</center>
> (closing her eyes)
> I'll be ready.

Lucky stands. Looks at his hands — the hands that built empires, then gardens, then weapons, then cures.

He flexes his fingers. The SolPen is warm in its holster.

FADE OUT.
