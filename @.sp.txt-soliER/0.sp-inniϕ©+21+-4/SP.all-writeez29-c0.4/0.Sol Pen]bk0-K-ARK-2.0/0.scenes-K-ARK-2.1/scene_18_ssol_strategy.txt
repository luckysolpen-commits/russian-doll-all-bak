INT. SSOL - SOL'S PRIVATE QUARTERS/STRATEGY ROOM - NIGHT

SOUND of a low, meditative hum, the soft pulse of holographic displays, the weight of secrets

Sol's private quarters are sparse — almost monastic. The walls are bare dark metal. The only furniture is a low platform that serves as a bed and a holographic strategy table that dominates the center of the room. The table is active, projecting a three-dimensional map of the solar system that fills the air with ghostly light.

LUCKY enters, his mohawk a deep midnight blue tonight, matching the dark silk shirt and diamond-studded Exo Suit modifications he's made. He carries himself differently now — more confident, more dangerous. The SolPen training has changed him.

Sol stands at the strategy table, his masked face illuminated by the holographic glow. He doesn't turn as Lucky enters.

<center>SOL</center>
> You've been training for weeks. Your SolPen mastery exceeds my projections. But mastery of the tool is only the beginning. You must understand the board.

Sol gestures. The holographic map shifts, zooming in on EARTH. The planet rotates, and Lucky can see it — not the Earth he knows, but a version overlaid with data. Red zones pulse across continents.

<center>SOL</center>
> These are the Clean Zones. Areas on Earth that have been secured, purified, protected from what's coming. Only the elite have access. The wealthy. The powerful. The connected. Everyone else is outside.

<center>LUCKY</center>
> Outside what?

<center>SOL</center>
> Outside the walls. Outside the protection. Outside the future.

The map shifts again. New markers appear — thousands of them, spreading across South America, Mexico, pushing north toward Texas.

<center>SOL</center>
> The clowns are not a metaphor, Lucky. They are a movement. A cultural infection that spreads through chaos, absurdity, and violence. They're taking over South America. Mexico is falling. Texas is next. And behind them, riding the chaos, are the Carcazods.

<center>LUCKY</center>
> The clowns and the Carcazods are connected?

<center>SOL</center>
> The clowns are the vanguard. The Carcazods follow the chaos they create. Where society breaks down, the Carcazods take root. They feed on disorder. They grow in the cracks of civilization.

Sol's hand moves across the hologram. The map shifts to MARS. The red planet is covered in markers — colonies, rebel zones, contested territories.

<center>SOL</center>
> Mars is the battleground. The colonies there are caught between my forces and the Black Queen's rebels. The Black Queen and Princess Megan8r lead the clone rebellion. They're not just defectors — they're being corrupted. Some of them are turning into clowns. Punk rock clowns, violent and chaotic. And the Carcazods are using them as hosts.

<center>LUCKY</center>
> So Mars is infected. With clowns AND Carcazods.

<center>SOL</center>
> Mars is the firewall. If we lose Mars, the Carcazods have a staging ground. They'll use the Martian colonies to reach Earth's Clean Zones. And Earth will fall.

Lucky stares at the map. The scope of it is overwhelming.

<center>LUCKY</center>
> Why me? You have an army of clones. You have this ship. You have technology that makes me look like a caveman. Why do you need ME?

Sol turns to face him. The mask's eyes glow faintly.

<center>SOL</center>
> Because you are creative in ways that clones cannot be. Clones follow patterns. You break them. The SolPen rewards creativity — the more original the thought, the more powerful the creation. You are the most original thinker I have encountered in centuries.

<center>LUCKY</center>
> Centuries?

Sol pauses. A rare moment of hesitation.

<center>SOL</center>
> There is much about me you don't know. Much I will reveal when the time is right. For now, you need to understand the threat.

The holographic map shifts one more time. It zooms out — past Earth, past Mars, past the solar system. Into the deep galaxy.

And there, in a distant quadrant, Lucky sees it.

A VOID. Not darkness — absence. A patch of space where stars should be but aren't. Where matter has been erased. It's shaped like a spreading stain, growing incrementally, consuming everything in its path.

<center>SOL</center>
> The Silent Shadow. Self-replicating nanites that consume all matter, all energy. They leave nothing behind. Not even radiation. Only absence.

<center>LUCKY</center>
> (quietly)
> How big is it?

<center>SOL</center>
> It has consumed three hundred star systems. It is growing exponentially. At current rate, it reaches this solar system in approximately one year.

<center>LUCKY</center>
> One year.

<center>SOL</center>
> The SolPen is the only force we know of that can counter it. It doesn't just create matter — it rewrites the fundamental code of reality. It can erase the eraser. But I cannot wield it alone. I need you.

Lucky stares at the void on the map. His mind — the mind that built a tech empire from nothing — is already calculating, planning, strategizing.

<center>LUCKY</center>
> So the clowns on Earth, the Carcazods on Mars, the Black Queen's rebellion, the Clean Zones... it's all a distraction from the real threat.

<center>SOL</center>
> Not a distraction. A complication. We cannot fight the Silent Shadow while Earth is being consumed by chaos. We need Earth stable. United. Focused. The clowns and Carcazods are preventing that. They must be stopped.

<center>LUCKY</center>
> And Mars?

<center>SOL</center>
> Mars is your proving ground. You will take the colony from the Black Queen. You will establish a foothold. And then you will prepare for what comes next.

<center>LUCKY</center>
> Which is?

<center>SOL</center>
> Earth. The clowns are out of control. The Carcazods are spreading. The Clean Zones won't hold forever. You need to go home, Lucky. And you need to save it.

Lucky looks from the holographic void to the holographic Earth. His home. The place he left behind. The place he swore he'd never return to.

He adjusts his midnight blue mohawk. Straightens his diamond-studded Exo Suit.

<center>LUCKY</center>
> When do we leave?

<center>SOL</center>
> You leave. I do not. My mission here is... specific. I cannot leave the Moon. Not yet.

<center>LUCKY</center>
> (narrowing his eyes)
> That sounds like you're planning to stay behind.

<center>SOL</center>
> I'm planning to ensure your success. The details are irrelevant. What matters is that you are ready.

Lucky studies Sol's masked face. There's something there — something hidden behind the voice modulator and the exo suit. Something he can't quite read.

<center>LUCKY</center>
> You're holding back. There's more to this than you're telling me.

<center>SOL</center>
> There is always more. But you have enough to act. That is what matters.

The holographic map dims. The strategy table goes dark. The room feels smaller, heavier.

<center>SOL</center>
> Rest. Tomorrow, we finalize the Mars deployment. After that... you're on your own.

Sol turns and walks toward the door. He pauses at the threshold.

<center>SOL</center>
> Lucky. One more thing.

<center>LUCKY</center>
> Yeah?

<center>SOL</center>
> Trust no one on Mars. Not even those who claim to be your allies. Especially not those who claim to be your allies.

Sol exits. The door seals behind him with a soft HISS.

Lucky stands alone in the dark room, the ghost of the holographic map still burning in his mind.

He touches his SolPen. Feels its warmth. Its power. Its potential.

<center>LUCKY</center>
> (to himself)
> Mars. Here I come.

FADE OUT.
