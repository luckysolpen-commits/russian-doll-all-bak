INT. LUCKY'S RANCH - CONTINUOUS

The pulsing glow from the package intensifies, casting strange shadows across the porch. Lucky and Astra are mesmerized, a mix of fear and wonder on their faces. Lucky's burgundy mohawk catches the eerie light, making him look like a warlock receiving a dark summons. Gem remains still, her gaze fixed on Loona.

<center>LUCKY</center>
> A warning? From whom?

Loona steps closer, her voice low, almost a whisper, yet it carries an undeniable authority.

<center>LOONA</center>
> From SOL. They've been watching you, Lucky. For a long time.

Lucky scoffs, a nervous laugh escaping him.

<center>LUCKY</center>
> SOL? The tech giant? Why would they be watching me? I'm retired. I'm out of the game.

<center>ASTRA</center>
> (To Lucky, urgently)
> Lucky, listen to her. This isn't a joke.

Astra's eyes dart to the glowing package, then back to Loona, a flicker of recognition, or perhaps dawning understanding, in her gaze.

<center>LOONA</center>
> You may be out of the game, Lucky, but the game isn't out of you. Your past... your creations... they have a way of attracting attention. Especially from those who seek to control.

She gestures vaguely towards the package, then to Lucky.

<center>LOONA</center>
> This package... it's a message. A glimpse of what's to come if you don't heed the warning. SOL has a vested interest in something you possess. Something they believe is rightfully theirs.

<center>LUCKY</center>
> What are you talking about? I don't possess anything they'd want. I sold everything.

Loona's enigmatic smile returns.

<center>LOONA</center>
> Not everything, Lucky. Some things... are not for sale. Some things are inherent.

She glances at Gem, a subtle, almost imperceptible shift in her expression. Gem remains impassive, a perfect mask.

<center>LOONA</center>
> They believe you hold the key to something vital. Something that could tip the balance of power. And they will stop at nothing to acquire it.

The glow from the package begins to dim, then flickers out entirely. The porch is plunged back into near darkness, the silence now heavy with unspoken threats.

Lucky looks from Loona to Astra, then to the now inert package. His peaceful retirement, so carefully constructed, feels like it's crumbling around him.

FADE OUT.
