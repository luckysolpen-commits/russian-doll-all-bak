INT. SSOL - SPACE - EN ROUTE TO MARS

SOUND of the SSOL's engines, a deep resonant hum, the quiet tension of a ship in transit

The SSOL moves through the void between Moon and Mars. Inside, the atmosphere is charged with purpose and underlying tension.

LUCKY stands on the observation deck, his mohawk a deep battle crimson today. He's fully trained, fully equipped — SolPen holstered, HALO HUD active, Light Exo Suit humming. He looks out at the stars, at the red dot growing larger in the distance.

ASTRA joins him, holding two vials of Type C blood. She hands him one.

<center>ASTRA</center>
> You haven't had your ration.

<center>LUCKY</center>
> (taking the vial)
> I'm not sure I'll ever get used to drinking blood.

<center>ASTRA</center>
> It's synthetic. And it's keeping us alive in a ship that shouldn't exist, heading to a planet we didn't know was inhabited, to fight a war we didn't know was happening. So. Drink the blood.

Lucky cracks the vial. Drinks. The familiar rush of oxygen and energy floods his system.

<center>LUCKY</center>
> You're right. As usual.

GEM enters. She's no longer hiding her role as a SOL agent — her posture is different now. Military. Purposeful.

<center>GEM</center>
> We're three hours from Mars orbit. Sol wants final preparations complete.

<center>LUCKY</center>
> (turning to her)
> Sol isn't coming with us.

<center>GEM</center>
> No. His mission is here. On the Moon. The SSOL doesn't leave lunar orbit.

<center>ASTRA</center>
> So we're going alone.

<center>GEM</center>
> You're going with me. And the clone detachment Sol has assigned. You won't be alone.

Lucky studies Gem. There's something in her expression he can't read. Something she's not saying.

<center>LUCKY</center>
> Gem. Is there something I should know? About Sol? About this mission?

Gem holds his gaze. For a moment, he thinks she'll tell him something. Instead:

<center>GEM</center>
> You know everything you need to know. The Black Queen is a threat. Mars is the battleground. You're the weapon Sol has prepared. That's all that matters.

But Lucky isn't satisfied. He thinks about the encrypted message he received weeks ago: "He's lying to you, Lucky. About everything."

He hasn't forgotten. He hasn't stopped wondering.

<center>LUCKY</center>
> (quietly)
> We'll see.

The ship shudders slightly — a course correction. Mars fills more of the viewport now. The red planet is no longer a dot. It's a destination.

<center>GEM</center>
> Get some rest. When we arrive, there won't be time for it.

Gem exits. Lucky and Astra stand in silence, watching Mars grow larger.

<center>ASTRA</center>
> You're worried.

<center>LUCKY</center>
> I'm not worried. I'm... cautious. Sol's been manipulating events for centuries. He brought me here for a reason. I just don't know if that reason is what he says it is.

<center>ASTRA</center>
> We'll figure it out. Together.

Lucky looks at her. Nods. Adjusts his crimson mohawk.

<center>LUCKY</center>
> Together.

The SSOL continues its journey through the void, carrying Lucky, Astra, and Gem toward Mars.

Toward war.

Toward the truth.

FADE OUT.
