INT. SHADOW SYNDICATE HEADQUARTERS - TOKYO - DAY

SOUND of bustling city, distant sirens, a low, industrial hum

Tokyo. A labyrinth of neon and steel. Lucky, dressed in a custom black silk suit with gold dragon embroidery, diamond-studded watch, and a platinum mohawk dyed electric silver, navigates the crowded streets. He carries a sleek, expensive briefcase. His demeanor is confident, almost arrogant – a perfect disguise. He looks like a tech mogul who moonlights as a hip-hop producer.

The building is nondescript, a grey monolith amidst the vibrant chaos of the city. No obvious signs, no grand entrance. Just a heavy, unmarked steel door.

Lucky approaches, his eyes scanning for surveillance, for any tell-tale signs of security. He finds them – subtle cameras, pressure plates, hidden sensors. He smiles faintly; they're good, but he's better.

He produces a small, sophisticated device from his pocket, a miniature EMP. A quick, almost imperceptible pulse. The cameras flicker, then stabilize. Just enough to create a momentary blind spot.

He knocks on the steel door. A small, almost invisible peephole slides open. A pair of hard, suspicious eyes scrutinize him.

<center>GUARD 1</center>
> State your business.

<center>LUCKY</center>
> I'm here to see Kaito. I have a proposition. One he'll find... lucrative.

The peephole closes. A moment of tense silence. Then, a series of heavy CLUNKS and HISSES as multiple locks disengage. The door slides open, revealing two hulking GUARDS, their faces impassive, their bodies radiating menace.

They frisk Lucky with practiced efficiency. He allows it, his expression bored. They find nothing. They eye his briefcase.

<center>GUARD 1</center>
> The briefcase stays.

<center>LUCKY</center>
> (A slight smirk)
> Not a chance. What's inside is for Kaito's eyes only. Unless you want to explain to him why you delayed a potential multi-million dollar investment?

The guards exchange a look. They've clearly been briefed on Kaito's priorities. Reluctantly, Guard 1 nods.

<center>GUARD 1</center>
> Follow us.

They lead Lucky through a maze of dimly lit corridors. The air grows colder, the sounds of the city fading, replaced by the low hum of unseen machinery. This is the heart of the Shadow Syndicate.

They arrive at a large, open chamber. Rows of computers hum, their screens displaying complex data. Technicians, pale and intense, tap away at keyboards. In the center, overseeing it all, is KAITO (40s), a man of sharp angles and colder eyes. He exudes an aura of quiet, dangerous power.

Kaito looks up as Lucky enters, his gaze piercing, assessing.

<center>KAITO</center>
> So, you're the one with the proposition. You have five minutes to impress me.

Lucky sets his briefcase on a nearby table, opens it with a flourish. Inside, nestled on a bed of velvet, is not cash, but a single, intricately designed, futuristic DEVICE. It pulses with a faint, internal light.

Kaito's eyes narrow, a flicker of genuine interest replacing his usual disdain.

<center>LUCKY</center>
> This, Mr. Kaito, is just a prototype. But it represents a technology that will revolutionize... everything. And I'm offering you a piece of the future.

Kaito circles the table, his eyes fixed on the device. He picks it up, turning it over in his hands.

<center>KAITO</center>
> Revolutionize everything, you say? Bold claim. What exactly does it do?

<center>LUCKY</center>
> It's a universal translator, a data siphon, and a localized EMP, all in one. But its true power lies in its ability to interface with any network, any system, and extract information without a trace. It's the ultimate tool for... acquisition.

Kaito's lips curl into a slight smile. This is a language he understands.

<center>KAITO</center>
> And you're offering this to me? Why?

<center>LUCKY</center>
> Because I understand the value of true power. And I believe the Shadow Syndicate is uniquely positioned to leverage this technology. I'm not looking for a quick sale, Kaito. I'm looking for a partnership. A mutually beneficial arrangement where we both profit immensely.

Kaito sets the device back down, his gaze returning to Lucky, sharper now.

<center>KAITO</center>
> A partnership with a ghost. You have no official ties, no public record. How do I know you're not a plant?

<center>LUCKY</center>
> (Leaning in, voice dropping)
> Because a plant would have a record. My anonymity is my greatest asset. It allows me to move where others can't. And it allows me to bring you opportunities no one else can. The CIA sent me here, Kaito. They want this device back. They believe it's "sensitive equipment" that fell into the wrong hands.

Kaito's eyes widen almost imperceptibly. This is unexpected.

<center>KAITO</center>
> The CIA? And you're telling me this?

<center>LUCKY</center>
> Because I'm not working for them. I'm working for myself. And right now, our interests align. They want the device. I want to use it to expose them. And you, Kaito, you want to expand your empire. This device, in your hands, will make you untouchable.

Kaito considers this, a slow, dangerous smile spreading across his face.

<center>KAITO</center>
> Untouchable, you say? I like the sound of that. What's your price?

<center>LUCKY</center>
> Not money. Access. I need to understand how this "sensitive equipment" was acquired. Who was involved. And I need your help to get it to a buyer who will truly appreciate its value. Someone who will use it to disrupt the very powers that seek to control us all.

Kaito's smile widens. He extends a hand.

<center>KAITO</center>
> You're a dangerous man, Lucky Shepherd. I think we're going to get along just fine.

They shake hands. The deal is struck.

FADE OUT.
