**Chapter 17: Martian Undercurrents**

Lucky trudged through the base’s corridors, the sterile, recycled air a constant reminder of his artificial existence. He felt like he was stuck in some kind of never-ending loop, a monotonous cycle of routine that chafed against his restless spirit. Every day was the same: wake up early, eat breakfast, attend meetings with Sol, and spend hours upon hours working on the mission, a mission whose true purpose remained shrouded in Sol’s enigmatic pronouncements. The vastness of space outside the panoramic windows, once a source of wonder, now seemed to mirror the vastness of the unknown that lay ahead, a future shrouded in mystery and potential peril.

Astra, on the other hand, seemed to have found a new sense of purpose here on the moon. She spent her days studying the lunar geology, poring over maps and data with Loona Twilight. Lucky would catch glimpses of them working together, their heads bent in concentration, their shared scientific curiosity a bond he sometimes envied. He couldn’t help but feel a twinge of jealousy, a longing for the intellectual engagement that seemed to elude him in his current role. As the days blended together, Lucky found himself growing more and more restless. He longed for the thrill of the chase, the rush of adrenaline as he outsmarted his enemies, the intellectual challenge of a problem to solve. This slow-paced life on the moon was driving him crazy, a gilded cage of advanced technology and philosophical ennui.

One day, as he was walking through the base’s central hub, Lucky caught sight of a group of clone soldiers playing some kind of lunar soccer. The game was chaotic and loud, with players leaping and diving across the makeshift field, their movements surprisingly fluid despite the low gravity. For a moment, Lucky felt his spirits lift – maybe this was what he needed, a little excitement to spice up his days, a brief escape from the crushing weight of his responsibilities. But as he watched the game, he realized that even this wasn’t about him or Astra. It was just another aspect of life on the moon base, something else to distract him from the real reason they were here in the first place: to uncover the secrets of the alien artifact, to understand the true nature of the SolPen, and to fulfill the cryptic prophecy of the woman in his vision. Lucky’s gaze drifted away from the game, his thoughts turning back to the mission and what lay ahead. He knew he couldn’t stay stuck in this rut forever – but for now, he was trapped, waiting for something – anything – to change.

He sat on the edge of his bunk, the cold, utilitarian surface a stark contrast to the plush comfort of his former life. His eyes, heavy with a weariness that went beyond physical exhaustion, stared blankly at the metallic wall, trying to process everything that had happened in the past few days. It felt like a lifetime ago that he had first set foot on Mars, full of a naive hope and dreams for a new beginning, a fresh start away from the complexities of Earth. Now, things were getting complicated, spiraling into a vortex of the bizarre and the terrifying. The ritual he had witnessed, the pulsating red mass, the woman with eyes that burned with an inner fire – it had left him with more questions than answers. What was the purpose of the red mass? What was its connection to the SolPen prototype? And what did it have to do with the clones, their silent, omnipresent forms a constant reminder of the unsettling reality of Lunaria? He sighed, rubbing his temples in a futile attempt to ease the growing tension in his head, a dull throb that mirrored the unsettling rhythm of his thoughts. He needed a distraction, something to pull his mind away from the abyss of the unknown.

That’s when he remembered the new female friends he had made on Mars. There was Sofia, the feisty and confident one, her laughter a bright, infectious sound that cut through the monotony of colony life. Lena, the quiet and reserved one, possessed a sharp intellect and a calming presence that Lucky found surprisingly comforting. And Maya, the bubbly and outgoing one, whose boundless energy and infectious optimism were a welcome antidote to the grim realities of their existence. They were all part of an exclusive group, the “Mars Settlers,” who had formed their own tight-knit community on this unforgiving planet, a testament to humanity’s enduring need for connection and belonging. Lucky thought about the times they had spent together, sharing meals in the communal mess hall, exploring the desolate beauty of the Martian terrain in their pressurized rovers, and simply enjoying each other’s company, a fragile bubble of normalcy in a world teetering on the brink of chaos. He smiled, a genuine, unforced expression, feeling a warmth spread through his chest. Yes, that was exactly what he needed – some time with his friends, a brief respite from the crushing weight of his new reality.

He got up from his bunk, stretching his arms above his head, the stiffness in his muscles a reminder of his recent ordeal. Time to get moving! First things first, though: he had to find out where those three people were that he had to… kill and take their souls. The thought made him queasy, a cold knot forming in his stomach. It was a barbaric concept, a violation of every moral code he had ever known, yet the woman in his vision had been clear. It was necessary for the Soul Pen prototype, a chilling requirement for unlocking its full potential. He pushed the revulsion down, burying it deep within, a necessary sacrifice for a greater, albeit still unclear, purpose.

He headed out of his quarters, making his way through the cramped, utilitarian corridors of the Mars Settlement. He passed by the mess hall, where Sofia, Lena, and Maya were gathered around a table, their voices a cheerful murmur, their laughter echoing in the sterile environment. Lucky’s heart skipped a beat at the sight of them, so vibrant, so alive, so unaware of the dark path he was being forced to walk. Maybe later, he thought, a silent promise to himself – he had some… business to attend to first.

He made his way to the settlement’s central hub, a large room filled with data terminals and shimmering holographic displays. The settlers were busy typing away on their keyboards or staring at the swirling patterns of code dancing across the screens, their faces illuminated by the cold, blue light. Lucky searched for any information about the three people he needed to find, the three individuals whose lives were now inextricably linked to his own. He scrolled through databases, scanned through personnel files, and even checked the settlement’s security logs – nothing. No one seemed to know anything about them, as if they had simply vanished into the thin Martian air.

Just as he was starting to get frustrated, a commotion erupted at the entrance of the hub. A group of rough-looking men, their faces scarred from years of living in harsh environments, stormed in. They were armed with heavy-duty tools, their movements aggressive, their eyes hard and unforgiving. They looked like they meant business, and Lucky’s instincts, honed by years of navigating dangerous situations, screamed at him to be wary.

One of them, a burly man with a menacing grin, spotted Lucky and strode over to him, his heavy boots thudding against the metallic floor. “You’re the new guy, right?” he said, his voice like a rusty gate, grating and unpleasant. “We’ve been sent by the Red Vipers to deal with some… matters.”

Lucky’s instincts screamed at him to get out of there – fast. But before he could move, the burly man continued, his eyes narrowing, a predatory glint in their depths. “You see, we have reason to believe that one of our own has gone rogue. And we’re here to take care of it.”

The man’s eyes locked onto Lucky, and for a moment, they just stared at each other, a silent battle of wills. Then, Lucky realized what was going on – these men were after the same three people he needed to find, the same three individuals whose souls were destined to fuel the SolPen. A sly grin spread across his face as he calculated his next move. This was getting interesting, a dangerous dance of deception and manipulation, and Lucky, despite his reservations, found himself drawn into its intricate steps.

***

Lucky’s grin grew wider as he watched the Red Vipers’ leader, the burly man, take a step closer to him. He knew that this was an opportunity too good to pass up, a chance to kill two birds with one stone, to achieve his own dark purpose under the guise of cooperation. “You’re looking for someone who’s gone rogue?” Lucky asked, his voice innocent and curious, a perfect facade of helpfulness. “I might be able to help you with that.”

The Red Viper leader raised an eyebrow, clearly skeptical of Lucky’s motives. “What makes you think you can help us?” His voice was laced with suspicion, his eyes searching Lucky’s face for any sign of deceit.

Lucky shrugged, trying to appear nonchalant, a master of misdirection. “I’ve heard rumors about some… unusual occurrences happening around Mars lately. Whispers of strange happenings and people acting out of character. I figure if there’s someone out there causing trouble, it’s probably connected to what we’re looking for.” He was feeding them just enough truth to make his lies believable, weaving a tangled web of half-truths and calculated omissions.

The burly man snorted, a sound of grudging amusement, but Lucky could tell he was intrigued. The bait had been taken. “What kind of rumors are you talking about?”

Lucky hesitated for a moment, feigning a thoughtful pause before deciding to reveal more information, carefully calibrated to serve his own agenda. “Just whispers of strange happenings and people acting out of character. I’m sure it’s nothing, but… well, you never know what might be going on.” He let the words hang in the air, allowing the Red Viper leader’s imagination to fill in the blanks, to conjure up images of rogue clones and hidden agendas.

The Red Viper leader narrowed his eyes, sizing Lucky up, his gaze piercing. After a few moments of tense silence, he seemed to come to a decision, a grudging acceptance of Lucky’s offer. “Alright, new guy,” he said, a hint of a smile playing on his lips, a predatory gleam in his eyes. “You can help us out. But don’t think this is a free ride or anything. We’ll be watching you closely.”

Lucky nodded enthusiastically, trying to appear eager and cooperative, a perfect picture of a man eager to please. He knew that he had just gained the Red Vipers’ trust, and with it, access to information about the rogue individuals they were seeking. It was a dangerous alliance, a pact with the devil, but Lucky was willing to pay the price.

As the burly man turned to leave, Lucky called out to him, his voice carefully casual. “Hey, wait a minute! I forgot to ask… what’s the name of this rogue individual we’re looking for?”

The Red Viper leader turned back around, his expression serious once more. “Her name is Elara. And she’s trouble, if you know what I mean.” His words were a warning, a subtle hint of the danger that Elara represented.

Lucky nodded thoughtfully, trying to appear like he was taking in the information seriously. In reality, he was already planning how to use this new knowledge to further his own agenda, to manipulate the Red Vipers, to turn them into unwitting pawns in his own game. As the Red Vipers left the hub, Lucky watched them go with a calculating gaze. He knew that he had just gained a valuable ally in his search for the three individuals he needed to find, the three souls destined to power the SolPen.

He turned back to the data terminals, his mind racing with possibilities and schemes. He was determined to use this new information to his advantage, no matter what it took. And so, Lucky set out on a new path, using the Red Vipers’ resources and connections to track down Elara and the other two individuals he needed to find. He knew that this would be a difficult and potentially treacherous journey, but he was ready for whatever lay ahead. For now, at least, his mission was back on track. And with the Red Vipers by his side, Lucky felt like he had finally found a way to make progress towards achieving his goal. But as he delved deeper into the unknown, Lucky couldn’t shake the feeling that he was being watched, waiting for him to make his next move, a silent, unseen presence observing his every step.

***

Lucky’s imagination was finally firing on all cylinders. He could feel it coursing through his veins like liquid fire, fueling the prototype soulpen device. As he rode a hoverboard across the desert landscape, the advanced Mars weapons at his disposal hummed with power. In front of him, a trail of human sacrifices lay waiting to be claimed by the soulpen. Lucky’s eyes narrowed as he spotted the first bounty victim, a former red viper and suspected clown, ring leader of a recent hit on the heart of the cities blood bank, a young woman fleeing on foot. With a flick of his wrist, he activated the hoverboard’s turbo boost, closing in on his target, a beautiful but deadly clown gangster. Although her deadliness seemed rather downplayed as Lucky cornered her here, alone.

The wind whipped through his hair as he swooped down, his Mars-enhanced senses picking up every detail. The woman’s fear was palpable, her heart racing like a drumbeat in his mind. Lucky smiled to himself, relishing the thrill of the hunt. Behind him, Astra led a group of female Mars mechs, their advanced sensors tracking Lucky’s movements with ease. She seemed to be enjoying the chase, her expression a mixture of curiosity and calculation.

Lucky reached out with the soulpen prototype, a tendril of energy snaking out to ensnare his prey. The woman let out a terrified scream as she was pulled towards him, her struggles futile against the device’s power. As he absorbed the sacrifice’s energy, Lucky felt his connection to the soulpen growing stronger. It was like he was tapping into an endless wellspring of power, and he reveled in the rush of adrenaline coursing through his veins.

But beneath the surface excitement, a calculating glint had crept into his eye. He knew that with each sacrifice, his bond to the soulpen would only grow more intimate. And with this newfound power, Lucky began to formulate a plan. He would take out Elara first, after… ‘taking care’ of her clone virginity. The thought sent a pang of guilt through him, but he pushed it aside. After all, she was just another pawn in his game of self-preservation. And besides, he’d gain her trust by making her think they shared something special.

Once Elara was taken care of, Lucky would turn on the Red Vipers. He’d claim self-defense and portray himself as a hero, turning the Mars Police against them for their involvement in blood smuggling with the Clowns. It was the perfect plan, one that would ensure his survival while cementing his position as the most powerful being on Mars. As he continued to hunt down his human sacrifices, Lucky’s imagination ran wild. He saw himself standing victorious over the Red Vipers’ defeated bodies, the Mars Police at his side. The image filled him with a sense of pride and accomplishment, one that would fuel his actions in the days to come.

But amidst the chaos of his thoughts, a tiny voice whispered doubts. Was this really what he wanted? Was it worth betraying those who had once been his friends? Lucky pushed the voice aside, focusing on the thrill of the hunt. After all, he’d always been driven by self-preservation. And in a world where only the strongest survived, he was willing to do whatever it took to stay on top. As the sun dipped below the horizon, casting the desert landscape in a fiery glow, Lucky’s imagination reached new heights. He saw himself as a hero, a savior of humanity, with the soulpen at his command. The thought sent a shiver down his spine, and he knew that nothing would stop him from achieving his goal.

***

Lucky’s imagination had him standing victorious over Elara’s defeated form, her clone virginity a distant memory as he prepared to make his move. He crept up behind her, the soulpen prototype humming in his hand. “It’s over, Elara,” Lucky whispered, his voice dripping with menace. “You and your precious Red Vipers are going down.”

Elara spun around, her eyes flashing with fear. “L-Lucky, what are you doing? You promised me-”

“Promises are meant to be broken, darling,” Lucky said, a cold smile spreading across his face. With a swift motion, he activated the soulpen and absorbed Elara’s energy. The Red Vipers’ leader let out a weak cry as her life force was drained away. Lucky watched with satisfaction as her body crumpled to the ground. “Time to take care of the rest,” he muttered to himself.

He dispatched several more Red Vipers, using his Mars-enhanced senses and advanced weapons to devastating effect. As the battle raged on, Lucky felt his connection to the soulpen growing stronger still. But just as he was about to deliver the finishing blow to the last Red Viper, a loud alarm pierced the air. The Mars Police had arrived, responding to an anonymous tip about a blood bust in progress.

Lucky’s eyes narrowed as he assessed the situation. He’d been so caught up in his own desires that he hadn’t considered the possibility of backup arriving. Now, with the police closing in, he needed to get out – fast. He activated his hoverboard and sped away from the chaos, leaving behind a trail of destruction and the bodies of his human sacrifices. The Mars Police gave chase, but Lucky was too quick, too powerful.

As he fled, he realized that his plan had gone awry. He’d been so focused on his own desires that he’d forgotten about the consequences. Now, he found himself facing the very authorities he’d tried to manipulate. Lucky’s hoverboard screeched to a halt outside a hospital, where he collapsed from exhaustion and injuries. As he lay in his bed, he couldn’t help but think about how things had gone wrong.

Astra appeared at his bedside, her expression a mixture of curiosity and concern. “Well, well, well. Look what the cat dragged in,” she said, her voice dripping with sarcasm. Lucky’s eyes snapped open, his mind racing to process the situation. “Astra, Gem? What are you doing here?”

“Following you, of course,” she replied, her gaze piercing. “I knew you were up to something. But why didn’t you help me keep an eye on things? Why did you get caught?”

Lucky’s face twisted in frustration. “You’re supposed to be my SECURITY DISPATCH! I thought we had each other’s backs!”

“AND u!?” Astra gazed at the ground sheepishly.

GEM shrugged. “I’m a Mars Mech, Lucky. My loyalty is to the Martian throne, not to your personal vendettas.”

Lucky seethed with anger, but before he could respond, Gem continued, her voice cold and detached. “You know, Lucky, I think it’s time for you to face the consequences of your actions. The Black Queen will be interested in hearing about this… mishap.”

With that, she turned and left the hospital room, leaving Lucky to stew in his own bitter regret. As he lay there, he realized that things had gone too far. He needed a new plan – one that didn’t involve getting caught by the authorities or betraying those around him. And so, with a newfound sense of determination, Lucky set his sights on a different goal: escape, and then… revenge.
