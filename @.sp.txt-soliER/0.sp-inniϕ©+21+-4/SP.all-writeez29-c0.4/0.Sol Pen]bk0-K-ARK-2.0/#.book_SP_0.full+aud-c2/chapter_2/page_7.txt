Lucky found himself standing in a vast, empty space, a void devoid of light or sound. The air was thick with the scent of blood and smoke, a chilling reminder of the battlefield he had left behind. He looked around, trying to get his bearings, his mind struggling to comprehend his new surroundings. Where was he? Was this a dream? A hallucination? Or something far more sinister?

As his vision cleared, Lucky saw that he was surrounded by rows upon rows of vials, each one containing the same pulsating liquid he had just encountered. He felt a strange sense of unease, a pervasive feeling of being watched, of unseen eyes scrutinizing his every move. The silence was deafening, broken only by the faint, rhythmic thrumming that seemed to emanate from the vials themselves.

Suddenly, a figure emerged from the shadows, materializing from the very fabric of the void. It was a woman with long, flowing hair and eyes that seemed to burn with an inner fire, a mesmerizing intensity that drew him in. Her presence filled the vast space, radiating an aura of immense power and ancient wisdom.

“Welcome, Lucky,” she said, her voice low and husky, a silken whisper that echoed in the emptiness. “I’ve been waiting for you.”

Lucky felt a surge of fear mixed with excitement. Who was this woman? What did she want from him? Was she friend or foe? His instincts, usually so reliable, were now a tangled mess of conflicting signals.

The woman reached out and touched his forehead. Lucky felt a sudden flood of visions and images, a torrent of information downloading directly into his brain, overwhelming his senses. He saw glimpses of ancient rituals, of blood and sacrifice, of power beyond human comprehension. He saw the creation of the SolPen, its true purpose, its terrifying potential.

“You are searching for the secrets of the Soul Pen prototype,” she said, her eyes burning with an inner intensity that seemed to pierce through his very soul. “I can help you. But first, you must prove yourself worthy.”

Lucky felt a surge of determination. He was going to get to the bottom of this mystery, no matter what it took. He would understand the SolPen, its power, and its true purpose. “What do I need to do?” he asked, his voice firm and resolute, a challenge to the unknown.

The woman smiled, her eyes glinting with amusement, a predatory gleam. “You will need to collect three souls,” she said, her voice a chilling pronouncement. “And then, you will be given the secrets you seek.”

Lucky felt a surge of excitement mixed with trepidation. What did this mean? How was he supposed to collect these souls? Was she speaking metaphorically, or literally? The implications were horrifying, yet the promise of power, of understanding, was too tempting to resist.

 The woman vanished as suddenly as she had appeared, leaving Lucky alone in the vast, empty space. He knew that he had to start collecting those souls. But how? And what would be the cost? As he stood there, wondering what to do next, he felt a strange sensation in his mind. It was as if someone – or something – was watching him from afar, a silent, unseen presence. And then, everything went black again.
