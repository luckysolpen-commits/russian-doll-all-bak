Astra, ever the curious scientist, gravitated towards the ship’s power core and life support systems. She spent hours in the engineering bays, her mind alight with questions, her fingers tracing the intricate conduits and energy relays. Loona Twilight, who seemed to be everywhere at once, often joined her, patiently answering Astra’s inquiries, her knowledge of the SSOL’s inner workings seemingly limitless. Astra discovered more about the ship’s power source, a clean, efficient energy generation system that seemed to defy the laws of thermodynamics. She learned about the advanced life support, a closed-loop system that recycled every molecule, ensuring a sustainable environment for the thousands of clones on board. She even stumbled upon a section dedicated to biological research, where new strains of flora and fauna were being cultivated, hinting at a long-term plan for terraforming or colonization.

One evening, while observing the energy conduits that snaked through the ship’s lower decks, Astra noticed a subtle anomaly. A faint, almost imperceptible flicker in the energy flow, a momentary dip that was quickly corrected. It was too small to be a malfunction, too precise to be random. She spent days analyzing the data, cross-referencing it with the ship’s energy logs. What she discovered sent a chill down her spine. The energy fluctuations weren’t random; they were rhythmic, almost like a heartbeat. And they corresponded precisely with the moments when new clones were being activated, when their “souls” were being integrated into the collective consciousness that fueled the SolPen. The ship wasn’t just powered by an advanced energy source; it was, in part, powered by the very essence of the clones themselves. It was a horrifying realization, a confirmation of Lucky’s earlier, chilling thought. The SSOL, the magnificent Mother Ship, was a gilded cage, a monument to a terrifying, unethical truth.
