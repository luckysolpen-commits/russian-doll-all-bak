Lucky and Astra were led by Sol through the bustling hangar. Lucky was overwhelmed, his mind struggling to comprehend the sheer scale of this hidden lunar society. It defied every scientific and engineering principle he knew. How could such a vast, self-sustaining city exist, undetected, within the Moon? His tech-savvy brain, accustomed to dissecting complex systems, found itself utterly outmatched. Astra, while still observant, seemed less surprised, almost as if she’d been here before, or possessed some prior knowledge of such places. Her eyes, though wide with wonder, held a flicker of recognition, a quiet understanding that Lucky couldn’t fathom.

“Clones?” Lucky whispered to Astra, his voice barely audible above the hangar’s hum, his gaze sweeping across the endless ranks of identical faces. “All of them?” The thought was unsettling, a chilling echo of a dystopian future he had only read about in science fiction.

“It appears so,” Astra replied quietly, her voice thoughtful. “A highly organized, self-sufficient society. Every resource optimized, every individual serving a specific function.” Her scientific mind, ever analytical, was already dissecting the implications, the sheer logistical marvel of such an undertaking.

They passed by sections of the base dedicated to research, development, and even what looked like vast agricultural zones, all bathed under the artificial, yet perfectly calibrated, light of the moon base. It was a fully functioning city, a hidden ecosystem thriving beneath the lunar crust, a testament to an advanced civilization that had mastered resource management and biological replication on an unprecedented scale.
