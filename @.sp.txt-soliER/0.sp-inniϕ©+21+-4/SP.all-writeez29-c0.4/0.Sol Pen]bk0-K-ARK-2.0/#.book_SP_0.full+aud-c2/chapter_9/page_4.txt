“An entire city… on the Moon,” Lucky murmured, his voice filled with a mixture of awe and disbelief. “How is this possible? How has it remained hidden?”

“Necessity,” Sol replied, his voice echoing in the vast space. “And the application of advanced principles. We have been here for centuries, observing, preparing. Waiting for the opportune moment.” His words were chilling, hinting at a long-term plan, a patient, meticulous strategy that dwarfed any human ambition.

They finally arrived at a vast training room. Here, clones were engaged in various exercises, some with what appeared to be rudimentary versions of the SolPen, creating simple constructs – geometric shapes, basic tools, glowing energy fields. It was a glimpse into the potential of the device Sol had shown him, a raw, untamed power being slowly harnessed.

Sol gestured to the room, his masked face turning towards Lucky. “This is where your true training begins, Lucky Shepherd. You will learn to master the SolPen. To harness the power of creation. To shape reality itself.”

Lucky looked at the clones, then at the SolPen in Sol’s hand, then back at the clones, a new kind of awe, mixed with a growing sense of purpose, beginning to replace his initial fear. The implications were staggering. He, Lucky Shepherd, the retired tech mogul, was about to be initiated into a secret lunar society, tasked with mastering a device that could literally reshape the world. His life, once so carefully controlled, had spiraled into an unimaginable future, a future where he was no longer just an observer, but a key player in a cosmic drama.
