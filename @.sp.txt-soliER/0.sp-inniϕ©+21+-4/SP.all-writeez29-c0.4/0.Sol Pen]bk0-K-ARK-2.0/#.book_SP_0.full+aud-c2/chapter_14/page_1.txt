The SSOL, a silent behemoth of advanced technology, cut through the void, leaving the Moon’s hidden city behind. Lucky and Astra, now seasoned residents of this alien vessel, found themselves summoned to Sol’s private quarters, a space that was as minimalist and enigmatic as the being who inhabited it. The room was devoid of personal touches, a stark contrast to the bustling, almost communal areas of the SSOL. It was a place of pure function, designed for contemplation and strategic planning, and it was here that Sol chose to unveil the true scope of his ambition.

Sol stood before a holographic projection of Earth, its familiar blue and green swirling in the silent air. His masked face, as always, betrayed no emotion, but his voice, deep and resonant, carried a new urgency. “The time for observation is over, Lucky Shepherd. The time for action has come. Earth is on the precipice of a cataclysm, a self-inflicted wound that threatens to consume it entirely.”
