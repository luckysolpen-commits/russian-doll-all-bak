“Our mission to Mars is paramount,” Sol continued, his voice unwavering. “The Black Queen has established a stronghold there, a base of operations from which she plans to unleash her ultimate weapon. We must stop her. You, Lucky, with the SolPen, are our greatest asset. Your ability to create, to adapt, to innovate, is crucial.”

Lucky’s mind raced, grappling with the enormity of the task. He had doubts, lingering questions about Sol’s ultimate goals, about the ethics of his methods, about the true nature of the “clone souls” that fueled the SolPen. Was Sol truly a savior, or merely another manipulator, albeit on a cosmic scale? But the urgency in Sol’s voice, the undeniable threat to Earth, left him with little choice. He had to commit. For Astra, for the fragile hope of humanity.

“What about Gem?” Lucky asked, remembering the enigmatic clone who had been observing his training. “What role does she play in all of this?”

Sol’s masked face seemed to acknowledge the question. “Gem is a loyal agent. She has been instrumental in gathering intelligence, in preparing the way. She will accompany you to Mars. Her knowledge of the Black Queen’s operations will be invaluable.”
