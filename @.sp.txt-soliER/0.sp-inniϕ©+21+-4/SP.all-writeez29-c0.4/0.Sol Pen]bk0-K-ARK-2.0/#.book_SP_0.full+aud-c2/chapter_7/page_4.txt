They scrambled up the stairs, the sounds of the battle growing louder again as they ascended, the muffled roars and explosions becoming clearer, more immediate. They burst onto the rooftop – a flat, exposed surface, offering no cover, no concealment. Below them, the battle continued, a swirling vortex of violence, a brutal, desperate dance of death. But on the rooftop, they were exposed, silhouetted against the star-strewn sky, vulnerable targets.

A group of terrorists, led by a particularly menacing figure, appeared from the opposite side of the roof, their weapons trained on Lucky and Astra. The Terrorist Leader, a man whose eyes held the cold, dead gaze of a fanatic, raised his hand, a gesture of command. “The device! Hand it over! Or we detonate the mini-nuke! This building, this city… it all goes! Our cover is blown!” His voice was a guttural snarl, filled with a chilling certainty. Lucky and Astra were cornered. The mini-nuke. It was real. And it was here, a ticking time bomb in the heart of a war-torn city.
