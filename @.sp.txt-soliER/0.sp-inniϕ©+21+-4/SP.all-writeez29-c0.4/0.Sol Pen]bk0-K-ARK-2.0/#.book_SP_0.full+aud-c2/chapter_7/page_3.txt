They leaned against the door, gasping for breath, the adrenaline still coursing through their veins, leaving a metallic taste in their mouths. The air inside was thick with dust and the scent of decay, but it was quiet, a fragile bubble of silence in the heart of the storm.

“What now?” Lucky managed to ask, his voice hoarse, his eyes scanning the dark, cavernous interior of the building.

“We find a way out,” Astra replied, her voice regaining some of its usual clarity. “And we figure out what that ‘nuke’ is. Because if it’s real, we have bigger problems than just getting out of here.” Her gaze was fixed on him, a silent challenge, a shared understanding of the terrifying implications of the word.

Suddenly, Astra’s eyes darted upwards, a flicker of something unreadable in their depths. She grabbed Lucky again, her hand finding his, pulling him towards a rickety staircase that seemed to spiral into the darkness above. “Up here!” she urged, her voice urgent, a new note of alarm in her tone.
