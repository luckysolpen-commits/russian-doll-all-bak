"And you are?" Lucky countered, his voice steady despite the sudden surge of adrenaline. He had faced down corporate raiders and hostile investors, but this felt different. This felt… official, and far more dangerous.

“Agent Thompson, CIA,” she stated, her eyes flicking to Astra for a fraction of a second before returning to Lucky. “We need to talk. Privately.” The emphasis on the last word was pointed, a clear dismissal of Astra.

Astra, however, was not easily dismissed. She stepped forward, her chin raised. “Anything you have to say to Lucky, you can say to me. We have no secrets.”

Thompson’s gaze hardened, a subtle tightening around her eyes. “I assure you, what I have to say is for Mr. Shepherd’s ears only. Unless you’d prefer we discuss it in a more… public forum?” Her tone was a veiled threat, delivered with a chilling politeness that made it all the more potent. The implication was clear: she had information, and she wasn’t afraid to use it.

Lucky, sensing the gravity of the situation, the sheer, unyielding force of the agency standing before them, nodded to Astra. “It’s alright, Astra. I’ll handle this.” He squeezed her arm reassuringly, though his own heart hammered against his ribs.
