Hours later, the ranch house, usually a sanctuary of peace, felt like a cage. The quiet evening, once a comforting embrace, was now suffocating, heavy with the unspoken threats from Loona Twilight’s visit. Lucky and Astra sat in the living room, the remnants of their dinner untouched, their minds reeling from the cryptic warning. Gem, who had been with them earlier, had quietly excused herself, her absence adding another layer to the unsettling atmosphere.

Lucky ran a hand through his greying hair, his gaze fixed on the now inert package on the coffee table. It looked so innocuous, so ordinary, yet it had been the conduit for a message that had shattered his carefully constructed peace. “SOL,” he muttered, the name tasting foreign on his tongue. “Who are they, really? And what could they possibly want with me?”

Astra, ever the pragmatist, picked up the package, turning it over in her hands. “Loona said they’ve been watching you for a long time. And that you possess something they believe is theirs.” Her voice was low, thoughtful. “You said you sold everything, disconnected from it all. Is there anything, Lucky? Anything at all you held back?”

Lucky shook his head, a frustrated sigh escaping him. “No. I liquidated every asset, every patent, every last piece of intellectual property. I wanted out, Astra. Completely. I wanted this.” He gestured around the comfortable, rustic living room, a stark contrast to the glittering towers of his past empire. “This quiet. This freedom.”

But the freedom felt illusory now, a fragile bubble about to burst. The thought of being watched, of unseen eyes scrutinizing his every move, sent a shiver down his spine. He had always been the one watching, the one anticipating, the one in control. The reversal of roles was deeply unsettling.
