She leaned in, her voice dropping to a conspiratorial whisper, as if sharing a profound secret, though her eyes remained as cold as ice. “A group of terrorists has gotten their hands on some… sensitive equipment. Equipment that could destabilize global security if it falls into the wrong hands. We need someone to infiltrate their organization and steal it back for us. Someone who can operate outside the usual channels. Someone who can disappear and reappear without a trace. Someone like you.”

Lucky stared at her, trapped. The walls of his peaceful life, his carefully constructed retirement, crumbled around him. The choice was no choice at all. He could fight, expose his past, and face the full, unyielding force of the CIA, or he could comply, become their pawn, and perhaps, just perhaps, protect Astra and the life they had built. The weight of his past, and the terrifying uncertainty of his forced future, settled heavily on his shoulders.

“What do you want me to do?” Lucky asked, the words a bitter taste in his mouth, a surrender he never thought he’d make.

Thompson’s smile widened, a chilling triumph that didn’t quite reach her eyes. “Excellent. I knew you’d see reason. We’ll provide you with the details. You leave tomorrow.” She turned, her agents already moving towards the door, their mission accomplished. Lucky watched them go, the silence they left behind even heavier than their presence. His peaceful life was over. The game, it seemed, had not only found him again, but had dragged him back into its most dangerous arena.
