Astra hesitated, her concern palpable, her eyes pleading with Lucky to reconsider. But she knew him. When he made a decision, especially one he believed protected her, he was unmovable. Reluctantly, she retreated, but stayed within earshot, her presence a silent sentinel, her worry a tangible weight in the air.

Thompson stepped inside, her gaze sweeping over the comfortable, unassuming living room, a hint of disdain in her eyes. The rustic charm, the lived-in warmth, seemed to offend her clinical sensibilities. She pulled out a sleek, government-issue tablet, its screen glowing with an internal light that seemed to mock the soft lamplight of the room. Her fingers, long and precise, swiped through files with practiced ease.

“Let’s not waste time, Mr. Shepherd,” Thompson began, her voice flat, devoid of any preamble. “We know about your… extracurricular activities. The offshore accounts. The shell corporations. The unsanctioned tech development. Even the little incident in Monaco with the data heist. Impressive, really. For a civilian.” Her words were delivered with a casual precision that was far more terrifying than any shout. Each phrase was a carefully aimed dart, striking at the hidden vulnerabilities Lucky had believed were buried deep, lost forever.
