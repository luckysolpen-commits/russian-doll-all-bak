The training room was vast, a cavernous expanse of polished chrome and shimmering holographic projections, yet it hummed with an almost artistic energy. It was here, in the heart of Lunaria, that Lucky Shepherd stood, the SolPen now a tangible weight in his hand. It felt lighter than he expected, almost an extension of his own will, a natural fit despite its otherworldly origin. The low, constant hum of the lunar base, a sound that had become a familiar backdrop to their new reality, seemed to intensify, resonating with the nascent power he felt stirring within the device.

Sol stood beside him, a silent, imposing presence. His masked face, devoid of expression, offered no clues to his thoughts, yet his instructions were precise, concise, delivered with an unwavering authority that brooked no argument. “Focus your intent, Lucky. Visualize the object. The SolPen translates thought into form.” His voice, a deep, resonant baritone, seemed to echo in the vast space, each word a command.

Lucky closed his eyes, picturing a simple cube. He focused, not just on the image, but on the *idea* of the cube, its perfect geometry, its solid form. He extended the SolPen. A shimmering, translucent cube materializes in the air before him, then solidifying into a perfect, metallic form, catching the artificial light of the training room. It was flawless, a testament to the device’s incredible precision.
