**Chapter 10: The SolPen's Power**

The training room was vast, a cavernous expanse of polished chrome and shimmering holographic projections, yet it hummed with an almost artistic energy. It was here, in the heart of Lunaria, that Lucky Shepherd stood, the SolPen now a tangible weight in his hand. It felt lighter than he expected, almost an extension of his own will, a natural fit despite its otherworldly origin. The low, constant hum of the lunar base, a sound that had become a familiar backdrop to their new reality, seemed to intensify, resonating with the nascent power he felt stirring within the device.

Sol stood beside him, a silent, imposing presence. His masked face, devoid of expression, offered no clues to his thoughts, yet his instructions were precise, concise, delivered with an unwavering authority that brooked no argument. “Focus your intent, Lucky. Visualize the object. The SolPen translates thought into form.” His voice, a deep, resonant baritone, seemed to echo in the vast space, each word a command.

Lucky closed his eyes, picturing a simple cube. He focused, not just on the image, but on the *idea* of the cube, its perfect geometry, its solid form. He extended the SolPen, a shimmering, translucent cube materializing in the air before him, then solidifying into a perfect, metallic form, catching the artificial light of the training room. It was flawless, a testament to the device’s incredible precision.

“Incredible,” Lucky breathed, a genuine sense of awe washing over him. He had built empires with technology, but this… this was something else entirely. This was creation from pure thought, a power that bordered on the divine.

“A rudimentary beginning,” Sol replied, his voice flat, unimpressed by Lucky’s wonder. “The SolPen draws its power from… unique sources. The collective consciousness of the clones. Their very essence.”

Lucky’s gaze snapped to the clones in the room, their identical faces impassive, their movements synchronized. A chilling understanding dawned on him. Their “souls” were the fuel for this device. It was a horrifying thought, a violation of every ethical boundary he had ever known, yet undeniably powerful. The implications were staggering, a moral dilemma that would haunt him, even as he wielded the power.

A MONTAGE of Lucky’s training began, a blur of intense learning and rapid mastery. Days bled into weeks, weeks into months, as he delved deeper into the mysteries of the SolPen, his innate genius finding its ultimate canvas.

He created increasingly complex objects: intricate gears that meshed with perfect precision, delicate machinery that hummed with unseen energy, even small, functional drones that buzzed around the room, their miniature rotors whirring softly. He learned to manipulate existing matter, reshaping and reconfiguring it with a flick of the wrist, a mere thought. A pile of scrap metal could be transformed into a gleaming, functional tool; a broken circuit board could be re-woven into a new, more efficient design. The possibilities seemed endless.

He practiced with Astra, who, while not wielding a SolPen herself, assisted him with technical specifications and design. She was fascinated by the device, her scientific mind eager to understand its mechanics, its underlying principles. She would sketch out complex designs, her brow furrowed in concentration, and Lucky would bring them to life with the SolPen, a seamless collaboration between human ingenuity and alien technology. Their shared passion for creation, for understanding the universe, deepened their bond.

He trained with the elite clone forces, using the SolPen to create defensive barriers that shimmered with energy, deflecting simulated attacks with ease. He conjured offensive projectiles, bolts of pure energy that struck their targets with devastating force. He even learned to create temporary structures in simulated combat scenarios, bridges that spanned impossible chasms, walls that rose from the ground to provide cover, all with a mere thought and a flick of the wrist. His movements became fluid, confident, a natural extension of the device.

As his training progressed, he received the HALO BIO SOFTWARE HUD, a sleek, almost invisible device that integrated directly with his optic nerve. It overlaid digital information onto his vision, a constant stream of data that enhanced his perception. He could see the SolPen’s energy levels, the structural integrity of his creations, and even potential weaknesses in his opponents, all displayed as a translucent overlay on his field of vision. It was like having a supercomputer directly linked to his brain, an unparalleled advantage.

He then donned the LIGHT EXO SUIT, a form-fitting, lightweight armor that enhanced his speed, strength, and agility, while also providing a direct neural link to the SolPen. He moved like a phantom, a blur of enhanced human capability, his every thought translated into action, his every movement amplified. He was no longer just Lucky Shepherd; he was a living weapon, a force of nature, a testament to the terrifying potential of the SolPen.

During the montage, subtle hints of Gem’s true nature were woven in. She was often seen observing Lucky’s training, her expression unreadable, a silent shadow in the periphery. Perhaps a brief, almost imperceptible communication with Sol, a shared glance that spoke volumes, a silent acknowledgment of a deeper, hidden agenda. She was a spy, gathering intelligence, her loyalty to Sol unwavering, her presence a constant, unsettling reminder that not everything was as it seemed in this hidden lunar society.

Weeks turned into months. Lucky’s mastery of the SolPen was undeniable. He was no longer just a tech mogul; he was a force of creation, a living weapon, a man who could shape reality itself. But with this power came a growing unease, a nagging suspicion that Sol’s intentions were not as benevolent as they seemed. The price of this power, he realized, might be far greater than he could ever imagine.
