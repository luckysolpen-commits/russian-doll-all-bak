INT. ARCHITECT'S UNDERGROUND MINE CAMP - AFRICA - DAY

Lucky, Gem, and Astra are in a smaller, more private chamber within the Architect's complex. The Architect stands before a holographic display, showing complex schematics of the Metal Sun and energy conduits.

ARCHITECT
(Calmly, gesturing to the display)
Loona's sacrifice, while tragic, was not in vain. Her interaction with the antimatter provided invaluable data. A catalyst, if you will, for the next stage of human evolution.

LUCKY
(Stepping forward, his voice tight with anger)
A catalyst? You're using her death to further your own twisted research?

ARCHITECT
(Turns to Lucky, his expression unreadable)
Progress often demands sacrifice, Lucky. I am merely accelerating the inevitable. Humanity is stagnant, bound by its limitations. I offer a path to transcendence, to merge with the very fabric of the universe.

GEM
(Her voice sharp)
By enslaving us to your grand design? By turning us into components of your Metal Sun?

ARCHITECT
(A faint, almost imperceptible smile)
Enslavement is a harsh word. Integration, perhaps. Imagine, Gem, a collective consciousness, unbound by physical form, experiencing the universe in its entirety.

ASTRA
(Her eyes flashing)
And what about free will? What about individuality?

ARCHITECT
(Shrugs slightly)
Minor considerations in the grand scheme. The universe is a symphony, Astra. And every note must play its part.

Lucky clenches his fists. Gem and Astra exchange a look of grim understanding. The Architect's true intentions are laid bare, and they are far more sinister than they had imagined.

FADE OUT.
