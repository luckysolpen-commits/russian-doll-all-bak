Their next class was a deeper dive
into the Soul Bank. This lesson was
not taught by the warm and encouraging
Professor Aurora, but by a being of
a very different sort. The professor was
a golem, the same golden golem they
had met at the bank. Its name
was Argent, and it did not speak
in melodies and metaphors, but in facts
and figures. "SOUL is the currency of
the multiverse," Argent began, its voice a
low, metallic rumble. "It is the measure
of creative potential, the fuel of all
creation. You have learned how to spend
it. Now, you will learn how to
earn it." The classroom was a stark,
utilitarian space, a sharp contrast to the
artistic chaos of Creation 101. The walls
were bare, the floor was cold, and
the only light came from the glowing
eyes of Argent. "You earn SOUL through
creation," Argent explained. "Every time you create
something new, something beautiful, something that adds
to the harmony of the universe, you
are rewarded with SOUL. The more complex,
the more original, the more powerful your
creation, the more SOUL you will receive."
It paused, its golden eyes scanning the
room. "But be warned," it continued, its
voice dropping to a low, ominous tone.
"The reverse is also true. If you
create something chaotic, something destructive, something that
disrupts the harmony of the universe, you
will lose SOUL. And if your SOUL
balance drops to zero, you will cease
to exist." A shiver went through the
room. This was a new and terrifying
thought. Their very existence was tied to
their creativity, to their ability to create
beauty and harmony. It was a heavy
burden, a great responsibility. But it was
also a powerful motivator. They had to
create. They had to be good. They
had to be... profitable. "Your creations are
not just works of art," Argent continued.
"They are assets. They have value. They
can be bought, sold, traded. The more
SOUL your creations generate, the more valuable
they become. And the more valuable you
become." It was a strange and unsettling
way to think about art, about creativity.
But it was the reality of The
Centroid. This was not just a school.
It was an economy. A marketplace of
ideas, of dreams, of souls. And they
were the entrepreneurs. They were the creators
of the next big thing. They were
the ones who would shape the future
of the multiverse, one creation at a
time. It was a daunting thought, but
it was also an exciting one. They
had the power to change the world,
to make a difference, to leave a
legacy. And they were ready to get
to work. They were ready to earn
some SOUL. The lesson continued with a
detailed explanation of the SOUL market, of
supply and demand, of investment and return.
It was a lot to take in, but
Lucky and Astra were quick studies. They
understood the system. They saw the potential.
They were ready to play the game.
And they were determined to win. They
would create the most beautiful, the most
powerful, the most profitable creations the multiverse
had ever seen. They would become legends.
They would become billionaires of SOUL. And
they would do it together. As they
always had. As they always would. For
they were Lucky and Astra. And they
were about to take the SOUL market
by storm.
