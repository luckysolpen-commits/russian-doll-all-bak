Just as they were about to depart,
a message came through from The Centroid.
It was a desperate, flickering transmission, the image
distorted by the growing influence of the
rift. It was Professor Aurora, her usually
radiant face pale and drawn, her golden
hair seeming to have lost some of
its luster. "We are holding on," she
said, her voice a strained whisper, "but
the darkness is spreading. The creative energy
of the school is at an all-time
low. The students are losing hope. The
sky is permanently gray. It is as
if the very soul of The Centroid
is being... erased." The image flickered, and
for a moment, they could see the
Great Hall behind her. It was no
longer the vibrant, bustling hub of creativity
they remembered. It was a silent, somber
place, the students sitting in listless groups,
their faces devoid of the usual spark
of imagination. The beautiful, domed ceiling, which
had once shown a swirling nebula of
colors, was now a dull, oppressive gray.
The sight was a punch to the
gut. It was a stark and painful
reminder of what they were fighting for.
This was not just an abstract battle
for the fate of the multiverse. This
was a fight for their home, for
their friends, for their way of life.
This was personal. "We have faith in
you," Professor Aurora continued, her voice regaining
a flicker of its usual warmth and
strength. "We know you will not fail
us. You are the children of The
Centroid. You are our light in this
darkness. You are our hope." The transmission
cut out, leaving behind a heavy silence
in the war room of the VINIX.
The team looked at each other, their
faces a mixture of sadness, of anger,
of a new and powerful resolve. They
had seen the despair in the eyes
of their friends. They had seen the
darkness that was consuming their home. And
it had lit a fire in their souls.
A fire of righteous anger. A fire
of fierce determination. A fire that would
not be extinguished. "Let's go," Astra said,
her voice a low, dangerous hum. "Let's
go get that seed. And let's go
save our home." The mood on the
VINIX had changed. The quiet contemplation, the
artistic theorizing, the gentle camaraderie, it was
all gone. Replaced by a cold, hard,
and focused rage. They were no longer
just artists and dreamers. They were warriors.
They were avengers. They were a storm
of creative fury that was about to
be unleashed upon the forces of nothingness.
The Nothing had made a grave mistake.
It had threatened their home. It had
hurt their friends. It had pissed them
off. And it was about to pay
the price. The VINIX, sensing the change
in its passengers, began to hum with
a new and dangerous energy. Its starlight
feathers shimmered with a predatory glow, its
eyes of wisdom burned with a cold,
hard light. It was no longer just
a ship, a guide, a guardian. It
was a warbird. And it was ready
for battle. The journey to the second
Angler Seed was no longer just a
quest. It was a rescue mission. It
was a declaration of war. It was
a promise of retribution. And it was
a promise they intended to keep. The
final preparations were complete. The team was
united. The mission was clear. The enemy
had been identified. And the battle for
the future of creation was about to
begin in earnest.
