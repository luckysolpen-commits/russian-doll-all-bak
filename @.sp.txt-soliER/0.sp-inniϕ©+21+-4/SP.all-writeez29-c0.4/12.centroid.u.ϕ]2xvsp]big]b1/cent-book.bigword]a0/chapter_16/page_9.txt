And what of Sir Reginald Fluffernutter the
Third? The chaotic and beloved mascot of
The Centroid? He, too, had a role
to play in the new, balanced universe.
He was the court jester, the holy
fool, the eternal reminder that even in
a universe of perfect harmony, there is
always room for a little bit of
nonsense. He was the embodiment of the
chaos that had leaked from the forbidden
book, a living, breathing, and incredibly annoying
testament to the power of the absurd.
He would spend his days causing minor
havoc throughout The Centroid. He would steal
the headmistress's favorite quill, he would redecorate
the Great Hall in a style that
can only be described as "cosmic kitsch,"
and he would sing his terrible, off-key
songs at the most inappropriate of moments.
He was a constant source of frustration,
of amusement, of a deep and abiding
love. For the students of The Centroid
understood that Sir Reginald was more than
just a pet. He was a teacher.
A teacher of the most important lesson
of all. The lesson of not taking
yourself too seriously. The lesson of finding
the joy in the ridiculous. The lesson
of embracing the beautiful and chaotic messiness
of life. He was a furry, feathery,
and utterly nonsensical guru of the absurd.
And he was an essential part of
the new balance. For a universe without
a singing space-poodle-chicken is a universe that
is not truly whole. A universe that
has forgotten how to laugh. A universe
that has taken itself a little too
seriously. And that, the children of The
Centroid had learned, was the most dangerous
kind of imbalance of all. The final
legacy of their story was not just
a new way to love, or a
new way to create, or a new
way to live. It was also a
new way to laugh. A laugh that
was born not of ridicule, but of
a deep and abiding affection for the
strange and beautiful and wonderfully absurd universe
in which we live. A laugh that
was the final, and most joyful, note
in their symphony of salvation. A laugh
that would echo through the ages. A
laugh that was the very sound of
a universe that was, for the first
time, truly, deeply, and ridiculously happy.
