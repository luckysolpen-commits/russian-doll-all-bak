The final part of their orientation was
perhaps the most important: the Sorting.
Just like the famous wizarding school they
kept being reminded of, The Centroid had
Houses. These were not just dormitories, but
communities, families, each with its own distinct
identity and purpose. The lore they had
been given, a small, leather-bound book titled
"#.olldump-post]HOUSES.txt", described them in detail.
There were five Houses in total, each
named after a constellation and a corresponding
mythical creature. First was Luminari, the House
of the Silver Fish, for the advanced
and the gifted. This was where Lucky
and Astra were expected to be placed.
Then there was Cirrope, the House of
the Rainbow Fish, for the popular, the
charismatic, and the carefree. Aetheria, the House
of the Blue Oyster, was for the normies,
the go-betweens, the ones who kept the
peace between the other Houses. Khthonoi, the
House of the Piranhas, was for the
ambitious, the cunning, and the... well, the
evil. And finally, there was Lethari, the
House of the Prawns, for the misfits,
the outcasts, the ones who marched to
the beat of a different drum. The
Sorting was not done by a talking
hat, but by a large, ornate Orrery
in the center of the main hall.
The Orrery was a magnificent contraption of
gold and silver, with planets and stars
that moved in a complex, celestial dance.
Each student would stand in the center
of the Orrery, and the planets would
align themselves, revealing the student's true nature,
their deepest desires, their ultimate destiny. It
was a moment of truth, a moment
of revelation, a moment that would define
their time at The Centroid. Lucky and
Astra stood in line, their hearts pounding
with a mixture of excitement and anxiety.
They were confident they would be placed
in Luminari, but there was still a
tiny sliver of doubt, a small voice
in the back of their minds that
whispered, "What if?" What if they weren't
as advanced as they thought? What if
they were placed in Lethari, the House
of the misfits? What if they were
separated? The thought was unbearable. They had
been together for millennia. They couldn't be
separated now. They needed each other. They
were a team. They were two halves
of a whole. They were Lucky and
Astra. And they would face this together,
as they always had. It was their
turn. They stepped into the center of
the Orrery, hand in hand. The planets
began to move, their celestial dance a
silent symphony of fate. The air grew
thick with anticipation, the silence broken only
by the soft hum of the Orrery.
And then, the planets aligned. A single,
bright star shone down on them, its
golden light a beacon of destiny. The
headmaster, a tall, imposing figure with a
long, white beard, smiled. "Luminari!" he boomed,
his voice echoing through the hall. A
wave of relief washed over them, so
powerful it almost brought them to their
knees. They had done it. They were
in Luminari. They were together. They were
home. The students of Luminari cheered, their
voices a chorus of welcome. They were
surrounded by their new family, their new
friends, their new peers. They were a
part of something bigger now. They were
a part of Luminari. They were a
part of The Centroid. And they were
ready to begin their new life. Together.
