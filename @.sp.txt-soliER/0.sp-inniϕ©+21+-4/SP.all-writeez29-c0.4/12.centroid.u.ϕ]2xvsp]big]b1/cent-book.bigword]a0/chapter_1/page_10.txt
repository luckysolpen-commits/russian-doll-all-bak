Their new home was a tower of
white marble and glass, its spire reaching
for the very stars themselves. It was
a place of light, of knowledge, of
wisdom. It was Luminari House. The inside
was just as breathtaking as the outside.
The common room was a vast, open
space with a domed ceiling that showed
the night sky in all its glory.
The furniture was made of polished silver
and soft, white velvet, and the air
was filled with the scent of old
books and fresh flowers. It was a
place of peace, of tranquility, of contemplation.
It was a place for scholars, for
artists, for dreamers. It was a place
for them. Their room was at the
very top of the tower, with a
balcony that overlooked the entire school. The
view was spectacular. They could see the
other Houses, the library, the greenhouse, the
quidditch pitch (or whatever the Centroid equivalent
was). They could see the entire world
of The Centroid laid out before them,
a map of their new life. The room
itself was simple but elegant, with two
beds, two desks, and a large window
that let in the light of the
stars. It was a blank canvas, waiting
for them to make it their own.
They unpacked their few belongings, their movements
slow and deliberate. They were savoring this
moment, this feeling of peace, of belonging,
of home. They had been wanderers for
so long, adrift in the sea of
eternity. But now, they had an anchor.
They had a home. They had each
other. And that was all that mattered.
As they settled in, they began to
meet their new housemates. They were a
diverse group, from all corners of the
multiverse, but they all had one thing
in common: a spark of genius, a
passion for creation, a thirst for knowledge.
There was a girl who could paint
with light, a boy who could build
entire cities out of thought, and a
non-binary being who could communicate with
the stars. They were all brilliant, all
talented, all a little bit strange. And
Lucky and Astra fit right in. They
spent the evening talking, sharing stories, laughing.
They felt a sense of camaraderie that
they had never felt before. They were
not just housemates. They were friends. They
were family. They were Luminari. As the
night wore on, they found themselves on
the balcony, looking out at the stars.
The sky was clear, and the constellations
shone with a brilliance they had never
seen before. They could see Andromeda, Pegasus,
Aquarius, Scorpius, and Cetus, the constellations that
gave their Houses their names. They were
a part of that celestial dance now,
a part of that cosmic story. They
were home. And they were happy. For
the first time in a very long
time, they were truly happy. They had
a purpose. They had a community. They
had a future. And they were ready
to face it. Together. As they always
had. As they always would. For they
were Lucky and Astra. And their adventure
had just begun. The call to The
Centroid had been answered. And their lives
would never be the same again. And
they wouldn't have it any other way.
