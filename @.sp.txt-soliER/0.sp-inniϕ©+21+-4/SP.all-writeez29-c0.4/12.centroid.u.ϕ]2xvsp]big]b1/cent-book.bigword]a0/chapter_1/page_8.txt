The concept was both fascinating and terrifying.
To trade a piece of your soul,
a part of your very being, for
something as abstract as "SOUL" was
a daunting prospect. But they were not
ones to back down from a challenge.
They were curious. They were brave. They
were ready to see what this new
currency could buy. Lucky went first. He
closed his eyes, his mind searching for
a memory to offer. He had so
many, a vast ocean of them stretching
back for millennia. He chose a small
one, a memory of a sunset on
a forgotten world, a moment of perfect
peace and solitude. He placed his hand
on the scale, and he could feel
the memory being drawn from him, a
tiny wisp of light and color. The
scale dipped, and a number appeared on
the golem's chest: 100. "You have 100
SOUL," the golem said. "Spend it wisely."
Lucky felt a strange sense of loss,
a tiny hole in the fabric of
his being where the memory used to
be. But he also felt a sense
of power, of potential, of a new
kind of wealth. He had SOUL.
And he was ready to use it.
Astra went next. She offered a dream,
a recurring dream of flying through a
sky of liquid silver. The scale dipped,
and the number 150 appeared on the
golem's chest. She had more SOUL than
Lucky. A friendly competition was already brewing
between them. They left the Soul Bank,
their minds reeling with the implications of
this new economy. They had a new
currency, a new way of measuring wealth,
a new way of interacting with the
world. It was a lot to take
in, but they were excited to see
what they could do with their newfound
SOUL. Their next stop was a shop
called "Tear It Up," a place that
sold "Tear It Cards." The name was
catchy, and the shop itself was a
chaotic explosion of color and creativity. The
walls were covered in torn pieces of
paper, each one a work of art
in its own right. The shopkeeper was
a small, energetic creature with skin like
bark and hair like moss. He introduced
himself as Pipsqueak, and he was very
enthusiastic about his wares. "Welcome, welcome!"
he chirped, his voice like the rustling
of leaves. "Come in, come in! You
look like you could use some Tear
It Cards!" He showed them the cards,
which were small, blank pieces of paper
with a single, perforated line down the
middle. "You write your desire on one
side of the card," he explained, his
eyes twinkling with mischief. "And then you
tear it! The energy of your desire
is released into the universe, and if
you're lucky, it will come true!" It
sounded like a simple child's game, but
they knew there was more to it
than that. This was The Centroid, after
all. Nothing was as simple as it
seemed. They bought a pack of cards,
their SOUL balance decreasing slightly. They were
eager to try them out, to see
what kind of magic they could create
with a simple piece of paper and
a heartfelt desire. They were learning the
rules of this new world, the tools
of this new trade, the currency of
this new economy. And they were having
the time of their lives. They were
students. They were creators. They were explorers.
And they were just getting started. The
adventure was just beginning to unfold, and
they were ready for whatever came next.
