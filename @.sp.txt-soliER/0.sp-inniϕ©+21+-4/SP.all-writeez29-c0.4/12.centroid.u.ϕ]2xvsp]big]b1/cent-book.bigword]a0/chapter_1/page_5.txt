The invitation had instructed them to report
to Diagonal Alley. The name was a
clear, almost comical, nod to a certain
famous wizarding world, and it brought a
smile to their faces. It seemed The
Centroid had a sense of humor. Finding
the alley, however, was a challenge in
itself. There were no signs, no maps,
no helpful wizards to guide them. It
was a test, they realized, the first
of many. They had to prove they
were worthy of The Centroid, that they
had the ingenuity and the intuition to
find their own way. They wandered the
city for hours, their frustration growing with
each passing moment. They were about to
give up, to admit defeat, when Astra
stopped, her head tilted to one side.
"Do you feel that?" she asked, her
voice a low whisper. Lucky paused, his
senses on high alert. And then he
felt it too. A faint hum, a
subtle vibration in the air, a pull
towards something unseen. It was the same
energy they had felt from the box,
from the Soul Pen. It was the
energy of creation. They followed the feeling,
their steps quickening with a renewed sense
of purpose. It led them to a
nondescript brick wall in a forgotten corner
of the city. There was nothing to
indicate that this was the entrance to
a magical alley, nothing to distinguish it
from any other wall in the city.
But they knew. They could feel it.
This was the place. "So, how do
we get in?" Lucky asked, his eyes
scanning the wall for any hidden levers
or buttons. Astra smiled, a mischievous
glint in her eye. "I have an
idea," she said. She took out
the Soul Pen, its golden light a
beacon in the dim alley. She didn't
draw anything, she didn't write anything. She
simply held the pen up to the
wall, its nib pointed at the center
of a particular brick. The brick began
to glow, to pulse with the same
golden light as the pen. And then,
slowly, silently, it began to move. The
bricks shifted, rearranged themselves, and then folded
in on themselves, revealing a dark, narrow
passageway. The air that flowed out was
thick with the scent of old books,
of strange spices, of magic. It was
the scent of Diagonal Alley. They looked
at each other, a shared smile of
triumph on their faces. They had done
it. They had passed the first test.
They had found their way. With a
deep breath, they stepped through the opening,
leaving the mundane world behind and entering
the world of The Centroid. The passageway
was dark and narrow, but it was
not frightening. It was filled with a
sense of wonder, of anticipation, of magic.
They could hear the sounds of the
alley ahead, the chatter of strange creatures,
the clanging of hammers on anvils, the
hum of magical devices. It was a
symphony of the strange and the wonderful,
a promise of the adventures to come.
And they were ready for it. They
were ready for Diagonal Alley. They were
ready for The Centroid. They were ready
for anything.
