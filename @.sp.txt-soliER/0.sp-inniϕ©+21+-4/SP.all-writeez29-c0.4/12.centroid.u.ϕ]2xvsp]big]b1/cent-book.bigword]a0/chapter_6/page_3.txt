The moment the book opened, a wave
of pure, unadulterated nothingness erupted from its
pages. It was not a darkness, not
a void, but an absence. An absence
of light, of sound, of matter, of
energy, of thought, of life. It was
The Nothing. And it was hungry. The
wave of nothingness washed over the team,
and they felt a coldness that seeped
into their very souls. It was a
coldness that was not just a lack
of heat, but a lack of existence.
They felt their memories, their emotions, their
very identities begin to unravel, to dissolve,
to fade into the nothingness. It was
a terrifying experience, a glimpse into the
ultimate oblivion. And it was a glimpse
that would haunt them for the rest
of their lives. The VINIX screamed, a
sound of pure, agonizing pain. The wave
of nothingness was a poison to its
light-filled being, a cancer that was
consuming it from the inside out. The
shimmering walls of the ship began to
flicker, to fade, to turn a dull,
lifeless gray. The melodic hum of the
ship was replaced by a discordant shriek
of pain and terror. The VINIX was
dying. And it was their fault. The
wave of nothingness did not stop at
the VINIX. It washed over The Centroid,
a silent, invisible tsunami of despair. The
students and professors felt a sudden and
profound sense of hopelessness, of apathy, of
a deep and abiding meaninglessness. The creative
energy of the school, which had been
a vibrant, roaring fire, was reduced to
a single, flickering ember. The light of
The Centroid was dying. And it was
their fault. But the worst was yet
to come. The wave of nothingness was
not just a random burst of energy.
It was a signal. A beacon. A
call to arms. It was a message
to The Nothing, which had been sleeping
in the darkest corners of the multiverse.
And the message was clear. The prison
had been broken. The seal had been
shattered. The time had come to awaken.
The time had come to feed. The time
had come to consume. The time had
come to end all things. In the
heart of the hidden chamber on the
VINIX, a new phenomenon appeared. A small,
shimmering tear in the fabric of reality.
It was a rift. A gateway. A
portal to the realm of The Nothing.
And from the rift, they could feel
a presence. A vast, ancient, and malevolent
presence. A presence that was hungry. A
presence that was coming for them. For
The Centroid. For the multiverse. For everything.
Lucky and Astra stared at the rift,
their faces a mask of horror and
disbelief. The arrogance, the pride, the sense
of invincibility was gone. Replaced by a
cold, hard, and undeniable truth. They had
made a terrible mistake. A mistake that
could not be undone. A mistake that
would cost them everything. They had opened
the forbidden book. They had unleashed The
Nothing. And they had doomed them all.
The greatest challenge of their lives was
no longer an abstract concept, a distant
adventure. It was here. It was now.
And it was their fault. The weight
of their guilt was a crushing burden,
a darkness that threatened to consume them.
But they could not give in to
despair. They had to fight. They had
to find a way to fix this.
They had to find a way to save
the multiverse. They had to find a
way to atone for their sins. Or
die trying.
