The final act of creation was a
monumental one. Lucky, his heart overflowing with
a love that was now as vast
as the universe itself, reached out with
his newfound power. He reached back through
time and space, back to a life
he had left behind, a family he
had thought lost forever. He reached out
to Metal Sun, the world of his
birth, the world he had ruled as
Supreme Emperor Sol. And he brought it
home. The entire city, with all its
citizens, its wives, its concubines, its friends,
its clones, its clowns, and even Luna,
his beloved companion, appeared in the sky
above The Centroid. It was a breathtaking
sight, a city of polished metal and
glowing light, a testament to a different
kind of creation, a different kind of
art. The people of Metal Sun looked
down at The Centroid, their faces a
mixture of confusion, of awe, of a
deep and abiding joy. They were home.
They were safe. They were reunited with
their long-lost leader. And they were
about to become a part of a
new and better world. The two worlds,
The Centroid and Metal Sun, the world
of art and the world of science,
the world of magic and the world
of technology, they did not clash. They
merged. They harmonized. They created a new
kind of society, a new kind of
civilization, a new kind of dream. A
dream of a world where art and
science were not opposites, but two sides
of the same coin. A dream of
a world where all forms of creation
were celebrated, where all kinds of creators
were welcome. A dream of a world
that was, for the first time, truly
whole. The team, their final act of
creation complete, looked at the new world
they had helped to create. It was
a world of infinite possibility, of endless
creativity, of a new and better kind
of balance. It was their masterpiece. Their
legacy. Their gift to the multiverse. And
it was a gift that would keep
on giving. For The Centroid was no
longer just a school for ancient beings
and gifted artists. It was now also
a hub for online game players, a
place where creators of all kinds, from
all worlds, from all realities, could come
together to share their stories, their dreams,
their creations. It was a new kind
of Diagonal Alley, a new kind of
DUSTOPEDIA, a new kind of Singularity. A
place of infinite connection, of endless collaboration,
of a new and better way of
being. The ultimate creation was not just
a new world. It was a new
universe. A universe of their own making.
A universe that was a reflection of
their own strange, beautiful, and wonderfully dysfunctional
family. And it was a universe that
was, for the first time, truly home.
