Their time in DUSTOPEDIA had changed them.
They had faced their inner demons, they
had healed a world, and they had
gained a universe of knowledge. But their
mission in Dustopia was not yet complete.
They had healed the heart of the
realm, but the people of Dustopia were
still lost in a fog of apathy
and despair. The creative energy of the
realm was still being hoarded by a
group of corrupt oligarchs who had grown
fat and powerful on the stolen dreams
of their people. Lucky and Astra and
their team had to set the magic
dust free. They had to bring the
light of creation back to the people.
They had to start a revolution. They
left the library, their minds buzzing with
new knowledge, their hearts filled with a
new sense of purpose. The Keeper of
Balance led them to the edge of
the vortex, the swirling dust parting to
let them pass. They stepped out of
the library and back into the gray,
dusty landscape of Dustopia. But the landscape
was no longer gray. The healing of
the heart had begun to spread, like
a ripple in a pond. The dust
was now tinged with subtle shades of
color, the air was filled with a
faint, sweet fragrance, and the silence was
broken by a soft, melodic hum. Dustopia
was coming back to life. But the
people of Dustopia were still trapped in
their gray, colorless towns, their lives devoid
of creativity, of joy, of hope. The
oligarchs had built their cities on the
foundations of despair, and they would not
give up their power easily. The team
made their way to the largest of
these towns, a sprawling metropolis of gray
concrete and steel. The town was a
monument to conformity, to mediocrity, to the
death of the imagination. The people moved
like automatons, their faces blank, their eyes
empty. They were a people who had
forgotten how to dream. The oligarchs lived
in a towering fortress at the center
of the town, a fortress built of
stolen dreams and broken promises. They were
guarded by an army of mindless drones,
their bodies made of dust and their
hearts filled with nothingness. It was a
formidable challenge. But the team was not
afraid. They had a plan. They would
not fight the oligarchs with force. They
would fight them with creativity. They would
not destroy their fortress. They would transform
it. They would not defeat their army.
They would awaken it. They would use
the lessons they had learned in Dustopia,
the knowledge they had gained in DUSTOPEDIA,
the power they had discovered within themselves.
They would use the power of balance,
of harmony, of creation. They would use
the power of art. They would use
the power of music. They would use
the power of stories. They would use
the power of dreams. They would start
a creative revolution. And they would set
the people of Dustopia free. The final
battle for the soul of Dustopia was
about to begin. And it was a
battle that would be fought not with
swords and shields, but with Soul Pens
and Tear It Cards. It was a battle
that would be won not by the
strongest army, but by the most beautiful
idea. It was a battle that would
change the world. Forever.
