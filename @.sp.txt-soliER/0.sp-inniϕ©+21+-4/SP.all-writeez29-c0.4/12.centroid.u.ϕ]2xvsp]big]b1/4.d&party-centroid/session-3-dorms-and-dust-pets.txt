# Title: "Dorms and Dust Pets"
# Date: April 11, 2026

# Continuity: Follows Session 2 — the 4-party has arrived, toured the campus, and witnessed the $29.99 SoulPen revelation

## SESSION TRANSCRIPT:

**MOTHER TMOJIZU:** The morning after the Centroid tour dawned in shades of gold and violet. The orbiting shooting star cast long, warm light across the obsidian platform, and the fuzzpet asteroids drifted lazily overhead — a whale-shaped one today, its back covered in a miniature rainforest, waterfalls cascading from its edges into nothingness. Dust pets fluttered between the crystal bridges, chirping in Golden Ratio frequencies, leaving trails of stardust that smelled like possibility. The 4-party stood in the central courtyard, their Tear It starter packs clutched in their hands, still processing everything they'd seen. Professor Pylorus approached, her silver hair catching the light, her robes shifting between deep blue and violet.

**PROFESSOR PYLORUS:** Welcome to your first full day at the Centroid. Before we begin your training, you need homes. Each of you will be sorted into a house — based on your personality, your skills, and your soul frequency resonance. Your house will be your family, your support system, and your team for Stardust Ball. *she smiles* Yes, Horli. Stardust Ball.

**HORLI:** *perking up immediately* Stardust Ball?! The sport with fuzzpets on vacuums?! I've been waiting my whole life for this and I didn't even know it existed! Tell me more. Tell me everything. Do I get a vacuum? Does my fuzzpet get a vacuum? Do I get a fuzzpet AND a vacuum?

**PROFESSOR PYLORUS:** *laughs* All will be explained. First, the sorting. *she pulls out a small crystal orb — not the Soul Oracle, but a smaller, personal version. It pulses with a soft, warm light.* Step forward when I call your name. The orb will read your soul frequency and assign your house.

**MOTHER TMOJIZU:** She turned to CHARA first.

**PROFESSOR PYLORUS:** CHARA. Ex-CIA. Tactical mind. Paranoid but brilliant. You see threats everywhere, but you also see solutions no one else can. Step forward.

**MOTHER TMOJIZU:** CHARA stepped forward cautiously, her hand instinctively near her concealed shortsword. She placed her hand on the crystal orb. It pulsed — once, twice — then flared with deep black and crimson light. The color of obsidian and blood. The color of intensity, strategy, and misunderstood brilliance.

**PROFESSOR PYLORUS:** Khthonoi. The Black Piranhas. *CHARA's eyes narrow slightly.* Don't let the name fool you. Khthonoi students are the most strategic, the most intense, the most fiercely loyal once they trust you. Your tower is black obsidian with red accents. Tactical training rooms on every floor. A library of forbidden knowledge in the basement. You'll fit right in.

**CHARA:** *taking her hand off the orb* Khthonoi. Black and red. Tactical. Forbidden knowledge. *a small smile* I already like it. Do the rooms have locks?

**PROFESSOR PYLORUS:** *smiling* Every room has three. Next: IQabella.

**MOTHER TMOJIZU:** IQabella stepped forward, her dust pet — the sparrow-sized one from yesterday — perched on her shoulder, chirping softly. She placed her hand on the orb. It pulsed gently, then glowed with soft blue and purple light. The color of twilight, of calm waters, of bridges between worlds.

**PROFESSOR PYLORUS:** Aetheria. The Blue Oysters. Balanced, mediators, go-betweens. Aetheria students are the heart of the Centroid — they connect houses, resolve conflicts, and make sure everyone feels welcome. Your tower is blue and purple crystal, with meditation gardens, tea rooms, and communal spaces on every floor. You'll love it.

**IQABELLA:** *tearing up slightly* Tea rooms? On every floor? *her dust pet chirps approvingly* I'm home. I'm actually home. Thank you, Professor.

**PROFESSOR PYLORUS:** *warmly* You're welcome, dear. Next: Horli Qwin.

**MOTHER TMOJIZU:** Horli practically bounced forward, her energy already making the nearby dust pets flutter in excitement. She slapped her hand on the orb without hesitation. It exploded with rainbow light — every color at once, shifting, dancing, pulsing to a rhythm only Horli could hear.

**PROFESSOR PYLORUS:** *laughing* Cirrope. The Clown Fish. Popular, social, party-oriented. Cirrope students are the life of the Centroid — performers, entertainers, connectors. Your tower is rainbow glass, with party rooms, music studios, and performance spaces on every floor. You're going to cause so much trouble.

**HORLI:** *grinning* Trouble? Professor, I'm not going to cause trouble. I'm going to cause LEGENDS. *her dust pet — a new one that just landed on her head — chirps in agreement* Rainbow glass? Music studios? I'm gonna turn this place into the greatest venue the multiverse has ever seen. DJ Squeaks is gonna have a residency.

**PROFESSOR PYLORUS:** *shaking her head, smiling* I'm sure he will. Finally: Sol-Supreme.

**MOTHER TMOJIZU:** Sol stepped forward slowly. His expression was still carrying the weight of yesterday's $29.99 revelation. He placed his hand on the orb. It pulsed — once, twice — then blazed with gold and black light. The color of ambition, of creation, of light in darkness. The same frequency as Lucky's. The orb flickered for a moment, as if confused, then settled firmly on gold and black.

**PROFESSOR PYLORUS:** Luminari. The Gold Angler Fish. Advanced, ambitious, creators. Luminari students are the brightest, the most driven, the most likely to change the multiverse. Your tower is dark stone with gold veins, glowing angler fish lanterns, and advanced labs on every floor. You'll be in good company. Lucky is Luminari.

**SOL-SUPREME:** *quietly* Of course he is. *he takes his hand off the orb* Gold and black. Advanced labs. Creators. *he looks at Lucky, who is leaning against the fountain nearby, watching with a small smile* I guess we're housemates.

**LUCKY:** *casually* Yep. Welcome to Luminari, man. Your room's on the fourth floor. Next to mine. The angler fish lantern on our floor is the brightest in the tower. It's... a lot. But you get used to it.

**SOL-SUPREME:** *nodding* Thanks. *pause* I'm still processing the $29.99 thing.

**LUCKY:** *laughs* Yeah, I figured. Don't worry about it. Your version is really well-made. Better than the campus store ones, honestly. You should sell them.

**SOL-SUPREME:** *sighs* I'm going to need a lot of time to process this.

**MOTHER TMOJIZU:** Professor Pylorus clapped her hands, drawing everyone's attention.

**PROFESSOR PYLORUS:** Houses are assigned. Now, your dorm tours. Your house prefects will show you to your towers, introduce you to your roommates, and help you settle in. After that, meet me at the Dust Pet Arboretum for your bonding ceremony. Every student at the Centroid bonds with a dust pet companion. It's not optional. They're your friends, your familiars, and occasionally your alarm clocks.

**HORLI:** *already walking backward toward the Cirrope tower* I'm gonna name mine DJ Squeaks Jr.! No, DJ Squeaks II! No, DJ Squeaks: The Sequel!

**MOTHER TMOJIZU:** The 4-party split up, each following their house prefect to their new tower. CHARA's Khthonoi tower was exactly as advertised: black obsidian, red accents, three locks on every door, and a basement library that smelled like old paper and secrets. Her roommate was a quiet, intense student named Vex who specialized in temporal cryptography. They nodded at each other. It was enough.

IQabella's Aetheria tower was a dream: blue and purple crystal, meditation gardens on every floor, tea rooms with views of the fuzzpet asteroids. Her roommate was a gentle student named Lumi who already had three dust pets and was making chamomile. They hugged immediately. It was more than enough.

Horli's Cirrope tower was chaos in the best way: rainbow glass, music blasting from every floor, a performance space on the ground floor where a student was already rehearsing a one-person show about a sentient fuzzpet asteroid. Her roommate was a flamboyant student named Prism who immediately offered to collaborate on a DJ set. Horli said yes before the sentence was finished.

Sol's Luminari tower was quiet, intense, and brilliant. Dark stone with gold veins that pulsed with energy. Advanced labs on every floor, each one equipped with Tear It workstations, molecular forges, and flexbox simulators. His room was on the fourth floor, next to Lucky's. The angler fish lantern in the hallway was indeed blinding. Lucky was already inside his room, working on something at his desk.

**LUCKY:** *without looking up* Hey. The lantern dims at night. Don't worry about it. The lab on the third floor has a flexbox simulator if you want to mess around. And the Tear It workstation on the second floor is the best one in the tower. Just... don't touch my stuff. I'm working on SoulPen 3.

**SOL-SUPREME:** *staring* SoulPen 3? You're already on version 3?

**LUCKY:** *shrugs* Yeah. Version 2 was the museum piece. Version 3 has elemental magic pack built-in. It's $49.99. Want one?

**SOL-SUPREME:** *sits down on his bed* I need to lie down.

**MOTHER TMOJIZU:** An hour later, the 4-party reconvened at the Dust Pet Arboretum. It was a garden of Golden Ratio spiral trees, their branches heavy with dust pets of every type: spiral dust pets with golden spiral eyes, stardust dust pets with fur that glowed like starlight, and the rare chrono dust pets that chirped in temporal frequencies. Professor Pylorus stood at the center, holding a basket of small, glowing seeds.

**PROFESSOR PYLORUS:** Dust pet bonding. Each of you will receive a seed. Plant it in the soil, focus your intention, and your dust pet companion will grow. This is not a choice — it's a resonance. Your dust pet will match your soul frequency. Choose carefully. Focus deeply. And don't rush it.

**MOTHER TMOJIZU:** Each student took a seed. CHARA planted hers first, focusing on precision, on protection, on tactical awareness. The soil glowed black and red, and a chrono dust pet emerged — its fur the color of midnight, its eyes ticking like a clock. It chirped in a frequency that made CHARA's datapad vibrate. She smiled. It was perfect.

IQabella planted hers next, focusing on warmth, on connection, on tea. The soil glowed blue and purple, and a stardust dust pet emerged — its fur glowing like twilight, its eyes soft spirals. It immediately flew to her shoulder and nuzzled her cheek. She cried. It was perfect.

Horli planted hers with zero focus and maximum energy. The soil exploded with rainbow light, and a spiral dust pet emerged — its fur shifting colors, its eyes spinning like disco balls. It immediately started beatboxing. Horli lost her mind. It was perfect.

Sol planted his last, focusing on creation, on theory, on the soul frequency that connected him to Lucky. The soil glowed gold and black, and a stardust dust pet emerged — its fur glowing like molten gold, its eyes sharp spirals. It chirped in a frequency that made Sol's SoulPen hum. He looked at Lucky, who had just arrived with his own dust pet — a gold spiral pet that chirped in the exact same frequency.

**LUCKY:** *smiling* Told you. Same frequency.

**SOL-SUPREME:** *quietly* Yeah. I'm starting to believe it.

**MOTHER TMOJIZU:** After the bonding ceremony, the students were given free time to practice with their Tear It Cards. CHARA went to the Khthonoi tactical range and manifested a full set of tactical gear: shields, armor, and a molecular blade that hummed with Van der Waals manipulation. IQabella went to the Aetheria tea garden and manifested an entire ecosystem: a pond with floating tea cups, cherry blossom trees (she didn't know about the Centroid's dust pet trees yet), and a gazebo made of crystallized sugar. Horli went to the Cirrope performance space and manifested a full DJ setup: speakers, mixers, and a fuzzpet-shaped microphone that dropped beats when she rapped into it.

Sol went to the Luminari flexbox simulator and experimented with his SoulPen. He wrote "light" — a orb appeared. He wrote "fire" — a flame appeared. He wrote "shield" — a molecular barrier formed. It worked. It always worked. And every time it worked, he thought about the $29.99 campus store version. He sighed.

**HORLI:** *bursting into the simulator* SOL. You gotta come see this. Stardust Ball tryouts are tomorrow. I already signed us up. All four of us. Luminari Anglers vs. Cirrope Clowns in the first round. It's gonna be LEGENDARY.

**SOL-SUPREME:** *looking up from his SoulPen* Stardust Ball? Tomorrow? I don't even know the rules.

**HORLI:** Rules? Rules are for people who don't have fuzzpets on vacuums. I'll teach you. It's easy. You ride a fuzzpet vacuum, you hit a stardust orb, you score goals. Golden Ratio overtime is 1.618x longer per minute. That's it.

**CHARA:** *walking in behind Horli* I've already read the rulebook. Forty-seven rules. I've memorized them all. We're going to win.

**IQABELLA:** *following CHARA, her dust pet on her shoulder* I made tea for everyone! Chamomile, 175 degrees. For focus. And for Professor Squeaks. *she holds up a tray of teacups, each one with a tiny dust pet floating on the surface*

**MOTHER TMOJIZU:** The four of them stood in the flexbox simulator, their dust pets chirping in harmony, their Tear It Cards and SoulPens humming with potential. For a moment, everything was perfect. The houses were assigned. The dust pets were bonded. The training was beginning.

But on the edge of the Centroid, beyond the crystal bridges and the fuzzpet asteroids, the Nihililator rift pulsed. It was growing. Slowly, quietly, at Golden Ratio speed. Professor Pylorus stood at the observatory, watching it with concern. Her silver hair caught the light of the orbiting shooting star, and her eyes — kind, warm, but with an edge of steel — narrowed.

**PROFESSOR PYLORUS:** *quietly, to herself* It's growing faster than I thought. We don't have much time. *she turns to leave* But they're ready. They have to be.

**MOTHER TMOJIZU:** The dust pets chirped. The fuzzpet asteroids drifted. The Centroid hummed with creative energy. And somewhere in the distance, a stardust orb waited for its first game.

The real education had begun.
