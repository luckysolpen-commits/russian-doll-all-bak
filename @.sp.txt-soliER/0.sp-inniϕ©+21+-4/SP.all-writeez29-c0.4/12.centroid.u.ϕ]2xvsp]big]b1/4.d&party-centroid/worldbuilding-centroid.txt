## 1. THE CENTROID — OVERVIEW

The Centroid is an enchanted academy built on the principles of the Golden Ratio (φ = 1.618...). It is the central hub for video game players across the multiverse — connecting TSOTS ONLINE and UNDERGROUND CIRCUS players into one shared play space. It orbits a shooting star (SS. VINIX — a magical bird/spaceship/shooting star powered by the "Holy Spirit") and is surrounded by living fuzzpet asteroids.

**Location:** Asteroid cluster orbiting SS. VINIX, in a stable multiversal nexus point.
**Population:** Thousands of students (all ancient beings in eternally young bodies), faculty, staff, dust pets, fuzzpet asteroids.
**Governance:** Headmaster/Headmistress (unnamed), Professor Pylorus (mentor/administrator), faculty council.
**Motto:** "Create. Connect. Contain." (referring to creation of realities, connection across multiverses, containment of threats like the Nihililator).

## 2. FUZZPET ASTEROIDS

The asteroids orbiting the Centroid are ALIVE. They are fuzzpets — ancient, massive, sentient beings that serve as both celestial bodies and living ecosystems.

### Types:
- **Mineral-based fuzzpets:** Ancient rocks that evolved consciousness. Their craters are filled with glowing ecosystems, their surfaces crawling with tiny creatures. They hum at frequencies that stabilize the Centroid's orbit.
- **Animal-based fuzzpets:** Shaped like whales, turtles, dragons, and other creatures. Their backs carry entire forests, rivers, civilizations of microscopic beings. They breathe slowly, creating tidal winds that ripple through the crystal bridges.
- **Hybrid fuzzpets:** Part mineral, part animal. The rarest type. Some have crystal fur that sings in Golden Ratio frequencies.

### Behavior:
- Orbit in Golden Ratio patterns (φ-spaced orbital resonances).
- Their breathing creates tidal winds — students learn to surf these winds on Tear It manifestations.
- They communicate through low-frequency hums that can be felt in the bones.
- Dust pets nest in their fur, serving as a symbiotic relationship (dust pets clean parasites, fuzzpets provide shelter).

## 3. DUST PETS

Tiny fuzzpets that grow on Golden Ratio spiral trees throughout the Centroid. They are the size of sparrows to cats, with fur the color of stardust and eyes that are tiny golden spirals.

### Behavior:
- Chirp in Golden Ratio frequencies (1.618 Hz, 2.618 Hz, 4.236 Hz, etc.).
- Leave trails of sparkling dust that smell like possibility (ozone, chamomile, crushed minerals).
- Nest in crystal bridges, perch on railings, sleep in the fur of fuzzpet asteroids.
- Bond with students — each student eventually has a dust pet companion.
- Can be manifested via Tear It Cards (with varying success — they have their own will).

### Types:
- **Spiral dust pets:** Most common. Eyes are golden spirals. Chirp in φ frequencies.
- **Stardust dust pets:** Rarer. Fur glows like starlight. Leave brighter trails.
- **Chrono dust pets:** Extremely rare. Can sense temporal shifts. Used in advanced chronoment training.

## 5. CREATION TOOLS

### Tear It Cards (Standard):
- Made by the Multiversal Tmojio Company.
- Magic sketchbook pages. Draw what you want, tear the page out, it becomes REAL.
- Quality depends on: skill, understanding of underlying chemistry, emotional connection.
- Types: Tactical (CHARA), Creative (IQabella), Performance (Horli), Standard (Sol — initially).
- Price: Free for students (provided by the Soul Bank).

### SoulPen (Rare):
- Invented by Lucky (and independently by Sol-Supreme — time twin resonance).
- Writes matter into existence directly. No drawing required — just write, and it manifests.
- Attuned to the user's soul frequency.
- Sold at the campus store for $29.99 (standard) / $49.99 (deluxe with elemental magic pack).
- Manufactured by the Multiversal Tmojio Company (Lucky owns the company).
- Lucky's version: SoulPen 2 (the second one he ever made; the first is in a museum).

### Tear It Sketchbook (Advanced):
- Full sketchbook, not just individual cards. More powerful than individual Tear It Cards.
- Allows for complex, multi-page manifestations (entire environments, ecosystems).
- Astra/Ava carries one. Only advanced students receive them.

## 8. STARDUST BALL

A sport played with fuzzpets on vacuums. The Centroid's most popular sport.

### Rules:
- **Teams:** 4 players per team.
- **Equipment:** Each player rides a fuzzpet mounted on a Tear It-manifested vacuum platform. The vacuum hovers and moves based on the fuzzpet's energy output.
- **Ball:** A stardust orb — a glowing sphere of compressed stardust that leaves trails of sparkling dust when moved.
- **Field:** A floating platform divided into two halves, surrounded by crystal barriers. The field shifts and rotates based on Golden Ratio frequencies.
- **Objective:** Score goals by getting the stardust orb through the opponent's goal ring. Goals are worth 1 point. Bonus points for style, fuzzpet tricks, and Golden Ratio alignment.
- **Gameplay:** Players direct their fuzzpets using Tear It Cards or SoulPens. The fuzzpet's energy determines speed and maneuverability. Players can pass, shoot, block, and perform tricks.
- **Fouls:** Intentional collision with another player's fuzzpet, using unauthorized creation tools, disrupting the field's Golden Ratio frequency.
- **Duration:** Three periods of 20 minutes each. Overtime is played in Golden Ratio time (each minute is 1.618x longer than the previous).

### Notable Teams:
- **Luminari Anglers** — Sol-Supreme's team. Strategic, precise, uses advanced chemistry to enhance fuzzpet performance.
- **Cirrope Clowns** — Horli Qwin's team. Flashy, unpredictable, crowd favorites.
- **Aetheria Oysters** — IQabella's team. Balanced, defensive, excels at passing and teamwork.
- **Khthonoi Piranhas** — CHARA's team. Aggressive, tactical, uses psychological warfare.

### Stardust Ball Vocabulary:
- **Ride:** Mounting and directing your fuzzpet vacuum.
- **Pass:** Throwing the stardust orb to a teammate.
- **Shoot:** Attempting to score a goal.
- **Block:** Defending your goal ring.
- **Trick:** Performing a maneuver with your fuzzpet for bonus points.
- **Golden Goal:** A goal scored during Golden Ratio overtime — worth 1.618 points.

## 9. HOT AIR BALLOON FUZZPETS

Transportation method to airship cities and surrounding areas.

### Description:
- Massive fuzzpets inflated with hot air, shaped like balloons but alive.
- They have baskets attached (Tear It-manifested) that carry passengers.
- They navigate using thermal currents and their own intuition.
- Each balloon fuzzpet has a personality — some are adventurous, some are cautious, some are mischievous.

### Routes:
- **Centroid → Airship City Alpha:** 15 minutes. Scenic route past three fuzzpet asteroids.
- **Centroid → Airship City Beta:** 30 minutes. Passes through a cloud of stardust.
- **Centroid → SS. VINIX Dock:** 45 minutes. Requires crossing a temporal rift (safe for trained balloon fuzzpets).
- **Centroid → Dustopia Gateway:** 2 hours. The longest route. Requires multiple balloon changes.

### Activities:
- Students ride balloon fuzzpets for leisure, exploration, and field trips.
- Balloon fuzzpet racing is a popular extracurricular.
- Some students bond with their balloon fuzzpets and ride them exclusively.

## 10. ANGLERS (THEORETICAL — "ANGLER EMPIRE" SERIES)

Anglers are fishlike humanoids who wield "sprite swords" — blades forged from pure creative energy. They are the warriors of the multiverse, seeded into flexbox dimensions to fight threats like the Nihililator.

### Description:
- **Appearance:** Humanoid fish-people with bioluminescent scales, antennae that sense multiversal disturbances, and eyes that glow with creative energy.
- **Sprite Swords:** Blades forged from the user's soul frequency. They cut through anything — matter, energy, even the rules of reality. Only anglers can wield them.
- **Soul Connection:** Students learn to soul-connect with anglers, controlling them and seeing through their antennae without entering dangerous dimensions themselves.
- **Seeding:** Anglers are "seeded" into flexbox dimensions using Angler Seeds — powerful artifacts created by the Celestial Scribes.

### Role in the Story:
- Students will LEARN about anglers in class (Angler Theory) but will NOT actually control them during the Centroid arc (Sessions 3-10).
- Actual angler control and the "Angler Empire" storyline will be a SEPARATE series.
- The 4-party will eventually seed anglers into flexbox dimensions to fight the Nihililator indirectly, without entering dangerous dimensions themselves.

## 11. THE NIHILILATOR

The primary threat. An entity that consumes the rules of reality itself.

### Nature:
- Not just a void — it's the UNMAKING of creation. It consumes chemical bonds, flexbox properties, soul frequencies, and the mathematical foundations of reality.
- Grows at Golden Ratio speed — 1.618x every second.
- Originated from the 4-party's Infinitiator experiment in FFU Session 13, but also exists as an ancient condition in the multiverse (they recreated something that already existed).
- Exists in a rift between dimensions, expanding into the Centroid's flexbox network.

### Defense Strategy:
- Students will NOT fight the Nihililator directly.
- Instead, they will learn to use flexboxes to control the multiverses inside them, link them together, and seed anglers to fight on their behalf.
- The 4-party will observe and direct the battle through angler antennae, never entering the dangerous dimensions themselves.

## 12. SOL & LUCKY — TIME TWIN DYNAMIC

### The Connection:
- Lucky is Sol's time twin — created unknowingly during Sol's Time Fu training. A fragment of Sol's temporal signature given independent form.
- They share the same soul frequency. Their SoulPens resonate with each other.
- Neither fully understands the connection yet. Professor Pylorus knows but isn't telling.

### Relationship Arc (Sessions 3-10):
- **Initial:** Sol is devastated by the $29.99 revelation. Lucky is casual, friendly, unknowingly devastating.
- **Growing:** They hang out more, exchange information, collaborate on projects. Sol respects Lucky's practical knowledge; Lucky respects Sol's theoretical brilliance.
- **Tension:** Sol becomes aggressively competitive — sparring with Lucky, trying to prove himself. Lucky doesn't understand why Sol is so intense.
- **Bonding:** They realize they complement each other. Sol is the theorist; Lucky is the practitioner. Together, they're unstoppable.
- **Revelation:** Eventually, they'll discover the time twin connection fully. This will be a major emotional beat.
