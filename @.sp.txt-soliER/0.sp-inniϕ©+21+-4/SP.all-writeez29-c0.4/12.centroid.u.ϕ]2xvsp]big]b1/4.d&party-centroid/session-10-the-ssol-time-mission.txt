## SESSION TRANSCRIPT:

**MOTHER TMOJIZU:** The SSOL hung in the temporal stream, its sleek, dark hull covered in Golden Ratio spirals, its engines humming with temporal energy. Inside, Sol sat at the controls, his temporal body suit humming with Golden Ratio frequency, holding his flickering atoms together. Horli navigated, her rainbow dust pet spinning slowly on her head — no beats today, just quiet focus. IQabella monitored temporal integrity, her stardust dust pets floating around the console, chirping in worried frequencies.

Behind them, the Centroid was burning.

**MOTHER TMOJIZU:** Before the SSOL could launch into the deep past, before Sol could begin his mission to save Lucky, a massive golden star pyramid ship descended on the Centroid. It was a futuristic, high-tech gold Egyptian aesthetic — gleaming pyramid structure, hieroglyphic energy fields pulsing with necromantic power, golden zombie clone armies pouring out of its hull like a river of molten metal. The ship was called the Necro-Star. And it was here to conquer.

**MOTHER TMOJIZU:** A young boy stepped out of the Necro-Star's golden ramp, his robes woven from stardust and death, his eyes glowing with bioluminescent energy. He called himself Rahweh the Necromantic, God of Life and Death. He was voiced by QOO — warm, professional, but with an edge of ancient power. And he was actually Lucky and Sol's son from Ikarus's Metal Sun — a child Sol had tried to kill in a past timeline. He'd returned. And he'd brought an army.

**RAHWEH:** *stepping onto the Centroid's obsidian platform, his golden zombie clones marching behind him* Well, well, well. Look at this place. Crystal bridges. Golden Ratio towers. Fuzzpet asteroids. *he smirks* Cute. Real cute. Hey, unk! You home?

**MOTHER TMOJIZU:** Sol heard the voice from the SSOL's observation deck. His face went pale. His temporal suit flickered — a piece of his hand dissolved, then reformed. He knew that voice. He'd heard it before. In another timeline. In another life.

**SOL-SUPREME:** *quietly, through the suit's voice modulator* No. It can't be. I killed him. I killed him.

**HORLI:** *at the navigation console* Sol? Who's that? Who's "unk"?

**SOL-SUPREME:** *not answering* Rahweh. The Necromantic. My... *he can't say it* ...son. From the Metal Sun. From Ikarus. I tried to kill him. I failed. And now he's here.

**MOTHER TMOJIZU:** Rahweh's voice echoed across the Centroid, amplified by the Necro-Star's hieroglyphic energy fields.

**RAHWEH:** *broadcasting to the entire Centroid* Listen up, crystal-bridge nerds and fuzzpet riders! I'm Rahweh the Necromantic, God of Life and Death, and I'm taking over this place. By submission or force. Your choice. But honestly? I prefer force. My golden zombie clones are bored. They need exercise. *he pauses* Oh, and old man Sol-Supreme — if you're listening — I know you're up there in that little timeship. Come out, come out, ancient relic. Let's have a family reunion.

**SOL-SUPREME:** *standing up, his temporal suit humming* I'm going out there.

**IQABELLA:** *grabbing his arm* Sol, no! You're wearing a temporal body suit! You're flickering! You can't fight him!

**SOL-SUPREME:** *pulling away* He's my son. I tried to kill him. He's here for revenge. And he's taking the Centroid. I have to stop him. *his hand flickers — five seconds* I have to try.

**MOTHER TMOJIZU:** Sol stepped out of the SSOL and onto the Centroid's obsidian platform. The temporal body suit covered him from head to toe, humming with Golden Ratio frequency, holding his flickering atoms together. His face was hidden behind the suit's visor. His voice came through the modulator — alien, otherworldly, powerful.

**SOL-SUPREME:** *through the modulator* Rahweh. Leave the Centroid. Now.

**RAHWEH:** *turning to face Sol, smirking* Well, well. Look who decided to show up. The fossil himself. *he walks closer, his golden zombie clones parting behind him* Nice suit, unk. What's the matter? Can't hold yourself together anymore? *he laughs* Death is a suggestion, old man. I chose to ignore it. You really thought you could off me? Cute.

**SOL-SUPREME:** *activating his SoulPen* You're not my son. I killed you. You're dead.

**RAHWEH:** *smirking* And yet, here I am. God of Life and Death. With an army of golden zombie clones. And a pyramid ship that makes your little timeship look like a toy. *he gestures to the Necro-Star* So. What's it gonna be, ancient relic? You gonna fight me? Or you gonna run away like you always do?

**MOTHER TMOJIZU:** Sol attacked. He used everything he had — SoulPen, Time Fu, flexbox manipulation, temporal displacement. He wrote "fire" — a flame erupted. He wrote "shield" — a molecular barrier formed. He used advanced Time Fu techniques — temporal acceleration, temporal deceleration, temporal phasing. He pushed hard, fast, relentless.

But Rahweh was ready. His golden zombie clones surged forward, absorbing Sol's attacks, reforming instantly. His necromantic energy countered Sol's temporal energy. His sprite sword — forged from death frequency, the inverse of Sol's soul frequency — clashed with Sol's SoulPen. Creation vs. unmaking. Life vs. death. Father vs. son.

**RAHWEH:** *parrying Sol's attacks* Is that all you got, old man? I've been dead for millennia. I've had a lot of time to practice. *he swings his sprite sword — it cuts through Sol's temporal shield* You really thought you could kill me? I'm the God of Life and Death. Death is my playground.

**MOTHER TMOJIZU:** Sol fought with everything he had. But Rahweh's golden zombie clones overwhelmed the Centroid's defenses. The fuzzpet asteroids fled, their breathing heavy, their surfaces tense. The dust pets scattered, chirping in terrified frequencies. The crystal bridges shattered under the weight of the golden zombie army. The Centroid was falling.

**PROFESSOR PYLORUS:** *appearing beside Sol, her silver hair catching the firelight, her robes shifting between deep blue and violet* Sol. You can't save the Centroid. But you can save yourself. Go back in time. Save Lucky. Fix the loop. The Centroid will fall, but the timeline can still be saved.

**SOL-SUPREME:** *breathing hard, his temporal suit flickering* I can't leave. I can't let him win.

**PROFESSOR PYLORUS:** He's already won. The Centroid is gone. But Lucky, Astra, and CHARA can still be saved. The loop can still be fixed. *she looks at his flickering suit* You don't have much time. Literally. Go.

**MOTHER TMOJIZU:** Sol looked at the burning Centroid. At Rahweh's golden pyramid ship hovering above the ruins. At the golden zombie clones marching through the crystal bridges. At the fuzzpet asteroids fleeing into the distance. At the dust pets scattered and terrified. The Centroid had fallen.

He nodded.

**SOL-SUPREME:** *turning back to the SSOL* Horli. IQabella. Launch the ship. Now.

**MOTHER TMOJIZU:** As Rahweh's golden zombie clones breached the SSOL's docking bay, Sol, Horli, and IQabella launched the ship. The SSOL's engines roared with temporal energy. The ship shot into the temporal stream, leaving the burning Centroid behind. Behind them, Rahweh's voice echoed across the ruins.

**RAHWEH:** *broadcasting* Run, old man! Run! But I'll find you. I always find you. Death is a suggestion, unk. And I'm the one who suggests it.

**MOTHER TMOJIZU:** The SSOL jumped into the deep temporal stream. The Centroid disappeared behind them. Rahweh's Necro-Star shrank into the distance. The golden zombie clones marched through the ruins. The fuzzpet asteroids fled. The dust pets scattered.

The Centroid was gone.

**SOL-SUPREME:** *at the controls, his temporal suit humming* Temporal stream navigation engaged. Destination: Earth. Past. CHAR.A.NON era. *he pauses* I'm coming, Lucky. I already did. Time twins. We save each other. That's what we do.

**MOTHER TMOJIZU:** The SSOL traveled through the temporal stream. Sol piloted, his temporal suit holding him together, his hand flickering at the edges. Horli navigated, her rainbow dust pet spinning slowly, sensing temporal shifts. IQabella monitored temporal integrity, her stardust dust pets floating around the console, chirping in worried frequencies.

They arrived on Earth — in the past, during the CHAR.A.NON era. Before CHARA joined the 4-party. Before the Centroid. Before the Nihililator trap. Before Rahweh.

**SOL-SUPREME:** *looking at the readouts* We're here. Earth. Past. CHAR.A.NON era. *his suit flickers — a piece of his shoulder dissolves, then reforms* I need to become Anon. I need to abduct past-CHARA. Before she enters the flexbox. Before the trap. Before everything goes wrong.

**HORLI:** *quietly* Sol... are you okay? Your suit. It's getting worse.

**SOL-SUPREME:** *through the modulator* I'm aware. The suit is failing. I have maybe... days. Hours. *he pauses* It doesn't matter. I need to save Lucky. That's all that matters.

**MOTHER TMOJIZU:** Sol took on the identity of "Anon" — a mysterious figure who had been manipulating events from the shadows. His temporal suit made him look alien, otherworldly — perfect for the Anon persona. He descended to Earth in a silent craft, his golden zombie clone decoys masking his temporal signature.

He found past-CHARA at Langley Headquarters — her office pristine, her secure phone lying centered on the desk, her ergonomic chair pushed back slightly as if she'd risen in haste. He abducted her in a Roswell-style operation: her car vanished on a desert road, strange metallic debris scattered, a silent craft hovered and ascended. The CIA covered it up as a "classified assignment." The SS.SOL was never shown or stated — only implied.

**MOTHER TMOJIZU:** Past-CHARA was brought aboard the SSOL. She was confused, angry, but Sol explained through his suit's voice modulator.

**SOL-SUPREME:** *through the modulator* You're going to help me save your future self.

**CHARA (PAST):** *staring at the alien figure in the temporal suit* Who are you? What do you want?

**SOL-SUPREME:** *through the modulator* I'm Anon. And I need you to trust me. Your future self is trapped. In a flexbox dimension. In the Netherlands. Her soul consumed by the Nihililator. Her angler fishman body fighting on autopilot. *he pauses* I'm going back in time to fix it. But I need your help.

**CHARA (PAST):** *processing* The Nihililator. The Netherlands. Flexbox dimensions. *she pauses* You're from the future. You're trying to change the timeline.

**SOL-SUPREME:** *through the modulator* I'm trying to save it.

**MOTHER TMOJIZU:** The SSOL traveled further forward in time — to the era of the Sol Pen screenplay. To Lucky's ranch. Sol had to abduct past-Lucky and past-Astra while avoiding the Melting Red Wax Time Police — temporal law enforcement who hunted unauthorized time travelers, literally melting red wax dripping from their bodies, temporal energy leaking, terrifying to behold.

Sol used stealth mode, Tear It-encoded decoys, and flexbox masking to slip past them. His temporal suit helped — it masked his flickering signature. Past-Lucky and past-Astra were abducted during a quiet moment on Lucky's ranch — a silent craft, a flash of light, and they were gone.

**MOTHER TMOJIZU:** Aboard the SSOL, Sol trained past-Lucky and past-Astra in SolPen mastery, flexbox engineering, and temporal mechanics. He was still wearing the suit, still flickering at the edges. His hand dissolved for three seconds, then reformed. His ear flickered in and out. The suit was failing.

**SOL-SUPREME:** *through the modulator, training past-Lucky* The loop is stable. But stable doesn't mean unbreakable. We're buying time. Finding a way. *his suit flickers — a piece of his hand dissolves, then reforms* I don't have much time. Literally.

**LUCKY (PAST):** *watching Sol's flickering suit* You're unraveling. Because of me. Because of future me.

**SOL-SUPREME:** *through the modulator* Because of the time twin connection. When your soul is consumed, a piece of me flickers out too. *he pauses* But I'm not stopping. Not until you're safe. Not until the loop is fixed.

**ASTRA (PAST):** *quietly* The gyroscope of timelines. You've already seen it. You know how it ends.

**SOL-SUPREME:** *through the modulator* I know how it starts. The ending... we're still writing.

**MOTHER TMOJIZU:** Past-CHARA was furious. She stood in the SSOL's training room, her chrono dust pet ticking rapidly, her tactical mind processing everything.

**CHARA (PAST):** *angrily* You're telling me I HAVE to get trapped? That's the plan? I have to enter the flexbox, soul-connect with an angler, and let the Nihililator consume my soul? *she pauses* That's insane.

**SOL-SUPREME:** *through the modulator* The loop requires it. If you don't enter the flexbox, the timeline collapses. Lucky, Astra, and your future self are already trapped. The only way to save them is to set up the loop. To create a stable timeline where they can eventually be rescued.

**CHARA (PAST):** *processing* So I'm a pawn. In my own future.

**SOL-SUPREME:** *through the modulator* You're a creator. In every timeline.

**MOTHER TMOJIZU:** The SSOL crew had the tools. They had the knowledge. They had the time twin resonance between Sol and Lucky. But the loop was not broken. Not yet.

The series ended with Sol looking out the SSOL's observation window at the stars. His temporal suit hummed, flickered, held. Past-Lucky and past-Astra trained in the simulation room. Past-CHARA studied temporal mechanics, determined to find a way out.

In the future, three autonomous fishmen fought endlessly inside a flexbox dimension, their souls trapped in the Netherlands.

The Centroid was gone. Rahweh ruled it now. His golden pyramid ship hovered above the ruins. His golden zombie clones marched through the crystal bridges. The fuzzpet asteroids were enslaved. The dust pets were scattered.

Professor Pylorus watched the flexbox from a hidden refuge. She whispered: "Hold on. We're coming."

**MOTHER TMOJIZU:** The SSOL jumped to another point in the temporal stream. Sol's suit flickered — a piece of his face dissolved, then reformed. The Melting Red Wax Time Police detected the jump and gave chase. The loop continued. But so did they.

And the gyroscope of timelines turned.
