**MOTHER TMOJIZU:** The flexbox spat them out onto a platform of polished obsidian, veined with gold in Fibonacci spirals. The Centroid rose before them: towers spiraling in golden ratios, their surfaces covered in iridescent panels that shifted color as the light hit them at different angles. Bridges of translucent crystal connected the towers, each one humming with a frequency that made their teeth vibrate. Students walked the bridges — hundreds of them, all appearing to be teenagers, all with eyes that held thousands of years of knowledge.

And above it all — the asteroids.

They weren't rocks. They were fuzzpets. Ancient, massive fuzzpets the size of mountains, their surfaces covered in fur that shimmered like moss and stone. Some were genuinely rocky — ancient asteroids that had somehow become alive, their craters filled with glowing ecosystems, their surfaces crawling with tiny creatures that had evolved in the shadow of the Centroid. Others weren't rock at all — they were animals. Enormous, gentle fuzzpets shaped like whales, like turtles, like dragons curled in sleep, their backs covered in entire forests, rivers, civilizations of microscopic beings who lived and died without ever knowing they were riding on the back of a living fuzzpet. They orbited the Centroid slowly, lazily, in Golden Ratio patterns, their breathing creating tidal winds that rippled through the crystal bridges.

The Centroid: School of the Multiverse. And it was alive.

**IQABELLA:** *clutching Horli's hand, tears in her eyes* It's like an enchanted academy designed by a mathematician who fell in love with anime. A figure approached them across the obsidian platform. She walked with the measured grace of someone who had lived for millennia and still enjoyed every step. Silver hair caught the light of the orbiting shooting star, and her eyes — kind, warm, but with an edge of steel — locked onto the 4-party. Professor Pylorus. In person, she was even more impressive than the hologram. She wore robes that shifted between deep blue and violet, embroidered with Golden Ratio spirals that seemed to move when you weren't looking directly at them.

**PROFESSOR PYLORUS:** You came. *her voice was warm, relieved* I wasn't sure the portal would hold long enough. But you're here. All four of you. *she looked at each of them in turn* CHARA. Sol-Supreme. IQabella. Horli Qwin. The creators of the Infinitiator. The ones who faced nihilili and lived. Welcome to the Centroid.

**PROFESSOR PYLORUS:** *sighs, the weight of eons in the sound* Since before this school existed. The Nihililator is... it's not just a threat. It's a condition. But this time... *she pauses* ...this time it's different. It's grown faster than ever before. And it's coming from YOUR dimension. From the rift you opened in Session 13. You didn't just create it. You gave it a doorway.

**SOL-SUPREME:** *voice tight* Then we'll close it. Tell us what we need to learn.

**PROFESSOR PYLORUS:** *smiles* First, you need tools. And a tour. Come.

**MOTHER TMOJIZU:** She led them across the obsidian platform and onto the nearest crystal bridge. Beneath their feet, the bridge hummed — a low, resonant frequency that Sol-Supreme recognized as the same Golden Ratio encoding from the flexbox. The bridge stretched between two towers: one covered in iridescent panels that shifted from gold to violet, the other wrapped in living vines that grew in Fibonacci spirals. Students passed them, some nodding respectfully, others staring curiously at the newcomers. A group of students in rainbow-colored robes — Cirrope house, Horli would later learn — stopped to watch Horli walk by, drawn by her presence, her energy, the way she moved like she owned every surface she touched.

**PROFESSOR PYLORUS:** First stop: the Soul Bank. *she gestured ahead*

The walls were lined with vaults, each one glowing with a soft, warm light. Inside each vault, they could see it: Tear It Cards. Stacks of them, organized by type, by power level, by elemental affinity. The air smelled like old paper and possibility. Behind the main counter, a massive crystal orb pulsed — the Soul Oracle, its surface swirling with colors that matched no spectrum they knew.

**PROFESSOR PYLORUS:** The Soul Bank is where you get your Tear It Cards. *she turned to the 4-party* Each of you will receive a starter pack. CHARA — tactical Tear It Cards. **HORLI:** *staring at her creation, voice cracking I made that. I MADE THAT. *the fuzzpet dances onto her shoulder Oh my god, it's on my shoulder. It's DJ Squeaks. Named after Professor Squeaks. *starts crying

**MOTHER TMOJIZU:** He walked out of the third tower, SoulPen in hand, and found the 4-party waiting in the central courtyard. CHARA was practicing with her tactical Tear It Cards, manifesting and dismissing shields with increasing speed. IQabella had created an entire tea garden — cups of tea floating on a pond, each one a different temperature, each one perfect, dust pets perched on the rims, sipping and chirping approvingly. **MOTHER TMOJIZU:** Professor Pylorus appeared at the edge of the courtyard, her silver hair catching the light of the orbiting shooting star. She watched the two of them — Sol-Supreme, devastated and brilliant, and Lucky, casual and unknowingly devastating — and smiled. She knew. She had always known.

**PROFESSOR PYLORUS:** *quietly, to herself* Time twins. Created unknowingly during Time Fu training. A fragment of one's temporal signature given independent form. They'll figure it out. **HORLI:** *still laughing, but with affection Sol, buddy, I love you. But you just spent three hours inventing something that costs $29.99 and comes with a free pencil case.

**MOTHER TMOJIZU:** He sat down. On a Golden Ratio bench. Next to Lucky. Who patted him on the shoulder and said, "Don't worry, man. Your version is really well-made. Better than the campus store ones, honestly. You should sell them." And Sol-Supreme, the bio-tech genius, the business mastermind, the dark Iron Man/Batman type, the inventor of the Infinitiator and the one who faced nihilili, said nothing. Because for the first time in his life, he had been out-invented by someone who didn't even know they were competing.

Yet.

**PROFESSOR PYLORUS:** *clapping her hands* Tour continues! Next stop: the Creation Class. Where you'll learn to use your Tear It Cards properly. And Sol — *she winks* — you can bring your SoulPen. We'll see if it works in a classroom setting.

**MOTHER TMOJIZU:** They walked toward the Creation Class tower, the courtyard behind them filled with the sound of DJ Squeaks and the smell of IQabella's tea. Dust pets fluttered around them, landing on their shoulders, leaving trails of stardust. Above them, the fuzzpet asteroids orbited slowly — whales, turtles, dragons, ancient rocks alive with ecosystems and wonder. The Nihililator was still growing. The rift was still expanding. But for this moment, in this garden of Golden Ratio spirals and living asteroids, the 4-party and Lucky and Astra — who had been watching quietly from the fountain, her Tear It sketchbook in hand, a dust pet curled in her hair, a small smile on her face — were together.

The real education had begun.

And Sol-Supreme would never live down the $29.99 revelation.
