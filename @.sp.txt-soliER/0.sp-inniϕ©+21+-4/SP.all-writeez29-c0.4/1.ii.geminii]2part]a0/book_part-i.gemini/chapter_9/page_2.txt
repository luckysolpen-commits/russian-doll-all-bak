apprehension. The Mall of the Dead, once a place of quiet

and mechanics, meticulously inspected every component
of the Steel Caravan. He tightened bolts, replaced worn
hoses, and even managed to scavenge a larger fuel tank

smell of burning metal filling the air. The rhythmic clang
of his hammer against metal, the hiss of the welding torch,

the treacherous terrain they would soon face, anticipating
every potential breakdown, every possible obstacle, every

skill that spoke of years of hands-on experience.

calculation, every decision, could mean the difference
