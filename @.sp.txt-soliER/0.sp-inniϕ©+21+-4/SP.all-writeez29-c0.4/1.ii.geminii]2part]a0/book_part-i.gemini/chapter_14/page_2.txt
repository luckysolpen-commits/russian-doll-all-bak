Gem, meanwhile, unleashed a torrent of fire at the advancing
clone guards. The flamethrower, though effective, was
not designed for sustained combat against armored targets,
and the sheer number of guards was overwhelming. The roar
of the ignited fuel filled the arena, painting streaks
of orange and red across the polished floor, forcing the
guards to momentarily halt their advance, their visors
gleaming in the inferno. But they were relentless, their
programmed directives overriding any instinct for self-preservation.
The guards, though momentarily deterred, continued their
relentless advance, their energy weapons carving lines
of destruction across the arena floor, the air thick with
the smell of ozone and burnt concrete. Gem felt the heat
of the flamethrower against her hands, the vibrations
rattling her bones, but she held firm, her bright pink
hair a defiant halo against the flames. She knew she couldn't
stop them all, but she could buy Lucky time, time to reach
The Architect.

Lucky, with a desperate surge of adrenaline, charged The
Architect. The man, surprisingly agile for his age, dodged
Lucky's initial swing, a faint, condescending smirk on
his face. He retaliated with a series of precise, almost
surgical, strikes, using a hidden blade that extended from
his wrist with a silent hiss. The blade, thin and razor-sharp,
glinted wickedly in the blue light, a silent promise of
pain. Lucky parried with his pipe, the clang of metal
ringing through the arena, a discordant note in the symphony
of battle. He felt the impact reverberate up his arm, a
jarring reminder of the Architect's unexpected strength.

"You think you can defeat me?" The Architect sneered, his
voice filled with a fanatical zeal, his eyes burning with
a disturbing light. "I am the future! I am order! You
are merely chaos, a relic of a dying world! Your struggles
are futile!" He moved with a fluid grace, a dancer of
destruction, his attacks relentless, each one aimed at
a vital point. Lucky gritted his teeth, his muscles screaming
in protest, but he held his ground, his own determination
a burning fire in his gut. He feigned a lunge, then spun,
aiming a powerful blow at The Architect's head. The Architect
blocked it with his forearm, but the force of the impact
sent a jolt up his arm, a flicker of pain crossing his
otherwise impassive face. It was a small victory, but a
victory nonetheless.

Astra, having disabled the last of the drones, their metallic
husks scattered across the arena floor like discarded toys,
turned her attention to the clone guards. She reconfigured
her stun gun, increasing its power output, the device humming
with a dangerous energy. She began to fire rapid bursts,
each one sending a guard reeling, their systems overloading,
their bodies twitching uncontrollably before collapsing
with a shower of sparks. The air filled with the acrid
smell of burning circuits, a grim testament to her efficiency.
She moved with a cold, calculated precision, her scientific
mind focused on maximizing damage, on creating a path
for Lucky. She knew that every second counted, every disabled
guard a step closer to their goal. Her face was set in
a grim line, her eyes burning with a fierce resolve. The
Architect's words, his casual disregard for life, had ignited
a cold fury within her, a determination to see him brought
to justice, no matter the cost.
