The salvage yard was a monument to a forgotten era,
a sprawling graveyard of twisted metal and shattered
glass. Rust-eaten hulks of cars, trucks, and buses lay
piled high, forming grotesque sculptures against the
sky. The air hummed with a metallic tang, a symphony
of decay. Lucky led the way, his eyes narrowed, searching
for a specific kind of beast amidst the wreckage.

"This is... overwhelming," Gem murmured, her usual artistic
enthusiasm muted by the sheer scale of destruction. She
ran a hand over the pitted chrome of a luxury sedan,
once a symbol of status, now just another piece of junk.

Astra, ever practical, pulled out a small, battered tablet.
"According to my scans, there's a higher concentration
of heavy-duty vehicles in sector Gamma-7. Military-grade
vehicles, possibly armored personnel carriers."

Lucky grunted in approval. "Good work, Astra. That's
exactly what we need. Something built to take a beating."
They navigated the treacherous terrain, climbing over
mounds of debris, squeezing through narrow gaps between
collapsed structures. The silence here was different from
the city's, a heavy, oppressive quiet, broken only by
the crunch of their boots on broken glass and the distant
creak of shifting metal.

As they approached sector Gamma-7, the vehicles grew
larger, more imposing. Tanks, armored personnel carriers,
and massive transport trucks lay scattered like discarded
toys. Then, Lucky saw it. A behemoth of a truck, its
frame still largely intact, despite the rust and bullet
holes that marred its surface. It was a military transport,
built for war, and it looked like it could withstand
an army of Carcazoids.

"There!" he exclaimed, pointing. "That's our new home."

Gem's eyes lit up. "It's magnificent! A true beast!"

Astra, ever the scientist, was already assessing its condition.
"The engine looks salvageable, but it'll need a complete
overhaul. The tires are flat, and the armor plating has
been compromised in several places. But yes, Lucky, this
has potential. Significant potential."

They spent the rest of the day meticulously examining
the truck, making a mental inventory of what they would
need to get it running. Lucky, with his mechanical aptitude,
identified key components that needed replacing or repairing.
Astra, using her knowledge of engineering, began to sketch
modifications for reinforcing the armor and creating
a self-sustaining power source. Gem, meanwhile, was already
envisioning the exterior, planning murals of defiance
and hope.

The sun began to dip below the horizon, casting long
shadows across the salvage yard. The air grew colder,
and the distant howls of Carcazoids began to echo through
the twilight. They knew they couldn't stay here overnight.
But they had found their steel caravan. And with it, a
renewed sense of purpose. The road ahead was still long,
but now, they had a vehicle to carry them through the
wasteland.
