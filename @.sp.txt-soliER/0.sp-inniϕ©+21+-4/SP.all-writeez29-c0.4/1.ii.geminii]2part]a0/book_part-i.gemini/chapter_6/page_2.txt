Their first encounter with the mutated wildlife was a terrifying
one, a brutal reminder that the wilderness held its own
horrors, distinct from the urban decay they had grown
accustomed to. A pack of feral, oversized wolves, their
furs matted and bristly, their eyes glowing with an unnatural,
phosphorescent green light, emerged from the dense undergrowth,
their movements silent and predatory. They were faster,
stronger, and more aggressive than any wolf Lucky had
ever seen, their teeth elongated and razor-sharp, their
claws leaving deep gouges in the soft earth. Their low,
guttural growls vibrated through the Steel Caravan's reinforced
hull, a chilling symphony of hunger.

"Stay inside!" Lucky yelled, his voice a sharp command
that cut through the sudden tension. He slammed his foot
on the accelerator, but the heavy vehicle struggled to
gain traction in the muddy terrain. The wolves, sensing
their vulnerability, began to circle the truck, their howls
echoing through the forest, a mournful, yet menacing, sound.
He activated the external defenses, the retractable spikes
extending with a hydraulic hiss, glinting ominously in
the dappled sunlight. The water cannons hissed, ready to
spray their corrosive acid, but Lucky hesitated, unsure
if it would be effective against these new, mutated beasts.

Astra, from the console, her face grim, aimed the water
cannons, now loaded with a high-frequency sonic blast.
She had recalibrated the system after their encounter
with the Golem, hoping to find a more versatile defense.
The sound, an agonizing, high-pitched shriek, tore through
the air, sending the wolves scattering, their ears pinned
back in pain, their glowing eyes wide with confusion. But
they didn't retreat far, their glowing eyes still fixed
on the Steel Caravan, their growls now laced with a new
kind of frustration. They were disoriented, but not defeated.

Gem, meanwhile, had found a new use for her flamethrower.
She aimed it at the trees, creating a wall of fire that
forced the wolves to keep their distance, the heat intense,
the flames licking at the low-hanging branches. The smell
of burning leaves and singed fur filled the air, a grim
perfume of battle. She watched, her eyes narrowed, as the
wolves snarled and snapped at the flames, their primal
instincts warring with their newfound fear. She felt a
strange satisfaction in turning the forest itself into
a weapon, a fiery barrier against the encroaching darkness.

They managed to escape the pack, the Steel Caravan finally
gaining traction and lurching forward, leaving the snarling
wolves behind. But the encounter was a grim reminder of
the dangers that lurked in this new wilderness. The forest
was not a sanctuary; it was another battleground, a different
kind of enemy, one that adapted and evolved with terrifying
speed. But they were resilient. They had faced the Carcazoids,
the clowns, and now, the mutated beasts of the wild. And
they would continue to fight, to adapt, to survive, their
resolve hardened by each new challenge. Lucky felt a renewed
sense of purpose. They were not just running; they were
learning, evolving, becoming something more than just survivors.
