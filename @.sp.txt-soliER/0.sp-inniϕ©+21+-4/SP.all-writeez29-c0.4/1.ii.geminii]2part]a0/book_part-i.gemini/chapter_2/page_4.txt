The first rays of dawn pierced through the grimy windows
of the auto shop, painting streaks of pale light across
the concrete floor. Lucky was already awake, his mind
racing. He had spent the night tossing and turning, the
idea of a mobile sanctuary taking root in his thoughts.

"Morning," Astra mumbled, rubbing the sleep from her
eyes. She looked around the cluttered garage, her gaze
lingering on the various tools and discarded vehicle parts.
"This place... it has potential."

Gem stretched, a wide yawn escaping her lips. "Potential
for what? A new art studio?" she quipped, though her
eyes held a serious glint. She understood the gravity
of their situation.

"Potential for freedom," Lucky stated, his voice firm.
He walked over to a dusty, oil-stained workbench, littered
with wrenches and spare parts. "We can't keep living
like this, scavenging from one ruin to the next, always
one step ahead of the Carcazoids. We need a base, a
moving fortress."

Astra's eyes widened. "You're talking about... an armored
vehicle?" she asked, a flicker of excitement in her voice.
Her scientific mind was already whirring, calculating
the possibilities.

"Exactly," Lucky confirmed. "Something that can withstand
the Carcazoids, something that can carry all our supplies,
and most importantly, something that can get us out of
here, permanently."

Gem's artistic imagination took flight. "A war wagon!
A mobile masterpiece of survival!" she exclaimed, her
previous weariness forgotten. "We can paint it, make
it look intimidating!"

Lucky managed a small smile. "First, we need to find
one. This city is a graveyard of vehicles, but we need
something heavy-duty, something that can be reinforced."
He gestured towards the large garage door. "There's a
salvage yard not far from here. It's a long shot, but
it's our best bet."

They spent the rest of the morning preparing. Astra meticulously
checked their remaining medical supplies, making a list
of what they still desperately needed. Gem, surprisingly
focused, helped Lucky gather tools and spare parts from
the auto shop, her artistic eye proving useful in identifying
potentially useful scrap metal.

By midday, they were ready. Their packs were lighter,
their resolve stronger. The fear of the Carcazoids was
still present, a constant hum beneath the surface, but
it was now tempered by a new sense of purpose. The idea
of a mobile home, a sanctuary on wheels, was a powerful
motivation.

As they slipped out of the auto shop, the city seemed
to hold its breath. The sun was high, casting harsh shadows
that offered little concealment. The journey to the salvage
yard would be dangerous, a trek through unknown territory.
But they were no longer just survivors. They were builders,
engineers of their own destiny. The steel caravan awaited.
