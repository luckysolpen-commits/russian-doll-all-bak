**SCENE 10: EXT. CITY OUTSKIRTS - DAY**

The relative safety of the mall was a memory now, replaced by the grim necessity of movement. The Behemoth, their salvaged armored truck, churned onward, leaving the echoing halls behind for the desolate highway. The clues from the security office had pointed them towards a major metropolis, a city of legends in their pre-fall world, now reduced to a sprawling, scarred scar on the landscape. As they approached the city's periphery, the scale of the destruction became overwhelming. Skyscrapers, once proud giants, now sagged like broken teeth, their windows dark, empty eyesores. Roads were buckled and choked with debris, and an unnatural silence, heavier than any they had encountered, pressed in on them.

"This is it," Lucky said, his voice low, scanning the horizon with a practiced, weary eye. "The city. The Nexus is supposed to be somewhere in the heart of this… mess." Astra consulted their crude map, pieced together from salvaged data and old road atlases. "The notes mentioned a 'central hub,' a place of order. A courthouse, maybe? It fits the description of a significant structure, something that might have survived better than most." Gem shivered, despite the truck's struggling heater. "A city this big… even with the Carcazoids, there's gotta be more than just random mutations lurking. What if it's the clowns? Or worse?" Lucky gripped the steering wheel, his gaze fixed on the approaching urban wasteland. "Whatever it is, we're going in. We have to. The Nexus is our only lead. Stay sharp." The truck rumbled onward, a solitary vessel pushing into the maw of a dead civilization, the promise of answers a faint beacon in the encroaching desolation.
