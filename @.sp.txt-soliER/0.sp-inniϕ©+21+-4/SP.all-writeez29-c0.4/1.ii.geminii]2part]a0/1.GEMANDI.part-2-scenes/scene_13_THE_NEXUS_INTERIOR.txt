**SCENE 13: INT. THE NEXUS - DAY**

The heavy gates of the courthouse swung inward with a near-silent hiss, revealing not a dusty, decaying hall of justice, but a world that seemed impossibly removed from the desolation they had endured. The trio stepped through, their boots meeting a floor so polished it reflected their weary faces like a mirror. Walls of seamless, stark white stretched upwards, broken only by subtle, glowing blue light strips that pulsed with a quiet, rhythmic hum. The air was cool, filtered, and carried a faint scent of ozone, a stark, almost sterile contrast to the choked, metallic stench of the outside world.

Their escort, the same silent clone guards from the gate, moved with an unnerving, synchronized precision, their visored heads impassive as they guided the trio through immaculate corridors. No dust, no decay, no sign of the apocalyptic war raging just beyond these walls. Gleaming panels hummed with unseen energy, and automated systems glided silently past, performing duties incomprehensible to the survivors. Lucky kept his shotgun lowered but ready, his eyes constantly scanning, trying to reconcile this place with the world they knew. Astra absorbed every detail, her scientist's mind buzzing with questions about the technology, the power source, the sheer organizational capacity required to maintain such a facility. Gem, usually so vibrant, felt subdued by the sterile perfection, her senses overwhelmed by the sheer alienness of it all. This was not just a secure building; it was a high-tech fortress, a testament to a power and order that felt both awe-inspiring and deeply unsettling.
