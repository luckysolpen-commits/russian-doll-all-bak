**SCENE 9: INT. MALL / SECURITY OFFICE - NIGHT**

Deeper within the cavernous mall, the trio found what looked like a functional security office, its reinforced door surprisingly intact. Lucky and Astra methodically cleared the small space, ensuring no immediate threats lurked among the overturned desks and defunct monitors. Gem, drawn by a glint of metal, began sifting through scattered papers and digital storage devices, her artist's eye for detail proving invaluable.

"Most of this is just garbage," Gem sighed, tossing aside a moldy sales flyer. "But wait... this computer terminal. It's still got a faint power hum. And look, a USB drive plugged into it!" Astra hurried over, carefully extracting the drive. "This is old tech, but maybe... maybe it's still readable." She plugged it into a portable scanner she'd salvaged earlier. Lines of code and fragmented text scrolled across the small screen. "These are security logs... from before the fall. And these other files... they're personal notes. Cryptic."

Lucky leaned closer. "What do they say?" Astra read aloud, her voice growing serious. "'Phase One complete. The Architect's initial gambit is working... Carcazoids are spreading as planned.' Architect? Who's that?" Gem pointed to another fragmented entry. "There's more... 'The Nexus is secure. The contingency plan is in place. They'll never find us there.'" Lucky’s mind raced. "An architect... orchestrating this? And a Nexus... a safe haven? This is bigger than random monsters and looters." The discovery hung heavy in the air, a terrifying revelation that their apocalypse wasn't random, but orchestrated, and that a distant sanctuary might hold answers, or perhaps, more danger.
