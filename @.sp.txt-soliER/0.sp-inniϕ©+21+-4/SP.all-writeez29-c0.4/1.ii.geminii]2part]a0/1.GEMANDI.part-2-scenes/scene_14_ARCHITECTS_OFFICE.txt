**SCENE 14: INT. ARCHITECT'S OFFICE - DAY**

The clone guards led them not to a grand chamber or a prison cell, but to what appeared to be a meticulously organized, sterile laboratory or office. Sleek consoles hummed softly, displaying complex schematics and data streams that flowed like alien rivers. In the center of the room, seated behind a minimalist, obsidian desk, was a man who radiated an unsettling aura of intellect and chilling ambition. His features were sharp, his eyes piercing, and a faint, almost imperceptible smirk played on his lips. This was The Architect.

"Welcome," his voice was smooth, cultured, yet laced with an unmistakable undercurrent of malice. "I've been expecting you. It's refreshing to see such… *determined* survivors. Most have simply crumbled." Lucky, Astra, and Gem exchanged wary glances, their hands instinctively moving towards their weapons. "You're The Architect?" Lucky’s voice was rough, laced with disbelief and barely suppressed rage. "You? You did this?"

The Architect chuckled, a dry, humorless sound. "Did what? Initiate the grand cleansing? Usher in a new epoch? Yes, I suppose I did. This world was stagnant, choked by its own mediocrity and sentimentality. It needed a reset. A catalyst. And I provided it. The Carcazoids, the mutations... they are merely the tools. The grand reset was necessary to forge a world worthy of my design." He gestured expansively with a manicured hand. "Imagine: a world rebuilt, rational, efficient. Free from the chaos of emotion and flawed humanity. And I, of course, will be its architect. A new order, perfected." The sheer, unadulterated narcissism and depravity of his confession struck the trio like a physical blow. Astra’s scientific curiosity warred with her horror, Gem’s optimism withered, and Lucky’s jaw tightened, the weight of understanding the true enemy pressing down on him.
