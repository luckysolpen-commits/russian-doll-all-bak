**SCENE 7: EXT. WOODS - DAY**

The dense canopy of the forest swallowed the sky, plunging the highway into a perpetual twilight. The armored truck, "The Behemoth," rumbled and groaned as Lucky navigated the treacherous, root-choked track that vaguely resembled a road. Gone were the predictable urban ruins; here, every shadow teemed with unknown threats. The trees, twisted and gnarled, seemed to watch them, their branches like skeletal fingers reaching out.

"Visibility is low, Lucky," Astra reported, her eyes scanning the dense undergrowth through the reinforced windshield. "And I'm picking up multiple bio-signatures – varied, none matching the Carcazoids we've encountered. Could be anything out there." Gem, perched in the back, kept a nervous watch through the small rear viewport, her crowbar resting across her lap. "Less organized than the city monsters, maybe. But probably just as hungry. And definitely harder to spot until they're right on top of us." A sudden crack of a branch nearby, followed by a low snarl, made Lucky slam on the brakes. A mutated wolf-like creature, its fur matted and patchy, eyes glowing with unnatural hunger, lunged towards the truck. Lucky swerved violently, the vehicle fishtailing, narrowly avoiding a collision. "Wild things are smarter than they look out here," he grunted, his knuckles white on the steering wheel. "We need to keep moving. This forest isn't giving up its secrets easily, and it sure as hell isn't welcoming us." The journey through the woods was a constant battle against both the terrain and the unseen horrors that lurked within.
