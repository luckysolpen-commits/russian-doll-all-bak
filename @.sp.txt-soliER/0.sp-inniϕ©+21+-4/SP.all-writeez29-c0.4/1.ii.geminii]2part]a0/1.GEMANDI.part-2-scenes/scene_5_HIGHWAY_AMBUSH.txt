**SCENE 5: EXT. HIGHWAY - NIGHT**

The armored truck, now christened "The Behemoth," offered a semblance of security, but as darkness fell, the highway became a more exposed and vulnerable place. Lucky had chosen a relatively clear stretch, hoping the open ground would provide early warning. They huddled inside, the meager light from a battery-powered lantern casting long, dancing shadows. Gem was sharing the last of the jerky, her usual spark dimmed by exhaustion, while Astra meticulously cleaned and reloaded their scavenged shotgun shells. The silence outside was heavy, punctuated only by the distant, unsettling echoes of the ruined world.

Suddenly, the silence shattered. A cacophony of maniacal laughter and guttural shrieks erupted from the surrounding darkness. Figures darted from the ditches, silhouetted against the faint moonlight – gaunt, unnaturally agile humans painted with garish, dried-blood streaks, their eyes wild with a rabid, predatory hunger. Clown thieves. They swarmed the truck, some banging on the reinforced windows with crude weapons, others trying to pry open the doors. "They're here!" Lucky yelled, grabbing the shotgun. "Gem, Astra, stay low! I'll try to push them back!" He blasted a opening volley, sending two figures stumbling back with pained screeches. Gem, crowbar in hand, braced herself by the passenger door, ready to defend their escape route. Astra, her face grim, produced a heavy wrench, her scientific mind calculating angles and weak points even as adrenaline coursed through her veins. The fight was brutal, close-quarters, a desperate struggle against feral desperation, with Lucky's shotgun blasts and Gem's powerful swings keeping the attackers at bay just long enough for Lucky to gun the engine and lurch the Behemoth forward, tires spitting gravel as they escaped the immediate swarm, leaving behind the terrifying howls of those they'd outrun.
