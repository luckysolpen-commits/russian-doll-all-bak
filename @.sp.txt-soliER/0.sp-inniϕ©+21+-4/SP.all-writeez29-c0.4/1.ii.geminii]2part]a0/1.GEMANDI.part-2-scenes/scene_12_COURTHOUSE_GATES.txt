**SCENE 12: EXT. COURTHOUSE GATES - DAY**

The imposing gates of the courthouse loomed, a barrier of polished chrome and silent, heavily armed clone guards. Lucky, Astra, and Gem stood before them, The Behemoth parked a respectful distance behind, its engine idling as if sensing the tension. One of the guards stepped forward, its voice a synthesized, monotone rasp that cut through the air. "Halt. State your purpose. Unauthorized personnel are not permitted entry."

"We have an appointment," Gem said, stepping forward with a confidence that belied the tremor in her hands. She kept her crowbar slung loosely, projecting an image of non-aggression but readiness. "We need to speak with whoever is in charge. We have information regarding the outside. About the chaos." The guard remained motionless, its visor reflecting the dull sky. "Your designation is not recognized. Access denied."

Lucky gripped his shotgun, ready to act, but Gem held up a hand. She took a breath, channeling her natural charisma. "Look, I get it. Rules are rules. But those 'rules' are what got us here, aren't they? Chaos. Destruction. We're not here to cause trouble. We're survivors. We've seen what's out there, and we have intel that might be useful. Maybe even to whoever's running this place. We just need five minutes. Five minutes to talk to whoever's in charge. Isn't that what this place is for? Justice? Order? Let us in for a brief audience, and if we can't help, you can send us back out to the wolves. But denying us this chance... that's not justice, is it?" Her words, a blend of earnestness and sharp logic, hung in the air. The guards remained impassive, but a subtle shift, a nearly imperceptible pause, suggested her words had registered. A fraction of a second later, a voice, slightly more nuanced than the guards', crackled from the lead guard's comm. "Stand by." A tense silence stretched as they waited, the fate of their entry hanging precariously in the balance.
