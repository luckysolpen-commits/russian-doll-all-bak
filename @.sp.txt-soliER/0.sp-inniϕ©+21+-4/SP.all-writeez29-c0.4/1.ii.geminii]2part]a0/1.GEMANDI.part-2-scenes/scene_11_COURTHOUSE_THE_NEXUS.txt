**SCENE 11: EXT. COURTHOUSE (THE NEXUS) - DAY**

Following the fragmented clues, the sprawling metropolis slowly solidified into a tangible destination. Amidst the skeletal remains of skyscrapers and choked avenues, one structure stood apart: a colossal courthouse, impossibly pristine. Its marble facade was unblemished, its grand windows gleamed, reflecting the dull sky like polished obsidian. It was an anomaly, a bastion of order in a world of chaos. This was The Nexus.

But the sanctuary was not unguarded. Standing sentinel at the imposing gates were figures that sent a fresh wave of unease through the trio. Identical, clad in stark, advanced tactical gear, their visors obscured their faces, rendering them featureless and unnerving. They were heavily armed, their weapons held with an unnerving stillness that spoke of rigorous training and absolute discipline. As The Behemoth rumbled to a halt before the gates, the figures moved with chilling synchronicity, blocking their path. Their movements were precise, devoid of emotion, a stark contrast to the feral desperation the survivors had grown accustomed to. Lucky's hand instinctively tightened on his shotgun. "Well, well," he muttered, his voice low. "Looks like The Nexus isn't exactly rolling out the welcome mat." Astra noted their gear with scientific interest, while Gem clutched her crowbar, a grim determination settling on her face. This was no haphazard group of survivors; this was something far more organized, and potentially, far more dangerous.
