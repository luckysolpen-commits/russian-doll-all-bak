**SCENE 18: EXT. NEXUS ROOFTOP - DAY**

With The Architect defeated and the vial of Mutagen secured, their immediate goal shifted from survival to escape. Lucky, shotgun still smoking, kicked open the reinforced door leading upwards. "This way! The logs mentioned a private craft! Rooftop!" They scrambled through the sterile, but now damaged, corridors of the Nexus, dodging automated defense systems that had activated in the wake of the Architect's defeat, and fending off the last few, seemingly autonomous, clone guards. The air grew thinner, and the distant sounds of the city’s decay seemed to seep even into this high-security bastion.

They burst onto the rooftop helipad, the wind whipping around them. And there it was: sleek, impossibly advanced, and utterly out of place against the ravaged cityscape. The Architect’s private spacecraft. It was a marvel of engineering, its hull shimmering, devoid of the clunky, utilitarian feel of salvaged tech. Lucky immediately moved to secure the cockpit, Astra scanning the ship's external systems for any immediate threats or traps. Gem, ever the pragmatist, was already securing the Mutagen vial in a padded container. "Let's not linger," Lucky urged, his eyes scanning the horizon. "This place probably isn't stable now. And who knows what else The Architect had waiting." With a final glance back at the crumbling, corrupted world below, they boarded the spacecraft, the future uncertain, but their mission clear: reach Mars.
