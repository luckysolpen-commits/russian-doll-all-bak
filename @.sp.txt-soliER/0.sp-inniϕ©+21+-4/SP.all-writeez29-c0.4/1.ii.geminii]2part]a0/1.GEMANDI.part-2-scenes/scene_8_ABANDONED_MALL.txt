**SCENE 8: EXT. ABANDONED MALL - DUSK**

Emerging from the oppressive woods, the trio spotted it on the horizon: a colossal structure, a monument to consumerism now choked by nature and decay. The "MegaPlex Mall," its faded sign barely legible, loomed like a wounded beast. Its vast parking lot was a wasteland of rusted car husks and encroaching weeds. As dusk settled, casting long, eerie shadows, the sheer scale of the place became apparent.

"Think it's safe?" Gem asked, her voice hushed, awed by the sheer emptiness. Lucky scanned the perimeter, his shotgun ready. "Safer than the woods, maybe. More defensible. But places this big... they can hide anything. Good or bad." Astra was already noting the architectural features. "It's a concrete fortress, Lucky. If we can secure the main entrances and clear a section, it could be a solid base. Think of the resources – the stores, the back rooms..."

Cautiously, they drove The Behemoth through a gaping hole in the main entrance, the crunch of debris under its tires echoing unnervingly within the cavernous space. Inside, the vastness was suffocating. Storefronts were dark, their windows shattered or boarded up. Ghostly mannequins stood sentinel in the dimming light, their plastic smiles mocking the desolation. The silence was profound, broken only by the drip of water and the distant, unsettling creaks of the building settling. It was a tomb, but for Lucky, Astra, and Gem, it represented a chance for a true sanctuary, a fortified stronghold from which to plan their next move, provided they could clear it of whatever horrors might have claimed it.
