**SCENE 4: EXT. SALVAGE YARD - DAY**

The endless expanse of rusted metal and skeletal vehicles stretched before them, a graveyard of forgotten machinery. Lucky had spotted it first – a hulking shape half-buried under a mountain of scrap, its reinforced plating and heavy-duty tires hinting at a purpose beyond simple transport. "That... that could be it," he breathed, the first flicker of genuine hope in days igniting in his eyes. "An armored truck. If we can get it running, it's a mobile base. Shelter, defense, range."

The next few days blurred into a gritty montage of sweat, grime, and relentless work. Lucky, drawing on his emergency services background, took the lead on mechanics, wrestling with seized engines and jury-rigging fuel lines with scavenged hoses. Astra, ever the scientist, worked on integrating their limited medical supplies into a dedicated compartment, fashioning a makeshift water purification system from salvaged pipes and filters, and even managed to coax a sputtering short-range radio to life. Gem, with her keen eye for detail, scoured the yard for anything useful – spare tires, sturdy tools, lengths of chain for makeshift defenses, and even found a surprisingly intact set of sleeping bags amidst the decay. They hammered, welded, and tinkered, their efforts a desperate symphony against the backdrop of the silent, dead city. Each challenge, from a broken alternator to a lack of usable welding rods, was met with ingenuity born of necessity. Slowly, painstakingly, the brute of a truck began to respond, its engine coughing to life, a rumbling testament to their combined will to survive and push onward.
