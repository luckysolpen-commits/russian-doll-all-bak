**SCENE 2: EXT. CITY STREETS - DAY**

The air inside the collapsed office building offered little comfort, only a temporary reprieve from the relentless decay. Lucky, his shotgun still warm from earlier tension, nudged Astra. "We can't stay put. We need supplies. Water, food, anything to keep us going. Those things outside, they're still out there." Astra nodded, already consulting a crumpled map scavenged from a desk drawer, her brow furrowed. "According to this, there might be a small commercial block a few blocks east. Less likely to be picked clean than major stores, but potentially more dangerous to reach."

They emerged cautiously, the street a treacherous obstacle course of overturned vehicles, shattered glass, and twisted rebar. Gem, ever practical, pointed her crowbar towards a glint of metal half-buried in debris. "Looks like a busted toolbox over there. Might be something useful, maybe a wrench or some scrap metal." As Lucky covered their approach with his shotgun, Astra scanned the upper floors of the surrounding buildings, her eyes sharp for any movement. It was Gem who heard it first – a wet, shuffling sound, amplified by the eerie quiet.

"Uh, Lucky? Sounds like company," she whispered, her usual levity gone, replaced by a cold focus. Emerging from a darkened alleyway ahead, a pack of Carcazoids – grotesque, biomechanical horrors – shambled into view, their multifaceted eyes glinting with predatory hunger. Lucky immediately pushed Astra and Gem behind a half-crushed delivery truck. "Astra, find us another route! Gem, stick close!" Gunshots cracked through the air as Lucky laid down suppressing fire, buying them precious seconds. Astra, darting between overturned cars, pointed to a narrow gap between two leaning buildings. "This way! It looks tight, but it might lead us away from them!" They sprinted, the guttural screeches of the Carcazoids echoing behind them, a harrowing chase through the urban graveyard that tested their teamwork and resolve to their limits.
