**SCENE 20: EXT. SPACE - DAY**

With a final, violent shudder, the spacecraft tore through the tattered remnants of Earth's atmosphere. The screaming roar of ascent softened to a powerful hum as they broke free, entering the silent vacuum of space. Below them, their home planet receded – a bruised, scarred marble against the infinite black. Continents were fractured, oceans seemed choked with a sickly haze, and the scars of the Architect's "grand cleansing" were horrifyingly apparent even from orbit. It was a tragic, broken world, but for the first time in months, a flicker of genuine hope ignited within the trio, held within a small, glowing vial secured in the cabin.

Lucky, Astra, and Gem looked out at the receding Earth, their faces illuminated by the distant starlight and the soft glow of the ship's controls. There was no triumph, only a grim, shared determination. They had survived, they had retrieved the potential cure, but the battle was far from over. Mars awaited, a distant, red promise holding both the possibility of salvation and the threat of new, unknown dangers. The Architect's legacy was a broken planet and a desperate mission. As the spacecraft picked up speed, leaving Earth behind, their grim faces reflected not just the exhaustion of their ordeal, but the steely resolve to see their fight through, no matter the cost. The red planet was their next destination, and whatever awaited them there, they would face it together.
