**SCENE 6: INT. ABANDONED HOUSE - DAY**

The abandoned house offered meager shelter, a fragile barrier against the harsh world outside. Dust motes danced in the slivers of light piercing through boarded-up windows, illuminating the sparse, decaying remnants of a life long gone. Lucky, still tense from the highway ambush, methodically checked their meager supplies while Gem, ever resourceful, tinkered with a broken flashlight, trying to coax it back to life. Astra, however, was hunched over a tattered map they’d salvaged, cross-referencing it with notations from a previous survivor’s journal they’d found.

"It doesn't make sense," she murmured, her finger tracing a series of almost imperceptible marks on the map, correlating them with recent Carcazoid sightings they'd noted. "The way they're moving... it's not random. Not anymore." Lucky paused, his shotgun still in hand. "What do you mean, Astra? They're monsters, they move where they smell prey."

"No, Lucky," she insisted, her scientific rigor kicking in. "Look at these clusters. They're not just converging on potential food sources. They're exhibiting coordinated flanking maneuvers, almost like they’re being directed. And these patterns... they're too precise, too deliberate for simple instinct." Gem, pausing her work, peered over Astra's shoulder. "Directed? Like... someone's telling them where to go?" A chill went down Lucky's spine. The Carcazoids were a horrifying threat, but the idea of an intelligence orchestrating their movements, guiding their destructive path, was far more insidious. It meant their struggle for survival was not just against mindless beasts, but against a cunning, unseen puppeteer.
