**SCENE 1: EXT. RUINED CITY - DAY**

The air tasted of pulverized concrete and something metallic, a grim perfume of the world's end. Lucky, his shotgun held at a low ready, scanned the skeletal remains of skyscrapers that clawed at a perpetually overcast sky. Dust motes danced in the weak shafts of light that pierced the urban decay, illuminating piles of rubble that choked once-grand boulevards. Each gust of wind carried the distant, guttural chitters of Carcazoids, a constant, unnerving reminder that they were never truly alone.

Beside him, Astra, her scientific mind already cataloging the environmental desolation, pointed a gloved finger towards a partially collapsed office building. "That structure might offer some initial cover, Lucky. The facade is heavily damaged, but the core seems more intact. We need to prioritize shelter before nightfall." Her voice was calm, analytical, but her eyes, usually bright with curiosity, were now sharp with the constant vigilance of a survivor.

Gem, her pink hair a defiant streak of color against the monochrome ruin, gripped her trusty crowbar tighter. "Cover's good, but what about actual *stuff*? My stomach's growling louder than those freaks outside. If we're gonna hole up, let's make sure we can actually *live* in it for more than an hour." Her usual artistic flair was muted, replaced by a more grounded, pragmatic desperation. A distant scraping sound, closer this time, made them all freeze. Lucky signaled for them to move, their movements economical and hushed as they began their cautious approach towards the dubious sanctuary, the ever-present threat of Carcazoids a palpable weight in the air. The hunt for survival had begun in earnest.
