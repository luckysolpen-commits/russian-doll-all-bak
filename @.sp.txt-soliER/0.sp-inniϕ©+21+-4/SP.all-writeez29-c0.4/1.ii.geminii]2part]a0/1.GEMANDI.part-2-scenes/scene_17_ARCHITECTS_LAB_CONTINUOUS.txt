**SCENE 17: INT. ARCHITECT'S LAB - CONTINUOUS**

The chamber became a maelstrom of destruction. Lucky, dodging energy blasts from the monstrosity’s integrated cannons, used his shotgun to target weak points in the hull, while Astra, dodging debris, fired off salvaged EMP grenades that momentarily crippled the colossal machine’s targeting systems. Gem, a whirlwind of furious energy, used her crowbar to pry open access panels on the lab floor, creating temporary diversions and hazards for the smaller mechanical drones that deployed from the behemoth.

With a desperate, coordinated assault, Lucky exploited a momentary vulnerability exposed by Astra's EMP burst, blasting away at a critical energy conduit. Simultaneously, Gem, with a surge of strength, jammed her crowbar into a joint, causing the behemoth to stagger. The combined assault proved too much. With a deafening shriek of tearing metal and overloading circuits, the monstrous machine convulsed, then crashed to the floor, its lights flickering out. The cockpit hissed open, revealing a battered, defeated Architect, his arrogance replaced by simmering fury. "You… you fools!" he rasped, spitting blood. "You've ruined everything! My perfect order… destroyed!"

He clawed at his console, but it was too late. Lucky was on him, the shotgun pressed to his temple. "It's over, Architect. What have you done?" The Architect, defeated but unbowed, forced a broken, triumphant smile. "Done? I have preserved the *potential*! Look!" He fumbled at his belt, producing a small, glowing vial filled with an iridescent, viscous liquid. "The Mutagen! The very key to reversing this… biological inconvenience! It’s the source of the Carcazoids, yes, but properly synthesized… it’s the cure! But Earth is too corrupted. The labs here… they're primitive. This Mutagen requires the advanced bio-engineering facilities on Mars to be refined into a usable virus. Take it. Go to Mars. It's your only chance… and mine." His words hung in the air, a final, bitter twist to their desperate victory.
