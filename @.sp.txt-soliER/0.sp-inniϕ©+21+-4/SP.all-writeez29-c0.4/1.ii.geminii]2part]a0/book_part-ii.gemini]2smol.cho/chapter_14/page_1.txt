The arena wall slid open, revealing not another horde of
monsters, but a single figure. The Architect. He stood
at the edge of a raised platform, bathed in a cold, blue
light, his cultured smile replaced by a look of chilling
determination. He was no longer the detached manipulator;
he was a combatant, and his eyes burned with a dangerous
intensity.

"So, you survived my little welcoming committee," The
Architect said, his voice echoing through the vast space.
"Impressive. But now, you face the true architect of your
doom."

He raised his hands, and the air around him shimmered.
From the floor of the platform, sleek, metallic drones
rose, their optical sensors glowing with an ominous red
light. They were armed with energy weapons, their movements
silent and precise. Behind him, the clone guards, who
had been observing from the sidelines, began to advance,
their weapons now aimed directly at the trio.

"He's not fighting fair!" Gem exclaimed, her flamethrower
ready.

"He never was," Lucky grunted, his pipe held defensively.
"Astra, target the drones! Gem, keep the guards off us!
I'll go for the Architect!"

The battle began. The drones, swift and agile, darted
through the air, firing bursts of energy. Astra, with
her scientific precision, aimed her stun gun, disabling
them one by one. Each successful hit sent a drone spiraling
to the ground, its red light fading.

Gem, meanwhile, unleashed a torrent of fire at the advancing
clone guards. The flamethrower, though effective, was
not designed for sustained combat against armored targets.
The guards, though momentarily deterred, continued their
relentless advance, their energy weapons carving lines
of destruction across the arena floor.

Lucky, with a desperate surge of adrenaline, charged The
Architect. The man, surprisingly agile for his age, dodged
Lucky's initial swing, a faint smirk on his face. He
retaliated with a series of precise, almost surgical,
strikes, using a hidden blade that extended from his
wrist. Lucky parried with his pipe, the clang of metal
ringing through the arena.

"You think you can defeat me?" The Architect sneered,
his eyes burning with a fanatical zeal. "I am the future!
I am order! You are merely chaos, a relic of a dying
world!"

"You're a madman!" Lucky retorted, pressing his attack.
He feigned a lunge, then spun, aiming a powerful blow
at The Architect's head. The Architect blocked it, but
the force of the impact sent a jolt up his arm.

Astra, having disabled the last of the drones, turned
her attention to the clone guards. She reconfigured her
stun gun, increasing its power output, and began to fire
rapid bursts, each one sending a guard reeling, their
systems overloading. The air filled with the smell of
burning circuits.

Gem, seeing Lucky struggling, aimed her flamethrower at
the platform, creating a wall of fire that forced The
Architect to retreat, momentarily breaking his concentration.

Lucky seized the opportunity, closing the distance. He
swung his pipe with all his might, aiming for The Architect's
arm, the one with the hidden blade. The blow connected
with a sickening crunch. The Architect cried out, his
blade clattering to the ground.

"The Mutagen!" Lucky demanded, pressing his advantage.
"Where is it?"

The Architect, his face contorted in pain and rage, glared
at him. "You think you've won? This is merely a setback!
My vision will prevail!"

"Not if we stop you," Astra said, her voice firm. She
had joined Lucky on the platform, her stun gun now aimed
directly at The Architect's head. Gem, flamethrower still
ready, covered their backs, keeping the remaining clone
guards at bay.

Cornered, defeated, The Architect finally broke. "It's
in the main lab!" he spat, his voice laced with venom.
"But it's useless to you! It requires specialized processing!
Only the advanced labs on Mars can refine it into a viable
virus!"

Lucky's eyes widened. Mars. The red planet. A new, impossible
challenge. But also, a new hope. "Show us," he commanded.

The Architect, defeated but still defiant, led them through
a hidden passage, deeper into the Nexus. The clone guards,
their programming seemingly overridden by their master's
defeat, stood down. They had won the battle, but the war,
they realized, was far from over. The fate of Earth, and
perhaps even Mars, now rested on their shoulders.
