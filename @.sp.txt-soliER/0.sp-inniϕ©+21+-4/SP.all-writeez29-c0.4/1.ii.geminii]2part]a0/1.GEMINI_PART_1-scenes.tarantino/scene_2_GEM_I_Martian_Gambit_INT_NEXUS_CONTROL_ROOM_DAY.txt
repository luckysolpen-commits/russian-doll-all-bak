**GEM&I: Martian Gambit**

**Scene 2**

**INT. NEXUS CONTROL ROOM - DAY**

CONTINUOUS

Astra turns back to her terminal, her fingers flying across the keyboard. She pulls up a new set of files from the Architect's encrypted logs.

<center>ASTRA</center>
> And according to the Architect's logs... such a thing exists.

A new window opens on the main screen. It shows sleek, multi-limbed robots, designed for terraforming and pest control on a planetary scale.

<center>ASTRA</center>
> They're on Mars. A massive shipment, ready for a now-defunct colonization project.

Lucky turns from the window, a spark of his old self returning to his eyes.

<center>LUCKY</center>
> Mars. Of course. The Architect had his escape route planned.

<center>ASTRA</center>
> He didn't just have an escape route. He had a fleet. Or at least, access to one. The logs mention a private spacecraft, the "Seraph's Folly," docked at the old Ares launch facility. It's our only shot.

Gem jumps to her feet, the joy-buzzer clattering to the floor.

<center>GEM</center>
> A road trip! To space! We haven't done that in a while.

<center>LUCKY</center>
> It won't be a joyride. The Ares facility is three hundred miles away. Three hundred miles of badlands, broken highways, and whatever is still crawling out there.

<center>ASTRA</center>
> And that's just the start. Once we get to Mars, we have to convince the colony there to just... give us their entire fleet of advanced robotics. The logs are encrypted, but it seems the colony is run by a corporation. The Black Queen Conglomerate.

The name sends a chill through the room. The Black Queen was a ghost in the machine of the old world, a shadowy figure tied to corporate espionage and illegal biotech.

<center>LUCKY</center>
> We'll handle it. We always do. First things first. We need a vehicle. Something sturdy, something we can fix, something that can handle the roads... and the things that live off them.

Gem grins, her eyes alight with a familiar, wild energy.

<center>GEM</center>
> I think I saw a salvage yard on the way in. Full of old trucks and buses. Let's go shopping.

**FADE OUT.**
