**GEM&I: Martian Gambit**

**Scene 14**

**INT. SERAPH'S FOLLY (MESS HALL) - SPACE**

The mess hall is as sleek and sterile as the rest of the ship. The trio is sitting around a table, eating nutrient paste from metallic pouches.

The initial adrenaline of the escape has worn off, replaced by the quiet monotony of space travel.

<center>GEM</center>
> I miss real food.

<center>ASTRA</center>
> This is real food. It has all the nutrients your body needs.

<center>GEM</center>
> But does it have soul?

<center>LUCKY</center>
> We have more important things to worry about than the soul of our nutrient paste.

He looks at Astra.

<center>LUCKY</center>
> The Black Queen Conglomerate. What do we know about them?

<center>ASTRA</center>
> Not much. They were a shadowy player in the corporate world before the collapse. Specialized in biotech, off-the-books research, that sort of thing. The kind of company you hire when you don't want a paper trail.

<center>LUCKY</center>
> So, not the kind of people who are going to give us an army of robots out of the goodness of their hearts.

<center>ASTRA</center>
> Unlikely. We'll have to be careful. We don't know what we're walking into.

<center>GEM</center>
> Maybe they're nice! Maybe they'll give us the robots and a lifetime supply of non-soul-crushing food.

<center>LUCKY</center>
> We prepare for the worst, and hope for the best.

<center>ASTRA</center>
> The worst, in this case, is a highly advanced, morally ambiguous corporation with a monopoly on the only habitable planet besides the one we just fled.

<center>GEM</center>
> So... not great odds.

<center>LUCKY</center>
> We've beaten worse.

He looks at them, his expression serious. The weight of their mission is palpable.

**FADE OUT.**
