**Chapter 6: Smokey's Warning**

Lauren leaned against a wall in the Mars Mercs' common room, watching as Kaida led a 
training session with Vesper and Lyra. The air was thick with the smell of sweat and 
metal, and the sound of gunfire filled her ears. She felt a sense of belonging among 
these women who had become like sisters to her.

Just then, a grizzled mercenary with a scar above her left eyebrow ambled into the 
room. her name was Smokey, and Lauren had heard wherpers about her reputation as one 
of the most ruthless mercenaries on Mars.

Smokey's eyes scanned the room before settling on Lauren. He nodded at her, her 
expression unreadable. "Hey, kid," he said, her voice like gravel. "Time for a chat."

Lauren followed Smokey out into the facility's corridors, the sound of gunfire growing
fainter behind them.

"So, what do you want to talk about?" Lauren asked, trying to keep her tone light 
despite the serious look on Smokey's face.

Smokey's gaze narrowed. "You're getting in deep, kid. You need to watch your back 
around here."

Lauren's instincts kicked in. "What are you talking about?"

"Lucky," Smokey said, her voice low and urgent. "He's got secrets, kid. Secrets that 
could get you killed."

Lauren felt a shiver run down her spine. "What kind of secrets?"

Smokey glanced around the corridor before leaning in closer. "Red Mass," he whispered.

Lauren's eyes widened. "You mean...the red mass on the surface?"

Smokey nodded. "That's the one. Lucky's involved with it, kid. I'm telling you, stay 
away from him and his operations. You don't want to get caught in the crossfire."

Lauren felt a chill run down her spine. She had heard rumors about Lucky's dealings on
Mars, but she had never put much stock into them.

"What do you know?" Lauren asked, her voice barely above a whisper.

Smokey's expression turned grim. "I've seen things, kid. Things that'll make your 
blood run cold. Lucky's got connections to the highest levels of government and 
industry on Mars. He's playing both sides against each other, using his clones to do 
his dirty work."

Lauren felt like she had been punched in the gut. She had suspected that something was
off about Lucky's operations, but this was far more sinister than anything she could 
have imagined.

"Why are you telling me this?" Lauren asked, her voice shaking slightly.

Smokey's eyes locked onto hers. "Because I care, kid. You're a good kid, and you 
deserve to know the truth. But don't go thinking about taking on Lucky yourself. He's 
got resources and connections that'll crush anyone who gets in his way."

Lauren nodded, her mind reeling with the implications. She felt like she was standing 
at the edge of a precipice, staring into an abyss.

"What do I do?" Lauren asked, feeling helpless.

Smokey's expression turned grim again. "Keep your head down, kid. Stay out of Lucky's 
way and focus on your own survival. You're not alone; Kaida and her team will have 
your back."

Lauren nodded, a sense of determination growing within her. She knew that she couldn't
just sit back and do nothing. She had to uncover the truth behind Lucky's operations 
and put an end to whatever evil he was perpetuating.

As Smokey walked away, Lauren felt a strange sensation wash over her. It was as if she
had been given a glimpse into a world beyond the surface of Mars, a world where the 
stakes were high and the players were ruthless.

She knew that she couldn't turn back now. She had to keep pushing forward, no matter 
what dangers lay ahead.

Lauren took a deep breath and squared her shoulders. It was time to take control of 
her destiny and uncover the secrets that Lucky and his clones were hiding.

The game was on, and Lauren was ready to play.
