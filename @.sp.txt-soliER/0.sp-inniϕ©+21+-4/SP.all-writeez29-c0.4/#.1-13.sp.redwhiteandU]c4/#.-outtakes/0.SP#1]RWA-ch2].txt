**SP: Book 1: Red White And...** 
**Chapter 2: The New Normal**

Lauren followed Astra through a maze of corridors, her senses overwhelmed by the 
sounds and smells of Mars' colony. Everything seemed so... different from what she 
remembered. The air was thick with the scent of recycled oxygen, and the hum of 
machinery filled the air.

As they walked, Lauren caught glimpses of people going about their daily lives. Some 
were dressed in casual attire, while others wore uniforms that seemed to signify their
roles within the colony. She saw Astra nodding to a few individuals, exchanging brief 
greetings or receiving small packets of data.

Lauren's mind whirled with questions. Who was she? What had happened before this... 
new start? And what did it mean that she was on Mars?

Astra led her to a larger room filled with rows of bunk beds and personal storage 
units. The air was thick with the smell of sweat and shampoo.

"Welcome to your quarters, Lauren," Astra said, gesturing to an empty bed near the 
back of the room. "You'll be sharing this space with three other women. We try to keep
the living areas as comfortable as possible, given our limited resources."

Lauren's gaze roamed the room, taking in the unfamiliar faces and routines. She felt 
like a fish out of water, unsure how to navigate this new environment.

Astra continued, "Your schedule will be packed with orientation sessions, training 
modules, and community activities. You'll also have access to counseling services if 
you need them."

As Astra finished speaking, Lauren noticed a figure lingering at the edge of the room.
He was tall, with messy blond hair and piercing blue eyes that seemed to bore into her
very soul.

"Lucky," Astra said, noticing Lauren's interest. "He's one of our more... enthusiastic
residents. You'll be working together on some projects, I think."

Lauren felt a flutter in her chest as Lucky smiled at her, his gaze warm and inviting.
She shook off the feeling, focusing on Astra instead.

"What about my past?" Lauren asked, trying to steer the conversation back to her own 
life. "Do you know who I am or what happened before... this?"

Astra's expression turned sympathetic. "I'm afraid that information is still being 
processed. You were brought here under a... sensitive program. We're working on 
uncovering your identity and history, but it's going to take some time."

Lauren felt a pang of frustration. She was stuck in limbo, unsure what her future held
or who she truly was.

As Astra continued to explain the colony's daily routines and expectations, Lauren's 
gaze kept drifting back to Lucky. There was something about him that drew her in, 
despite his obvious heterosexuality.

She pushed the thought aside, focusing on adapting to her new life on Mars. But as she
settled into her bunk bed that night, Lauren couldn't shake the feeling that Lucky 
would play a significant role in her journey...
