SCENE 10

INT. LUNAR BASE - COMMUNICATIONS ROOM - DAY

SOUND of frantic keyboard clicks, static

Lucky is hunched over a communications console, his brow furrowed in frustration. He's trying to establish a secure line to Earth, specifically to Saoirse, but the system keeps cutting him off.

LUCKY
(Muttering to himself)
Come on, come on... why is this so jammed?

Edge Danger Man enters the room, casually leaning against the doorframe, a knowing smirk on his face.

EDGE
Having trouble with your long-distance calls, boss? Maybe the cosmic phone lines are just a little busy.

Lucky looks up, annoyed.

LUCKY
This isn't funny, Edge. Astra just had a... a very disturbing encounter. Something's wrong with Asteroid-6. It's heading for Earth.

Edge's smirk falters for a split second, then he recovers, shrugging.

EDGE
Asteroid-6? Oh, that old thing. Probably just a sensor glitch. Happens all the time. No need to panic the entire planet over a little space rock.

LUCKY
(Standing up, his voice rising)
It's not a little space rock! Loona Twilight herself warned Astra. It's hollow, it's got dark energy, and it's accelerating!

Edge steps forward, his tone becoming more serious, though still with an underlying casualness.

EDGE
Look, Lucky, I get it. You're worried. But think about the optics. We just got everyone to invest billions in this venture. If we start screaming about rogue asteroids and dark energy, it'll be chaos. And our little moon base? Suddenly not so secret anymore.

LUCKY
(Eyes narrowing)
What are you talking about, 'not so secret'? What do you know about this, Edge?

EDGE
(Shifting uncomfortably)
Just... things. Things that are better left unsaid. Things that could put a lot of people in a lot of trouble. Including me. And my family back on Earth.

Lucky stares at him, realizing the depth of Edge's concern. It's not just about the money; it's about something far more personal.

LUCKY
(Quietly)
Are you saying you're deliberately jamming the comms?

Edge avoids his gaze, walking over to the console and tapping a few keys. The static on Lucky's screen intensifies.

EDGE
Let's just say... I'm ensuring a certain level of operational security. For everyone's benefit. We need to handle this ourselves, Lucky. No outside interference. Not yet.

Lucky tries to override Edge's actions, but the system is locked down. He glares at Edge, a mix of anger and dawning understanding in his eyes.

LUCKY
(Through gritted teeth)
This is insane, Edge. You're putting Earth at risk!

EDGE
(Turning back, his expression grim)
And you're putting *us* at risk by trying to be a hero. Sometimes, Lucky, the best way to save the world is to keep your mouth shut.

FADE OUT.
