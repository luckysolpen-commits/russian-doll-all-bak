SCENE 12

INT. LUNAR BASE - TEMPORARY UN CONFERENCE ROOM - NIGHT

SOUND of hushed, tense conversation

The room is stark, functional. A long, polished table dominates the space. DR. PATEL (50s, sharp, no-nonsense, UN representative) sits at one end, flanked by two other UN OFFICIALS. Edge Danger Man sits opposite them, alone, his usual flamboyant attire replaced by a more subdued, but still stylish, dark suit.

DR. PATEL
(Her voice crisp)
Mr. Danger Man. Let's be clear. The United Nations is facing an unprecedented global crisis. We are not here for theatrics.

EDGE
(A charming, confident smile)
And I, Dr. Patel, am here for solutions. Practical, effective solutions. My team and I have been at the forefront of deep-space exploration for months. We have intimate knowledge of the asteroid belt, and more importantly, of 'The Diabolus' itself.

UN OFFICIAL 1
(Skeptical)
Your team's initial reports on Asteroid-6 were... less than comprehensive. In fact, they were quite dismissive.

Edge doesn't flinch.

EDGE
(Smoothly)
Early days. Preliminary data. We've since conducted more in-depth analysis. We understand its unique properties. Its trajectory. Its... potential.

Dr. Patel leans forward, her gaze piercing.

DR. PATEL
And what do you propose, Mr. Danger Man? We have our own teams, our own resources. Why should we entrust this critical mission to you?

EDGE
(Leaning back, a calculated confidence)
Because we're already here. We have the infrastructure, the experience, and frankly, the audacity to get the job done. You send in your teams, you waste precious time and resources. We offer a streamlined, efficient approach. And in return...

He pauses, letting the silence hang.

EDGE (CONT'D)
...we require full operational autonomy. And... immunity. From any and all past inquiries. A clean slate, Dr. Patel. For services rendered in saving your planet.

Dr. Patel's eyes narrow. She exchanges a look with her officials. The audacity is breathtaking, but the offer is tempting. Time is of the essence, and Edge's team is already on site.

DR. PATEL
(After a moment)
Immunity for what, precisely, Mr. Danger Man?

EDGE
(A slight shrug)
Let's just say... my past is colorful. And entirely irrelevant to the current crisis. Focus on the future, Doctor. The very immediate future of Earth.

Dr. Patel considers him, then nods slowly.

DR. PATEL
If your team can present a viable plan to deflect or destroy 'The Diabolus,' and execute it successfully... we will grant your request for immunity. And provide additional resources as needed. But failure, Mr. Danger Man, is not an option.

Edge grins, extending a hand across the table.

EDGE
Deal, Dr. Patel. You won't regret it. We're about to make history.

Dr. Patel shakes his hand, her expression still stern, but with a hint of reluctant acceptance. Edge has gotten his way.

FADE OUT.
