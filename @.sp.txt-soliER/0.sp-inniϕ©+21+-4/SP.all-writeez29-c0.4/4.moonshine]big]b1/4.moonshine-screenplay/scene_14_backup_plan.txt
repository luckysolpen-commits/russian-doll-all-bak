SCENE 14

INT. LUNAR BASE - SECURE CONFERENCE ROOM - NIGHT

SOUND of hushed, urgent conversation

Lucky, Astra, Gem, Jin, Min, and Yoon are gathered around a holographic table. The mood is grim. Edge's presentation to the UN plays silently on a smaller screen in the corner, its absurdity highlighted by their serious expressions.

LOONA (Hologram, shimmering softly)
(Her voice a low, concerned whisper)
His plan is... inefficient. And dangerous. It will not work.

GEM
(Frustrated)
'Inefficient' is an understatement, Luna. It's a cosmic clown show! We're wasting precious time and resources on vintage laser cannons!

ASTRA
(Pacing)
And the UN is buying it. They're desperate. But this isn't just about deflecting an asteroid anymore. It's about... something else. Something Loona Twilight warned me about.

Lucky slams his fist on the table, a spark of determination in his eyes.

LUCKY
Then we do it ourselves. We come up with a real plan. A backup plan.

Jin, Min, and Yoon, the Korean tear-it users, exchange glances. They've been quietly observing, their expertise in energy manipulation a potential game-changer.

JIN
(Hesitantly)
We've been researching new applications for Tear-It technology. High-powered energy sources. Beyond anything currently deployed.

MIN
(Nodding)
If we could harness enough raw Tear-It power... a focused blast... it could vaporize The Diabolus.

GEM
(Eyes widening)
Vaporize? You mean... completely? No fragments? No ice age?

YOON
(Nodding)
Potentially. But the energy required... it's immense. Unprecedented.

Astra looks at them, a dawning horror in her eyes.

ASTRA
(Voice trembling)
But... the research. The latest Tear-It applications. They involve... clones. Sacrificing clones to generate that kind of power.

Silence descends upon the room. The ethical implications are enormous. Loona's holographic form flickers, her expression unreadable.

LUCKY
(His voice low, but firm)
Think about it, Astra. The alternative. Earth. An ice age. Billions of lives. If it means sacrificing some clones... to save humanity...

He looks at each of them, his gaze unwavering. Gem looks conflicted, but the Koreans nod slowly, their faces grim but resolute. They understand the stakes.

ASTRA
(Her voice barely a whisper)
This is... a terrible choice.

LUCKY
(Nodding)
It is. But sometimes, terrible choices are the only ones we have left. We need to explore this. We need to know if it's even possible.

FADE OUT.
