Edge's grin returned, wider and more triumphant than before, a predatory
smile that spoke of victory and cunning. He extended a hand across
the table towards Dr. Patel, his movements fluid and confident, as
if sealing a deal that had been predetermined from the start. "Deal,
Dr. Patel. You won't regret it. We're about to make history. The kind
of history that gets written in textbooks, not just whispered in back
alleys." His eyes sparkled with a mixture of triumph and a subtle,
almost imperceptible hint of something darker, something that spoke
of a past he was eager to leave behind. He knew that such immense
profit often came with equally immense risks, and his instincts, honed
by years of navigating treacherous situations, were screaming caution.
But with Lucky at the helm, he felt a cautious optimism, a belief
that they could overcome any obstacle. She was ready to embrace the
unknown, to build something truly extraordinary. The silence that
followed was not empty, but filled with the unspoken understanding,
the shared commitment to this audacious new dream. The war was over,

scientific breakthroughs that lay just beyond their grasp. This was
a chance to be pioneers, to write their names in the stars, not with
the blood of war, but with the sweat of innovation and the triumph
of human ingenuity. He felt a surge of adrenaline, a familiar rush

something new, something lasting, something that would benefit all
of humanity. And he, Lucky, was at the forefront of it all.
