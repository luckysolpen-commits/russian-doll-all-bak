Edge gave a slight shrug, a gesture of feigned nonchalance, a subtle
movement that belied the intense calculation happening behind his eyes.
He knew he had them cornered, and he was enjoying every moment of it.
"Let's just say... my past is colorful," he began, his voice smooth
and unruffled, a hint of amusement in his tone. "A tapestry woven
with threads of daring escapades, a few minor... misunderstandings
with various planetary authorities, and a general disregard for conventional
boundaries. But entirely irrelevant to the current crisis. Focus on
the future, Doctor. The very immediate future of Earth. Because right
now, your planet is facing an existential threat, and I'm the only
one with the balls, and the skills, to do something about it." He leaned
forward slightly, his gaze fixed on Dr. Patel, a silent challenge in
his eyes. He knew that such immense profit often came with equally
immense risks, and his instincts, honed by years of navigating treacherous
situations, were screaming caution. But with Lucky at the helm, he
felt a cautious optimism, a belief that they could overcome any obstacle.
She was ready to embrace the unknown, to build something truly extraordinary.
The silence that followed was not empty, but filled with the unspoken
understanding, the shared commitment to this audacious new dream. The
war was over, but their journey had just begun. And for the first
time in a long time, Lucky felt truly alive. He imagined the vast,
unexplored reaches of the asteroid belt, the untold riches waiting
to be discovered, the scientific breakthroughs that lay just beyond
their grasp. This was a chance to be pioneers, to write their names
in the stars, not with the blood of war, but with the sweat of innovation
and the triumph of human ingenuity. He felt a surge of adrenaline,
a familiar rush that had once accompanied him into battle, but now
fueled by a different kind of ambition. This was a fight for a better
future, a fight he was more than ready to lead. He looked at Luna,
who was watching him with a subtle, almost imperceptible nod of approval,
a silent acknowledgment of his leadership. He knew that their partnership,
though born of necessity, had the potential to be truly transformative.
The future, he realized, was not just about survival; it was about
creation, about building something new, something lasting, something
that would benefit all of humanity. And he, Lucky, was at the forefront
of it all.

Dr. Patel considered him for a long moment, her gaze unwavering, her
mind racing through the implications of his audacious demand. She knew
that granting him immunity was a dangerous precedent, a potential political
nightmare. But the alternative, the silent, inexorable approach of
'The Diabolus,' was far worse. The decision was made, driven by desperation
and the ticking clock, a grim calculus of survival. She slowly nodded,
a subtle, almost imperceptible movement that conveyed the immense weight
of her decision. "If your team can present a viable plan to deflect
or destroy 'The Diabolus,' and execute it successfully... we will grant
your request for immunity. And provide additional resources as needed.
But failure, Mr. Danger Man, is not an option. The fate of Earth rests
in your hands." Her voice was low, but firm, each word imbued with
the gravity of the situation. She watched him, searching for any sign
of doubt, any flicker of hesitation. But Edge's face remained impassive,
his eyes gleaming with a quiet triumph. He had gotten his way, securing
his freedom and a leading role in humanity's desperate fight for survival.
