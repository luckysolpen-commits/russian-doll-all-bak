Edge didn't flinch. He met the official's gaze with a smooth, unperturbed
demeanor, a subtle smirk playing on his lips. He had faced tougher
crowds, more hostile interrogations, and this was child's play compared
to some of the situations he'd talked his way out of. "Early days,"
he began, his voice calm and even, a master of deflection. "Preliminary
data. We were focused on resource extraction, not existential threats.
Asteroid-6, as you call it, was just another rock in a very big, very
profitable, cosmic pond." He paused, allowing his words to sink in,
a subtle shift in his posture, a hint of seriousness replacing his
usual bravado. "We've since conducted more in-depth analysis. We understand
its unique properties. Its trajectory. Its... potential." He leaned
forward slightly, his voice dropping to a more conspiratorial tone,
as if sharing a secret. "And yes, we understand its true nature now.
It's not just a rock, is it? It's something else entirely. Something
that requires a very specific set of skills to handle. Skills that,
frankly, only I possess." He watched their faces, searching for any
sign of belief, any flicker of hope in their grim expressions. The
air in the room, devoid of any personal touches, felt cold and impersonal,
a perfect setting for the high-stakes negotiations about to unfold.
The faint hum of the lunar base's life support systems provided a
low, constant background drone, a reminder of their fragile existence
on this alien rock. Every detail, from the subtle vibrations of the
ground to the distant glint of metal on the horizon, seemed to heighten
the sense of anticipation, the feeling that something extraordinary
was about to unfold. He was a man who lived for the spotlight, and
the lunar landing pad, with its stark lighting and captive audience,
was his stage. He felt a surge of adrenaline, a familiar rush that
had once accompanied him into battle, but now fueled by a different
kind of ambition. This was a fight for a better future, a fight he
was more than ready to lead. He looked at Luna, who was watching him
with a subtle, almost imperceptible nod of approval, a silent acknowledgment
of his leadership. He knew that their partnership, though born of necessity,
had the potential to be truly transformative. The future, he realized,
was not just about survival; it was about creation, about building
something new, something lasting, something that would benefit all
of humanity. And he, Lucky, was at the forefront of it all.
