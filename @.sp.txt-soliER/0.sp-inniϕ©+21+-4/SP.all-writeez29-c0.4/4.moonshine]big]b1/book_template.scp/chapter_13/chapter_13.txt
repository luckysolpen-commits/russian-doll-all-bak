Chapter 13: The Architect's Summons

The return to the lunar base was not a triumphant homecoming, but a somber procession. The team, once vibrant with hope and the thrill of discovery, moved with a heavy, collective grief. Loona's sacrifice, the ultimate act of selflessness that had made The Diabolus disappear, had left an unfillable void. The moon base, usually a bustling hub of activity, felt eerily quiet, as if even the machinery mourned her loss.

A grand mourning party and funeral were held for Loona on the Hollow Moon. Holographic projections of her, vibrant and full of life, flickered across the main plaza, her melodic voice echoing through the somber gathering. Tears flowed freely, a testament to the profound impact she had made on each of them. She was honored as their queen, a beacon of hope and wisdom who had given everything to save a world that barely knew her.

In the days that followed, a fragile peace settled over the team, a quiet understanding born of shared loss. But this peace was soon shattered by a cryptic summons. A message, seemingly from nowhere, appeared on Lucky's personal comms unit, then on Gem's, Astra's, and the Koreans'. It was from The Architect, a name whispered with a mix of awe and apprehension in certain circles. The message was brief, almost imperious: a summons to his hidden African mine camp, under the guise of "Earth security."

Despite their lingering grief and a deep-seated unease about The Architect's true intentions, they had little choice but to comply. The journey to Earth was swift, filled with a tense silence. As their shuttle descended through Earth's atmosphere, the familiar blue marble seemed both a salvation and a reminder of the immense cost of its preservation.

The African mine camp was a stark contrast to the futuristic elegance of the lunar base. It was a sprawling, industrial complex carved into the rugged landscape, a place of raw power and hidden secrets. Upon their arrival, the team was immediately separated. Lucky found himself ushered into a private, almost spartan, chamber, isolated from his friends. A sense of foreboding settled over him, a chilling premonition that something was terribly wrong.

Meanwhile, Gem, Astra, and the Koreans were led to a briefing room, where The Architect himself awaited them. His presence was commanding, his eyes sharp and calculating. It was there, in that sterile room, that his true, sinister agenda was revealed. He spoke of Loona's sacrifice not as a tragedy, but as a scientific breakthrough, a means to an end. He detailed his research into antimatter technology and warp drives, his voice devoid of emotion as he outlined his plans to exploit the very energies that had consumed Loona. He saw her not as a hero, but as a catalyst, a stepping stone to his own dangerous technological advancements. The realization dawned on them with a sickening lurch: The Architect had orchestrated much of this, manipulating events for his own gain, and now, he sought to capitalize on their pain and Loona's ultimate sacrifice. Lucky's isolation was not a courtesy, but a calculated move, ensuring he couldn't interfere as The Architect laid bare his chilling vision for a future built on stolen power and unchecked ambition.
