"China's threat," Astra murmured, her voice low, almost a whisper,
but sharp enough to cut through the low hum of the command center.
Her eyes, usually so expressive, were narrowed in a way that Lucky
knew meant she was analyzing, dissecting, searching for the hidden
motives behind the sudden peace. "That's what did it. Forced their
hand. They were losing too much, too fast, on the eastern front."
She gestured vaguely towards a holographic map of Earth, where glowing
red lines, representing the Chinese advance, had abruptly halted.
Her fingers, slender and quick, tapped a rhythm against the worn
fabric of her combat trousers, a nervous habit she'd picked up during
the long, tense hours of waiting for battle reports. The air around
her seemed to crackle with her unspoken questions, her inherent distrust
of easy solutions.

Gem, also in his thirties, energetic and always on the lookout for
an advantage, paced excitedly. His movements were quick, almost jerky,
a stark contrast to Lucky's weary stillness. A wide, almost manic
grin stretched across his face as he shed his combat gear, tossing
his helmet onto a stack of empty ration packs with a clatter. He then
peeled off his armored vest, revealing a surprisingly clean, almost
new-looking jumpsuit underneath. The fabric, a light gray, seemed
to absorb the harsh fluorescent light, making him appear even brighter,
more alive. He ran a hand through his short, spiky hair, a gesture
of pure, unadulterated relief. He bounced on the balls of his feet,
his eyes darting around the room, as if searching for something, anything,
to expend his pent-up energy on. He was a man who thrived on action,
and the sudden cessation of hostilities left him buzzing with an almost
frantic energy.

"Can you believe it?" Gem exclaimed, his voice filled with disbelief
and elation, echoing slightly in the cavernous space. He threw his
hands up in a gesture of triumph, then let them fall to his sides,
shaking his head in wonder. "A truce! Just like that! One minute
we're dodging Carc-Zillas on Europa, the next we're shaking hands
with the NewKoreans! No more Carc-Zillas, no more NewKorean skirmishes...
for now, anyway." He added the last part with a slight shrug, a flicker
of his usual pragmatism breaking through his euphoria. He then picked
up a discarded data pad, idly flipping through its contents, his mind
already racing ahead to the next potential opportunity, the next venture.

Lucky sighed, a deep, weary exhalation that seemed to carry the weight
of the entire war. He ran a hand through his hair, pushing back the
strands that had fallen across his forehead. His gaze remained fixed
on the flickering screen, the headlines of peace feeling strangely
insubstantial, like a mirage. "It feels... unfinished," he murmured,
his voice rough with fatigue. "Like the calm before another storm."
He shifted his weight, the worn fabric of his suit rustling softly.
His eyes, usually so keen and observant, were clouded with a deep-seated
unease. He had seen too many truces broken, too many promises shattered,
to fully trust this sudden quiet. The silence, instead of being a comfort,
felt like a heavy blanket, muffling the sounds of the world, but also
the sounds of his own thoughts, leaving him adrift in a sea of uncertainty.
He longed for the familiar rhythm of battle, the clear objectives,
the simple, brutal clarity of survival. This peace, this sudden, unexpected
peace, was far more unsettling.
