"'Inefficient' is an understatement, Luna," Gem burst out, frustration
lacing his voice, his usual boisterousness replaced by a raw, unbridled
anger. He slammed his fist on the holographic table, sending ripples
of light across its surface. "It's a cosmic clown show! A suicide
mission disguised as a heroic endeavor! We're wasting precious time
and resources on vintage laser cannons and outdated strategies, while
Earth hurtles towards its doom!" He paced, his movements agitated,
his hands running through his short, spiky hair, a gesture of pure,
unadulterated exasperation. He imagined the billions of lives on Earth,
oblivious to the charade playing out on the moon, trusting in the
UN's leadership, in Edge's supposed expertise. The thought made his
stomach churn. He was a man of action, of practical solutions, and
Edge's plan was anything but. He felt a surge of adrenaline, a familiar
rush that had once accompanied him into battle, but now fueled by a
different kind of ambition. This was a fight for a better future, a
fight he was more than ready to lead. He looked at Luna, who was watching
him with a subtle, almost imperceptible nod of approval, a silent
acknowledgment of his leadership. He knew that their partnership, though
born of necessity, had the potential to be truly transformative. The
future, he realized, was not just about survival; it was about creation,
about building something new, something lasting, something that would
benefit all of humanity. And he, Lucky, was at the forefront of it
all. The crisp, cold air of the launchpad, tinged with the scent of

out into the vast, unknown expanse of space.

Astra paced, her movements agitated, a nervous energy radiating from
her that seemed to fill the confined space of the conference room.
