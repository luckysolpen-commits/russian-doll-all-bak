Luna's confidence was unwavering, a testament to her meticulous preparation
and her unshakeable belief in her own vision. She met Mr. Kim's gaze
directly, her eyes sparkling with a cool, intelligent light, betraying
not a hint of nervousness. "An initial investment of $500 million,"
she stated, her voice clear and precise, devoid of any hesitation.
She paused, allowing the figure to resonate in the opulent silence
of the habitat. "This covers establishing a lunar base, a state-of-the-art
facility capable of housing our operations and personnel. It includes
the acquisition of cutting-edge extraction equipment, designed to efficiently
and safely harvest resources from even the most challenging asteroid
compositions. And, crucially, it covers the recruitment and training
of a world-class team – engineers, geologists, pilots, and security
personnel, all hand-picked for their expertise and dedication." She
gestured to a new holographic projection that materialized above the
table, displaying a detailed architectural rendering of a sprawling
lunar complex, complete with docking bays, refining facilities, and
living quarters. The image was crisp, vibrant, and utterly convincing.
"As for returns..." she continued, a subtle, almost imperceptible
smile playing on her lips, "we're projecting at least 500% within
the first five years. Dividends distributed quarterly, ensuring a
steady and substantial return on your investment." She watched Mr.
Kim's face, searching for any sign of doubt, any flicker of skepticism.
But his expression remained impassive, his dark eyes unreadable. She
knew that for a man of his caliber, such numbers, while impressive,
were merely a starting point for negotiation. The faint scent of floral
air freshener seemed to intensify, a subtle reminder of the luxurious
world she was offering, a world where such astronomical figures were
not just dreams, but tangible realities. She was not just presenting
a business plan; she was presenting a new paradigm of wealth, a future
where the limits of terrestrial resources were no longer a constraint.

Astra exchanged a look with Lucky, her eyebrows slightly raised, a
silent communication passing between them. The numbers Luna had just
quoted were staggering, almost unbelievable, even for someone who had
seen the immense wealth generated by wartime industries. Her mind, ever
analytical, was already running through the calculations, trying to
find the flaw, the hidden risk that would justify such an astronomical
return. She thought of the countless hours she had spent poring over
financial reports, the meticulous audits she had conducted, and never
had she encountered projections quite so audacious. The sheer scale
of the proposed venture was breathtaking, almost overwhelming. She
shifted in her seat, the plush fabric feeling suddenly too soft, too
luxurious for the gravity of the situation. Her gaze flickered from
Luna to Mr. Kim, then back to Lucky, searching for some shared understanding,
some confirmation that they were all hearing the same thing. The faint
scent of lavender, her small comfort, seemed to deepen, a subtle reminder
of her own grounded nature amidst the dizzying heights of Luna's ambition.
She knew that such immense profit often came with equally immense
risks, and her instincts, honed by years of navigating treacherous
situations, were screaming caution. She was a soldier, accustomed to
calculating risks in terms of lives, not dollars, and the sheer magnitude
of the financial figures felt almost abstract, divorced from the tangible
realities of their existence. She wondered what hidden costs, what
unforeseen challenges, lay beneath the glittering surface of Luna's
presentation. Her hand, resting on the table, instinctively clenched
into a fist, a silent manifestation of her internal struggle. She was
intrigued, yes, but also deeply wary. The ambient music, which had
once seemed soothing, now felt almost ominous, a subtle underscore
to the immense gamble they were being asked to consider.
