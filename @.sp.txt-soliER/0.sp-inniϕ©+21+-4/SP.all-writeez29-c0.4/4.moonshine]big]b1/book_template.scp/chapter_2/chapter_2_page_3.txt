Luna gestured gracefully to the holographic display, her hand sweeping
through the air with an almost ethereal elegance. The display, which
had been shimmering with celestial bodies, now zoomed in, focusing
on a dense, swirling cloud of debris, a cosmic tapestry of rock and
ice. "For years, the focus has been on terrestrial resources," she
began, her voice a silken thread, weaving through the ambient music.
"We've plundered our planet, fought wars over dwindling reserves, and
ignored the boundless wealth that lies just beyond our atmosphere."
She paused, allowing her words to sink in, her gaze sweeping over the
faces of her audience, gauging their reactions. "But the true treasure,
the untapped fortune, lies out there. In the newly discovered asteroid
belt." Her finger, tipped with a faint, almost imperceptible glow,
pointed directly at the holographic projection, highlighting a particularly
dense cluster of asteroids, their surfaces glinting with imagined riches.
She watched their eyes, seeing the flicker of avarice, the dawning
comprehension of the immense scale of her proposition. The air in
the habitat seemed to thicken with anticipation, charged with the
potential of unimaginable wealth. She was a master storyteller, and
her narrative was one of boundless opportunity, a siren song to the
ambitious and the daring. The subtle scent of floral air freshener
seemed to intensify, a subtle reminder of the luxurious world she
was about to unveil. She knew that to truly capture their attention,
she needed to paint a picture not just of profit, but of a new frontier,
a new era of prosperity that would leave the old world behind. And
she was doing it with effortless grace, her every movement, every word,
a carefully choreographed performance designed to enthrall and convince.

The display shifted again, responding to an unseen command from Luna,
highlighting specific asteroids, their projected mineral content glowing
with an almost ethereal light. Each highlighted asteroid pulsed with
a different color, representing a different precious metal or rare
earth element. "We're talking rare earth elements – neodymium, dysprosium
– crucial for our burgeoning electric vehicle and renewable energy
sectors," Luna continued, her voice gaining a quiet intensity, a subtle
urgency that underscored the importance of her words. She moved closer
to the display, her hand tracing the glowing outlines of the asteroids,
as if she could already feel the raw power contained within them.
"These are the building blocks of the future, gentlemen. The very
materials that will power our cities, transport our people, and transform
our lives." She then shifted her focus, her finger moving to another
cluster of asteroids, their glow a brilliant, almost blinding white.
"And precious metals: platinum, palladium, rhodium. The demand is
skyrocketing. Earth's reserves are dwindling, and the cost of extraction
is becoming prohibitive. But out here... out here, these elements
are abundant, waiting to be claimed." Her voice was a hypnotic murmur,
weaving a spell of wealth and opportunity. She painted a vivid picture
of a world transformed by these resources, a world of clean energy,
of advanced technology, of limitless possibilities. She saw the subtle
shifts in their expressions, the widening of eyes, the slight parting
of lips, as they absorbed the magnitude of her proposition. The ambient
music seemed to swell, a triumphant symphony accompanying her words,
underscoring the grandeur of her vision. She was not just selling
a venture; she was selling a dream, a future, and her audience was
beginning to believe.

Mr. Kim, who had been silently observing, his dark eyes missing nothing,
finally spoke. His voice was calm, authoritative, cutting through the
air of excitement like a finely honed blade. "And the investment?
The projected returns?" His questions were precise, direct, devoid
of any emotional inflection, a stark contrast to Gem's earlier exuberance.
He held his antique fan still for a moment, his gaze fixed on Luna,
waiting for her to lay bare the financial realities of her grand vision.
He was a man of numbers, of concrete figures, and while the dream
was appealing, the bottom line was paramount. He had seen countless
schemes, countless promises of untold wealth, and he knew that often,
the most glittering proposals hid the most significant risks. His
expression remained unreadable, a mask of polite interest that concealed
a sharp, calculating mind. He was not easily swayed by rhetoric or
charisma; he required data, projections, a clear and concise breakdown
of the financial implications. The faint scent of sandalwood and old
paper, emanating from him, seemed to ground the ethereal atmosphere
of Luna's habitat, bringing a touch of pragmatic reality to the soaring
ambition. He was the anchor, the voice of reason, in a room filled
with dreams of cosmic riches. He watched Luna, waiting for her to
prove that her vision was not just a fantasy, but a tangible, profitable
reality. His silence had been a powerful statement, a subtle challenge
to Luna to back up her soaring rhetoric with solid financial projections.
And now, he was ready to hear them.
