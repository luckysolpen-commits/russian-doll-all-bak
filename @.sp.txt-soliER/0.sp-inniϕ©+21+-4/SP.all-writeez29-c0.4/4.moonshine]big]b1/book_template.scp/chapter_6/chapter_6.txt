Chapter 6: First Exploration

The sleek, multi-person drilling vessel cut through the silent, star-dusted expanse of the asteroid belt. Inside, the Chaebol team, clad in their space suits, gazed out of the viewports at a dizzying panorama of countless rocks, from tiny pebbles to small mountains, tumbling in silent, ancient orbits. The air inside the vessel hummed with the whirring of machinery and the faint, distant echoes of asteroid impacts.

At the controls, Edge Danger Man was a blur of motion, his face alight with pure exhilaration. He expertly maneuvered the vessel, weaving through the treacherous asteroid field with a reckless abandon that made Lucky's jaw clench. "This is it, folks!" Edge whooped through the comms, his voice filled with unbridled joy. "The wild frontier! And I'm your trusty steed!"

Lucky, seated beside him, gripped the armrests, a muscle twitching in his jaw. He glanced at Astra and Gem, who were trying, and failing, to suppress their amusement. "Edge," Lucky said, his voice strained through the comms, "maybe a little less... flair? And a little more focus on the mission parameters?"

Edge waved a dismissive hand, not taking his eyes off the swirling chaos outside. "Mission parameters are for rookies, Lucky! We're making history here! And speaking of history, behold! Asteroid Alpha-1!"

He pointed to a relatively small, rocky asteroid ahead. With a flourish, he activated the vessel's gravity beam, a shimmering blue light extending from the front. With practiced ease, he began to draw in smaller rocks from the asteroid's surface, pulling them into the vessel's cargo hold.

"See?" Edge crowed. "Child's play! Just a little cosmic vacuuming!" He performed a series of unnecessary, acrobatic maneuvers with the gravity beam, twirling the collected rocks before depositing them. Jin, Min, and Yoon cheered from the back, their comms crackling with excitement as they recorded every move.

"Amazing, Edge!" Jin exclaimed. "Do it again!"

Edge grinned, basking in the adoration. He glanced at Lucky, a triumphant smirk on his face. "Still think your drone skills compare, Lucky? This is real piloting!"

Lucky clenched his jaw. He knew Edge was good, infuriatingly good, but his showboating was grating on his nerves. He forced himself to focus on the data screens, trying to ignore the spectacle unfolding before him.

"Just... get the job done, Edge," Lucky said, his voice tight with suppressed frustration.

Edge laughed, a carefree sound that echoed through the comms. "Oh, I'll get the job done, Lucky. And I'll make it look good while I'm at it!"
