Zig and Zack, particularly adept at navigating the temple’s hidden passages, acted as scouts, their tiny, telepathic voices a constant stream of tactical information. They zipped ahead, their buzzing energy forming intricate patterns as they mapped the labyrinthine corridors, relaying information about the Kings’ forces, their numbers, their frustration. *They are enraged!* Zig’s thoughts vibrated, a wave of pure defiance. *Their thoughts are like boiling acid!* Zack’s thoughts added, a chorus of eager anticipation. The Extremophile Kings, their telepathic shouts echoing through the temple, grew increasingly frustrated. Their thoughts were a mix of fury and disbelief, a chaotic jumble of rage and confusion. They were accustomed to overwhelming force, to brute strength, to simple, direct confrontation. They were not accustomed to cunning, to strategy, to being outsmarted. *They are like ghosts!* Extrmophile King 1’s thoughts raged, a voice like grinding stone, filled with a chilling amusement. *Always one step ahead!* Extrmophile King 2’s thoughts followed, a voice like a winter wind, cold and sharp. *This temple… it fights against us!* Extrmophile King 3’s thoughts concluded, a voice like a poisoned blade, dripping with frustration. Lucky felt a strange sense of exhilaration, a thrill that transcended the fear and the disgust. This was what he was trained for. This was what he was good at. Problem-solving. Infiltration. Analysis. He was a scientist, and this was the ultimate experiment. He was learning to be an Extrmophile. And the more he learned, the more he realized how little he truly knew about this strange, terrifying, and utterly fascinating civilization. He was a weapon, a tool, a means to an end. And he was ready to be wielded. He looked at Lyra, her single golden eye meeting his. He felt her resolve, her determination, her fierce loyalty. They were in this together. And together, they would find a way. They had to. The temple hummed around them, a silent, predatory beast, waiting for them to make a mistake. But they were ready. They were prepared. They were undercover. And they were coming for the Fuzzballs.
