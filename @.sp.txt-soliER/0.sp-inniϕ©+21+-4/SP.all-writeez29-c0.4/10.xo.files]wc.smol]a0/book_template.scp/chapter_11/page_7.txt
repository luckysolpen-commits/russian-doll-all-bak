They continued their retreat, leaving a trail of chaos and destruction in their wake. The temple, as if sensing their intent, seemed to cooperate, its hidden mechanisms responding to their presence, to their desperate need for escape. They triggered ancient pressure plates, their telepathic commands a silent symphony of destruction. Sections of the floor would give way with a groan of ancient stone, dropping more enforcers into unseen pits, their telepathic screams echoing through the darkness. Lucky felt a strange sense of exhilaration, a thrill that transcended the fear and the disgust. This was what he was trained for. This was what he was good at. Problem-solving. Infiltration. Analysis. He was a scientist, and this was the ultimate experiment. He was learning to be an Extrmophile. And the more he learned, the more he realized how little he truly knew about this strange, terrifying, and utterly fascinating civilization. He was a weapon, a tool, a means to an end. And he was ready to be wielded. He looked at Lyra, her single golden eye meeting his. He felt her resolve, her determination, her fierce loyalty. They were in this together. And together, they would find a way. They had to. The temple hummed around them, a silent, predatory beast, waiting for them to make a mistake. But they were ready. They were prepared. They were undercover. And they were coming for the Fuzzballs.
