Lucky, with a monumental effort, forced his new, scaly limbs to move. It was a slow, agonizing process, like moving through thick mud, every muscle screaming in protest. His mind, still battling Lyra’s telepathic hold, felt like a battlefield, a constant struggle between his will and her insidious control. But he pushed through the mental resistance, a desperate, primal urge for freedom overriding the paralysis. He zig-zagged, his movements erratic, making it harder for Lyra to anticipate his next move, to reassert her dominance. He was a broken puppet, but he was moving. He was fighting. He was defying. He felt a surge of grim determination. He was a scientist, a man of logic and reason. But here, his logic was useless. His reason was meaningless. He was a man, and he was utterly, hopelessly lost. But he was not defeated. Not yet. He had survived worse. He had to believe that. He had to believe that they would find a way. They had to. The castle hummed around them, a silent, predatory beast, waiting for them to make a mistake. But they were ready. They were prepared. They were undercover. And they were coming for the Fuzzballs.
