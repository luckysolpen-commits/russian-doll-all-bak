He avoided the main thoroughfares, the grand, crystal-lined avenues where the newly born Extrmophiles now swarmed, their minds a chaotic jumble of primal hunger and nascent intelligence. Instead, he stuck to the shadowed, less-traveled paths, the narrow, winding corridors that snaked through the ancient heart of the Fuzz Castle. He could feel the castle itself, its ancient stones, its hidden passages, its forgotten chambers. It was as if the temple, in its silent protest against Lyra’s desecration, was subtly guiding him, its ancient consciousness a faint whisper in his mind, a counterpoint to the overwhelming roar of the Extrmophile brood. He was a ghost in the machine, a shadow in the light, a silent, unseen force working from within. He felt a surge of grim determination. He was a scientist, a man of logic and reason. But here, his logic was useless. His reason was meaningless. He was a man, and he was utterly, hopelessly lost. But he was not defeated. Not yet. He had survived worse. He had to believe that. He had to believe that they would find a way. They had to. The castle hummed around them, a silent, predatory beast, waiting for them to make a mistake. But he was ready. He was prepared. He was undercover. And he was coming for the Fuzzballs.
