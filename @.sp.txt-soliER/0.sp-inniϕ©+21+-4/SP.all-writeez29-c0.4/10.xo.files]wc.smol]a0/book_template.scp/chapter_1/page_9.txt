Lucky stared at the girl, his mind struggling to process what he was hearing. English. Here. In the middle of an alien jungle, on a planet that wasn’t on any of the charts. It was impossible. It was a miracle. “I… I am… Lucky,” he stammered, the name sounding absurd in this context. “My ship… it crashed. I am… lost.” The girl translated his words for the old male, her voice a soft, melodic counterpoint to his harsh, guttural clicks. The old male listened intently, his dark eyes never leaving Lucky’s face. He said something to the girl, his voice low and gentle. “He says… you are lucky to be alive,” the girl translated. “The jungle does not suffer outsiders gladly.” The old male gestured towards the fire, a silent invitation. “He says… come. Share our fire. Share our food. Tell us your story.” Lucky hesitated for a moment. He was still wary. He was still afraid. But he was also exhausted, and hungry, and desperate. And there was something in the old male’s eyes, a deep, ancient wisdom, that he found himself trusting. He nodded, a single, jerky movement of his head. He took a tentative step forward, then another. The villagers parted to let him pass, their dark eyes following him, their expressions still unreadable. But there was no hostility in their gaze. Only a quiet, watchful curiosity. He sat down by the fire, the warmth of the flames chasing away the chill of the jungle night. A woman offered him a bowl of steaming, savory stew. He took it, his hands trembling. He ate, the food so delicious, so comforting, that he almost wept. He was safe. He was alive. He was… home. Or at least, a home. For now. He looked at the faces around the fire, the strange, beautiful, alien faces. He was a stranger in a strange land. But for the first time since he had crashed on this godforsaken planet, he didn’t feel completely alone.
