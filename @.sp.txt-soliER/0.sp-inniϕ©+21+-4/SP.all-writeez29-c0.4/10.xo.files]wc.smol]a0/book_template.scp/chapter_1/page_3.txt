His training kicked in. Not the scientific training, the other kind. The kind they give you when you sign up for deep space exploration, the kind you hope you’ll never have to use. Survival, Evasion, Resistance, and Escape. SERE. He found a thick, gnarled branch, heavy and solid. He snapped it off a low-hanging tree, the wood splintering with a satisfying crack. He tested its weight, swinging it through the air. It was no plasma rifle, but it had a comforting heft. It was a weapon. A symbol of his will to live. He felt a flicker of his old self, the man who had faced down corporate spies and rogue AI. He was not a victim. He was a survivor. He tore strips of fabric from his tattered UNOMAN suit. The expensive, high-tech material, designed to withstand the vacuum of space, was now reduced to rags. He wrapped the strips around his body, creating a crude, makeshift camouflage. He looked like a caricature of a prehistoric hunter, a caveman in a world of impossible technology. But it was necessary. He had to blend in, to become a part of this savage landscape. He had to become a predator, not prey. The twin suns of Planet X began their descent, casting long, eerie shadows that danced and writhed like living things. The jungle, already a place of unsettling beauty, transformed into a realm of nightmarish wonder. The bioluminescent plants glowed with a more intense, almost feverish light. The air grew colder, and the sounds of the jungle changed. The chittering and chirping of the day creatures gave way to the low growls and guttural calls of the night predators. He knew he was being watched. He could feel it, a prickling sensation on the back of his neck, a cold knot of dread in the pit of his stomach. He was an intruder here, an alien. And the jungle wanted him gone.
