The world was a symphony of screaming metal and blaring alarms. Lucky’s head throbbed, a dull, persistent drum against the inside of his skull. He tasted blood, coppery and sharp. His UNOMAN suit, his top-of-the-line exploration armor, was a coffin of twisted metal and shattered composites. The viewscreen, once a crystal-clear window to the cosmos, was a spiderweb of cracks, displaying a fractured image of an alien sky. He was alive. That was something. He unbuckled himself, the click of the mechanism deafeningly loud in the sudden silence. The suit’s emergency systems had failed, but the core structure had held. Barely. He pushed open the mangled hatch, the metal groaning in protest. The air that hit him was thick, humid, and overwhelmingly alien. It smelled of damp earth, of strange, sweet blossoms, and something else, something wild and musky. He stumbled out of the wreckage, his legs unsteady. The suit was a write-off. A multi-billion-dollar piece of hardware, reduced to a heap of scrap. He was alone. On a world that wasn’t on any of the charts. A wave of dizziness washed over him, and he leaned against the hull of the ruined suit, his breath coming in ragged gasps. The jungle that surrounded him was a riot of impossible colors. Towering trees, their bark a shimmering, bioluminescent blue, reached for the sky like skeletal fingers. The ground was a carpet of phosphorescent moss, glowing with an eerie, internal light. Strange, bird-like creatures with leathery wings and razor-sharp beaks circled overhead, their cries a discordant symphony. He was a scientist, a man of logic and reason. But this place… this place defied all logic. It was a world of savage beauty, a world that felt ancient and untamed. And he was a trespasser. A castaway. He had to survive. He had to find a way back. But first, he had to get away from the crash site. The explosion would have attracted attention. And he had a feeling that the attention wouldn’t be friendly. He took a deep breath, the alien air filling his lungs. It was time to move.
