The peace of the Afink village was a fragile thing, a delicate bubble in a sea of primal chaos. And now, the bubble was beginning to tremble. It started as a low, almost imperceptible hum, a subtle vibration that seemed to emanate from the very air itself. At first, Lucky thought it was his imagination, a phantom echo of the vision he had experienced at the Heart of the Ink. His senses, still buzzing with a newfound acuity, were playing tricks on him. But then he saw the way the villagers looked at each other, their dark eyes wide with a silent, shared anxiety. He saw the way they cast furtive glances at the jungle, as if expecting something to emerge from the shadows. He saw the way their hands trembled as they went about their daily chores. The hum was real. And it was growing stronger. It was a sound that was not a sound, a feeling that was not a feeling. It was a pressure in his ears, a vibration in his bones, a sense of… wrongness. The jungle, which had once been a symphony of strange and beautiful sounds, was now eerily silent. The creatures that had once filled the air with their calls were now hiding, their instincts telling them that something was coming. Something big. Something dangerous. The air grew thick and heavy, charged with a static electricity that made the hairs on his arms stand on end. The sky, once a brilliant tapestry of alien constellations, was now a dull, featureless gray. The world was holding its breath. And Lucky, the man of science, the man of logic, felt a primal fear that he had not felt since the day he had crashed on this godforsaken planet. He didn’t know what was coming. But he knew that it was not friendly. He knew that it was not natural. And he knew that it was coming for them.
