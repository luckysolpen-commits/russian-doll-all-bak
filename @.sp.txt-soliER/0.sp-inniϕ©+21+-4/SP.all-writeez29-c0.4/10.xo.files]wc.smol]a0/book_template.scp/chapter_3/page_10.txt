The hum reached a deafening crescendo, a physical force that shook the very foundations of the village. The ground trembled, the trees swayed, the air crackled with an unseen energy. The Afink’s chant faltered, their voices drowned out by the overwhelming roar of the awakening city. And then, a new sound. A sound that was unlike anything Lucky had ever heard before. It was a tearing, a ripping, a shredding of the very fabric of reality itself. It was the sound of a veil being torn, of a dimension being breached, of a cage being broken. The sky, which had been a dull, featureless gray, began to shimmer, to distort, to… dissolve. A crack appeared in the heavens, a jagged, luminous fissure that spread across the sky like a spider’s web. And through the crack, he could see it. The city. The floating, impossible, terrifying city of the Extrmophiles. It was no longer a vision, no longer a whisper, no longer a dream. It was real. It was here. And it was coming for them. The whispers became a roar, a tidal wave of alien thought that crashed over the village, extinguishing the last vestiges of the Afink’s chant. The villagers fell to their knees, their faces buried in their hands, their bodies trembling with a mixture of terror and ecstasy. They were not just dancing with the storm. They were being consumed by it. And Lucky, the man of science, the man of logic, the man who had tried so desperately to find a rational explanation for it all, could only stare in mute, horrified fascination as the world he had come to know was torn apart. The awakening was no longer a subtle premonition. It was a violent, imminent, and utterly cataclysmic reality. The Extrmophiles were here. And the world would never be the same.
