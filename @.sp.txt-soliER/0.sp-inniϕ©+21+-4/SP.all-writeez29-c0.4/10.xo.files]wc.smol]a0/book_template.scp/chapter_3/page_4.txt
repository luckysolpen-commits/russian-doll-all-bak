Skeptical, but with a sliver of desperate curiosity, Lucky approached the wall. He reached out a hesitant hand, his fingers brushing against the rough, sun-baked clay. It was warm to the touch, but that was all. He felt nothing. No vibration, no energy, no ancient memories. Just a wall. “I don’t feel anything,” he said, a note of frustration creeping into his voice. He felt foolish, a man chasing ghosts in a world he didn’t understand. Lyra looked at him, her dark eyes filled with a patient understanding that bordered on pity. “You are trying too hard, Lucky,” she said softly. “You are trying to force it, to analyze it, to dissect it with your mind. But this is not something you can understand with your mind. It is something you must feel with your spirit.” She took his hand, her small, delicate fingers surprisingly strong. She placed his palm flat against the wall, then covered his hand with her own. “Close your eyes,” she whispered. “And just… listen.” He did as she said, feeling a reluctant sense of surrender wash over him. He closed his eyes, shutting out the world, and focused on the point of contact between his hand, Lyra’s hand, and the wall. At first, there was nothing. Just the warmth of the clay, the gentle pressure of Lyra’s hand, the distant sounds of the village. But then, slowly, gradually, he began to feel it. A faint, almost imperceptible vibration, a low, resonant hum that seemed to be coming from deep within the wall itself. It was the same hum that he had been hearing for days, but this time, it was different. It was… clearer. More focused. It was as if Lyra was acting as a conduit, an amplifier, a bridge between his world and theirs. The vibration grew stronger, and he could feel it traveling up his arm, into his chest, into his very soul. And then, he heard them. The whispers.
