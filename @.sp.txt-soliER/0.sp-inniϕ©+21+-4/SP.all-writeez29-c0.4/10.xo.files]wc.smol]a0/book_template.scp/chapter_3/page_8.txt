He closed his eyes, trying to make sense of it all. He tried to empty his mind, to let go of his preconceived notions, his scientific biases, his human fears. He tried to do what Lyra had said. He tried to… listen. Not with his ears, but with his spirit. And as the Afink’s chant reached a fever pitch, as the hum of the unseen city grew to a deafening roar, something inside him… broke. The whispers were no longer a faint, distant echo. They were a raging torrent, a flood of alien consciousness that poured into his mind, threatening to drown him in its depths. He felt their power, a force so immense it could shatter stars and reshape galaxies. He felt their age, an ancient, timeless existence that made his own fleeting lifespan seem like a meaningless flicker. He felt their intelligence, a cold, crystalline logic that was devoid of all emotion, all compassion, all… humanity. And he felt their hunger. It was not the simple, physical hunger of a predator for its prey. It was a deeper, more profound hunger. A hunger for knowledge, for experience, for… existence. They were a race of cosmic vampires, feeding on the very fabric of reality itself. And they were waking up. And they were hungry. The whispers were no longer just whispers. They were voices. A thousand, a million, a billion voices, all speaking at once, a chorus of alien thought that was both beautiful and terrifying. He was drowning in their song, losing himself in their vast, collective consciousness. He was becoming a part of them. And he was terrified.
