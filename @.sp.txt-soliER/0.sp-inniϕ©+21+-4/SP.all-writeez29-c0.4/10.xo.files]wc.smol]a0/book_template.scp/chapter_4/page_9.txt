They continued their stealthy progress through the labyrinthine alleys, their senses on high alert. The city was a monument to power and control, devoid of warmth or life, save for the faint, rhythmic thump of unseen machinery and the distant, guttural chant that seemed to emanate from its very foundations. They saw movement ahead, a group of Extrmophiles gathered in a wide, open plaza. They ducked behind a massive, crystalline structure, its surface reflecting their distorted images like a funhouse mirror. From their hiding place, they watched, horrified, as a group of Extrmophiles herded a line of smaller, four-legged creatures. The creatures were unlike anything Lucky had ever seen, their bodies covered in a coarse, matted fur, their limbs thick and powerful. But it was their eyes that truly disturbed him. They were dull, vacant, devoid of all life and intelligence. They moved with a sluggish, shuffling gait, their heads bowed, their bodies swaying with an unnatural rhythm. They were clearly enslaved, their wills broken, their spirits crushed. The Extrmophiles, their obsidian-like skin gleaming in the alien light, moved among them with an air of casual cruelty, prodding them with long, slender staffs that crackled with a faint, internal energy. They were not speaking, but Lucky could feel their thoughts, a cold, calculating indifference that sent a shiver down his spine. They viewed these creatures as nothing more than tools, resources to be exploited, their lives meaningless. “Enslaved,” Lyra whispered, her voice filled with a profound horror. “The Ink of the Ancients holds sway over this world. It binds them.” Lucky’s heart sank. The vision from the Heart of the Ink, the one that had filled him with such awe and wonder, was not just a prophecy of a city. It was a warning. The Extrmophiles were not guardians. They were enslavers. And the ink, the very essence of the Afinkers’ culture, the source of their power and wisdom, was their tool of subjugation. He felt a cold dread settle in his stomach. They were trapped in a hostile, advanced civilization, surrounded by beings who viewed life as a resource to be exploited. They were in the lion’s den. And they were utterly, completely alone.
