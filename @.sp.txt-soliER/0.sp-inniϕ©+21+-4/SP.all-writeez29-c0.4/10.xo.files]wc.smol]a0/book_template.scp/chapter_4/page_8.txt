They heard them before they saw them. A rhythmic, heavy thud, like massive drums beating in unison. It was the sound of their footsteps. And then, they appeared. Three towering figures, their forms twisted and contorted in impossible ways, emerged from the shadows at the end of the alleyway. Their skin was like polished obsidian, absorbing the faint, internal lights of the city, making them seem like moving shadows. Their single, golden eyes glowed with an unnatural, predatory intensity, scanning the darkness with an unnerving precision. These were the Extrmophiles. And they were magnificent. And terrifying. They moved with a predatory grace, their powerful limbs carrying them silently over the obsidian-like plaza. They were guards, their bodies encased in segmented armor that seemed to be a part of their very flesh. They carried long, slender weapons that hummed with a faint, internal energy. Lucky pressed himself against the cold, smooth wall, pulling Lyra even closer. He could feel her trembling, a small, fragile bird caught in a storm. He held his breath, his heart hammering against his ribs, a frantic drumbeat in the suffocating silence. He could feel their presence, a cold, calculating intelligence that radiated from them like a palpable force. They were not just animals. They were sentient beings, intelligent, ancient, and utterly ruthless. They were the apex predators of this world, and he and Lyra were nothing more than prey. The guards passed by their hiding place, their golden eyes sweeping over the shadows, missing them by mere inches. Lucky felt a wave of relief so profound it almost brought him to his knees. They were safe. For now. But the encounter had changed something in him. The scientific curiosity, the detached observation, had been replaced by a primal fear. He was no longer a witness. He was a victim. And he knew, with a chilling certainty, that his survival depended on his ability to remain unseen, unheard, and utterly, completely hidden.
