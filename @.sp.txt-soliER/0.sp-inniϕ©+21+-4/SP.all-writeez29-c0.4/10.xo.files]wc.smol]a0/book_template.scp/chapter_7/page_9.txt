As they delved deeper into the city, they encountered more of the enslaved creatures. They were everywhere, their dull, vacant eyes a constant reminder of the Extrmophiles’ cruelty, a chilling testament to the power of the Ink of the Ancients. They saw them toiling in vast, underground caverns, their bodies swaying with an unnatural rhythm as they extracted glowing crystals from the earth. They saw them herding strange, bioluminescent livestock in vast, enclosed pens, their movements sluggish, their wills broken. They saw them serving as beasts of burden, pulling massive carts laden with alien goods through the labyrinthine streets. Each encounter reinforced their resolve, a burning fire in their alien hearts. They had to find the Fuzzballs. They had to free them. They had to expose the Extrmophile Kings. But the moral dilemma of their disguise weighed heavily on Lucky. He was becoming what he despised, a predator in a world of prey. He felt the insidious pull of the Extrmophile instincts, the hunger for dominance, the cold logic, the predatory urges. He fought it, desperately clinging to the last vestiges of his humanity, to the memories of his old self, the scientist, the man of peace. He was Lucky. He was a human scientist. He was undercover. And he would not succumb. He looked at Lyra, her single golden eye meeting his. He felt her resolve, her determination, her fierce loyalty. They were in this together. And together, they would find a way. They had to. The city hummed around them, a silent, predatory beast, waiting for them to make a mistake. But they were ready. They were prepared. They were undercover. And they were coming for the Fuzzballs.
