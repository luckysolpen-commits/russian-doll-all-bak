Lyra, with a confidence that belied her small stature, approached a group of Extrmophiles haggling over glowing crystals. Their voices, a series of clicks and guttural sounds, were sharp with negotiation. Their thoughts, a chaotic jumble of greed and suspicion, were almost deafening to Lucky’s newly sensitive mind. He watched, fascinated, as Lyra inserted herself into the conversation, her sharp mouth curling into a sly, almost imperceptible smile. She spoke in the guttural, clicking language of the Extrmophiles, her voice surprisingly resonant, her words flowing with an effortless grace. Lucky, still struggling with the nuances of the alien tongue, could only pick out fragments of the conversation. But he could feel the shift in the Extrmophiles’ thoughts, a subtle change from suspicion to curiosity, then to a grudging respect. Lyra was a natural. She was a master of manipulation, a weaver of words, a dancer in the intricate ballet of alien politics. “These crystals,” Lyra said, her voice cutting through the cacophony of the market. “Their luminescence is… weak. Where does one find the true source of power in this city?” The Extrmophiles, initially wary, responded to her confident demeanor and their shared physical traits. Their thoughts, now accessible to Lucky, revealed a mixture of pride and disdain for outsiders. *The true source?* an Extrmophile merchant’s thoughts echoed, a ripple of smugness. *Deep within the Temple of Whispers. But it is not for the weak. Only the chosen may approach.* Lucky filed this information away, a new piece of the puzzle falling into place. The Temple of Whispers. It aligned with the Fuzzball temple he had heard about in the Afink village. The pieces were beginning to connect, forming a clearer picture of the Extrmophiles’ true intentions. He felt a surge of grim determination. They were getting closer. Closer to the truth. Closer to the Fuzzballs. Closer to their mission.
