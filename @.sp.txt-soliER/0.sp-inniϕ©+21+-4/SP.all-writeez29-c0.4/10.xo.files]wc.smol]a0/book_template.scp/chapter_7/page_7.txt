Lucky, ever the scientist, began to experiment with his newfound telepathic abilities. At first, it was simply a matter of receiving, of filtering the overwhelming torrent of alien thought. But then, he began to subtly project. Not words, not coherent thoughts, but feelings, emotions, subtle suggestions. He would focus on a passing Extrmophile, projecting a feeling of calm, of indifference, of belonging. He would watch their reactions, their subtle shifts in posture, the flicker in their golden eyes. He was testing the limits of his new power, pushing the boundaries of his own consciousness. He practiced blending in, mimicking Extrmophile mannerisms, their fluid, predatory movements, their subtle head tilts, the way they held their sharp, turtle-like mouths. He learned to control his own thoughts, to create a mental shield that would protect him from the probing minds of the Extrmophiles. He learned to project a facade of cold, calculating indifference, a mask that would allow him to move unseen, unheard, in plain sight. It was a dangerous game, a constant tightrope walk between his human self and his alien disguise. He felt the insidious pull of the Extrmophile instincts, the hunger for dominance, the cold logic, the predatory urges. He felt them trying to consume him, to reshape him, to make him one of them. But he fought back, desperately clinging to the last vestiges of his humanity, to the memories of his old self, the scientist, the man of peace. He was Lucky. He was a human scientist. He was undercover. And he would not succumb. He would use this power, this disguise, this alien form, to achieve his mission. He would use it to find the Fuzzballs. He would use it to expose the Extrmophile Kings. He would use it to find a way home. He was a weapon, a tool, a means to an end. And he was ready to be wielded.
