At the same time, Lyra sent a powerful, telepathic command, a beacon, directly to the EXTRMOPHILE KINGS. It was a signal, a summons, a declaration of victory. Lucky felt it, a cold, sharp wave of triumph that resonated with his own newly awakened Extrmophile instincts. Their thoughts, previously distant, now surged into Lucky’s mind, a torrent of raw, unadulterated glee. *She has delivered them!* Extrmophile King 1’s thoughts resonated, a voice like grinding stone, filled with a chilling amusement. *The Fuzzballs are ours!* Extrmophile King 2’s thoughts followed, a voice like a winter wind, cold and sharp. *And the human… a bonus. His power will be ours,* Extrmophile King 3’s thoughts concluded, a voice like a poisoned blade, dripping with triumph. Lucky felt a cold dread settle in his stomach. The betrayal was complete. He had been so focused on freeing the Fuzz Balls, on exposing the Extrmophile Kings, that he had walked right into their trap. He had been a pawn, a tool, a fool. And now, the Fuzz Balls, whom he had risked everything to save, were about to be enslaved once more. He looked at Lyra, her single golden eye wide with a mixture of horror and despair. She had seen it too. The brutal, ugly truth. They had been used. And now, they were trapped. The Extrmophile Kings, their golden eyes burning with malevolent amusement, watched them, their thoughts a chorus of triumph. *You are but children, playing in a world you do not understand. Your efforts were… amusing. But ultimately, futile.* Lucky felt a profound sense of helplessness, a crushing weight that threatened to suffocate him. But even as despair threatened to consume him, a flicker of his old self remained. A spark of defiance. A stubborn refusal to be consumed by this alien darkness. He was Lucky. He was a human scientist. And he would not be a pawn. Not anymore. He would fight. He would make them pay. He would find a way. He had to.
