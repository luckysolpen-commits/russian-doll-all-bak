Days turned into weeks. Lucky settled into the rhythm of village life. He learned to hunt with the men, to track the strange, elusive creatures of the jungle. He learned to fish in the crystal-clear streams, to identify the edible plants, to build shelters from the elements. He learned the language of the Afink, the clicks and hums that had once sounded so alien, but now felt as natural as his own. He learned their customs, their traditions, their beliefs. He learned about the inks. The inks were the heart of their culture, the source of their power, the essence of their being. They were not just pigments, they were… magic. He watched as the hunters painted their bodies with Zhilak, the ink of strength, their muscles bulging, their senses sharpening. He watched as the elders painted their foreheads with Korvath, the ink of wisdom, their eyes glowing with an inner light. He watched as the young warriors painted their faces with Ember, the ink of courage, their hearts filled with a fire that made them fearless. He was a scientist, a man of logic and reason. He tried to understand the inks, to analyze them, to break them down into their chemical components. But he couldn’t. The inks defied all known laws of science. They were a mystery, a paradox. And the more he learned about them, the less he understood. He was a man of science in a world of magic. And he was beginning to realize that there were some things that science couldn’t explain. There were some things that you just had to… believe. He was starting to believe. He was starting to see the world through their eyes, to feel the magic in the air, to hear the whispers of the jungle spirits. He was changing. He was becoming one of them. And he wasn’t sure if he should be scared or excited. He was losing himself, his old self, the man he used to be. But he was also finding something new, something… more. He was finding a new way of seeing, a new way of being. He was finding… himself.
