As he stared at the impossible city, a voice echoed in his mind. It was Gorn’s voice, but not as he had ever heard it before. It was not the clicks and hums of the Afink language, but a pure, telepathic transmission of thought and emotion. *Behold, Lucky. The city of the Extrmophiles. The guardians of the deep. The keepers of the ancient knowledge.* The words flowed through him, filling him with a sense of awe and wonder. Guardians? Keepers of knowledge? The words seemed at odds with the cold, predatory feeling that emanated from the city. He tried to peer closer, to see the inhabitants of this incredible place. The vision shifted, and he was suddenly looking at the beings themselves. They were tall and slender, their bodies a mosaic of shimmering, iridescent scales. Their limbs were long and graceful, their movements fluid and precise. But their faces… their faces were the stuff of nightmares. They had a single, large, golden eye that seemed to see into his very soul. Their mouths were sharp and turtle-like, lined with rows of needle-sharp teeth. They were beautiful and terrifying, a perfect fusion of grace and lethality. They were the Extrmophiles. And they were nothing like the gentle, peaceful Afink. *They are not like us,* Gorn’s voice echoed in his mind, as if sensing his thoughts. *They are… different. They are the other half of our world. The darkness to our light. The storm to our calm. But they are not evil. They are… necessary.* Lucky struggled to understand. How could a race that looked so predatory, so… hostile, be considered necessary? What purpose did they serve? *They are the guardians of the deep,* Gorn’s voice repeated. *They protect the secrets of this world, the secrets that the Afink are not meant to know. They are the balance. Without them, our world would be… incomplete.* The vision began to fade, the city of the Extrmophiles dissolving into a shimmering mist. But the image of the beings, their golden eyes, their sharp teeth, was burned into his mind. He had seen the other half of this world. And he had a feeling that his destiny was intertwined with theirs. He had a feeling that he would meet them, face to face. And he had a feeling that when he did, the fate of this world would hang in the balance.
