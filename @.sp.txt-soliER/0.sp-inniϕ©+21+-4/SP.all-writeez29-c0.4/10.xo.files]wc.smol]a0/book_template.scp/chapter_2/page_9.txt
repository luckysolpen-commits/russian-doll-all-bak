The world snapped back into focus with a jarring suddenness. He was back in the clearing, the scent of damp earth and woodsmoke filling his nostrils. The Heart of the Ink rested gently in Gorn’s hands, its light now a soft, gentle pulse. The hum was gone, but he could still feel it, a faint, residual vibration deep within his bones. He felt… different. The vision had changed him in some fundamental way he couldn’t yet comprehend. The world seemed sharper, more vibrant. He could see the intricate patterns on a moth’s wings, hear the faint rustle of a leaf as it fell from a tree, smell the subtle fragrance of a flower that was miles away. His senses were heightened, amplified. It was as if a veil had been lifted from his eyes, and he was seeing the world for the first time. He looked at Gorn, and he saw not just an old, frail Afink, but a being of immense power and wisdom. He looked at Lyra, and he saw not just a young, innocent girl, but a soul that was as old as the jungle itself. He looked at himself, and he saw… a stranger. A man who had been stripped of his identity, his past, his very humanity. But he was not empty. He was filled with something new, something… more. He was filled with the energy of the Heart of the Ink, the magic of this world, the destiny that awaited him. He felt a surge of power, a feeling of… potential. He was no longer just a survivor. He was a player. A piece on a cosmic chessboard that he was just beginning to understand. And he was ready to make his move. He was ready to face his destiny. He was ready to meet the Extrmophiles. He was ready for whatever this world had to throw at him. He was ready to fight. He was ready to win. He was ready to be… Lucky.
