Lucky’s scientific mind screamed in protest. Visions? Destiny? This was the stuff of fantasy novels, not astrophysics. He wanted to object, to demand a rational explanation, but the look in Gorn’s eyes silenced him. There was an intensity there, a conviction so absolute it was unnerving. Before he could formulate a response, Gorn gently pushed the orb towards him. “Do not be afraid,” Lyra translated, her own voice a mixture of reassurance and apprehension. “The Heart knows your spirit. It will not harm you.” Lucky hesitated, his hand hovering over the shimmering surface of the orb. His every instinct, honed by years of rigorous scientific training, told him to back away, to reject this… this primitive superstition. But another part of him, a deeper, more primal part that had awakened in the jungle, urged him forward. It was the part that had clubbed the hounds to death, the part that had embraced the raw, savage beauty of this world. It was the part that was curious. He took a deep breath and touched the orb. The moment his fingers made contact, the world dissolved. It was not a gentle fading, but an instantaneous, violent shattering of reality. The clearing, the trees, Gorn, Lyra—they all vanished, replaced by a swirling vortex of pure, incandescent light. He felt a sensation of falling, of being pulled apart and reassembled at a molecular level. All his senses were on fire. He saw colors that had no names, heard sounds that were beyond the range of human hearing, felt emotions that were alien and overwhelming. It was terrifying. It was exhilarating. He was no longer Lucky, the human scientist. He was a disembodied consciousness, a mote of dust in a cosmic storm. He was everything and nothing. The chaos lasted for an eternity and a microsecond. And then, as suddenly as it had begun, it stopped. The swirling vortex of light coalesced, solidified, and he found himself standing on a precipice, looking out at a scene that was both impossible and terrifyingly real.
