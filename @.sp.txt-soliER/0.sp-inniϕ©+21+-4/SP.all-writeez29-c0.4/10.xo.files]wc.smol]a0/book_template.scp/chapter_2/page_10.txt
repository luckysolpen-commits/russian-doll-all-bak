Gorn placed a gentle hand on his shoulder, his ancient eyes filled with a mixture of pride and concern. He spoke, his voice a low, resonant hum, and Lyra translated, her own voice tinged with awe. “The Heart of the Ink has chosen you, Lucky. It has shown you a vision of the other side, a glimpse into the heart of our world. This is a great honor, a great burden.” Lucky looked from Gorn to Lyra, his mind still reeling. “Chosen me? For what?” “We do not know,” Lyra admitted. “The Heart’s ways are a mystery, even to us. But it has never shown such a vision to an outsider before. It means you have a part to play in the destiny of this world. A great purpose.” A great purpose. The words echoed in the silence of the clearing, heavy with unspoken meaning. He thought of the floating city, so beautiful and so terrifying. He thought of the Extrmophiles, their golden eyes burning with a cold, ancient intelligence. He felt a shiver of apprehension, a premonition of the dangers that lay ahead. He was just a man, a scientist, a castaway. How could he possibly have a role to play in the fate of an entire world? But as he looked at the Heart of the Ink, now resting peacefully on its pedestal, he knew that Gorn was right. He had been chosen. He had been given a gift, a power, a purpose. He didn’t know what that purpose was, or where it would lead him. But he knew that he could not ignore it. The image of the floating city was burned into his mind, a shimmering enigma that beckoned to him from across the sea of stars. He felt its pull, a gravitational force that was as irresistible as it was terrifying. His journey was no longer about survival. It was about something much, much bigger. And as the twin suns of Planet X began to set, casting long, eerie shadows across the clearing, he felt a sense of grim determination settle over him. He would face his destiny. He would uncover the secrets of the Extrmophiles. He would find his purpose. And he would not fail.
