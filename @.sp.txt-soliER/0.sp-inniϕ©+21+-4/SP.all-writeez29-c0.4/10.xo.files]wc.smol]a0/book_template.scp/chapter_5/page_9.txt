The tall Extrmophile woman’s single eye narrowed, her gaze piercing as she surveyed them. Her voice, when she spoke, was like dry leaves rustling, a low, guttural sound that was surprisingly melodic. “We are Kha’Zix,” she said, her voice echoing in the dimly lit market square. “We trade in rare and precious goods. What do you have to offer?” Lucky hesitated, his mind racing. He had nothing. No currency, no technology, no knowledge that would be of any value to these advanced beings. He was a scientist, not a merchant. He was about to admit his lack, to apologize for his intrusion, when Lyra nudged him, a subtle, almost imperceptible movement. He looked at her, and in her eyes, he saw a flicker of something. A memory. The ink flowers. He remembered the small, vibrant ink flowers they had found near the crash site, tucked away in a pouch he had fashioned from his tattered suit. They were beautiful, unlike anything he’d seen on Earth, their petals glowing with a faint, iridescent light. He had collected them out of scientific curiosity, a small, personal treasure in a world of overwhelming alienness. He reached into his pouch, his fingers fumbling with the unfamiliar material. He pulled out a handful of the glowing, iridescent flowers, their light illuminating his face in the dim market square. “I… I have something,” he stammered, holding them out. “These flowers. They are rare and unique. Perhaps they would be of interest to you?” The Kha’Zix woman’s single eye widened almost imperceptibly. A low, guttural sound, almost a purr, escaped her. It was a sound of surprise, of recognition, of… desire. “Ah,” she breathed, her voice softening slightly. “Ink flowers. We have not seen those in ages. How much will you ask for them?”
