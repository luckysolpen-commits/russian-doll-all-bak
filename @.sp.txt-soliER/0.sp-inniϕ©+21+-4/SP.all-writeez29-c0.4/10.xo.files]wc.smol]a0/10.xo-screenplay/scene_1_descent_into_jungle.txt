SCENE 1: THE DESCENT INTO PLANET X'S JUNGLE

[EXT. PLANET X - JUNGLE - DAY]

LUCKY, battered and bruised, stumbles out of the wreckage of his UNOMAN SUIT. The suit, once pristine, is now a mangled mess of twisted metal and sparking wires.

The air is thick with the scent of alien flora and the guttural calls of unseen creatures. Towering, bioluminescent trees, their branches like skeletal fingers, loom over him. The ground beneath his feet is a spongy, phosphorescent moss.

Lucky surveys the alien landscape, his breath ragged. He knows he has to move, and fast. The jungle pulsates with a malevolent energy, a primal hum that vibrates through his very bones.

He spots a faint clearing in the distance and pushes forward, his eyes darting, scanning for any sign of danger. The vegetation grows denser with every step, the sounds of the jungle intensifying.

Lucky's training kicks in. He snaps off a thick, gnarled branch, testing its weight. It's crude, but it will serve as a makeshift club. He tears strips of fabric from his tattered suit, fashioning a rough, protective garb, mimicking the furs of ancient hunters.

As the twin suns of Planet X begin their descent, casting long, eerie shadows, Lucky spots movement. Two alien hounds, their bodies sleek and muscular, emerge from the deepening twilight. Their eyes, a piercing, otherworldly gold, fix on him.

They circle, low growls rumbling in their throats. Lucky tightens his grip on his makeshift club. He fends off their snapping jaws, the air thick with tension as he clashes with the beasts.

With a surge of adrenaline, Lucky lands a solid blow, sending one of the hounds yelping into retreat. The other, momentarily stunned, backs away, its golden eyes still fixed on him before it too vanishes into the undergrowth.

Breathless and shaken, Lucky continues his journey. The trees grow taller, their trunks gnarled and twisted. The scent of damp earth and decay fills his nostrils.

He pushes through a final curtain of shimmering vines and emerges into a clearing. In the center, a village. AFINK VILLAGE. Thatched huts cluster around a central fire pit, smoke curling lazily into the evening sky, carrying the savory aroma of cooking meat.

Lucky stumbles towards the village, his eyes scanning for any sign of danger or life. The flickering flames illuminate the faces of villagers gathered around the fire. They watch him, their expressions unreadable.

[FADE OUT.]
