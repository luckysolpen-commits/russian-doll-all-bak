SCENE 6: THE HUNT AND THE TRADE

[INT. EXTRMOPHILE CITY - NARROW ALLEYS - NIGHT]

LUCKY and LYRA move like shadows through the labyrinthine alleys of the Extrmophile City. The towering, obsidian structures loom over them, casting deep, oppressive shadows. The air is cold, metallic, and carries the faint, unsettling hum of unseen machinery.

They press themselves against a cold, smooth wall as a patrol of EXTRMOPHILE GUARDS marches past. Their forms are grotesque, their movements precise and menacing. Their single, golden eyes scan the darkness with an unnerving intensity. Lucky holds his breath, his heart pounding against his ribs.

LYRA
(Whispering, urgent)
Stay close. We don't want to attract attention.

They continue their stealthy progress, ducking into alcoves, hiding behind strange, crystalline sculptures that line the deserted thoroughfares. The city is a monument to power and control, devoid of warmth or life, save for the enslaved creatures they occasionally glimpse, their eyes vacant.

After what feels like an eternity, they hear a faint, discordant CHATTER. It's not the guttural chants of the guards, but something different, almost... mercantile. They cautiously approach the source of the sound.

[INT. EXTRMOPHILE CITY - MARKET SQUARE - NIGHT]

They peer around a corner into a dimly lit MARKET SQUARE. It's a stark contrast to the desolate streets. Here, a handful of Extrmophiles are gathered, their forms slightly less rigid, their movements more fluid. The air is thick with the scent of exotic, pungent spices and the low murmur of haggling.

Lyra's eyes lock onto a group of EXTRMOPHILE WOMEN. Their skin, like polished obsidian, gleams under the faint, pulsating lights of the market. Their single, golden eyes, while still intense, hold a flicker of something akin to curiosity, or perhaps, calculation.

They are gathered around a stall, examining strange, glowing artifacts. Their voices, though still alien, have a softer cadence than the guards.

LYRA
(Whispering)
Perhaps... we can find some... allies among these women.

Lucky is skeptical. He remembers the user's warning: these creatures are hostile, bullies. But Lyra's gaze is fixed, a spark of hope in her eyes. He trusts her instincts.

As they watch, one of the women, taller than the others, with intricate, glowing patterns etched into her scales, looks up. Her single eye narrows, her gaze piercing as she surveys the shadows. She sees them.

The other women turn, their faces mirroring her curiosity. Lyra, with a surge of courage, steps forward, her hands held out in a gesture of peace. Lucky follows, his guard still up.

LYRA
We mean no harm. We're just travelers seeking shelter and trade.

The tall Extrmophile woman's gaze lingers on Lyra, then shifts to Lucky. He feels her eye like a physical touch, assessing, probing. He resists the urge to flinch.

KHA'ZIX WOMAN
(Voice like dry leaves rustling)
We are Kha'Zix. We trade in rare and precious goods. What do you have to offer?

Lucky hesitates, unsure. Then, he remembers the small, vibrant INK FLOWERS they had found near the crash site, tucked away in a pouch. They were beautiful, unlike anything he'd seen on Earth.

LUCKY
I... I have something. These flowers. They are rare and unique. Perhaps they would be of interest to you?

He produces a handful of the glowing, iridescent flowers. The Kha'Zix woman's single eye widens almost imperceptibly. A low, guttural sound, almost a purr, escapes her.

KHA'ZIX WOMAN
(Voice softening slightly)
Ah. Ink flowers. We have not seen those in ages. How much will you ask for them?

Lucky glances at Lyra, who gives him a subtle nod.

LUCKY
We'll trade them for shelter and food. And perhaps some... information about the city.

The Kha'Zix woman's eye gleams with a calculating intensity. A slow, unsettling smile spreads across her sharp, turtle-like mouth.

KHA'ZIX WOMAN
I think we can come to an arrangement. Follow me.

She turns, her movements fluid and predatory, leading them deeper into the market. Lucky and Lyra exchange a look. They are walking into the lion's den, but it's their only way forward.

[FADE OUT.]
