[EXT. OLD WAREHOUSE - NIGHT (MIDNIGHT)]

SOUND of distant city hum, faint lapping of water

Chara’s car pulls up a block away from the old warehouse on 5th and Main. The area is deserted, a forgotten industrial wasteland. The warehouse itself is a hulking, dark silhouette against the moonlit sky, its broken windows like vacant eyes.

She checks her reflection in the rearview mirror, then secures her concealed weapon. She’s wearing dark, practical clothing. Her comms are active, but silent. She’s alone, as requested, but she has a contingency plan. A silent alarm, linked to a trusted contact, will trigger if she doesn't check in every fifteen minutes.

As she approaches the warehouse on foot, she notices something unusual. Two sleek SPEEDBOATS are docked at a makeshift pier behind the building, their powerful engines humming softly, almost imperceptibly, in the night air. They look ready to launch at a moment's notice.

Chara’s instincts scream trap. But the lure of answers, the need to understand Omicorp's connection to the horrors she's uncovered, propels her forward. She slips through a broken section of a fence, moving silently towards a partially open loading bay door.

[INT. OLD WAREHOUSE - NIGHT]

SOUND of Chara's cautious footsteps, faint dripping water

The interior of the warehouse is vast, cavernous, filled with shadows and the scent of dust and decay. Moonlight streams through the broken skylights, creating pools of silver on the concrete floor. Old crates and forgotten machinery are scattered like skeletal remains.

In the center of one of these moonlit pools, a figure stands waiting. It’s a WOMAN, with short, dark hair and piercing green eyes. She’s dressed in a black leather jacket and dark jeans, exuding an aura of dangerous competence. This is MAYA.

MAYA
(Her voice calm, clear, echoing slightly)
Agent Chara. Punctual. I like that.

Chara stops, her hand hovering near her weapon. She assesses the space, looking for hidden threats, other figures. Nothing. Just Maya.

CHARA
(Voice steady)
Who are you? And what do you want?

MAYA
(A faint smile plays on her lips)
My name is Maya. And I want the same thing you do, Agent. The truth. About Omicorp. About the Order of Providence. About the web they've spun.

Chara’s eyes narrow. Maya knows too much. She’s not just a messenger.

CHARA
(Suspicious)
How do you know about the Order? About Omicorp?

MAYA
(Taking a step closer)
Let's just say I have… unique sources. And a vested interest in seeing their empire crumble. They're not just playing with money, Agent. They're playing with lives. And souls.

Suddenly, the loading bay door behind Chara SLAMS SHUT with a deafening clang. The soft hum of the speedboats outside intensifies, growing into a low GROWL. The air crackles with tension.

MAYA
(Her smile widening, but with no warmth)
Looks like our little chat has attracted some unwanted attention. Pity. I was just getting to the good part.

From the shadows, figures emerge. Three, then five, then more. They are heavily armed, dressed in dark tactical gear, their faces obscured by balaclavas. They move with professional precision, fanning out, cutting off Chara's escape routes. They are Omicorp security, or perhaps something more sinister.

CHARA
(Drawing her weapon, aiming at Maya)
This was a setup!

MAYA
(Shaking her head, a genuine regret in her eyes)
Not by me, Agent. I told you, Omicorp is just the tip of the iceberg. They have eyes everywhere. But don't worry. I always have a backup plan.

Before the armed figures can react, Maya throws a small, metallic sphere to the ground. It explodes with a blinding FLASH and a deafening BANG, filling the warehouse with thick, acrid SMOKE. Chara is momentarily disoriented, her ears ringing.

When her vision clears, Maya is gone. The armed figures are shouting, coughing, their movements hampered by the smoke. Chara doesn't hesitate. She fires a warning shot, then ducks behind a stack of crates, using the smoke as cover.

GUNFIRE erupts. Bullets ping off metal, splinter wood. Chara returns fire, aiming for non-lethal hits, trying to create a diversion. She hears shouts, the thud of bodies hitting the ground. She moves fast, a phantom in the smoke, heading for the back of the warehouse, towards the pier.

She bursts through a back door, into the cool night air. The pier is just ahead. The speedboats are still there, their engines roaring now, ready to depart. Maya is already in one, untying the mooring lines.

CHARA
(Shouting)
Maya! Don't!

More armed figures pour out of the warehouse, firing. Chara sprints, dodging bullets, her feet pounding on the wooden pier. She leaps onto the second speedboat, just as it begins to pull away from the dock.

[INT. SPEEDBOAT - NIGHT]

SOUND of roaring engines, wind rushing, gunfire

Chara lands hard in the speedboat, scrambling for cover. The boat lurches forward, picking up speed. Bullets splash in the water around them. The other speedboat, with Maya at the helm, is already a blur in the distance.

Chara looks at the driver of her boat. He's a burly man, his face grim, focused on the open water. He glances at her, then back at the pursuing figures on the pier, who are now scrambling into a waiting CAR.

DRIVER
(Shouting over the wind)
They're gaining! We need to lose them!

The speedboat slices through the water, leaving a white wake behind it. The car on the pier speeds along the shoreline, trying to keep pace. Gunfire continues, sporadic, desperate.

Chara looks back. The car is fast, but the water is their domain. The driver expertly maneuvers the speedboat, weaving through buoys, taking sharp turns that send spray high into the air. The car struggles to keep up on the winding coastal road.

Suddenly, a second CAR, a black SUV, appears on the road, cutting off the first. It's Omicorp. They're coordinating. The SUV swerves, trying to block their path, but the speedboat is too agile.

Chara sees Maya's speedboat ahead, now a tiny speck on the horizon. She's getting away. The driver of Chara's boat pushes the throttle, the engine screaming in protest. They gain a little distance, but the Omicorp vehicles are relentless.

DRIVER
(Gritting his teeth)
They're not giving up! We need to make a break for it!

He swerves sharply, heading directly for a narrow channel, barely wide enough for the boat. The Omicorp cars are forced to slow, unable to follow. Chara looks back one last time. The cars are still there, their headlights like angry eyes, but they are falling behind.

The speedboat bursts out of the channel, into the open sea. The wind whips through Chara's hair. The roar of the engine fills her ears. They are free. For now.

She looks at the driver. He nods, a grim satisfaction on his face. They made it.
[FADE OUT.]
