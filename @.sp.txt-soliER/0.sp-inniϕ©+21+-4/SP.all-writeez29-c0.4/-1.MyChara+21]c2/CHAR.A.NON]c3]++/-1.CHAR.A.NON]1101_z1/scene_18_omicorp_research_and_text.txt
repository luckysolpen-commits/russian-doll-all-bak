[INT. CHARA'S APARTMENT - NIGHT]

SOUND of keyboard clicks, news reports playing softly in background

Chara is back at her workstation, the images of the crime scenes and the survivor's vacant stare haunting her. The presence of the Omicorp agents at the hospital has ignited a new, dangerous line of inquiry. She pulls up every public and classified database she can access, focusing solely on Omicorp.

News reports, financial records, leaked documents – she sifts through it all. The picture that emerges is disturbing. Omicorp, a seemingly legitimate biotech giant, has a long history of questionable practices. Whispers of unethical human trials, experimental medical procedures with disastrous side effects, and aggressive suppression of dissenting scientists.

CHARA
(Muttering to herself)
They’re not just a biotech firm. They’re a law unto themselves.

She finds a series of articles from obscure medical journals, detailing a controversial research project from two decades ago, involving neurological mapping and consciousness transfer. The project was officially shut down due to ethical concerns, but the articles hint at continued, clandestine research.

CHARA
(A cold realization)
Consciousness transfer… headless bodies…

The pieces are starting to click into place, forming a horrifying mosaic. The Order of Providence, the seances, the headless victims, and Omicorp's dark past. It all points to a terrifying, interconnected web.

Just as she uncovers a particularly damning report about a failed clinical trial that resulted in multiple fatalities, her phone BUZZES. It’s an unknown number. She hesitates, then answers.

No voice. Just a text message, appearing on her screen:

"Meet me at the old warehouse on 5th and Main at midnight. Come alone."

Chara stares at the message. It's a direct invitation, a lure. It could be a trap. It almost certainly is. But the information she's just uncovered, the sheer audacity of Omicorp's past, compels her. This could be the break she needs, the missing link in this horrifying chain of events.

She looks at the clock. Midnight is only a few hours away. She knows the risks. But the truth, however dangerous, is calling to her.
[FADE OUT.]
