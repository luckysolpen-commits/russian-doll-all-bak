The discovery of the videos in the monastery’s server room sent
a chill through Chara that no amount of mountain air could dispel.
This wasn’t just about a secret society manipulating global events;
this was about human experimentation, about a chilling disregard
for life, about a darkness that went far beyond anything she had
ever encountered. The images of the tortured subjects, the branding
of the eye symbol, the raw, unadulterated fear in their eyes –
it was a testament to the Order’s depravity. And the seance video.
That was the most disturbing of all. The sudden flash of light,
the scream, the abrupt silence. It was a ritual, a sacrifice, a
desperate attempt to harness the Whispering One’s power. And it
had gone horribly wrong. Chara directed her team to secure all
the data, to meticulously catalog every video, every document,
every scrap of information. This was the evidence they needed,
the smoking gun that would expose the Order of Providence to the
world. But she knew it wouldn’t be easy. The Order had infiltrated
every level of power, and they would fight tooth and nail to keep
their secrets buried. As the tech specialists worked, Chara’s gaze
returned to the flickering screen displaying the darknet forum.
Anonymous had been right. The whispers were indeed louder than
the screams. And these whispers, these digital breadcrumbs, were
leading her down a path far darker than she had ever imagined.
She opened a secure channel to Anonymous. “The monastery was deserted,”
she reported, her voice grim. “But they left a lot behind. Videos.
Experiments. And a seance gone wrong.” Anonymous was silent for
a moment, then his modulated voice filled her earpiece. “I warned
you, Chara. The Order is not what it seems. Their pursuit of power
is absolute, their methods… extreme.” “What do you know about
this seance?” Chara pressed. “The video. It ended abruptly. What
happened?” “The ritual was incomplete,” Anonymous replied. “They
attempted to draw the Whispering One into our dimension, to fully
manifest it. But they lacked the proper conduit, the necessary
understanding. The energy backlash… it consumed them. Left nothing
but a void.” Chara felt a cold dread. A void. That explained the
absence of bodies, the lack of struggle. They had simply ceased
to exist. “And the darknet forum?” she asked. “It’s a hub for their
recruitment,” Anonymous explained. “They lure in desperate individuals,
those seeking power, knowledge, or a sense of belonging. They promise
them enlightenment, a deeper understanding of the universe. And
then… they use them.” Chara clenched her fists. The sheer cynicism
of it, the cold, calculated manipulation of human vulnerability.
“We need to find out where these rituals are taking place,” Chara
said, her voice hard. “We need to stop them.” “I’m already working
on it,” Anonymous replied. “The video contained subtle clues, geographical
markers. I’m cross-referencing them with known ley lines, with
areas of high energy concentration. I believe I have a lead. Several
leads, in fact. All pointing to… crime scenes.” Chara felt a chill
creep up her spine. Crime scenes. The seance had not been an isolated
incident. The Order was still conducting their rituals, still sacrificing
human lives in their desperate pursuit of power. And she was going
to stop them. No matter the cost.
