The assault on the monastery was swift and decisive. CIA operatives,
led by Chara, swarmed the ancient fortress, their movements precise,
their training evident in every synchronized step. The initial
breach was met with silence, an eerie stillness that sent a prickle
of unease down Chara’s spine. There were no guards, no resistance,
no signs of the Order of Providence. The monastery was deserted.
“Clear!” a voice crackled in her earpiece. “Main entrance secured.
No hostiles detected.” Chara moved through the silent halls, her
pistol raised, her senses on high alert. The air was still, heavy
with the scent of dust and something else, something faintly metallic
and sweet, like dried blood. It was the same scent she had encountered
in the windmill, a chilling reminder of the Order’s dark rituals.
They made their way to the central chamber, the one where Chara
had faced the Test of Wisdom. The vast library was untouched, the
shelves still overflowing with ancient texts. But the inner sanctum,
the hidden chamber where she had retrieved the scrolls, was different.
The energy barrier was gone, and the door stood ajar, revealing
a scene of controlled chaos. The room was a mess, not of destruction,
but of hasty departure. Papers were scattered across the floor,
equipment lay abandoned, and the faint glow of residual energy
still pulsed from the central altar. It was clear the Order had
left in a hurry, but not without taking what they deemed most
important. “They’re gone,” Arianna whispered, her voice filled
with a mixture of relief and apprehension. “They knew we were
coming.” Chara nodded, her gaze sweeping across the room. “Anonymous
must have given them a heads-up. Or perhaps they have their own
intelligence network.” She moved towards the altar, her eyes scanning
the scattered papers. Most were indecipherable, filled with arcane
symbols and complex equations. But a few were different. They
were printouts, modern documents, filled with scientific jargon
and chilling diagrams. “Chara, I’m detecting residual energy signatures
from the main server room,” Anonymous’s voice crackled in her
earpiece. “It seems they left something behind. Data. A lot of
it.” Chara’s eyes widened. This was it. The evidence they needed.
She quickly assembled a team of CIA tech specialists, directing
them to the server room. The scene there was even more disturbing.
Rows of abandoned servers hummed faintly, their data drives still
spinning. But it was the content on the screens that sent a chill
down Chara’s spine. Videos. Dozens of them. All playing simultaneously.
They showed gruesome experiments, human subjects strapped to tables,
their bodies contorted in agony, their minds subjected to unspeakable
horrors. The eye symbol was everywhere, branded onto the subjects’nskin,
projected onto the walls, a silent witness to the atrocities.
“My God,” one of the tech specialists whispered, his face pale.
“What is this?” Chara felt a wave of nausea. This was the true
face of the Order of Providence. Not just a secret society, but
a group of sadistic scientists, experimenting on human beings,
trying to unlock the secrets of the Whispering One. She noticed
a single, flickering screen, displaying a URL. A darknet forum.
And a video. A seance gone wrong. The image was grainy, but the
content was clear. A group of people, chanting, their faces contorted
in fear. And then, a sudden flash of light, a deafening scream.
And then, nothing. Only silence. Chara felt a cold dread settle
in her stomach. This wasn’t just research; it was a prelude. A
warning. The Order was still out there. And they were still playing
for keeps.
