Chara arrived at the old clock tower precisely at midnight.
The air was cool and crisp, carrying the faint scent of rain.
The towering structure, a relic of a bygone era, stood silhouetted
against the pale glow of the city lights, its ancient gears long
silent. She scanned the area, her senses heightened, every shadow
a potential threat. The street was deserted, save for a lone
figure standing beneath the clock tower’s base, cloaked in a
dark, hooded jacket. Anonymous. He was taller than she had imagined,
lean and wiry, with an almost ethereal stillness about him. His
face was obscured by the deep shadows of his hood, a deliberate
act of anonymity that both frustrated and intrigued her. Chara
approached cautiously, her hand resting instinctively on the
hilt of the concealed knife at her hip. Old habits died hard.
“You came,” a voice said, low and modulated, devoid of any discernible
emotion or gender. It was the same voice from the video transmission,
the one that had spoken of the Quinault prayer stick. “I did.”
Chara’s voice was steady, betraying none of the tension coiling
in her gut. “You have something to tell me.” Anonymous nodded
slowly, his gaze, though hidden, felt intense, piercing. “The
eyes are watching you, Agent Chara.” The words hung in the air,
a chilling pronouncement that sent a shiver down her spine. “Whose
eyes?” she demanded, her voice sharper now. “The Order of Providence.”
The name, spoken aloud, felt heavy, laden with a sinister weight.
“You know about them.” It wasn’t a question. “I do. And so do
they know about you. Your inquiries have not gone unnoticed.”
Anonymous’s voice was a low hum, a vibration that seemed to resonate
with the very stones of the clock tower. “They are everywhere,
Agent Chara. In the shadows, in the light, in the very fabric
of your society. They manipulate, they control, they orchestrate.
And they do not tolerate interference.” Chara felt a cold dread
settle in her stomach. This was worse than she had imagined.
“What do they want?” she asked, her voice barely a whisper.
“Control. Absolute control. They believe they are the rightful
guardians of humanity, the shepherds of a flock too blind to
see its own salvation. And they will stop at nothing to achieve
their vision.” Anonymous paused, and for a fleeting moment, Chara
thought she saw a flicker of something in the depths of his hood,
a hint of regret, perhaps even fear. “You are in danger, Agent
Chara. Grave danger. They will come for you.”
