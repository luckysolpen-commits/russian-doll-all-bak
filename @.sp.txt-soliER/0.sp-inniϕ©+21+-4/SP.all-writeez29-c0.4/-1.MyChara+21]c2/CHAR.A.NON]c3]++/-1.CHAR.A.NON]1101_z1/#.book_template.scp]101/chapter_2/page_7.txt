The old windmill stood silhouetted against the bruised twilight
sky, its broken sails like skeletal arms reaching for something
that wasn’t there. A chill wind, carrying the scent of salt and
decay, whipped around Chara as she approached, her footsteps muffled
by the overgrown weeds and crumbling asphalt. The place reeked
of neglect, a forgotten monument to a bygone era. Perfect. She
checked her gear: a silenced pistol, a tactical knife, a small
kit of lock-picking tools. She was a ghost in the night, moving
with the silent efficiency of a predator. Anonymous had given
her the location, but not the details. She had to assume the worst,
prepare for anything. The video had shown robed figures, chanting.
Was this a cult? A ritual? Or something far more mundane, a front
for some illicit operation? She circled the windmill, her eyes
scanning for any signs of entry, any hidden cameras or tripwires.
The main door was ajar, a sliver of darkness inviting her in.
She drew her pistol, the cold steel a familiar comfort in her
hand, and slipped inside. The air within was heavy, thick with
the scent of dust, old wood, and something else, something metallic
and faintly sweet. Blood? Her heart hammered against her ribs,
a frantic drumbeat in the oppressive silence. A single beam of
moonlight pierced a hole in the roof, illuminating a circular
chamber. In the center, a crude altar, stained with dark, dried
liquid. Around it, scattered on the floor, were remnants of a
ritual: burnt candles, strange symbols drawn in chalk, and a few
feathers, black as night. No bodies. No robed figures. Just the
echo of something terrible that had happened here. Chara moved
closer to the altar, her eyes tracing the symbols. They were similar
to the ones she had seen in the video, but more intricate, more
complex. She recognized a few, ancient alchemical symbols, sigils
of forgotten gods. This was not a simple cult; this was something
else entirely. As she examined the altar, a faint sound reached
her ears. A whimper. It came from a dark corner of the chamber,
hidden behind a pile of discarded machinery. Chara raised her
pistol, her finger hovering over the trigger, and moved cautiously
towards the sound. She found her. A young woman, huddled in a
ball, trembling, her eyes wide with terror. Her clothes were torn,
her skin bruised, and a thin stream of blood trickled from a
cut on her forehead. She was clutching something tightly in her
hand. A Quinault prayer stick. Chara lowered her pistol. “Are
you alright?” she asked, her voice soft, reassuring. The woman
looked up, her eyes darting around the room, as if expecting some
monster to emerge from the shadows. “They… they were here,” she
whispered, her voice hoarse with fear. “The Order. They… they
tried to… to awaken it.”
