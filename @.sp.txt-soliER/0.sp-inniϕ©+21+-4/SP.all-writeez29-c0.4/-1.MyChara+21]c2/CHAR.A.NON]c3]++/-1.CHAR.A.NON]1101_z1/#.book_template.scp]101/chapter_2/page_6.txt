The next message from Anonymous was different. It wasn't a
digital breadcrumb, or an encrypted file. It was a video, routed
through a series of untraceable proxies, and it played automatically
on her secure terminal. The image was grainy, flickering, but the
content was chillingly clear. It showed a dimly lit room, filled
with strange artifacts and arcane symbols. In the center, a group
of robed figures stood in a circle, chanting in a language Chara
didn't recognize. And then, the focus shifted to a single object
in the center of the circle: a Quinault prayer stick, identical
to the one Anonymous had shown her before. The camera zoomed in,
revealing intricate carvings, symbols that seemed to writhe and
shift in the flickering light. A modulated voice, Anonymous's
voice, overlaid the chanting. "They are attempting to activate
it, Agent Chara. To unlock its power. This is what they seek."
The video cut to a series of rapid-fire images: ancient texts,
scientific diagrams, schematics of strange machinery, all interspersed
with the eye symbol. It was a torrent of information, overwhelming
in its intensity, yet strangely compelling. Chara felt a cold
sweat break out on her forehead. This wasn't just about political
or economic control. This was about something far more primal,
far more dangerous. The video ended abruptly, leaving Chara in
the silence of her safe house, the chilling images burned into
her mind. She knew what she had to do. She had to find the source
of that video, the location of that ritual. Anonymous had given
her the key; now she had to unlock the door. She sent a brief,
encrypted message back to Anonymous: "Location?" The reply was
almost instantaneous: "Old windmill. Abandoned. Near the coast."
Chara knew the place. It was a derelict structure, a forgotten
landmark on the outskirts of the city, a place where teenagers
went to drink and graffiti artists left their mark. It was the
perfect place for a secret ritual, a place where no one would
look. She gathered her gear, her mind racing, formulating a plan.
She was no longer just an agent; she was a warrior, fighting a
war she barely understood, against an enemy she could barely
comprehend. But she was not alone. Anonymous was with her, a digital
shadow, a guiding light in the darkness. And together, they would
face whatever horrors the Order of Providence had unleashed.
