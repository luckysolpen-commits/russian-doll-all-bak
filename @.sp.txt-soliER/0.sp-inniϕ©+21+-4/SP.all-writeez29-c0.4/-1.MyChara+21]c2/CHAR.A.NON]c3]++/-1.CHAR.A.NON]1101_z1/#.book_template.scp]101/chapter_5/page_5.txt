Chara grabbed the Quinault prayer stick from Arianna’s trembling
hand. The wood felt warm, almost alive, in her grasp. She remembered
the knowledge she had just absorbed, the true nature of the stick
as a conduit, a key to interdimensional travel. She didn’t know
exactly how to activate it, but she had a strong, intuitive sense
of what to do. She focused her will, channeling the raw energy
of her determination into the stick. The symbols carved into its
surface began to glow with a faint, ethereal light, and a low
hum filled the chamber, growing in intensity. The air shimmered,
distorting the images of the approaching guards, turning them
into wavering phantoms. “What are you doing?” Arianna whispered,
her eyes wide with a mixture of fear and awe. “Opening a door,”
Chara replied, her voice strained with effort. The hum intensified,
becoming a high-pitched whine that vibrated through her bones.
The barrier at the entrance began to ripple, its shimmering surface
contorting, twisting, as if struggling against an unseen force.
The guards, now visible through the distorted air, hesitated, their
weapons raised, their faces etched with confusion and fear. They
had never encountered anything like this. Chara pushed harder,
her mind focused on a single image: escape. The light from the
prayer stick intensified, bathing the chamber in a blinding white
glow. The hum reached a crescendo, a deafening roar that seemed
to tear at the very fabric of reality. And then, with a sound like
ripping silk, the barrier at the entrance tore open, revealing
not the passage they had entered through, but a swirling vortex
of pure energy, a gateway to… somewhere else. “Go!” Chara yelled,
pushing Arianna towards the vortex. Arianna, her eyes still wide
with terror, stumbled forward, disappearing into the swirling
light. Chara followed, clutching the satchel of scrolls tightly,
her gaze fixed on the approaching guards. They were recovering,
their confusion giving way to anger. They raised their weapons,
preparing to fire. Chara leaped, diving headfirst into the vortex,
the last thing she saw was the muzzle flashes of their guns, the
last thing she heard was the roar of their anger. And then, nothing.
Only the swirling, disorienting chaos of interdimensional travel.
She felt herself being pulled, stretched, and compressed, her
body a mere plaything in the hands of an unseen force. It was
a terrifying, exhilarating experience, a complete surrender to
the unknown. And then, just as suddenly as it began, it ended.
She landed hard on solid ground, the impact jarring her teeth.
The air was cool and fresh, carrying the scent of damp earth and
ancient stone. She opened her eyes, blinking against the sudden
brightness. She was in a vast, circular chamber, its walls adorned
with intricate carvings and glowing runes. In the center, a massive
stone altar pulsed with a faint, ethereal light. Arianna was already
there, standing beside the altar, her face pale but unharmed.
“Where are we?” Chara asked, her voice hoarse. Arianna looked
at her, a faint smile touching her lips. “The Order’s true stronghold,
Chara. The place where they conduct their most sacred rituals.
The place where they planned to complete the awakening.” Chara
looked around the chamber, a sense of awe washing over her. This
was it. The heart of the beast. The place where the Order of Providence
had cultivated its power for centuries. And she was here. Ready
to fight.
