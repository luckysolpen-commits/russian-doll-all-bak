The EMP burst hit with a blinding flash and a deafening crackle,
sending a shockwave that rippled through the entire facility.
Lights flickered, screens went dark, and the hum of the servers
died down, replaced by an eerie silence. Chara and Maya, shielded
behind the server racks, felt the concussive force of the blast,
the air crackling with residual energy. “Go, Chara, go!” Anonymous’s
voice, though distorted by the EMP, was still urgent. “The window
is closing!” Chara burst from cover, her pistol raised, her eyes
scanning the now-darkened server farm. The NovaSpire guards, disoriented
by the EMP, stumbled blindly, their weapons useless. This was their
chance. She sprinted towards the main server rack, Maya a blur
beside her, their movements precise and synchronized. The air was
thick with the smell of ozone and burnt electronics. They reached
the server rack, a towering monolith of blinking lights and humming
drives. Chara pulled out the data bomb, a small, sleek device that
glowed faintly in the darkness. “Anonymous, where do I plug this
thing in?” she whispered, her voice tight with urgency. “Mainframe
port, Chara! It’s on the top right, disguised as a diagnostic port!
Be quick!” Anonymous’s voice was strained, indicating the immense
pressure he was under. Chara’s fingers flew across the server
panel, searching for the disguised port. She found it, a small,
almost invisible opening, and with a click, she inserted the data
bomb. The device glowed brighter, its light pulsating, and a series
of complex algorithms began to scroll across its tiny screen. The
data bomb was active. Just as the first NovaSpire guards began
to recover from the EMP, their weapons systems slowly coming back
online, Chara and Maya were already moving, retreating towards
the access point. The alarm sirens began to wail, a piercing shriek
that echoed through the facility, signaling their breach. “We’re
out, Anonymous!” Chara yelled into her comms. “The data bomb is
in!” “Understood, Chara! Now get out of there! They’re locking
down the facility!” Anonymous’s voice was filled with a new sense
of urgency. They raced through the labyrinthine corridors, the
sound of approaching guards growing louder, closer. The facility
was coming back online, its defenses reactivating, its security
personnel converging on their position. They reached the access
point, the steel grate still open, a beacon of freedom in the darkness.
They scrambled through, Maya pulling the grate shut behind them,
the metallic clang echoing in the narrow confines of the maintenance
tunnel. They were out. They had escaped. But the fight was far
from over. The data bomb was now active, its digital tendrils
burrowing deep into NovaSpire’s network, extracting and disseminating
their darkest secrets. The world was about to learn the truth.
And Chara knew, with a chilling certainty, that the Order of Providence
would not take this lying down. The war had just begun. And she
was ready for it.
