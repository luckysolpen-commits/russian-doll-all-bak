[INT. DATA STORAGE ROOM - NIGHT]

The reinforced door to the data storage room stands imposing, a testament to the value of what lies within. CHARA examines the keypad, a complex array of glowing numbers and symbols. She pulls out a small, specialized device, connecting it to a hidden port near the lock.

Her fingers fly across the device's holographic interface, lines of code scrolling rapidly. A faint HUM emanates from the keypad as she bypasses the initial security protocols. A green light flashes, and with a soft CLICK, the heavy door slides open, revealing a cavernous room.

Rows upon rows of servers hum with a low, constant energy. Data cables snake across the floor and up the walls, a digital nervous system. Holographic displays flicker, showing complex algorithms and schematics of the rogue mechs. The air is cool, almost sterile, a stark contrast to the humid jungle outside.

Chara moves swiftly, her eyes scanning for the main data nexus. She spots a central console, larger and more intricate than the others. This is it.

As she approaches, a laser grid materializes, crisscrossing the room in a deadly web. Motion sensors, she deduces. She pulls out her EMP device, adjusting its frequency. With a precise flick of her wrist, she activates it. The lasers flicker, then vanish. The motion sensors go dark. She has a brief window.

She plugs her portable data drive into the main console. A progress bar appears on the screen, slowly filling as gigabytes of critical information are transferred. Schematics, operational plans, financial records, communication logs – a treasure trove of intelligence.

While the data downloads, Chara quickly scans the holographic displays. She sees detailed blueprints of a new, larger mech, codenamed "BEHEMOTH." Its capabilities are terrifying, far beyond anything she's encountered. This facility is just the tip of the iceberg.

Suddenly, a faint BEEP. The EMP device's effect is wearing off. The laser grid begins to flicker back to life. She glances at the progress bar – 98% complete. Almost there.

Footsteps echo from the corridor outside. Guards. They've detected something.

The progress bar hits 100%. Chara yanks the data drive free, the device warm in her hand. The heavy door begins to slide shut, a metallic groan filling the air.

She turns, just as the first PMC GUARD bursts through the closing gap, weapon raised. Chara is already moving, a blur of motion, disappearing into the shadows of the server racks as the door CLANGS shut behind her.
