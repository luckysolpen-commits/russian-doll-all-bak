Chara’s mind was a whirlwind of tactical possibilities, each one
weighed and discarded in a fraction of a second. They
could try to outrun the patrol, but in the dense
jungle, that was a risky proposition. They could try to
hide, but the patrol was too close, their search pattern
too thorough. That left only one option: to fight. But
not on the enemy’s terms. On their own.

She pointed to a narrow ravine to their right, a
deep scar in the earth that was choked with vegetation.
It was a natural bottleneck, a perfect place for an
ambush. Giana nodded her understanding, her eyes glinting with
a cold, hard light. They moved with a silent, fluid
grace, their bodies low to the ground, their movements
synchronized with the rustle of the leaves and the sigh
of the wind. They were two predators, and they were
about to set a trap for the hunters.

They reached the ravine and scrambled down its steep
sides, the loose earth giving way under their feet. They
found cover behind a thicket of bamboo, its dense stalks
providing a natural screen. They were now hidden from view,
their position a perfect vantage point from which to spring
their trap. They were the spiders in the web, and
the flies were about to blunder in.

They could hear the patrol getting closer, the sound
of their voices a low murmur in the still air.
They were speaking in a language that Chara didn’t recognize,
a guttural, harsh-sounding tongue that seemed to be at
odds with the natural beauty of the jungle. They were
intruders here, a discordant note in the symphony of the
wild. And Chara and Giana were about to restore the
harmony.

They waited, their patience a tangible thing, a physical presence
in the small, confined space. The patrol was now at
the edge of the ravine, their figures silhouetted against the
dappled sunlight. They were so close that Chara could see
the sweat on their faces, the dirt under their fingernails,
the casual arrogance in their eyes. They were confident, complacent,
secure in their superiority. They were about to learn a
hard lesson.

Chara gave the signal, a soft click of her tongue
against the roof of her mouth. It was a small
sound, almost inaudible, but to Giana, it was a thunderclap.
They rose from their hiding place, their silenced pistols spitting
death. The first two guards went down without a sound,
their bodies slumping to the ground like discarded puppets. The
other two guards, startled by the sudden, unexpected attack, had
just enough time to register a flicker of movement before
they too were cut down, their lives extinguished in a
heartbeat. The ambush was over in a matter of seconds,
a brutal, efficient, and utterly silent display of deadly
force. The hunters had become the hunted, and the
jungle had reclaimed its own.
