Slipping through the newly created gap in the fence was
like passing through a membrane into another world. The air
inside the perimeter was different, charged with a low-level tension
that was almost a physical force. The jungle’s chaotic symphony
was replaced by the monotonous hum of machinery, the sterile,
soulless sound of a world devoid of nature. Chara and
Giana were now in the belly of the beast, two
foreign bodies in a closed, hostile system. They moved with
an even greater sense of caution, their every sense screaming
with a heightened awareness.

They hugged the shadows, their black tactical gear rendering them
all but invisible in the dim, artificial light of the
facility. They moved with a fluid, boneless grace, their bodies
seeming to flow around obstacles, their feet making no sound
on the cold, hard concrete. They were ghosts, wraiths, specters
of death in a place that was already a tomb.

They rounded a corner and came face to face with
their first real test, a two-man patrol making its rounds.
The guards were armed with assault rifles, their faces illuminated
by the harsh, unforgiving glare of a nearby floodlight. They
were young, their faces still fresh, their eyes not yet
hardened by the cynicism of their trade. They were boys
playing at being men, their uniforms and their weapons a
thin veneer of authority over their youthful uncertainty.

Chara and Giana reacted with a cold, brutal efficiency that
was born of a hundred such encounters. There was no
hesitation, no second thought, no flicker of emotion in their
eyes. They were professionals, and this was their trade. Chara
moved in a blur of motion, her hand striking out
like a venomous snake, her fingers finding the pressure point
on the side of the first guard’s neck. He crumpled
to the ground without a sound, his eyes wide with
a mixture of surprise and terror.

Giana was equally swift, equally deadly. She closed the distance
to the second guard in a heartbeat, her hand clamping
over his mouth, stifling the cry that was forming in
his throat. Her other hand, holding a combat knife with
a blade as black as the night, found the soft
spot under his ribs, the knife sliding home with a
sickening, wet thud. The guard’s body went rigid, his
struggles ceasing as his lifeblood drained away into the thirsty
earth.

They dragged the bodies into the shadows, their movements swift
and sure. They were leaving no trace of their presence,
no evidence of their intrusion. They were the ghosts in
the machine, and they would not be exorcised. The first
blood had been shed, the first price paid. The mission
was now well and truly underway. There was no turning
back, no room for regret. There was only the objective,
the cold, hard reality of the task at hand. And
the certain knowledge that more blood would be shed before
the night was over.
