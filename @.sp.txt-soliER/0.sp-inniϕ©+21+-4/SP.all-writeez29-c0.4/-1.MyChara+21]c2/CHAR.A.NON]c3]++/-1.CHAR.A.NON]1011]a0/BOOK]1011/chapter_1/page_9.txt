Chara lowered her weapon, a silent signal to Giana. There
would be no more killing tonight, not if she could help
it. They had shed enough blood, and the mission was
far from over. They needed to be smart, to be
surgical, to be ghosts in the truest sense of the word.

Giana understood immediately. She moved with a silent, fluid grace,
her hand reaching out to the operator’s shoulder. She squeezed,
her fingers finding the pressure point with a practiced ease.
The operator’s body went rigid, his eyes wide with a
mixture of surprise and pain. He opened his mouth to
cry out, but no sound came out. Giana’s other hand
was already there, clamping over his mouth, stifling the sound.

Chara moved to the console, her fingers flying across the
keyboard. She was not the hacker that Giana was, but
she knew her way around a computer. She quickly located
the command that would loop the surveillance footage, creating a
continuous, repeating image of an empty, silent facility. It was
a simple trick, but an effective one. To anyone watching
the monitors, everything would appear to be normal. The guards
would see what they expected to see, and they would
remain blissfully unaware of the two intruders in their midst.

With the surveillance system neutralized, they turned their attention to
the operator. He was a young man, no older than
twenty, his face a mask of terror. He was a
pawn in a much larger game, a cog in a
machine that he did not understand. He was not a
soldier, not a killer. He was just a boy who
had taken a job that paid well, a boy who
was in way over his head.

Chara looked into his eyes, and she saw not an
enemy, but a reflection of her own past. She saw
a young, scared kid who had been thrust into a
world of violence and death, a world that they had
not chosen. She saw herself.

“We’re not going to hurt you,” she said, her voice
a low, soothing murmur. “But you’re going to do exactly
as we say. Do you understand?”

The boy nodded, his eyes wide with a mixture of
fear and relief. He was a prisoner, but he was
also a survivor. He would do whatever it took to
stay alive.

They bound and gagged him, securing him to his chair
with a set of plastic zip ties. He was their
insurance policy, their guarantee that the alarm would not be
raised. They were now in control of the C&C center,
the nerve center of the entire facility. They were the
ghosts in the machine, and they were about to wreak
havoc on the system.
