Chara crawled towards Giana, her heart a leaden weight in
her chest. Giana was pale, her breathing shallow, her eyes
flickering with pain. The bullet had struck her in the
shoulder, a clean shot that had gone straight through. It
was a serious wound, but not a fatal one. Not yet.
But in the middle of the jungle, with no medical supplies,
no backup, it might as well have been. Chara tore
a strip of cloth from her sleeve and pressed it
against Giana’s wound, trying to stem the flow of blood.
It was a futile gesture, but it was all she
could do.

The gunfire intensified, the bullets tearing through the leaves above
them, kicking up dirt and debris. They were being suppressed,
pinned down, their options dwindling with every passing second. Chara
looked at Giana, her eyes filled with a desperate plea.
“We need to move,” she whispered, her voice hoarse with
emotion. “I can’t leave you here.”

Giana shook her head, a faint smile touching her lips.
“Go,” she rasped, her voice barely audible. “You have the
data. The mission is more important than me.”

“No!” Chara cried, her voice filled with a fierce determination.
“We’re a team. We get out of this together.” She
tried to lift Giana, but Giana cried out in pain,
her body going limp. She was too weak, too injured.
Chara was faced with an impossible choice: save her partner,
or save the mission. It was a choice that no
soldier should ever have to make.

And then, a voice. A familiar voice, a voice that
she had not expected to hear again. “Looks like you
could use a hand, little bird.”

Chara looked up, her eyes wide with disbelief. Standing over
them, a towering figure silhouetted against the setting sun, was
Agent Klondike. He was a force of nature, a whirlwind
of destruction, his presence a beacon of hope in the
darkness. He was armed with a rocket launcher, its massive
barrel resting on his shoulder. He grinned, a feral, predatory
smile that promised pain and destruction. “Looks like I’m
just in time for the party.”

He fired the rocket launcher, the sound a deafening roar
that echoed through the jungle. The rocket streaked through the
air, a fiery arrow of death, and struck the tree
line where the snipers were concealed. The explosion was massive,
a fiery inferno that consumed everything in its path. The
sound of gunfire ceased, replaced by the screams of the
wounded. Klondike had arrived, and the tide of the battle
had turned. The hunters had become the hunted, and the
jungle was about to become a graveyard.
