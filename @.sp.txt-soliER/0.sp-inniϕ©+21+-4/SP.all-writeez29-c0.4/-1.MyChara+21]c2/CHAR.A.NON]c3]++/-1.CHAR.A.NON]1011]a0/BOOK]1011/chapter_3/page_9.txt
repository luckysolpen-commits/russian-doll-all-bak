The infirmary at Mothra Base was a sterile, utilitarian space,
its walls a stark white, its air thick with the
smell of antiseptic. Giana lay on a cot, her face
pale, her breathing shallow. A medical drone hovered above her,
its robotic arm gently tending to her wound. Klondike sat
beside her, his massive frame a silent sentinel, his eyes
fixed on her face. He was a man who rarely
showed emotion, but Chara could see the concern etched
in the lines around his eyes, the subtle tremor in
his hand as he reached out to touch Giana’s forehead.

“She’s stable,” the medical drone chirped, its synthesized voice devoid
of emotion. “The bullet passed cleanly through. No major damage.
She will make a full recovery.”

Chara felt a wave of relief wash over her, a
physical sensation that almost brought her to her knees. Giana
was going to be okay. That was all that mattered.

“We need to get her out of here,” Chara said,
her voice filled with a fierce determination. “ANON knows we
have the data. He knows we know about his betrayal.
This base isn’t safe anymore.”

Klondike nodded, his eyes burning with a cold, hard light.
“I’ve already made arrangements. There’s a cargo plane leaving for
Langley in an hour. It’s a direct flight. No stops.
No questions.”

“And you?” Chara asked, her eyes fixed on his face.
“Are you coming with us?”

Klondike shook his head. “My place is here. I need
to tie up some loose ends. And I need to
make sure ANON doesn’t try anything else.” He paused,
his eyes meeting Chara’s. “You have the data. You have
the proof. You’re the only one who can expose him.
Don’t let me down, little bird.”

Chara nodded, her eyes filled with a fierce determination. “I
won’t.” She looked at Giana, a silent promise passing between
them. She would protect her, and she would avenge her.

They left the infirmary, the hum of the medical drone
fading behind them. The base was still a hive of
activity, but now, it felt different. It felt like a
trap, a cage. They were leaving it behind, its secrets
exposed, its power shattered. The mission was complete. The first
act of their deadly play was over, and the curtain
was about to fall on the unsuspecting inhabitants of this
isolated outpost of civilization. They had survived, they had triumphed,
and they were ready for whatever came next. The jungle
awaited, and with it, the next chapter of their lives.
