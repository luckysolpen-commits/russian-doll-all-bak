The helicopter was a beacon in the encroaching twilight, its
rotors a blur against the darkening sky. The pilot, a grizzled
old veteran with a thousand-yard stare, was already revving the
engines, a clear sign that he was eager to leave this
hellhole behind. Chara and Klondike, with Giana cradled carefully
in his arms, made a dash for the chopper, the sounds
of distant gunfire and explosions still echoing through the jungle.
They were leaving a trail of destruction in their wake,
a testament to Klondike’s brutal efficiency.

They scrambled into the helicopter, the interior a cramped, utilitarian
space filled with the smell of aviation fuel and sweat.
Klondike gently laid Giana down on the floor, her face
pale, her breathing shallow. He quickly assessed her wound, his
medical training kicking in. “It’s a clean shot,” he rumbled,
his voice surprisingly gentle. “Went straight through. No major arteries
or organs hit. She’ll be fine, but she needs a
doctor, and she needs one now.”

Chara nodded, her eyes fixed on Giana. A wave of
relief washed over her, a physical sensation that almost brought
her to her knees. Giana was alive. That was all
that mattered. The mission, the data, the betrayal – it
all faded into the background, replaced by the overwhelming gratitude
that her partner, her friend, was going to make it.

The helicopter lifted off, its rotors slicing through the humid
air, gaining altitude with a surprising speed. Below them, the
jungle receded, a vast, green carpet that stretched to the
horizon. The facility, now a distant memory, was a small
scar on the landscape, its lights twinkling like malevolent eyes
in the gathering darkness. They were leaving it behind, its
secrets exposed, its power shattered. The mission was complete.

But the feeling of triumph was short-lived, replaced by a
gnawing sense of unease. The betrayal still stung, a festering
wound that refused to heal. ANON had set them up,
and he would pay. The game was far from over.
It had only just begun. Chara looked at Klondike, his
face grim, his eyes burning with a cold, hard light.
He was a man of few words, but his actions spoke
volumes. He was a loyal friend, a fierce protector, and
a relentless hunter. And he was on their side. With
him, they were unstoppable. The helicopter continued its ascent, carrying
them away from the chaos, away from the danger, away
from the past. But the past was not so easily
left behind. It clung to them like a shadow, a
constant reminder of the darkness that lurked beneath the surface.
And Chara knew, with a chilling certainty, that they would
meet it again. Soon.
