The journey back to Mothra Base was a blur of motion,
the helicopter slicing through the night sky, its powerful engines
pushing them ever onward. Giana was unconscious, her breathing shallow,
but steady. Klondike sat beside her, his massive hand resting
on her forehead, his eyes fixed on her face. He
was a man of few words, but his concern for
her was palpable, a silent testament to the bond they
shared. Chara watched them, a sense of warmth spreading through
her chest. They were a strange family, forged in the
crucible of violence and danger, but a family nonetheless.

They landed on the helipad at Mothra Base just as
the first rays of dawn were beginning to paint the
sky. The base was a hive of activity, its lights
burning brightly against the fading darkness. Medics rushed to meet
them, their faces grim, their movements swift and efficient. They
lifted Giana onto a stretcher and whisked her away, Klondike
following close behind, his massive frame a silent sentinel.

Chara was left alone on the helipad, the silence a
welcome reprieve after the chaos of the past few hours.
She looked out at the rising sun, its golden rays
illuminating the vast expanse of the jungle. It was a
new day, a new beginning. But for Chara, it was
also a reminder of the darkness that still lurked in
the shadows, the enemies that still sought to destroy them.

She made her way to the briefing room, the same
shipping container where ANON had given them their mission. He
was waiting for her, his pale eyes fixed on her
face. He was a man who rarely showed emotion, but
Chara could see the flicker of something in his eyes,
something that might have been concern, or perhaps, just curiosity.

“Report,” he said, his voice a dry whisper. “The data
core. Is it secure?”

Chara nodded, her hand resting on her datapad. “It’s secure.
And we have a lot more than just data. We have
evidence. Evidence of a betrayal.”

ANON’s eyes narrowed, a flicker of something that might have
been surprise in their depths. “Betrayal? Explain.”

Chara laid out the events of the past few hours,
the ambush, the snipers, the PMC. She spoke of Giana’s
injury, of Klondike’s timely arrival, of the brutal efficiency with
which he had neutralized the threat. She spoke of the
PMC’s knowledge of their movements, their precise timing, their intimate
understanding of their mission. It was clear that someone had
tipped them off, someone from the inside. Someone who knew
their every move. Someone who had betrayed them.

ANON listened in silence, his face unreadable. When Chara had
finished, he remained silent for a long moment, his pale
eyes fixed on her face. “This is a serious accusation,
Chara,” he said, his voice a low rumble. “Are you
sure of your facts?”

“I’m sure,” Chara replied, her voice unwavering. “Someone knew.
Someone wanted us dead. And I think I know who.”
