Klondike’s presence was a seismic shift in the jungle’s delicate
balance. Where before there had been the methodical, predatory movements
of the PMC, now there was chaos, a whirlwind of destruction
that tore through the carefully constructed ambush. His rocket launcher,
a weapon of brutal, indiscriminate power, had not just destroyed
the sniper’s nest; it had shattered the enemy’s morale, their
confidence. The screams that followed the explosion were not just
the cries of the wounded; they were the sounds of
men who had suddenly realized they were no longer the
hunters, but the hunted.

He moved with a terrifying efficiency, his massive frame a
blur of motion through the dense foliage. His pulse rifle
roared, spitting out a continuous stream of high-velocity rounds that
tore through the jungle, ripping through flesh and bone. He
was a force of nature, a one-man army, and the
PMC, for all their training and their numbers, were no
match for him. They scattered, their disciplined formation breaking into
a panicked rout. They were running, fleeing from the monster
they had unwittingly awakened.

Chara, still prone beside the injured Giana, watched in awe.
She had seen Klondike in action before, but never like
this. He was a berserker, a warrior consumed by a primal
fury. He was not just fighting; he was punishing. He
was making them pay for daring to lay a hand
on his team, on his family. And in that moment,
Chara felt a surge of something akin to pride. She
was part of this team, part of this family. And
they were unbreakable.

Klondike returned, his face grim, his eyes burning with a
cold, hard light. He was covered in blood, but it
wasn’t his own. He knelt beside Giana, his massive hand
gently touching her forehead. “She’s lost a lot of blood,”
he rumbled, his voice a low growl. “We need to
get her out of here. Now.”

He scooped Giana into his arms, cradling her as if
she were a fragile doll. He moved with a surprising
gentleness, his massive body a shield against the dangers of
the jungle. Chara scrambled to her feet, her own injuries
forgotten in the urgency of the moment. They had to
move. They had to get Giana to safety. The mission
was still paramount, but now, it was personal.

They began their retreat, Klondike leading the way, his pulse
rifle still spitting death, clearing a path through the remaining
resistance. Chara followed, her silenced pistol providing cover, her eyes
constantly scanning their flanks. The jungle was still a dangerous
place, but now, they had a protector, a guardian angel
with a rocket launcher and a thirst for vengeance. The
ambush had failed. The hunters had been hunted. And the
ghosts were about to disappear once more, leaving nothing but
destruction in their wake.
