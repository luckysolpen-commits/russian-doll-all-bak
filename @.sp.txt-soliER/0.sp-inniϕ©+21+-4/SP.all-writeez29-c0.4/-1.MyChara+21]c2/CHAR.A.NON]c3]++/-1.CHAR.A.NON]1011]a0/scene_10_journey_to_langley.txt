[EXT. HELICOPTER / INT. PLANE - DAY]

The helicopter slices through the morning sky, leaving Mothra Base a distant speck on the horizon. CHARA sits in the cabin, her gaze fixed on the receding jungle. The hum of the rotors is a familiar lullaby, but today it feels different, a soundtrack to an ending.

She pulls out her encrypted tablet, reviewing the data from the mech facility. The blueprints of the "BEHEMOTH" mech, the intricate network of Cerberus, the sheer scale of their operation. It's a lot to process, even for her.

Her mind drifts back to Giana and Klondike. The easy camaraderie, the shared jokes, the silent understanding. She'll miss them. The field, for all its dangers, had a raw honesty that Langley often lacked.

[INT. SMALL AIRFIELD - DAY]

The helicopter lands on a dusty, remote airfield. A single, small private jet waits on the tarmac. Chara disembarks, the wind whipping her hair. A lone PILOT stands by the jet, a brief nod exchanged between them.

[INT. PRIVATE JET - DAY]

The interior of the jet is plush, a stark contrast to the utilitarian helicopter. Chara settles into a comfortable leather seat. The engines WHINE to life, and the jet begins its ascent.

She looks out the window as the landscape changes. The dense jungle gives way to rolling hills, then cultivated fields, then sprawling urban centers. Each mile takes her further from the chaos and adrenaline of the mission, closer to the structured, predictable world of intelligence analysis.

She closes her eyes, a wave of exhaustion washing over her. The past few days have been a blur of high-stakes action, quick decisions, and constant vigilance. A rest, she admits, is long overdue.

[EXT. LANGLEY - DAY]

The jet descends, the familiar, sprawling complex of LANGLEY, CIA HEADQUARTERS, coming into view. It's a monument to bureaucracy and information, a stark concrete and glass structure that feels miles away from the vibrant, dangerous world she just left.

The jet touches down smoothly on the private runway. Chara gathers her minimal belongings, the portable data drive secured in her tactical vest.

She steps out onto the tarmac, the crisp, cool air of Virginia a sharp contrast to the humid jungle. The sounds are different too: the distant hum of traffic, the murmur of voices, the sterile quiet of a well-oiled machine.

She looks up at the imposing facade of the building. The "boring Langley," as she had called it. But perhaps, for now, boring is exactly what she needs. A chance to regroup, to analyze, to prepare for the next battle, whatever form it may take.

She walks towards the entrance, a solitary figure, ready for the next phase of her mission.
