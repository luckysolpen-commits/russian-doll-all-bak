SCENE 11

[INT./EXT. CLANDESTINE MEETING LOCATION - NIGHT]

SOUND of city hum, distant sirens, a gentle rain

Rain streaks down the grimy window of a forgotten warehouse, somewhere on the industrial outskirts of the city. Inside, the air is cold, damp, and smells of rust and decay. A single, bare bulb hangs precariously from the ceiling, casting long, dancing shadows.

CHARA sits on an overturned crate, her encrypted tablet open, displaying the chilling files she uncovered. The data chip she found in the archives lies beside it. She’s been here for hours, waiting. Her patience is wearing thin, but her resolve is iron-clad.

Suddenly, a faint SCRAPE of metal. The heavy warehouse door creaks open, revealing a sliver of the rainy night. ANON steps inside, her silhouette framed by the city lights. She’s dressed differently now, less like a desert nomad, more like a ghost in the urban sprawl. Her eyes, however, still hold that same unsettling intensity.

ANON
(Voice soft, but clear)
I knew you'd come. You always were the persistent one.

Chara rises, her hand instinctively going to the small pistol concealed beneath her jacket. She doesn't trust easily anymore.

CHARA
(Coldly)
Klondike is dead. My team is dead. Because of your "necessary deception." Because of Project Little Endians.

ANON doesn't flinch. She walks further into the warehouse, the door closing silently behind her. The rain outside intensifies.

ANON
Klondike's death was not my intention. He was a good man. A necessary sacrifice, perhaps, but not one I made lightly. As for Project Little Endians… it's far bigger than you know. And far more dangerous.

Chara gestures to the tablet.

CHARA
I know enough. Energy device. Weaponization. High-ranking officials. Some within the agency. This goes deep.

ANON nods, her gaze meeting Chara's. There's a flicker of something in her eyes – regret, perhaps, or a shared burden.

ANON
It does. And it involves your past, Chara. More directly than you can imagine.

Chara stiffens. Her past. The blurred face, the suppressed memories. She thought she had buried them.

CHARA
What are you talking about?

ANON
(Walking towards a dusty workbench, picking up a discarded piece of metal)
Klondike wasn't just your mentor. He was… protecting you. From this. From them. He knew what you were capable of. What you could become.

ANON turns, holding up the data chip Chara found. Chara's eyes widen. How did she know?

ANON (CONT'D)
This. This is the key. It contains Klondike's final message. His true debriefing. And the real reason he faked his death all those years ago. It also contains… a truth about your family. About your parents.

Chara feels a jolt. Her parents. They died when she was young, in a supposed accident. The agency had always been vague about the details.

CHARA
My parents? What about them?

ANON
They were involved. In the early stages of Project Little Endians. They tried to stop it. They were… silenced. Klondike knew. He tried to warn them. And when he couldn't, he took you. Hid you. Trained you. So you could finish what they started.

The words hit Chara like a physical blow. Her entire life, a carefully constructed lie. Her training, her purpose, all orchestrated by Klondike, not just for the agency, but for a personal vendetta, a legacy of vengeance.

CHARA
(Voice trembling with a mix of anger and grief)
He used me?

ANON
He protected you. He prepared you. He loved you, Chara. He knew this day would come. He knew you were the only one who could truly expose them. Because you have nothing left to lose. And everything to gain.

ANON inserts the data chip into a small, portable reader she pulls from her pocket. A holographic projection flickers to life. It's KLONDIKE, younger, but with the same weary eyes. He looks directly at Chara, as if speaking to her across time.

KLONDIKE (HOLO)
(Voice raspy, but clear)
Chara. If you're seeing this, then I'm gone. And the truth is out. They killed your parents. They tried to kill me. And they will try to kill you. This project… it's a cancer. It will consume everything. You are the only one who can stop it. The real asset isn't a device, Chara. It's the truth. And you are the one to wield it.

The hologram flickers, then dies. The silence in the warehouse is deafening, broken only by the drumming rain.

Chara stares at the blank space where Klondike's image had been. The grief, the anger, the betrayal, all coalesce into a burning resolve. Her past isn't just a memory; it's a weapon. And she's ready to use it.

ANON
(Softly)
Now you understand. Are you ready to fight?

Chara looks at ANON, her eyes hard, determined. The rain outside continues its relentless assault.

CHARA
(Voice low, dangerous)
Ready.
[FADE OUT.]
