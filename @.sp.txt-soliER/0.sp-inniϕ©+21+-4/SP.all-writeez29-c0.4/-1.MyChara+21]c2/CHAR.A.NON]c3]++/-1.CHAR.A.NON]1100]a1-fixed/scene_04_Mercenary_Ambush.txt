SCENE 4

[EXT. AFROTTA - MERCENARY AMBUSH - DAY]

SOUND of wind, distant shouts, then sudden gunfire

CHARA moves like a ghost through the rocky, uneven terrain, her two operatives flanking her. They use every available rock and crevice for cover, their movements synchronized and silent. The sun beats down relentlessly, baking the earth.

Suddenly, a CRACK of a rifle shot echoes through the canyon. A bullet RICHOCHETS off a rock near Chara's head. She drops instantly, signaling her team to do the same.

CHARA
(Into comms, low)
Contact! North ridge, 200 meters. Sniper.

Another shot, closer this time. Dirt sprays as it hits the ground where one of her operatives had been a second ago.

OPERATIVE 1
(Into comms)
Suppressing fire! I see movement in the dry riverbed, too. At least three.

Chara pulls out a pair of compact binoculars, scanning the ridge. She spots the glint of a scope. Without hesitation, she raises her assault rifle, takes aim, and fires a controlled burst. The glint disappears.

CHARA
(Into comms)
Sniper down. Move to the riverbed. Flank them.

She leads the charge, moving with a brutal efficiency. They descend into the dry riverbed, using the eroded banks for cover. The air fills with the sharp reports of automatic weapons as the mercenaries open fire.

Chara returns fire, her shots precise and deadly. She moves, shoots, reloads, a blur of motion. One mercenary drops, then another. Her operatives provide covering fire, their training evident.

As she closes in, Chara sees a mercenary attempting to reload, fumbling with a magazine. She doesn't hesitate, closing the distance in a few powerful strides. A swift, brutal strike with the butt of her rifle, and the mercenary crumples.

She checks his pulse – nothing. Her gaze lingers for a moment on his face, a flicker of something unreadable in her eyes. A brief, almost imperceptible pause. She’s seen this before. Too many times.

OPERATIVE 2
(Approaching, winded)
All clear, Chara. They won't be bothering anyone else.

Chara nods, holstering her weapon. She scans the desolate landscape, the silence returning, broken only by the wind. The adrenaline begins to recede, leaving a hollow ache.

Her mind, for a fleeting moment, drifts. A face, blurred by time and memory, flashes in her mind’s eye. A figure from a past she rarely allows herself to revisit. The thought is quickly suppressed, pushed back into the dark corners of her consciousness.

CHARA
(Voice flat)
Let's move. We're losing daylight.

She turns, leading her team deeper into Afrotta, leaving the fallen mercenaries behind. The sun begins its slow descent, casting long, ominous shadows across the desert.
[FADE OUT.]
