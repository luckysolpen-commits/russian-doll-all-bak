The world swam back into focus, a kaleidoscope of pain and disorientation. Chara lay
on her back, the rough fabric of a stretcher digging into her wounded side. The rhythmic
thump-thump of helicopter blades was a familiar, yet unwelcome, sound. She was being
extracted. The mission, a catastrophic failure, was over. The memory of Klondike’s lifeless
eyes, the chaos of the village battle, the searing pain in her side – it all flooded back,
a torrent of agonizing images. She tried to move, to sit up, but a sharp pain shot through
her, forcing a gasp. A hand, gentle yet firm, pressed her back down. “Easy, Chara. You’re
safe. For now.” It was Anya, her face grim, her eyes reflecting the same defeat Chara felt
in her soul.

The journey back to Mothra Base was a blur of pain and fragmented thoughts. The desert
streaked by below, an indifferent expanse that had witnessed her failure. Each bump of
the helicopter, each gust of wind, sent fresh waves of agony through her body. But the
physical pain was nothing compared to the gnawing emptiness in her gut, the crushing weight
of regret. Klondike was dead. The legendary agent, the asset they were supposed to protect,
was gone. And it was her fault. She had failed. The word echoed in her mind, a relentless
torture. She had been so confident, so prepared, and yet, in the end, it hadn’t been enough.

Sleep offered no escape, only a descent into a nightmare of gunfire and Klondike’s dying
gasp. She woke with a jolt, drenched in sweat, the phantom pain in her side a cruel reminder
of the reality. The helicopter was still flying, the sun now a fiery orb on the horizon,
painting the sky in hues of orange and purple. Night was falling, bringing with it a chilling
realization: the darkness outside mirrored the darkness within her. She was wounded, yes,
but the deeper wounds were not visible, etched into her very being. The mission had not
just failed; it had shattered something fundamental within her, a belief in her own infallibility,
in the clear lines between right and wrong.

She closed her eyes, trying to block out the images, the sounds, the crushing weight of
her failure. But they persisted, a relentless assault on her psyche. The faces of the villagers,
their fear, their silent pleas for help, haunted her. She had promised them safety, and
she had delivered only chaos and death. The weight of their suffering, added to the burden
of Klondike’s demise, was almost unbearable. She was a CIA operative, trained to be detached,
to compartmentalize, to suppress emotion. But this was different. This was personal. This
was a wound that would not heal easily, a scar that would forever mark her. The helicopter
continued its relentless journey, carrying her away from the battlefield, but not from the
consequences of her actions. The retreat was not just physical; it was a retreat into herself,
into a world of grief and unanswered questions. The shadow of the crow had not only fallen
on Klondike, but had also cast its long, dark pall over Chara’s soul.
