SCENE 5

[EXT. AFROTTA - ISOLATED VILLAGE - DAY]

SOUND of distant wind, a child's faint cry, general village murmurs

The sun hangs high, a relentless orb in the pale sky. CHARA and her two operatives approach a small, isolated village, a cluster of mud-brick dwellings baking under the intense heat. Unlike the previous village, this one feels eerily quiet, almost deserted.

Chara signals her team to hold back, taking point. She moves cautiously, her senses on high alert. The usual sounds of village life – chatter, cooking, children playing – are absent. Only a faint, unsettling murmur can be heard.

As they enter the village square, they see the villagers. They are not going about their daily lives. Instead, they are huddled together in small, tight groups, their faces etched with fear and apprehension. Their eyes dart nervously, not at Chara, but at something, or someone, beyond her.

Chara’s eyes narrow. This isn't just fear; it's a palpable tension, a collective holding of breath.

CHARA
(Low, to her team)
Something's wrong. Stay sharp.

She takes a step forward, intending to approach one of the huddles, when a figure emerges from the deep shadow of a large, ancient baobab tree at the edge of the square. The villagers flinch, recoiling further.

It’s a WOMAN (30s-40s, striking, with an air of quiet authority and eyes that seem to hold ancient secrets). She moves with a deliberate, almost theatrical slowness, her gaze fixed directly on Chara. She wears simple, yet elegant, desert attire that seems to blend with the environment.

Chara instinctively reaches for her sidearm, but the woman makes no threatening move. Her expression is unreadable, a faint, knowing smile playing on her lips.

WOMAN
(Voice calm, clear, with a subtle, unplaceable accent)
Agent Chara. You're precisely on schedule. Or perhaps, just a little late.

Chara freezes. How does this woman know her name? And the mission's timing?

CHARA
Who are you?

WOMAN
(A soft chuckle)
I am ANON. Your informant. And your asset.

Chara’s mind races. ANON? The trusted, yet unconventional, source? This woman? And she's the asset? This changes everything. The mission parameters, the risk assessment, the very nature of their objective.

CHARA
(Slightly incredulous)
You're the asset? But… the intel said…

ANON
(Interrupting, her smile widening slightly)
The intel was… incomplete. A necessary deception. To ensure the right people came for me. And to ensure the right people didn't.

She gestures with a graceful hand towards the huddling villagers, then back towards the path Chara took.

ANON (CONT'D)
They're here, you know. The others. The ones who truly want what I possess. And they're not interested in a quiet transport.

Before Chara can respond, ANON turns her head slightly, her gaze shifting to a point just behind Chara, her eyes widening almost imperceptibly. A flicker of genuine surprise, then a profound sadness, crosses her face.

ANON (CONT'D)
And it seems… he's here too.

Chara spins around, following ANON's gaze. Standing at the entrance to the village, silhouetted against the harsh sunlight, is a figure. Tall, broad-shouldered, with a familiar, almost legendary bearing. He looks older, scarred, but unmistakably…

CHARA
(Whispering, disbelieving)
Klondike?

AGENT KLONDIKE (50s, rugged, weary, but with an undeniable presence) stands there, alive. The man presumed dead for years. The legend. He looks at Chara, a ghost of a smile on his face, then his eyes meet ANON's. A silent, profound understanding passes between them.
[FADE OUT.]
