SCENE 9

[INT. CIA HEADQUARTERS - CHARA'S OFFICE - DAY]

SOUND of muffled office chatter, distant phones ringing

WEEKS LATER. The sterile, fluorescent-lit confines of a small, nondescript office. CHARA sits at a metal desk, staring blankly at a stack of paper files. The vibrant chaos of Afrotta feels a million miles away, replaced by the monotonous hum of bureaucracy.

Her arm is still bandaged, a constant, irritating reminder of the mission. Her face is drawn, a weariness etched around her eyes that wasn't there before. The sharp, focused intensity has been replaced by a dull, almost vacant stare.

The files are mundane: expense reports, inventory checks, security clearances for new recruits. Desk work. The kind of work she used to scoff at, the kind of work that now feels like a cruel punishment.

She picks up a pen, but her hand hovers over the paper, unable to start. Her mind is elsewhere, replaying the last moments in Afrotta: the hail of gunfire, Klondike's fall, the desperate, futile fight. The image of his eyes, wide and unseeing, is burned into her memory.

A KNOCK at the open door. GIANA NGUYEN stands there, holding a steaming mug. She enters, her expression softer than usual.

GIANA
(Quietly)
Coffee. Black, just how you like it.

Chara nods, taking the mug. The warmth seeps into her hands, but does little to thaw the coldness in her heart.

GIANA (CONT'D)
How are you holding up?

CHARA
(Voice flat)
I'm here. Doing my part. Pushing paper.

Giana sighs, pulling up a chair opposite the desk. She looks at the stack of files, then back at Chara.

GIANA
I know this isn't what you're used to. But you need time to… process. To heal. We can't afford to lose you, Chara. Not now.

CHARA
(A bitter laugh)
Lose me? I lost Klondike. I lost my team. I lost the mission. What's left to lose?

Giana reaches across the desk, placing a hand gently on Chara's arm. Chara flinches, but doesn't pull away.

GIANA
Your resolve. Your purpose. That's what's left. Klondike wouldn't want you to drown in this, Chara. He'd want you to fight.

Chara looks at her, a flicker of something in her eyes. Anger. Grief. But also, a nascent spark of the old fire.

CHARA
Fight what? Ghosts? Lies? They got away with it, Giana. Whoever set this up. They won.

GIANA
(Leaning forward, her voice low and conspiratorial)
Did they? The debriefing you wrote… it's… compelling. There are questions, Chara. Big ones. And not just from me.

Chara looks at her, a new intensity in her gaze. Questions. That's something she can work with. Something tangible.

GIANA (CONT'D)
It's not over, Chara. Not if you don't want it to be. But you have to be ready. Ready to dig. Ready to face what you might find.

Chara stares at the stack of mundane files, then at the coffee mug, then back at Giana. The emptiness is still there, but now, a new feeling begins to stir within her. A familiar, almost comforting, sense of purpose. The need to know. The need for justice.

She picks up the pen again, this time with a firmer grip. But instead of the expense reports, she pulls a blank sheet of paper towards her. Her eyes, though still weary, hold a newfound determination.
[FADE OUT.]
