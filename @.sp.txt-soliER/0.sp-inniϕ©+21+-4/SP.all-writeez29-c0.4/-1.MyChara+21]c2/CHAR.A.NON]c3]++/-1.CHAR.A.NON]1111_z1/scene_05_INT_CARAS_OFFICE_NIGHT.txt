SCENE 5: INT. CARA'S OFFICE - NIGHT

SOUND of a gentle rain against the window, distant city sounds

[INT. CARA'S OFFICE - NIGHT]

The city outside Cara's office window is now a blur of shimmering lights, distorted by the steady fall of rain. The office itself is a sanctuary of quiet intensity. Cara is still at her desk, surrounded by printouts and glowing screens, but the frantic energy of the lab has been replaced by a focused, almost meditative concentration.

She sifts through reports on Project Nightingale, her mind racing. The more she reads, the more unsettling the picture becomes. Decades of suppressed information, hushed investigations, and unexplained phenomena. It's a rabbit hole far deeper than she ever imagined.

Her secure phone, usually silent unless she initiates contact, suddenly CHIMES. It's an unknown number, encrypted, bypassing all standard protocols. Cara's instincts, honed by years of dealing with the clandestine, immediately go on high alert.

She hesitates for a moment, then answers, her voice guarded.

CARA
(Into phone)
Director Cara.

A smooth, cultured voice, with a faint, almost imperceptible European accent, replies. It's calm, confident, and utterly devoid of emotion.

VICTOR LAGRAINE (V.O.)
(Voice, calm, confident)
Director Cara. A pleasure to finally make your acquaintance. My name is Victor LaGraine. And I believe I have information that will be… enlightening regarding your current predicament.

Cara's grip tightens on the phone. She doesn't recognize the name, but the confidence in his voice, the directness of his approach, is unnerving.

CARA
(Skeptical)
My "predicament"? And what makes you think you have anything I need, Mr. LaGraine?

VICTOR LAGRAINE (V.O.)
(A soft chuckle)
Let's just say I have a keen interest in… anomalies. And the recent global cyber events, coupled with your agency's sudden fascination with Project Nightingale, have certainly piqued my curiosity. I know who is behind these attacks, Director. And more importantly, I know why.

Cara leans forward, her eyes narrowing. This isn't a random hacker. This is someone with deep knowledge, someone who moves in the same shadowy circles as Anon, but with a different agenda.

CARA
(Cautious)
And what do you want in return for this… enlightenment?

VICTOR LAGRAINE (V.O.)
(Direct, no hesitation)
Access, Director. Access to your most classified files on anomalous aerial phenomena. Your agency's deepest secrets regarding extraterrestrial life. A fair exchange, I believe, for the truth you seek.

Cara scoffs, a bitter laugh escaping her lips. He's asking for the keys to the kingdom. The very information her agency has spent decades suppressing.

CARA
(Firmly)
That's not going to happen, Mr. LaGraine. We don't negotiate with… extortionists.

VICTOR LAGRAINE (V.O.)
(His voice remains calm, almost amused)
Extortion? No, Director. A simple transaction. You see, I have what you need to stop this escalating crisis. And you, in turn, possess the final pieces of a puzzle I have been assembling for a very long time. Think of it as a mutually beneficial arrangement. The alternative, I assure you, is far more… chaotic.

Cara is silent, weighing her options. Her gut screams danger, but her analytical mind recognizes the potential. This man, LaGraine, clearly has information. And the situation is spiraling out of control.

CARA
(After a long pause)
Prove it. Give me something concrete. Something I can verify.

VICTOR LAGRAINE (V.O.)
(A satisfied hum)
Excellent. I knew you were a pragmatist, Director. I will send you a small… sample. A taste of what I possess. And then, perhaps, we can discuss a more formal arrangement. Expect a message shortly. And remember, Director, time is of the essence.

The line disconnects. Cara stares at the phone, a new kind of dread settling in. She's just opened a door to an unknown player, a man who seems to know too much, and who is asking for everything. The rain outside intensifies, mirroring the storm brewing within her.
[FADE OUT.]
