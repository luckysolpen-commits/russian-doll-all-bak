SCENE 21: INT. CARA'S OFFICE / EXT. LANGLEY GROUNDS / VARIOUS - DAWN TO DAY

SOUND — The low, sterile hum of fluorescent lights slowly giving way to the distant thrum of morning traffic and helicopter rotors.

[INT. LANGLEY HEADQUARTERS - CARA'S OFFICE - DAWN]

Pale dawn light bleeds through the panoramic windows, cold and unforgiving. The office is eerily pristine. Every trace of the previous night’s breakdown has been erased: no coffee mug, no scattered files, no personal items. The generic landscape print still hangs in place of Anon’s photograph.

Cara’s ergonomic chair sits empty, pushed back slightly as if she rose in haste. Her secure phone lies centered on the desk, screen black, battery nearly dead. A single notification pulses faintly: “NO SIGNAL.”

The door stands slightly ajar.
[CUT TO:]
[INT. LANGLEY HEADQUARTERS - CORRIDOR - DAWN]

Agent Thompson strides down the hallway, folder in hand. He knocks sharply on Cara’s door and pushes it open.

THOMPSON
(voice tight with concern)
Director? Overnight reports on the breaches—

He freezes. The room is vacant. No jacket. No tablet. No sign of life.

THOMPSON
(calling louder)
Director Cara?

Only the HVAC hum answers.
[CUT TO:]
[EXT. LANGLEY GROUNDS - SECURE PARKING GARAGE - MORNING]

Dr. Patel stands beside Cara’s reserved spot. Empty. Her black government sedan is gone. Patel checks her watch, then frantically dials her phone.

DR. PATEL
(voice rising)
This is Dr. Patel in Cyber Intelligence. Has Director Cara logged any movement? …Nothing? She never misses the 0600 briefing.
[CUT TO:]
[INT. CARA’S FAMILY HOME - KITCHEN - DAY (INTERCUT)]

Cara’s SISTER (mid-30s, exhausted, cradling a toddler) paces, phone pressed to her ear.

SISTER
(voice breaking)
She promised she’d call after work last night. The CIA says she left the building but never came home. No trace. They’re calling it a “classified assignment” now… but I know my sister. Something’s wrong.

She sinks into a chair, tears falling.

SISTER
(whisper)
Please… she’s all we have.
[CUT TO:]
[INT. CIA SITUATION ROOM - DAY]

A tense briefing. The same MAN and WOMAN from Scene 20 stand at the head of the long table. Wall screens display looping satellite feeds, traffic camera stills, and cell-tower pings — all cutting off abruptly at 11:47 PM the night before.

MAN
(flat, rehearsed)
Director Cara’s vehicle last registered leaving Langley at 2243. No further pings. No financial activity. No surveillance hits within a fifty-mile radius. We are treating this as a potential hostile abduction… or voluntary disappearance linked to her recent instability regarding Project Nightingale.

WOMAN
(colder)
In light of the sensitive materials she accessed — materials tied to decades-old incidents involving unidentified debris — both scenarios remain active. The official narrative will be that she is on an extended classified assignment. No leaks. No speculation.

Agent Thompson leans forward, barely containing his anger.

THOMPSON
She wouldn’t vanish. Not after you threatened her. Not after what she saw in that windmill — the metal on the altar, the way it didn’t behave like anything on Earth. Just like the stories from ’47. The rancher finds something strange, you call it a weather balloon, then you make the whole thing disappear.

The Man and Woman exchange a brief, unreadable glance.

MAN
(icy whisper)
Careful, Agent. History has shown that some wreckage is better left explained as simple balloons and radar targets. The public doesn’t need another Roswell. And neither do we.

A heavy silence falls. On the central monitor, red text updates in real time:

STATUS: MISSING — PRESUMED COMPROMISED
PROJECT NIGHTINGALE ACCESS: TERMINATED

Then, as if from an external encrypted source, a final line types itself across every screen:

“Some truths are recovered… and some carriers are recovered with them.”
[CUT TO:]
[EXT. REMOTE DESERT ROAD / NIGHT SKY - DAWN (SUBTLE, DREAMLIKE MONTAGE)]

A brief, desaturated sequence:
- Cara’s government sedan pulled off a lonely desert road, door open, interior lights still glowing.
- Strange, lightweight metallic fragments scattered on the ground — thin, iridescent, almost foil-like but impossibly strong — glinting under the rising sun.
- In the far distance, against the pale desert sky, a silent, enormous craft hovers for a moment — sleek, disc-like silhouette — before ascending without sound and vanishing into the brightening horizon.

No figures. No struggle. Just absence.

BACK TO:

[INT. CIA SITUATION ROOM - DAY]

The screens flicker once, then go dark. The officials begin to disperse as if nothing extraordinary has occurred.
[FADE TO BLACK.]
END OF SCENE 21 / END OF “CHARANON - THE FINAL CHAPTER”
