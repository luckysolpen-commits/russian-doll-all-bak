SCENE 18: INT. CARA'S OFFICE - DAY

SOUND of a faint, persistent BEEPING, the distant hum of office activity

[INT. CARA'S OFFICE - DAY]

Cara's eyes flutter open, heavy and disoriented. The first thing she registers is a faint, persistent BEEPING. Her head throbs, a dull ache behind her eyes. She blinks, trying to clear the fog from her mind, and finds herself staring at the familiar ceiling of her office at CIA Headquarters.

Sunlight, bright and unforgiving, streams through the panoramic window, illuminating dust motes dancing in the air. The hum of office activity, the distant murmur of voices, the rhythmic click of keyboards – it's all so normal, so mundane, after the terrifying intensity of the windmill.

She pushes herself up, wincing as a wave of dizziness washes over her. She's in her ergonomic chair, her suit rumpled, her hair disheveled. The tablet on her desk is dark, the holographic displays gone. The office is exactly as she left it, pristine and orderly, a stark contrast to the chaos she remembers.

CARA
(Voice hoarse, a whisper)
Ariana? Victor?

No answer. Only the persistent beeping. She looks around, a growing sense of unease settling in. There's no sign of the alien technology, no lingering scent of ozone, no shattered crystals. It's as if the entire experience was a vivid, terrifying dream.

She reaches for her head, her fingers brushing against a small, tender lump behind her ear. It's real. The pain is real. The memory of the blinding flash, the overwhelming energy, the feeling of falling… it was all real.

Her eyes fall on her secure phone, lying innocently on the desk. She picks it up, her fingers trembling slightly. No missed calls, no messages from unknown numbers. The last call logged is from Agent Thompson, hours ago.

She tries to access her personal, encrypted files, the ones containing the video LaGraine sent, the data she and Victor had poured over. Access denied. Every file, every piece of evidence, is gone. Wiped clean.

Panic begins to set in. Was she drugged? Hypnotized? Or worse… was she compromised?

Suddenly, a small, folded piece of paper catches her eye. It's tucked beneath the base of her monitor, almost hidden. She picks it up. It's a simple, handwritten note, in a delicate, elegant script she doesn't recognize.

NOTE (O.S.)
(Handwritten on paper)
"Meet me at the old oak tree in town square. Come alone. The truth is still out there."

Cara stares at the note, her mind racing. The handwriting is unfamiliar, yet the message is clear. It's a continuation. A thread. Someone knows. Someone is still out there, trying to guide her.

She looks out the window at the bustling city, the ordinary world that seems so oblivious to the cosmic chess game being played beneath its surface. The headache persists, a constant reminder of the impossible truth she encountered. She is back in her office, but she is not the same. The world is not the same.
[FADE OUT.]
