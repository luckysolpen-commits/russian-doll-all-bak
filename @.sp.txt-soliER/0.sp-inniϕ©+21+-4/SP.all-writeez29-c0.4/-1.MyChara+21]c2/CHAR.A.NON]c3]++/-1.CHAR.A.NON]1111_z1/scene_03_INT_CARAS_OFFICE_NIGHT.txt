SCENE 3: INT. CARA'S OFFICE - NIGHT

SOUND of distant city hum, occasional police siren

[INT. CARA'S OFFICE - NIGHT]

The panoramic window of Cara's office now reflects the twinkling lights of the city, a stark contrast to the grim reality inside. Empty coffee cups and discarded printouts litter her desk. The tablet, still displaying Anon's cryptic message, sits accusingly.

Cara is hunched over her desk, her face illuminated by the glow of her laptop. Lines of complex code scroll rapidly across the screen, but her mind is elsewhere, replaying the day's events. The sheer scale of the cyber attack, the bizarre connection to "Project Nightingale" and UFOs… it's too much to process.

She runs a hand through her hair, frustration mounting. Every instinct tells her this isn't just a random act of cyber terrorism. It feels orchestrated, a deliberate unraveling of carefully guarded secrets.

Her gaze falls on the framed photograph of Anon. He always knew. He always had a piece of the puzzle she was missing. Despite the risks, despite the unconventional nature of their relationship, he was her only true confidant in this shadowy world.

With a decisive sigh, Cara picks up her secure satellite phone. She dials a long, complex sequence of numbers, a sequence she knows by heart.

The line rings once, twice, then a click. A low, gravelly voice, distorted by a voice modulator, answers.

ANON (V.O.)
(Voice modulated, calm, almost detached)
Director. I trust you received my earlier message.

CARA
(Voice tight with urgency)
Anon, what the hell is going on? The attacks… they're hitting everything. And Thompson just told me about Project Nightingale. UFOs? Seriously?

A beat of silence on the other end. Cara can almost feel Anon's presence, his analytical mind processing her words.

ANON (V.O.)
(A faint, almost imperceptible sigh)
I warned you, Cara. The game has indeed changed. This is not merely about data theft. It's about control. And the truth, as you are beginning to discover, is far stranger than fiction.

CARA
(Frustrated)
Stranger how? What aren't you telling me? Is this connected to… everything else? The corruption, Russia, China?

ANON (V.O.)
(Voice growing colder, more distant)
All threads lead to the same loom, Director. But be warned. You are treading on ground far more dangerous than you can imagine. There are forces at play, ancient and powerful, that do not wish their secrets disturbed.

CARA
(Pleading)
Anon, I need more than cryptic warnings. People are losing their livelihoods, their privacy. The country is on the brink of chaos. What do I do?

ANON (V.O.)
(A pause, then a chilling whisper)
Trust no one, Cara. And prepare for the unknown. This is only the beginning.

The line goes dead. Cara stares at the phone, the dial tone a mocking echo in the silent office. Anon's words hang heavy in the air, a chilling prophecy. "Trust no one." But if she can't trust Anon, who can she trust?

She looks out at the city, now a sprawling tapestry of indifferent lights. The weight of the world, and the terrifying secrets it holds, settles firmly on her shoulders.
[FADE OUT.]
