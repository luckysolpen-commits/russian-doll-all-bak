Cara finally looks at them, her eyes hollow, but with a flicker
of defiance that refuses to be extinguished, that burns low
and steady like a pilot light in a house that's being demolished.
"Stability?" she says, and the word is a question and an accusation.
"You call this stability? A world built on lies, on manipulation,
on suppressing the truth? On keeping billions of people in the dark
about the most fundamental facts of their own existence?" The man
steps closer, his voice dropping to a menacing whisper that carries
more threat than any shout could. "We understand your idealism,
Director. But the world is a complex place. Some truths are best
left buried. For the greater good. For order. For control.
You will cease all investigations into Project Nightingale.
You will destroy all data, all contacts, all memories of your
recent experiences. And you will publicly denounce any claims
of extraterrestrial involvement as baseless conspiracy theories.
Your career, Director, depends on it. Your freedom, perhaps
even your life." The words hang in the air like a sentence,
which is exactly what they are. Cara closes her eyes, and a single
tear traces a path down her cheek, hot and shameful and real.
The weight of their words, the crushing reality of her situation,
settles upon her like a physical weight, like the pressure
in the windmill lab, like the force that pushed her into darkness.
She thinks of Victor, of Ariana, of the impossible truths they
revealed, of the metal on the altar that pulsed with alien light.
And then, she thinks of Anon. His warnings. His unwavering belief
in her. His love, the one real thing in a world of manufactured
fiction. The faces before her are unyielding. The threat is real.
She has fought battles she couldn't win before. This one would cost
her everything. "I understand," she whispers, and the words taste
like ash.
