The Man and Woman exchange a brief, satisfied glance, the kind
of glance that predators exchange when prey has stopped running,
and then they turn to leave, their mission accomplished, the loose
end neatly tied. As the door clicks shut behind them, the silence
they leave behind is different from the silence they found —
it is the silence of a room where something has died, not loudly
or dramatically but quietly, efficiently, the way things die
in this building, in this world, in the space between what is known
and what is permitted. Cara reaches for her secure phone.
Her fingers tremble as she dials Anon's number, the sequence
now a painful memory, each digit a step backward through a door
she is closing forever. The line rings once, twice, then a click.
His modulated voice, so familiar, so comforting, the last human
voice she will ever truly trust, answers. "Cara. I was beginning
to worry." Her voice is choked with tears, barely audible,
a child's voice, a broken voice, the voice of someone who has
just surrendered everything that made her who she was.
"Anon… it's over. I… I can't. They know. They're going to silence me.
They're going to make me destroy everything. It's not worth it.
None of it. I… I have to let you go." A long, agonizing silence
stretches between them, a silence that contains everything they
will never say to each other, everything they will never do,
every future they will never have. "Cara…" he says, and the single
word carries a weight of sorrow that nearly breaks her completely.
"I love you, Anon. I always will. But I can't… I can't risk you.
I can't risk anyone else. This is goodbye." She hears a faint,
almost imperceptible sound on the other end — a gasp, a choked sob,
or simply the static of a dying connection, of a line going dead
in more ways than one. Then, the line goes dead. The silence
in the office is absolute, profound, total. She stares at the phone,
then slowly lowers it to her desk. Her hand lingers on the cold
plastic, as if trying to hold onto the last vestiges of a connection
that has been severed. She has chosen. She has sacrificed everything.
