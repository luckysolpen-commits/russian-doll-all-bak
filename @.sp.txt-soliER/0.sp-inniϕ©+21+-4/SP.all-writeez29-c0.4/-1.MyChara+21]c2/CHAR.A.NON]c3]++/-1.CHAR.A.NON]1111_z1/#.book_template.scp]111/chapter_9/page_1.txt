Cara stands transfixed before the altar, the iridescent black metal
pulsating with a faint, internal light that seems to breathe,
to rhythm, to pulse in time with something vast and patient
and utterly alien. Ariana watches her, a serene, almost melancholic
expression on her face, the kind of look a teacher might give
a student who is about to learn something that cannot be unlearned.
The air in the chamber grows heavy, charged with an unseen energy
that makes the hair on Cara's arms stand up and her skin prickle
with static. The rhythmic hum of the alien technology intensifies,
a low thrum that vibrates through Cara's very bones, through her teeth,
through the fluid in her inner ear, finding frequencies that human
music has never reached and human language has never named.
"This changes everything," Cara whispers, her voice barely audible,
a breath shaped into words by lips that feel numb. "The entire history
we've been taught — every war, every religion, every empire,
every revolution — it's all a lie. Or worse — it's all a side note.
A footnote in a story we weren't even allowed to read." Ariana's
response is soft, almost gentle, the way one might speak to someone
standing on the edge of a cliff. "Truth is often a difficult burden,
Director. But it is also liberation. The question is not whether
you can bear it. The question is whether you can bear not knowing."
