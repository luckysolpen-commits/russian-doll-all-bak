Darkness holds her like a womb, or a tomb — perhaps both,
perhaps the distinction is meaningless here, in this place
between places, this moment between moments, this breath
that is neither in nor out but suspended, eternal. Time has
no meaning here. There is no up, no down, no before, no after.
Only the absence of everything she knew, the stripping away
of every certainty, every assumption, every foundation upon which
she built her understanding of the world and her place in it.
Fragments drift through the void like debris after an explosion —
a flash of green light that sears her memory, Ariana's voice
stretched across frequencies, the impossible metal on the altar
pulsing with its alien rhythm, Victor's fevered eyes reflecting
blue light, Anon's warnings echoing from a distant shore like
a lighthouse beam cutting through fog. Trust no one. Prepare
for the unknown. This is only the beginning. But what is
the beginning? What did she see? What did she touch? What did
she become? The questions dissolve before they can form,
swallowed by the dark like water poured into sand. Somewhere,
far away, impossibly far, something hums. A frequency too low
to hear, too deep to feel with anything but the part of her
that has always listened to the spaces between sounds, the part
that made her the best at what she does, the part that heard
the patterns in the noise that everyone else dismissed as static.
The humming grows louder, or perhaps she is growing closer to it,
drawn toward it like a moth toward a flame she cannot see
but can feel warming her frozen wings. Or perhaps it was always
there, waiting for her to notice, to remember, to wake, to choose.
The darkness shifts, changes, becomes less absolute. A faint
pressure behind her eyes, like the first hint of consciousness
after the deepest sleep. A taste of copper on her tongue, metallic
and ancient. The sense of falling, not through space but through
layers, through membranes of reality, through the thin places
between what is and what cannot be, between the world she knew
and the world that was always there, waiting beneath the surface.
And then —
