The transparent containment unit in the corner, previously empty,
suddenly glows with an intense, blinding white light that makes
the green light from the altar look dim by comparison. A faint,
almost human-like scream echoes from within — or from beyond,
from someplace that the containment unit is a window into rather
than a container of — and the sound is cut short with a finality
that makes Cara's blood freeze. She tries to move, to reach Ariana,
to grab her hand or her arm or anything human in this room
that is rapidly becoming inhuman, but the force of the energy
is overwhelming. She feels a powerful, unseen hand push her back,
not a hand exactly but a pressure, a field, a gradient of force
that increases from the altar outward like ripples from a stone
dropped in a pond, except the ripples are pushing her away.
A crushing pressure on her chest makes it hard to breathe.
Her vision blurs, the green light morphing into a swirling vortex
of colors that have no names, that exist outside the spectrum
her eyes were designed to process. Ariana's voice reaches her,
fading, distorted, stretched across frequencies: "Remember, Cara!
The truth — it will set you free!" But the words are breaking apart,
dissolving into noise, into the roar that is now everything,
the sound of the universe tearing itself open or closing itself
forever. The roar reaches a crescendo, a deafening, all-consuming
sound that vibrates through every cell of Cara's body, every molecule,
every atom, shaking her down to her constituent particles.
The green light explodes into an unbearable white flash,
consuming everything — the altar, the metal, Ariana, the consoles,
the crystalline walls, the room itself — and Cara feels herself
falling, tumbling into an abyss of pure, overwhelming energy,
a place where up and down have no meaning, where time is a concept
that belongs to a different universe, where she is everything
and nothing simultaneously. And then, nothing. Absolute, profound,
total blackness. The kind of darkness that exists before creation,
before light, before the first word was spoken into the void.
