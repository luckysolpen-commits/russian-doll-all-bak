A figure emerges from the crowd, moving with an almost unnatural
fluidity, the way something moves when it has learned to mimic
human motion but hasn't quite mastered the imperfections.
It's Ariana. But something has changed. Something fundamental.
Something that makes Cara's trained instincts scream at her
to run, to draw her weapon, to do anything but stand here
and let this thing approach. Her eyes, usually so calm and knowing,
now possess a faint, almost imperceptible glow, a shimmering
quality that seems to shift with the light, that catches the sun
and bends it, that makes looking directly at her feel like looking
into a lens that focuses something larger than light. Her smile
is too wide, too fixed, a caricature of warmth painted on a face
that is almost right but not quite, like a mask crafted by someone
who has studied photographs of humans but has never touched one.
"Cara. You came. I knew you would." The voice is Ariana's,
but layered beneath it is something else, a resonance, a harmonic,
a second voice singing the same notes an octave below hearing.
"The truth, once glimpsed, is impossible to ignore." Cara stares
at her, a cold dread creeping up her spine like frost climbing
a windowpane. "What happened? At the windmill? And how did I get
back to Langley? My files — they're all gone. Everything we saw,
everything you showed me — it's been erased." Ariana chuckles,
a sound that sends a shiver down Cara's spine, a sound devoid
of genuine mirth, more like the rustling of dry leaves in a wind
that comes from somewhere very far away. "The signal was disrupted.
They tried to silence the truth. But truth, Director, is a persistent
thing. It finds a way. It finds cracks. It finds you."
