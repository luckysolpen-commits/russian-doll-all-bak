She takes a step closer, and the air between them seems to thicken,
to charge, to become a medium for something that is not speech
but is not silence either. "You are close, Cara. So very close.
The truth that will change everything — it is within your grasp.
But you must embrace it fully. Let go of the old world, the old lies.
Open your mind to the true reality. The grand design." Ariana's voice
takes on a hypnotic quality, each word carrying a weight that presses
against Cara's consciousness like fingers pressing against her eyelids,
trying to close them, trying to make her see what she is being shown
rather than what she chooses to see. The sounds of the town square
seem to fade, receding like a tide going out, replaced by a low,
resonant hum that she recognizes from the windmill, from the lab,
from the darkness — the frequency of the alien technology, the song
of the metal, the voice of something that does not have a voice
in any human language. Ariana's eyes are now fully luminous,
a swirling vortex of green light that pulls at Cara like gravity,
that makes her knees buckle and her vision narrow to a tunnel
at the end of which is Ariana's face, beautiful and terrible.
Images flash through her mind uninvited: swirling galaxies seen
from outside, ancient symbols carved in stone older than civilization,
faces of unknown beings with eyes like the metal on the altar,
black and shifting and aware. The world around her distorts,
the vibrant colors of the town square melting into a chaotic blur
of sensation without meaning. She is losing control, her mind
a battlefield, her will a thin wall against a rising tide.
With a desperate surge of will, Cara forces herself to focus
on the one thing that grounds her, the one name that belongs
to the world before: Anon. His voice, his warnings, his unwavering
presence in her life when everything else was shifting sand.
"Trust no one." But also, beneath the warning, the love.
"Anon…" she whispers. The name seems to break the spell,
if only for a moment. Ariana's eyes flicker, the glow dimming.
"He is a distraction, Cara. A relic of the old world. Let him go."
