The night passed in fragments — brief, fitful sleep on the office couch,
interrupted by dreams of green text and blurred faces. Dawn came
reluctantly, painting the skyline in shades of gray and pale gold,
and Cara was already awake, already deep in the Nightingale files.
Her team arrived in ones and twos, their faces drawn, their movements
carrying the heavy efficiency of people who had spent the night
fighting a war that couldn't be won with weapons. Thompson arrived
with coffee and bad news, Patel with data and worse conclusions.
"Director," Thompson said, standing before the holographic display.
"We've got something new. Project Nightingale wasn't just monitoring
sightings. It was actively collecting and analyzing data from a global
network of researchers — amateur and professional, civilian and military.
Think of it as a crowdsourced intelligence operation, but for
extraterrestrial phenomena." The word extraterrestrial hung in the air
like smoke. "And the attacks?" Cara asked. Patel turned from her console.
"Not just data, Director. We've detected a secondary objective.
A highly sophisticated data-mining operation, sifting through
the compromised systems for specific keywords and patterns.
It's almost as if they're looking for something very particular
within the UFO research. Non-terrestrial material analysis.
Energy signatures. Propulsion systems." The lab went very quiet.
Non-terrestrial material. The phrase belonged in science fiction,
not in the CIA Cyber Intelligence Lab. "Are you telling me someone
is hacking into government systems to steal information about
alien artifacts?" Thompson shifted uncomfortably. "The data doesn't lie,
Director. The attack patterns, the targets, the specific files being
accessed — it all points to a deliberate effort to acquire information
related to these anomalies." Cara walked closer to the holographic display,
her eyes tracing the glowing lines that connected compromised systems
across the globe. The pattern was unmistakable — the attacks weren't
aimed at financial records or military secrets. They were aimed
at the fringes of the Nightingale database, at the files that dealt
with physical evidence, with material that had been recovered from
crash sites and sighting locations, with samples that had been analyzed
and classified and locked away in facilities that didn't officially exist.
