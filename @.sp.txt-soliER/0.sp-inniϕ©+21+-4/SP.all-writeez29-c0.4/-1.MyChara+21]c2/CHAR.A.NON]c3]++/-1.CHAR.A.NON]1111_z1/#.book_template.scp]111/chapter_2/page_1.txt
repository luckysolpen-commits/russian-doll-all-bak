Night fell over Washington like a curtain, heavy and absolute,
and Cara's office became an island of light in a sea of darkness.
The city outside was a tapestry of indifferent stars, each one
a window in a building full of people living their ordinary lives,
unaware that the foundations of their reality were being shaken.
Inside the office, the atmosphere had shifted from the controlled
urgency of the lab to something more intimate, more dangerous.
Empty coffee cups crowded the desk like a small army of paper soldiers,
and discarded printouts lay scattered in chaotic patterns.
The tablet still displayed Anon's message, the green text
a silent accusation in the dim light. Cara sat hunched
over her laptop, lines of complex code scrolling across the screen,
but her mind wasn't on the code. It was on the conversation
with Anon, his voice distorted but unmistakable, carrying
warnings that grew more chilling with each passing hour.
Trust no one. Prepare for the unknown. This is only the beginning.
She picked up the secure satellite phone, the device heavy
and cold in her hand, and dialed the number from memory.
The line rang once, twice. Then a click. A low, gravelly voice,
distorted by a modulator that stripped away everything human.
"Director. I trust you received my earlier message."
"Anon," she said, her voice tight with urgency. "What the hell
is going on? The attacks — they're hitting everything.
And Thompson just told me about Project Nightingale. UFOs? Seriously?"
The silence on the other end stretched. "I warned you, Cara.
The game has indeed changed. This is not merely about data theft.
It's about control. And the truth, as you are beginning to discover,
is far stranger than fiction." "Stranger how? What aren't you
telling me?" His voice grew colder. "All threads lead to the same loom,
Director. But be warned. You are treading on ground far more dangerous
than you can imagine. There are forces at play, ancient and powerful,
that do not wish their secrets disturbed." The word ancient sent
a shiver through her. "Anon, I need more than cryptic warnings.
What do I do?" Another pause. "Trust no one, Cara. And prepare
for the unknown. This is only the beginning." The line went dead.
