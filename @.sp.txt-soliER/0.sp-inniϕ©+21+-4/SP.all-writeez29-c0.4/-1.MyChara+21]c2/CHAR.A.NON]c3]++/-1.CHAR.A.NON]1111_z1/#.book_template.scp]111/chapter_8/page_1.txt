Ariana turns, her movements fluid and silent, and gestures towards
a section of the circular wall. What appeared to be solid timber
now slides open with a soft hiss, revealing a hidden passage.
A faint, pulsating light emanates from within, casting strange
shadows that dance across the dusty floor of the windmill above.
"Come," she says softly. "The truth awaits." Cara hesitates
for a moment, her pistol still clutched in her hand, every fiber
of her being screaming caution. But the allure of the unknown,
the promise of answers that have eluded her entire career,
is too strong to resist. She follows Ariana into the passage.
The passage is short, narrow, the walls lined with something
that feels less like stone and more like metal — smooth, warm,
almost alive beneath her fingertips as she brushes them against
the wall for balance. The air changes as they descend, growing
cleaner, sharper, carrying a faint ozone tang that she recognizes
from the most advanced laboratories she has visited. This is not
a crude hiding place. This is a facility, purposeful and maintained.
The passage opens into a surprisingly spacious, circular chamber
beneath the windmill, and Cara stops dead in her tracks. Unlike
the dusty, decaying exterior of the windmill, this space is clean,
sterile, and filled with an array of bizarre, otherworldly equipment.
The pulsating light, a soft, iridescent green, emanates from
a central console, illuminating intricate holographic displays
that shimmer with complex, alien symbols — geometries that hurt
her eyes to look at, angles that seem to exist in more dimensions
than the human mind can process. "What is all this?" Cara whispers,
her voice hushed, filled with awe. "I've never seen anything like it."
