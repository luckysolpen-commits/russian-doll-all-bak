Ariana walks to the central console, her fingers gliding over
its smooth, unearthly surface with the familiarity of someone
who has done this a thousand times. The symbols on the holographic
displays shift and dance in response, rearranging themselves
into patterns that Cara cannot decipher but can feel — a pressure
behind her eyes, a vibration in her teeth, a sense that the air
itself is thick with information she cannot access. "This, Director,
is a nexus," Ariana says, and her voice takes on a deeper,
more resonant quality, as if the room itself is amplifying her words.
"A point of convergence. For decades, your governments have been
in contact with them. Not just observing, not just studying —
but actively collaborating. Exchanging technology, information,
even personnel." Cara's eyes widen, scanning the room with new urgency.
There are strange, crystalline structures embedded in the walls,
glowing faintly with an inner light that pulses in rhythms
she cannot predict. Devices that hum with an unseen energy,
their purpose beyond her comprehension but their sophistication
undeniable. And in one corner, a large, transparent containment unit,
empty but clearly designed for something — someone — non-human.
"Collaborating?" Cara says, and the word tastes wrong in her mouth,
like a lie she's being asked to swallow. "With aliens? That's insane.
LaGraine said they were just covering up evidence, manipulating
the narrative." Ariana turns, and the sadness in her eyes is ancient,
immeasurable, the kind of weariness that belongs to something
that has watched civilizations rise and fall like tides. "Victor LaGraine,
for all his brilliance, only sees what he is allowed to see.
He is a pawn in a much larger game, a useful tool for those who
truly pull the strings. The cover-up was real, yes. But it was also
a smokescreen for the deeper, more insidious truth."
