Ariana extends a hand towards the pedestal at the center of the room.
The shimmering energy field around it dissipates with a soft pop,
like a bubble bursting, revealing its contents. Cara takes a step closer,
her breath catching in her throat, every instinct telling her
that she is about to see something that will change everything.
Resting on the altar, bathed in the soft, iridescent green light,
is a piece of metal. It is unlike anything Cara has ever seen,
anything she could have imagined in her most speculative moments.
It is not a dull, earthly gray, but a shifting, iridescent black,
absorbing and reflecting light in impossible ways that make her eyes
water and her mind rebel. Its surface is smooth, almost liquid,
yet it holds a rigid, geometric form that suggests intention,
design, purpose. There are no seams, no rivets, no signs of fabrication,
no weld marks or tool strokes. It looks as if it grew, organically,
into its perfect shape, the way a crystal grows or a bone forms,
following rules of matter that human science has never encountered.
"What is this?" Cara whispers, her voice barely audible over the hum
of the room. "It's not from here." Ariana's voice resonates with
a quiet power that seems to emanate from the walls themselves.
"No, Director. It is not. This is a fragment. A piece of a vessel
that traversed the void between stars. Recovered decades ago,
meticulously studied, and then, meticulously hidden. It is the ultimate
proof. The undeniable evidence of their presence." Cara reaches out
a hand, drawn by an irresistible force that she cannot name,
but stops short of touching it. She can feel a faint energy
radiating from the metal, a subtle vibration that hums in her
fingertips and travels up her arm, settling somewhere behind
her sternum. It is cold, impossibly cold, yet somehow alive,
as if the metal itself is aware of her presence and is watching back.
