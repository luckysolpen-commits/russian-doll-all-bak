Cara's grip tightened on the phone. She didn't recognize the name,
but the confidence in his voice, the directness of his approach,
was unnerving. "My predicament? And what makes you think you have
anything I need, Mr. LaGraine?" A soft chuckle traveled down the line,
dry and humorless. "Let's just say I have a keen interest in anomalies.
And the recent global cyber events, coupled with your agency's sudden
fascination with Project Nightingale, have certainly piqued my curiosity.
I know who is behind these attacks, Director. And more importantly,
I know why." Cara leaned forward, her eyes narrowing. This wasn't
a random hacker. This was someone with deep knowledge, someone who moved
in the same shadowy circles as Anon, but with a different agenda.
"And what do you want in return for this enlightenment?"
"Access, Director. Access to your most classified files on anomalous
aerial phenomena. Your agency's deepest secrets regarding
extraterrestrial life. A fair exchange, I believe, for the truth you seek."
Cara scoffed, a bitter laugh escaping her lips. He was asking for
the keys to the kingdom. The very information her agency had spent
decades suppressing. "That's not going to happen, Mr. LaGraine.
We don't negotiate with extortionists." His voice remained calm,
almost amused. "Extortion? No, Director. A simple transaction.
You see, I have what you need to stop this escalating crisis.
And you, in turn, possess the final pieces of a puzzle I have been
assembling for a very long time. Think of it as a mutually beneficial
arrangement. The alternative, I assure you, is far more chaotic."
Cara was silent, weighing her options. Her gut screamed danger,
but her analytical mind recognized the potential. This man, LaGraine,
clearly had information. And the situation was spiraling out of control.
"Prove it. Give me something concrete. Something I can verify."
A satisfied hum traveled down the line. "Excellent. I knew you were
a pragmatist, Director. I will send you a small sample. A taste
of what I possess. And then, perhaps, we can discuss a more formal
arrangement. Expect a message shortly. And remember, Director,
time is of the essence." The line disconnected. Cara stared at the phone,
a new kind of dread settling in. She had just opened a door to an unknown
player, a man who seemed to know too much, and who was asking for everything.
The rain outside intensified, mirroring the storm brewing within her.
