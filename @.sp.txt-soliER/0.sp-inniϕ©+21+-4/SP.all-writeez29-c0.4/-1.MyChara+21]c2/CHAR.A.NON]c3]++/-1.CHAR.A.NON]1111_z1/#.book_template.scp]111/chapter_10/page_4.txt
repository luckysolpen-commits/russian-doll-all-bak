Suddenly, a small, folded piece of paper catches her eye,
so ordinary in a room full of extraordinary technology that
it almost seems planted — because it is. It's tucked beneath
the base of her monitor, almost hidden, positioned at exactly
the angle where someone sitting in her chair, desperate for
anything solid, anything real, would notice it. She picks it up.
It's a simple, handwritten note, in a delicate, elegant script
she doesn't recognize, the kind of handwriting that suggests
a person who still believes in the intimacy of ink on paper.
"Meet me at the old oak tree in town square. Come alone.
The truth is still out there." Three sentences, each one
a door opening onto a room she's not sure she wants to enter.
Cara stares at the note, her mind racing, trying to triangulate
meaning from context, from timing, from the sheer audacity
of a physical note appearing in the most secure office
in the Cyber Intelligence division. The handwriting is unfamiliar,
yet the message is clear. It's a continuation. A thread.
Someone knows. Someone is still out there, watching, waiting,
trying to guide her through a maze that has no walls she can see.
She looks out the window at the bustling city, the ordinary world
that seems so oblivious to the cosmic chess game being played
beneath its surface, the game whose pieces include truths
that would shatter every belief system humanity has constructed.
The headache persists, a constant reminder of the impossible
truth she encountered, of the lump behind her ear, of the darkness
that held her and then released her back into a world that
pretends none of it happened. She is back in her office,
but she is not the same. The world is not the same. Something
has shifted, cracked, opened. She folds the note carefully,
slips it into her pocket, and stands. The truth is still out there.
