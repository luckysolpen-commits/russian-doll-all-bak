SCENE 8: INT. OLD WAREHOUSE - NIGHT

SOUND of dripping water, distant creaks, Cara's cautious footsteps

[INT. OLD WAREHOUSE - NIGHT]

The heavy, rusted door groans in protest as Cara pushes it open, revealing a cavernous, inky blackness within. The air inside is thick with the smell of damp concrete, dust, and something metallic, almost electrical. The rain outside, though still audible, is muffled, creating an oppressive silence.

Cara steps inside, her hand instinctively going to the tactical flashlight clipped to her belt. She clicks it on, and a narrow beam of light cuts through the gloom, revealing a vast, empty space. Dust motes dance in the beam, illuminated like tiny, frantic stars.

The warehouse is immense, its high ceilings lost in shadow. Skeletal remains of old machinery, draped in cobwebs, stand like forgotten giants. Puddles of water reflect the flashlight's beam, remnants of leaks from the decaying roof. Every sound echoes, amplified by the emptiness – the drip, drip, drip of water, the distant creak of settling metal, and the soft, cautious scuff of Cara's boots on the concrete floor.

She moves slowly, methodically, her head swiveling, the flashlight beam sweeping across the vast expanse. Her senses are on high alert, every shadow a potential threat, every sound a possible warning. She checks behind rusted pillars, peers into dark alcoves, her hand hovering near her concealed weapon.

Then, a faint GLOW in the distance. A soft, pulsating blue light, almost imperceptible against the overwhelming darkness. It draws her in, a beacon in the void.

Cara quickens her pace, her footsteps becoming more purposeful. As she approaches the source of the light, the metallic, electrical scent grows stronger, a faint hum now audible beneath the dripping water.

The blue glow emanates from a section of the warehouse that seems… different. Less derelict, more purposeful. A makeshift barrier, constructed from salvaged metal sheets, partially obscures the view. Beyond it, the hum intensifies, a low thrum that vibrates through the concrete floor.

Cara reaches the barrier, her heart pounding a steady rhythm against her ribs. She peers through a narrow gap in the metal sheets. The blue light is brighter now, revealing glimpses of complex machinery, wires snaking across the floor, and the unmistakable silhouette of a figure standing before it.

It's VICTOR LAGRAINE. He stands with his back to her, his posture one of intense concentration, bathed in the ethereal blue glow. He seems to be interacting with something, a console perhaps, that is the source of the light and the hum.

Cara takes a deep breath, steeling herself. She has found him. And whatever he's doing, it's clearly not ordinary.
[FADE OUT.]
