SCENE 14: INT. OLD WINDMILL - NIGHT

SOUND of the wind whistling through gaps in the wood, Cara's cautious footsteps, the faint creak of old timbers

[INT. OLD WINDMILL - NIGHT]

The heavy wooden door of the windmill groans as Cara pushes it open, revealing a circular, dusty interior. The air is cold and still, carrying the faint scent of old wood and something else, something metallic and ozone-like. Moonlight filters through cracks in the ancient timbers, casting eerie, shifting patterns on the floor.

Cara steps inside, her tactical pistol drawn, held steady in both hands. Her eyes scan the gloom, every sense on high alert. The space is larger than it appears from the outside, a central shaft rising into darkness, surrounded by a narrow, circular walkway. Dust motes dance in the faint light, disturbed by her entry.

CARA
(Voice firm, echoing slightly)
Who's there? Show yourself!

Silence. Only the mournful whistle of the wind through the gaps in the walls answers her. Cara moves slowly, methodically, her footsteps soft on the creaking floorboards. She checks behind a stack of old, rusted machinery, then peers into a dark alcove.

Suddenly, a figure emerges from the deepest shadows, so silently that Cara almost misses it. It's a WOMAN (30s-40s, striking, with an otherworldly beauty and eyes that seem to hold ancient secrets). She moves with an unnerving grace, her dark clothing blending seamlessly with the shadows.

ARIANA
(Voice soft, melodic, yet with an underlying power)
I've been expecting you, Director Cara. Or should I say… the one who seeks the truth.

Cara spins, her pistol leveling instantly at the woman's chest. Her finger hovers over the trigger, but something in the woman's calm demeanor, her almost serene expression, makes her hesitate.

CARA
(Voice sharp)
Who are you? And how do you know my name?

ARIANA
(A faint, knowing smile)
My name is Ariana. And I know many things, Director. Things that have been hidden from humanity for far too long. Things that even your esteemed Victor LaGraine only partially comprehends.

Cara's eyes narrow. LaGraine. So this woman is connected to him, or at least to the same hidden world.

CARA
(Lowering her pistol slightly, but still wary)
What do you want? The voice on the phone… was that you?

ARIANA
(Nods slowly)
It was. I needed to ensure you came alone. The information I possess… it is not for casual ears. It concerns the true nature of the government's involvement with… extraterrestrial entities. And it is far more pervasive, and far more terrifying, than you can possibly imagine.

Cara stares at her, a cold dread settling in. She had thought LaGraine's revelations were the peak of the conspiracy. But Ariana's words suggest an even deeper, darker layer. The wind outside howls, a mournful cry that seems to echo the secrets about to be unveiled.
[FADE OUT.]
