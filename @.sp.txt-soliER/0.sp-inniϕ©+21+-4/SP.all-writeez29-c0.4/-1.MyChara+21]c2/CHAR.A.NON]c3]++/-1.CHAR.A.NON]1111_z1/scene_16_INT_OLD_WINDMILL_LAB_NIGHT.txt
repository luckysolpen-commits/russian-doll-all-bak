SCENE 16: INT. OLD WINDMILL - LAB - NIGHT

SOUND of the rhythmic hum, a faint, almost musical resonance from the altar

[INT. OLD WINDMILL - LAB - NIGHT]

Ariana gestures towards the pedestal. The shimmering energy field around it dissipates with a soft POP, revealing its contents. Cara takes a step closer, her breath catching in her throat.

Resting on the altar, bathed in the soft, iridescent green light, is a piece of metal. It's unlike anything Cara has ever seen. It's not a dull, earthly gray, but a shifting, iridescent black, absorbing and reflecting light in impossible ways. Its surface is smooth, almost liquid, yet it holds a rigid, geometric form. There are no seams, no rivets, no signs of fabrication. It looks as if it grew, organically, into its perfect shape.

CARA
(Voice hushed, filled with awe and disbelief)
What… what is this? It's… it's not from here.

ARIANA
(Her voice resonating with a quiet power)
No, Director. It is not. This is a fragment. A piece of a vessel that traversed the void between stars. Recovered decades ago, meticulously studied, and then, meticulously hidden. It is the ultimate proof. The undeniable evidence of their presence.

Cara reaches out a hand, drawn by an irresistible force, but stops short of touching it. She can feel a faint energy radiating from the metal, a subtle vibration that hums in her fingertips. It's cold, yet somehow alive.

CARA
(Looking at Ariana, then back at the metal)
But… why is it on an altar? Is this some kind of… cult? Do you believe it's an angel? A devil?

Ariana's expression softens, a flicker of ancient sorrow in her eyes.

ARIANA
(A sigh, almost imperceptible)
Humanity, in its fear and its ignorance, often seeks to categorize the unknown. To place it within familiar frameworks. For some, it was indeed a deity, a source of divine power. For others, a demonic entity, a harbinger of destruction. And for the governments who recovered it, it was simply a tool. A source of unimaginable power and knowledge to be exploited.

She gestures to the intricate symbols on the holographic displays, which now seem to mirror the patterns on the metal fragment.

ARIANA (CONT'D)
But it is neither, Director. It is simply… technology. Advanced beyond your current comprehension, yes. But technology nonetheless. And its placement on this 'altar' is a testament to the profound impact it had on those who first encountered it. A symbol of their awe, their fear, and their desperate attempt to control what they could not understand.

Cara circles the altar, her mind racing. The implications are staggering. This isn't just a piece of a spaceship; it's a key. A key to understanding the true history of humanity's interaction with extraterrestrial life. A key to unlocking secrets that could shatter the very foundations of civilization.

CARA
(Voice firm, a new resolve in her tone)
And you want to expose this? To show the world?

ARIANA
(Nods, her gaze unwavering)
It is time, Director. The lies have gone on for too long. The manipulation, the control… it has stifled humanity's true potential. This fragment, this truth, must be revealed. Not as a weapon, but as a catalyst. A catalyst for true understanding, for genuine evolution.

Cara looks at the iridescent metal, then at Ariana. The weight of the decision, the sheer magnitude of what they are about to unleash, settles heavily upon her. But there is also a sense of profound purpose. This is the truth. And it deserves to be known.
[FADE OUT.]
