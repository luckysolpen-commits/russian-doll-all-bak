SCENE 7: EXT. OLD WAREHOUSE - NIGHT

SOUND of heavy rain, distant thunder, the rhythmic squeak of an old sign

[EXT. OLD WAREHOUSE - NIGHT]

The city has long since gone to sleep, but the night is far from quiet. A relentless downpour lashes against the deserted streets, washing the asphalt clean. Distant thunder rumbles, a low, ominous growl.

Cara's sleek, unmarked government vehicle glides silently through the rain-slicked streets. The wipers work furiously, barely keeping pace with the deluge. Her hands grip the steering wheel, knuckles white. Her face, illuminated by the dashboard lights, is a study in grim determination.

She navigates through a forgotten industrial district, past abandoned factories and crumbling brick buildings. The air grows heavy with the scent of damp concrete and decay. This is not a place for casual visitors.

Finally, she pulls up to a massive, derelict warehouse. It looms out of the darkness like a skeletal beast, its corrugated metal siding rusted and peeling, its windows boarded up with rotting plywood. A faded, almost illegible sign, hanging precariously from a single bolt, creaks rhythmically in the wind, a mournful lament.

The building is a monument to neglect, a silent testament to forgotten industry. But tonight, it holds a different kind of promise – or threat. The single, flickering streetlamp across the road casts long, dancing shadows, making the warehouse seem to breathe, to watch.

Cara cuts the engine. The sudden silence in the car is deafening, broken only by the drumming of rain on the roof. She takes a deep breath, the cold, damp air filling her lungs. Every instinct, every fiber of her being, screams at her to turn back. This is a trap. This is madness.

But the image of the grainy video, the chilling words of the shadowy figures, flashes in her mind. The truth. She has to know. She has to see.

She reaches for the door handle, her hand hesitating for a fraction of a second. Then, with a resolute push, she opens the door, stepping out into the unforgiving rain. The cold water immediately soaks through her clothes, but she barely notices. Her eyes are fixed on the dark, imposing entrance of the warehouse. The game has indeed changed. And she's walking right into the heart of it.
[FADE OUT.]
