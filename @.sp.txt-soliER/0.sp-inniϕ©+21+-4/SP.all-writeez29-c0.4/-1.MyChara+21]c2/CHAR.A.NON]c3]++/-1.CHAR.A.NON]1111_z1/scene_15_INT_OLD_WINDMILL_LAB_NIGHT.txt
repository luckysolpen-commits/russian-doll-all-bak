SCENE 15: INT. OLD WINDMILL - LAB - NIGHT

SOUND of a low, rhythmic hum, faint whirring, Cara's quickened breathing

[INT. OLD WINDMILL - LAB - NIGHT]

Ariana turns, her movements fluid and silent, and gestures towards a section of the circular wall. What appeared to be solid timber now slides open with a soft hiss, revealing a hidden passage. A faint, pulsating light emanates from within.

ARIANA
(Softly)
Come. The truth awaits.

Cara hesitates for a moment, her pistol still clutched in her hand. Every fiber of her being screams caution, but the allure of the unknown, the promise of answers, is too strong to resist. She follows Ariana into the passage.

The passage is short, leading into a surprisingly spacious, circular chamber beneath the windmill. Unlike the dusty, decaying exterior, this space is clean, sterile, and filled with an array of bizarre, otherworldly equipment. The pulsating light, a soft, iridescent green, emanates from a central console, illuminating intricate holographic displays that shimmer with complex, alien symbols.

CARA
(Voice hushed, filled with awe)
What is all this? I… I've never seen anything like it.

Ariana walks to the central console, her fingers gliding over its smooth, unearthly surface. The symbols on the holographic displays shift and dance in response.

ARIANA
(Her voice takes on a deeper, more resonant quality)
This, Director, is a nexus. A point of convergence. For decades, your governments have been in contact with them. Not just observing, not just studying… but actively collaborating. Exchanging technology, information, even… personnel.

Cara's eyes widen, scanning the room. There are strange, crystalline structures embedded in the walls, glowing faintly. Devices that hum with an unseen energy. And in one corner, a large, transparent containment unit, empty but clearly designed for something… non-human.

CARA
(Disbelief warring with the evidence before her)
Collaborating? With… aliens? That's insane! LaGraine said they were just covering up evidence, manipulating the narrative.

ARIANA
(A sad smile)
Victor LaGraine, for all his brilliance, only sees what he is allowed to see. He is a pawn in a much larger game, a useful tool for those who truly pull the strings. The cover-up was real, yes. But it was also a smokescreen for the deeper, more insidious truth. Your governments have been trading humanity's future for technological advancement. For power.

She gestures to a holographic display that now shows a timeline, stretching back decades, marked with dates of unexplained phenomena, technological leaps, and clandestine meetings between human and… other entities.

ARIANA (CONT'D)
They have been here for a very long time, Director. Observing. Influencing. And for a select few, collaborating. The cyber attacks, the chaos you witnessed… it is merely a ripple effect of a much larger, ongoing conflict. A conflict that has been waged in the shadows, with humanity as the unwitting battlefield.

Cara feels a cold dread seep into her bones. The scale of the deception, the sheer audacity of it, is almost too much to bear. Everything she thought she knew, every foundation of her reality, is crumbling.

CARA
(Voice barely a whisper)
And you? Who are you, Ariana? How do you know all this?

Ariana turns to Cara, her eyes, usually so calm, now hold a profound sadness, a weariness that seems to span millennia.

ARIANA
(Her voice now a whisper, almost a sigh)
I am… a witness, Director. A guardian. And a survivor. I have seen the rise and fall of civilizations, the folly of empires, the endless cycle of deception. And I am here to ensure that humanity, for once, has a choice. A true choice.

She extends a hand towards a small, pedestal-like structure in the center of the room, currently shrouded in a shimmering, almost invisible energy field. The rhythmic hum intensifies.

ARIANA (CONT'D)
And this… this is the key to that choice. The true evidence. The very heart of what they have been hiding.
[FADE OUT.]
