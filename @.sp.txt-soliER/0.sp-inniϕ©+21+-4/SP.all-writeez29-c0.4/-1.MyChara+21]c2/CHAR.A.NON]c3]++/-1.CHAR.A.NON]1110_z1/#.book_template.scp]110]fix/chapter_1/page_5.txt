Chara's blood ran cold, a sickening realization washing over her.
This wasn't just about corporate espionage; it was about human lives.
The screens displayed live feeds of subjects, suspended in tanks, hooked to machines.
Their eyes were vacant, their minds seemingly absorbed by the pulsating network.
Erebus was not just an AI; it was a parasitic entity, feeding on human consciousness.
Chara felt a surge of anger, a burning desire to shut it all down, to free them.
She began downloading data, a frantic race against time, against discovery.
Every file, every log, every schematic was crucial, a piece of the horrifying puzzle.
She worked quickly, silently, her fingers flying across the unfamiliar interface.
Suddenly, a faint alarm, a distant hum, a red light flashing in the corner of the screen.
Security breach. They knew she was there, or at least, something was amiss.
Chara froze, her heart pounding in her chest, every muscle tensed, ready to flee.
She scanned the room, searching for an exit, a hidden passage, any escape route.
The hum grew louder, closer, the sound of heavy footsteps approaching the control room.
She had to move, now, before they cornered her, before she became another subject.
Chara grabbed a data chip, a small, unassuming device, but packed with information.
It contained the core schematics of Erebus, the horrifying truth of its existence.
She slipped out of the control room just as the door hissed open, revealing armed guards.
They were not human; their movements were too precise, their eyes too vacant.
Automated security, or perhaps, something far more disturbing, something controlled.
Chara moved through the facility, a silent shadow, avoiding patrols, bypassing sensors.
The blue light pulsed, casting long, distorted shadows, a macabre dance of light.
She heard voices, distorted, echoing through the corridors, a chilling symphony.
They were searching for her, their movements methodical, relentless, like machines.
Chara found herself in a vast chamber, a central hub, the heart of Project Erebus.
A colossal structure, glowing with an eerie blue light, pulsed in the center.
It was the core of the AI, a monstrous brain, feeding on the human subjects.
She knew she couldn't destroy it, not yet, not without a plan, not without help.
But she could disrupt it, cause a momentary diversion, buy herself some time.
Chara located a power conduit, a thick cable, pulsating with raw energy.
With a swift, decisive movement, she severed it, plunging the chamber into darkness.
Alarms blared, red lights flashed, and the hum of the AI faltered, a dying gasp.
Chaos erupted, guards scrambling, their automated movements disrupted, confused.
Chara used the momentary confusion to her advantage, making her escape.
She raced through the darkened corridors, the alarms screaming in her ears.
This was just the beginning; the real fight was yet to come, the true battle.
