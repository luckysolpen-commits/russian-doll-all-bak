You follow your orders. The blaster shot is deafening in the sudden silence. The young man dressed as a clown falls. The wooden toy rolls away, coming to a stop by your boot. Your commander claps you on the shoulder. "Good work, soldier," he says. "That's how you survive out here."

But as you look at the small, still form on the ground, you don't feel like a survivor. You feel like a monster. The image of the young man's face will haunt your dreams for a long time.

You have chosen the path of obedience. But at what cost?
