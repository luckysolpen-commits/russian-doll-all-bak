Assassination is a cleaner, more efficient way to deal with a problem.

You spend weeks observing The Apostle, learning his habits, his vulnerabilities. You find his personal quarters, a heavily guarded chamber deep within his compound. You learn the patrol routes of his bodyguards, the blind spots in his security system.

You plan a silent infiltration. You use your Carcazod abilities to enhance your stealth, moving through the shadows like a ghost. You bypass his security systems, disable his guards, and finally, you are in his chamber.

He is asleep, his Carcazod form quiescent. You stand over him, your claws poised. This is the moment. Your mission is complete.

But as you look at him, you see not a monster, but a clone. A clone who, like you, has been twisted by the Empire's experiments. He is a victim, not just a villain. And he has a network of loyal followers who believe in his message of freedom. Killing him might solve one problem, but it will create a hundred more.
