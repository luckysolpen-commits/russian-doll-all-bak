You wait. You gather more data. You learn the names of corrupt officials, the locations of secret prisons, the shipping routes for weapons-grade materials. You become a walking encyclopedia of the Empire's sins.

Your patience pays off. One day, you intercept a coded message. It's from a high-ranking official in the Solar Empire to a known associate of the Blood Queen Monarchy. It's a kill order. And the target is The Proctor, your old commander on the moon. It seems your former mentor has become too powerful, too ambitious, and a political liability.

This is the opportunity you've been waiting for. You have information that could throw the Imperial high command into chaos. You could leak the information to the clowns, letting them take advantage of the infighting. You could warn The Proctor, earning the loyalty of a powerful and dangerous ally. Or you could do nothing, letting the assassination happen and watching the fallout from the sidelines.
