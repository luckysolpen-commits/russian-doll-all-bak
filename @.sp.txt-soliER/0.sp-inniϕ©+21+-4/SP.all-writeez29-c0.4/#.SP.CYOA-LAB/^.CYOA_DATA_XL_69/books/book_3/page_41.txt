You are the scalpel, not the sledgehammer.

The hunger is a physical force, threatening to overwhelm you. But your training, your years of discipline, hold it at bay. You are in control.

You ignore the two screaming officials and focus on your target: Cassian. He is fumbling for a panic button under his desk. You move with unnatural speed, a black blur of motion. The other two officials are frozen in terror, unable to process what they are seeing. You could kill them. The beast inside you screams for it. But your orders were to eliminate the target, and to make it look like a clown attack. Clowns are messy, but they are also opportunists. They might not have killed everyone. Leaving witnesses, terrified and unreliable witnesses, could actually enhance the deception.

You let out a terrifying, inhuman roar. You smash the desk with one powerful claw. You look at the two officials, and you give them a look that promises death if they move. Then you turn and crash through the window, disappearing into the Martian night.

You have followed your orders, and you have maintained control. You have proven that you can wield the beast. The Proctor will be pleased.
