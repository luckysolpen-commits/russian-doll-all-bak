The clown is enemy combatant. His life is secondary to the intelligence he might be carrying. You leave him bleeding on the ground and climb into the wreckage of his ship.

The inside is a mess of sparking wires and damaged consoles. An emergency light strobes, illuminating the cramped space. You find the pilot's station and check the nav-computer. It's fried, completely useless.

Frustrated, you turn your attention back to the clown himself. He's still conscious, barely. You frisk him, your movements efficient and impersonal. Tucked into an inner pocket of his suit, you find it: a small, hardened data chip.

As you pull it free, the clown grabs your wrist with surprising strength. "Monster," he chokes out, his eyes filled with a desperate, burning hatred. Then the light fades from them, and his hand goes limp.

You have the data chip. But your ship is wrecked, and you are alone. The chip is a mystery. It could contain vital intelligence, or it could be a trap. Your only hope is to get back to a friendly base and have it analyzed. For now, you need to survive.

You begin the long, arduous process of salvaging parts from the two wrecks to try and get your own comms back online. It will take hours, and you are exposed.
