"I accept," you say, without hesitation. This is your only path back.

You are sent back to Mars, to a remote observation post near the polar ice caps. The landscape is a desolate expanse of red ice and frozen carbon dioxide. Your mission is to locate the pirate broadcasting station. For a week, you do nothing but listen. You scan the radio spectrum, searching for the faint, encrypted signals of the clown broadcast.

Finally, you find it. It's hidden in a network of ice caves, shielded from orbital detection. The entrance is guarded by two of the new "adaptive" drones. They are sleek, insectoid machines that patrol in a random, unpredictable pattern.

Your only way in is to get past them. Your equipment includes a cyberfare kit that might be able to temporarily disable one of the drones, but it's risky. A direct assault is out of the question. This is a mission of stealth, not strength.

You watch the drones for hours, studying their patterns, looking for a window. You find one. A 17-second gap when their patrol routes diverge. It's barely enough time to slip through.
