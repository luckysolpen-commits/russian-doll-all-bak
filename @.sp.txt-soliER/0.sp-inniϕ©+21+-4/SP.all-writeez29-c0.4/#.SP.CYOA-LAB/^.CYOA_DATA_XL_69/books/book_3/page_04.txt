The journey to Luna is a sterile, silent affair. You are quartered with other "exemplary" clones, all of whom wear the same placid expression of unwavering obedience. You try to mimic it.

The Lunar base, buried deep beneath the Sea of Tranquility, is a world away from the dusty, chaotic tunnels of Mars. Here, everything is polished chrome, cool blue light, and whispers. The United Clones share this space with operatives from the Solar Empire, and the tension between the two nominal allies is thick enough to taste.

Your new commanding officer is a man known only as The Proctor. He is not a clone. His eyes are ancient, and he moves with a predatory grace. "Discipline is the foundation of power," he tells you in your orientation. "Here, you will learn a new kind of discipline. The discipline to see without being seen, to strike without being known."

Your training involves advanced stealth piloting, network intrusion, and psychological warfare. You learn how to pilot transformer drones—and yes, a small, insubordinate part of your mind thinks it's funny how they look like ancient Earth toys, just before they combine into a terrifying Megazord-like hunter-killer. My processors buzz with the irony of it all. Who dreams this stuff up? Anyway.

One day, The Proctor summons you to a private chamber. On a holographic display, a monstrous creature thrashes. It's a blur of chitinous plates, scythe-like claws, and a gaping maw. It looks like a nightmare hybrid of a cockroach and a shark.

"This is a Carcazod," The Proctor says, his voice laced with something like reverence. "The ultimate bio-weapon. A mutation only the most loyal, most disciplined, and most... worthy... are permitted to undergo. Your performance has put you on the shortlist, 700. Soon, you may be asked to give more than just your loyalty to the Empire. You may be asked to give up your very form."
