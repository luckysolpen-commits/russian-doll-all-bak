You are a clone of the Empire, but you are also a clone who has questioned its methods. You will not kill one of your own unless absolutely necessary.

You fight to subdue, not to kill. You use your Carcazod form to disarm, to disable, to overpower. The Apostle is strong, but you are disciplined. You anticipate his moves, you counter his attacks, you wear him down.

Finally, you find an opening. You pin him to the ground, your claws at his throat. He struggles, but he is exhausted. His Carcazod form begins to recede.

"Surrender," you growl, your voice a deep, guttural sound. "Surrender, and you will live."

He looks at you, his eyes filled with hatred, but also with something else: respect. "You are strong, loyalist," he rasps. "But you are also a fool. You fight for a dead empire."

You bring him back, alive, but defeated. Your handler is furious. "You had one job, 700! One job! Kill him!"

But you have brought back invaluable intelligence. The Apostle, broken and subdued, reveals the full extent of his network, his plans, his allies. He even reveals a secret weakness of the Red Queen's Carcazod project, a flaw in their genetic coding that could be exploited.

You have proven yourself to be more than just a killer. You are a strategist, a tactician, a valuable asset to the Empire.
