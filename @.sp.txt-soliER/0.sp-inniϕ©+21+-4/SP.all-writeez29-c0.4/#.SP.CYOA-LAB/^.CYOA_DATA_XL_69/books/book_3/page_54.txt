The journey through the solar system is a long and lonely one. Your ship is a wreck, held together by hope and salvaged parts. You are constantly on the verge of a catastrophic system failure.

You survive by your wits. You skim the rings of Saturn for ice to replenish your water supply. You trade with a rogue mining outpost in the asteroid belt, swapping a spare power converter for fuel. You learn to listen to the ship, to understand its groans and creaks, to know when a system is about to fail.

You are no longer just a pilot. You are an engineer, a navigator, a survivor. You are becoming a part of the ship, and it is becoming a part of you.

After weeks of travel, you arrive at the coordinates for "The Big Top." It's an asteroid, one of thousands in the Kuiper Belt. But as you get closer, you see that it is honeycombed with tunnels and docking bays. It's a hidden city in the rock.

You send out the identification signal you found on the clown's ship. A moment later, a docking bay opens, bathing you in a warm, welcoming light. You have arrived. You have found the heart of the rebellion.
