Patrols mean checkpoints and questions you can't answer. Your only hope is to go where the Empire isn't. You turn your back on the familiar and march into the deep, dark, unknown.

The tunnels here are older, rougher. The air is stale. Your suit's life support is your only friend. For two days you walk, guided by nothing but a sense of downward descent. You eat your single nutrition bar on the first day. Hunger begins to gnaw at you.

On the third day, you see it. A light. Not the clean, sterile light of an Imperial outpost, but a flickering, orange glow. As you get closer, you hear music, a distorted, pulsing beat that echoes through the rock. You round a corner and find the source: a makeshift settlement, cobbled together from scrap metal and stolen tech, built into a vast cavern.

Graffiti covers the walls in vibrant, clashing colors. Clones in customized, patched-up suits mingle, laughing and drinking. This is a clown town. A haven for deserters, rebels, and runaways. But a haven for you? Or a trap? You are still wearing the remnants of your Imperial uniform.
