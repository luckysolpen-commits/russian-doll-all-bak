You throttle back the Digger's engines, its metallic claws settling onto the tunnel floor. Every instinct screams at you to rush in, but your training screams louder. An order is an order. You switch to a secure channel and report your position, relaying the garbled distress call to your commander.

"Understood, 700," the voice on the other end is cold and clipped. "Hold position. A strike team is being diverted. Your job is to maintain a perimeter and report any enemy movement. Do not engage. Do not play the hero."

The minutes stretch into an eternity. The distress signal from the Redplanet Labs outpost dies. Silence. Half an hour later, three heavily armed military craft scream past your position and vanish down the tunnel. An hour after that, they return, one of them showing signs of battle damage.

The official report comes down later. The outpost was a total loss. All personnel and research... gone. The clowns were long gone by the time the strike team arrived. You are commended for your discipline and adherence to protocol. You did exactly what you were supposed to do, and for that, you are rewarded with a promotion and a transfer to a more prestigious unit on the Lunar base, a place of advanced training and even greater responsibility. But as your transport leaves the red dust of Mars behind, you can't shake the feeling that your perfect record is stained with the ghosts of the scientists you didn't save.

You have proven your loyalty. Now it's time to sharpen it into a weapon.
