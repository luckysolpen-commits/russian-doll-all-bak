"A rogue Carcazod," you say, your mind racing. "How is that possible? I was told the transformation was irreversible, and that the subjects were completely loyal."

Your handler smiles, a cold, knowing expression. "You were told what you needed to know," she says. "The Carcazod project is not as... stable... as we would like. There have been... failures. Subjects who have broken their conditioning, who have learned to control the transformation in ways we did not anticipate. The Apostle is one of those failures."

She leans forward. "He is not just a rebel leader. He is a threat to the very secrecy of the Carcazod project. He is a loose end that must be tied up. And you, 700, are the perfect tool for the job. You are either a loyal clone with a conscience, or you are a monster who can be controlled. Either way, you are uniquely qualified to hunt one of your own."

Her words are a chilling revelation. The Carcazod project is not the perfect weapon you were led to believe. It is a dangerous, unstable experiment. And you are a part of it.
