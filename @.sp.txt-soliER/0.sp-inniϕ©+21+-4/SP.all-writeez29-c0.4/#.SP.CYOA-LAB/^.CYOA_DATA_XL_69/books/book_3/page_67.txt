"I'm a soldier," you say, pushing the data chip back towards them. "A pilot. I can fight. I can kill. I can fly anything you put me in. But I'm not a slicer. If you want to test my loyalty, give me a target."

Ranch, the old smuggler, lets out a harsh laugh. "A killer, are you? We've got plenty of killers. What we need are brains. But if you're determined to get your hands dirty, so be it."

Zi looks at you, a thoughtful expression on her face. "Very well," she says. "We have another job. A much messier one. An Imperial informant has infiltrated one of our cells. We need him eliminated. He's a clone, like you. His name is 812, and he works as a cargo loader at the Olympus Mons spaceport."

She sends the information to your personal datapad. A picture of Clone 812, his work schedule, his home address. "Make it clean," she says. "Make it look like an accident. And bring us his datapad as proof."

This is a test of a different kind. Not of your skills, but of your ruthlessness. Can you kill one of your own brothers, in cold blood, for the cause?
