Orders are just suggestions when lives are on the line.

You slam the throttle forward, pushing the Digger's engines to their screaming limit. The patrol craft lurches and rockets down the dark, narrow tunnel, the rock walls a blur just meters from your cockpit view. You're running dark, all non-essential systems powered down to minimize your signature.

As you approach the coordinates, you see the carnage. The entrance to the Redplanet Labs outpost is a mangled wreck of durasteel and rock. Smoke billows out. Two battered clown-modified mining vehicles are parked nearby, their crews hastily loading stolen cargo.

They spot you a second after you spot them. A warning shot from a clown's plasma cutter skips off your cockpit, leaving a nasty scorch mark. This is it. Your first real taste of combat, and you're alone.

Your console targets the two enemy vehicles. Your weapon systems are hot. You could try to disable their engines, or you could go for a kill shot on their weapon mounts. Or perhaps... there's another way. A hail and a bluff?
