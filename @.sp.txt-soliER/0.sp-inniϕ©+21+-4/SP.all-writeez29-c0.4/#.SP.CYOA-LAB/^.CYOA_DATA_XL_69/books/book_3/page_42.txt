You can't do it. You are a soldier, not a monster. Not yet.

You key your comm. "Handler, this is 700. I have a problem. My bio-monitors are showing a critical error in the transformation sequence. I cannot guarantee control. I am aborting the mission."

The silence on the other end is heavy and cold. You know what this means. You have failed. You have proven yourself to be a flawed weapon.

"Understood, 700," the handler's voice finally comes, stripped of all warmth. "Return to the rendezvous point. Immediately."

This is a death sentence. Returning means "decommissioning." Recycling. You are a billion-credit piece of hardware that has just proven defective. They will not let you walk away.

Your only chance is to run. You are on Mars, a planet riddled with tunnels, rogue settlements, and secrets. It's a better place to disappear than the sterile, controlled environment of the moon. You have your training, your wits, and a deep-seated fear of what you almost became.
