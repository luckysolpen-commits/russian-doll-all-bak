"I don't know what you're talking about," you say, your voice a flat, emotionless monotone. "I was on patrol. I was ambushed. I survived. That is all."

The intelligence officer sighs, a theatrical, disappointed sound. "Very well, 700. If that is your story, we will proceed accordingly."

He leaves you in the cell. Hours later, you are taken to a military tribunal. You are accused of desertion, of destroying Imperial property, and of collaborating with the enemy. Your protestations of innocence are ignored. Your story is dismissed as a fabrication.

You are found guilty. The sentence is death. But not a quick, clean death. You are to be made an example of. You are to be "honorably" sacrificed to the S.S. SOL, your soul used to fuel the great ship.

As you are marched to the transport that will take you to your final destination, you realize the terrible irony. Your loyalty, your adherence to the rules, your refusal to deviate from your programming - it has all led to this. You were a perfect clone, and you will die for it.

THE END
