"I understand," you say. "I will not fail."

You are sent to the Mutangia region, a hot, dusty area of Mars known for its fierce, independent clans. You are given a new identity: a down-on-his-luck mercenary looking for work.

You quickly find your way into the organization of the charismatic leader, a man known only as "The Apostle." He is a powerful, mesmerizing figure, and his followers are devoted to him with a religious fervor. He preaches a gospel of clone liberation, of a new era of freedom from the tyranny of the Empire.

You also confirm the intelligence reports. The Apostle is a Carcazod. You see it in the way he moves, in the predatory gleam in his eyes. He is a monster, but he is a monster in complete control of his transformation. He is a new breed, a more powerful and dangerous foe than you have ever faced.

You have two objectives: determine the extent of his power, and eliminate him. You have managed the first. Now for the second. You could try to challenge him to a duel, a tradition among the clans. Or you could try a more subtle approach, assassination.
