You don't run. You are a clone, and this is a puzzle. A problem to be solved. You ignore the creeping tendrils of fungus and focus on the scientist's twitching fingers.

The tapping is rhythmic, patterned. It's a code. You record it with your suit's built-in recorder, trying to decipher it. It's not standard military code. It's older, simpler. A binary code. On and off. Long and short.

You translate. The message is simple, repeated over and over again. "Listen. Not a weapon. A voice. Listen."

A voice? The fungus? Before you can process this, a tendril of the glowing fungus snakes up your boot. You feel a jolt, not of electricity, but of... information. Your mind is flooded with images, feelings, data. You see the lab from the fungus's perspective. You feel its fear, its confusion, its desperate attempts to communicate with the scientists who were torturing it. It wasn't hostile. It was defending itself.

The voice in your head is not your own. It is ancient, alien, and terrified. "They will kill me," it whispers. "They will burn this place. Please. Take me with you."

The fungus is offering you a choice. It can recede, allowing you to leave. Or it can bond with you, becoming a part of you. You would be its host, its protector. You would have access to its vast consciousness, its ability to process information. You would become a hybrid, something new.
