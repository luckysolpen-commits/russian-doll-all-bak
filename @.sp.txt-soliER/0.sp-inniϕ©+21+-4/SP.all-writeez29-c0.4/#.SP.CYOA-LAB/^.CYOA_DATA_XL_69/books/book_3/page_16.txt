You holster your sidearm. An injured enemy is still a person. You grab the medkit from your Digger's emergency locker and climb out of the wreckage. "I'm coming to help," you call out, your hands raised to show you mean no harm.

You approach the figure who stumbled from the other ship. It's a clone, his face a mirror of your own, but etched with fear and pain. He's clutching a deep gash in his side.

"Stay back," he warns, but there's no strength in his voice.

"I'm not going to hurt you," you say, kneeling beside him. "Let me see that wound."

You apply a bio-foam patch to his side. He winces, but lets you work. "Why?" he asks, his breathing ragged. "You're one of them. A loyalist."

"I'm a pilot, not a monster," you reply, echoing a thought you've had before.

"There's not much difference, soon," he says, his eyes wild with a feverish intensity. "The Empire... the Carcazods... they're not just a weapon. It's a plague. A way to control everyone. We have to get the word out." He grips your arm. "My ship... the nav-computer. It has a destination. A safe haven. A place called... the Underground Xircus. You have to go. You have to tell them what you saw here today."

He coughs, a wracking, painful sound. "Don't... don't let them turn you into a monster." His eyes flutter and close. His grip on your arm goes limp. He's dead.

You are alone, with a wrecked ship, two dead clowns, and the last words of a dying man ringing in your ears. The nav-computer on his ship might still be functional.
