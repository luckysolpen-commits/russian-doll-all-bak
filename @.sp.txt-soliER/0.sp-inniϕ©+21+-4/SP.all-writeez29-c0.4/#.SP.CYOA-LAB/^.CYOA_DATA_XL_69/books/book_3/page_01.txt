You are Clone 700, a top-percentile graduate of the United Clones training barracks near the Olympus Mons caldera. Your reflexes are sharp, your loyalty is supposed to be absolute, and your piloting skills are second to none. Below the red dust of the Martian surface, the real world hums and thrives in a labyrinth of tunnels and subterranean cities.

You're currently in the cockpit of your "Digger" class patrol craft, running a routine sweep through a sector of the Valles Marineris tunnel system. The comms crackle with the usual chatter from SOLARCOM, INC. traffic controllers. It's boring, and that's good. Boring means no clowns.

Suddenly, a priority alert flashes on your console. A distress signal. It's from a Redplanet Labs (RPL) science outpost, deep in an unmapped spur of the tunnels. The signal is garbled, but you make out key phrases: "...attack... clowns... specimens breached..."

Your standing orders are clear: await backup from the main fleet. But the distress signal is weakening, and the location is only a ten-minute high-burn away. Dozens of lives, and valuable RPL assets, are on the line. Your commander's voice isn't on the comms, but you can hear it in your head: "Stick to the plan, 700. No heroics."

But the static-laced screams from the outpost play in your ears.

What do you do?
