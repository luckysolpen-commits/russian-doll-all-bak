Analysis and tactics are for cowards. Sometimes, you just have to hit things. Hard.

You aim your Digger's reinforced prow at the nearer of the two clown ships and firewall the throttle. The ship is a repurposed asteroid miner, bulky and slow to react. The clown pilot's eyes go wide in his cockpit display a moment before you hit.

The impact is catastrophic. Metal shrieks and tears as your patrol craft, designed for boring through rock, plows into the side of the mining vessel. The clown ship is thrown sideways into the tunnel wall, crumpling like a cheap can. Alarms blare on your own console. Your port engine is offline and the entire craft is vibrating violently.

The second clown ship, stunned by your suicidal maneuver, hesitates for a fatal second. It's all the time you need. Your remaining weapon systems get a lock and you fire, a savage, point-blank barrage that rips through their cockpit. The ship explodes in a brilliant, silent fireball.

You've won. But you're stranded. Your craft is a wreck, deep in an unmapped tunnel, with a very angry—and very dead—clown crew as company. Your comms are fried from the impact. You're completely and utterly alone. Or so you think. A flicker of movement from the wreckage of the first ship catches your eye. A hatch is creaking open.
