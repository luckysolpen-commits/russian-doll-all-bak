Page 29: The Carcazod Hunting Mission

Colonel Argent briefs you on the most dangerous assignment: "Intelligence suggests that failed carcazod experiments have created unstable beings that threaten the stability of our entire social structure. These entities exist outside all faction boundaries and are reportedly capable of transforming other beings against their will. Your mission is to eliminate these threats, but survival is not guaranteed."

You're equipped with specialized anti-carcazod weapons and deployed to the Fractured Zones – tunnel sections where the boundaries between different faction territories have broken down, creating neutral spaces where transformed beings can hide. The area is marked by unusual energy readings and the kind of organic-technological hybrid architecture associated with carcazod presence.

Your sensors detect three distinct areas of activity: a section where the tunnel walls have begun to exhibit organic properties, suggesting carcazod integration with the environment; a chamber where energy patterns indicate active transformation processes; and a hideout where the creatures appear to be coordinating their activities.

As you proceed deeper into the Fractured Zones, you realize that your targets may not be mere monsters – their behavioral patterns suggest a form of society, possibly even culture. Some of their modifications appear to enhance rather than merely transform their original capabilities.

Communication intercepts reveal that these beings might be the result of secret joint experiments between all four factions – an attempt to create the ultimate soldiers that went beyond all intended parameters.
