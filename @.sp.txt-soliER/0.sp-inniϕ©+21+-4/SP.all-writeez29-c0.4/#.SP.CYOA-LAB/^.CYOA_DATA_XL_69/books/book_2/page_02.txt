Page 2: The Pilot's Duty

You step out of the transport pod and head toward the Drone Command center, where pilots are gathering around holographic displays of the Martian tunnel network. The facility hums with activity as operators prepare for their assigned missions. A tall figure in Solar Empire uniform approaches you – Captain Vex, one of the most experienced tunnel pilots on Mars.

"Unit CL7X-914," she says, consulting her tablet. "Your skills in drone operation are noted in our records. Today you have a choice of missions: a reconnaissance flight through the unmapped Northern Tunnels, an escort mission for a high-value transport bound for the Eastern Hubs, or a deep-penetration surveillance mission into the contested Central Zone. Each path offers different opportunities for advancement within the Empire."

You study the three mission briefings displayed on the hologram. The Northern Tunnels are largely unexplored and potentially dangerous but offer the chance for significant discovery. The Eastern Escort mission is more routine but involves protecting valuable resources and personnel. The Central Zone mission is highly classified and dangerous, but would bring you into contact with the underground networks of clowns who hunt other clowns.

Captain Vex watches you carefully. "Choose wisely, pilot. Your selection will determine not only your immediate future but your standing within our hierarchy. Each mission leads to different outcomes and opportunities for advancement."

Through the command center windows, you can see the sleek drone craft waiting in the hanger bay. Each one specialized for different types of operations in the Martian depths. A sense of destiny presses upon you as you consider your options.
