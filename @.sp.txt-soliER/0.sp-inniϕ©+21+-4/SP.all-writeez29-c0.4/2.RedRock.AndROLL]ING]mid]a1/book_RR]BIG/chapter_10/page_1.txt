The *Starlight Serenade* was a blur of motion, a silver dart
-against the inky blackness of space. Inside the cockpit, the
air was thick with the scent of burnt ozone and the adrenaline
of a desperate gamble. Astra, her face a mask of intense
-concentration, wrestled with the controls, coaxing every last
-ounce of speed from the sleek vessel. The distant battle
-around Mars was a fading, fiery nebula in their rearview, a
-testament to the sacrifices made to get them this far.

“Approaching UN envoy ship, *Diplomat One*,” Gem’s voice,
-calm and precise, cut through the hum of the engines. Her
-fingers danced across the navigation console, a flurry of
-calculated movements. “They’re hailing us. Standard
-identification protocols.”

Lucky, still clutching the heavily shielded container, felt a
-knot tighten in his stomach. This was it. The moment of
-truth. The diplomatic standoff. He looked at the container,
the weight of its contents a tangible presence in his hands.
The fate of two worlds, perhaps even three, rested on this
-fragile package.

“Patch them through, Gem,” Lucky said, his voice steady
-despite the tremor in his hands. He took a deep breath,
-preparing for the inevitable interrogation.
