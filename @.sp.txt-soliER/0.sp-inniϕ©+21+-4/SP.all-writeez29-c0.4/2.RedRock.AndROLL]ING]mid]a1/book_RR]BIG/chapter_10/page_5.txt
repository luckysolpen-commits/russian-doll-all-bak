Rostova hesitated, her gaze flicking between the incoming
-threat on her tactical display and the desperate faces of the
-trio. The weight of her decision was palpable. Trust a known
-mercenary, a renegade clone, and a former corporate shark?
Or risk the destruction of her ship and the potential
-annihilation of Earth?

She was a soldier, trained to follow orders, to trust her
-intelligence. And her intelligence was telling her that these
-people were the enemy. But her gut, her intuition, was
-screaming at her that they were telling the truth.

She looked at Lucky’s eyes, and she saw not a mercenary, but
a man who was fighting for something more than just money. She
-saw a man who was willing to die for his cause.

And she made her choice.

“Open the bay doors!” she roared, her voice filled with a
-newfound authority. “And prepare for immediate docking. All
-security to the hangar bay! And someone get me a direct line
to the UN Council. Now!”
