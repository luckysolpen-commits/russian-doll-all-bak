Astra, meanwhile, found herself in an unfamiliar role:
-trainer. The Queen, in an act of surprising trust, had
-assigned her a small squad of loyalist Martian mercenaries.
They were rough around the edges, fiercely loyal to their
Queen, but lacking the tactical finesse Astra had honed over
-years of combat. She pushed them hard, drilling them in
-close-quarters combat, stealth infiltration, and urban
-warfare. She taught them how to move like shadows, how to
-strike with precision, and how to survive when the odds were
-stacked against them. It was frustrating at times, but she
-saw the potential in their raw determination. They would be
her scalpel, ready to strike when the time was right.

She found a strange satisfaction in the role. She was not
just a warrior; she was a teacher, a mentor. She was passing
on her knowledge, her experience, to a new generation of
-fighters. It was a legacy she had never considered, but one
that she found surprisingly fulfilling.

She saw a reflection of her younger self in their eyes. The
-same fire, the same hunger, the same desperate desire to
-prove themselves. She pushed them hard, but she also cared
for them. They were her new crew, her new family. And she
-would not let them down.
