The Architect. The name echoed in the silence of the room,
a chilling premonition of a new, terrifying reality. The
-Carcazod was not just a monster; it was a message. A
-declaration of war from an enemy they did not yet understand.

Lucky stared at the single word on his comm unit, his mind
-racing. Who was the Architect? What did it want? And how
-could they possibly fight an enemy that could manipulate
-life itself?

He felt a familiar fear, the fear of the unknown, of a
-power far greater than his own. But he also felt a familiar
-thrill. The thrill of the game, of the challenge. He was a
-gambler, and the stakes had just been raised to an
-unimaginable level.

He was no longer just fighting for his own survival, for the
-survival of his crew, for the survival of two worlds. He was
-fighting for the survival of life itself.

And he was not alone.
