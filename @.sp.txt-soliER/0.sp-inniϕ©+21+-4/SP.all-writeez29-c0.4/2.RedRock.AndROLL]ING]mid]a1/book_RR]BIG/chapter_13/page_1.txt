The UN Council Chambers, once a stage for petty squabbles
and political posturing, was now a scene of stark, terrifying
-unity. The news of the evolving Carcazod threat, confirmed
by the frantic reports from Atlantis Prime, had shattered the
-fragile peace and exposed the true nature of Zara’s mutagen.
The holographic display, once showing Zara’s fabricated
-evidence, now projected the monstrous image of the Carcazod,
its eyes burning with an unnatural intelligence.

Dr. Thorne, his voice hoarse from exhaustion, stood before
the council, the unweaponized mutagen sample held aloft.
“The bio-marker,” he explained, his finger tracing a complex
-pattern on the vial. “It’s a Carcazod signature. Not just
any Carcazod. An evolved strain. Zara’s mutagen, in its
-attempt to control populations, inadvertently accelerated
the Carcazods’ own evolution. She didn’t create a weapon for
-herself; she created a catalyst for something far older, far
-more dangerous.”
