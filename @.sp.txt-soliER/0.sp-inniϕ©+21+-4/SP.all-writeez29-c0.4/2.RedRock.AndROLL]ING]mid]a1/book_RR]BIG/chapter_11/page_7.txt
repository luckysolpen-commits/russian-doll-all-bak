The room was a whirlwind of activity. UN scientists, their
-faces grim, hunched over monitors, analyzing the data from
the deep-space sensors. The Carcazod was unlike anything
they had ever seen. It was larger, faster, more intelligent.
And it was heading directly for them.

The council members, their political differences forgotten,
-began to work together, to formulate a plan. They were a
-disparate group, a collection of rivals and enemies. But
-now, they were united by a common goal: survival.

The Red Queen, her eyes filled with a new, fierce
-determination, offered her knowledge of Martian technology,
of ancient texts that spoke of a force that sought to
-‘re-sculpt’ life.

Zara, her corporate empire crumbling around her, offered
her knowledge of the mutagen, of the bio-marker that was the
-key to understanding the Carcazods.

And Lucky, Gem, and Astra, the unlikely heroes, found
-themselves at the center of it all. They were the ones who
had brought the truth to light. They were the ones who had
-exposed the true nature of the threat.

They were no longer outcasts, no longer criminals. They were
-the last, best hope for a universe on the brink of
-destruction.
