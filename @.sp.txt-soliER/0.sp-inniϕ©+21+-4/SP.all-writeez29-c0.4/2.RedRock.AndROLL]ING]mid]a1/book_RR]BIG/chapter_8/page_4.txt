“Then what?” the Queen asked, her voice laced with despair.
“Is there no hope?”

Lucky’s eyes narrowed. “There’s one way. Zara’s sending a
-single, unmarked cargo vessel to the UN envoy ship. It’s
-carrying the mutagen. If we can intercept it, if we can
-deliver the *real* mutagen – the unweaponized sample we
-extracted from her lab – and the peace agreement you’ve
-drafted, we might be able to expose her. We can show the UN
the truth.”

“It’s a race against time,” Gem said, her voice grim. “That
-cargo vessel is already underway. We’d have to move fast.
And we’d need a ship that can outrun Zara’s interceptors.”

The Queen’s eyes lit up. “The *Starlight Serenade*. My
-personal transport. It’s the fastest ship in my fleet. It’s
-lightly armed, but it’s built for speed and stealth.”

“Perfect,” Lucky said, a plan rapidly forming in his mind.
“Astra, you’ll pilot. Gem, you’re on comms and navigation.
I’ll handle the package. Ratchet, you’ll need to create a
-diversion, something big enough to draw Zara’s attention
away from the *Serenade*.”

Ratchet, who had been silently observing, nodded. “Consider
it done. I know a few ‘Vampires’ who are itching for a fight.
And a few strategically placed explosives can always liven
things up.”
