Zara’s flagship, the *Iron Will*, hung like a predatory shark
in orbit above Mars, its sleek, black hull absorbing the
-distant sunlight. It was a vessel of immense power, a symbol
of Zara’s corporate might. Inside, the command bridge was a
-symphony of hushed efficiency and cold, calculated ambition.
Zara, resplendent in a tailored uniform that exuded power,
-addressed her inner circle of commanders. Holographic displays
-flickered around them, showcasing troop movements, supply
-routes, and the chillingly precise trajectory of a single,
-unmarked cargo vessel headed for Earth.

“The final phase is upon us,” Zara’s voice, amplified and
-devoid of warmth, reverberated through the bridge. “The
Queen’s pathetic attempts at resistance have been…
-neutralized. Her loyalists are scattered, her ‘Vampires’ are
on the verge of collapse. Mars is ours.”

A ripple of satisfaction went through her commanders. They
-were a collection of hardened corporate enforcers and
-disillusioned Martian military, drawn to Zara’s promise of
order and power. They believed in her vision, a universe
-reshaped by her will.

“The mutagen, as you know, has been perfected,” Zara
-continued, a chilling smile playing on her lips. “It is no
-longer a crude weapon of destruction, but a tool of…
-transformation. It will not kill. It will pacify. It will
-ensure compliance. And it will be delivered to the UN, disguised
as a desperate plea from the Queen for aid against a fabricated
Carcazod resurgence.”
