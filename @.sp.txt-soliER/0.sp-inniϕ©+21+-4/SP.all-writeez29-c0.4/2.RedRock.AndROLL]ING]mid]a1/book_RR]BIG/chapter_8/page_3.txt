Meanwhile, in the Queen’s war room on Mars, a stark
-contrast to Zara’s opulent bridge, the atmosphere was one of
-frantic urgency. The Queen, her face etched with worry,
-stood over a tactical display, surrounded by Lucky, Gem,
Astra, and a handful of her most trusted advisors. Ratchet’s
-latest intel, a fragmented but chillingly clear transmission
from Zara’s flagship, had just come through.

“She’s going to frame me,” the Queen whispered, her voice
-trembling with a mixture of fear and fury. “She’s going to
-unleash that… that abomination on Earth and blame me.”

“And she’s already got the UN council primed,” Lucky added,
his fingers flying across a console, cross-referencing data.
“Her agents have been feeding them disinformation for weeks.
They’re expecting a bio-weapon from Mars. They just don’t
know it’s coming from Zara, not you.”

“We have to stop her,” Astra declared, her hand instinctively
-going to the hilt of her sidearm. “We hit the *Iron Will*.
We take out the ship, we take out the mutagen.”

“Too risky,” Gem countered, her cybernetic eye scanning the
-tactical display. “The *Iron Will* is heavily shielded, and
Zara’s fleet is formidable. A direct assault would be suicide.
And even if we succeeded, the mutagen might still be released.”
