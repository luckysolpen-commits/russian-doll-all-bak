The Red Queen stared at him, a mixture of surprise and
-skepticism on her face. “You speak with a great deal of
-confidence, Mr. Starr. For a man who has been a ghost for
the past ten years.”

Lucky smiled, a charming, disarming smile that had gotten
him out of more than one tight spot. “I find that confidence
is a useful tool, Your Majesty. Especially when dealing with
-people who have lost their own.”

He saw a flicker of anger in her eyes, and he knew he had
-struck a nerve. He was pushing her, testing her, trying to
-see if the warrior queen was still there, buried beneath
the layers of desperation and defeat.

“I am not here to play games, Mr. Starr,” she said, her
-voice cold, regal. “I am here to stop a monster from
-destroying two worlds. And I will do whatever it takes to
-succeed.”

“Good,” Lucky replied, his smile fading, his expression
-becoming serious. “Because I am going to need your help.
I need your intelligence, your resources, your knowledge of
Zara’s operations. I need you to be my partner in this.”

He was offering her an alliance, a chance to reclaim her
-power, her dignity. It was a dangerous offer, a deal with
a devil she did not know. But it was the only offer she
had.

She looked at him, her eyes searching his face, trying to
-read the man behind the mask. She saw the cunning, the
-ruthlessness, the ambition. But she also saw something
-else. A flicker of honor, of loyalty, of a man who was
-tired of running from his past.

She made her decision. “I will help you,” she said, her
-voice firm, resolute. “But on one condition. When this is
-over, you will help me to reclaim my throne.”

Lucky nodded. “You have my word, Your Majesty.”

It was a promise he had no intention of keeping. But it
was a promise that would get him what he wanted.
