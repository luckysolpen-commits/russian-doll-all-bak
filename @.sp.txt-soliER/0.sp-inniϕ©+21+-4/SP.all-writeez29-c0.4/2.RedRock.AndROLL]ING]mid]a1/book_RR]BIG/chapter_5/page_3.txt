The UN security commander, a stern-faced woman with a no-
-nonsense demeanor, stepped forward, her hand resting on the
-butt of her plasma pistol. “Commander, your vessel is under
-quarantine. State your purpose.”

The Martian envoy, a tall, gaunt man with haunted eyes,
-pushed back his hood. His face was pale, his lips cracked
and dry. He looked like a man who had seen hell.

“A message for the Queen,” he said, his voice a hoarse
-whisper, filled with a chilling dread. “Urgent. From Mars.
Zara… Zara has accelerated the plan. She’s twisted it. It’s
no longer an invasion. It’s… a plague.”

The word hung in the air, a cold, heavy stone. Plague.
Lucky felt a jolt of recognition, a memory of the Carcazod
-plague that had ravaged the surface world. This was
-different. This was man-made. Zara. The ruthless corporate
-warlord. Of course. It had her name written all over it.

“What plan?” the UN commander demanded, her voice sharp,
-suspicious.

“The Queen’s original plan… to reclaim Earth,” the envoy
-gasped, his eyes wide with terror. “A show of force. A
-political maneuver. But Zara… she’s weaponized the mutagen.
She’s going to unleash it. Not to conquer, but to… cleanse.”

Lucky’s mind reeled. The Queen’s invasion plan. A long-
-standing threat, but one that had always seemed distant, a
-political maneuver. Now, Zara had taken it, perverted it,
-turned it into something far more sinister. A plague. A
-cleansing. It was a move of breathtaking audacity, of
-unimaginable cruelty.

He exchanged a quick, meaningful glance with Astra and Gem.
Their faces mirrored his own shock and dawning horror. They
-were no longer just curious onlookers. They were involved.
The *Red Dust* had brought more than just a message from Mars.
It had brought a new, terrifying chapter in humanity’s
-struggle for survival.
