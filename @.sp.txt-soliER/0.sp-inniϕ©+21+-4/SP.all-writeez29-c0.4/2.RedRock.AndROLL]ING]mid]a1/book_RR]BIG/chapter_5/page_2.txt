Then he saw her. A woman with a rifle slung over her back,
her mercenary gear, though toned down for a civilian setting,
-unmistakable. Her eyes, sharp and assessing, met his across
the bustling hangar. A flicker of recognition, then a subtle
-nod. Astra. The hot-headed, impulsive warrior who had been
his right hand, his confidante, his lover. The woman he had
-betrayed. The sight of her was a punch to the gut, a flood
of memories and regrets.

And then, a few paces behind her, another ghost from his
-past. Gem. The renegade clone with the cybernetic eye, her
-posture radiating a quiet alertness. The intel specialist
who had been the brains of their operation. The woman who
had always seen through his masks, his deceptions.

The trio, brought together by an unseen hand, stood on the
-precipice of something significant. The air crackled with
-unspoken history, with old wounds and unresolved tensions.
It was a reunion he had both dreaded and, in a secret corner
of his heart, longed for.

A ramp extended from the *Red Dust*, and a small contingent
of Martian guards, the Red Queen’s elite, descended. They
-looked haggard, their crimson uniforms dusted with red
-Martian soil, their faces etched with exhaustion and a
-desperate urgency. Behind them, a single figure, cloaked
and hooded, was carefully escorted down the ramp.

“That’s the Queen’s envoy,” a voice whispered beside Lucky.
He turned to see Ratchet, his clone contact, his face grim.
“Came in hot. No official clearance. Something’s seriously
wrong.”

Lucky nodded, his eyes fixed on the cloaked figure. The
-pieces of the puzzle were starting to come together, and
the picture they were forming was a dangerous one. The Red
Queen, Zara, the ‘Vampire’ problem. It was all connected.
And he was standing right in the middle of it.
