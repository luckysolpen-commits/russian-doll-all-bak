As they worked, Lucky found himself drawn back into the
-world he had tried so hard to escape. The world of shadows
and secrets, of violence and betrayal. The world where he
felt most alive.

He found himself drawn to Astra, to the fire in her eyes,
the passion in her soul. He remembered the feel of her in
his arms, the taste of her lips. He remembered the pain he
had caused her, the betrayal that had torn them apart.

He knew he could never have her back. The wounds were too
-deep, the scars were too real. But he could earn back her
-respect. He could prove to her that he was a man worthy
of her loyalty.

He found himself drawn to Gem, to her quiet strength, her
-unwavering resolve. He remembered her as the conscience
of their crew, the one who had always tried to keep them
on the right path. He had not listened to her then. He
-would listen to her now.

He was a different man now. A better man. Or so he told
-himself. But as he looked at the faces of his old crew,
he knew that the past was never truly past. It was a part
of him, a shadow that would always be there.

But for the first time in a long time, he was not afraid
of the darkness. He was ready to face it. He was ready to
-embrace it. He was ready to use it as a weapon.

He was Lucky Starr. And he was back in the game.
