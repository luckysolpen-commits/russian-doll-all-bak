The UN commander, her face pale, barked orders into her
-comm unit. The hangar bay doors began to seal, the heavy
-durasteel groaning into place. They were trapped. Trapped
with a desperate envoy, a terrifying message, and the
-chilling realization that the cold war between factions was
-about to ignite into a full-blown catastrophe. The gilded
-cage of Atlantis Prime was about to become a very real
-prison.

Lucky felt a familiar surge of adrenaline, a feeling he
-had not experienced in years. The thrill of the game, the
-dance on the edge of a knife. He was back in his element.
The mask of the respectable businessman was slipping, and
the man underneath, the mercenary, the survivor, was
-emerging.

He looked at Astra, at the fire in her eyes. She was a
-warrior, and she was ready for a fight. He looked at Gem,
at her calm, analytical gaze. She was the strategist, the
-brains of the operation. And he… he was the leader. The
-one who had to make the hard choices, the one who had to
-carry the weight of their collective fate.

He felt a sense of responsibility, a feeling he had tried
to escape, but that had always been a part of him. He was
-responsible for these people, for his crew. He had betrayed
them once. He would not do it again.

He knew what he had to do. He had to get them out of here.
He had to get to the Queen. He had to find a way to stop
Zara. He had to save them all.

It was an impossible task. But Lucky Starr had always
-specialized in the impossible.
