The Triton Tower Hangar Bay was a cavernous space, a symphony
of industry and controlled chaos. The air, usually thick with
the tang of ozone and hydraulic fluid, now carried a faint,
-unsettling metallic scent. It was the smell of fear, of
-desperation. Lucky Starr, his usual composure a thin veneer
-over a growing unease, watched as the Martian transport ship,
the *Red Dust*, settled onto its landing pad with a hiss of
-compressed air. Its hull, scarred and pitted, spoke of a
long, arduous journey. It was a wounded animal, seeking
-refuge in the heart of its enemy’s territory.

UN security forces, clad in their distinctive blue and silver
-armor, swarmed the perimeter, their energy rifles held at the
ready. A quarantine. That was the official word. A routine
-health check for a vessel arriving from a known contaminant
-zone. But the tension in the air was palpable, a silent alarm
-ringing in Lucky’s gut. This was no routine quarantine. This
was a lockdown.

He’d received an urgent, encrypted message, a summons from
an unknown source, instructing him to be here. The message
had been brief, cryptic, and had hinted at a situation far
-more dire than a simple quarantine. It had been a ghost from
his past, a whisper from a life he had tried to forget. And
-like a moth to a flame, he had come.

He scanned the faces in the crowd, looking for any sign of
the sender, but found only the usual mix of hangar personnel
and curious onlookers. He felt a familiar prickle of paranoia
on the back of his neck. He was exposed here, vulnerable.
He was a man who preferred to operate from the shadows, and
-this was a very public stage.
