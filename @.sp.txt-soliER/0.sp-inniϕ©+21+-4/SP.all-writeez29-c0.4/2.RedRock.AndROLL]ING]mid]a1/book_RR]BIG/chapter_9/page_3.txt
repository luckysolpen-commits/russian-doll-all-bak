Meanwhile, in the black expanse of space above Mars, a
-different kind of battle was raging. The Queen’s small,
-battered fleet, a collection of aging patrol craft and
-hastily re-purposed freighters, faced off against Zara’s
-gleaming armada. Lasers crisscrossed the void, painting
-streaks of emerald and crimson against the backdrop of
-distant stars. Explosions bloomed like deadly flowers,
-silent and beautiful in the vacuum.

The Queen, her face a mask of grim determination, stood on
the bridge of her flagship, the *Crimson Dawn*. “Protect the
*Serenade* at all costs!” she commanded, her voice echoing
-through the tense silence. “She must get through!”

Her ships, outmatched and outnumbered, fought with a
-suicidal bravery. They were shields, sacrifices, buying
-time for the tiny, almost invisible *Starlight Serenade*
to slip through the chaos. Zara’s fleet, confident in their
-superior firepower, focused their attacks on the larger,
-more obvious targets, unaware of the true prize they were
-allowing to escape.
