The Martian supply depot was a sprawling, utilitarian complex,
a maze of corrugated metal, towering cargo containers, and
-automated forklifts. Today, however, it was a battlefield.
The air, usually thick with the dust of commerce, now choked
with the acrid scent of plasma fire and the metallic tang of
-spilled blood. Ratchet, his face grim, barked orders into
his comm unit, coordinating the movements of his motley crew
of “Vampire” loyalists.

“Hold the line! Don’t let them reach the launchpad!” he
-roared, unleashing a burst of concentrated energy from his
-modified plasma rifle. Zara’s corporate enforcers, clad in
-sleek, black armor, advanced relentlessly, their weapons
-spitting lethal beams. They were well-trained, well-equipped,
and utterly ruthless.

This was the diversion. A desperate, brutal skirmish designed
to draw Zara’s attention, to buy precious minutes for the
*Starlight Serenade* to make its escape. Ratchet’s “Vampires,”
-fueled by a potent mix of desperation and loyalty to the
Queen, fought with a ferocity born of years of oppression.
They were fighting not just for their Queen, but for their
-very existence.
