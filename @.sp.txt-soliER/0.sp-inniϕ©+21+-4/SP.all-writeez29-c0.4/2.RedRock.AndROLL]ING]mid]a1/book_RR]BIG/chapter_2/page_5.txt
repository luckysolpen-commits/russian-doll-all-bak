“Where is this lab?” Gem asked, her voice a low, urgent whisper.
“Do you have any idea?”

Ratchet shook his head, a flicker of frustration in his eyes.
“No. It’s deep black. Off the books. Zara’s best kept secret.
But I have my feelers out. If anyone can find it, I can.”

Gem knew he was right. Ratchet had a network of informants
and spies that rivaled the UN’s. He was a master of the
-underworld, a man who knew where all the bodies were buried.
If there was a secret lab on Mars, he would find it.

She pushed a small, unmarked data chip across the bar, her
-hand shielding the movement from any prying eyes. “This is a
-secure comm channel. Encrypted. Untraceable. Anything you
-hear, anything at all, send it to this frequency. And be
-careful, Ratchet. Zara plays for keeps. She won’t hesitate
to eliminate anyone who gets in her way.”

He nodded, pocketing the chip with a sleight of hand that
was almost invisible. “You too, Gem. The shadows are getting
longer. Even in the Nova.” He drained his glass, the amber
-liquid disappearing in a single swallow. He gave her a final,
-meaningful look, then slid off the stool and disappeared into
the pulsating, anonymous crowd.

Gem was left alone with her thoughts, the throb of the music
a dull, meaningless roar in her ears. The whispers of war on
the horizon had become a deafening shout. The fragile peace
was not just shattering; it was being systematically dismantled.
And at the center of it all was Zara, a spider weaving a web
of deception and death.

She finished her drink, the synthetic taste a bitter reminder
of the artificial world they had built. A world that was now
threatened by the very same human failings that had destroyed
the old one. Greed. Ambition. The lust for power.

She knew she couldn’t just sit back and watch it happen. She
was not a passive observer. She was a player. And she had a
-decision to make. To report this to her superiors at the UN,
who would likely get bogged down in bureaucracy and protocol?
Or to take matters into her own hands? To reach out to the
-only other people she knew who were crazy enough, skilled
-enough, to take on a threat like Zara?
