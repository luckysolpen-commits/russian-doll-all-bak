She prepared for her meeting with Lucky, her movements
-precise, economical. She chose her clothes carefully. Not
the tactical gear of a soldier, but the discreet, functional
-attire of a corporate security consultant. A gray pantsuit,
a simple, high-collared blouse. An outfit that was both
-professional and anonymous. An outfit that would allow her
to blend in, to be underestimated.

She checked her sidearm, a sleek, compact plasma pistol.
It was a custom job, modified for silence and accuracy.
She holstered it in a concealed rig under her jacket. An
-old habit. A necessary precaution. She was walking into
the lion’s den, and she would not go unprepared.

She thought about the man she was about to meet. The Lucky
Starr she had known was a brilliant, ruthless tactician,
a man who was always three steps ahead of his enemies. He
was also a man who was haunted by his past, a man who was
-capable of both great loyalty and great betrayal. She had
-seen both sides of him. She had trusted him with her life,
and she had seen him make choices that had almost gotten
them all killed.

What kind of man was he now? The successful entrepreneur,
the pillar of the community? Or was that just a mask? A
-facade to hide the same old Lucky, the man who would do
-anything to win, no matter the cost?

She didn’t know. But she was about to find out. The game
was afoot. The players were being assembled. And the fate
of two worlds hung in the balance.

She took one last look at her reflection in the darkened
-window. Her face was a mask of calm, controlled composure.
But her eyes, one gray, one cybernetic, held a spark of
-something else. A spark of anticipation. A spark of fear.
A spark of hope.

The whispers in the Nova had started a fire. And she was
-ready to watch it burn.
