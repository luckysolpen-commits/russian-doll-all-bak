She arrived at her apartment, a small, functional space
in one of the city’s less fashionable sectors. It was a
-stark contrast to Lucky’s opulent penthouse, a place of
-spartan efficiency, not luxury. She preferred it that way.
It was a space that reflected her own personality. No
-frills, no sentimentality. Just the bare essentials.

She sat down at her terminal, a custom-built machine that
was a marvel of illegal, black-market technology. She
-bypassed the standard UN net, diving deep into the dark
-web, the hidden corners of the digital world where information
was a currency and secrets were a commodity.

She began to build a file on Zara. A comprehensive profile
that went far beyond the UN’s official records. She delved
into her corporate dealings, her political connections, her
-personal history. She was looking for a weakness, a crack
in the armor of the corporate titan. Everyone had one. It
was just a matter of finding it.

She worked for hours, her fingers flying across the keyboard,
her cybernetic eye processing data at a speed that would have
-overwhelmed a normal human brain. She was a digital phantom,
a ghost in the machine, moving through firewalls and encryption
-protocols with an effortless grace. This was her true element,
the world where she was most alive, most powerful.

She found whispers of Zara’s secret lab, fragmented data
-packets that hinted at a location deep in the volcanic
-calderas of Mars. She found encrypted communications that
-spoke of a ‘mutagen’ that could ‘pacify’ populations, that
-could ‘ensure compliance.’ The pieces of the puzzle were
-starting to come together, and the picture they were forming
was more terrifying than she could have imagined.

Zara wasn’t just planning an invasion. She was planning a
-subjugation. A biological coup that would leave her in
-control of not just Mars, but Earth as well. She was a
-monster, a woman who would sacrifice entire populations
for her own twisted vision of order.

Gem felt a cold, hard anger settle in her gut. This was
-no longer just a mission. It was personal. She had seen
-enough tyrants in her time, enough men and women who were
-willing to burn the world to rule over the ashes. She would
-not let it happen again. Not on her watch.
