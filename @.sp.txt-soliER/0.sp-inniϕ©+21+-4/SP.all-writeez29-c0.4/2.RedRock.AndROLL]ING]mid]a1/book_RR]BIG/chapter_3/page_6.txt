As their transport ship, a heavily modified, unmarked
-freighter, made its way towards Atlantis Prime, Astra
-briefed her team. She kept it simple, direct. The mission
was to meet a contact, to gather intelligence. It was a
-reconnaissance mission, not an assault. At least, not
yet.

She saw the disappointment in Jax’s eyes. He was itching
for a fight. But he was a professional. He understood the
-importance of discipline, of following orders. He would
-wait for her signal.

She felt a sense of unease as they approached Triton Tower.
It was the heart of the UN’s power, a fortress of bureaucracy
and regulation. They were walking into the lion’s den, and
they were doing it on the word of a ghost.

She thought about the last time she had been in a place
like this. A high-end corporate tower, on a mission that
had gone horribly wrong. A mission that had ended with her
-crew decimated, her reputation in tatters. A mission that
had been orchestrated by a man she had trusted.

She pushed the memory away. This was different. This time,
she was in control. She would not make the same mistakes.
She would not trust anyone blindly. Especially not the ghost
who had summoned them.

They docked at a deserted cargo bay, away from the main
-hangar. The air was thick with the smell of ozone and
-hydraulic fluid. The silence was unnerving. It was too
-quiet. Too clean. It felt like a trap.

She signaled for her team to take up defensive positions.
They moved with a silent, deadly grace, their weapons at
the ready. They were a pack of wolves in a field of sheep,
and they were ready for anything.

Astra stood in the center of the bay, her sniper rifle
-held in a low-ready position. She scanned the shadows,
her eyes, augmented with thermal and night vision, piercing
the darkness. She was waiting. For the ghost to show its
face.
