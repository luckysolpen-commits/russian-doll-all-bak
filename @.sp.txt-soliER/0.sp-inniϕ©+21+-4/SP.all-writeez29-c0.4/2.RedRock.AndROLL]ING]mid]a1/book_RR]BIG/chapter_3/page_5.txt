The mood in the hideout shifted instantly. The listless
-boredom was gone, replaced by a palpable energy, a buzz
of excitement. The pack had a purpose again. They were
-no longer caged wolves; they were hunters, preparing for
the chase.

They moved with a practiced efficiency, a silent, well-
-oiled machine. They checked their weapons, their gear,
their cybernetic implants. They were professionals, and
they took pride in their work. This was what they were
-born to do.

Astra watched them, a sense of pride swelling in her
-chest. This was her crew. Her family. And she would lead
them into hell and back. She felt the familiar weight of
-responsibility settle on her shoulders, but this time, it
was a welcome burden. It was the weight of command, of
-purpose.

She selected her team carefully. Jax, of course. His
-firepower was essential. Kael, for his stealth and
-reconnaissance skills. And a handful of others, the best
of the best. A small, elite squad, capable of infiltrating
any facility, of taking on any target.

She felt a flicker of unease. The voice on the comm… it
-still bothered her. It was a ghost from a past she had
-tried to bury. A past that was intertwined with betrayal
and loss. She was walking into a situation she didn’t
-fully understand, and that was a dangerous thing.

But the lure of action, of a real mission, was too strong
to resist. She was a creature of instinct, a predator.
And her instincts were screaming at her that this was a
-chance she had to take. A chance to reclaim her identity,
to prove that she was still the best in the business.

She looked at the data-pad on the table, the paltry
-escort contract still displayed on the screen. She smiled,
a cold, humorless expression. She picked it up and, with
a flick of her wrist, sent it crashing against the far
-wall, the screen shattering into a million pieces.

“We’re done with that,” she said, her voice firm, resolute.
“We’re back in the game.”
