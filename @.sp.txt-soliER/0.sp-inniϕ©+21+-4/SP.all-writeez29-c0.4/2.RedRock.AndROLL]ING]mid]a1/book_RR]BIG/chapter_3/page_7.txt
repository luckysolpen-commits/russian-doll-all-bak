A figure emerged from the shadows. A woman. She was dressed
in a simple, functional pantsuit, her movements precise,
-economical. She was unarmed, but she carried herself with
an air of quiet confidence, of authority. Astra recognized
her instantly. Gem. The renegade clone with the cybernetic
eye. The intel specialist from her old crew.

Astra felt a surge of conflicting emotions. Relief, at
-seeing a familiar face. Anger, at the memory of their last
-mission together, the mission that had torn them apart.
And a flicker of something else. A deep, buried affection
for the woman who had been her friend, her confidante, her
-sister-in-arms.

“Gem,” Astra said, her voice a low growl. “You’ve got a
lot of nerve, showing your face here.”

Gem’s expression was unreadable. Her cybernetic eye glowed
with a faint, internal light, its gaze analytical, detached.
“It’s good to see you too, Astra,” she said, her voice calm,
-level. “It’s been a long time.”

“Not long enough,” Astra retorted, though her words lacked
their usual bite. “What’s this all about, Gem? Why the
-melodramatic summons? And who was the ghost on the comm?”

“The ghost was me,” Gem replied, a faint, almost imperceptible
-smile touching her lips. “I needed to get your attention.
And I knew that a direct approach would be… unwise.”

Astra snorted. “Unwise? Or cowardly?”

Gem’s smile faded. “Call it what you will. We have a
-situation. A serious one. And I need your help.”

“My help?” Astra laughed, a harsh, bitter sound. “After
what happened last time? You’ve got to be kidding me.”

The memory of their last mission was a raw, open wound.
A betrayal that had cost them dearly. A betrayal that had
-been orchestrated by the man Gem had been working for.
A man Astra had once trusted.
