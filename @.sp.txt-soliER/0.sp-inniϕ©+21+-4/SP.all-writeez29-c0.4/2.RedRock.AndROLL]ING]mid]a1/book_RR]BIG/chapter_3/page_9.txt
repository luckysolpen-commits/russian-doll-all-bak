She was torn. Her loyalty to her crew, her hatred of Zara,
her complicated, unresolved feelings for Lucky. It was a
-maelstrom of emotions, a storm that threatened to tear her
apart.

She looked at Gem, at the desperation in her eyes. She saw
the same fear, the same determination, that she felt in her
own heart. They were in this together, whether she liked
it or not.

And she knew, with a sinking feeling, that Gem was right.
They needed Lucky. They needed his cunning, his resources,
his ruthlessness. He was a monster, but he was their monster.
And they were about to unleash him on a world that was not
prepared for him.

“Fine,” Astra said, her voice tight with a mixture of anger
and resignation. “We’ll play it your way. For now. But if
he crosses us, Gem, if he even thinks about betraying us
again, I will kill him myself. Do you understand?”

Gem nodded, a flicker of relief in her eyes. “Understood.”

Astra turned to her crew. “You heard the lady. We’ve got
a new mission. We’re going to war.”

A grim smile spread across Jax’s face. “It’s about damn
time.”

The restlessness was gone. The boredom, the frustration,
the despair. It had all been replaced by a cold, hard
-purpose. They were mercenaries. They were warriors. And
they were about to remind the universe why they were the
best.

The game had changed. The stakes were higher than ever
before. And Astra, for the first time in a long time, felt
truly, completely alive.
