As she prepared for her journey, the Queen felt a sense
of liberation she had not felt in years. She was no longer
a prisoner of her throne, of her legacy. She was a player
in a new, dangerous game. And she was ready to play.

She shed her crimson robes, the symbol of her fading power.
She donned the simple, functional clothing of a refugee.
She was no longer the Red Queen of Mars. She was a woman
-on a mission. A woman fighting for her life, for her people,
for her world.

She looked at her reflection one last time. She saw a woman
who was tired, who was scared. But she also saw a woman who
was determined. A woman who would not be broken.

She was a survivor. And she would survive this. She would
-find Lucky Starr. She would forge an alliance. And together,
they would bring down Zara’s empire.

The red dust of Mars swirled outside her window, a final,
-farewell kiss. She was leaving her home, her world. But
she would be back. And when she returned, she would not
be a refugee. She would be a conqueror.

The fading grip of the Red Queen was about to become the
-iron fist of a warrior.
