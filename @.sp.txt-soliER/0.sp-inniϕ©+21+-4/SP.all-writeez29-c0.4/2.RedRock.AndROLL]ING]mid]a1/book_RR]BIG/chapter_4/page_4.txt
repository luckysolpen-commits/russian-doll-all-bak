“What do you want, Zara?” the Queen finally asked, her voice
weary, defeated. The words were a surrender, a concession
of her own powerlessness. And she hated herself for it.

“Control,” Zara replied, her eyes gleaming with a predatory
-satisfaction. “Complete control of Martian resources. And
your… cooperation in establishing a new order. One free from
the… inefficiencies of your current regime.”

It was a declaration of conquest, delivered with a smile.
Zara was not just offering a deal; she was dictating terms.
She was demanding the complete and utter surrender of Martian
-sovereignty.

The Queen’s hand clenched on the armrest of her throne,
her knuckles white. To yield to Zara would be to surrender
-everything she had fought for, everything her ancestors had
-built. It would be to betray her people, her legacy, her
very identity.

But to resist… to resist meant certain destruction. Not
just for her, but for her people, including the very clones
she was trying to protect. She was caught in an impossible
-dilemma, a choice between two different kinds of death.

She thought of her father, the previous king. He had been
a strong, proud man, a warrior who had fought to protect
Mars from all threats, both external and internal. What
-would he do in her position? He would fight. He would
-never surrender. But he had lived in a different time, a
-simpler time. A time before corporate empires and biological
-warfare.

She was not her father. She was a different kind of ruler,
a ruler who had been forced to adapt to a changing universe.
A ruler who understood the art of the compromise, the
-necessity of survival.

But this… this was not a compromise. This was an annihilation.
