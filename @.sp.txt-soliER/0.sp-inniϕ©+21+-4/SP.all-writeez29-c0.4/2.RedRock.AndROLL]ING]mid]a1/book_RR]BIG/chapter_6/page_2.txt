Hours crawled by, marked only by the changing shifts of the
-guards. The silence in the cell was thick with unspoken
-tensions, with the ghosts of their shared past. Lucky could
feel Astra’s anger, a simmering volcano of resentment and
-betrayal. He could feel Gem’s quiet, analytical gaze, her
-mind no doubt dissecting their situation, calculating the
-odds.

He wanted to speak, to break the silence, to try and bridge
the chasm that had opened up between them all those years
ago. But the words wouldn’t come. What could he say? That he
was sorry? That he regretted the choices he had made? It all
-sounded so hollow, so inadequate.

He had been a different man then. A man driven by greed and
-ambition, a man who had been willing to sacrifice anything,
and anyone, to get what he wanted. He had changed. Or so he
-told himself. But as he sat there, in that sterile cage,
he wasn’t so sure.

Finally, the force field shimmered and retracted. A different
UN officer, this one with a slightly less rigid posture,
-gestured for them to follow. “The Queen will see you.”

The three of them exchanged a look. This was it. The next
-move in a game they didn’t fully understand. They followed
the officer, their footsteps echoing in the silent, sterile
-corridors. It felt like they were walking to their own
-execution.
