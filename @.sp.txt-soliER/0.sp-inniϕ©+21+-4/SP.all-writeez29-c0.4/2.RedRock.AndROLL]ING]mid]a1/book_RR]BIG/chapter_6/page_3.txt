They were led through a labyrinth of sterile corridors, past
-heavily armed patrols, until they arrived at a suite of
-rooms that, despite their temporary nature, exuded an aura
of regal authority. The Red Queen stood by a panoramic
-window, gazing out at the shimmering cityscape of Atlantis
Prime. Her crimson robes, though travel-worn, still commanded
-respect. She was a queen, even in exile.

She turned as they entered, her eyes, once sharp and
-commanding, now held a flicker of desperation. She looked
at them, one by one, her gaze lingering on Lucky for a
-moment too long. She saw the mercenary, the survivor, the
man she had gambled everything on.

“You are the ones who arrived on the *Red Dust*,” she stated,
her voice surprisingly soft, yet with an undercurrent of
-steel. “The envoy… he spoke of a plague.”

Lucky stepped forward, taking charge of the situation.
“Your Majesty, Zara has weaponized the mutagen. She plans
to unleash it on Earth, framing you, and then seize control
of both Martian and Earth forces.”

The Queen’s face, already pale, seemed to drain of all
-color. “I… I had suspected something was amiss. Her
-communications have been… evasive. But a plague… I never
-imagined she would stoop so low.” She walked to a small
-table, pouring herself a glass of water with a trembling
-hand. “My original plan… it was a desperate gamble. A show
of force, yes, but never… never this.”
