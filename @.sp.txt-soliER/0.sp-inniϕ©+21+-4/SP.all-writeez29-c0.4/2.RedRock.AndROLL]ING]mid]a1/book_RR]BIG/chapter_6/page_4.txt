“Your ‘Vampire’ guards are growing restless, Your Majesty,”
Gem interjected, her voice calm, analytical. “Ratchet, my
-contact, says they’re on the verge of open rebellion. Zara’s
-exploiting their desperation.”

The Queen nodded, her gaze distant. “They are my children,
in a way. Created to serve, to protect. But the lack of
-resources… it has driven them to the brink. Zara offers
them a false hope, a promise of salvation that will only
-lead to their destruction, and ours.” She turned back to
them, her eyes hardening with resolve. “I need your help.
All of you. My forces on Mars are stretched thin. My
-loyalists are fighting a losing battle. If Zara succeeds,
not only will Earth fall, but Mars will be enslaved.”

Astra scoffed. “And what’s in it for us, Your Majesty?
We’re not exactly known for our altruism.”

The Queen’s lips curved into a faint, bitter smile.
“Survival. And perhaps… a chance to reclaim what was lost.
I have technology, knowledge, resources that would be
-invaluable to Earth. If we stop Zara, if we stabilize my
-rule, I will share it all. A true alliance. Not one built
on fear, but on mutual respect and the shared goal of
-survival.”

It was a tempting offer. A chance to not only save the
-world, but to profit from it as well. Lucky could see the
-appeal in Astra’s eyes. She was a mercenary, after all.
And this was the score of a lifetime.
