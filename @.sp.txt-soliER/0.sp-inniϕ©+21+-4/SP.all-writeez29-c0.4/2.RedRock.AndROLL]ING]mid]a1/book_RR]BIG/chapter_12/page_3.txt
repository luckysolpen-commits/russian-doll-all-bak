“What is that?” Commander Rostova demanded, rushing to the
-monitor.

“Carcazod,” a UN security officer reported, his voice laced
with disbelief. “But… it’s massive. And it’s moving with
-purpose. It’s not just a random attack. It’s… testing our
-defenses.”

Lucky’s blood ran cold. A Carcazod. Here? In the deep ocean?
It was impossible. They couldn’t swim. They were land-
-dwelling creatures, confined to the surface world by the
-very ocean that protected Atlantis Prime.

“It’s evolving,” Thorne whispered, his eyes wide with
-dawning horror. “The bio-marker… the mutagen… it’s not
just for humans and Martians. It’s for the Carcazods too.
Zara wasn’t just trying to control us. She was trying to
-control *them*.”

The realization hit them like a physical blow. Zara, in her
-insatiable lust for power, had tampered with forces she
-couldn’t possibly comprehend. She had taken the Carcazod
-plague, the very thing that had driven humanity into the
-oceans, and had tried to weaponize it, to turn it into her
-own personal army. But something had gone wrong. Or perhaps,
-something had gone exactly as planned, but not by Zara.
