The Atlantis Prime Medical Bay, usually a sanctuary of healing
and quiet efficiency, was now a hive of frantic activity. UN
-scientists, their faces grim, hunched over monitors, analyzing
the mutagen sample Lucky had delivered. The initial findings
-were alarming. This wasn’t just a bio-weapon; it was something
-far more complex, far more insidious.

Dr. Aris Thorne, a leading xenobiologist with a reputation
for both brilliance and eccentricity, adjusted his spectacles,
his eyes wide with a mixture of horror and fascination.
“It’s… it’s a genetic accelerant,” he murmured, more to
-himself than to the assembled team. “It doesn’t just mutate.
It *evolves*. It forces rapid adaptation, pushing organisms
-beyond their natural limits.”

“But to what end?” a junior scientist asked, her voice
-trembling.

“To control,” Thorne replied, his gaze fixed on a swirling
-holographic projection of the mutagen’s molecular structure.
“It’s designed to integrate with existing biological pathways,
to subtly alter neural networks, to… to make the host more
-receptive to external influence. A form of biological
-subjugation.”
