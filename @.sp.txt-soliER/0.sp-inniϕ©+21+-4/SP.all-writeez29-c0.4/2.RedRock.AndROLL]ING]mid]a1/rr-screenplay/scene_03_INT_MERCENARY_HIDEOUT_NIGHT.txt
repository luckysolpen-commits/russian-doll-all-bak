**SCENE 3**

**INT. MERCENARY HIDEOUT - NIGHT**

SOUND of clinking glasses, gruff laughter

The hideout is a grimy, repurposed maintenance tunnel deep in the underbelly of Atlantis Prime. A stark contrast to Lucky's office. Maps and weapon schematics are pinned to the walls.

ASTRA (30s, a natural leader, with the easy confidence of a seasoned warrior) shares a drink with a handful of her old MERCENARY crew. They are a rough-looking bunch, their gear well-worn.

<center>MERC 1</center>
> ...so I tell the UN regulator, 'That's not a weapons modification, that's a 'cargo stabilization unit.''

The mercs laugh.

<center>ASTRA</center>
> They're getting soft. This whole city is soft. All this... peace. It's making us obsolete.

<center>MERC 2</center>
> She's right. Last contract I took was 'security escort' for a shipment of decorative sea slugs. Sea slugs!

<center>ASTRA</center>
> We used to be warriors. Now we're glorified bodyguards.

She takes a long drink, her eyes scanning the faces of her crew.

<center>ASTRA</center>
> What's the word from the Red Planet?

<center>MERC 1</center>
> It's a powder keg. The Queen and Zara are at each other's throats. The whole planet is a warzone waiting to happen.

<center>ASTRA</center>
> And we're stuck here, babysitting fish.

<center>MERC 2</center>
> There's credits to be made in a war.

<center>ASTRA</center>
> It's not about the credits. It's about... purpose.

She looks at her hands, then at her crew.

<center>ASTRA</center>
> This peace... it's an illusion. And when it shatters, they're going to need people like us again.

She raises her glass.

<center>ASTRA</center>
> To purpose.

The mercs raise their glasses, a chorus of "To purpose."

**FADE OUT.**
