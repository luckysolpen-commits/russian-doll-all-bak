INT. SCRIBE'S HOLLOW - DAY

SOUND of creaking wood, distant gulls

Elara slips through the alleys of Quillhaven, avoiding the main square. Her INKLING, a small, shimmering creature made of living ink, perches on her shoulder, its form subtly shifting with her movements. She reaches the SCRIBE'S HOLLOW, a crumbling tower at the village's edge. Its walls are etched with runes no one can read anymore.

The iron door is rusted shut. Elara pulls a hairpin and a thin blade from her satchel. Her hands shake, not from cold, but from the weight of what she's doing.

CLOSE UP - Elara's hands expertly working the hairpin and blade into the lock.

CLICK. The lock gives. The door GROANS open, revealing a cavernous darkness within.

Elara hesitates for a moment, then steps inside. The Inkling shimmers, its form momentarily solidifying as if sensing the ancient magic within. The air is thick with dust and the scent of old parchment. She lights a small lantern from her satchel, its flame casting dancing shadows on shelves that stretch into the gloom. Books, scrolls, and loose pages lie in disarray.

The Soulpen in her sleeve feels heavier now, almost pulling her toward a spiral staircase at the room's center. The Inkling pulses faintly, its form elongating towards the staircase. Elara begins to climb, the lantern casting jagged shadows on the ancient walls.
