INT. OLD SCRIPTORIUM - DAY

SOUND of distant echoes, the occasional scuttling of unseen creatures

Elara, Torren, and Kai return to the Old Scriptorium, exhausted but exhilarated. Elara clutches a glowing, jagged VEIL-SHARD CRYSTAL. Her INKLING, perched on her shoulder, pulses with a soft, triumphant glow. Her face is smudged with dirt, her hair disheveled, but her eyes sparkle with a new kind of confidence.

KAI
(whistling)
Well, that was a bit more exciting than I bargained for. But profitable!

His MISCHIEF GREMLIN, clinging to his hair, chitters excitedly, holding up a tiny, shiny pebble it found.

Torren nods, a rare, almost-smile on his face. He looks at Elara with a newfound respect. His SHADOW SPRITE flits around his head, as if celebrating their success.

TORREN
You handled yourself well, Elara. That barrier... impressive.

Elara feels a surge of pride, but it's quickly followed by a wave of dizziness. She stumbles, pressing a hand to her temple. The Inkling shimmers wildly, its form distorting. A brief, chilling vision flashes through her mind: a fragmented memory of Lirien, her mother, looking desperate, writing furiously with the Soulpen, surrounded by swirling shadows. Then, a flash of pain, a sense of profound loss, not her own, but Lirien's.

ELARA
(shaking her head, trying to clear it)
It... it cost. A memory. Or a piece of one. My mother's.

Torren's expression darkens. He sits heavily on a fallen pillar, his gaze distant. The Shadow Sprite settles on his shoulder, its tiny form almost invisible.

TORREN
(voice low, haunted)
My family... they were keepers of the veil. Guardians. We tried to warn her. Lirien. She was so consumed by her grief, by her desire to bring back... someone. She thought the Soulpen was the answer. She didn't listen. The veil broke. And my family... they were caught in the unraveling. I was the only one who escaped. That's why I want the pen gone. It's a curse.

Elara looks at him, a new understanding dawning. His cynicism, his warnings, they stem from a deep, personal pain. The Soulpen is not just a tool; it's a weapon that has destroyed lives.

Suddenly, a new presence. A powerful, enigmatic MAGE, LORCAN (30s, sharp features, piercing blue eyes, dressed in elegant, dark robes), steps out of the shadows. He carries a staff topped with a glowing crystal. Floating beside him is a miniature, shimmering ARCANE GOLEM, a construct of pure magical energy that pulses with a soft light. He surveys the group, his gaze lingering on Elara and the Soulpen.

LORCAN
(voice smooth, authoritative)
I felt a surge of raw magic. Uncontrolled. And a familiar resonance. The Soulpen. It seems it has found a new wielder. And you, Torren. Still chasing shadows?

Torren tenses, his hand instinctively going to his blade. The Shadow Sprite lets out a low growl.

TORREN
Lorcan. Still meddling where you don't belong?

LORCAN
(a faint, knowing smile)
On the contrary. I believe I am precisely where I belong. This artifact... it is a danger. To all of us. Tell me, girl. What do you know of its true power? And its true cost?

Elara clutches the Soulpen, wary. The Inkling shimmers, its form becoming more defined, as if preparing for a challenge. Lorcan's presence is commanding, his questions probing. A tense, intellectual sparring match begins, as Elara tries to gauge his intentions and protect the secrets of the Soulpen.
