INT. SCRIBE'S HOLLOW - UPPER CHAMBER - DAY

Elara glares at Torren, her fear turning to anger. Her INKLING shimmers on her shoulder, its form agitated.

ELARA
(voice tight)
Don't talk about her like you knew her better than me.

Torren's lips twitch, not quite a smile. He steps closer, his eyes fixed on Elara's sleeve where the Soulpen is hidden. His SHADOW SPRITE flits silently from his shoulder to perch on a nearby bookshelf, its tiny head cocked.

TORREN
I didn't say that. But I know what you're holding. The Soulpen isn't a toy, girl. It's a key, and keys open things best left locked.

Elara takes a step back, her shoulder brushing a shelf. A scroll falls, unraveling across the floor.

ELARA
If you want it, you'll have to take it.

Torren laughs, a dry sound like breaking twigs. The Shadow Sprite twitches its ears at the sound.

TORREN
I don't want it. I want it gone. Destroyed, before it wakes what it shouldn't.

Elara's mind races. Destroy the Soulpen? Her mother had guarded it, maybe died for it.

ELARA
Why should I trust you?

TORREN
You shouldn't. But you'll want to hear me out. The Soulpen doesn't just write. It binds. It takes. Every word you write with it costs something—your will, your memories, your soul, if you're not careful.

Elara's hand drifts to her sleeve, where the pen's warmth pulses against her skin. The Inkling on her shoulder pulses in sync with the pen.

ELARA
You're lying.

TORREN
Am I? Write something now. Something small. See what happens.

Elara hesitates, then pulls the pen out, grabbing another scrap of parchment from the desk. The Inkling leans forward, watching intently. She writes:

CLOSE UP - The words appear:
"The lantern lights."

Suddenly, the extinguished lantern flares to life, its flame blue and unnatural. Elara GASPS, dropping the pen. The Inkling shimmers wildly, its form distorting. Her head THROBS, a sharp pain behind her eyes, and a brief, unsettling VISION flashes through her mind: a glimpse of a swirling, chaotic void.

Torren's expression darkens. The Shadow Sprite flits back to his shoulder, its tiny eyes fixed on Elara.

TORREN
You feel it, don't you? The cost.

Elara presses a hand to her temple, the pain fading but leaving a hollow ache. The Inkling settles, its form returning to normal.

ELARA
What was that?

TORREN
The Soulpen's price. It draws from you to make the words real. Small truths cost little. Big ones...

He trails off, glancing at the glowing lantern, then back at Elara.

TORREN (CONT'D)
Your mother wrote something big, Elara. Too big. She crossed the veil, and it unleashed chaos. That's why she's gone.
