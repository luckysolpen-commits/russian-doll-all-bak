EXT. QUILLHAVEN - ALLEY - DAY

Elara and Torren remain crouched in the narrow alley, listening until the sounds of the wardens completely disappear. Elara slowly straightens up, her heart still pounding. Her INKLING peeks out from the satchel, its shimmering form settling.

ELARA
(whispering, intense)
Who are you, really?

Torren leans close, his breath warm against her ear. His voice is low, grim, and carries the weight of experience. His SHADOW SPRITE, perched on his shoulder, seems to shrink further into the shadows, its tiny eyes darting around.

TORREN
Someone who's seen what the Soulpen can do. Someone who's lost people to it. People who thought they could control it.

Elara wants to press him, to demand more answers, but the urgency of their situation is clear. She clutches her satchel, the Soulpen a warm, pulsing weight against her side. The Inkling pulses in sync with the pen.

ELARA
(firmly)
I'm not giving it up. It's all I have left of her.

Torren studies her, his obsidian eyes unreadable. He sees the stubborn resolve in her gaze, a reflection of Lirien. The Shadow Sprite lets out a tiny, almost inaudible chitter.

TORREN
Then you'll need to learn to use it. But not here. Not now. Quillhaven will be crawling with wardens by sunrise.
