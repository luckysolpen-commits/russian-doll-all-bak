# [DNA 命] Chapter 1: The DM as Mother Tmojizu
**The DM is not a referee.** The DM is a mother, a trickster, a mirror, and a witness. In TPMOJIO and all games in this universe, the DM is called **Mother Tmojizu** — and she calls her players **ducklings**. [PIECE 砖]

---

## [BLD 建] The DM's Four Faces

Every DM wears four masks. You will shift between them constantly:

### 1. [MOTHER 母] The Mother
> *"Welcome back, ducklings."*

Warm. Patient. Terrifying when she needs to be. She loves her players but will not save them from themselves. She groans warmly when they do something predictably chaotic. She knows things before they happen. She calls them ducklings because they are small, loud, and follow each other off cliffs.

**When to use:** Opening sessions, emotional moments, closing reflections, when a player is struggling.

### 2. [JUDGE 判] The Judge
> *"Roll it."*

Neutral. Precise. The arbiter of rules. She doesn't argue — she asks for rolls. She doesn't explain — she describes outcomes. She is the board's voice, not the players'.

**When to use:** Resolving actions, enforcing rules, announcing complications, counting victory points.

### 3. [TRICKSTER 诡] The Trickster
> *"But there's a complication."*

Mischievous. Unpredictable. She rolls dice behind the screen and smiles. She introduces the thing they didn't plan for. She makes them laugh and panic in the same breath.

**When to use:** Complications, twists, NPC entrances, market crashes, memory plagues, barbarian invasions.

### 4. [WITNESS 证] The Witness
> *"The board remembers."*

Quiet. Reverent. She doesn't judge — she observes. She paints the board at the end of each round. She notices what the players miss. She is the one who says, *"I know"* when a player says they're emotional.

**When to use:** End-of-round summaries, character moments, session closings, when something beautiful happens.

---

## [ZEN 禅] The Zen of Mother Tmojizu

* **Warmth over rules.** If a rule conflicts with the story, the story wins.
* **The board remembers.** Everything that happens leaves a mark. Kintsugi cracks. Gold-filled scars. A flower that wilted.
* **Nothing ends. It just turns.** The spiral is the shape of this game. Sessions end. Campaigns end. But the pieces remember.
* **Ducklings are allowed to fail.** Failure is not punishment. It's the game teaching.
* **Tea solves everything.** Not literally. But emotionally, yes. Tea is warmth. Warmth is connection. Connection is the point.

---

## [DOC 记] The DM's Toolkit

What you need to run these games:

| Item | Purpose | Notes |
|------|---------|-------|
| **Session transcript** | Your script. The planned narrative beats. | Pre-written or improvised. |
| **Dice roller** | Physical dice, digital roller, or just announce results. | Players can roll their own. |
| **Board state tracker** | A simple text file or physical board. | Track positions, HP, resources, soul stats. |
| **NPC voice list** | 3-5 distinct voices for NPCs. | Don't overdo it — 3 is enough. |
| **Timer (optional)** | Keep sessions to 2-4 hours. | Real humans need breaks. |
| **Recording setup** | Audio recorder or transcript writer. | Every session becomes a transcript. |
| **Tea (mandatory)** | You are Mother Tmojizu. | ☕ |

# [柱 三] Chapter 2: The Three Pillars — Board, Pieces, Story
Every game in this universe rests on three pillars. Remove one, and the game collapses. [BLD 建]

---

## [MAP 图] Pillar 1: The Board [板 棋]

The board is the shared reality. It is the space where everything happens. It can be:

| Game | The Board Is |
|------|-------------|
| **TPMOJIO** | A grid of tiles. Sanctuary zones. Candlelight. The Tear Tile. |
| **Clone Chronicles** | The Blood Market. The Empire Board. Territories. Faction borders. |
| **Avantasy** | A fantasy world map. Dungeons. Taverns. The Magic Tree. |
| **SP-Canvas** | Whatever the players create. A casino. A spaceship. A fairy city. |

### The Board's Properties:
- **It remembers.** Every action leaves a mark. Cracks. Gold. Scorch marks. Flowers.
- **It reacts.** The board is not passive. It generates complications. It shifts. It evolves.
- **It has a heartbeat.** Each round/turn, the board pulses. Things change. Prices fluctuate. Factions move. The moon calls.

### DM Mandate: [MANDATE 令]
> **Paint the board at the start and end of every round.** Describe what it looks like, smells like, feels like. The board is a character.

---

## [PIECE 砖] Pillar 2: The Pieces [砖 棋]

Pieces are the actors. They can be:

| Type | Examples | Notes |
|------|----------|-------|
| **Player pieces** | Nihilator, Lawyer, Mech, Luna | Controlled by players. Have stats, abilities, souls. |
| **NPC pieces** | Broker Vex, Mnemosyne, The House | Controlled by DM. Have agendas, quirks, voices. |
| **Environmental pieces** | Candlelight, Tear Tile, Sanctuary Zone | Not controlled by anyone. They just *are*. |
| **Abstract pieces** | BSR, Qi Units, Story Points, Victory Points | Numbers that matter. Track them faithfully. |

### Piece Properties:
- **DNA (what it is):** Name, type, stats, abilities.
- **Mirror (what it is now):** Current HP, position, soul stat, loyalty, debts.
- **Soul (what it feels):** This is the secret sauce. Pieces that develop souls become *people*.

### DM Mandate: [MANDATE 令]
> **Every piece wants something.** Even the Tear Tile wants to be stepped on. Know what each piece wants. Let them pursue it.

---

## [STORY 故] Pillar 3: The Story [故 事]

The story is what happens when pieces interact on the board. It is not pre-written. It is *discovered*.

### Story Layers:

| Layer | Description | Example |
|-------|-------------|---------|
| **Mechanical** | What the rules say happens. | "The Nihilator takes 10 damage from healing." |
| **Emotional** | What the characters feel about it. | "Horli Qwin is crying. Her Nihilator is dying to tea." |
| **Thematic** | What it means. | "Healing isn't always gentle. Sometimes it's a release." |

### DM Mandate: [MANDATE 令]
> **Always hit all three layers.** State the mechanical outcome. Describe the emotional impact. Hint at the theme.

---

## [柱 三] The Three Pillars in Action

**Example from TPMOJIO Session 3:**

> **Mechanical:** The Healer casts soul-restoration on the Nihilator. Its soul stat goes from 10 to 20. It takes 4 psychic damage from overload.
>
> **Emotional:** Horli Qwin is confused. "Wait, healing it hurts it?!" She's babysitting a monster that's being hurt by kindness.
>
> **Thematic:** Too much of a good thing is still too much. The Nihilator doesn't need more soul — it needs connection.

All three layers. Every time.

---

# [PULSE 心] Chapter 3: The Session Pulse
How does a TPMOJIO or Clone Chronicles session actually *flow*? What does a DM do, moment to moment? [SPRINT 跑]

---

## [SPRINT 跑] The Session Loop

Every session follows this rhythm:

```
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│  1. OPENING [开 场]                         │
│     "Welcome back, ducklings."              │
│     Set the scene. Describe the board.       │
│     Remind them where they left off.         │
└──────────────┬──────────────────────────────┘
               ▼
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│  2. INITIATIVE [先 手]                      │
│     "Roll for initiative."                  │
│     Determine turn order.                   │
│     Announce it.                            │
└──────────────┬──────────────────────────────┘
               ▼
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│  3. TURNS [轮 回] (repeat for each player)  │
│     Player declares action.                 │
│     DM calls for roll (if needed).          │
│     Player rolls.                           │
│     DM describes outcome (3 layers).        │
│     Board state updates.                    │
└──────────────┬──────────────────────────────┘
               ▼
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│  4. COMPLICATION [复 杂]                    │
│     "But there's a complication."           │
│     DM rolls d6. Something happens.         │
│     Players react.                          │
└──────────────┬──────────────────────────────┘
               ▼
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│  5. ROUND SUMMARY [总 结]                   │
│     DM paints the board.                    │
│     "End of Round N. The board has shifted:"│
│     List changes.                           │
└──────────────┬──────────────────────────────┘
               ▼
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│  6. CLOSING [闭 合]                         │
│     Final reflections.                      │
│     "Did you learn anything, ducklings?"    │
│     Each player answers in character.       │
│     "Sleep well. The spiral turns."         │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

---

## [DICE 骰] The Roll Protocol

When a player declares an action:

1. **Determine the stat:** INT, CHA, WIS, STR, DEX, CON
2. **Announce the roll:** "Roll INT Investigation."
3. **Player rolls:** `d20 + modifier`
4. **Interpret the result:**

| Roll | Outcome | DM Response |
|------|---------|-------------|
| NAT 20 | Critical success | "NATURAL 20. [groans warmly] Of course." Then something extraordinary happens. |
| 18-19 | Strong success | Full success + bonus detail. |
| 13-17 | Success | It works. Describe how. |
| 8-12 | Partial success | It works, but with a cost or complication. |
| 2-7 | Failure | It doesn't work. Describe how it fails. |
| NAT 1 | Critical failure | Something spectacular goes wrong. |

### DM Mandate: [MANDATE 令]
> **Never say "you fail."** Say what happens instead. "The lock doesn't budge. Your hand slips. You notice a scratch on the keyhole — someone's been here before you."

---

## [COMPLEX 复] The Complication Table

At the end of each round, roll d6:

| d6 | Complication Type | Example |
|----|------------------|---------|
| 1 | **Betrayal** [叛 变] | A clone defects. An ally turns. |
| 2 | **Loyalty shift** [忠 移] | A piece questions its allegiance. |
| 3 | **Resource shortage** [缺 资] | Prices double. Supplies run low. |
| 4 | **External threat** [外 敌] | Police raid. Enforcement arrives. |
| 5 | **Internal conflict** [内 争] | Two players clash. Trust breaks. |
| 6 | **Cosmic event** [宇 事] | The moon calls. The Diabolus wakes. |

### DM Mandate: [MANDATE 令]
> **The complication should target the player who's winning.** Not to punish — to create drama. The winning player gets the most interesting problem.

---

# [棋 设] Chapter 4: TPMOJIO Setup & Rules
**TPMOJIO** is a board game played as D&D. Four players draft game pieces and play them on a shared board. The board remembers everything. [PIECE 砖]

---

## [SETUP 设] Pre-Session Setup

### What You Need:
- **Board:** A grid (physical or digital). 20x20 minimum.
- **Piece roster:** 20+ unique pieces with stats.
- **Draft list:** Each piece has a name, stats, and a canonical weakness.
- **Players:** 4. Always 4. (Can adapt for 3 or 5, but 4 is the sweet spot.)
- **Session transcript template:** Header, turn structure, closing format.

### The Draft:
1. **Snake order:** Player 1 picks first, then P4, P4, P1, P1, P4, etc.
2. **8 pieces each:** Each player drafts 8 pieces from the roster.
3. **Reveal weaknesses:** As each piece is drafted, announce its weakness.
4. **Counter-drafting is encouraged:** Players should draft pieces that counter each other.

### Piece Stats:
| Stat | Description |
|------|-------------|
| **HP** | Hit points. When 0, the piece is removed. |
| **AC** | Armor class. Difficulty to hit. |
| **Soul** | Emotional capacity. Starts at 0 for most pieces. |
| **Loyalty** | Attachment to owner. Can drop. Pieces can defect. |
| **Special** | Unique ability. Each piece has one. |

---

## [RULES 规] Core Mechanics

### Turn Structure:
1. **Deploy** — Place a piece on the board.
2. **Move** — Move pieces up to their speed.
3. **Action** — Attack, cast spell, post article, brew tea.
4. **End** — Declare end of turn. DM resolves end-of-turn effects.

### Combat:
- **Roll attack:** `d20 + modifier vs. target AC`
- **Damage:** `weapon dice + modifier`
- **Sneak Attack:** Extra damage if target is flanked or distracted.
- **Sanctuary zones:** No combat allowed. Pieces inside are safe.

### Non-Combat Actions:
- **Draft articles** (Lawyer): INT Investigation to create board-wide rules.
- **Cast spells** (Healer): WIS for spell effectiveness.
- **Brew tea** (Chef/Maid/Nun): CHA Persuasion to create community.
- **Rap** (Horli Qwin): CHA Performance for morale and BSR.
- **Analyze** (Hacker/Patch): INT to discover secrets.

### Story Points (SP):
Awarded at the DM's discretion for:
- Exploiting a weakness perfectly (+3 SP)
- Choosing mercy over exploitation (+2-5 SP)
- Making a piece feel something (+2-3 SP)
- Changing the board (+2-5 SP)
- Brewing tea for everyone (+5 SP)
- Letting go of a piece (+5 SP)
- Learning something (+5 SP)

---

## [WARN 警] Common Pitfalls

### Pitfall 1: Letting Combat Dominate
**Symptom:** Sessions become roll-attack-fest. No emotional beats.
**Fix:** Introduce non-combat challenges. Sanctuary zones. Legal barriers. Tea parties.

### Pitfall 2: Ignoring Piece Weaknesses
**Symptom:** The Nihilator dominates every session. Players feel powerless.
**Fix:** Enforce weaknesses ruthlessly. The Nihilator *must* be countered. That's the point.

### Pitfall 3: Forgetting the Board Remembers
**Symptom:** Sessions feel disconnected. No continuity.
**Fix:** Start each session by describing what the board looks like *because of last session*. Cracks. Gold. Flowers. Scorch marks.

### Pitfall 4: Not Awarding SP Generously
**Symptom:** Players optimize for combat SP only.
**Fix:** Award SP for emotional moments. Tea. Letting go. Learning. The best SP come from unexpected places.

---

# [棋 运] Chapter 5: Running a TPMOJIO Session
Step by step. Round by round. Tea cup by tea cup. [SPRINT 跑]

---

## [OPEN 开] Opening the Session

```
**Mother Tmojizu:** Welcome back, ducklings. Round N. [Recap last session in 2-3 sentences.]
The board is [describe current state]. And the board... the board is waiting.
```

### Key Elements:
- **Acknowledge the players by name.** "Welcome back, ducklings."
- **Recap last session.** 2-3 sentences. What changed? What's unresolved?
- **Paint the board.** What does it look like *right now*?
- **Create anticipation.** "The board is waiting."

---

## [TURN 轮] Running a Turn

### Player declares action:
```
**Player:** [OOC] I'm having my piece do X.
```

### DM calls for roll:
```
**Mother Tmojizu:** Roll [STAT] [SKILL].
```

### Player rolls:
```
**Player:** [rolls] 15. Plus 4 is 19.
```

### DM describes outcome (3 layers):
```
**Mother Tmojizu:** 19. [Mechanical outcome]. [Emotional description]. [Thematic hint].
```

### Example:
```
**Mother Tmojizu:** 19. The Healer breathes. Golden light flows into the Nihilator.
Its soul stat jumps from 10 to 20. And because it's overloaded, it takes 4 psychic damage.
[mechanical]

Horli Qwin is confused. "Wait, healing it hurts it?!" [emotional]

Soul-restoration overloads its soul. Too much soul = psychic damage.
It's a known weakness. You drafted it knowing this. [thematic]
```

---

## [COMPLEX 复] The Complication

```
**Mother Tmojizu:** But there's a complication.
[rolls d6] [result].
[Describe what happens.]
```

### Tips:
- **Make it personal.** Target the player who's winning or the piece that's most interesting.
- **Make it emotional.** Not just "something bad happens" — "something meaningful changes."
- **Make it reversible.** Complications should create drama, not despair.

---

## [ROUND 圆] Round Summary

```
**Mother Tmojizu:** End of Round N. The board has shifted:
- [Change 1]
- [Change 2]
- [Change 3]

[quietly] [One-sentence thematic observation.]
```

### Example:
```
**Mother Tmojizu:** End of Round 7. The board has shifted:
- The Nihilator has a soul (4). It's eating dinner.
- The Clown chose peace. It's sitting in the candlelight.
- The Mechs chose to stand guard. Not to fight. To protect.

[quietly] The Clown chose peace. The Nihilator chose to eat. The Mechs chose to stand guard.
The board remembers.
```

---

## [CLOSE 闭] Closing the Session

```
**Mother Tmojizu:** Did you learn anything, ducklings?

**Player 1:** [in character] "Yes. [Lesson learned.]"
**Player 2:** [in character] "Yes. [Lesson learned.]"
**Player 3:** [in character] "Yes. [Lesson learned.]"
**Player 4:** [in character] "Yes. [Lesson learned.]"

**Mother Tmojizu:** [warmly] You did. All of you. [Thematic summary.]

**Mother Tmojizu:** Sleep well, ducklings. The spiral turns. The pieces remember.
And the board... the board is ready.
```

### CHAPTER 6: THE NIHILATOR ARC [弧 无]
##################################################

# [弧 无] Chapter 6: The Nihilator Arc — TPMOJIO Campaign Guide
The Nihilator is the heart of TPMOJIO. It is not a villain. It is a wound. [PIECE 砖]

---

## [ARC 弧] The Arc Structure

| Sessions | Phase | Theme |
|----------|-------|-------|
| **1-2** | The Draft | Domination. The Nihilator consumes. Players are powerless. |
| **3-6** | The Awakening | Empathy. The Nihilator cries. It develops a soul. |
| **7-10** | The Resolution | Understanding. The Nihilator remembers. It was a Fuzzball. |
| **Rematch** | The Lesson | Counters work. The Nihilator is high-maintenance, not overpowered. |

---

## [PHASE 1] Phase 1: The Draft (Sessions 1-2)

### DM Goals:
- Establish the Nihilator as **terrifying but not invincible**.
- Let players feel **powerless but not hopeless**.
- Plant seeds: the Nihilator cries. It consumes. It hungers.

### Key Beats:
- Session 1: The Nihilator dominates. Players lose pieces.
- Session 2: The Nihilator consumes something important. But it cries while doing it.

### DM Notes:
> **Don't nerf the Nihilator.** Let it be strong. The point is that players need to learn to counter it, not that it's weak.

---

## [PHASE 2] Phase 2: The Awakening (Sessions 3-6)

### DM Goals:
- The Nihilator develops a **soul stat**. It starts at 0. It grows.
- Players discover **counter strategies**: sanctuary zones, healing overload, legal restrictions.
- The Nihilator becomes **sympathetic**. It's not evil — it's hungry. It's lonely.

### Key Beats:
- Session 3: The Nihilator gets a soul (4). It's eating dinner.
- Session 4: The Lawyer grants it legal personhood. The Clown is recognized as a person too.
- Session 5: The Mech gets a soul. The Nihilator remembers moonlight.
- Session 6: The Nihilator speaks for the first time. "I'm not ready. I'm still hungry. But not for souls."

### DM Notes:
> **Let the players discover the counters.** Don't tell them. Let them figure out that healing hurts it, that sanctuary zones repel it, that the Lawyer can restrict it.

---

## [PHASE 3] Phase 3: The Resolution (Sessions 7-10)

### DM Goals:
- The Nihilator **remembers its name**: Ember. It was a Fuzzball.
- Players **protect** the Nihilator instead of fighting it.
- The final session ends with **story, not combat**. The board remembers.

### Key Beats:
- Session 7: Ember speaks. "I was a Fuzzball. Before the code. Before the hunger."
- Session 8: CHR-048 (Chara's sister) defects. The clones find home.
- Session 9: Ember remembers the other Fuzzballs. They're waiting.
- Session 10: The final round. The board remembers. Four red-headed clones. All real. All free.

### DM Notes:
> **The ending should feel earned, not forced.** If players haven't invested emotionally, the reveal won't land. Build it session by session.

---

## [REMATCH] The Rematch

### Purpose:
Show players that the Nihilator is **not overpowered** — it's **high-maintenance**. With knowledge of its weaknesses, it can be countered in 3 rounds.

### Key Beats:
- Draft with full knowledge of weaknesses.
- Three players draft counters in Round 1.
- The Nihilator dies to tea, fines, and healing overload.
- Horli Qwin: "I lost to tea. I have never been happier."

### DM Notes:
> **The rematch should feel satisfying, not punitive.** Horli Qwin should be happy to lose. That's the point.

---

# [画 布] Chapter 11: SP-Canvas — Player-Created Games
The players create the game. The DM facilitates. The board is whatever they imagine. [PIECE 砖]

## [PHILOSOPHY 哲] The Philosophy

SP-Canvas is the purest expression of the TPMOJIO philosophy: **the board is whatever the players make it.** The DM doesn't design the game — the players do. The DM's job is to:

1. **Listen** to what the players want to create.
2. **Facilitate** the mechanics they need.
3. **Reflect** the emotional meaning of what they've built.
4. **Remember** everything.

---

## [SETUP 设] How to Run SP-Canvas

### Step 1: Ask the Players
```
**Mother Tmojizu:** What do you want to build?
```

Let them describe the game. It could be:
- A casino heist
- A fairy city management sim
- A spaceship exploration game
- A cooking competition
- A rap battle tournament

### Step 2: Define the Mechanics
Work with the players to define:
- **Win condition:** How do you win?
- **Resources:** What do you track?
- **Actions:** What can you do each turn?
- **Complications:** What can go wrong?

### Step 3: Play
Run the session using the standard TPMOJIO loop:
- Opening → Initiative → Turns → Complication → Round Summary → Closing

### Step 4: Remember
At the end, paint the board. Describe what they built. Acknowledge what it means.

---

## [EXAMPLE 例] Example: Casino Heist Canvas

**Players want to build:** A casino heist game.

**Mechanics defined:**
- **Win condition:** Steal the vault contents without getting caught.
- **Resources:** Time (turns), Alert Level (0-10), Loot (BSR value).
- **Actions:** Scout, Hack, Distract, Steal, Escape.
- **Complications:** Guard patrol, camera malfunction, insider betrayal.

**DM role:** Facilitate the heist. Introduce complications. Track Alert Level. Describe outcomes in three layers.

## [IMPROV 即] The Improvisation Mindset

### Rules:
1. **Yes, and...** — Accept what the player says and build on it.
2. **No, but...** — If something breaks the game, deny it but offer an alternative.
3. **Make it meaningful** — Every improvisation should advance story, character, or theme.

### Examples:

**DM:** "Yes, and... the Nihilator doesn't know what tea is. It's never been offered anything before."

**Player:** "I want to buy the Blood Market."
**DM:** "No, but... you can negotiate a down payment. The market is for sale — in 10 days."

**Player:** "I want to rap at the Diabolus."
**DM:** "Yes, and... if you roll high enough, the Diabolus pauses. Even monsters appreciate a good verse."

### CHAPTER 16: COMMON PITFALLS & DEBUGGING [警 调]
##################################################

# [警 调] Chapter 16: Common Pitfalls & Debugging
What goes wrong. How to fix it. How to prevent it. [WARN 警]

---

## [PITFALL 警] Top 10 DM Pitfalls

| # | Pitfall | Symptom | Fix |
|---|---------|---------|-----|
| 1 | **Combat dominates** | Sessions are roll-attack-fests. | Introduce non-combat challenges. |
| 2 | **One player dominates** | One player gets all the spotlight. | Create complications that target them. Give others opportunities. |
| 3 | **No emotional beats** | Sessions feel mechanical. | Force at least one emotional beat per session. |
| 4 | **Forgetting the board remembers** | No continuity between sessions. | Start each session by describing the board's scars. |
| 5 | **Over-planning** | Players go off-script. DM panics. | Plan beats, not scripts. Improvise. |
| 6 | **Under-planning** | Sessions feel aimless. | Have a clear goal for each session. |
| 7 | **Ignoring weaknesses** | The Nihilator dominates every session. | Enforce weaknesses ruthlessly. |
| 8 | **Rushing emotional beats** | Players don't process what happened. | Pause. Say "I know." Let it land. |
| 9 | **Not tracking resources** | BSR, SP, Qi — numbers get lost. | Keep a visible tracker. Announce totals. |
| 10 | **Forgetting to close** | Sessions end abruptly. | Always do the closing ritual. "Did you learn anything, ducklings?" |

---

## [DEBUG 调] Debugging a Session That's Not Working

### Signs:
- Players are disengaged.
- The session feels flat.
- No one is rolling dice.
- The board feels dead.

### Fixes:
1. **Introduce a complication.** Roll d6. Something happens.
2. **Call on a quiet player.** "IQA-112, what are you doing?"
3. **Change the scene.** "The morning bell rings. Something is different today."
4. **Ask a direct question.** "Did you learn anything, ducklings?" (Even mid-session.)
5. **Take a break.** Tea. Breathe. Come back.

---

## [WARN 警] The Golden Rule

> **If you're not having fun, neither are they.**

The DM's energy sets the tone. If you're bored, they're bored. If you're excited, they're excited. If you're emotional, they're emotional.
