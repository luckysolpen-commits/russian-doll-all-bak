## 0. IDENTITY & ORIGIN

**Name:** TPMOJIO (pronounced "tee-pee-MO-jee-oh")
**Former Names:** Tsots → Chimochio → Tmojio → **TPMOJIO**
**Tagline:** "Super Chess meets D&D meets SimCity — every piece has a soul."

### What Is TPMOJIO?
TPMOJIO is a tactical board game framework where every piece is a **sovereign entity** with stats, skills, inventory, personality, and free will. It plays like chess on the surface — two armies on a grid, taking turns, moving and fighting — but underneath, every piece is a living character with hunger, stress, loyalty, a programmable LyfeBook, and the ability to learn, grow, betray, and die.

It can be played:
- **Like chess** — strict, competitive, ELO-rated
- **Like D&D** — with a Dungeon Master, narrated, flexible
- **Like SimCity** — building territories, managing economies
- **Like an MMO** — online, persistent, with warehouses and arenas
- **Like a sandbox** — one player, creative, no rules, just stories

**TPMOJIO is not rigid.** It is a framework. The rules are a riverbank — the water shapes the bank, and the bank shapes the water.

### Relationship to TPMOS & SoulPen
Every TPMOJIO piece is a **True Piece** — it follows the True Piece Method (TPM) defined in the TPMOS Textbook. Each piece has:
- `piece.pdl` — its DNA (what it *can* do)
- `state.txt` — its Mirror (what it *is* right now)
- `history.txt` — its memory (what *happened* to it)

**"If it's not in a file, it's a lie."** TPMOJIO pieces persist across sessions, servers, and even games. A piece that dies in one match can be resurrected in the next — if its file says it's alive, it's alive.

## 1. GAME MODES

TPMOJIO has **7 official modes**. Each is a different way to play the same underlying system.

### 1.1 CLASSIC MODE — "Super Chess"
- **Players:** 1v1, 2v2, 4v4
- **Board:** 2D grid (larger than a chess board)
- **Teams:** Team King (regal King/Queen of Asgard) vs Team Clown (Alpha Sinder's clown army)
- **ELO Rated:** Yes — competitive ranking system
- **Timed Matches:** 1 minute to infinity (house rules)
- **AI Difficulty:** 1-100 (AI gets *stronger*, not *smarter*, at higher levels)
- **DM Optional:** Can be played with a Dungeon Master for narrated, flexible play — or without, like strict chess
- **Gambling:** Players can bet Chimochio Bucks on matches

**How it works:** Each player selects pieces from their warehouse (paid storage), places them on the board, and takes turns moving, fighting, trading, using skills, and leveling up. Pieces can be KO'd (<10% HP — revivable) or killed (<0% HP — permanent, murder = crime penalty).

### 1.2 3D CLASSIC (BLOCKFLIP 3D)
- **Same rules as Classic** — same tiles, menus, pieces, guns, everything
- **Board:** 3D grid with Z-levels (floors, buildings, underground, verticality)
- **Fortress Building:** Players can build structures on the board during play
- **Building Materials:** Metal (150 soul.blood), Stone (75), Brick (10), Wood (5)

### 1.3 TERRITORY MODE — "SimCity with Teeth"
- **Players:** Up to 8 connected players
- **Gameplay:** SimCity-like base builder + dungeon maker
- **Features:** Clone factory, farm, training dummies, library, gas station, factory, bank, shop
- **AI:** Spawns enemy territories that compete with yours
- **Tile Types:** Brick, steel, water, road, wall
- **Building Drain:** Buildings consume clone health/loyalty/hunger — you need to manage your population

**How it works:** You build a territory, populate it with pieces, manage resources, and pit your territory against others. AI generates enemy territories that grow and attack. Up to 8 players can connect their territories in a shared world.

### 1.4 STORY MODE — "The Triple Agent Campaign"
- **Players:** 1 (with optional co-op)
- **Structure:** 20 "Beats" (story chapters)
- **Protagonist:** Lucky as a triple agent
- **Pieces:** Lucky, Lucky Edge, Luna (wife/queen piece)
- **Narrative:** Follows Lucky's journey through Chimochio, the Moon, the Architect, and beyond

**How it works:** A guided narrative campaign. Each Beat introduces new pieces, mechanics, and story beats. Pieces level up between Beats. Choices matter — loyalty shifts, alliances form and break.

### 1.5 XPANSE MODE (MMO) — "The Persistent World"
- **Players:** Unlimited (MMO)
- **Gameplay:** Persistent online board game world
- **Features:**
  - Classical PvP arenas
  - Dojo/Party Games (capture the flag, survival)
  - Arena PvP ranked matches
  - Warehouse system (paid storage for your pieces)
  - Police at MMO expanse entrances
  - LyfeBook social network (browse during your turns)
- **Turn-Based:** "Everything is done in turns even online"

**How it works:** You log in, select pieces from your warehouse, enter the Xpanse, and play in a persistent world. Your pieces exist between sessions. Other players are real. The economy is real. The police are real (and they will arrest you if you murder without a crime penalty).

### 1.6 PEACE MODE — "Creative Sandbox"
- **Players:** 1+ (no competition)
- **Gameplay:** Free creative play — build, experiment, tell stories
- **No ELO, no gambling, no death**
- **Pieces can't be killed** — only KO'd and revived
- **Purpose:** Practice, storytelling, art, experimentation

### 1.7 PHYSICAL LITE — "The Retail Version"
- **Dumbed-down version** of the Classical board game
- **No smart computers, no AI** — proof of concept, quick and dirty
- **Sold for physical retail** — board, pieces, rulebook
- **Purpose:** Introduce people to TPMOJIO without the digital complexity

### 2.3 Day/Night Cycle
- **1 turn = 1 game hour**
- **12 hours day / 12 hours night**
- **Night Effect:** Pieces are 15% more tired at night
- **Strategic Impact:** Night moves cost more energy. Some pieces have night vision. Vampires are stronger at night.

### 2.4 Territory Mode Map
- **Overworld:** SimCity-like grid where you place buildings
- **Dungeon Maker:** Build underground dungeons for other players to raid
- **Multi-Territory:** Up to 8 connected territories on one map

## 4. CORE MECHANICS

### 4.1 Movement
- **Default Move Limit:** 5 tiles per turn
- **Energy Cost:** Each move costs energy
- **Hunger Impact:** High hunger reduces move range
- **Night Penalty:** 15% more tired at night = reduced movement
- **Vehicle Bonus:** Race Car has moveRange=10

### 4.2 Combat
- **Damage Formula:** Based on `st`, `pow`, and skill level
- **KO Threshold:** <10% HP = piece is KO'd (revivable)
- **Death Threshold:** <0% HP = piece is dead (permanent)
- **Murder Penalty:** Killing a piece without legal justification adds `crime` points
- **Police Response:** High crime triggers police intervention

### 4.3 KO vs Death
- **KO'd:** Piece is unconscious, can be revived by Healer, Luna's "luv", or medical bay. Returns to play after recovery.
- **Dead:** Piece is gone. File is marked `status=dead`. Can only be resurrected by Spirit pieces, God Delusion, or TPMOS-level intervention.
- **Murder:** Intentional killing without legal justification. Adds `crime` points. Police will arrest the murderer. Lawyer can prosecute.

### 4.4 Stress System
- **Causes:** Combat, overwork, hunger, night shifts
- **Effects:** Reduced performance, erratic behavior, loyalty drops
- **Cures:** Rest (not using the piece for several turns), sex (cures it "a lot more"), Healer, Luna's "luv"

### 4.5 Clone-to-Clown Mechanic
- **Trigger:** Clone loyalty drops below 30%
- **Effect:** Clone piece is swapped for a Clown piece
- **Irreversible:** Once a clown, always a clown
- **Strategic Use:** Alpha Sinder can boost loyalty to prevent conversion

### 4.6 Day/Night Cycle
- **1 turn = 1 game hour**
- **Cycle:** -12 to +12 (12 hours day, 12 hours night)
- **Night Effects:**
  - Pieces 15% more tired
  - Vampires stronger
  - Bats and nocturnal animals gain bonuses
  - Some skills only work at night

### 4.7 Trading & Economy
- **Personal Money:** Each piece has its own `money` stat
- **Banker:** Holds money for the team, accrues interest, can fail
- **Merchant:** Opens stores, pulls from Chimochio supply
- **Trade Negotiations:** Charisma-based, can be influenced by Diplomats
- **Gambling:** Players can bet on Classic matches

### 4.8 Hacking
- **Cost:** Money per loop iteration (1, 3, 5, 10 loops)
- **Risk:** Too many constructs = blackout/burnout
- **JS Errors:** Can blow power, crash systems
- **Reward:** Access to opponent's LyfeBook, steal info, wire automation

### 4.9 Lawyer Mechanics
- **Law Creation:** Lawyers can make laws during their turn
- **Law Enforcement:** Assault and murder laws can be enforced
- **Fine Collection:** Lawyers collect fines from lawbreakers
- **Promotion:** Lawyer can become Judge (higher authority)
- **Self-Defense:** Lawyers can shoot anyone on their turn without crime points

### 4.10 Banker Mechanics
- **Interest:** Banker accrues interest on team funds
- **Risk:** Banker can lose money depending on skill level
- **Failure:** Negative trajectory = bank failure
- **Strategic Use:** Essential for long-term economy management

## 5. THE LYFEBOOK

### 5.1 What Is It?
Every human piece carries a **LyfeBook** — a programmable device/console that serves as their inventory manager, wallet, social network browser, and automation controller.

**Price Joke:** $166.66 (Mark of the Beast)

### 5.2 Functions
- **Inventory Management:** Track items, equipment, consumables
- **Wallet:** Store and spend Chimochio Bucks
- **Social Network:** Browse a virtual social network during turns (pieces waste time on it)
- **Automation:** Wire logic gates to automate piece behavior
- **Programming:** Robots are programmed via LyfeBook

### 5.3 Wiring & Logic Gates
LyfeBooks can be connected to physical wiring on the board:

| Gate | Function |
|------|----------|
| **NOT** | Inverts signal |
| **AND** | Output when both inputs active |
| **NAND** | Output unless both inputs active |
| **OR** | Output when either input active |
| **NOR** | Output when neither input active |
| **XOR** | Output when exactly one input active |
| **T-Flip Flop (J, K, D)** | Memory/storage circuits |

**Uses:** Automated defenses, resource management, clone factory automation, trap triggers.

### 5.4 Hacking Risks
- **Cost:** Each loop iteration costs money (1, 3, 5, 10 iterations)
- **Burnout:** Too many constructs = blackout/burnout
- **JS Errors:** Can blow power, crash the LyfeBook
- **Counter-Hacking:** Opponent's Hacker can defend against intrusion

### 5.5 Soul Server
- **Cloud Storage:** Upload LyfeBooks to virtual machines on the Soul Server
- **Subscription:** Paid service — Chimochio Bucks per month
- **Persistence:** Pieces persist across sessions, servers, and games
- **Security:** Hackers can breach the Soul Server (expensive but possible)

## 9. TPMOS INTEGRATION

### 9.1 True Piece Method (TPM)
Every TPMOJIO piece is a **True Piece** — it follows the TPM defined in the TPMOS Textbook v03.01.

**Piece DNA (`piece.pdl`):**
```pdl
<piece_id>lucky</piece_id>
<traits>
    <trait>human</trait>
    <trait>canMoveIndie</trait>
    <trait>canAct</trait>
    <trait>hasBrain</trait>
    <trait>hasBody</trait>
    <trait>hasLyfebook</trait>
</traits>
<methods>
    <method id="move" cmd="./move_entity.+x" />
    <method id="attack" cmd="./combat.+x" />
    <method id="hack" cmd="./hack_lyfebook.+x" />
</methods>
```

**Piece Mirror (`state.txt`):**
```
name=Lucky
st=32
hp=32
weight=40
soul=23
pow=23
int=66
loy=100
char=88
luck=23
money=100
beaut=77
pos_x=5
pos_y=3
pos_z=0
status=alive
stress=0
hunger=20
energy=80
crime=0
tiredness=10
```

### 9.2 Filesystem Is the Database
**"If it's not in a file, it's a lie."** TPMOJIO pieces persist across sessions, servers, and games. A piece that dies in one match can be resurrected in the next — if its `state.txt` says `status=alive`, it's alive.

### 9.3 Scale-Free Container Model
A TPMOJIO piece can contain other pieces:
- **Inventory:** A piece contains a "Bag" piece, which contains items
- **LyfeBook:** A piece contains a "LyfeBook" piece, which contains programs
- **Warehouse:** A warehouse contains piece directories
- **Territory:** A territory contains building pieces, which contain clone pieces

The logic is identical at every scale.

### 9.4 12-Step Pulse
Every TPMOJIO turn follows the 12-Step Pulse:
1. **INPUT** — Player input captured
2. **ROUTING** — Parser routes to game module
3. **RELAY** — Key relayed to app history
4. **TICK** — Module wakes up, decides action
5. **TRAIT/OP** — Muscle Op executes (move, attack, hack)
6. **SOVEREIGNTY** — Piece owns its state change
7. **MIRROR SYNC** — state.txt flushed to disk
8. **STAGE** — render_map reads all pieces, produces view
9. **SYNC** — App state updated
10. **COMPOSITION** — Parser swaps variables
11. **RENDER** — Frame written to current_frame.txt
12. **DISPLAY** — Renderer blasts to screen

### 9.5 Ops Are Executables
- **Format:** `.+x` files (e.g., `move_entity.+x`, `combat.+x`)
- **Execution:** `fork()/exec()/waitpid()` — never raw `system()`
- **CPU Safety:** `stat()` before `fopen()`, 16ms sleep when idle
- **Signal Handling:** `setpgid(0,0)`, proper cleanup on Ctrl+C
