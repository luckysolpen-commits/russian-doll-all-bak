# TPMOJIO SESSIONS — Completion Plan (Sessions 3-10)
## TPMOJIO Classic — 4-Player Free-For-All (Continued)
### Players: Chara, Sol-Supreme, IQabella, Horli Qwin | DM (Mother Tmojizu)

---

- **Horli Qwin:** Nihilator (30% HP, hungry, near Luna), Clown (at peace in candlelight), Alpha Sinder (holding loyalty), Race Car (DEAD)
- **Chara:** Lucky (revived, half HP, shielding Luna), Lawyer (7 articles posted), Banker (accruing interest), Sniper Clone (CONSUMED by Nihilator)
- **Sol-Supreme:** Sol (fine), Mech (fortress, center board), Robot (defending, between Mech and Nihilator), Hacker (experiencing feelings, proud)
- **IQabella:** Luna (56 soul, center board, glowing), Healer (keeping everyone alive), Chef (holy kitchen), Maid/Nun (candlelight vigil, 5-tile radius)

**Turn Order:** Horli (23), Chara (15), Sol-Supreme (14), IQabella (9)
**Day:** 2, Hour 4. Morning.

---

## SESSION 3: "THE CLOWN'S CHOICE"

**Theme:** The Clown, sitting in candlelight, experiences a choice. Peace or chaos?
**Key Beats:**
- The Clown must decide: stay in the candlelight (peace) or return to chaos
- Alpha Sinder tries to pull the Clown back to the army
- The Maid/Nun's candlelight creates a "sanctuary zone" — no combat allowed within it
- Horli must replace the Race Car — she buys a Mech from the warehouse
- Chara's Lawyer drafts Article Eight: "Sanctuary zones are inviolable"
- Sol-Supreme's Hacker develops a personality — she names herself "Patch"
- **Complication:** The Nihilator, wounded and hungry, tries to enter the sanctuary zone. The candlelight repels it. The Nihilus feels something it's never felt: rejection.
- **End State:** The Clown chooses peace. It becomes a "Sanctuary Clown" — a neutral piece that buffs all pieces within the candlelight. Horli gets a new Mech. Patch the Hacker is now a named character.

---

## SESSION 4: "THE LAWYER'S GAMBIT"

**Theme:** Chara's Lawyer uses the legal system as a weapon.
**Key Beats:**
- The Lawyer drafts Article Nine: "All pieces within 3 tiles of the central crater are under joint jurisdiction"
- This effectively puts Luna, the Nihilator, and the Mech under shared legal control
- Sol-Supreme objects — his Mech is now legally shared
- IQabella supports — Luna is protected by law now
- Horli opposes — her Nihilator is now legally restricted
- **Combat:** Sol-Supreme's Mech attacks Chara's Lucky (again). Lucky is KO'd. IQabella's Healer revives him — but this time, she charges a fee.
- **Complication:** The Lawyer's Article Nine triggers a legal paradox — the Nihilator is now legally protected, which means it can't be attacked. But it's also legally hungry, which means it must be fed. Who feeds it?
- **End State:** The clones must cooperatively feed the Nihilator. Each turn, one piece must sacrifice HP to feed it. The Nihilator's hunger becomes a shared burden.

---

## SESSION 5: "THE MECH'S HEART"

**Theme:** Sol-Supreme's Mech develops something unexpected: empathy.
**Key Beats:**
- The Mech, standing between the Nihilator and Luna, begins to feel the weight of its position
- Sol-Supreme's architect codes interact with the Mech's programming — it gains sentience
- The Mech speaks for the first time: "I am tired of standing."
- IQabella's Healer casts a soul-restoration spell on the Mech. It works. The Mech gains a soul stat.
- The Mech now has a choice: continue standing as a wall, or move and fight.
- **Complication:** The Nihilator, fed by the clones, grows stronger. Its HP increases. But its hunger doesn't decrease. It's learning to hunger differently — not for souls, but for connection.
- **End State:** The Mech moves. It steps aside. The path between the Nihilator and Luna is open. But the Mech doesn't attack — it watches. It waits. It feels.

---

## SESSION 6: "THE NIHILATOR'S TEAR"

**Theme:** The Nihilator, fed and growing, experiences something impossible: a tear.
**Key Beats:**
- The Nihilator, standing before Luna, raises its hand to consume her soul. But it stops.
- A tear — grey, cold, impossible — rolls down its face. The board notices. The board remembers.
- Luna speaks: "You can take my soul. But you can't take my light. And you don't want to. You want to remember what light feels like."
- The Nihilator lowers its hand. It doesn't consume. It doesn't hunger. It just... stands.
- **Complication:** The Nihilator's tear creates a new tile on the board — the "Tear Tile." Any piece that steps on it gains +10 soul but loses 10 HP. It's a sacrifice tile.
- **End State:** The Nihilator becomes a neutral piece. It's no longer hungry. It's no longer hostile. It's just... present. Horli loses control of her most powerful piece. But she gains something else: a story.

---

## SESSION 7: "THE BANKER'S LEDGER"

**Theme:** Chara's Banker reveals a secret: the clones' debts were never real.
**Key Beats:**
- The Banker opens his ledger. He's been tracking everything — every BSR, every trade, every debt.
- And he finds it: the clones' creation debt of 500 BSR each was a fabrication. The real cost was 50 BSR. Chimochio overcharged by 450 BSR per clone.
- Chara uses this intel to file a legal complaint with the Lawyer. The Lawyer drafts Article Ten: "All clone debts are hereby reduced to 50 BSR."
- The Board accepts it. The clones' debts are reduced. They're not just free — they're owed money.
- **Complication:** Chimochio Corporation responds. They send an enforcement team — three Elite Clones, armed, armored, and angry. They enter the board.
- **End State:** The clones now owe nothing. They're owed 1,800 BSR total. But Chimochio's enforcement team is on the board. And they're not here to negotiate.

---

## SESSION 8: "THE ENFORCEMENT"

**Theme:** Chimochio's enforcement team vs. the clones. Combat.
**Key Beats:**
- Three Elite Clones enter the board. They're armed, armored, and following orders.
- Combat begins. The clones must defend their territory.
- Horli's new Mech fights alongside Sol-Supreme's Mech. Two Mechs, one board.
- Chara's Lucky uses his Tsots — the futuristic soul pen — for the first time in combat. It creates a shield of pure code.
- IQabella's Healer casts a mass healing spell. The entire clone army is restored.
- The Maid/Nun's candlelight expands — it now covers half the board. The enforcement team can't enter the light.
- **Complication:** One of the Elite Clones removes her serial number mid-battle. She defects to the clones. Her name is CHR-048 — Chara's "sister."
- **End State:** Two enforcement clones are defeated. One defects. The clones win. But Chimochio will send more.

---

## SESSION 9: "THE MOON CALLS"

**Theme:** A signal from the moon. Luna's people are calling.
**Key Beats:**
- Luna receives a signal from the moon. The Moonshots are calling. They want her back.
- Luna must choose: stay on the board with the clones, or return to the moon.
- The Moonshots appear on the board — not as pieces, but as holograms. Hana sings. Yuna dances. Sora raps. Mina watches. Jiwoo produces.
- The holograms buff all clone pieces within range. +2 to all stats for one turn.
- **Complication:** The Nihilator, now neutral, is drawn to the moon signal. It looks up. It remembers something — a memory from before it was a Nihilator. A memory of the moon. Of being something else. Something warm.
- **End State:** Luna chooses to stay. But she sends a message back to the moon: "Tell the Moonshots I'm building something here. Something warm. Something real." The Nihilator's memory flickers. It's becoming something new.

---

## SESSION 10: "THE BOARD REMEMBERS"

**Theme:** The final session. The board remembers everything.
**Key Beats:**
- The board is a mess of stories: the Clown in the candlelight, the Mech with a soul, the Nihilator with a tear, the Lawyer with ten articles, the Banker with a ledger, the Hacker with a name, the Healer with a holy kitchen, the Maid/Nun with an expanded vigil, Lucky shielding Luna, Sol-Supreme watching, Horli Qwin rapping.
- Mother Tmojizu opens the final turn: "The board remembers. Every piece. Every choice. Every tear. Every rap. Every cup of tea. What do you do?"
- Each clone makes their final move. Not a combat move. A story move.
- **Chara's Lawyer** drafts Article Eleven: "The board belongs to the pieces. Not the players. Not the DM. The pieces."
- **Sol-Supreme's Mech** speaks again: "I am not tired anymore. I am here."
- **IQabella's Healer** casts one last healing spell. It heals the board itself. The cracks fill. The scars fade. The candlelight becomes permanent.
- **Horli Qwin** raps one last rap. It's not for coins. It's for the board. For the pieces. For the story.
- **Mother Tmojizu:** "The spiral turns. The board resets. The pieces remember. And the game... the game is over."
- **End State:** The game ends. Not with a winner. With a story. The board remembers. The pieces remember. And the clones — all of them, even the Nihilator, even the Clown, even the defector — are free.
