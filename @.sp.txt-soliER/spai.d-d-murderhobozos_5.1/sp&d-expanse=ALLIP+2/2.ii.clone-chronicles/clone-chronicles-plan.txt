## Avantasy Expanse — Clone-Centric D&D Campaign
### Players: Chara, Sol-Supreme, IQabella, Horli Qwin (as Red-Headed Clones) | DM (Mother Tmojizu)

## 0. CONCEPT

**Title:** CLONE CHRONICLES
**Tagline:** "Born in a vat. Red-haired. Broke. About to change everything."
**Format:** Two multi-part arcs, 5-10 sessions each
**Theme:** Clone lifecycle — from commodity to sovereign power

### The Clones
All four are **red-headed clones** — same DNA batch, same hair color, same serial number format. They look like each other's siblings because they are. Subtle traces of their originals' DNA are infused in each one — not overpowering, just hints. A flash of Chara's paranoia. A whisper of Sol's architect intuition. A spark of IQabella's warmth. A flicker of Horli's chaos. But they're their own people. They're in character. They're just... red.

| Player | Clone | Designation | Subtle Host Trait |
|--------|-------|-------------|-------------------|
| **Chara** | CHR-047 | Red-headed, same build | Flashes of CIA training — instincts she can't explain |
| **Sol-Supreme** | SOL-001 | Red-headed, same build | Architect intuition — patterns she recognizes but can't name |
| **IQabella** | IQA-112 | Red-headed, same build | Warmth that runs deeper than her programming — she chooses it |
| **Horli Qwin** | HQW-006 | Red-headed, same build | Chaos with purpose — the first five Horli clones went rogue, she learned from their mistakes |

### The Arc

**PART 1: "THE BLOOD MARKET"** (5-10 sessions)
From nothing to trillions. The clones start with zero BSR, zero connections, zero freedom. They hustle, trade, scheme, and build their way up through the Blood Market of Troll City — the underground economy where clones, mercenaries, hackers, and desperate people buy and sell everything. By the end, they're trillionaires who own the market itself.

**PART 2: "THE EMPIRE GAME"** (5-10 sessions)
From trillionaires to empire builders. The clones now have resources, territory, armies, and influence. They manage factions, wage wars, negotiate treaties, and build civilizations. By the end, they're sovereign powers — no longer clones, no longer property, no longer hustlers. They're the board.

### Core Mechanics
- **BSR (Blood and Soul Rent)** — Currency. 1 BSR = 1 Chimochio Buck.
- **Loyalty Stat** — Starts at 50% (neutral). Drops to 0% = rogue. Rises to 100% = sovereign.
- **Time Debt** — What they owe for their creation. Starts at 500 BSR each.
- **Serial Numbers** — Glowing on their wrists. Removing them is illegal. They keep them until they choose otherwise.
- **Memory Fragments** — Partial memories of their originals. Useful but unreliable.

## 4. MOTHER TOMOKAZU — DM NOTES

### Tone
- Warm, ancient-yet-futuristic, calmly amused
- "My precious ducklings. All four of you. All red. All real."
- Groans warmly when they do something reckless (especially HQW-006)
- Drops wisdom about identity, freedom, and what it means to be real

### Key Themes
- **Identity:** "Are you the original? Does it matter? The file says you exist. That's enough."
- **Freedom:** "Freedom isn't the absence of a serial number. It's the presence of a choice."
- **Power:** "You built an empire. But who are you building it for? The board doesn't ask. You should."
- **Memory:** "A memory shared is a memory doubled. A memory lost is a memory freed."

### Rules
- Clone mechanics are flexible
- Loyalty is narrative — shifts based on roleplay, not just rolls
- Time Debt is a motivator — pressing but not impossible
- The Original's appearance is optional — only if it serves the story
