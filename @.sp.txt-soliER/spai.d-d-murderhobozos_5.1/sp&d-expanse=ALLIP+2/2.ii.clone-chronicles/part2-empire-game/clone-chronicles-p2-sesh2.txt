# CLONE CHRONICLES — PART 2, SESSION 2: "THE FIRST CLAIM"
## Part 2: The Empire Game — Session 2 of 10
### Players: CHR-047 (Chara), SOL-001 (Sol-Supreme), IQA-112 (IQabella), HQW-006 (Horli Qwin) | DM (Mother Tmojizu)

---

**Mother Tmojizu:** Welcome back, ducklings. Part 2, Session 2. Turn 2. You've claimed your territories. You've built your foundations. And today, you expand.

**HQW-006:** [OOC] T-Mo, I'm expanding into SOL-001's territory.

**Mother Tmojizu:** [groans warmly] I knew you were going to say that.

**Mother Tmojizu:** The morning bell rings. The Empire Board wakes up. Four factions. Four territories. And today, the first trade deals are ready.

Each clone makes their Turn 2 business decisions:

**CHR-047:** [OOC] I'm hiring 3 more units (HR), spending 150 BSR on R&D for advanced shield technology, spending 100 BSR on Marketing, building 2 IronCrabClaw tanks (Manufacturing), and paying back 100 BSR of my loan (Finance).

**Mother Tmojizu:** Noted. Your Crustacean Bastion grows. Shield Walls thicken. IronCrabClaw tanks multiply. Your territory is an impenetrable fortress.

**SOL-001:** [OOC] Tomo, I'm hiring 2 more units (HR), spending 200 BSR on R&D for unicorn lancer technology, spending 100 BSR on Marketing, building 2 unicorn lancers (Manufacturing), and paying back 100 BSR of my share sale (Finance).

**Mother Tmojizu:** Noted. Your Aether Wings grow. Unicorn lancers patrol the borders. Owl scouts expand their range. Your territory sees everything — including HQW-006's expansion.

**IQA-112:** [OOC] Mom, I'm hiring 3 more units (HR), spending 100 BSR on R&D for centaur medical technology, spending 200 BSR on Marketing (warmth advertising — it's working), building 2 scout units (Manufacturing), and paying back 50 BSR of my loan (Finance).

**Mother Tmojizu:** [warmly] Noted. Your Thunderhoof Legion grows. Centaur cavalry gallop. Scouts map the Wild. Your territory is fast, warm, and expanding.

**HQW-006:** [OOC] T-Mo, I'm hiring 10 more units (HR), spending 0 BSR on R&D, spending 0 BSR on Marketing, building 10 Rat Swarms (Manufacturing), and borrowing 500 more BSR (Finance). I'm going ALL-IN on numbers.

**Mother Tmojizu:** [groans] I knew you were going to say that. Your Primate Swarm doubles in size. WarApeFist bruisers pound the ground. Rat Swarms fill every crack. Your territory is loud, aggressive, and everywhere. And you're expanding into SOL-001's territory.

**SOL-001:** [in character] "HQW-006, your Rat Swarms are in my territory. Pull them back."

**HQW-006:** [in character] "Rats go where they want, bestie."

**Mother Tmojizu:** Roll CHA Persuasion to de-escalate.

**SOL-001:** [rolls] 10. Plus 1 is 11.

**Mother Tmojizu:** 11. It's not enough. The Rat Swarms remain. The border tension escalates. But it doesn't become war — not yet.

**IQA-112:** [OOC] Mom, I'm mediating again. I'm riding my centaur cavalry to the border. I'm offering tea to both sides. And I'm proposing a trade deal: SOL-001 gets access to my Wild resource nodes, and HQW-006 pulls the Rat Swarms back. CHA Persuasion.

**Mother Tmojizu:** Roll it.

**IQA-112:** [rolls] 17. Plus 4 is 21.

**Mother Tmojizu:** 21. IQA-112 rides her centaur cavalry to the border. She sets up a tea stand. She offers tea to the Rat Swarms and the owl scouts. They drink. They pause. They look at each other. And then — a trade deal.

**IQA-112:** [in character] "SOL-001, you get access to my Wild resource nodes. HQW-006, you pull the Rat Swarms back. Everyone wins."

**SOL-001:** [in character] "Fine. But I get 60% of the resource node yields."

**HQW-006:** [in character] "Fine. But I get 40% of the owl scout intel."

**Mother Tmojizu:** The trade deal is struck. SOL-001 gets 60% of IQA-112's Wild resource nodes. HQW-006 gets 40% of SOL-001's owl scout intel. And the Rat Swarms pull back.

**Mother Tmojizu:** But there's a complication.

**Mother Tmojizu rolls d6 behind the screen.**

**Mother Tmojizu:** [rolls] 3.

**Mother Tmojizu:** Resource shortage. Manufacturing costs double for one turn.

**Mother Tmojizu:** A resource shortage hits the Empire Board. Manufacturing costs double for this turn. All clones must pay double for unit production.

**CHR-047:** [OOC] Ma'am, I was planning to build 2 IronCrabClaw tanks. That's now double the cost.

**Mother Tmojizu:** It is. You can build them at double cost, or delay production.

**CHR-047:** [in character] "I'll delay. Shields first. Tanks later."

**SOL-001:** [OOC] Tomo, same. I'll delay the unicorn lancers.

**IQA-112:** [OOC] Mom, I'll delay the scouts. Tea first. Empire second.

**HQW-006:** [OOC] T-Mo, I'm building the Rat Swarms anyway. Double cost. I don't care. Numbers win wars.

**Mother Tmojizu:** [chuckles] Of course you are.

**Mother Tmojizu:** End of Turn 2. Let's count resources:

- **CHR-047 (Crustacean Bastion):** 600 - 150 (R&D) - 100 (Marketing) - 100 (loan payback) = **250 BSR** + 18 units + Shield Walls
- **SOL-001 (Aether Wings):** 600 - 200 (R&D) - 100 (Marketing) - 100 (share payback) = **200 BSR** + 15 units + Owl Flyers + 60% Wild resource nodes
- **IQA-112 (Thunderhoof Legion):** 400 - 100 (R&D) - 200 (Marketing) - 50 (loan payback) = **50 BSR** + 18 units + Centaur Cavalry + trade deal with SOL-001
- **HQW-006 (Primate Swarm):** 900 - 0 (R&D) - 0 (Marketing) - 1,000 (double-cost manufacturing) + 500 (borrowed) = **400 BSR** + 30 units + WarApeFist Bruisers + 40% owl scout intel

**Mother Tmojizu:** [quietly] The first claim is made. Trade deals struck. Border tensions mediated. Resource shortages weathered. And the empire grows.

**CHR-047:** [in character] "Tomorrow, we expand. Tomorrow, we build. Tomorrow, we win."

**SOL-001:** [in character] "Tomorrow, I open a door I don't understand. Again. But this time, it's a resource node door."

**IQA-112:** [in character] "Tomorrow, I brew more tea. And I keep the peace."

**HQW-006:** [in character] "Tomorrow, I rap for more coins. And definitely steal territory."

**CHR-047:** [in character] "No."

**HQW-006:** [in character] "Maybe."

**Mother Tmojizu:** [smiles] Sleep, ducklings. The empire will be here in the morning. The spiral turns. The pieces remember.
