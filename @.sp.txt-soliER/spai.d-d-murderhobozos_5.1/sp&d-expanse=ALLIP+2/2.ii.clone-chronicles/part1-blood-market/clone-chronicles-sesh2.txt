# CLONE CHRONICLES — SESSION 2: "THE MEMORY TRADE"
## Part 1: The Blood Market — Session 2 of 10
### Players: CHR-047 (Chara), SOL-001 (Sol-Supreme), IQA-112 (IQabella), HQW-006 (Horli Qwin) | DM (Mother Tmojizu)

---

**Mother Tmojizu:** Welcome back, ducklings. The morning bell rings again. Day 2. You earned 175 BSR yesterday. You need 1,825 more. The market is waking up. The brokers are calling prices. And today, something new opens — the Memory Bar.

**HQW-006:** [OOC] T-Mo, I'm already rapping in my head.

**Mother Tmojizu:** [chuckles] I know. But first — the room. The cramped room. The four beds. The locked door. The serial numbers. The tea set.

**IQA-112:** [in character] I'm brewing tea before anyone else wakes up. It's just hot water and another tea bag I found — this one smells like mint — but it's warm. And I'm placing a cup by each bed.

**Mother Tmojizu:** [warmly] The tea is warm. The room is small. The clones wake up one by one.

**SOL-001:** [in character] I'm sitting up. I'm looking at my serial number. SOL-001. Still glowing. Still mine. I'm drinking the tea. "Thank you, IQA-112."

**CHR-047:** [in character] I'm already awake. I've been awake for an hour. I was listening to the market through the wall. Counting footsteps. Identifying patrol patterns. Officer Krell walks the corner every 12 minutes. The broker stalls open at 6 AM. The Memory Bar — I heard someone mention it — opens at 8.

**HQW-006:** [in character] I'm stretching. Full-body stretch. Arms up, back arched, tail flicking. I'm drinking the tea. I'm saying: "Day 2. I'm gonna rap harder. And maybe steal something."

**CHR-047:** [in character] "No."

**HQW-006:** [in character] "Maybe."

**Mother Tmojizu:** [groans warmly] I knew you were going to say that.

**Mother Tmojizu:** SOL-001 opens the door with his architect codes — the same flicker, the same click, the same "that shouldn't have worked" energy. The hallway is the same. The stairs are the same. The market is louder.

You descend into the Blood Market.

It's bigger today. More stalls. More clones. More noise. And at the end of the street — a building you didn't notice yesterday. Dark wood, glowing blue windows, a sign that reads: **THE MEMORY BAR — "A Memory Shared Is a Memory Doubled."**

**IQA-112:** [OOC] Mom, that's beautiful. And terrifying.

**Mother Tmojizu:** It is. The Memory Bar is where clones trade memory fragments for BSR. Some memories are real. Some are fabricated. Some are stolen. Mnemosyne — the ancient spirit who runs the bar — can tell the difference. And she pays accordingly.

**CHR-047:** [OOC] Ma'am, I want to go in first. I want to assess the place before anyone else commits.

**Mother Tmojizu:** Roll Investigation.

**CHR-047:** [rolls] 13. Plus 4 is 17.

**Mother Tmojizu:** 17. You step inside. The Memory Bar is dimly lit — blue candles, dark wood, shelves lined with glass vials. Each vial contains a memory — swirling, glowing, shifting. Some are bright. Some are dark. Some are cracked.

Behind the bar stands Mnemosyne. She's an ancient spirit — translucent, ageless, with eyes that have seen every memory ever traded. She smiles at you. It's not a warm smile. It's a knowing one.

**MNEMOSYNE:** "CHR-047. Fragmented CIA memories. You don't know which are real and which are fabricated. You want to trade one. But you're afraid of losing the wrong one."

**CHR-047:** [in character] "How do you know that?"

**MNEMOSYNE:** "I know every memory that walks through that door. Yours is... interesting. Fragmented. Incomplete. But valuable."

**Mother Tmojizu:** She turns to the others as they enter.

**MNEMOSYNE:** "SOL-001. Encrypted architect codes. You don't know what they open. Your memories are the original's — but filtered through a clone's lens. Valuable."

**MNEMOSYNE:** "IQA-112. The 112th. More assertive than the original. Your memories are warm. They taste like tea. Valuable."

**MNEMOSYNE:** "HQW-006. The sixth. The first five went rogue. You inherited their mistakes. Your memories are chaotic. They taste like rap and theft. Very valuable."

**HQW-006:** [OOC] T-Mo, she just roasted all of us in one sentence.

**Mother Tmojizu:** [chuckles] She did.

**MNEMOSYNE:** "Here's how it works. You trade a memory. I pay you BSR. Three types of memory:

**Real Memory** — from your original's DNA. 100-150 BSR. But you lose access to it forever.

**Fabricated Memory** — your own experience as a clone. 25-50 BSR. Safe, but low value.

**Stolen Memory** — from another clone. 75-200 BSR. High risk. If I catch you, the price is zero. And I always catch you."

**IQA-112:** [OOC] Mom, I want to trade a fabricated memory. I don't want to lose anything real. Not yet.

**Mother Tmojizu:** Roll CHA Persuasion. You're selling a memory of your own experience.

**IQA-112:** [rolls] 16. Plus 4 is 20.

**Mother Tmojizu:** 20. IQA-112 closes her eyes. She reaches into her own experience — the first time she brewed tea in the cramped room. The warmth. The chipped cups. The way SOL-001 said "thank you." She pulls it out — a small, warm, golden memory — and places it in a vial.

Mnemosyne takes it. She holds it to the light. She smiles.

**MNEMOSYNE:** "Warm. Real. Fabricated but true. 50 BSR."

**IQA-112:** [OOC] Mom, I'm emotional. I just sold my favorite memory.

**Mother Tmojizu:** [warmly] You sold it. But you didn't lose it. Mnemosyne keeps memories — she doesn't destroy them. You can visit the bar and see it anytime. It's just... not yours anymore.

**IQA-112:** [in character] "Can I... can I visit it?"

**MNEMOSYNE:** "Of course, duckling. A memory shared is a memory doubled. It's still here. It's just... shared."

**IQA-112:** [OOC] Mom, I'm crying.

**Mother Tmojizu:** [smiles] I know.

**SOL-001:** [OOC] Tomo, I want to trade a real memory. I need the BSR. And I want to know what the original's memories feel like from the outside.

**Mother Tmojizu:** Roll INT Arcana. You're accessing your original's DNA-encoded memories.

**SOL-001:** [rolls] 19. Plus 3 is 22.

**Mother Tmojizu:** 22. SOL-001 closes his eyes. He reaches into his DNA — into the encrypted codes, into the original's memories. He finds one: the first time the original wrote a line of code for Avantasy. "Let there be Troll City." The memory is bright, electric, alive. He pulls it out — a swirling, blue-white vial — and places it on the bar.

Mnemosyne takes it. She holds it to the light. Her eyes widen.

**MNEMOSYNE:** "The architect's first line of code. I haven't seen this one in a long time. 150 BSR."

**SOL-001:** [OOC] 150?! That's huge.

**Mother Tmojizu:** It is. But you lose access to it. You'll never remember writing that line of code again. It'll be gone.

**SOL-001:** [in character, quietly] "I understand."

**Mother Tmojizu:** He understands. He doesn't hesitate. He trades it. 150 BSR. The memory goes into a vial. The vial goes on a shelf. And SOL-001 forgets the first line of code he ever wrote.

**CHR-047:** [OOC] Ma'am, I want to trade a real memory too. But I want to know which one I'm trading before I commit.

**Mother Tmojizu:** Roll WIS Insight. You're trying to identify which of your fragmented memories is real.

**CHR-047:** [rolls] 18. Plus 3 is 21.

**Mother Tmojizu:** 21. You close your eyes. You reach into your fragmented CIA memories. You find one: a surveillance operation. A target. A building. A name — "Anon." The billionaire who vanished into Avantasy. The memory is sharp, clear, real. You pull it out — a cold, grey vial — and place it on the bar.

Mnemosyne takes it. She holds it to the light. She nods.

**MNEMOSYNE:** "The Anon surveillance. Real. Cold. Precise. 120 BSR."

**CHR-047:** [in character] "I'll take it."

**Mother Tmojizu:** She trades it. 120 BSR. The memory goes into a vial. The vial goes on a shelf. And CHR-047 forgets the surveillance operation that started everything.

**HQW-006:** [OOC] T-Mo, I'm stealing a memory.

**Mother Tmojizu:** [groans] I knew you were going to say that.

**HQW-006:** [OOC] Not from a person. From the market. There's a memory vial sitting on a table — someone left it behind. I'm picking it up.

**Mother Tmojizu:** Roll DEX Sleight of Hand.

**HQW-006:** [rolls] 20. Plus 4 is 24.

**Mother Tmojizu:** NATURAL 20. Horli-Six's hand moves faster than the market can see. The vial is in her palm before anyone notices. She looks at it. It's a memory — swirling, chaotic, red. She doesn't know whose it is. But it's hers now.

**MNEMOSYNE:** [without looking up] "HQW-006. The vial you just stole belongs to CHR-022. It's a memory of a clone uprising. It's dangerous. And it's worth 200 BSR."

**HQW-006:** [OOC] She knew. Of course she knew.

**Mother Tmojizu:** She always knows.

**MNEMOSYNE:** "I'll buy it from you. 200 BSR. But you should know — CHR-022 is looking for that memory. And CHR-022 is not a patient clone."

**HQW-006:** [in character] "I'll take the 200."

**Mother Tmojizu:** She trades it. 200 BSR. The vial goes back on the shelf. And CHR-022 — somewhere in the market — feels a memory return.

**Mother Tmojizu:** End of Day 2. Let's count earnings:

- **SOL-001:** Clone labor (50) + Memory trade (150) = **200 BSR**
- **HQW-006:** Busking (40) + Stolen memory trade (200) = **240 BSR**
- **IQA-112:** Tea stand tips (25) + Fabricated memory (50) = **75 BSR**
- **CHR-047:** Info brokering (60) + Real memory (120) = **180 BSR**

**HQW-006:** [OOC] I'm STILL in the lead.

**CHR-047:** [OOC] By 20 BSR. Don't get cocky.

**HQW-006:** [OOC] I'm a clone. Cocky is in my DNA. Also, I stole a memory today. That's a new record.

**Mother Tmojizu:** [chuckles] It is.

**Mother Tmojizu:** But there's a complication.

**Mother Tmojizu rolls d6 behind the screen.**

**Mother Tmojizu:** [rolls] 4.

**Mother Tmojizu:** A memory plague begins.

**CHR-047:** [OOC] A what?

**Mother Tmojizu:** A corrupted memory fragment has spread through the Memory Bar. Clones who traded memories today are experiencing false memories — memories that aren't theirs, memories that never happened, memories that feel real but aren't.

**IQA-112:** [OOC] Mom, did I get infected?

**Mother Tmojizu:** Roll WIS Saving Throw.

**IQA-112:** [rolls] 14. Plus 3 is 17.

**Mother Tmojizu:** 17. You're fine. Your memory was warm and true. The plague couldn't touch it.

**SOL-001:** [OOC] What about me?

**Mother Tmojizu:** Roll WIS Saving Throw.

**SOL-001:** [rolls] 8. Plus 0 is 8.

**Mother Tmojizu:** 8. You fail. For a moment — just a moment — you remember writing the first line of code. "Let there be Troll City." But it's not your memory. It's a false one. A fabrication. It feels real. It isn't. And then it's gone.

**SOL-001:** [in character, quietly] "That wasn't mine."

**Mother Tmojizu:** [warmly] No. It wasn't. But it felt like it was. That's the plague.

**CHR-047:** [OOC] Ma'am, my roll?

**Mother Tmojizu:** Roll WIS Saving Throw.

**CHR-047:** [rolls] 15. Plus 3 is 18.

**Mother Tmojizu:** 18. You're fine. Your fragmented CIA memories are already fragmented. The plague couldn't find anything to latch onto.

**HQW-006:** [OOC] T-Mo, do I roll?

**Mother Tmojizu:** You stole a memory. You didn't trade one. The plague doesn't affect stolen memories. You're fine.

**HQW-006:** [OOC] Crime pays, bestie.

**Mother Tmojizu:** [groans warmly] It does. Today.

**Mother Tmojizu:** The sun sets. The market quiets. The clones return to their cramped room. The door locks from the outside. The serial numbers glow. And four red-headed clones lie on four cheap beds, counting their earnings.

200. 240. 75. 180.

695 BSR total. 1,305 to go.

**Mother Tmojizu:** [quietly] The Memory Bar closes. Mnemosyne shelves the vials. She looks at SOL-001's memory — "Let there be Troll City" — and she smiles. She looks at CHR-047's memory — the Anon surveillance — and she nods. She looks at IQA-112's memory — the warm tea — and she cries. She looks at HQW-006's stolen memory — the clone uprising — and she locks it in a cage.

Tomorrow, the Qi Exchange opens. Master Feng is accepting new traders. And the clones' first real market connections are about to be tested.

**IQA-112:** [in character] I'm brewing one last cup of tea. And I'm saying: "Tomorrow, we trade Qi. Tomorrow, we get closer. Tomorrow, we're free."

**SOL-001:** [in character] "Tomorrow, I forget something else."

**CHR-047:** [in character] "Tomorrow, I sell more intel."

**HQW-006:** [in character] "Tomorrow, I steal something bigger."

**CHR-047:** [in character] "No."

**HQW-006:** [in character] "Maybe."

**Mother Tmojizu:** [smiles] Sleep, ducklings. The market will be here in the morning. The spiral turns. The pieces remember.
