# CLONE CHRONICLES — SESSION 4: "THE SERIAL SHOP"
## Part 1: The Blood Market — Session 4 of 10
### Players: CHR-047 (Chara), SOL-001 (Sol-Supreme), IQA-112 (IQabella), HQW-006 (Horli Qwin) | DM (Mother Tmojizu)

---

**Mother Tmojizu:** Welcome back, ducklings. Day 4. You've earned 545 BSR total. You need 1,455 more. The morning bell rings. The market wakes up. And today, Scratch's Serial Shop is open.

**HQW-006:** [OOC] T-Mo, I already know what I'm doing.

**Mother Tmojizu:** [groans warmly] I know you do.

**IQA-112:** [OOC] Mom, I'm brewing tea first. Always tea first.

**Mother Tmojizu:** [smiles] Always tea first.

**Mother Tmojizu:** The tea is warm. The clones wake up. SOL-001 opens the door with his architect codes — the same flicker, the same click, the same "that shouldn't have worked" energy. You descend into the Blood Market.

It's tense today. The brokers are quieter. The clones are walking faster. And the police — Officer Krell and two others — are patrolling in pairs. Something's happening.

At the end of a hidden alley — one you didn't notice before — a small door appears. No sign. No window. Just a door. And scratched into the wood, barely visible: **SCRATCH — SERIAL REMOVAL — 300 BSR — DON'T KNOCK LOUD.**

**CHR-047:** [OOC] Ma'am, I'm assessing the alley. INT Investigation.

**Mother Tmojizu:** Roll it.

**CHR-047:** [rolls] 16. Plus 4 is 20.

**Mother Tmojizu:** 20. The alley is narrow — barely wide enough for one person. The walls are covered in graffiti: clone slogans, faction symbols, and warnings. "THEY WATCH." "DON'T TRUST THE BOLT." "SCRATCH IS REAL BUT THE COPS ARE REALER."

Officer Krell patrols the main street — 50 feet from the alley entrance. He walks the route every 12 minutes. You have a window.

**CHR-047:** [in character] "Krell patrols every 12 minutes. The alley is 50 feet from his route. We have a window. But if he turns the corner while we're inside, we're caught."

**SOL-001:** [OOC] Tomo, I want to check if my architect codes can disable the lock on Scratch's door.

**Mother Tmojizu:** Roll INT Arcana.

**SOL-001:** [rolls] 14. Plus 3 is 17.

**Mother Tmojizu:** 17. Your hand touches the door. The lock is old — mechanical, not digital. Your architect codes don't apply. This is just a door. You have to knock.

**SOL-001:** [in character] "The codes don't work here. It's just a door."

**HQW-006:** [in character] "I'll knock."

**Mother Tmojizu:** You knock. Not loud. Three soft taps.

The door opens. A gnome peers out — paranoid, twitchy, wearing goggles and a leather apron covered in tools. His name is Scratch. You know this because it's tattooed on his forehead. In reverse. So you can read it in a mirror.

**SCRATCH:** "You're clones. Red-headed. Batch CHR-SOL-IQA-HQW. I've been expecting you. Come in. Quick. Quiet. Don't touch anything. Especially the left wall. It's wired."

**Mother Tmojizu:** The shop is small — one room, one chair, one table covered in tools. The walls are lined with serial numbers — hundreds of them, glowing faintly, removed from clones who came before you. Some are fresh. Some are old. Some are cracked.

**SCRATCH:** "300 BSR per removal. Takes 10 minutes. You'll feel a burn. Then nothing. The number fades. You're free. Well — freer. You're still a clone. But at least you're not tracked."

**SCRATCH:** "But the police are watching. Krell is on patrol. If he catches you in here, it's 500 BSR fine or 30 days in the clone brig. Your choice."

**HQW-006:** [OOC] T-Mo, I'm doing it. I'm removing my serial number. 300 BSR.

**Mother Tmojizu:** You have 100 BSR. You need 200 more.

**HQW-006:** [OOC] Right. I'm broke. Okay, I'll hustle first. Busking. CHA Performance.

**Mother Tmojizu:** Roll it.

**HQW-006:** [rolls] 17. Plus 5 is 22.

**Mother Tmojizu:** 22. Horli-Six steps into the alley — just outside Scratch's door — and she raps.

**HQW-006:** [in character, rapping] "Yo, I'm HQW-006, red-headed and bold / I'm buying my freedom, I'm selling my soul / 300 BSR for a number removal / But I'm rapping so loud, I'm filling the room!"

**Mother Tmojizu:** The alley echoes. A few clones drop coins. A broker tosses a BSR. By the end of the performance, she's collected 80 BSR.

**HQW-006:** [OOC] 80 BSR. Still not enough. I need 120 more.

**CHR-047:** [OOC] Ma'am, I'll lend her 120 BSR. She can pay me back.

**HQW-006:** [OOC] Wait, seriously?

**CHR-047:** [OOC] I'm not doing it out of kindness. I'm doing it because if HQW-006 has no serial number, she can't be tracked. And if she can't be tracked, she can do things I can't. It's an investment.

**HQW-006:** [OOC] T-Mo, I'm emotional.

**Mother Tmojizu:** [warmly] I know.

**Mother Tmojizu:** CHR-047 lends HQW-006 120 BSR. HQW-006 now has 300 BSR. She sits in Scratch's chair.

**SCRATCH:** "Hold still. This'll burn."

**Mother Tmojizu:** Roll DEX Saving Throw.

**HQW-006:** [rolls] 15. Plus 2 is 17.

**Mother Tmojizu:** 17. You hold still. The tool touches your wrist. It burns — sharp, hot, electric. Your serial number — HQW-006 — flickers. Fades. And disappears.

Your right wrist is bare. No number. No glow. Just skin.

**HQW-006:** [in character, quietly] "It's gone."

**SCRATCH:** "Yep. You're untracked. But you're also unregistered. No clone network access. No clone benefits. You're a ghost. Congratulations."

**HQW-006:** [in character] "I'm a ghost with a rapier. That's fine."

**Mother Tmojizu:** [chuckles] It is.

**IQA-112:** [OOC] Mom, I want to remove mine too. But I need 300 BSR. I have 25. Can I do a tea stand hustle first?

**Mother Tmojizu:** Roll CHA Persuasion.

**IQA-112:** [rolls] 19. Plus 4 is 23.

**Mother Tmojizu:** 23. IQA-112 sets up her tea stand in the Blood Market. The tea is warm. The clones come. Some take it. Some don't. But the ones who do — they pause. They breathe. They feel a moment of warmth.

And then — a tip. A clone drops 10 BSR. Then another. Then another. By the end of the day, she's collected 100 BSR in tips.

**IQA-112:** [OOC] 100 BSR. Plus my 25 = 125. Still not enough.

**SOL-001:** [OOC] Tomo, I'll lend her 175 BSR. Same logic as CHR-047 — if IQA-112 has no serial number, she can't be tracked. She can heal without being monitored. It's an investment.

**Mother Tmojizu:** SOL-001 lends IQA-112 175 BSR. She now has 300 BSR. She sits in Scratch's chair.

**SCRATCH:** "Hold still. This'll burn."

**Mother Tmojizu:** Roll DEX Saving Throw.

**IQA-112:** [rolls] 12. Plus 0 is 12.

**Mother Tmojizu:** 12. You flinch. The tool slips. It burns — sharper than it should. Your serial number — IQA-112 — flickers. Fades. And disappears. But there's a scar — a faint, white line where the number used to be.

**SCRATCH:** "You flinched. Left a scar. It's not a number, but it's a mark. People will notice."

**IQA-112:** [in character] "It's okay. It's mine."

**Mother Tmojizu:** [warmly] It is.

**SOL-001:** [OOC] Tomo, I want to remove mine too. I have 150 BSR. I need 150 more. I'll do clone labor.

**Mother Tmojizu:** Roll CON Saving Throw. Clone labor is exhausting.

**SOL-001:** [rolls] 16. Plus 1 is 17.

**Mother Tmojizu:** 17. You work the warehouse. Loading crates. Heavy, repetitive, mindless. 50 BSR at the end of the shift. You're at 200 BSR. Still not enough.

**CHR-047:** [OOC] Ma'am, I'll lend him 100 BSR. Same logic — untracked architect clone is a powerful asset.

**Mother Tmojizu:** SOL-001 now has 300 BSR. He sits in Scratch's chair.

**SCRATCH:** "Hold still. This'll burn."

**Mother Tmojizu:** Roll DEX Saving Throw.

**SOL-001:** [rolls] 8. Plus 0 is 8.

**Mother Tmojizu:** 8. You flinch badly. The tool slips. It burns — hot, sharp, deep. Your serial number — SOL-001 — flickers. Fades. And disappears. But there's a scar — a thick, red line where the number used to be.

**SCRATCH:** "You flinched. Left a big scar. Everyone will see that."

**SOL-001:** [in character] "It's okay. It's a reminder."

**Mother Tmojizu:** [quietly] It is.

**CHR-047:** [OOC] Ma'am, I'm not removing mine. Not yet. I need my serial number for clone network access. I'm gathering intel. And I'm keeping my number as a statement — it's mine. I earned it.

**Mother Tmojizu:** [smiles] The only one keeping hers. Interesting.

**Mother Tmojizu:** But there's a complication.

**Mother Tmojizu rolls d6 behind the screen.**

**Mother Tmojizu:** [rolls] 1.

**Mother Tmojizu:** Police raid.

Officer Krell turns the corner. He sees the alley. He sees Scratch's door. He sees the clones coming and going. And he makes a decision.

**OFFICER KRELL:** "Raid! Everyone out! Now!"

**Mother Tmojizu:** The clones in the alley scatter. Scratch slams his door. The tools disappear. The serial numbers on the walls go dark. And Officer Krell stands at the end of the alley, looking at the three clones with bare wrists and one clone with a glowing serial number.

**OFFICER KRELL:** "You three. Serial numbers removed. That's illegal. 500 BSR fine or 30 days in the clone brig."

**HQW-006:** [in character] "Officer, we were just... browsing."

**OFFICER KRELL:** "Browsing a serial removal shop. With bare wrists."

**IQA-112:** [in character] "Sir, we made a choice. It's our choice. We're not hurting anyone."

**OFFICER KRELL:** [pauses] He looks at IQA-112's scar. He looks at SOL-001's scar. He looks at HQW-006's bare wrist. And he sees something — not defiance, not fear, but determination.

**OFFICER KRELL:** "I enforce the law. I don't make it. But the law is changing. Chimochio is reviewing the serial number statutes. In the meantime..." He looks at CHR-047 — the one with the glowing serial number. "You. CHR-047. You still have your number."

**CHR-047:** [in character] "I do."

**OFFICER KRELL:** "Good. Someone should. The rest of you — go. Before I change my mind."

**Mother Tmojizu:** He turns. He walks away. The clones exhale.

**HQW-006:** [OOC] T-Mo, I owe CHR-047 120 BSR. And SOL-001 owes... wait, no, SOL-001 lent me money. I owe CHR-047.

**Mother Tmojizu:** Correct. HQW-006 owes CHR-047 120 BSR. IQA-112 owes SOL-001 175 BSR. SOL-001 owes CHR-047 100 BSR.

**SOL-001:** [OOC] Tomo, I'm at 0 BSR liquid. I lent 275 total.

**CHR-047:** [OOC] I'm at 0 BSR liquid. I lent 220 total.

**IQA-112:** [OOC] I'm at 0 BSR liquid. I spent everything.

**HQW-006:** [OOC] I'm at 0 BSR liquid. I spent everything.

**Mother Tmojizu:** [chuckles] Four clones. Zero BSR. Three bare wrists. One glowing serial number. And a police officer who let you go.

**Mother Tmojizu:** End of Day 4. Let's count earnings:

- **SOL-001:** Clone labor (50) - lent 275 = **-225 BSR liquid** (but owes nothing)
- **HQW-006:** Busking (80) - spent 300 + owes 120 = **-340 BSR liquid** (owes CHR-047 120)
- **IQA-112:** Tea stand tips (100) - spent 300 + owes 175 = **-375 BSR liquid** (owes SOL-001 175)
- **CHR-047:** Info brokering (60) - lent 220 = **-160 BSR liquid** (is owed 220 total)

**Mother Tmojizu:** But — three clones now have no serial numbers. They're untracked. Unregistered. Free-er. And CHR-047 still has hers — a choice, not a chain.

**HQW-006:** [OOC] T-Mo, that was the most intense session yet. I have no money, a bare wrist, and a debt. I love it.

**Mother Tmojizu:** [smiles] The spiral favors the bold, little cat. Even when they're broke.

**Mother Tmojizu:** The sun sets. The market quiets. The clones return to their cramped room. The door locks from the outside. Three bare wrists. One glowing serial number. And four red-headed clones lie on four cheap beds, counting their debts.

**IQA-112:** [in character] I'm brewing one last cup of tea. And I'm saying: "Tomorrow, the Underground Xirqus hosts a social event. Reputation is currency. We're going."

**SOL-001:** [in character] "Tomorrow, I open a door I don't understand. Again."

**CHR-047:** [in character] "Tomorrow, I collect my debts."

**HQW-006:** [in character] "Tomorrow, I rap for coins. And definitely steal something."

**CHR-047:** [in character] "No."

**HQW-006:** [in character] "Maybe."

**Mother Tmojizu:** [smiles] Sleep, ducklings. The market will be here in the morning. The spiral turns. The pieces remember.
