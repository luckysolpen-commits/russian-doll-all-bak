# CLONE CHRONICLES — SESSION 1: "THE MORNING BELL"
## Part 1: The Blood Market — Session 1 of 10
### Players: CHR-047 (Chara), SOL-001 (Sol-Supreme), IQA-112 (IQabella), HQW-006 (Horli Qwin) | DM (Mother Tmojizu)

---

**Mother Tmojizu:** Welcome back, my precious ducklings. Tonight, you're not the originals. You're the copies. Red-headed, vat-born, serial-numbered, and broke. Each of you owes 500 BSR for your creation. You have nothing. No money. No connections. No freedom. Just a cramped room, a glowing serial number, and the morning bell about to ring.

**HQW-006:** [OOC] T-Mo, I'm ready. I've been waiting to play a clone since session one. Also, I'm red-headed. I love that.

**Mother Tmojizu:** [chuckles] You're all red-headed. Same DNA batch. Same vat. Same room. But not the same person. You never were.

**CHR-047:** [OOC] Ma'am, confirm — I have fragmented CIA memories. They're useful but unreliable. Correct?

**Mother Tmojizu:** Correct. They flash at inconvenient times. Sometimes they're real. Sometimes they're your brain filling in gaps. You won't know which is which until it matters.

**CHR-047:** [OOC] Perfect. I don't trust anything anyway. This is consistent.

**SOL-001:** [OOC] Tomo, my encrypted architect codes — I can access restricted areas but I don't know why. Do I know that I have them, or do I just... have them?

**Mother Tmojizu:** You know you have them. You don't know what they open. You'll find out when you try.

**SOL-001:** [OOC] That's terrifying. I love it.

**IQA-112:** [OOC] Mom, I'm the 112th clone. I'm more assertive than the original. I still make tea, but I won't be pushed around.

**Mother Tmojizu:** [smiles] You never were pushable, duckling. The vat just gave you permission to say no.

**IQA-112:** [OOC] That's... actually really nice.

**Mother Tmojizu:** Okay. Let's begin.

---

**Mother Tmojizu:** The morning bell rings.

It's a deep, resonant sound — like a gong struck inside a cathedral. It echoes through the cramped room you share. Four beds. Four red heads. Four serial numbers glowing on wrists. The walls are thin. You can hear the Blood Market waking up outside.

Brokers calling prices: "Clone labor, 50 BSR a day!" "Memory fragments, fresh batch!" "Qi Energy, buy now, sell later!"

The smell of street food — something greasy, something sweet, something you can't identify — seeps through the cracks in the walls. The floor is cold. The window is small. The door is locked from the outside.

You're clones. You're property. You owe 500 BSR each.

What do you do?

**CHR-047:** [in character] I'm checking the door. Is it locked from the outside or just locked?

**Mother Tmojizu:** Roll Investigation.

**CHR-047:** [rolls] 16. Plus 4 is 20.

**Mother Tmojizu:** 20. The door is locked from the outside — a heavy bolt on the other side. But the lock is old, the wood is warped, and the frame is cracked. With enough force, you could break it. Or with enough finesse, you could pick it.

**CHR-047:** I'm not breaking it. I don't want to start our first day with property damage charges. I'm checking the window.

**Mother Tmojizu:** The window is small — about two feet wide, three feet tall. It looks out onto the Blood Market street. You can see brokers setting up stalls, clones walking to work, and a police officer patrolling the corner.

**HQW-006:** [in character] I'm stretching. I'm doing a full-body stretch — arms up, back arched, tail flicking. And I'm looking at my serial number. HQW-006. Glowing on my right wrist. I'm saying out loud: "Six. I'm the sixth one. The first five went rogue. I'm gonna be the one who succeeds."

**Mother Tmojizu:** You say it. The room is quiet for a moment. The other clones hear you. The morning bell fades. The market gets louder.

**IQA-112:** [in character] I'm sitting up. I'm looking at my serial number — IQA-112. Glowing on my left wrist. And I'm saying: "I'm the 112th. That means 111 IQabellas came before me. And I'm still here. Still warm. Still making tea." I reach under my bed and pull out my small tea set. It's the only thing I have that's mine.

**Mother Tmojizu:** [warmly] The tea set is chipped. The cups don't match. But it's yours. Inherited from the original's memory. You don't remember getting it. You just remember that it matters.

**IQA-112:** [in character] I'm brewing tea. It's not much — just hot water and a tea bag I found under the bed — but it's warm. And I'm offering it to everyone.

**SOL-001:** [in character] I'm sitting up. I'm looking at my serial number — SOL-001. Glowing on my right wrist. The first one. Not the original — the first clone. I'm saying: "Zero-one. I'm the prototype. They made me first to see if it would work." I'm looking at my hands. They're the same hands as the original. But they're not. They're mine.

**Mother Tmojizu:** [quietly] The tea is warm. The room is small. The market is loud. And four red-headed clones are sitting on four cheap beds, trying to figure out who they are.

**CHR-047:** [in character] I'm taking the tea. Thank you, IQA-112. And I'm saying: "We have 500 BSR each to pay off. That's 2,000 total. We need a plan. We need to earn money. And we need to do it without getting arrested, exploited, or turned into Clown pieces."

**HQW-006:** [in character] "Or we could just steal something."

**CHR-047:** [in character] "No."

**HQW-006:** [in character] "Okay, but what if we stole something *and* had a plan?"

**CHR-047:** [in character] "Still no."

**Mother Tmojizu:** [chuckles] The tea is gone. The room is quiet. The market is waiting.

**Mother Tmojizu:** Okay. You need to leave the room. The door is locked from the outside. How do you get out?

**SOL-001:** [in character] I'm checking if my architect codes work on the door. I'm placing my hand on the lock and... trying to access it. Like I'm opening a restricted area.

**Mother Tmojizu:** Roll Intelligence.

**SOL-001:** [rolls] 12. Plus 3 is 15.

**Mother Tmojizu:** 15. Your hand touches the lock. For a moment, nothing. Then — a flicker. A code. A pattern. The lock recognizes something in your DNA. Not the original's DNA. *Your* DNA. The bolt slides back. The door clicks open.

**SOL-001:** [OOC] Tomo, did I just... open a locked door with my clone codes?

**Mother Tmojizu:** You did. You don't know why it worked. You just know that it did.

**SOL-001:** [in character, quietly] "That shouldn't have worked."

**CHR-047:** [in character] "Noted."

**HQW-006:** [in character] "Can you open *all* the doors?"

**SOL-001:** [in character] "I don't know."

**HQW-006:** [in character] "We're gonna find out."

**Mother Tmojizu:** [groans warmly] I knew you were going to say that.

**Mother Tmojizu:** The door opens. The hallway is narrow, dimly lit, and smells like old clone. Other doors line the walls — each one housing more clones, more debts, more serial numbers. At the end of the hallway, a staircase leads down to the Blood Market street.

You descend. The market opens up before you.

It's overwhelming. Stalls line both sides of the street. Brokers call prices. Clones walk in groups, heads down, serial numbers glowing. Police officers patrol the corners. The sky above is a perpetual twilight purple, shot through with golden ϕ-spiral auroras.

And everywhere — everywhere — you see clones. Red-headed, brown-haired, bald, tattooed, scarred, glowing. All of them with serial numbers. All of them with debts. All of them trying to survive.

**IQA-112:** [in character] I'm holding my tea set. I'm looking at the market. And I'm saying: "This is where we start. This is where we earn our freedom. One cup of tea at a time."

**Mother Tmojizu:** [smiles] One cup of tea at a time. Yes.

**Mother Tmojizu:** Okay. You're in the Blood Market. You have zero BSR. You need to start earning. What's your first hustle?

**CHR-047:** [OOC] I want to gather information first. I'm walking through the market, listening to prices, watching brokers, assessing the landscape. INT Investigation.

**Mother Tmojizu:** Roll it.

**CHR-047:** [rolls] 14. Plus 4 is 18.

**Mother Tmojizu:** 18. You walk through the market. You listen. You watch. You assess. And you learn:

- Clone labor pays 50 BSR a day. Hard work, guaranteed pay.
- Memory trading at the Memory Bar pays 25-75 BSR per memory. Risk: you might lose a real memory.
- Street vending requires a permit (10 BSR) but can earn 10-40 BSR per day.
- Information brokering pays 20-60 BSR per deal. High risk — you might learn something dangerous.
- The Qi Exchange opens tomorrow. Master Feng is accepting new traders.
- Scratch's Serial Shop is in a hidden alley. He removes serial numbers for 300 BSR. It's illegal. The police are watching.

**CHR-047:** [OOC] I'm noting all of this. I'm also watching the police officer on the corner. Officer Krell, I assume?

**Mother Tmojizu:** Roll Perception.

**CHR-047:** [rolls] 11. Plus 3 is 14.

**Mother Tmojizu:** 14. You see Officer Krell — stern, by-the-book, walking his patrol route. He's not looking at you. Not yet. But he will. Clones with fresh serial numbers always attract attention.

**SOL-001:** [OOC] I want to try clone labor. It's guaranteed 50 BSR. I need a baseline. I'm walking to the Clone Exchange.

**Mother Tmojizu:** The Clone Exchange is a massive building — part warehouse, part auction house, part prison. Clones line up outside, waiting to be assigned work. Broker Vex stands at the entrance — a tiefling with sharp horns, sharper eyes, and a ledger that never closes.

**BROKER VEX:** "New clones. Red-headed. Batch CHR-SOL-IQA-HQW. I've been expecting you." He looks at his ledger. "500 BSR each. Creation debt. You work it off, or you don't. Your choice. Clone labor is 50 BSR a day. Ten days to pay off your debt. Or you can hustle. Hustle pays more. Hustle also gets you arrested. Your choice."

**SOL-001:** [in character] "I'll take the labor."

**BROKER VEX:** "Smart. Boring, but smart. You're assigned to the warehouse. Loading crates. 50 BSR at the end of the day. Don't drop anything. Don't talk to anyone. Don't look at the merchandise."

**SOL-001:** [in character] "What's the merchandise?"

**BROKER VEX:** [stares] "Don't look at the merchandise."

**Mother Tmojizu:** Sol-Supreme heads to the warehouse. The other three are still in the market.

**HQW-006:** [OOC] T-Mo, I'm doing street vending. I don't have a permit, but I'm gonna hustle anyway. CHA Performance.

**Mother Tmojizu:** You need a permit for street vending. It's 10 BSR. You have zero BSR.

**HQW-006:** [OOC] Can I... perform without a permit? Like, busking?

**Mother Tmojizu:** Busking is legal. You're not selling anything. You're performing. CHA Performance.

**HQW-006:** [rolls] 18. Plus 5 is 23.

**Mother Tmojizu:** 23. Horli-Six steps into the middle of the Blood Market street. She draws her rapier — "The Mic" — and she starts rapping.

**HQW-006:** [in character, rapping] "Yo, I'm HQW-006, red-headed and alive / Six clones came before me, five didn't survive / I got a serial number glowing on my wrist / But I got a flow so hot, you can't resist / I'm hustling in the market, making BSR by the beat / And if you don't tip me, I'm stealing your seat!"

**Mother Tmojizu:** The market stops. Clones turn. Brokers pause. Even Officer Krell looks over. And then — a coin. A BSR coin. It lands at Horli's feet. Then another. Then another. By the end of the performance, she's collected 40 BSR.

**HQW-006:** [OOC] LET'S GO! Forty BSR from one rap!

**Mother Tmojizu:** [smiles] The spiral favors the bold, little cat.

**IQA-112:** [OOC] Mom, I want to set up a tea stand. Not a vending stand — a tea stand. I'm offering free tea to clones who look like they're having a hard day. And I'm hoping for tips.

**Mother Tmojizu:** That's not a hustle. That's a kindness.

**IQA-112:** [OOC] Is that allowed?

**Mother Tmojizu:** [warmly] Kindness is always allowed. Roll CHA Persuasion.

**IQA-112:** [rolls] 15. Plus 4 is 19.

**Mother Tmojizu:** 19. IQA-112 sets up her tea stand — a small table, her chipped tea set, and a pot of hot water. She offers free tea to every clone who walks by. Some take it. Some don't. But the ones who do — they pause. They breathe. They feel a moment of warmth in a cold market.

And then — a tip. A clone drops 5 BSR into her cup. Then another. Then another. By the end of the day, she's collected 25 BSR in tips.

**IQA-112:** [OOC] Mom, that's the most beautiful thing that's happened to me all session.

**Mother Tmojizu:** [smiles] It is.

**CHR-047:** [OOC] I'm doing information brokering. I'm walking through the market, talking to clones, gathering intel. INT Investigation + CHA Persuasion.

**Mother Tmojizu:** Roll Investigation first.

**CHR-047:** [rolls] 17. Plus 4 is 21.

**Mother Tmojizu:** 21. You talk to clones. You listen. You piece together intel. And you learn something big: the clones' debts are artificially inflated. The real cost of creation is 200 BSR, not 500. Chimochio Corporation is charging 300 BSR extra per clone — a "processing fee" that doesn't exist.

**CHR-047:** [OOC] That's huge. Can I sell this intel?

**Mother Tmojizu:** You can. Roll Persuasion to find a buyer.

**CHR-047:** [rolls] 12. Plus 2 is 14.

**Mother Tmojizu:** 14. You find a buyer — a clone named CHR-033 who's been in the market for years. He pays you 60 BSR for the intel. "I've suspected this for years," he says. "Now I know. Thank you."

**CHR-047:** [in character] "Don't thank me. Use it."

**Mother Tmojizu:** End of Day 1. Let's count earnings:

- **SOL-001:** Clone labor — 50 BSR
- **HQW-006:** Busking — 40 BSR
- **IQA-112:** Tea stand tips — 25 BSR
- **CHR-047:** Information brokering — 60 BSR

**HQW-006:** [OOC] T-Mo, I'm in the lead.

**CHR-047:** [OOC] By four BSR. Don't get cocky.

**HQW-006:** [OOC] I'm a clone. Cocky is in my DNA.

**Mother Tmojizu:** [chuckles] The sun sets. The market quiets. The clones return to their cramped room. The door locks from the outside. The serial numbers glow. And four red-headed clones lie on four cheap beds, counting their earnings.

50. 40. 25. 60.

175 BSR total. 1,825 to go.

**Mother Tmojizu:** [quietly] The morning bell will ring again tomorrow. The market will wake up. The brokers will call prices. The police will patrol. And you'll be back out there — hustling, trading, scheming, surviving.

But tonight, you sleep.

**IQA-112:** [in character] I'm brewing one last cup of tea. And I'm saying: "Tomorrow, we earn more. Tomorrow, we get closer. Tomorrow, we're free."

**SOL-001:** [in character] "Tomorrow, I open a door I don't understand."

**CHR-047:** [in character] "Tomorrow, I sell more intel."

**HQW-006:** [in character] "Tomorrow, I rap for more coins. And maybe steal something."

**CHR-047:** [in character] "No."

**HQW-006:** [in character] "Maybe."

**Mother Tmojizu:** [smiles] Sleep, ducklings. The market will be here in the morning. The spiral turns. The pieces remember.
