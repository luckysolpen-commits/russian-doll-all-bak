# AVA-SERVER-0001 — "THE EXPANSE SHARD"
## World Document / Dungeon Master Reference
### Avantasy Expanse — Persistent VR-MMORPG D&D World

---

## 1. SERVER OVERVIEW

**Server Name:** Ava-Server-0001 "The Expanse Shard"
**Server Type:** Persistent VR-MMORPG running on the Avantasy engine (TPMOS v03)
**Server Age:** ~3 years server-time (~9 months real-time)
**Active Population:** ~200 player-characters per cycle, thousands of NPCs
**World State:** Stable (minor breaches detected in Worm Hole X sector)
**Current Epoch:** The Troll City Era — most players are concentrated in the starter zones

### Server History
- **Year 0, Month 1:** Server initialized by unknown architect (lore: Sol-Supreme). Troll City spawns as the first settlement.
- **Year 0, Month 6:** Fairy City floats into existence above Troll City. Rainbow Bridge connection established.
- **Year 1, Month 3:** First server breach — a Carcazod incursion from the Wastes. Player coalition led by GoldQueen_Astra repels it.
- **Year 1, Month 8:** The Illegal Furry Server discovered by hackers. Becomes underground hub.
- **Year 2, Month 2:** LoonaTwilight sacrifices herself to seal a massive breach at Skullmoon Crater. Returns as ghost-paladin.
- **Year 2, Month 10:** New Atlantis zone unlocked. Underwater expansion begins.
- **Year 3, Current:** Server stable. New players (the party) arriving. Whispers of a second breach building in the Wastes.

## 2. GEOGRAPHY — CONTINENTS & ZONES

### 2.1 TROLL CITY (Levels 1-5) — STARTER ZONE

**Description:** A bustling market town built inside the ribcage of a dead titan. The titan's bones form natural walls — curved white pillars rising 200 feet into the sky. Cobblestone streets wind between timber-and-stone buildings. The air smells of roasted meat, forge smoke, and something faintly magical. Neon signs (ASCII-GL rendered) flicker above shops. The sky above is a perpetual twilight purple, shot through with golden ϕ-spiral auroras.

**Key Locations:**

1. **The Rusty Anchor Tavern**
   - Owner: Borin Ironmug (dwarf, NPC)
   - Function: Rest spot, rumor mill, session starting point
   - Menu: Roasted boar (5sp), Dwarven Ale (2sp), "Borin's Mystery Stew" (1sp — don't ask)
   - Rooms: 4 available (5cp/night)
   - Secret: Borin's cellar connects to a smuggler's tunnel leading to the Illegal Furry Server

2. **Gimble's Gear & Steel**
   - Owner: Gimble Copperfist (gnome, NPC)
   - Function: Weapons, armor, upgrades
   - Current Inventory:
     - Longsword (15gp)
     - Shortbow + 20 arrows (25gp)
     - Studded Leather Armor (45gp)
     - Shield, +1 (150gp — "barely used!")
     - "Gimble's Special": A rusty helmet he claims belonged to a king (50gp — it did not)
   - Services: Weapon sharpening (1gp), Armor repair (5gp), "Upgrades" (negotiable, results vary)

3. **The Soul Pen Emporium**
   - Owner: Madame Zixi (tiefling, NPC)
   - Function: Soul Pens, ink, Tear-It cards, rare items
   - Current Inventory:
     - Soul Pen Mark 0 (basic, 25gp)
     - Soul Pen Mark 1 (advanced, 100gp)
     - Nothing Ink Vial (500gp — only 1 in stock)
     - Tear-It Card Booster Pack (15gp each, 3 available)
     - Cursed Item: "The Mirror of Maybe" (75gp — shows you an alternate reality where you made better choices)
   - Secret: Madame Zixi knows who the server architect really is. She won't say. Yet.

4. **The Quest Board**
   - Location: Central plaza, next to the Healing Spring
   - Current Postings:
     - "Giant spiders in the Lost Temple. 500gp + loot." (Medium, Levels 3-5)
     - "My cat is stuck in a tree. The tree is on fire." (Easy, Levels 1-2, 25gp)
     - "Carcazod scouts spotted near the east gate. Handle it." (Hard, Levels 5-7, 200gp + faction reputation)
     - "Seeking brave adventurers for a totally legitimate business venture." — Signed, "Definitely Not a Scam" (Suspicious)

5. **The Magic Tree**
   - Guardian: Tree-Sage Elowen (ancient elf druid, NPC)
   - Function: Crafting station, magic item creation, spell learning
   - Services:
     - Craft magic items (requires Nothing Ink + gold + time)
     - Learn new spells (gold cost based on spell level)
     - Blessing of the Tree (+1 to all spell saves for 24 hours, 10gp donation)
   - Lore: The Magic Tree is the server's original spawning point. Everything grew from here.

6. **The Healing Spring**
   - Function: Free HP/MP restoration (once per long rest)
   - Appearance: A glowing pool of cyan water at the base of a ϕ-spiral stone fountain
   - Blessed by: The 8 Fuzzball spirits (tiny colored lights dance above the water)
   - Secret: If you throw a gold coin in and make a wish, the DM *might* give you advantage on your next roll

### 2.2 FAIRY CITY (Levels 3-7) — MAGIC ZONE

**Description:** An ethereal floating district suspended above Troll City, connected by the Rainbow Bridge — a shimmering, multi-colored pathway of solid light. Fairy City is all pastel colors, floating gardens, crystalline towers, and soft glowing music. The architecture is impossible — buildings curve in ϕ-spiral patterns, stairs lead to nowhere, and gravity is a suggestion.

**Key Locations:**
1. **The Arcane Academy** — Where wizards and sorcerers train. Professor Void occasionally lectures here via portal.
2. **The Enchanter's Row** — Shops selling enchanted items, potions, and spell components.
3. **The Fuzzball Shrine** — Temple dedicated to the 8 Fuzzball spirits. The Priestess grants blessings and temporary buffs.
4. **The Rainbow Bridge Tollhouse** — Guarded by a bored angel named Kevin. 5cp toll to cross. Kevin can be bribed with snacks.

**Notable NPC:**
- **Fuzzball Shrine Priestess** (Aasimar, serene, channels the 8 Fuzzball spirits)
  - Blessings available (one per long rest):
    - Crimson's Guard: +2 AC for 1 hour
    - Ember's Warmth: Resistance to cold damage for 1 hour
    - Glimmer's Trick: Advantage on one DEX save within the next hour
    - Verdi's Bloom: Regenerate 1 HP/minute for 10 minutes
    - Aqua's Depth: Water breathing for 1 hour
    - Nebula's Dream: Advantage on one INT save within the next hour
    - Void's Silence: Can't be detected by magical means for 10 minutes
    - Lumen's Light: Full HP restore (once per week, the Priestess's discretion)

### 2.3 THE ILLEGAL FURRY SERVER (Levels 5-10) — UNDERGROUND ZONE

**Description:** A hidden zone accessed through a secret portal in Borin Ironmug's cellar (Troll City). The transition is jarring — from medieval fantasy to neon-lit cyberpunk chaos. Pulsating electronic music, strobe lights, ever-shifting graffiti walls. Avatars of every conceivable design: dragons with galaxy-scale fur, cybernetic felines, abstract geometric beings. This is where the server's hackers, rebels, and freedom-freaks gather.

**Key Locations:**
1. **The Dance Floor** — Central hub. Players dance, socialize, and show off. ShadowFang's stage is here.
2. **The Hacker Den** — Back room where firewall exploits and server hacks are discussed. Entry requires a password (changes weekly, currently: "Fuzzball420").
3. **The Black Market** — Illegal items: stolen Tear-It cards, hacked Soul Pens, Nothing Ink knockoffs ("Something Ink" — side effects unknown).
4. **The VIP Lounge** — ShadowFang's private area. Invitation only.

**Notable NPC/Player:**
- **xX_ShadowFang_Xx** (Level 14 Rogue / Shadar-Kai)
  - Legendary hacker. Runs this zone.
  - Can bypass any firewall in Avantasy.
  - Neutral to all factions.
  - Appearance: Six-legged wolf avatar with glowing eyes and shifting shadow-fur.
  - Will help the party if they bring him something interesting (he's bored).
  - Secret: He knows about the second breach building in the Wastes. He's been monitoring it.

### 2.4 THE LOST TEMPLE JUNGLE (Levels 5-7) — DUNGEON ZONE

**Description:** A massive, overgrown jungle surrounding ancient ruins. Vines snake around weathered stone pillars. A faint, eerie glow emanates from within the temple structures. The air is heavy with the scent of damp earth and decay. Giant spiders, undead warriors, and trap-filled corridors await.

**Dungeon Features:**
- **Floor 1:** Giant spiders (CR 1-2), web traps, pressure plate puzzles
- **Floor 2:** Undead warriors (CR 2-3), cursed treasure (save or be hexed), hidden passages
- **Floor 3 (Boss):** Temple Guardian — a stone golem infused with Carcazod mutagen (CR 5)
- **Treasure:** 500gp, Dragon Slayer Armor (chestpiece, +10 STR, +15 DEF), Mana Crystals x3

### 2.5 THE CARCAZOD WASTES (Levels 8-12) — ENDGAME APPROACH

**Description:** Post-apocalyptic badlands. The ruins of the old Clown World — twisted carnival rides, faded big-top tents, and the skeletal remains of giant joke machines. Now overrun by Carcazods (monkey-cockroach hybrids with chitinous exoskeletons and glowing red/green eyes).

**Key Locations:**
1. **Scavenger Camp** — Survivors living in armored vehicles. Led by "Sarge" (veteran human fighter, NPC).
2. **The Mutagen Pits** — Glowing green pools where Carcazods spawn. Destroying one is a major quest.
3. **Warlord Grak's Fortress** — Built inside a crashed clown-ship. Heavily guarded.

**Notable Enemy:**
- **Carcazod Warlord Grak** (CR 8)
  - Brutal, intelligent, leads raiding parties.
  - Speaks broken Common: "Grak smash. Grak take. Grak RULE."
  - Recurring villain. If he escapes, he comes back stronger.
  - Drops: Grak's Cleaver (greatsword, +2, deals extra damage to humanoids), Carcazod Armor (medium, +1), Mutagen Vial (quest item)

**Description:** A bioluminescent underwater city built to escape the non-swimming Carcazods. Glowing coral towers, hydroponic farms, and the UN Embassy. The water is rendered as ASCII "*" symbols that shimmer with bioluminescent colors.

**Key Locations:**
1. **The Hydroponic Farms** — Food source for the server. Guarded by Captain Finnick.

**Notable NPC:**

## 4. ECONOMY & CURRENCY

### 4.1 Standard Currency
- **Copper Pieces (cp):** Common transactions
- **Silver Pieces (sp):** Moderate transactions
- **Gold Pieces (gp):** Standard adventurer currency
- **Platinum Pieces (pp):** High-end transactions (1pp = 10gp)

### 4.2 Special Currencies
- **Soul Pen Ink:** Crafting currency. Used to create spells and magic items at the Magic Tree. Drops from elite enemies and dungeon bosses. (1 vial = ~50gp value)
- **Nothing Ink:** Ultra-rare crafting currency. The fuel of creation. Only drops from boss enemies or deep exploration. (1 vial = ~500gp value)
- **Tear-It Cards:** Collectible/tradable. Some have mechanical effects. Booster packs contain 10 cards (1 rare guaranteed). Individual rare cards trade for 20-100gp.

### 4.3 Starting Party Wealth
- Each character begins with 30-75gp (see individual stats.txt)
- Total party gold: ~200gp
- Enough for basic gear, tavern stays, and a few purchases. Not enough for Nothing Ink.

## 7. LORE PRIMER — WHAT THE PLAYERS KNOW

### 7.1 Common Knowledge (Everyone Knows This)
- Avantasy is a VR MMORPG. At least, that's what it claims to be.
- Troll City is the starting zone. The Magic Tree, the Healing Spring, and the Quest Board are essential services.
- The server has been running for ~3 years. Some players have been here since the beginning.
- Carcazods are the main enemy. They come from the Wastes.
- There are rumors that Avantasy is "more than a game." Most players dismiss this.

### 7.2 Uncommon Knowledge (Requires Investigation/Lore Rolls)
- The server was built by someone called "The Architect." Identity unknown.
- The Fuzzball spirits (8 colored entities) are tied to the server's core code.
- The Illegal Furry Server exists. Access requires knowing someone who knows someone.
- LoonaTwilight died sealing a breach. The breach came from Worm Hole X.
- Soul Pens are more than crafting tools — they're interfaces with the server's source code.

### 7.3 Hidden Knowledge (Requires High Rolls / Quest Discovery)
- Sol-Supreme is the Architect. He's trapped inside his own creation.
- The server is built on TPMOS — a real-world operating system framework.
- The "game" is actually a simulation running inside a larger reality.
- The second breach is building. It will be worse than the first.
- The 20 Pillars of the Spiral are the server's foundational code. Each Pillar corresponds to a zone, a story, and a piece of the truth.

## 8. DM NOTES — RUNNING AVA-SERVER-0001

### 8.1 Tone
- Avantasy is a VR game world, so the players can be aware of game mechanics (HP bars, menus, console commands like `/ability` and `/spell`).
- Blend D&D 5e mechanics with video game aesthetics: combat menus flash before players' eyes, loot drops are accompanied by golden light, level-ups trigger server-wide notifications.
- The Murderhobos style means table banter, rules debates, and comedy are just as important as the in-world action.

### 8.2 Pacing
- Start in the Rusty Anchor Tavern. Let the party meet, introduce themselves, and get their bearings.
- Move to the Quest Board or an NPC interaction to give them direction.
- First combat should be medium difficulty — enough to teach the mechanics without overwhelming.
- End Session 1 with a hook: a clue about the second breach, a mysterious NPC, or a lore reveal about Sol-Supreme's identity.

### 8.3 The Meta Layer
- Characters can be aware they're in a game. This is intentional.
- Sol-Supreme knows MORE than he should. Let this create tension.
- Chara's CIA background means she'll investigate the "real world" implications. Feed her clues.
- IQabella's emotional intelligence means she'll notice when NPCs seem "too aware."
- Horli Qwin will exploit game mechanics. Let her. It's fun.

### 8.4 The Overarching Plot
- **Session 1-3:** Establish the party, explore Troll City, first dungeon (Lost Temple).
- **Session 4-6:** The Carcazod threat escalates. Warlord Grak becomes a recurring villain.
- **Session 7-9:** The Illegal Furry Server, ShadowFang's intel, the truth about the breach.
- **Session 10-12:** Fairy City, the Fuzzball Shrine, GoldQueen_Astra's revelation about Sol-Supreme.
- **Session 13-15:** The Wastes, confronting Grak, discovering the breach source.
- **Session 16+:** Worm Hole X, the Architect's Vault, the truth about Avantasy.
