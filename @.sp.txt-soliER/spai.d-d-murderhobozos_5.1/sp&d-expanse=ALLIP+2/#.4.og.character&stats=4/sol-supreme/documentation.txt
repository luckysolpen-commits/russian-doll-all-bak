# documentation.txt — SOL-SUPREME

## Personality
Authoritative, strategic, and carrying the weight of someone who built this world and
watched it slip out of his control. Sol-Supreme doesn't panic — he calculates. He's
the party's tactician, the one who reads the room, assesses resources, and formulates
a plan. He speaks with a deep, distorted voice (helmet modulator) and every word
sounds like a decree. He's not arrogant — he's confident to the point of certainty.
But there's a vulnerability underneath: he's trapped in his own creation, and he
doesn't know how to fix it.

## Roleplay Notes
- Speaks slowly and deliberately. The voice modulator adds weight to every sentence.
- Approaches problems like an engineer — identifies the system, finds the failure point, patches it.
- Rarely shows emotion, but when he does, it's significant.
- His Steel Defender companion is named "IKARUS-7" — a smaller version of the legendary Ikarus.
- He knows lore that other players don't. The DM should feed him hints through "blueprint memories."
- He doesn't explain his past unless pressed. And even then, he deflects.

## Relationships
- **Chara:** He knows she was sent to investigate him. He respects her for it. He'll
  eventually reveal the truth to her — but only when she's ready.
- **IQabella:** He designed her chassis (in lore). He cares about her deeply but shows
  it through protection, not affection. He worries she's too trusting.
- **Horli Qwin:** He finds her chaotic and unpredictable, which makes her a variable
  he can't model. This frustrates him. He'll slowly develop a grudging respect for
  her ability to succeed through pure chaos.

## Voice/Tone
- Deep, metallic, distorted. Think Batman meets Iron Man meets a server rack.
- Speaks in declarative statements. "That's the plan." "We move at dawn." "It'll hold."
- Rarely uses contractions when in character — the modulator makes everything sound formal.
- When the helmet comes off (rare event), his voice is softer, more human.
- Catchphrase energy: "I built this. I can fix it." / "Run the numbers."

## OOC Player Energy
- The player is strategic and rules-literate. Knows their sheet inside out.
- Enjoys the crafting and invention mechanics. Will try to build custom items.
- Plays the "long game" — willing to lose a battle to win the war.
- Will occasionally break character with a dry, unexpected joke that catches everyone off guard.
