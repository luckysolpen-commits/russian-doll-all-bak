# TPMOJIO CAFE — Community Programming & Events
## "The Store Is the Stage. The Community Is the Show."

---

## The Philosophy

TPMOJIO Cafe isn't a store that hosts events. **It's an event that happens to have a store.**

Every day is programming. Every visit is an experience. Every member is a participant, not a customer.

---

## Weekly Programming (Every Location)

### Monday: Quiet Day
- **Morning:** Open cafe. Board games available. Quest board active.
- **Afternoon:** "Study Hall" — quiet space for students, remote workers, writers.
- **Evening:** "Lore Night" — read TPMOJIO transcripts, listen to sessions, discuss lore.
- **Vibe:** Relaxed. Low energy. Good for introverts.

### Tuesday: Learning Day
- **Morning:** Open cafe. Tech help desk (free, drop-in).
- **Afternoon:** "Code & Coffee" — coding workshop (Python, web dev, AI).
- **Evening:** "DM School" — learn to run TPMOJIO, D&D, or other TTRPGs.
- **Vibe:** Educational. Supportive. Beginners welcome.

### Wednesday: Game Night
- **Morning:** Open cafe. Board game library open.
- **Afternoon:** "Open Play" — bring your own games or play ours.
- **Evening:** "TPMOJIO Night" — live TPMOJIO session (recorded for podcast).
- **Vibe:** Energetic. Competitive. Fun.

### Thursday: Creative Day
- **Morning:** Open cafe. Streaming setup available for members.
- **Afternoon:** "Cosplay Workshop" — build, paint, sew, photograph.
- **Evening:** "Open Mic" — comedy, music, poetry, storytelling, rap battles.
- **Vibe:** Expressive. Supportive. Creative.

### Friday: Social Night
- **Morning:** Open cafe. Quest board matching session.
- **Afternoon:** "Community Hour" — meet other members, form groups, plan events.
- **Evening:** "Tournament Night" — board game tournaments, TTRPG one-shots, esports.
- **Vibe:** Social. Competitive. Loud.

### Saturday: Family Day
- **Morning:** Open cafe. Family-friendly board games.
- **Afternoon:** "Kids' Quest" — age-appropriate quests for kids (parents get a break).
- **Evening:** "Featured Event" — guest DM, special session, live recording, guest comedian.
- **Vibe:** Family-friendly. Welcoming. Diverse.

### Sunday: Community Day
- **Morning:** Open cafe. "Brunch & Board Games."
- **Afternoon:** "Community Meeting" — members vote on cafe decisions, suggest events, share ideas.
- **Evening:** "Chill Night" — low-key gaming, tea, conversation.
- **Vibe:** Community. Democratic. Relaxed.

## Monthly Programming

### First Weekend: TPMOJIO Campaign Session
- **What:** Live TPMOJIO session (recorded for podcast/YouTube).
- **Who:** Regular cast + guest players from the community.
- **Where:** Event corner (streamed to main screen).
- **Revenue:** Ticketed ($10-20). Members get free entry.

### Second Weekend: Tournament
- **What:** Board game tournament, TTRPG one-shot competition, or esports tournament.
- **Who:** Open to all. Prizes for winners.
- **Where:** Gaming lounge + event corner.
- **Revenue:** Entry fee ($5-15). Members get discounted entry.

### Third Weekend: Guest Event
- **What:** Guest comedian, musician, author, DM, or creator.
- **Who:** Open to all. Meet & greet after.
- **Where:** Event corner (or rented venue if bigger).
- **Revenue:** Ticketed ($15-40). Members get discounted tickets.

### Fourth Weekend: Community Showcase
- **What:** Members showcase their creations: fan fiction readings, cosplay fashion show, fan art gallery, homebrew TPMOJIO sessions.
- **Who:** Members only (but guests can attend).
- **Where:** Entire store.
- **Revenue:** Free for members. $5 for guests.

## The Quest Board (Core Programming)

### What It Is:
A physical + digital board where community members post and take quests.

### Quest Types:
| Type | Example | Value |
|------|---------|-------|
| **Game Quest** | "Need 2 players for TPMOJIO Thursday" | Fills sessions. Builds community. |
| **Learning Quest** | "Teach me to code Python" | Skill sharing. Mentorship. |
| **Community Quest** | "Help me move Saturday" | Mutual aid. Trust building. |
| **Creative Quest** | "Looking for a D&D writer" | Collaboration. Content creation. |
| **Business Quest** | "Need feedback on my app" | Networking. Entrepreneurship. |

### How It Works:
1. **Post a quest.** Physical board + digital app.
2. **AI matches.** Quest posters are matched with quest takers based on skills, interests, availability.
3. **Complete the quest.** Quest takers report completion. Quest posters rate the experience.
4. **Earn reputation.** Completed quests build reputation. High-reputation members get perks (priority seating, event discounts, membership upgrades).

### Why It Matters:
The quest board is the **community engine**. It turns a store into a network. It's the reason people come back. It's the thing competitors can't copy.

## The Honest Truth About Events

**Events are hard.** They require planning, staffing, marketing, execution. Most events lose money.

**But the right events are worth it.** They build community. They generate content. They attract new members. They create memories.

**Start small.** One event per week. See what works. Double down on what works. Cut what doesn't.

**Measure everything.** Attendance, revenue, engagement, member feedback. If an event doesn't deliver, change it or cut it.

**The best events are the ones the community creates.** Your job isn't to program every event. It's to create the space where the community programs itself.
