# TPMOJIO CAFE — Master Concept Document
## "The Third Place for Nerds, Nerds-in-Training, and Nerds-at-Heart"

---

## The Core Idea

A physical space that combines:
- **Cafe** (tea, coffee, baked goods — quality, not afterthought)
- **Board game / TTRPG lounge** (play, buy, learn)
- **Tech retail** (computers, phones, peripherals — curated, not overwhelming)
- **Community center** (events, quests, networking, classes)
- **Cultural venue** (exhibits, cosplay, streaming, open mics, comedy)

**The vibe:** Barnes & Noble's comfort × Apple Store's curation × Hot Topic's subculture energy × YMCA's community function × a game store that doesn't smell like despair.

---

## Why This Doesn't Exist Yet

| Existing Place | What They Do | What They Miss |
|---------------|-------------|----------------|
| **Barnes & Noble** | Books, cafe, events | No gaming, no tech, sterile |
| **Apple Store** | Tech, workshops | No food, no community, corporate |
| **GameStop** | Games, merch | No space to play, no community |
| **Board game cafe** | Games, food | Usually small, no tech, no events |
| **YMCA** | Community, classes | No retail, no subculture, dated |
| **Hot Topic** | Subculture merch | No space, no food, no community |

**The gap:** No one has combined quality food + curated tech + gaming space + community programming + subculture identity into one coherent brand.

## The "Quest Board" Concept

A physical + digital board where community members post:
- **Game quests** — "Need 2 players for TPMOJIO session Thursday"
- **Learning quests** — "Teach me to code Python" / "Teach me to paint miniatures"
- **Community quests** — "Help me move" / "Need a ride to the airport"
- **Creative quests** — "Looking for a D&D writer" / "Need a streamer collab"
- **Business quests** — "Looking for a co-founder" / "Need feedback on my app"

This is the **community engine**. It turns a store into a network.

## Location Strategy

| Location Type | Size | Cost | Audience | Notes |
|--------------|------|------|----------|-------|
| **Mall kiosk** | 200-400 sqft | Medium | Foot traffic | Proof of concept. Limited function. |
| **Strip mall** | 1,500-3,000 sqft | Low-Med | Suburban families | Standard model. Cafe + games + tech. |
| **Near college** | 2,000-4,000 sqft | Medium | Students, faculty | Heavy on events, classes, late hours. |
| **Near library** | 1,500-3,000 sqft | Low | Readers, families, seniors | Partnership potential. Quiet + loud zones. |
| **Downtown** | 3,000-6,000 sqft | High | Professionals, tourists | Full model. Events, exhibits, streaming. |
| **Multi-story** | 10,000+ sqft | Very High | Destination | Hotel rooms, gym, full community center. |

**Start small. Prove the model. Scale what works.**

## Open Source + Creative Commons Strategy

**Why:**
- If the stores fail, the IP survives.
- If the stores succeed, the open source community becomes a marketing engine.
- Investors can't hold the IP hostage. The community owns it.

**How:**
- **Software:** Open source (MIT/Apache). TPMOJIO digital, quest board app, session tools.
- **IP:** Creative Commons BY-NC-SA. Anyone can create TPMOJIO content. Commercial use requires license.
- **Merch:** Trademarked. Only we sell official merch. But fan art is encouraged.
- **Audio:** Free to listen. Free to share. Free to remix (non-commercial).

**Investor pitch:** "We're not selling IP. We're selling experience. The IP is the moat — it's so open that everyone uses it, but the stores are where the magic happens."

## Bootstrap vs. Investor Path

### Bootstrap Path:
1. **Year 1:** Build software (TPMOJIO digital, quest board app). Sell licenses. $50-100K revenue.
2. **Year 2:** Pop-up events at existing venues (libraries, colleges, cons). Build community. $20-50K revenue.
3. **Year 3:** First physical location (strip mall, 2,000 sqft). Funded by software revenue + pre-sold memberships. $200-400K revenue.
4. **Year 4-5:** Second location. Refine model. $500K-1M revenue.

### Investor Path:
1. **Seed:** $500K-1M for first location + software development.
2. **Series A:** $2-5M for 3-5 locations.
3. **Series B:** $10-20M for regional expansion.
4. **Exit:** Acquisition by Barnes & Noble, GameStop, or IPO.

**Recommendation:** Bootstrap as long as possible. Investors will push for speed over quality. This concept needs quality.

## Risks & Mitigations

| Risk | Likelihood | Impact | Mitigation |
|------|-----------|--------|------------|
| **Food service is hard** | High | High | Start with pre-made quality (baklava, fudge, toffee). Add kitchen later. |
| **Tech retail margins are thin** | High | Medium | Curated selection only. No competing with Amazon on price. Compete on experience. |
| **Community doesn't show up** | Medium | High | Pre-build community online. Quest board before store opens. Events before doors open. |
| **Membership model fails** | Medium | Medium | Free tier always available. Paid tiers must deliver real value (rooms, discounts, access). |
| **IP dilution** | Low | Medium | CC BY-NC-SA protects commercial rights. Trademark protects brand. |
| **Location costs kill us** | Medium | High | Start small. Prove unit economics. Don't sign long leases without proof of concept. |
| **Competition copies us** | Medium | Medium | First-mover advantage + IP moat + community loyalty. Hard to copy culture. |
