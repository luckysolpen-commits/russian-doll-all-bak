## "The IP Is the Moat. The Community Is the Product."

## The Core Philosophy

**We give away the IP. We sell the experience.**

This is counterintuitive. Most businesses hoard IP. We're doing the opposite. Here's why:

1. **If the stores fail, the IP survives.** Creative Commons means the community keeps creating. The universe lives on.
2. **If the stores succeed, the open IP becomes a marketing engine.** Every fan creation is free advertising.
3. **Investors can't hold the IP hostage.** The community owns it. We can't be acquired for the IP alone.
4. **It's the right thing to do.** The TPMOJIO universe was born from community sessions. It belongs to the community.

## IP Licensing Framework

### Software: MIT License
- **What:** All software (TPMOJIO Digital, Quest Board App, Session Tools, AI tools).
- **Terms:** Anyone can use, modify, distribute, sell. No restrictions.
- **Why:** Maximum adoption. Developers can build on it without legal friction.
- **Exception:** TPMOJIO Cafe brand name is trademarked. You can fork the code, but you can't call your store "TPMOJIO Cafe."

### Creative Content: CC BY-NC-SA 4.0
- **What:** Session transcripts, audio files, character art, lore documents, videos.
- **Terms:** Anyone can share, remix, adapt. Must attribute. Non-commercial only. Same license for derivatives.
- **Why:** Fans can create fan fiction, fan art, fan sessions. They can't sell it. They must share their creations under the same license.
- **Exception:** Commercial licensing available by agreement. If someone wants to make a TPMOJIO video game, they negotiate with us.

### Trademarks: Registered
- **What:** "TPMOJIO," "TPMOJIO Cafe," "Clone Chronicles," "HARD_VVAR," "Sol Pen," character names, logos.
- **Terms:** Only we can use these commercially. Fans can use them in non-commercial fan works.
- **Why:** Brand protection. No one else can open a "TPMOJIO Cafe" without our permission.
- **Enforcement:** Light touch. We don't sue fans. We sue competitors who try to confuse customers.

### Merchandise: Trademark + Copyright
- **What:** Plushies, figures, apparel, prints, physical products.
- **Terms:** Only we sell official merch. Fan art is encouraged but can't be sold as official.
- **Why:** Merch is a revenue stream. We protect it.
- **Exception:** Fan art marketplace. We host a section of our website where fans can sell their TPMOJIO fan art. We take 15%. They keep 85%.

## Investor Pitch: "Why Open IP Makes Us More Valuable"

### The Traditional Model:
- Company owns IP → Company monetizes IP → Company sells IP to acquirer.
- **Risk:** If the company fails, the IP dies with it.
- **Valuation:** Based on IP ownership.

### The TPMOJIO Model:
- Community owns IP → Company monetizes experience → Company can't be acquired for IP.
- **Risk:** If the company fails, the IP lives on. Community continues.
- **Valuation:** Based on community size, recurring revenue, physical locations, software platform.

### Why Investors Should Care:
1. **Lower risk.** The IP can't be stolen, copied, or competed away. It's already everywhere.
2. **Higher engagement.** Open IP means more fan content, more community growth, more brand awareness.
3. **Defensible moat.** Competitors can't copy the community. They can't copy the AI models. They can't copy the physical locations.
4. **Acquisition optionality.** We can be acquired for the brand, the locations, the software platform, the community. Not just the IP.

### The Counterargument (and Why It's Wrong):
**"If the IP is free, why would anyone pay?"**

Answer: People pay for **experience**, not content. They pay for:
- The cafe atmosphere (can't be downloaded)
- The community (can't be pirated)
- The membership perks (can't be copied)
- The physical merch (can't be 3D printed... yet)
- The events (can't be streamed with the same energy)
- The AI tools (trained on proprietary data, not public content)

**Content is free. Experience is priceless.**

## Fan Content Guidelines

### What We Encourage:
- Fan fiction set in the TPMOJIO universe
- Fan art of characters, bots, arenas
- Fan sessions (run your own TPMOJIO game, record it, share it)
- Fan merch (non-commercial, or sold through our fan marketplace)
- Fan videos (YouTube, TikTok, Twitch — TPMOJIO content is great content)
- Fan translations (make TPMOJIO accessible in every language)

### What We Don't Allow:
- Selling TPMOJIO content as your own (plagiarism)
- Using TPMOJIO trademarks to confuse customers (fake "TPMOJIO Cafe" stores)
- Hate speech, harassment, or toxicity in TPMOJIO community spaces
- Commercial use without a license (selling TPMOJIO video games, books, etc.)

### How We Enforce:
- **Light touch.** We don't sue fans. We talk to them.
- **Education first.** If someone violates guidelines, we explain why and help them comply.
- **Legal action only for competitors.** If another business tries to use our IP commercially, we enforce.

## The "Scientology Problem" (And How We Avoid It)

The user's note: *"they are just tmojio sessions, clones / msr and d&d stuff its not explicitly soulpen ;) its like scientology"*

**The concern:** If the content is too insular, too self-referential, too "inside baseball," it becomes a cult, not a community.

**How we avoid it:**
1. **TPMOJIO Cafe is not about TPMOJIO.** It's about community. TPMOJIO is the flavor, not the substance.
2. **Open to all.** You don't need to know TPMOJIO lore to enjoy the cafe. You just need to like coffee, games, and people.
3. **Multiple entry points.** Some people come for the food. Some for the games. Some for the tech. Some for the events. Some for the lore. All are valid.
4. **No gatekeeping.** No "you haven't listened to all 40 sessions? You're not a real fan." Everyone starts somewhere.
5. **Evolving content.** New sessions, new universes, new characters. The universe grows. It doesn't stagnate.
