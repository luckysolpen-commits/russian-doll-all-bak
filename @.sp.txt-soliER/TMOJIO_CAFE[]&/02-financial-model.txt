## Break-Even Analysis

**Fixed costs:** $38,300/mo (operating costs)
**Variable costs:** ~35% of revenue (COGS)
**Contribution margin:** ~65%

**Break-even revenue:** $38,300 / 0.65 = **$58,923/mo**

You need ~$59K/month in revenue to break even. That's achievable but requires:
- 200+ paying members ($15-75/mo tier)
- $20K+/mo in F&B (67 customers/day at $10 avg)
- $15K+/mo in retail
- $10K+/mo in events, classes, software, space rental

## The Membership Math (Key Lever)

Memberships are your **highest-margin, most-predictable revenue**. They're the backbone.

| Members Needed | Avg Tier | Monthly Revenue | Covers Fixed Costs? |
|---------------|----------|-----------------|-------------------|
| 50 | Player ($15) | $750 | No |
| 100 | Player ($15) | $1,500 | No |
| 200 | Mix ($30 avg) | $6,000 | No, but helps |
| 300 | Mix ($30 avg) | $9,000 | No, but getting close |
| 500 | Mix ($35 avg) | $17,500 | Covers 46% of fixed costs |
| 750 | Mix ($40 avg) | $30,000 | Covers 78% of fixed costs |
| 1,000 | Mix ($45 avg) | $45,000 | **Covers 117% of fixed costs** |

**Key insight:** If you can get 500+ members, the store is viable even if other revenue streams underperform. **Memberships are the make-or-break metric.**

## The Honest Truth

**This is a hard business.** Food service is brutal. Retail margins are thin. Community building takes years. Most cafes fail in year one. Most game stores never scale past one location.

**But here's why it could work:**

1. **You have IP.** Most cafes don't have a built-in universe of content, characters, and community.
2. **You have audio content.** 40+ sessions of free marketing. Every listener is a potential customer.
3. **You have open source ethos.** The community builds with you, not for you.
4. **Software is the hedge.** Even if stores fail, software can sustain the business.
5. **The concept is defensible.** No one else is combining cafe + tech + gaming + community + IP in one brand.

**What you need to succeed:**
- **Relentless focus on community.** The store is a vehicle. The community is the product.
- **Pre-sell everything.** Memberships before opening. Software before stores. Events before space.
- **Start small.** Prove the model at one location before scaling.
- **Be willing to pivot.** If food doesn't work, focus on events. If retail doesn't work, focus on software. If memberships don't work, focus on classes.

## Your Personal Situation

You have an MBA. You understand the numbers. What you haven't done is **run a business that makes money**. That's okay. Here's what to focus on:

1. **Don't try to build everything at once.** Start with software + pop-up events. Prove demand.

3. **Talk to 100 potential members before spending a dollar.** If they won't pre-pay, the concept isn't ready.
4. **Track one metric obsessively:** Monthly recurring revenue (memberships + software). Everything else is noise.
5. **Be okay with slow.** This is a 5-year business, not a 1-year business.
