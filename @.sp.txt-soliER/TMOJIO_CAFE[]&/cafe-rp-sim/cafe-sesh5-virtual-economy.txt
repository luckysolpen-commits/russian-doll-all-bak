**Mother Tmojizu:** NATURAL 20. Chara pulls up the legal framework:

### 1. Virtual Goods (Robot Pieces, Skins, Blueprints)
**Legal status:** Generally legal. Precedent: V-Bucks (Fortnite), WoW Gold, CS:GO Skins.
**Key requirements:**
- No cash-out. BSR can't be converted to dollars.
- Terms of service: virtual goods have no real-world value.
- No secondary market. Users can't sell pieces to each other for BSR (prevents gray market).
- Creator shops are "licensed" by the platform. Creators earn BSR as a "service reward," not as a sale.

**Chara's recommendation:** *"Virtual goods are legal as long as they're non-transferable, have no cash value, and can't be sold on a secondary market. Structure creator shops as 'service rewards' — creators earn BSR for contributing to the platform, not for selling goods."*

### 2. In-Game Businesses (Creator Shops)
**Legal status:** Legal if structured correctly. Precedent: Roblox Creator Economy, Minecraft Marketplace.
**Key requirements:**
- Creators are "platform contributors," not business owners.
- BSR earned is a "contribution reward," not revenue.
- No real-money payouts. BSR stays in the ecosystem.
- Creators can't incorporate, hire employees, or operate as real businesses. They're hobbyists earning reputation points.

**Chara's recommendation:** *"In-game businesses are legal as long as they're part of the platform's reward system. Creators earn BSR for contributing content. They don't own businesses. They don't pay taxes on BSR (because it has no cash value). They're hobbyists, not entrepreneurs."*

### 3. Virtual Stocks (Synthetic Business Tokens)
**Legal status:** Gray. Very gray. Precedent: Prediction markets, fantasy sports, social tokens.
**Key requirements:**
- Must be clearly labeled as entertainment, not investment.
- No cash-out. BSR can't be converted to dollars.
- No expectation of profit. "Stocks" are reputation trackers, not securities.
- Dividends are "bonus rewards," not financial returns.
- Geofence jurisdictions where prediction markets are illegal.

**Chara's recommendation:** *"Virtual stocks are the riskiest element. Structure them as 'creator reputation trackers.' Users can 'support' creators by locking BSR. If the creator is popular, supporters earn bonus BSR as a 'community reward.' It's not investing. It's tipping with upside. The SEC can't touch it because there's no investment contract — no expectation of profit, no cash-out, no secondary market."*

**Chara:** [in character] Here's the bottom line: if BSR has no cash value, can't be transferred, and can't be cashed out — the entire economy is legal. It's a loyalty program with game mechanics. It's V-Bucks with extra steps. It's WoW Gold with AR robots. It's legal because it's closed-loop. The moment someone can cash out BSR for dollars, the moment a secondary market emerges, the moment real money enters the equation — we're in securities law territory.
