It was Zara who found the first clue. Her angler, attuned to
the subtle whispers of the cosmos, picked up a faint, distorted
signal. It was a distress call, a desperate plea for help
from a world she had never heard of before. The signal
was weak and fragmented, but the message was clear: a great
'Madness' had descended upon their world, a plague of insanity
that was tearing their civilization apart. The name of the
planet was Xerxes, a technologically advanced but chaotic world, home
to a species of brilliant but volatile inventors.

"I've got something," Zara announced, her voice cutting through the quiet
hum of the bridge. "A distress call from a planet called
Xerxes. They're talking about a 'Madness,' a plague of insanity.
It sounds... similar to what we encountered on Neutrino Prime."
Kael, his fingers already dancing across the holographic star chart, located
Xerxes in a matter of seconds. It was a remote and
isolated world, far from the established trade routes, a place that
the XVS had long since forgotten. "Xerxes," he mused, his
brow furrowed in thought. "I've read about it in the
archives. A world of brilliant inventors, but also a world of
endless conflict. They were always on the brink of self-destruction."

"It sounds like the perfect breeding ground for the 'Darkness,'" Jax
said, his voice grim. "A world already teetering on the
edge of chaos. It wouldn't take much to push them over."
He looked at his team, his eyes filled with a steely
resolve. "Set a course for Xerxes," he commanded. "We're going
in." The 'Stargazer' changed course, its engines humming with a
newfound purpose. They were no longer adrift in the cosmic
ocean, but a guided missile, a beacon of hope aimed at
the heart of a new and terrifying darkness.

The journey to Xerxes was a long and arduous one. The
'Stargazer' was pushed to its limits, its hull groaning under the
strain of navigating through treacherous asteroid fields and volatile nebulae.
But the Anglers were undeterred. They were a team forged in
the crucible of battle, and they would not be stopped. As
they drew closer to Xerxes, the signs of the 'Madness' became
more and more apparent. The planet was surrounded by a debris
field of shattered satellites and derelict starships, a testament to
the chaos that had consumed its people. The planet itself was
a chaotic patchwork of sprawling, half-finished cities and smoldering ruins,
a world that was literally tearing itself apart.
