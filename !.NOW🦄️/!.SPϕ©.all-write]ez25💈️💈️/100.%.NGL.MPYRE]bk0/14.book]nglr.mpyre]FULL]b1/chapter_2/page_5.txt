Kato emerged from the shadows as silently as he had entered
them, his dark angler absorbing the chaotic light of the burning
city. His face was grim, his eyes holding a new, deeper
understanding of the 'Madness' that had consumed Xerxes. "I found
it," he said, his voice a low, hushed whisper that cut
through the cacophony of the city. "It's not a creature,
not like the 'Darkness' on Neutrino Prime. It's a machine."
He paused, letting the weight of his words sink in. "A
vast, planet-sized machine, buried deep beneath the surface of the
planet. It's a psychic amplifier, a device that takes the
Xerxes' own creativity and ambition and twists it into a
self-destructive frenzy."

"A machine?" Luna said, her eyes wide with a mixture of
horror and fascination. "That's... brilliant. And terrifying." She had always
known that the Xerxes were capable of great things, but she
had never imagined that they would be capable of creating a
device that could so completely and utterly destroy them. "Who
built it?" she asked. "And why?" Kato shook his
head. "I don't know," he said. "The machine is ancient,
far older than the current generation of Xerxes. It's possible
that they didn't even know it was there. They may have
simply stumbled upon it, and in their arrogance, they activated
it, unleashing a plague of their own making."

"So, we're not fighting an external enemy," Kael said, his mind
already racing through the tactical implications of this new information.
"We're fighting the Xerxes themselves, their own inner demons amplified
a thousand times over." He looked at Jax, his eyes filled
with a new sense of urgency. "We need to shut down
that machine," he said. "It's the only way to save
them." But how could they shut down a planet-sized machine
that was buried deep beneath the surface of a world consumed
by madness? It was an impossible task, a suicide mission.

But the Anglers were not ones to back down from a
challenge. They were the XVS, and they would find a way.
"Kato," Jax said, his voice calm and steady, "can you lead
us to the machine?" Kato nodded. "I can," he said.
"But it won't be easy. The machine is protected by a
series of intricate defense systems, and the closer we get to
it, the stronger the 'Madness' will become." He looked at
his teammates, his eyes filled with a silent warning. "Some
of us might not make it back."
