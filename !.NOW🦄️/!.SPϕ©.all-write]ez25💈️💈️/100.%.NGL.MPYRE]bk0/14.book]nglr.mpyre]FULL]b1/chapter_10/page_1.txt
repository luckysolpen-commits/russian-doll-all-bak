The secret war against the Sol Pen was a war of shadows and whispers, a delicate dance of deception and misdirection. The crew of the ‘Straylight,’ now the self-appointed guardians of a fragile galactic peace, had become masters of this new, unorthodox form of warfare. They were a ghost ship, a phantom, their legend growing with every successful act of sabotage, every carefully planted piece of misinformation. They were a virus in the system of the Sol Pen, a quiet, persistent, and ultimately irresistible force for chaos.

But the Sol Pen were not a monolithic entity. They were a vast, ancient, and deeply fractured organization, a collection of rival factions and competing interests, united only by their shared belief in the power of manipulation and control. The ‘Straylight’s’ campaign of chaos, while effective, had an unintended consequence: it was exacerbating the existing tensions within the Sol Pen, pushing the delicate balance of power to a breaking point.

The first signs of the schism appeared on a desolate, windswept world called Kith, a planet that was a major hub for the Sol Pen’s vast, intergalactic smuggling operations. The ‘Straylight’ arrived to find the planet in a state of open civil war, two rival factions of the Sol Pen locked in a brutal, bloody struggle for control. The secret war had just become a very public, and very messy, one.
