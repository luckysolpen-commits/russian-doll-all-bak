The ‘Straylight,’ its path cleared by a song, swooped in and rescued the trapped miners, their small freighter a beacon of hope in a world that had forgotten the meaning of the word. They had not won the war, but they had won a single, beautiful, and utterly chaotic battle. They had not defeated the Sol Pen, but they had reminded them of a truth they had long since forgotten: that even in the darkest of times, there is still music. There is still joy. There is still hope.

As the ‘Straylight’ flew away from the war-torn world of Kith, a new, and dangerous, thought began to form in the minds of the crew. They had been fighting a secret war, a war of shadows and whispers. But what if that was not enough? What if the only way to truly defeat the Sol Pen was not to fight them in the shadows, but to drag them into the light? What if the only way to win the war was to declare it?
