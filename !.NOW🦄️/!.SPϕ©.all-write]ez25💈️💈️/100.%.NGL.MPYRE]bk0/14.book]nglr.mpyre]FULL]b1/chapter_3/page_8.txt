"Then we'll starve it," Jax's voice cut through the illusion, his mental
fortress holding firm. The phantom paradise wavered, the image of the
proud official flickering like a faulty hologram. Jax wasn't fighting the
illusion; he was ignoring it. He focused on the raw hunger of
the entity, and he projected a single, unshakeable concept: Duty. It
was not an emotion. It was a principle. A cold, hard, and
indomitable fact of his existence. It was the antithesis of the
entity's chaotic, shifting nature. The dream-eater recoiled, the concept of
selfless, unwavering duty a foreign, indigestible meal.

Hana followed his lead. The images of her smiling, resurrected comrades
began to weep, their faces contorting in accusation. "You left us!"
they screamed, their voices a chorus of betrayal. But Hana stood her
ground. She focused on the love she felt for them, a love
that was now a shield of fierce, protective loyalty. "I will
not dishonor your memory by succumbing to a lie," she projected,
her voice a blade of pure conviction. She wasn't offering the entity
her grief to feed on, but her loyalty, a complex, paradoxical
emotion rooted in both love and loss. The entity shrieked, unable
to process the bitter, beautiful taste of it.

They were holding it at bay, but it was a battle of
attrition. The entity was vast, its hunger infinite. They were mortal,
their wills finite. They could not stand against it forever. It
was Zara, her mind connected most deeply to the psychic predator, who
saw the path to victory. It wasn't enough to offer it indigestible
concepts. They had to force it to confront the one thing
it could not bear: its own emptiness.

"Stop fighting it!" she commanded, her voice echoing in their minds.
"Don't project anything. Just be. Be real." She turned her own
focus inward, away from the overwhelming symphony of the multiverse, and
centered on a single, simple truth: "I am Zara." It was
a statement of pure, unadorned existence. Jax and Hana followed her
lead, their minds becoming silent, solid anchors of reality in the
swirling sea of illusion. Jax was no longer a commander; he
was Jax. Hana was no longer a chief; she was Hana.

The entity faltered, confused. Its prey had gone silent. It had
nothing to mirror, nothing to feed on. It was like a predator
that had lost its senses, blind and deaf in a world of
its own making. It was in this moment of confusion that
Zara made her move. "It's a mirror," she explained, her voice
a calm, steady current in their minds. "It shows us what
we want, what we fear. I'm going to show it itself."

It was a terrifying gamble. She was going to open her mind
completely, not to project, but to reflect. She would become a
perfect, psychic mirror, taking the full force of the entity's immense
power and turning it back on its source. She would force the
vast, empty, lonely creature to look at its own reflection for
the first time. The psychic feedback would be immense. It could
shatter her mind, leaving her a hollow shell, just like the
creature she was fighting. But it was the only way. "Hold
on to me," she whispered to Jax and Hana. "I'm going
in."
