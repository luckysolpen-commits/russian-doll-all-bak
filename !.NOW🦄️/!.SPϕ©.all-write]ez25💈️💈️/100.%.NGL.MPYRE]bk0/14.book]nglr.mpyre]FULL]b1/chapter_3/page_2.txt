The ‘Stargazer’ banked away from the scarred face of Xerxes, leaving
the nascent embers of its reconstruction behind. The mood on the
bridge was somber. The victory felt hollow, a single battle won
in a war whose true scale they were only just beginning to
comprehend. The name ‘Sol Pen’ was a ghost that haunted their
thoughts, a puzzle box of terrifying possibilities. But duty was a
relentless master, and their new orders were clear. Aether awaited.

“Aether,” Kael said, pulling up the planet’s file from the XVS
archives. The viewscreen filled with images of a world that was
the complete antithesis of Xerxes. It was a gas giant, a
swirling masterpiece of pastel clouds and shimmering atmospheric rivers. There
was no solid ground, no cities, no technology. “The Aetherians are
a species of pure energy,” he read aloud. “They live in the
planet’s upper atmosphere, communicating through empathic waves. They are a peaceful,
contemplative race with no history of violence.”

“A world of empaths,” Zara mused, her antenna-like angler pulsing softly.
“If the Sol Pen are psychic predators, this world would be a
feast for them.” The thought sent a chill through the bridge.
A species that fed on madness would find a world of pure
emotion an irresistible target. The ‘Darkness’ on Neutrino Prime had
been a blunt instrument. The ‘Madness’ on Xerxes had been a
complex, insidious machine. What form would the enemy take on a
world of pure thought and feeling?

“This will be a different kind of battle,” Jax stated, his gaze
fixed on the swirling image of Aether. “We can’t fight this
with pulse rifles and energy swords. This will be a battle
for the mind, for the heart.” He looked at Zara, then at
Luna. “You two will be our primary assets on this mission.
Your skills will be crucial.” Luna nodded, her mind already racing,
formulating theories. Zara, however, felt a knot of apprehension. To
open her mind completely to a world of empaths was to make
herself incredibly vulnerable, especially with a psychic predator on the loose.

As the ‘Stargazer’ entered the Aetherian system, they found the silence
unsettling. Aether was a major hub for XVS scientific research, a
place of constant communication and data exchange. But the subspace
channels were dead. The XVS outpost, a floating research station designed
to withstand the planet’s atmospheric pressures, was not responding to their
hails. It hung in the upper atmosphere, a silent, metallic ghost
in the pastel clouds. “I’m getting no life signs from the
outpost,” Hana reported, her voice tight with concern. “But the station’s
power is still on. All systems are nominal.” It was a ghost
ship, a Marie Celeste in the clouds of a gas giant.
They were flying into a trap. They all knew it. But
they had no other choice. “Take us in, Kael,” Jax ordered,
his voice a low, steady rumble. “Slow and steady. I want
full sensor sweeps. I want to know if a single molecule
on that station is out of place.” The ‘Stargazer’ began its
descent, a silver shark gliding into a beautiful, deadly, and silent
sea.
