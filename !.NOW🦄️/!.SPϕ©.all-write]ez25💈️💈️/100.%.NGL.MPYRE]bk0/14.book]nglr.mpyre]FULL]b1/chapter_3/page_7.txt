"It feels... lonely," Zara whispered, her voice filled with a strange, unexpected
pity. The entity in the eye of the storm, for all its
power, felt hollow. It was a parasite, a mirror, reflecting the
emotions of others but possessing none of its own. It was a
vast, empty vessel, and it was desperate to be filled. As
soon as they entered the calm eye of the tempest, the
entity turned its full attention upon them. The shuttle's cockpit dissolved
away, the illusion so perfect that they felt the wind on
their faces.

They were standing on a platform of pure, white light, suspended
in the heart of the storm. The entity hovered before them,
its form shifting rapidly, trying to find the perfect image to seduce
them. For Jax, it became the high-ranking official from XVS command,
his face beaming with pride. "Your war is over, Commander," the
figure said, its voice a perfect imitation. "You have won. The
multiverse is at peace. You can finally lay down your sword.
Rest, old soldier. You've earned it." A phantom weight lifted
from Jax's shoulders, a lifetime of command and responsibility dissolving into
a promise of serene, blissful retirement.

For Hana, the entity became her fallen comrades, their faces young
and smiling, their uniforms pristine. "We're not dead, Chief," her first
lieutenant said, his voice filled with a joyous warmth. "We made
it. We all made it. We're waiting for you. Come home."
The illusion was so powerful, so filled with love and camaraderie,
that tears welled in Hana's eyes. It was everything she had
ever wanted, a second chance to undo her greatest failure.

But it was on Zara that the entity focused its most potent
temptation. It didn't take a single form. Instead, it became
a swirling vortex of pure information, a direct feed into the
collective consciousness of the multiverse. She could feel the thoughts of
a billion billion beings, hear the songs of dying stars, taste
the joy of a newborn species taking its first breath. It was
the ultimate empathic experience, a promise of infinite knowledge, of complete
and total connection. It was a siren song tailored for her very
soul, and it was almost impossible to resist.

"It's a lie," she gritted out, her voice a lifeline for the
others. "It's a cage. A beautiful, perfect cage." She focused
her mind, her angler glowing with a fierce, defiant light. She
didn't try to block the entity's illusions. Instead, she sent out
a single, powerful question, a pure, empathic query that cut through
the phantom paradise. *Why?* The entity recoiled, its shifting forms
stuttering. It had expected fear, or desire, or anger. It had
not expected a question. It did not understand curiosity. It responded
with a wave of pure, primal hunger, a feeling so vast and
empty that it almost extinguished Zara's own light. It didn't want
to communicate. It wanted to consume. It was a predator, and
they were its prey. The time for talking was over.
