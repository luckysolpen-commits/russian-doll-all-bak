Zara took a deep breath, a silent prayer in the storm of
thought, and then she opened the floodgates of her mind. She
dropped her shields, her defenses, her very sense of self, and
became a perfect, flawless mirror. The dream-eater, sensing a sudden,
undefended opening, lunged with the full force of its infinite hunger.
It poured its entire being into Zara’s mind, expecting to consume
her, to fill its own emptiness with her life, her memories,
her emotions. But it found nothing to grasp. No resistance, no
fear, no desire. It found only its own reflection, amplified a
thousand times over.

For the first time in its long, parasitic existence, the entity
was forced to see itself for what it was: a hollow, lonely
void. A being of pure, insatiable hunger, defined only by what
it consumed. It saw the stolen dreams, the manufactured paradises, the
weaponized despair. It saw the faces of the comatose Aetherians, their
life force being siphoned away to fill a void that could
never be filled. It saw the raw, terrifying truth of its
own non-existence. The psychic feedback was cataclysmic. A silent, mental
scream of pure, existential agony erupted from the entity, a sound
that shattered the very fabric of the dreamscape.

The platform of light beneath the Anglers’ feet dissolved. The Heart
of the Tempest, the swirling vortex of psychic energy, began to unravel.
The beautiful, terrible illusions shattered like glass, the dream-castle crumbling, the
light-birds dissolving into motes of dust. The entity thrashed in the
agonizing grip of its own self-awareness, its shifting forms collapsing into
a chaotic, meaningless blur of color and light. Zara felt the
full force of this psychic death-spiral, a tsunami of pure chaos
threatening to tear her mind apart. Her body went rigid, and
a thin trickle of blood ran from her nose.

But she was not alone. Jax and Hana were her anchors.
They poured their own mental fortitude into her, not as a shield,
but as a tether, a lifeline back to reality. Jax’s unshakeable
resolve, Hana’s fierce loyalty – they wrapped around her consciousness, protecting
her from the worst of the feedback, holding the pieces of
her psyche together as the storm raged. They were a three-person
phalanx, standing firm against the dying screams of a god of
dreams. The entity, unable to bear the sight of its own
emptiness, began to implode, its vast consciousness collapsing in on
itself, a black hole of pure negation in the center of
the storm.
