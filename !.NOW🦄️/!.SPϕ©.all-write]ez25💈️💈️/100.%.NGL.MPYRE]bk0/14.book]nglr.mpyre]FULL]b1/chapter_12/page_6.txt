The ‘Darkness’ shrieked, a silent, psychic scream of pure agony and rage. It was being consumed, not by fire, but by light. Not by destruction, but by harmony. It was being reabsorbed into the very network it had sought to corrupt, its malevolent energy transformed into a pure, cleansing force. The Xylosian network pulsed with a newfound vibrancy, its ancient trees humming with a song of pure, unadulterated joy. The ‘Darkness’ was gone. The planet was free.

The Anglers, exhausted but triumphant, watched as the Xylosians, their minds clear, their hearts filled with gratitude, began to rebuild their world. They had won, not by fighting, but by healing. They had won, not by destroying, but by transforming. They had won, not by imposing their will on the universe, but by embracing the messy, beautiful, and necessary chaos of life itself.

But their victory had come at a cost. The ‘Darkness’ on Xylos was not an isolated incident. It was a symptom of a larger, more insidious disease. The Sol Pen, the shadowy architects of the previous war, were still out there, their motives as inscrutable as ever. And they were learning. Each victory was a lesson, each defeat a new data point in their ever-evolving strategy. The war was far from over. It was just getting started.
