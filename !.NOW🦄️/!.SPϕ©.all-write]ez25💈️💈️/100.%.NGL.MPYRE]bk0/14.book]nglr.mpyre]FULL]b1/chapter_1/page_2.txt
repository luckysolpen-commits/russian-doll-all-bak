The seven Anglers stood at attention, their combined presence filling the
vast chamber with a palpable energy. Before them, a holographic
projection flickered to life, coalescing into the stern visage of
a high-ranking official. His uniform was adorned with the sigil
of the XVS, a stylized representation of a swirling galaxy
held in the grasp of a celestial hand. His name was
not important; he was a voice of authority, a conduit for
the will of the multiversal government. His eyes, ancient and
wise, seemed to pierce through the very fabric of their beings,
gauging their readiness for the task that lay ahead.

"Anglers," his voice boomed, echoing in the silent chamber, "you have
been chosen for a mission of the highest priority. We have
detected a series of anomalous energy signatures emanating from a distant,
aquatic planet known as Neutrino Prime. These are not random
fluctuations; they are deliberate, calculated, and they bear the unmistakable
mark of a hostile intelligence. Your mission is to investigate these
occurrences, to identify the source of this disturbance, and to
restore balance to the planet."

The official's gaze swept across the faces of the seven, lingering
for a moment on each of them. "You are the XVS,"
he continued, his voice softening slightly, "the guardians of the multiverse.
It is your duty to maintain order, to protect the innocent,
and to ensure that the delicate tapestry of existence is not
unraveled by the forces of chaos. This is not just a
mission; it is a sacred trust. The fate of Neutrino Prime,
and perhaps countless other worlds, rests in your hands."

He paused, letting the weight of his words sink in. "Vorith,"
he said, and the name hung in the air, heavy with
an unspoken history, "one of our most promising commanders, has
been monitoring this situation. His reports are... troubling. He speaks
of a 'Darkness' that has taken root on the planet, a
pervasive corruption that has begun to twist the very fabric of
reality. It is a vague and unsettling description, but Vorith is
not one for hyperbole. We must take his warnings seriously."

The mention of Vorith sent a subtle ripple through the team.
He was a legendary figure in the XVS, a brilliant strategist and
a ruthless tactician. If he was troubled, then the situation
on Neutrino Prime was far more dangerous than they had initially
imagined. The official's projection flickered again, and a star chart
materialized in the air, a glowing, three-dimensional map of the sector
containing Neutrino Prime. A single, pulsating red dot marked their
destination. "Your vessel, the 'Stargazer,' is ready for departure. Go
now, Anglers. And may the light of the multiverse guide your
way."

The hologram vanished, leaving the seven Anglers alone in the vast
chamber. For a moment, there was only silence, the weight of
their mission settling upon them like a physical shroud. Then, Jax
- his voice calm and steady - broke the silence. "You heard
the man," he said, his gaze sweeping over his team. "Let's
get to work."
