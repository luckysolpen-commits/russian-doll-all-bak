The battle for Archon was a brutal, desperate affair. The ‘Darkness,’ now a sentient, malevolent storm of pure fear, was a far more formidable opponent than the nascent entity they had faced on Neutrino Prime. It didn’t just create monsters; it turned the Archons themselves into living nightmares, their bodies twisted into grotesque parodies of their deepest fears. The Anglers fought with a grim, desperate courage, but they were outmatched. Their weapons were useless against an enemy that was not a physical being, but an emotion.

It was Jureth who saw the path to victory. He saw that the ‘Darkness’ was not a force of nature, but a parasite, and its greatest strength was also its greatest weakness. It fed on fear, but it was also terrified of its own extinction. It was a creature of pure, unadulterated terror, and it could be defeated by a single, simple, and utterly terrifying act: the complete and total absence of fear.

He proposed a radical, desperate plan. The Anglers would lower their shields, open their minds, and willingly, consciously, embrace the ‘Darkness.’ They would not fight it, but invite it in, and then, in the heart of that storm of terror, they would find a single, quiet point of absolute, unshakeable calm. It was a suicide mission, a journey into the heart of madness from which they might never return. But it was the only way. One by one, the Anglers, their faces a mixture of terror and resolve, agreed. They would face their fears, not as soldiers, but as mystics, as philosophers, as explorers of the darkest corners of the soul.
