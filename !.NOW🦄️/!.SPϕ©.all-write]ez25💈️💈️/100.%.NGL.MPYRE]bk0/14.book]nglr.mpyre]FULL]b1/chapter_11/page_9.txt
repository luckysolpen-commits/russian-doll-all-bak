The climax of the battle of broadcasts was not a sudden, dramatic
explosion, but a slow, agonizing unraveling. Vorith’s broadcast, for all
its power, was a rigid, unyielding monolith. It could not adapt,
could not evolve, could not embrace the messy, beautiful chaos of
life. Jureth’s counter-broadcast, for all its apparent chaos, was a living,
breathing, constantly evolving work of art. It was a river, and
Vorith’s broadcast was a dam. And the river was about to
break.

The galaxy, its mind and soul torn between the two opposing philosophies,
began to choose. Not as a single, unified entity, but as
a billion different individuals, each making their own choice, each casting
their own vote in the silent, invisible war for the soul
of the galaxy. The votes were cast not with words, but
with actions, with choices, with the very way they lived their
lives.

Vorith’s broadcast began to falter. Its perfect, orderly harmonies dissolved into
a chaotic jumble of desperate, pleading notes. The promise of peace,
of stability, of a return to the comforting predictability of the
old ways, began to ring hollow. The price of Vorith’s peace
was too high, its cost measured in the quiet death of
individuality, of freedom, of the messy, beautiful chaos that is the
essence of life.

Jureth’s counter-broadcast, for all its apparent chaos, was a living, breathing,
constantly evolving work of art. It was a river, and Vorith’s
broadcast was a dam. And the river was about to break.
