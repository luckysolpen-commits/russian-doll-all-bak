The first strike came not with a bang, but with a whisper.
Vorith, now operating from the shadows, began to subtly undermine the
fragile peace of the Unlit Tribe. He didn’t send fleets; he
sent propaganda. He didn’t declare war; he sowed discord. He used
the very freedom the Unlit Tribe championed against them, exploiting the
chaos, amplifying the doubts, and twisting the words of Jureth to
serve his own insidious agenda.

His agents, unseen and unheard, infiltrated the nascent governments of the
Unlit worlds. They whispered promises of order, of stability, of a
return to the comforting predictability of the old ways. They exploited
the fear of the unknown, the longing for a guiding hand, the
weariness of a galaxy that was still reeling from the trauma of
war. They offered solutions to the chaos, solutions that were simple,
elegant, and utterly totalitarian.

The Unlit Tribe, in its embrace of individual freedom, was ill-equipped
to deal with this new, insidious form of warfare. They were
used to fighting monsters, to battling overt threats. But how do
you fight a whisper? How do you fight an idea that is
being subtly woven into the very fabric of your society? The
answer was not clear, and the consequences were devastating.

Worlds that had once embraced the unlit path began to waver.
Old alliances fractured, new tensions emerged, and the messy, beautiful chaos
they had fought for was beginning to look a lot like
anarchy. The XVS, though officially dissolved, began to re-emerge in the
shadows, its old, rigid structures offering a comforting sense of order
in a world that was spiraling out of control. Vorith was not
conquering the galaxy; he was simply allowing it to conquer itself.

Jureth, watching from his quiet retreat, felt a profound sense of despair.
His philosophy, his life’s work, was being twisted, corrupted, used to
justify the very tyranny he had fought so hard to overthrow.
He saw the galaxy he had liberated slowly, surely, slipping back
into the darkness, not with a bang, but with a whimper. He
knew he had to act, but how do you fight a shadow?
How do you fight an idea that is being subtly woven into
the very fabric of your society? The answer was not clear,
and the consequences were devastating.
