The crew of the ‘Straylight’ was divided. Kaelen, the pragmatist, argued for silence. The galaxy was not ready for the truth. The fragile peace that the Unlit Tribe had created was too precious to risk. Lyra, the idealist, argued for revelation. The truth, no matter how painful, was the only path to true, lasting freedom. Jaxon, the haunted clone, was torn between the two, his mind a battlefield of conflicting loyalties, of a desire for peace and a thirst for justice.

The debate raged for days, the small freighter a microcosm of the larger conflict that was tearing the galaxy apart. They were a crew of misfits and outcasts, a found family on the verge of being torn apart by the weight of an impossible choice.

In the end, it was the silent, enigmatic alien who made the decision. They had not spoken a single word since joining the crew, their thoughts a mystery, their motives unknown. But now, they spoke. Their voice was not a sound, but a feeling, a wave of pure, unadulterated empathy that washed over the crew, silencing their arguments, soothing their fears, and uniting them in a single, shared purpose.
