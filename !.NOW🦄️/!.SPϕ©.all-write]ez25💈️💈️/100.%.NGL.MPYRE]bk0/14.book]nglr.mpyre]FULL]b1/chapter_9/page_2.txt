Our story now focuses on a new tribe of seven, a group of young adventurers from a dozen different worlds, drawn together by a shared thirst for the unknown. They were not soldiers, or scientists, or spies, but explorers, their vessel not a warship, but a small, fast, and perpetually malfunctioning freighter called the ‘Straylight.’ They were a motley crew, a found family of misfits and outcasts, united by a shared love for the unlit void and a healthy disrespect for authority.

There was Kaelen, the pilot, a hotshot prodigy who could fly the ‘Straylight’ through an asteroid field with his eyes closed. He was the grandson of the original Kael, and he had inherited his grandfather’s strategic mind, but none of his discipline. He was a gambler, a risk-taker, a man who lived for the thrill of the unknown.

Then there was Zara’s daughter, a young woman named Lyra. She had inherited her mother’s empathic abilities, but she was not a diplomat. She was a musician, her instrument a strange, multi-stringed device of her own invention. She could play the songs of the unlit void, melodies that could soothe a savage beast or stir a revolution.

There was Jax’s clone, a young man named Jaxon, created by a rogue geneticist who had been obsessed with the legendary commander. Jaxon had all of Jax’s strength, his courage, his tactical genius. But he was haunted by the ghost of his original, a man he could never hope to live up to.
