The crew of the ‘Straylight’ had fired the first shot in their secret war. They had not won, but they had drawn first blood. They had not exposed the Sol Pen, but they had made them afraid. They had not started a war, but they had joined one. A secret war, a war of whispers and shadows, a war that would be fought not for the fate of the galaxy, but for its soul.

And so, the ‘Straylight’ flew on, a small, insignificant ship in a vast, uncaring universe. But it was also a spark of light in the darkness, a single, defiant note of hope in a symphony of despair. It was a ghost ship, a phantom, a rumor on the solar winds. It was the Unlit’s unlit, the darkness that fought the darkness, the shadow that hunted the shadows. It was the beginning of a new legend, a new song, a new story in the endless, beautiful, and necessary chaos of the universe.
