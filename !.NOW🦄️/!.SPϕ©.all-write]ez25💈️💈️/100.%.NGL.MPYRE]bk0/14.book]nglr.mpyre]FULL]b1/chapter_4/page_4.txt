The plan was audacious, bordering on suicidal. A full frontal assault was
out of the question. The golden fleet would annihilate them before they
even reached the planet’s atmosphere. They had to be smarter, more
subtle. They had to use the Nexus’s own arrogance and ambition
against them. The mission would be a delicate, high-stakes dance of
deception and infiltration, a masterpiece of strategic misdirection orchestrated by Kael
and executed by the entire team. It was a plan with a
thousand moving parts, and if a single one failed, they would
all be consumed by the golden fire.

The first step was to create a diversion. Jack, the master
mechanic, modified a series of asteroids, fitting them with cloaking devices
and powerful, short-range warp drives. On Kael’s command, these ‘ghost
rocks’ began to appear on the edge of the Nexus system, their
erratic movements and strange energy signatures designed to mimic a new,
unidentified threat. The Nexus, in their arrogance, would not be able
to resist the temptation of a new potential vassal or rival.
They would dispatch a portion of their golden fleet to investigate,
leaving the throne world momentarily less defended.

While the ghost rocks danced on the edge of their sensors, the
‘Stargazer’ itself would make a bold, desperate move. They would approach
Nexus not as an enemy, but as a supplicant. Zara, her
empathic abilities now fully recovered, would broadcast a message of surrender,
of awe, of a desire to bask in the glorious light of
the Nexus’s power. It was a humiliating, galling deception, but a
necessary one. They would offer themselves up as willing converts, their
ship and its crew a tribute to the new emperor. The
Nexus, blinded by their own divine radiance, would see it as
their due. They would allow the ‘Stargazer’ to approach, to enter
their holy space, a moth drawn to a flame.

Once in orbit, the final, most dangerous phase of the plan
would begin. A small, specialized team – Jax, Hana, and Kato –
would use a short-range teleporter to beam directly into the emperor’s
throne room. The teleporter was a risky piece of technology, prone
to interference, especially in an environment saturated with the golden energy.
They would have only a few precious seconds to act before the
emperor’s personal guard, the elite of the hive mind, overwhelmed them.
Their goal was not to kill the emperor, but to sever his
connection to the ‘Power.’ Luna had developed a device, a ‘psychic
siphon,’ designed to do just that. It was a dangerous, untested piece
of technology, and its effects were entirely theoretical. It could sever
the connection, or it could overload and kill them all.

It was a plan built on a knife’s edge, a symphony of
maybes and what-ifs. But it was the only plan they had.
As the ghost rocks began their cosmic ballet and Zara composed
her honeyed words of surrender, the Anglers prepared for their most
daring gamble yet. They were no longer just soldiers; they were
actors, saboteurs, and assassins, playing their part in a grand, cosmic
theatre of deception. The fate of a thousand worlds rested on
their performance.
