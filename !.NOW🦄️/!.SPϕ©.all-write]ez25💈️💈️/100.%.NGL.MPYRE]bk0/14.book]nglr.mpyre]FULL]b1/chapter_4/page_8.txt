The emperor made no move to defend himself as Jax charged towards the throne. He was a god, and gods do not concern themselves with the struggles of insects. He simply watched, a faint, arrogant smile on his transfigured face, confident in his absolute, unassailable power. Jax, his lungs burning, his body screaming in protest, pushed himself to the limit, his energy sword held high. He was a single, defiant spark of blue in a universe of overwhelming gold. He reached the throne, his sword arcing down towards the emperor’s head.

And then, nothing. His sword stopped a hair’s breadth from the emperor’s brow, held fast by an invisible wall of pure force. The emperor’s smile widened. “Your will is strong, little Angler,” his voice echoed in the chamber. “But will is nothing against the tide of true power.” He raised a single, ethereal finger, and Jax was sent flying backwards, his body wracked with a pain so intense that it almost shattered his consciousness. He landed in a heap at the foot of the throne, his energy sword clattering to the crystal floor.

Hana cried out, her voice a mixture of anger and despair. She redoubled her assault, her grenades exploding with a futile fury against the golden shields of the guards. Kato, having sown his seeds of disruption, was now engaged in a deadly game of cat and mouse with the guards whose connection he had severed. They were no longer perfect puppets, but they were still powerful, and they were furious. The plan was failing. They had underestimated the sheer, raw power of the emperor.

But Jax was not defeated. He pushed himself to his knees, his body trembling, his vision swimming. He reached for the psychic siphon, the black cylinder that was their last, desperate hope. With a final, defiant roar, he activated it. The device hummed to life, a low, dissonant sound that was a stark contrast to the harmonious hum of the throne room. A beam of black, anti-energy shot out from the siphon, striking the emperor in the chest. For the first time, the emperor’s smile faltered. He looked down at the black beam, a flicker of surprise, of annoyance, in his golden eyes. He had felt that.
