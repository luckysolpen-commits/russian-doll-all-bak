The new Anglers, their first mission a success, found themselves at a crossroads. They could continue their quiet, patient work, nurturing the fragile seeds of freedom, fighting the subtle battles of ideas and philosophies. Or they could take a more active role, seeking out the remnants of the old order, confronting the lingering shadows of the Sol Pen, and actively shaping the future of the galaxy.

The decision was not an easy one. The galaxy was a messy, beautiful, and chaotic place, and the Unlit Tribe was not a monolith. There were those who argued for caution, for a slow, deliberate approach. And there were those who argued for action, for a bold, decisive strike against the forces of darkness.

In the end, the decision was made not by a council, or a government, or a single, charismatic leader. It was made by a billion different individuals, on a thousand different worlds. It was made in the quiet, secret chambers of a billion different hearts. The galaxy chose to act. Not as a single, unified army, but as a beautiful, chaotic, and constantly evolving mosaic of resistance.
