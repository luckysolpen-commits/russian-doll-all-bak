The war for the soul of the galaxy was a long, slow, and brutal one. Vorith’s golden armada, for all its power, was helpless against an enemy that was not a fleet, but an idea. The more he tried to crush the Unlit Tribe, the more their message of freedom spread. The more he tightened his grip, the more systems slipped through his fingers. He was a man trying to hold back the tide with a broom, a king screaming at the wind.

The Unlit Tribe, for their part, suffered greatly. They were hunted, persecuted, their homes destroyed, their people scattered. But they did not break. They did not surrender. They held fast to their belief in a different way, a better way. They were a small, flickering candle in a vast, encroaching darkness, but they were a candle that would not be extinguished.

And then, the tide began to turn. The XVS, its resources stretched thin, its morale at an all-time low, began to crumble from within. The seeds of doubt that Jureth had sown had taken root, and they were beginning to bear fruit. The soldiers of the golden armada, their faith in Vorith’s rigid, dogmatic ideology shaken, began to question. They began to listen to the songs of the Unlit Tribe, to read the words of the Book of Jureth, and they began to see the truth.
