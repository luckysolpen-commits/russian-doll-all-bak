The question, a single, tiny seed of chaos, spread through the crystalline consciousness like a wildfire. The Pyrians, their minds unequipped to deal with a concept that had no logical answer, began to falter. Their perfect, geometric movements became clumsy, their silent, harmonious thoughts dissolving into a cacophony of confused, contradictory voices. The perfect, beautiful, and utterly fragile machine of their collective consciousness was beginning to break.

The planet itself began to react to the growing chaos. The perfect, crystalline cities began to crack, to shatter, their beautiful, geometric forms collapsing into a chaotic jumble of sharp, dangerous angles. The petrified forests began to crumble, their silent, lifeless beauty dissolving into a cloud of gray, sterile dust. The silent, orderly universe of the Pyrians was coming apart at the seams.

The Anglers, their minds their own once more, watched in horror as the world they had come to save destroyed itself. They had won, but their victory was a hollow, bitter one. They had shattered the beautiful, silent, and utterly lifeless order of the Pyrians, but they had replaced it with a chaotic, terrifying, and equally lifeless void. They had not saved the Pyrians; they had simply given them a different kind of death.
