The end of the war came not with a great, climactic battle, but with a quiet, simple act of defiance. A single ship in Vorith’s golden armada, its crew having embraced the philosophy of the unlit path, refused to fire on a colony of Unlit refugees. It was a small act, a single, quiet note of dissent in the symphony of Vorith’s power. But it was enough.

The act of defiance spread like a wildfire. Ship after ship, fleet after fleet, laid down their arms. The golden armada, the symbol of Vorith’s absolute, unquestioning authority, dissolved into a thousand points of individual, dissenting light. Vorith, his power broken, his ideology in ruins, was left alone on the bridge of his flagship, a king with no kingdom, a god with no believers.

The war was over. The Unlit Tribe had won. They had not conquered the galaxy, but had liberated it. They had not brought peace, but a new, more interesting, and ultimately more beautiful kind of chaos. The age of the XVS was over. The age of the Unlit had begun.
