The aftermath of the Pyra mission was a disaster. The planet was a ruin, its inhabitants a scattered, broken people, their minds shattered by the sudden, violent return to a chaotic, unpredictable universe. The Anglers had won the battle, but they had lost the war. They had proven Vorith right. Their unorthodox methods were not just dangerous; they were destructive.

The Anglers were recalled to the XVS central command, not as heroes, but as criminals. They were stripped of their rank, their ship, their very identities. They were put on trial, their every action, every decision, every unorthodox method, scrutinized, dissected, and condemned. They were a symbol of a failed experiment, a cautionary tale against the dangers of thinking outside the box.

But the trial was a sham, a piece of political theater orchestrated by Vorith to consolidate his own power, to reaffirm the old, rigid, and utterly unquestioning ways of the XVS. The Anglers knew they had no hope of a fair hearing. They were being set up to take the fall for a war that was far bigger, far more complex, than the XVS was willing to admit.
