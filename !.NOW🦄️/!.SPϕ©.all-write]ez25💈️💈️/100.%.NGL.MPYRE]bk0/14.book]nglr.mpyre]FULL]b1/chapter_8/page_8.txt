And Jureth, the reluctant prophet, the young cartographer who had started a revolution with a song, returned to his maps. He spent the rest of his days charting the unlit void, not as a place of darkness and despair, but as a place of infinite possibility, of a quiet, peaceful, and ultimately more honest way of being. His ‘Book of Jureth,’ once a heretical text, became a classic, its message of balance and acceptance a guiding light for a galaxy that was finally, truly, free.

The age of the Anglers was over. The age of the Unlit had begun. It was not an age of peace, or of order, or of a single, absolute truth. It was an age of chaos, of creativity, of a messy, beautiful, and necessary imperfection. It was an age of art. And it was beautiful.
