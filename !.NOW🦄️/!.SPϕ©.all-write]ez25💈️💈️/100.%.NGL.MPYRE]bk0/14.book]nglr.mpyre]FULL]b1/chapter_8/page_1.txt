Jureth’s declaration of war, his sermon on the unlit void, shattered the sterile, controlled atmosphere of the tribunal. Vorith, his face a mask of cold, controlled fury, saw his carefully orchestrated political theater collapsing into a genuine ideological schism. He had intended to make the Anglers an example, a symbol of failure. Instead, Jureth had transformed them into martyrs, symbols of a new, dangerous, and undeniably compelling philosophy. The tribunal was no longer a trial; it was a battlefield of ideas, and the war for the soul of the XVS was being fought not with weapons, but with words.

The Anglers, who had entered the tribunal as disgraced criminals, now found themselves at the center of a burgeoning revolution. Jureth’s words had resonated with a deep, unspoken current of doubt and dissatisfaction that had been growing within the XVS for years. The war against the Sol Pen had exposed the cracks in their rigid, dogmatic ideology, and Jureth’s philosophy offered a new, more flexible, and ultimately more honest way of understanding the universe.

The XVS was split in two. On one side were the traditionalists, the loyalists, led by Vorith. They clung to the old ways, to the belief in a single, absolute truth, to the idea of a universe where light and dark were locked in an eternal, uncompromising struggle. On the other side were the revolutionaries, the heretics, the followers of the ‘unlit path,’ with Jureth as their reluctant, unassuming prophet. They saw a universe of shades, of balance, of a necessary, beautiful, and ultimately liberating chaos. The Anglers, the seven who had seen the face of the enemy in all its terrifying, multifaceted forms, were the heart of this new, burgeoning movement.
