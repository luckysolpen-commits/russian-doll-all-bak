Vorith, his face a mask of cold, disbelief, watched as his perfect, orderly universe crumbled around him. He had lost. He had been defeated, not by a superior force, but by a superior idea. He had been defeated by the simple, profound, and ultimately irresistible power of a song.

In a final, desperate act of nihilistic rage, he turned his ultimate weapon not on the refugee moon, but on himself. He would not live in a universe that did not conform to his rigid, dogmatic, and ultimately lifeless ideology. He would rather be erased than be wrong.

The golden flagship, the symbol of his absolute, unquestioning authority, vanished in a silent, instantaneous flash of utter nothingness. The war was over. The tyrant was dead. The galaxy was free.
