The original seven Anglers, their mission complete, their purpose fulfilled, went their separate ways. They were no longer soldiers, but legends, their stories told and retold across a thousand different worlds. Jax, the stoic commander, retired to a quiet, peaceful world, his days of war finally over. Hana, the fierce guardian, dedicated her life to protecting the innocent, not with a pulse rifle, but with a fierce, unwavering compassion. Kael, the brilliant strategist, became a teacher, his lessons not of war, but of peace. Zara, the empathetic communicator, became a diplomat, her voice a bridge between a thousand different cultures, a thousand different ways of being.

Luna, the brilliant scientist, dedicated her life to the study of the unlit void, her work not to conquer it, but to understand it. Jack, the master mechanic, became an artist, his hands, once used to build weapons, now used to create beautiful, whimsical, and utterly useless machines. And Kato, the silent observer, vanished into the shadows, his presence a quiet, unseen reminder that the universe was a place of infinite mystery, of a beauty that was not always visible to the naked eye.
