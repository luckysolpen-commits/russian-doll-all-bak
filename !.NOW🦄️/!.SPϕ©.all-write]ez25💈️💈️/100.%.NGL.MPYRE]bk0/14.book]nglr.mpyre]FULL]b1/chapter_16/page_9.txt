The new Anglers, their mission now clear, set a new course. They were no longer just guardians, but active agents of change, their vessel not a warship, but a beacon of hope in the vast, unlit void. They would seek out the lingering shadows of the old order, they would confront the insidious manipulations of the Sol Pen, and they would actively shape the future of the galaxy.

Their journey took them to the forgotten corners of the galaxy, to worlds that had been untouched by the revolution, to places where the old ways still held sway. They encountered resistance, not from fleets and armies, but from fear, from ignorance, from the comforting predictability of the past. They fought not with weapons, but with words, with ideas, with the simple, profound, and ultimately irresistible power of a different way of being.

They were a small, insignificant ship in a vast, uncaring universe. But they were also a spark of light in the darkness, a single, defiant note of hope in a symphony of despair. They were the Unlit’s unlit, the darkness that fought the darkness, the shadow that hunted the shadows. They were the Straylight, and their story was just beginning.
