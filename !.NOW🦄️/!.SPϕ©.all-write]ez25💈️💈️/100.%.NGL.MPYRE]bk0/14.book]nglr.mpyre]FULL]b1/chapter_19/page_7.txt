The new Anglers, their first mission a success, returned to the ‘Straylight’ with a newfound sense of purpose. They had faced a new kind of darkness, and they had emerged victorious. They had proven that Jureth’s philosophy, the unlit path, was not just a theory, but a practical, powerful way of navigating the messy, beautiful, and dangerous chaos of the universe. They were a new generation, a new hope, and they were ready for whatever the unlit void could throw at them.

But the galaxy was a vast and complex place, and the echoes of the past were not so easily silenced. Vorith, the ambitious XVS officer who had been swept away by the tide of revolution, was still out there, whispering promises of control and stability to those who longed for the comforting predictability of the past. His ‘Art of Subtle Dominion’ continued to spread, a subtle manipulation of the very chaos that the Unlit Tribe embraced. He was a master of the long game, a patient predator waiting for the perfect moment to strike.

And the Sol Pen, the shadowy architects of the previous war, remained an enigma. They had vanished after the fall of the XVS, their motives and their true nature still shrouded in mystery. Were they defeated? Were they simply waiting? Or were they, perhaps, something far more insidious, a force that transcended the petty squabbles of galactic politics, a force that was simply observing, learning, and adapting?
