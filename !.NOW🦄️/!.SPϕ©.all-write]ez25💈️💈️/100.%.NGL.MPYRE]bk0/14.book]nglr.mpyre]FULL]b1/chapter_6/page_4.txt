One by one, the Anglers followed Jureth’s lead. They stopped fighting, stopped resisting. They opened their minds completely, allowing the ‘Darkness’ to wash over them, to consume them. And in the heart of that storm of absolute, soul-crushing terror, they found it. A single, quiet point of perfect, unshakeable calm. It was the eye of the storm, the silent, still center of their own being. It was the part of them that was not a soldier, or a scientist, or a spy, but something more, something eternal. It was their soul.

The ‘Darkness’ recoiled, confused. It had expected resistance, a struggle, a feast of fear. It had not expected this quiet, unshakeable acceptance. It was like a fire that had been starved of oxygen, a storm that had lost its wind. It began to dissipate, to unravel, its power fading, its form dissolving into a fine, black dust. The Anglers stood in the silent, empty cavern, their minds their own once more. They were bruised, they were battered, they were forever changed by their journey into the heart of fear. But they were alive. And they were free.
