The Celestials were not fighting back. They were not cowering in fear, or lost in a blissful dream, or consumed by a lust for power. They were simply… watching. They stood amidst the chaos, their ancient, timeless faces a mask of serene, unreadable calm. They were observing the Sol Pen’s assault as they would observe a storm, a supernova, a natural, cosmic event. They were a species that had transcended the very idea of conflict, of struggle, of a universe where one side had to win and the other had to lose.

The Anglers were faced with a new, and final, challenge. How do you save a people who have transcended the very idea of salvation? How do you fight a war for a species that does not believe in war? They were not soldiers on a battlefield, but children in a library, their noisy, violent struggle a meaningless distraction in a place of quiet, timeless wisdom.

It was in this moment of profound, humbling insignificance that the Anglers finally understood. They saw the Sol Pen not as an enemy, but as a symptom of a larger, cosmic imbalance. They saw the XVS not as a force for good, but as a rigid, dogmatic institution that was just as blind, just as trapped in its own beautiful, orderly, and ultimately lifeless ideology, as the enemies they were fighting. They saw that the true war was not between light and dark, but between a universe that was a machine, and a universe that was a work of art.
