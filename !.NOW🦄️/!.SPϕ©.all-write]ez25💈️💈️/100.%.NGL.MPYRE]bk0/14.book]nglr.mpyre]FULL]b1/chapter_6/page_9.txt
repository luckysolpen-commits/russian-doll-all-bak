The Anglers laid down their arms. They stopped fighting, stopped resisting. They simply stood with the Celestials, their minds open, their hearts at peace. They were no longer soldiers, but students, and the lesson was the universe itself.

The Sol Pen’s assault, deprived of its audience, of its purpose, of its very reason for being, began to falter. The ‘Darkness,’ and the ‘Madness,’ and the ‘Dream,’ and the ‘Power,’ and the ‘Hope,’ and the ‘Control,’ all dissolved into a meaningless jumble of chaotic, contradictory notes. The great symphony of cosmic chaos, without a listener, without a critic, without a single, dissenting voice to give it meaning, simply faded away.

The Anglers had won, not by fighting, but by surrendering. They had won, not by destroying, but by understanding. They had won, not by imposing their will on the universe, but by accepting the messy, beautiful, and necessary chaos of a universe that was not a machine, but a living, breathing, and constantly evolving work of art.
