The final mission of the original seven Anglers was to a world called Celestia, a planet inhabited by a species of beings who were said to be the oldest, wisest, and most powerful in the galaxy. They were the keepers of the great library of the cosmos, the silent observers who had watched empires rise and fall, who had seen the birth and death of stars. And they, too, had been touched by the Sol Pen.

The energy signature on Celestia was a terrifying amalgamation of everything they had faced before. It was the ‘Darkness,’ and the ‘Madness,’ and the ‘Dream,’ and the ‘Power,’ and the ‘Hope,’ and the ‘Control,’ all woven together into a single, monstrous symphony of cosmic chaos. The Sol Pen had thrown everything they had at the Celestials, a final, desperate attempt to break the will of the oldest and wisest beings in the universe.

The Anglers knew that this was their final test. They knew that Vorith was watching, that the fate of their burgeoning revolution rested on the outcome of this mission. They descended to the surface of Celestia, a world of breathtaking, impossible beauty, a world that was now a battlefield of warring ideologies, a canvas for the Sol Pen’s most terrifying, most beautiful, and most destructive work of art.
