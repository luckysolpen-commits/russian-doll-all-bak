EXT. NEUTRINO PRIME - LANDING ZONE - DAY

The Anglers’ sleek XVS shuttle descends through a shimmering, bioluminescent atmosphere. Below, a world of living light unfolds: towering coral-like structures pulse with soft blues and greens, schools of glowing fish dart through crystalline waters, and the very air hums with vibrant energy.

Jax, Kael, Zara, Jack, Luna, Hana, and Kato disembark. Their angler features, usually subtle, seem to react to the overwhelming light of Neutrino Prime, their horns glowing with increased intensity.

ZARA
(Awe in her voice)
Incredible. The energy readings are off the charts. Pure, unadulterated life force.

But as they take a few steps, the vibrant hues around them begin to dim. Patches of bioluminescence flicker and die, leaving behind dull, grey areas. The humming energy falters, replaced by an unsettling silence.

LUNA
(Frowning, scanning with a handheld device)
Readings are fluctuating wildly. There’s a… drain. A systemic decay.

JACK
(Kicking at a patch of dead coral)
Looks like something’s sucking the life out of this place.

Suddenly, a NEUTRINO (a graceful, glowing humanoid) emerges from the dimness. Its bioluminescence is weak, flickering erratically, and its movements are slow, almost sluggish. It stares at the Anglers with vacant eyes.

ZARA
(Stepping forward, her horn glowing brightly, attempting to communicate)
Greetings. We are from the XVS. We mean no harm. We are here to help.

Zara’s horn pulses with a complex array of colors, attempting to mimic the Neutrino’s natural light patterns. But the Neutrino only blinks slowly, its light dimming further, and it turns away, drifting back into the shadows.

ZARA (CONT'D)
(Frustrated)
It’s not responding. It’s like… it’s not even registering my presence. The communication channels are there, but the will to engage isn’t.

KATO
(Quietly, observing the retreating Neutrino)
Not just a lack of will, Ensign. A lack of… everything. It’s hollow.

HANA
(Hand on her spright-saber)
Whatever this corruption is, it’s deep. And it’s affecting their very essence.

Jax surveys the fading beauty of the world, his expression grim.

JAX
This isn’t just an anomaly. This is a sickness. We need to find the source. And quickly.

FADE OUT.
