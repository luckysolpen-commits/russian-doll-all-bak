INT. XVS SHUTTLE - COMMON AREA - NIGHT

The atmosphere in the XVS shuttle is thick with unspoken tension. Jureth’s philosophy of the “unlit void” has taken root, subtly dividing the team. Jureth, Luna, and Kato are often found in quiet discussion, their horns glowing with a shared, almost conspiratorial light. Jax, Kael, Hana, and Jack maintain a more traditional stance, their loyalty to the Empire unwavering, yet a flicker of unease is present.

Jax watches Jureth, Luna, and Kato from across the common area. His horn pulses with a slow, heavy rhythm.

JAX
(To Kael, his voice low)
The boy’s words… they’re spreading. Like a virus.

KAEL
(His strategic horn calculating, but with a hint of frustration)
It’s not a virus, Commander. It’s an idea. And ideas, once planted, are difficult to eradicate. Luna’s scientific mind is drawn to its novelty. Kato… well, Kato is always drawn to the shadows.

Hana approaches, her security horn scanning the subtle shifts in body language among the team.

HANA
The cohesion is… fracturing. Small things. Disagreements over mission parameters. Subtle challenges to authority.

Jack, who has been listening, speaks up, his horn’s light a steady, practical glow.

JACK
Look, I get it. The Empire’s got its rules. But Jureth… he’s got a point. We’ve seen some messed up stuff out there. And sometimes, the Empire’s way… it just doesn’t feel right.

Jax turns to Jack, surprised by his candor.

JAX
Are you questioning our mission, Private? Our loyalty?

JACK
(Shifting uncomfortably, but holding Jax’s gaze)
No, sir. Just… questioning the narrative. If the Empire’s so perfect, why are there so many broken worlds? Why do we keep finding these… corruptions?

The question hangs in the air. Zara, who has been quiet, her horn flickering with internal debate, finally speaks.

ZARA
(Her voice soft, but firm)
The Aethers. They were living a lie. A beautiful one, yes. But a lie nonetheless. And the Empire… it promises a beautiful order. But at what cost?

Kael sighs, running a hand over his horn.

KAEL
The cost is stability, Ensign. The cost is peace. The Hydroverse is a dangerous place. The Empire provides structure. Without it… chaos.

LUNA
(Approaching, her scientific horn glowing with conviction)
But what kind of peace? A stagnant peace? A peace built on suppression? True peace, Kael, comes from understanding. From embracing all aspects of reality, not just the ones that fit a predefined narrative.

Kato, ever the silent observer, nods in agreement with Luna.

Jax looks at his team, the faces of those he trusts, now divided by a philosophical chasm. He sees the genuine conviction in Jureth’s eyes, the scientific curiosity in Luna’s, the quiet understanding in Kato’s. And he sees the unwavering loyalty in Hana’s, the pragmatic doubt in Jack’s, the internal struggle in Zara’s.

JAX
(His voice heavy)
We are Anglers. We are sworn to protect the Hydroverse. But what if the threat… is within? What if the very order we uphold… is the source of the imbalance?

The question hangs unanswered. The shuttle hums with the silent tension of a team on the brink of a profound shift.

FADE OUT.
