INT. UNLIT TRIBE CAMP - RIFT'S EDGE - NIGHT

The Unlit Tribe camp is quiet, the battle of philosophies having settled into a tense stalemate. Jureth, now frail with age, sits by the rift, gazing into its swirling depths. His horn glows with a soft, contemplative light.

Suddenly, a figure emerges from the shadows, cloaked in the grey of the Empire. It is TAMIN, Jureth’s brother, his horn blazing with a cold, hard light, reflecting Vorith’s influence. He is older, his face hardened by years of Imperial service.

Jureth looks up, a flicker of recognition, then sorrow, in his eyes.

JURETH
Tamin. My brother. You have come.

TAMIN
(Voice devoid of warmth, echoing Vorith’s rhetoric)
I have come to end this madness, Jureth. To bring you back to the light. To the order of the Empire. This “unlit void” is a delusion. A path to ruin.

JURETH
(A gentle smile)
The void is not ruin, Tamin. It is truth. It is freedom. The Empire’s light… it blinds you to the vastness of existence.

Tamin’s horn flares with anger.

TAMIN
You speak of freedom, but you sow chaos! You have turned our parents against the Empire! You have fractured our family!

JURETH
(His voice soft, filled with pain)
Our family was fractured by the Empire’s rigid demands, Tamin. Not by truth.

Tamin raises his spright-saber, its blade humming with Imperial energy.

TAMIN
(Voice trembling with a mix of anger and pain)
Then I will cut out the cancer! I will restore order!

Jureth does not move. He simply looks at his brother, his eyes filled with a profound sadness.

JURETH
(Whispering)
Do what you must, Tamin. But know this: the unlit path will endure.

Tamin lunges, his spright-saber piercing Jureth’s side. The ancient sage gasps, a single tear tracing a path down his weathered cheek. His horn flickers violently, then dims.

ZORATH and MIRAH, drawn by the sudden surge of energy, rush to the rift’s edge. They see Tamin, his spright-saber still in Jureth’s side, and Jureth, collapsing.

MIRAH
(A guttural scream of anguish)
TAMIN! What have you done?!

ZORATH
(His horn flaring with a raw, primal grief)
My son! My son!

Tamin pulls his spright-saber free, his face a mask of conflicted emotions. He looks at his parents, their faces contorted with grief and horror. The cold, hard light of his horn wavers.

TAMIN
(Voice broken)
I… I did what was necessary. For the Empire. For order.

Mirah rushes to Jureth, cradling him in her arms. Zorath stands over them, his body trembling with rage and sorrow.

MIRAH
(To Tamin, her voice filled with a searing hatred)
You are no son of mine! You have murdered your brother! You have murdered our hope!

Tamin recoils, the weight of his betrayal crushing him. He looks at Jureth, lying still in his mother’s arms, his horn almost extinguished. The vibrant light of the unlit void, which had once flowed from Jureth, now seems to drain away.

Tamin turns and flees into the shadows, the sound of his parents’ grief echoing in his ears.

Zorath and Mirah weep over Jureth, their horns dim with sorrow. The rift pulses, as if mourning with them. The Unlit Tribe, witnessing this profound betrayal, stands in stunned silence, their own horns dimming in shared grief.

FADE OUT.
