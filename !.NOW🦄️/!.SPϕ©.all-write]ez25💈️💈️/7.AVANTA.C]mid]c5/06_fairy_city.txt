INT. AVANTA.C - TROLL CITY - DAY

Leaving the vibrant chaos of the Renaissance Fair behind, Lucky and Astra find themselves in a starkly different landscape: Troll City. It's a sprawling, industrial metropolis built on a series of massive, interconnected islands that float precariously above a churning, green-tinged sea. Bridges of rough-hewn stone and salvaged metal connect the landmasses, some swaying precariously in the virtual wind.

The architecture is imposing and functional, dominated by towering, crude stone structures and buildings fashioned from what looks like salvaged spaceship parts. The air here is thick with the smell of ore, molten metal, and something vaguely sulfuric. The ground beneath their feet vibrates with the rhythmic CLANG of hammers on anvils and the roar of unseen furnaces.

Giant, grotesque STATUES of trolls, some with exaggerated features and others with menacing grins, are carved into the very rock faces of the islands, or stand as imposing sentinels at the entrance to dark, cavernous tunnels. Their eyes, often glowing with a dull red light, seem to follow Lucky and Astra as they pass.

The streets are a labyrinth of winding alleys and crowded thoroughfares. Market stalls, unlike the colorful ones at the fair, are more utilitarian, displaying raw gemstones, gleaming ingots of unknown metals, and crudely fashioned but sturdy tools. Trolls, with their thick hides, tusks, and often grumpy expressions, haggle loudly over goods, their voices a guttural rumble.

Astra, seemingly unfazed by the intimidating environment, navigates them through the throng with practiced ease. She points out a particularly large, fortified structure in the distance.

ASTRA
(Shouting over the din)
That's the Citadel! Home of the legendary Troll King. They say he hoards more gold than the EMPRIE has credits.

Lucky stares at the Citadel, a mix of awe and apprehension. He notices a group of smaller, younger trolls attempting to pickpocket a passing merchant, only to be caught and playfully swatted by a larger, older troll.

LUCKY
(Observing)
They're... different than I expected.

ASTRA
(Grinning)
Oh, they're still trolls. Just with a bit more... entrepreneurial spirit. They value strength and wealth above all else. And they're fiercely loyal to their own.

They pass a stall where a troll is meticulously polishing a massive, uncut diamond. The gem gleams with an inner fire, momentarily distracting Lucky from the oppressive atmosphere.

ASTRA
(Cont.)
This city is a treasure trove for those who know where to look. And for those who aren't afraid to get their hands a little dirty.

Lucky nods, taking it all in. Troll City, for all its rough edges, holds a strange allure. It's a place of raw power and untamed ambition, a stark contrast to the polished beauty of the city square and the whimsical charm of the Renaissance Fair. He realizes that Avanta.C is far more complex and diverse than he initially imagined.
