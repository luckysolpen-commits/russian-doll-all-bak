Chapter 2: Arrival in Avanta.C

Page 1

Lucky stood before a grimy, abandoned terminal, its once-sleek casing
now caked with layers of dust and grime, in a forgotten, dimly lit
corner of Ikarus. The air hung heavy, thick with the metallic tang of
ozone, the stale, acrid scent of burnt circuitry, and the faint, cloying
aroma of neglect, a testament to years of disuse and decay. The terminal
itself seemed to sag under the weight of its own obsolescence, its
screen a dull, lifeless grey. He clutched the data crystal the Blue
Bird had given him, its faint, ethereal glow, a soft, pulsating blue,
illuminating his determined, yet anxious face. His fingers, still raw
and calloused from his life on Earth, traced the smooth, cool surface
of the crystal, feeling the subtle hum of latent energy within. This
small, unassuming object held the promise of a new beginning, a gateway
to a world beyond the EMPRIE’s oppressive reach, but also the terrifying
uncertainty of the unknown. His heart hammered against his ribs, a
frantic drumbeat against the silence, echoing the chaotic rhythm of his
thoughts. He could feel the tremor in his hands, a physical manifestation
of the immense pressure he felt. This was it. The point of no return.
Every fiber of his being screamed caution, a lifetime of survival instincts
warning him of the inherent dangers of the unfamiliar. Yet, a deeper,
more primal urge, the desperate yearning for freedom, for a life beyond
fear, propelled him forward. He took a shaky breath, the stale air of
Ikarus filling his lungs, a final taste of his old reality. He closed
his eyes for a brief moment, picturing the endless, grey skies of Earth,
the ever-present surveillance drones, the gaunt faces of his fellow
survivors. He pushed the images away, focusing on the faint, blue light
of the crystal, a tiny beacon in the vast darkness of his uncertainty.

Page 2

He inserted the crystal into a dusty, almost-hidden port on the side
of the terminal, the ancient machinery groaning in protest, a chorus
of rusty gears and protesting circuits. The sound was a harsh, grating
screech that sent a shiver down his spine, a stark reminder of the
terminal’s age and precarious state. The screen flickered to life,
a sickly green light illuminating the dust motes dancing in the air,
displaying a crude, black-market bot account interface. Lines of cryptic
code, a dizzying cascade of alphanumeric characters, scrolled rapidly
across the screen, too fast for his weary eyes to follow, a language
he barely understood. Then, with an abrupt, jarring halt, the scrolling
ceased, and a stark, unsettling prompt appeared, its stark white letters
blazing against the dark background: "INITIATING AVANTA.C CONNECTION...
UNAUTHORIZED ACCESS DETECTED. PROCEED? (Y/N)" The words seemed to hang
in the air, a silent challenge, a direct confrontation with the unknown.
Lucky hesitated for a moment, his heart pounding in his chest like a
drum, each beat reverberating through his entire body. This was it.
No turning back. The weight of his decision pressed down on him, a
crushing burden. He could still choose to walk away, to remain in the
relative, albeit miserable, safety of Ikarus. But the thought was fleeting,
a mere wisp of a possibility. He had come too far, endured too much,
to falter now. He typed ‘Y’, his finger trembling slightly on the worn,
greasy key, the plastic warm beneath his touch. The act felt monumental,
a silent declaration of war against his past, a desperate plea for a
future. The screen flashed, a blinding white light that forced him to
squeeze his eyes shut, momentarily disorienting him. When he opened them
again, a new interface had appeared. It was sleek, vibrant, and impossibly
clean, a stark contrast to the grimy terminal and the squalor of Ikarus.
It was the Avanta.C login screen, a gateway to a new reality.

Page 3

A synthesized female voice, smooth and inviting, filled the small, dusty
space, its melodic tones a stark contrast to the harsh sounds of Ikarus.
“Welcome, new soul. Prepare to transcend.” The words resonated deep within
him, a promise of something more, something beyond the mundane and the
oppressive. The screen shifted, dissolving into a kaleidoscope of colors
and shapes, presenting a dizzying array of AVATAR OPTIONS. Holographic
projections of fantastical beings, shimmering with an inner light, spun
before him, each one a masterpiece of digital artistry. There were towering
warriors, their virtual muscles rippling beneath intricate armor, their
weapons gleaming with an otherworldly sheen. Ethereal mages, their robes
flowing like liquid starlight, their hands crackling with latent energy.
Nimble rogues, cloaked in shadows, their movements hinting at impossible
speed and stealth. Each one was rendered in exquisite detail, a testament
to Avanta.C’s immersive power, to the boundless imagination of its creators.
He saw avatars with glowing eyes that seemed to pierce his very soul,
intricate armor adorned with impossible filigree, and flowing robes that
danced with an unseen breeze. Each one was a promise of a different life,
a chance to shed the skin of his past and become someone entirely new.
His breath hitched in his throat, a gasp of pure wonder. He had never
imagined such beauty, such freedom of expression. On Earth, individuality
was a luxury, a dangerous indulgence. Here, it was celebrated, amplified.
He felt a strange sense of liberation, a lightness he hadn’t known in
years. He reached out a hand, as if to touch the shimmering projections,
but his fingers passed through them, a reminder of the digital divide.
Yet, the illusion was so perfect, so compelling, that for a moment, he
forgot his physical surroundings, lost in the sheer spectacle of it all.

Page 4

Lucky scrolled through the options, his fingers hovering over various
choices, a silent debate raging within him. He considered a hulking,
armored figure, a brute of a warrior, imagining the raw power he could
wield, the sheer force he could bring to bear against any threat. Then,
he paused on a nimble, cloaked rogue, quick and elusive, envisioning
himself moving through shadows, striking unseen, a phantom of vengeance.
Each choice represented a different facet of himself, a suppressed desire
to be strong, to be free, to be something more than just a victim. He
paused on a figure that seemed to embody both strength and agility, a
balanced warrior with a hint of mystery, its features obscured by a deep
cowl, its posture radiating a quiet confidence. It felt right. It felt
like a new beginning, a chance to redefine himself, to shed the weight
of his past and embrace a new identity. This avatar, with its blend of
practicality and subtle power, resonated with something deep within him.
It wasn’t about brute force or flashy magic; it was about resilience,
adaptability, and a quiet determination. He selected it, his finger
pressing a virtual button that shimmered with a soft, golden light.
The chosen avatar glowed, a soft, warm light emanating from its form,
then solidified, its features becoming clearer, more defined. It was a
lean, athletic figure, clad in practical, yet stylish, light armor that
seemed to mold itself to its form, hinting at flexibility and speed.
Its face was obscured by a cowl, hinting at a hidden identity, a fresh
start, a chance to be someone new, unburdened by the ghosts of his past.
He felt a strange sense of connection to this digital representation of
himself, a feeling of finally being seen, of finally being able to express
a part of himself that had been suppressed for so long. The armor felt
light, almost like a second skin, yet he could sense its protective qualities.

Page 5

Again, the synthesized voice spoke, its tone now more affirming, a gentle
encouragement that soothed his frayed nerves. “Avatar selected. Identity
confirmed. Preparing for immersion.” The words were a final confirmation,
a point of no return. The terminal hummed, a low, resonant thrum that
vibrated through the floor, up his legs, and into his very bones, a deep,
pervasive vibration that seemed to shake him to his core. The holographic
projections of Avanta.C’s world began to swirl around him, forming a
vortex of color and light, a dizzying maelstrom of digital energy.
The colors deepened, intensified, pulling him in, drawing him deeper into
the heart of the virtual world. He felt a strange sensation, as if his
very atoms were being stretched, pulled apart, and then reassembled.
It was not painful, but profoundly disorienting, a sensation unlike
anything he had ever experienced. The air around him crackled with
energy, a faint, electrical scent filling his nostrils. He closed his
eyes, a sense of anticipation and trepidation washing over him in equal
measure, a chaotic blend of excitement and fear. He took a deep breath,
the stale air of Ikarus filling his lungs one last time, a final farewell
to the world he was leaving behind. He could feel the dust on his tongue,
the lingering scent of decay, the cold, hard reality of his past. He held
it for a moment, savoring the last vestiges of his old life, then slowly
released it, exhaling his fears, his doubts, his regrets. He was ready
to step into his new life, his new reality, whatever it might hold. He
was ready to embrace the unknown, to face whatever challenges Avanta.C
would present. He felt a profound sense of surrender, a letting go of
all that had defined him. The hum intensified, becoming a roar, a deafening
symphony of digital transformation. He felt a sensation of falling, then
a sudden, exhilarating rush, as if his very being was being reassembled,
pixel by pixel, into a new reality. The darkness behind his eyelids swirled
with vibrant colors, a chaotic dance of light and shadow.

Page 6

His mind raced, a whirlwind of thoughts and emotions. What would Avanta.C
be like? Would it truly be the paradise the legends spoke of, a haven
from the EMPRIE’s tyranny? Or would it be just another gilded cage,
another illusion designed to trap the unwary? He remembered the Blue
Bird’s cryptic warnings, her subtle hints of danger lurking beneath the
surface of this digital utopia. He knew that freedom, true freedom, was
never easily won, especially not in a world controlled by powerful,
unseen forces. Yet, despite the lingering doubts, a fragile seed of hope
had taken root within him, a desperate yearning for a life unburdened by
fear. He imagined himself soaring through digital skies, wielding powers
he could only dream of on Earth, forging his own destiny. The thought
sent a thrill through him, a spark of excitement that ignited a long-dormant
fire within his soul. He felt a strange sense of weightlessness, as if
his physical body had ceased to exist, replaced by pure consciousness.
The hum intensified, becoming a resonant thrum that vibrated through
his very essence, a symphony of digital creation. He felt a gentle pull,
a sensation of being drawn forward, deeper into the swirling vortex of
light and code. The colors behind his eyelids began to coalesce, forming
patterns, then shapes, then fleeting glimpses of a vibrant, alien landscape.
He could almost smell the digital air, taste the virtual wind on his tongue.
His senses, once dulled by hardship, were now heightened, alive, eager to
experience this new reality. He felt a profound sense of anticipation,
a childlike wonder he hadn’t experienced since before the EMPRIE’s rise.
This was more than just a game; it was a second chance, a rebirth.
He was shedding the skin of his past, emerging into a new existence.

Page 7

The sensation of falling abruptly ceased, replaced by a gentle, almost
imperceptible landing. Lucky felt a subtle shift, a reorientation of his
senses, as if his consciousness was settling into a new vessel. He slowly
opened his eyes, and the blinding white light that had enveloped him
vanished, replaced by a breathtaking panorama of vibrant colors and
intricate details. He stood in the heart of a city, a place so vibrant
and fantastical it stole his breath away, leaving him gasping in silent
awe. Towering spires of iridescent crystal, shimmering with an inner light
that shifted through the spectrum, pierced a sky of impossible blues and
greens, a canvas of pure, digital artistry. The sky itself seemed alive,
pulsating with soft, ethereal light, like a living aurora borealis.
Shimmering waterfalls, composed not of water but of pure, liquid light,
cascaded down the sides of colossal, moss-covered structures, their luminous
streams feeding into crystal-clear rivers that wound gracefully through
the city’s heart. The water, impossibly clear, reflected the glowing flora
and the vibrant sky, creating a mirror world beneath his feet. Lush,
bioluminescent flora, their petals unfurling in impossible geometries,
glowed softly from planters and hanging gardens, casting a gentle,
otherworldly light that painted the scene in ethereal hues of violet,
pink, and gold. The air, impossibly clean and fresh, hummed with a
symphony of sounds: the melodic chirping of unseen creatures, a delicate,
bell-like chorus that seemed to emanate from the very air itself; the
distant, rhythmic clang of a blacksmith’s hammer, a steady, reassuring
beat that spoke of industry and creation; and the murmur of countless
voices, a harmonious hum of virtual life, a symphony of digital existence.

Page 8

Lucky took a deep, involuntary breath, and his senses were assaulted by
new, delightful scents: the sweet perfume of exotic blossoms, a heady,
intoxicating aroma that filled his lungs, mixed with the comforting
aroma of freshly baked bread, a surprising, homely scent in this fantastical
world. This was Avanta.C. It was more real, more vivid, more alive than
anything he could have possibly imagined, a dream made tangible, a world
spun from pure imagination and code. He looked down at his hands. They
were no longer gaunt and trembling, the hands of a refugee on the brink
of starvation and despair. Instead, they were strong and capable, clad
in the light armor of his chosen avatar, the material smooth and cool
against his skin. He flexed his fingers, feeling the unfamiliar strength
that coursed through them. He felt a surge of energy, a lightness he
hadn’t known in years, a freedom that resonated deep within his very
being, a sense of boundless possibility. His thoughts, for a fleeting
moment, drifted back to Earth – the oppressive EMPRIE, the relentless
pursuit, the desperate struggle for mere survival, the Blue Bird Bounty
Hunter’s unexpected, almost miraculous rescue. Those memories were still
there, a faint echo in the back of his mind, a lingering shadow, a scar
on his soul. But here, in Avanta.C, they felt distant, almost like a
dream, a nightmare from another life, a story told by someone else. Avanta.C
offered a fresh start, a chance to be someone new, to build a life free
from the crushing weight of his past, free from the ever-present shadows
that had haunted his every waking moment. He felt a profound sense of
relief, a shedding of burdens he hadn’t realized he was carrying. The
colors of Avanta.C seemed brighter, the sounds clearer, the air sweeter.
He was truly here, truly present, in a way he hadn’t been in years.

Page 9

As he wandered through the bustling square, his eyes wide with wonder,
he marveled at the sheer diversity of the inhabitants. It was a living
tapestry of fantasy and technology, a vibrant melting pot of digital
life. Elves with graceful, pointed ears and eyes like polished emeralds
haggled playfully with burly dwarves, their beards braided with intricate
runes, over enchanted trinkets that shimmered with latent magic. Gnomish
engineers, their faces smudged with grease, tinkered with intricate
clockwork devices, their tiny tools glinting in the bioluminescent light,
a symphony of clicks and whirs. Human adventurers, clad in gleaming armor,
stride purposefully through the crowds, their weapons at their sides,
their gazes fixed on unseen quests and distant horizons, their faces
lined with determination and a hint of weariness. He spotted a group
gathered around a large, ornate stone pedestal in the center of the
square, its ancient carvings glowing softly. A woman with fiery red hair,
her movements fluid and captivating, strummed a lively tune on a lute,
her fingers dancing across the strings with impossible speed and grace.
Her name, he overheard someone whisper, was Aria, a bard whose charisma
was as potent as her melodies. Her music was infectious, a vibrant blend
of ancient melodies and modern beats that made his feet tap involuntarily.
Others danced and cheered in unison, their laughter echoing through the
square, a joyous chorus of virtual revelry. Lucky found himself drawn
to the energy, a faint, genuine smile touching his lips, a smile he hadn’t
realized he was capable of. He felt an inexplicable urge to join in, to
shed the weight of his past and simply exist in this vibrant, new world,
to lose himself in the moment, if only for a little while. He closed his
eyes, letting the music wash over him, a cleansing wave that momentarily
erased the scars of his past. He felt a sense of belonging, a feeling
he had never truly experienced before.

As he stood there, captivated by the music and the joy, a figure emerged
from the periphery of his vision. She moved with an almost unnatural
grace, her steps silent, her presence commanding, drawing his attention
like a moth to a flame. She was tall, slender, yet radiated an aura of
immense power, a quiet strength that seemed to ripple around her. Her
robes, a deep, midnight blue, seemed to absorb the light, and her hood
was drawn low, obscuring most of her face. Only a hint of sharp,
intelligent eyes, the color of deep amethyst, and a delicate, yet firm,
chin were visible. She stopped a few feet from him, her gaze seemingly
piercing through his cowl, directly into his soul, a silent assessment
that made him feel utterly exposed. “You’re new here, aren’t you?” her
voice was low, melodious, yet carried an undercurrent of ancient wisdom,
a subtle resonance that hinted at untold depths. “I can sense the raw
potential within you. And the fear. And the hope.” Lucky, surprised by
her directness, could only nod, his throat suddenly dry. “My name is
Lucky,” he managed, his voice a little rough, unused to speaking freely.
“I… I just arrived.” She offered a small, knowing smile, a subtle curve
of her lips that hinted at a shared understanding. “I am Astra,” she
replied, her voice softening slightly, a hint of warmth entering her tone.
“And I believe our paths were meant to cross. This world, Avanta.C, is
vast and full of wonders. But it is also dangerous, especially for the
uninitiated. Many come here seeking freedom, only to find a different
kind of servitude.” She gestured around the bustling square, her hand
moving with an elegant grace. “You see the beauty, the life, the endless
possibilities. But beneath the surface, there are currents, powers at
play that seek to control, to dominate. I can help you navigate these
waters, Lucky. I can teach you to harness your potential, to survive,
and perhaps, even to thrive.” Lucky looked at her, a flicker of suspicion
mixed with a desperate hope warring within him. “Why?” he asked, the
single word heavy with his past experiences, a lifetime of betrayal and
disappointment. “Why would you help me?” Astra’s eyes, though partially
hidden, seemed to twinkle with amusement, a playful glint that belied
the seriousness of her words. “Let’s just say I have my reasons. And
perhaps, a shared enemy. But for now, let’s focus on your immediate
needs. Do you have a guild? Any resources? Any allies?” Lucky shook his
head, a sense of vulnerability washing over him. “No. Just… just me.”
Astra nodded slowly, her expression thoughtful. “Then you have me. For
now. Come. There is much to learn, and little time. The shadows in
Avanta.C are growing longer, and you, Lucky, are about to step into
their light.” She turned, her robes swirling around her, and began to
walk away, her movements fluid and silent, a phantom in the crowd.
Lucky hesitated for only a moment, then followed, a silent promise to
himself to trust this mysterious stranger. He didn’t know who Astra was,
or why she was helping him. But in this strange, new world, she was the
only hand offered. And he was desperate enough to take it. His journey
in Avanta.C had truly begun, and with Astra by his side, he felt a
flicker of something he hadn’t felt in a long time: hope.
