Chapter 6: The Illegal Furry Server

Page 1

After the serene beauty of Fairy City, its shimmering light and melodic
chimes still echoing in Lucky’s mind, Astra led him down a path less
traveled, a digital rabbit hole that promised a glimpse into a different,
wilder side of Avanta.C. The transition was abrupt, almost jarring.
The air, once clean and sweet with the scent of blossoms, grew thick
with a strange energy, a mix of rebellion and unbridled self-expression,
a palpable hum that vibrated through his very being. They passed through
a shimmering, almost invisible portal, its surface rippling like disturbed
water, and the world around them shifted dramatically, dissolving the
ethereal landscape of Fairy City into a chaotic, vibrant new reality.
Gone were the glowing trees and melodic chimes, replaced by a sprawling,
chaotic landscape that defied conventional architecture. Buildings were
hazardously constructed from mismatched parts: scavenged spaceship hulls,
their metal scarred and dented, now serving as makeshift walls; glowing
neon signs, their colors clashing violently, advertising illicit goods
and services; and even what looked like oversized animal bones, bleached
white and intricately carved, forming grotesque archways and support
structures. The sky above was a perpetual twilight, a bruised purple
and orange, illuminated by flickering holographic advertisements for
illicit goods and services, their garish colors painting the clouds in
sickly hues. The ground beneath their feet was a patchwork of cracked
concrete, exposed wiring, and discarded tech, a testament to the server’s
anarchic nature. This was the Illegal Furry Server, a hidden corner of
Avanta.C where the rules were few and the avatars were… unique. The
inhabitants were a riot of color and form, their digital bodies transformed
into an astonishing array of anthropomorphic creatures, each one a vibrant
expression of individuality. There were towering wolves with cybernetic
limbs, their metal gleaming in the dim light, their eyes glowing with
an inner fire; lithe foxes with glowing eyes that seemed to pierce the
darkness, their tails twitching with restless energy; playful otters in
steampunk goggles, their fur matted with grease and oil, tinkering with
unknown devices; and even a majestic lion with a mane of crackling
electricity, its roar a low, resonant hum that vibrated through the air.

As they explored the chaotic environment, Lucky felt a strange desire to
become something more than his current human form. "Astra," he said, his
voice filled with curiosity, "is it possible to change my avatar completely?
To become... one of them?" Astra smiled knowingly. "Of course it is. This
is the Illegal Furry Server, after all. There are avatar customization
parlors where the best coders in the underground work. But be warned,
the process is illegal on the main servers. Here, though..." She gestured
toward a cramped, neon-lit establishment called "PIXEL'S DIGITAL TRANSFORMATION
WORKSHOP," where a grizzled multi-armed spider-furred hacker was deep in
conversation with a group of exotic avatars.

They entered the workshop where Pixel, the multi-armed hacker, was working
on avatar conversions. "Welcome, travelers. I can give you anything you
desire. Dragon, pony, cat, wolf... whatever your heart desires. The question
is: what do you want to become?" Lucky immediately knew his answer. "I want
something powerful. Something that matches what I'm going through. A dragon,
maybe?" Astra considered her options and decided on a pony form that would
enhance her agility. After a complex conversion process, Lucky's human form
began to shimmer and morph. His skin sprouted scales that shimmered with
an iridescent blue and gold pattern. His limbs grew more powerful, his
face elongated into a dragon snout, and magnificent wings unfurled from
his back. Astra's transformation was equally dramatic as a coat of soft,
purple fur grew across her body, her legs became sturdy pony legs with
a flowing mane and tail. The process cost 500 gold coins each, but Pixel
had a tip for them - underground gaming circuits in the server featuring
Beat Saber hacking battles with real stakes and real rewards.

Page 1.2

Following Pixel's advice, Lucky and Astra navigated to a packed underground
arena where players were engaged in intense Hacker's Beat Saber battles.
The environment pulsed with hypnotic, neon rhythms as players slashed through
incoming blocks to the beat of electronic music. But this wasn't ordinary
Beat Saber - each successful hit represented a successful system penetration,
and each missed block represented a security breach. The stakes were high,
and the rewards were real gear, gold, and hacking tools. A humanoid DJ-figure
with cybernetic enhancements controlled the music and hacking challenges
from a central platform, announcing, "Tonight's theme: 'System Override'.
Winners take home premium gear and code fragments. Losers... well, they'll
be debugging their avatars for days!" 

Lucky's dragon form provided him with enhanced reflexes as he faced a cybernetic
wolf in his first match. His dragon claws shifted into virtual sabers as he
hacked through the blocks with surprising agility. Astra's pony form allowed
her to move with incredible grace and speed, her sabers made of pure light
as she danced through the beat with elegant precision. They worked their way
up through increasingly difficult matches, winning gold and gear. Their
final challenge came against the house champion - a massive, tentacled creature
named 'Virus Vortex'. Working in perfect synchronization with Lucky handling
the heavy attacks while Astra provided support and precision strikes, they
defeated the champion and received rare gear upgrades: "Cybernetic Dragon Claws"
for Lucky that boosted his attack power, and "Enchanted Pony Hooves" for Astra
that increased her speed and agility. Their victories quickly spread through
the server's underground network, making them known figures among the hackers.

Page 1.3

After their victory in the Beat Saber arena, Lucky and Astra were approached
by three mysterious figures emerging from the shadows. The figures were clearly
members of the Metal Mafia, recognizable by their metallic, armored avatars
and glowing red accents. The Metal Mafia leader's voice was metallic and
synthesized: "Well, well... what have we here? Two upstarts thinking they
can make names for themselves in our territory?" Lucky's dragon form tensed
up as he replied, "We're not looking for trouble." But the Metal Mafia
leader wasn't interested in de-escalation: "Trouble found you. Word's already
spread about your Beat Saber victory. And we don't take kindly to outsiders
trying to make a name for themselves without paying the proper... respects."

The encounter moved to a virtual arena with metallic, industrial design where
the three parties engaged in lightsaber combat. Three lightsabers materialized
on the ground - one blue for Lucky, one purple for Astra, and three red ones
for the Metal Mafia members. The duel was intense: Lucky used his dragon form
with his Cybernetic Dragon Claws to add additional defense while Astra
danced between attacks with her enhanced pony agility and a LightShield
spell. Despite being outnumbered, their teamwork from the Beat Saber battles
gave them the edge. They fought back-to-back, covering each other's weaknesses,
and ultimately defeated the Metal Mafia group. The leader acknowledged their
skills with grudging respect: "Impressive. You've got skills. And you work
well as a team. The Metal Mafia remembers this. Don't make us your enemy,
but don't expect us to make this easy for you either." The three Metal Mafia
members retreated, but Lucky and Astra knew they were now on the organization's
radar.

Page 1.4

Word of Lucky and Astra's victories had spread through the illegal server,
and they were invited to an exclusive underground party. The space was unlike
anything they'd seen - a multi-dimensional environment that shifted and morphed
in impossible ways, filled with swirling colors and mind-bending geometry.
The party was in full swing with all manner of furry avatars. The air was
thick with digital inebriants - virtual substances that altered perception
without affecting actual health. The effects were purely sensory, creating
enhanced feelings of euphoria, connection, and freedom. Neon signs flickered
with names of exotic inebriants: "DATA DREAMS," "CODE FOG," "BINARY BLISS."
The music was an overwhelming blend of electronic beats, and the dance floor
undulated like liquid metal. 

Lucky's dragon form made him feel connected to the very code that made up
this world, while Astra's pony form made her feel like she could run forever.
They were approached by a group of wild party-goers - a six-limbed feline,
a multi-headed dragon, and a constantly-shifting abstract creature - who
welcomed them as the legendary Beat Saber hackers who had defeated Virus Vortex
and the Metal Mafia. The party reached a fever pitch with rhythmic, synchronized
movements that seemed to be part dance, part ritual, part combat practice.
The "humping orgy" took the form of an intense, energetic experience where
the boundaries between avatars became fluid - an intense shared virtual
experience where the participants temporarily fused with other avatars,
exchanging experiences and emotions in ways that transcended their individual
forms. They experienced what it was like to see through the eyes of dragons,
move with the grace of ponies, and think with multiple minds of collective
avatars. The experience was overwhelming, euphoric, and deeply communal - a
celebration of the freedom that the illegal server represented. As the effects
began to fade, everyone returned to their individual avatars, but with a
deeper sense of connection and understanding. Suddenly, warning alarms began
to scream through the virtual space: "EMPEROR'S BORDER POLICE APPROACHING!
ALL ILLEGAL ACTIVITIES DETECTED!"

The party came to an abrupt end as Emperor's Border Police avatars materialized
throughout the space. They were imposing, military-grade characters with stark
black armor, red accents, and glowing visors. Their presence was overwhelming
and authoritative. The Border Police Captain's voice boomed with authority:
"This is an illegal server raid! All participants must submit for identity
verification! No exceptions!" Panicked party-goers were shifting back to their
standard humanoid forms as the inebriant effects faded. Lucky called out to
Astra, "We need to get out of here, now!" Astra spotted the mounted section
of the server where virtual mounts were kept - exotic creatures for rapid
transportation. "These will work! The system will adapt!" she called out as
they mounted two cybernetic pegasus creatures, part horse and part dragon,
with metallic wings and glowing circuits.

The Emperor's Border Police Captain announced, "Don't let anyone escape! And
especially don't let Lucky get away - the EMPRIE has a special interest in
capturing him!" Lucky was shocked - they were specifically after him. The
mounted chase began through the narrow passages and shifting digital landscape
of the illegal server. The cybernetic mounts proved their worth as Lucky and
Astra utilized both their dragon and pony avatars' natural abilities - Lucky's
dragon form providing additional power to urge the mount faster, while Astra's
pony agility helped her navigate the treacherous path. The police tried to
follow, but the maze-like structure of the illegal server created natural
obstacles. As they approached the digital boundary between the illegal furry
server and the main Avanta.C network, the police hit an access barrier - they
couldn't cross without legal warrants. "That was too close," Lucky breathed
as they slowed down on the other side. "They were definitely after you, Lucky.
The EMPRIE connection must be deeper than we thought," Astra observed. They
returned their mounts to the virtual stables, hearts still racing from the
escape, but now with enhanced gear, new avatars, and a reputation in the
underground.

Lucky stared, wide-eyed, at the spectacle, his senses overwhelmed by the
sheer audacity of it all. He had seen diverse avatars in the main city,
but nothing prepared him for this uninhibited display of self-expression.
Here, the line between player and character was blurred, almost erased,
each furry seemed to embody the spirit of their chosen form, moving with
an animalistic grace and an uninhibited freedom that was both exhilarating
and a little unsettling. “Welcome to the wild side,” Astra said, a wry
smile playing on her lips, a rare glimpse of amusement in her usually
stoic demeanor. “This server operates outside the main Avanta.C protocols.
It’s a haven for those who feel constrained by the mainstream, by the
corporate rules and regulations, or those who simply enjoy a bit of anarchy,
a taste of true digital liberation.” The sounds here were a cacophony of
growls, purrs, howls, and the rhythmic thumping of bass-heavy music emanating
from makeshift clubs, their beats reverberating through the very ground.
Market stalls, fashioned from old shipping containers, their metal walls
covered in vibrant graffiti, offered custom fur patterns, exotic cybernetic
enhancements that pulsed with strange energies, and even illegal data chips
that promised forbidden abilities, their illicit nature hinted at by the
furtive glances of the vendors. Lucky watched a group of fox-like furries
engaged in a high-stakes card game, their tails twitching with excitement,
their eyes narrowed in concentration. Nearby, a bear-like figure with glowing
tattoos that snaked across his massive arms was arm-wrestling a sleek,
black panther, the ground trembling with their efforts, the air thick with
the scent of sweat and ozone. He noticed the intricate details of their
avatars, the way their fur rippled with movement, the subtle shifts in their
expressions, the almost human emotions reflected in their animalistic faces.
This was not just a game; it was a living, breathing subculture, a vibrant
community forged in rebellion. He felt a strange sense of fascination,
a desire to understand the unspoken rules of this chaotic world.

“Why is it illegal?” Lucky asked, his voice barely audible over the din,
the question a whisper against the roar of the server. He couldn’t shake
the feeling that this place, for all its chaos, held a strange allure,
a raw authenticity that was missing from the more polished, regulated
parts of Avanta.C. Astra’s expression grew serious, the amusement fading
from her eyes, replaced by a quiet intensity. “The main Avanta.C system
is designed for control. Order. Predictability. It’s a carefully constructed
reality, designed to keep players within certain parameters. This server…
it’s the opposite. It’s a place where players can truly be free, without
the watchful eye of the system, without the corporate overlords dictating
their every move. But that freedom comes with risks. There’s no official
moderation, no safety nets, no one to catch you if you fall. Anything
can happen here, and often does.” She led him through a narrow alleyway,
the walls covered in vibrant, defiant graffiti depicting a stylized rebellion
against a faceless, corporate entity, its logo a stark, oppressive symbol.
“Many of these players are refugees from other servers, or those who simply
reject the corporate grip on Avanta.C. They believe in true digital
liberation, in a world where players, not corporations, control their own
destinies.” The air in the alleyway was thick with the scent of synthetic
smoke and the faint, metallic tang of illicit data transfers. They passed
by shadowy figures exchanging glowing data chips, their faces obscured by
hoods and masks, their movements furtive and quick. Lucky felt a prickle
of unease, a familiar sensation from his days on Earth, but this time, it
was mixed with a strange sense of morbid fascination. This was the underbelly
of Avanta.C, the place where the rules were broken, where freedom was fought
for, even if it meant living on the fringes. He saw a group of furries
gathered around a flickering holographic screen, their faces illuminated
by the shifting images of a clandestine data stream. He wondered what secrets
they were sharing, what forbidden knowledge they were seeking. He felt a
sense of kinship with these outcasts, a shared understanding of what it meant
to live outside the system.

They entered a dimly lit den, its entrance marked by a tattered, neon-lit
curtain that pulsed with a faint, erratic glow. The interior was filled
with the scent of synthetic smoke, a sweet, cloying aroma that hung heavy
in the air, and the low hum of data streams, a constant, almost subliminal
buzz that vibrated through the floor. Furry avatars lounged on worn cushions,
their eyes glowing in the gloom, like embers in a dying fire. The atmosphere
was relaxed, almost languid, yet there was an underlying current of tension,
a sense of constant vigilance. A large, imposing wolf with a scarred muzzle,
its fur a deep, midnight black, looked up as they entered, his glowing eyes
narrowing in assessment. His posture was relaxed, yet his presence commanded
respect. “Astra,” the wolf rumbled, his voice deep and gravelly, like stones
rolling down a mountain. “Didn’t think you’d brave these parts again.” His
tone was devoid of malice, yet held a hint of challenge, a playful jab.
“Just showing a new friend the sights, Fenris,” Astra replied, her tone
surprisingly warm, a rare display of affection. “Lucky, this is Fenris,
one of the unofficial leaders here, a true pioneer of this server.” Fenris’s
glowing eyes narrowed as he regarded Lucky, a silent assessment passing
between them, a quick scan of his aura, his intentions. “Another lost soul
seeking refuge?” he asked, his voice laced with a hint of cynicism, a weary
understanding of the human condition. Lucky felt a strange sense of kinship
with these outcasts. He, too, was a refugee, seeking freedom from oppression,
a survivor of a system that sought to control every aspect of his life.
“Something like that,” Lucky admitted, a faint smile touching his lips.
Fenris grunted, a sound that could have been amusement, or perhaps a sigh
of resignation. “Well, in here, you can be whoever you want to be. No EMPRIE,
no corporate overlords. Just… us. And the consequences of our choices.” His
words were a stark reminder of the freedom and the danger that coexisted
in this anarchic realm. Lucky felt a sense of belonging, a quiet acceptance
that he hadn’t felt since before the EMPRIE’s rise. This was a place where
he could truly be himself, unburdened by expectations or judgment.

This was more than just a game; it was a digital war, a constant struggle

profound shift within him. The Illegal Furry Server was a stark reminder

place of raw, untamed freedom, a mirror to the wildness that still existed

shimmering portal that would take them back to the more structured parts
of Avanta.C. As they walked, Lucky reflected on the contrasts he had witnessed.
The pristine, ordered beauty of the main city, the whimsical charm of the
Renaissance Faire, the industrial might of Troll City, the ethereal serenity
of Fairy City, and now, the raw, untamed chaos of the Illegal Furry Server.
Avanta.C was a world of endless possibilities, a canvas upon which players

participant in the grand narrative of Avanta.C. And with Astra by his side,

before them, a gateway to another reality. Lucky took a deep breath, the
scent of synthetic smoke and rebellion filling his lungs one last time.
