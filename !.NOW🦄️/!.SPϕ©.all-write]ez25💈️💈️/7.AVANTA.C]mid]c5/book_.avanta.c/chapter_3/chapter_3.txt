Chapter 3: Meeting Astra

Page 1

Lucky opened his eyes, and the grimy terminal, the stale air of Ikarus,
the oppressive weight of his past, all vanished as if they had never
existed. He stood, not on a dusty, forgotten asteroid, but in the heart
of a city, a place so vibrant and fantastical it stole his breath away,
leaving him gasping in silent awe. Towering spires of iridescent crystal,
shimmering with an inner light that shifted through the spectrum of a
rainbow, pierced a sky of impossible blues and greens, a canvas of pure,
digital artistry. The sky itself seemed alive, pulsating with soft,
ethereal light, like a living aurora borealis, constantly shifting and
reforming. Shimmering waterfalls, composed not of water but of pure,
liquid light, cascaded down the sides of colossal, moss-covered structures,
their luminous streams feeding into crystal-clear rivers that wound
gracefully through the city’s heart. The water, impossibly clear, reflected
the glowing flora and the vibrant sky, creating a mirror world beneath
his feet. Lush, bioluminescent flora, their petals unfurling in impossible
geometries, glowed softly from planters and hanging gardens, casting a
gentle, otherworldly light that painted the scene in ethereal hues of
violet, pink, and gold. Every surface seemed to hum with a subtle energy,
a silent symphony of digital life. The very air seemed to shimmer with
magic, a palpable presence that tingled on his skin. He felt a profound
sense of disorientation, a delightful confusion as his senses struggled
to process the sheer beauty and complexity of his new surroundings. His
heart, which had been a frantic drumbeat of fear for so long, now beat
with a rhythm of pure wonder. He took a tentative step forward, as if
fearing that this breathtaking vision might dissolve like a dream.

Page 2

The air was alive with a symphony of sounds, a harmonious blend of the
natural and the digital: the melodic chirping of unseen creatures, a
delicate, bell-like chorus that seemed to emanate from the very air
itself, weaving through the gentle rustle of bioluminescent leaves. In
the distance, the rhythmic clang of a blacksmith’s hammer echoed, a
steady, reassuring beat that spoke of industry and creation, a counterpoint
to the ethereal melodies. And beneath it all, the murmur of countless
voices, a harmonious hum of virtual life, a symphony of digital existence,
each voice a thread in the rich tapestry of Avanta.C. Lucky took a deep,
involuntary breath, and his senses were assaulted by new, delightful scents:
the sweet perfume of exotic blossoms, a heady, intoxicating aroma that
filled his lungs, mixed with the comforting aroma of freshly baked bread,
a surprising, homely scent in this fantastical world. He could almost
taste the sweetness on his tongue, a stark contrast to the bland nutrient
paste he had subsisted on for so long. This was Avanta.C. It was more
real, more vivid, more alive than anything he could have possibly imagined,
a dream made tangible, a world spun from pure imagination and code. Every
detail, from the intricate patterns on the crystal spires to the individual
petals of the glowing flowers, was rendered with a breathtaking fidelity
that blurred the line between reality and simulation. He felt a profound
sense of liberation, a lightness he hadn’t known in years, a freedom that
resonated deep within his very being, a sense of boundless possibility.
His mind, once a prison of fear and despair, now felt expansive, capable
of anything. He looked down at his hands. They were no longer gaunt and
trembling, the hands of a refugee on the brink of starvation and despair.
Instead, they were strong and capable, clad in the light armor of his
chosen avatar, the material smooth and cool against his skin. He flexed
his fingers, feeling the unfamiliar strength that coursed through them.

Page 3

His thoughts, for a fleeting moment, drifted back to Earth – the oppressive
EMPRIE, the relentless pursuit, the desperate struggle for mere survival,
the Blue Bird Bounty Hunter’s unexpected, almost miraculous rescue. Those
memories were still there, a faint echo in the back of his mind, a
lingering shadow, a scar on his soul. He could almost feel the cold,
damp air of the warehouse, hear the relentless drumming of the rain on
the corrugated iron roof. But here, in Avanta.C, they felt distant,
almost like a dream, a nightmare from another life, a story told by
someone else. The vividness of his new surroundings seemed to push the
memories further away, blurring their sharp edges. Avanta.C offered a
fresh start, a chance to be someone new, to build a life free from the
crushing weight of his past, free from the ever-present shadows that had
haunted his every waking moment. He felt a profound sense of relief, a
shedding of burdens he hadn’t realized he was carrying. The colors of
Avanta.C seemed brighter, the sounds clearer, the air sweeter. He was
truly here, truly present, in a way he hadn’t been in years. He took
another deep breath, letting the clean, sweet air fill his lungs, a
cleansing balm for his weary soul. He felt a smile, genuine and unforced,
spread across his face, a sensation so unfamiliar it almost startled him.
He looked up at the impossible sky, at the shimmering spires, at the
dancing light, and a single thought formed in his mind: *This is real.
This is my chance.* He felt a surge of exhilaration, a childlike wonder
he hadn’t experienced since before the EMPRIE’s rise. He was a blank
slate, a tabula rasa, ready to be written upon by the experiences of
Avanta.C. He was ready to explore, to discover, to live.

Page 4

As he wandered through the bustling square, his eyes wide with wonder,
he marveled at the sheer diversity of the inhabitants. It was a living
tapestry of fantasy and technology, a vibrant melting pot of digital
life, each individual avatar a unique expression of its player’s
imagination. Elves with graceful, pointed ears and eyes like polished
emeralds haggled playfully with burly dwarves, their beards braided
with intricate runes, over enchanted trinkets that shimmered with latent
magic, their gruff voices softened by laughter. Gnomish engineers, their
faces smudged with grease, tinkered with intricate clockwork devices,
their tiny tools glinting in the bioluminescent light, a symphony of
clicks and whirs. Human adventurers, clad in gleaming armor that bore
the marks of countless battles, strode purposefully through the crowds,
their weapons at their sides, their gazes fixed on unseen quests and
distant horizons, their faces lined with determination and a hint of
weariness. Lucky noticed the subtle details, the way a dwarf’s beard
twitched with amusement, the focused intensity in a gnome’s eyes, the
confident swagger of a seasoned adventurer. He felt a sense of belonging,
a quiet acceptance in this diverse crowd, a stark contrast to the constant
scrutiny he had faced on Earth. He spotted a group gathered around a
large, ornate stone pedestal in the center of the square, its ancient
carvings glowing softly, emanating a faint, warm light. A woman with
fiery red hair, her movements fluid and captivating, strummed a lively
tune on a lute, her fingers dancing across the strings with impossible
speed and grace. Her name, he overheard someone whisper, was Aria, a
bard whose charisma was as potent as her melodies. Her music was infectious,
a vibrant blend of ancient melodies and modern beats that made his feet
tap involuntarily, a rhythm that resonated deep within his soul. Others
danced and cheered in unison, their laughter echoing through the square,
a joyous chorus of virtual revelry. Lucky found himself drawn to the
energy, a faint, genuine smile touching his lips, a smile he hadn’t
realized he was capable of. He felt an inexplicable urge to join in,
to shed the weight of his past and simply exist in this vibrant, new
world, to lose himself in the moment, if only for a little while.

Page 5

He closed his eyes, letting the music wash over him, a cleansing wave
that momentarily erased the scars of his past, the lingering fear, the
constant anxiety. He felt a sense of belonging, a feeling he had never
truly experienced before, a quiet comfort in the midst of this vibrant
chaos. When he opened his eyes, the world seemed even brighter, more
alive. As he stood there, captivated by the music and the joy, a figure
emerged from the periphery of his vision. She moved with an almost
unnatural grace, her steps silent, her presence commanding, drawing his
attention like a moth to a flame. She was tall, slender, yet radiated
an aura of immense power, a quiet strength that seemed to ripple around
her, a subtle distortion in the air around her. Her robes, a deep,
midnight blue, seemed to absorb the light, and her hood was drawn low,
obscuring most of her face. Only a hint of sharp, intelligent eyes, the
color of deep amethyst, and a delicate, yet firm, chin were visible.
Her posture was regal, yet approachable, a paradox that intrigued him.
She stopped a few feet from him, her gaze seemingly piercing through
his cowl, directly into his soul, a silent assessment that made him feel
utterly exposed, as if she could read every thought, every fear, every
hope within him. “You’re new here, aren’t you?” her voice was low,
melodious, yet carried an undercurrent of ancient wisdom, a subtle
resonance that hinted at untold depths, at secrets yet to be revealed.
“I can sense the raw potential within you. And the fear. And the hope.”
Lucky, surprised by her directness, by the uncanny accuracy of her words,
could only nod, his throat suddenly dry. He tried to speak, but the words
caught in his throat, a lump of emotion. “My name is Lucky,” he managed,
his voice a little rough, unused to speaking freely, to sharing anything
about himself. “I… I just arrived.” He felt a blush creep up his neck,
a rare sensation. He was not used to being the center of attention.

Page 6

She offered a small, knowing smile, a subtle curve of her lips that
hinted at a shared understanding, a recognition of something deeper.
“I am Astra,” she replied, her voice softening slightly, a hint of
warmth entering her tone, like sunlight breaking through clouds. “And
I believe our paths were meant to cross. This world, Avanta.C, is vast
and full of wonders. But it is also dangerous, especially for the
uninitiated. Many come here seeking freedom, only to find a different
kind of servitude.” Her gaze swept across the bustling square, a subtle
warning in her eyes. “You see the beauty, the life, the endless
possibilities. But beneath the surface, there are currents, powers at
play that seek to control, to dominate. I can help you navigate these
waters, Lucky. I can teach you to harness your potential, to survive,
and perhaps, even to thrive.” Her words were a lifeline, a beacon in
the swirling sea of his uncertainty. Lucky looked at her, a flicker of
suspicion mixed with a desperate hope warring within him. He had been
betrayed so many times, his trust shattered by those he had believed
in. Could he trust her? Could he afford not to? “Why?” he asked, the
single word heavy with his past experiences, a lifetime of betrayal and
disappointment. “Why would you help me?” He searched her eyes, trying
to find a hint of deception, a flicker of ulterior motive. Astra’s eyes,
though partially hidden, seemed to twinkle with amusement, a playful
glint that belied the seriousness of her words. “Let’s just say I have
my reasons. And perhaps, a shared enemy. But for now, let’s focus on
your immediate needs. Do you have a guild? Any resources? Any allies?”
Her questions were direct, probing, yet not intrusive. Lucky shook his
head, a sense of vulnerability washing over him. “No. Just… just me.”
He felt a pang of loneliness, a familiar ache in his chest. He had always
been alone, a solitary figure against the world.

Page 7

Astra nodded slowly, her expression thoughtful, a hint of compassion
in her eyes. “Then you have me. For now. Come. There is much to learn,
and little time. The shadows in Avanta.C are growing longer, and you,
Lucky, are about to step into their light.” Her words were a challenge,
an invitation, a promise. She turned, her robes swirling around her,
and began to walk away, her movements fluid and silent, a phantom in
the crowd, blending seamlessly with the digital throng. Lucky hesitated
for only a moment, his mind racing, weighing the risks against the
rewards. He didn’t know who Astra was, or why she was helping him. But
in this strange, new world, she was the only hand offered, the only beacon
of guidance in the vast, uncharted territory of Avanta.C. And he was
desperate enough to take it. He took a deep breath, a silent promise
to himself to trust this mysterious stranger, to embrace this unexpected
opportunity. He quickened his pace, falling into step beside her, his
heart filled with a newfound sense of purpose. His journey in Avanta.C
had truly begun, and with Astra by his side, he felt a flicker of
something he hadn’t felt in a long time: hope. A fragile, yet persistent
hope, like a tiny flame flickering in the darkness, threatening to ignite
into a roaring fire. He looked at her, her profile serene and determined,
and a sense of quiet confidence began to bloom within him. He was no
longer alone. He had an ally, a guide, a partner in this new adventure.
He wondered what challenges lay ahead, what secrets Avanta.C held, but
for the first time in a long time, he felt ready to face them. The city
around them continued to hum with life, a vibrant symphony of digital
existence, and Lucky felt himself becoming a part of it, a thread in its
rich tapestry. He was no longer just a refugee; he was an adventurer,
a participant in the grand narrative of Avanta.C. He felt a strange sense
of belonging, a feeling he had never truly experienced before.

Page 8

As they walked, Astra began to explain the basic mechanics of Avanta.C,
her voice a calm, steady stream of information that helped ground Lucky
in this overwhelming new reality. She spoke of experience points, of skill
trees, of crafting and resource gathering. She explained the concept of
guilds, of player-run factions that controlled territories and influenced
the economy. Lucky listened intently, absorbing every detail, his mind
working to piece together the complex puzzle of this virtual world. He
asked questions, sometimes hesitant, sometimes eager, and Astra answered
patiently, her explanations clear and concise. He learned about the different
classes of avatars – warriors, mages, rogues, and healers – and the unique
abilities each possessed. He discovered that his chosen avatar, the balanced
warrior, offered a versatile playstyle, allowing him to adapt to various
combat situations. Astra, he learned, was a powerful mage, specializing
in arcane spells and elemental manipulation. Her knowledge of Avanta.C’s
magic system was encyclopedic, and she spoke of ancient runes and forgotten
incantations with a reverence that bordered on spiritual. He felt a growing
sense of respect for her, not just as a guide, but as a master of her craft.
They passed by bustling market stalls, where vendors hawked their wares
with enthusiastic cries. Lucky saw gleaming weapons, intricately designed
armor, shimmering potions, and exotic ingredients he couldn’t even begin
to identify. Astra pointed out a few key locations: the Grand Exchange,
where players traded goods and services; the Guild Hall, a massive, ornate
structure where powerful factions held court; and the Training Grounds,
a sprawling arena where players honed their combat skills. He felt a surge
of excitement, a desire to explore every nook and cranny of this vast,
intricate world. He imagined himself battling monstrous creatures, uncovering
hidden treasures, and forging his own legend in the annals of Avanta.C.

Page 9

They stopped at a small, unassuming shop tucked away in a quiet alley,
its wooden sign creaking softly in the digital breeze. The interior was
filled with the scent of polished leather and aged parchment. The shopkeeper,
a wizened old gnome with spectacles perched on his nose, looked up as they
entered, his eyes twinkling with curiosity. “Greetings, travelers,” he chirped,
his voice surprisingly robust for his small stature. “How may I assist you?”
Astra explained their needs, requesting basic equipment for Lucky, something
to get him started. The gnome nodded, his fingers flying across a holographic
interface, pulling up various options. Lucky chose a simple, yet sturdy,
leather jerkin and a basic short sword, feeling the familiar weight of a
weapon in his hand once more. He also acquired a small, worn backpack, its
contents surprisingly spacious, and a few basic healing potions. Astra, in
turn, purchased a new spellbook, its cover adorned with intricate arcane
symbols, and a handful of mana crystals. As they left the shop, Lucky felt
a renewed sense of purpose. He was no longer just a refugee; he was an
adventurer, equipped and ready to face the challenges of Avanta.C. He looked
at Astra, her face illuminated by the soft glow of the city lights, and a
sense of gratitude swelled within him. She had given him a chance, a new
beginning, and he would not squander it. He felt a quiet determination
settle deep within his bones. He would learn, he would grow, he would become
strong enough to protect himself, and perhaps, even to protect others. The
path ahead was still uncertain, but for the first time in a long time,
Lucky felt a sense of control over his own destiny. He was no longer just
surviving; he was living. He was playing. And he was ready for whatever
Avanta.C had in store for him. He took a deep breath, the clean, digital
air filling his lungs, and a faint smile touched his lips. This was his
new reality, and he was ready to embrace it.

As the virtual sun began to dip below the horizon, casting long, digital
shadows across the city, Lucky and Astra found themselves on a quiet
overlook, a place where they could see the sprawling metropolis bathed
in the soft, ethereal glow of twilight. The crystal spires shimmered
with a thousand lights, and the rivers of liquid light flowed like veins
of pure energy through the city’s heart. The sounds of the city, once
an overwhelming cacophony, now blended into a harmonious hum, a lullaby
of digital life. Lucky looked out at the vast expanse of Avanta.C, a
world of endless possibilities stretching out before him. He thought of
the EMPRIE, of Earth, of the life he had left behind. Those memories
were still there, a faint ache in his heart, but they no longer held
the power to paralyze him. He was here, now, in Avanta.C, and he was
ready to embrace its wonders and its dangers. He looked at Astra, her
profile silhouetted against the glowing city, and a sense of quiet
resolve settled within him. She was an enigma, a mystery he was eager
to unravel, but she was also his guide, his ally, his first true friend
in this new world. He felt a profound sense of gratitude for her presence,
for her willingness to help a lost soul like him. He knew that their
journey would be fraught with peril, that Avanta.C held secrets and
challenges he couldn’t even begin to imagine. But with Astra by his side,
he felt a flicker of something he hadn’t felt in a long time: hope. A
fragile, yet persistent hope, like a tiny flame flickering in the darkness,
threatening to ignite into a roaring fire. He took a deep breath, the
clean, digital air filling his lungs, and a faint smile touched his lips.
His journey in Avanta.C had truly begun, and he was ready to face whatever
awaited him in the digital realm. He was ready to live. He was ready to
thrive. He was ready to fight. And he was ready to discover who he truly
was, beyond the shadow of the EMPRIE. The adventure had just begun.
