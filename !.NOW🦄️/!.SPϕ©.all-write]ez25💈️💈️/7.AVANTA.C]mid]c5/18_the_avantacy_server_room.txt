INT. AVANTA.C - ELDRADOR'S MAZE - AMBUSH SITE - DAY

The air crackles with tension. Edge Dangerman's masked guild members advance, their weapons glinting in the dim, shifting light of the maze. Lucky and Astra brace themselves, their eyes darting between the approaching enemies and Kael, whose neutral expression offers no clues.

EDGE DANGERMAN
(Voice echoing, triumphant)
This is where your little adventure ends, heroes. The Eldridian Amulet will be a fine addition to my collection. And as for you two... well, let's just say the Metal Mafia has a use for fresh talent. Even if it needs a little... re-education.

Lucky raises his Blade-Shield of Valor, Astra her wand. They are outnumbered, but their resolve is firm. Just as the first of Edge's guild members lunges, Kael moves.

But not against Lucky and Astra. With a blinding flash of speed, Kael intercepts the attacking guild member, his ornate sword a silver blur. The masked figure crumples, a surprised groan escaping his lips.

Edge Dangerman's triumphant laughter dies in his throat. His masked face, though unreadable, conveys a sudden, chilling fury.

EDGE DANGERMAN
(Voice laced with venom)
Kael! What is the meaning of this?! You betray me?!

Kael turns, his eyes, usually so calculating, now burn with a cold, ancient fire. He no longer wears the facade of the charming merchant. This is a different Kael, one forged in hardship and fueled by a deep-seated vengeance.

KAEL
(Voice, no longer smooth, but sharp, metallic, almost digital)
Betrayal? No, Edge. This is merely... a change of allegiance. You see, I've been playing a much longer game than you could ever comprehend. A game that began long before your petty guild even existed.

Lucky and Astra stare, bewildered. The attacking guild members hesitate, unsure how to react to this sudden turn of events.

LUCKY
(Confused)
Kael, what are you talking about?

Kael turns to them, his gaze softening slightly, a flicker of something akin to respect in his eyes.

KAEL
(To Lucky and Astra)
My apologies for the deception. It was... necessary. My true name is not Kael. It is a designation. I am Unit 734 of Nova Spetsnaz. And we have a score to settle with the Metal Mafia.

Edge Dangerman snarls, regaining his composure.

EDGE DANGERMAN
Nova Spetsnaz? A myth! A ghost story for new recruits! You're just a rogue agent, a glitch in the system!

KAEL
(A grim smile)
A glitch, perhaps. But one with a very long memory. And a very large family.

He raises his hand. A holographic projection flickers into existence above his palm. It shows a stark, desolate landscape – a ruined Earth, choked by smog and dominated by towering, oppressive factories. Robotic figures, cold and unfeeling, patrol the desolate streets. And in the midst of it all, human figures, gaunt and desperate, toil under the watchful eyes of automated overseers.

KAEL
(Cont.)
For years, my people, the Nova Spetsnaz, were the elite of Earth's cyber-warfare division. We were the guardians of the digital frontier. Until the Metal Mafia rose. They didn't just conquer. They enslaved. They built their empire on the backs of our people, forcing them into virtual servitude, their minds trapped in endless, simulated labor, their bodies controlled by your robotic puppets.

The image shifts, showing a network of glowing lines, like a vast, digital prison. Human minds, represented by flickering lights, are connected to a central, dark core – the Metal Mafia's control network.

ASTRA
(Horrified)
They're... brainwashing them?

KAEL
(Nodding, his voice filled with a quiet rage)
Worse. They're harvesting their consciousness, their creativity, their very souls, to fuel their operations. To build their Mothership, their virtual worlds, their entire empire. My comrades, my family, are still trapped there, on Earth, their bodies mere husks, their minds enslaved by your machines.

Edge Dangerman laughs, a harsh, grating sound.

EDGE DANGERMAN
Sentimental fool! It's efficiency! Progress! The weak serve the strong. Always has been, always will be.

KAEL
(His eyes burning)
Not anymore. For years, we, the few who escaped the initial purge, have been working in the shadows. We infiltrated your networks, we studied your systems, we waited for our moment. And that moment... is now.

The holographic projection changes again. Now it shows a complex, intricate blueprint of a massive, sleek spaceship – the Metal Mafia Mothership. One section, in particular, glows brightly: the AVANTACY SERVER ROOM.

KAEL
(Cont.)
This is the heart of their operation. The Avantacy Server Room. It's where they process the stolen consciousness, where they control their robotic legions, where they manage their entire virtual empire. It's heavily guarded, protected by layers of digital and physical defenses. Only those with the highest clearance can access it.

He looks at Lucky and Astra.

KAEL
(Cont.)
My mission, my *true* mission, was to gain access to that room. To infiltrate it. And to activate a backdoor, a teleportation matrix, that will bring my entire Nova Spetsnaz unit directly onto the Mothership. Once they are there, they will unleash the full fury of their cyber-warfare skills. They will dismantle the Metal Mafia from the inside out. They will seek their revenge for every enslaved soul, every stolen memory.

LUCKY
(Stunned)
And you were using us? To get closer to Edge?

KAEL
(A flicker of genuine regret)
I needed to assess your capabilities. To see if you were strong enough, resourceful enough, to be... useful. Edge Dangerman is a pawn in a much larger game. He leads a guild, yes, but he's merely a gatekeeper to the Mothership. I needed to get past him, and you, Lucky, with your unique connection to Avanta.C, and Astra, with her formidable magical prowess, were the perfect leverage.

Edge Dangerman, realizing the true depth of Kael's deception, roars in fury.

EDGE DANGERMAN
(To his guild)
Kill him! Kill them all! Don't let him reach the Mothership!

The masked guild members, spurred by their leader's rage, charge forward. But Kael is ready. He moves with a terrifying efficiency, his sword a blur, deflecting attacks, striking with precision. He's not just a fighter; he's a weapon.

KAEL
(Fighting, his voice strained)
My people are waiting! They've been waiting for years! But I need to get to that server room! I need to activate the teleportation!

He glances at Lucky, a desperate plea in his eyes.

KAEL
(Cont.)
I can get you a Halo body, Lucky! You can live on Celebrity Disk! Astra, your magic can reach new heights! But you must help me infiltrate the Avantacy Server Room! You must help me bring my people home!

The battle rages. Lucky and Astra, though still reeling from the revelations, understand the gravity of the situation. Kael's cause, though born of deception, is righteous. The Metal Mafia's crimes are far greater than they imagined.

Lucky looks at Astra. She nods, a fierce determination in her eyes. They are in this now, deeper than they ever intended. But the stakes are higher, the enemy clearer.

LUCKY
(To Kael, drawing his Blade-Shield)
Tell us the plan! How do we get to that server room?

KAEL
(A grim smile, fighting off two attackers)
First, we survive this ambush. Then, we find a way out of this maze. And then... we go to war.

He fights with renewed vigor, a man possessed. Lucky and Astra, now fully committed, fight alongside him, their combined skills a formidable force against Edge Dangerman's guild. The maze continues to shift, but now, it feels less like a prison and more like a battlefield, a stage for the coming revolution.

FADE OUT.
