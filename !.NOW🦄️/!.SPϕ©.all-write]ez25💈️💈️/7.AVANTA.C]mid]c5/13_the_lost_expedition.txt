INT. AVANTA.C - BUSTLING TOWN SQUARE - DAY

Lucky and Astra are making their way through a bustling town square, a different one from their initial entry point, but equally vibrant. They're discussing their next move, perhaps heading towards a Mission Board they spotted earlier. The usual cacophony of Avanta.C – vendor calls, player chatter, distant music – fills the air.

As they walk, Lucky feels a prickle on the back of his neck. A familiar sensation. He glances over his shoulder, subtly, but sees nothing out of the ordinary. He dismisses it, attributing it to lingering paranoia from his Earth days.

They stop at a stall selling exotic potions, examining the shimmering concoctions. Lucky feels it again, stronger this time – the distinct feeling of being watched. He turns sharply. This time, he catches a glimpse of a figure, cloaked and hooded, melting into the shadows of an alleyway. The figure is always just out of sight, always observing.

LUCKY
(Low voice to Astra)
Are you feeling that?

ASTRA
(Without looking up from a vial of glowing liquid)
Feeling what?

LUCKY
Like we're being watched. I've seen someone, or something, following us for a while now. Always just out of sight.

Astra's casual demeanor drops. Her eyes narrow, and she subtly scans their surroundings. Her hand instinctively goes to the hilt of her wand.

ASTRA
(Voice tight)
I noticed it too. A faint ripple in the ambient magic. Someone's been very careful.

They continue to move, pretending to be engrossed in the market, but their senses are heightened. They lead their unseen tail through a winding path, past crowded taverns and quiet backstreets, trying to flush them out.

Finally, as they emerge into a smaller, less crowded plaza, the figure steps out from behind a large fountain. He's TALL and lean, dressed in fine, dark merchant's robes, but there's an underlying tension in his posture. His face is handsome, with sharp, intelligent eyes that seem to miss nothing. He carries no visible weapons, only a small, ornate satchel.

He approaches them with a confident, almost theatrical stride, a charming smile playing on his lips.

KAEL
(Voice smooth, cultured)
Forgive my intrusion, esteemed travelers. But I couldn't help but notice your... unique aura. And your recent exploits in the Lost Temple. My name is Kael, a humble traveling merchant. And I believe we might have interests in common.

Lucky and Astra exchange skeptical glances. Kael's words are too precise, too knowing. He knows about the Lost Temple, a quest they only recently completed.

LUCKY
(Guarded)
Interests? What kind of interests?

KAEL
(Spreading his hands in a gesture of openness)
Information, opportunity, perhaps even... mutual benefit. I have a keen eye for talent, and you two, I must say, possess it in spades. I've heard whispers of your prowess, your rapid ascent through Avanta.C's ranks. It's quite remarkable.

Astra's eyes narrow. She can sense a powerful, hidden energy emanating from him, carefully masked. He's more than just a merchant.

ASTRA
(Coolly)
And what exactly do you sell, Kael? Besides flattery?

KAEL
(Chuckles, unperturbed)
Ah, a discerning mind. I sell access. To rare goods, to hidden knowledge, to... opportunities that others might overlook. And sometimes, I simply offer my services as a guide, or a partner, to those embarking on particularly... lucrative ventures.

He pauses, his gaze lingering on Lucky.

KAEL
(Cont.)
Like, for instance, a certain expedition that vanished in the northern mountains. A very intriguing prospect, wouldn't you agree?

Lucky's jaw tightens. He knows Kael is referring to "The Lost Expedition," a quest they've been considering. Kael's knowledge is too specific to be coincidence.

Lucky and Astra exchange another look. They decide to play along, to pretend to be taken in by his charm, while keeping a very close eye on his movements. This Kael is dangerous, but he might also be useful.

LUCKY
(A forced smile)
Perhaps. What did you have in mind, Kael?

KAEL
(His smile widening, a hint of triumph in his eyes)
Excellent. I believe we're going to be very good friends.

FADE OUT.
