CAUTION: Plotline and Timeline Consistency

This document outlines potential areas of conflict between the "Red Blu & U" clone storyline and the main "Soul Pen Franchise" timeline. It is intended to serve as a guide to prevent plotlines from becoming entangled and to maintain lore consistency.

**1. Clone Storyline Placement:**

The story of Clone 734 and the "Red Blu & U" tangential franchise primarily takes place within the earlier parts of the "Soul Pen" timeline. The main settings are Earth, Mars, the lunar base, the Metal Sun, and the Sol system. The clones' adventures should be grounded in these locations.

**2. Limited Crossover with Later Books:**

While the possibility of clones venturing into the wider galaxy exists (e.g., through Star-Trek-like expeditions from Sol), direct involvement in the events of later books, such as the Planet X/Extremophiles storyline or the Time Fu/Land of Time arc, should be avoided. These events are specific to Lucky Shepherd's journey and introducing clones could create continuity issues.

**3. Character Interactions:**

Interactions between the clones and main franchise characters (Lucky, Astra, Gem, etc.) should be handled with care. While clones may encounter these characters, their roles should be tangential to the main plot of the 13 books. The focus of the "Red Blu & U" story should remain on the clones' perspective and their struggle against the Red Queen.

**4. Worldbuilding Consistency:**

The worldbuilding elements introduced in the clone storyline (e.g., the planet Xylos) should be considered additions to the existing lore, but should not contradict the established settings and rules of the "Soul Pen" universe. For example, Xylos can be a planet within the Sol system or a nearby system that the smugglers frequent, rather than a distant, unexplored world.

By adhering to these guidelines, we can ensure that the "Red Blu & U" storyline remains a compelling and consistent part of the larger "Soul Pen Franchise."
