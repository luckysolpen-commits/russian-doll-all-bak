Page 66: Dismantling the Artificial Support Beams

You focus on dismantling the sophisticated support beams that are artificially maintaining the tunnel collapse. Your drone's analysis systems reveal that these beams are not just structural supports but part of a complex tunnel control system that can manipulate the underground environment for tactical purposes.

Three critical beam systems are identified: The Primary Structural Supports, which maintain the collapse pattern and require precise dismantling to prevent further cave-ins; The Navigation Disruption Array, where the beams generate electromagnetic fields that confuse navigation systems and trap targets in the collapsed area; and The Emergency Seal Network, which can instantly close off the entire tunnel section when activated.

As you begin dismantling the beam system, your sensors detect that it's connected to a larger tunnel management network that spans vast sections of the Martian underground. This suggests the ambush is not just a local operation but part of a coordinated effort using the tunnel system itself as a weapon.

The artificial nature of the collapse becomes more apparent as you work - the beams are not just maintaining the rubble but actively shifting it to maintain the blockage while keeping escape routes sealed. The sophistication of this system exceeds anything in the standard faction equipment manuals.

Your dismantling efforts trigger secondary systems that attempt to reconfigure the collapse pattern. The tunnel system is fighting your efforts to clear a path, suggesting a level of autonomy or external control that's deeply concerning.
