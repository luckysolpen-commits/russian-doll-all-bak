Page 68: Creating a New Route Around the Collapse

Rather than clearing the existing collapse, you decide to use your drone's demolition charges to create an entirely new route around the blocked tunnel section. This approach would bypass the artificial collapse entirely, but requires precise calculations to avoid triggering further cave-ins or creating a route that's unusable for the transport convoy.

Your drone's mapping systems identify three potential new routes: The Lower Tunnel Bypass, which goes beneath the collapse through older geological layers; The Parallel Shaft Detour, which follows the natural tunnel formation to create a side route; and The Vertical Access Route, which uses the tunnel's vertical dimensions to go above the collapse area.

As you prepare to implement the new route, your sensors detect that the tunnel walls in these areas are not as stable as they appear. The artificial collapse may be the result of attempts to use similar demolition techniques, causing you to reconsider the risks of creating a new route.

The transport's size and equipment requirements limit your options - the new route must not only bypass the collapse but accommodate the convoy's dimensions and capabilities. Your demolition charges are powerful enough to clear the path, but miscalculation could make the situation worse.

You realize that the ambushers may have counted on a new route being created, potentially setting up additional obstacles or defensive positions along the most likely alternative paths.
