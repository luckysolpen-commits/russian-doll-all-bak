Page 83: Coordinating the Multi-Point Strike

"Team Alpha, secure the eastern approach. Team Beta, target the northern defenses. Team Gamma, prepare for the western assault," you coordinate as your forces spread across multiple vectors to attack the Blood Queen Monarchy outpost simultaneously. The goal is to overwhelm their defenses through coordinated pressure on multiple fronts.

Your tactical analysis identifies three critical targets for simultaneous assault: The Command Nexus, where the outpost's central control systems coordinate all defensive operations; The Honor Guard Barracks, where the elite defenders are quartered and from which reinforcements can be deployed; and The Transformation Research Labs, where the carcazod experiments that prompted this mission are being conducted.

As your teams move into position, you realize that the Monarchy's honor-based defensive protocols may actually work in your favor during a multi-point assault - their formal combat systems require individual challenges and formal responses that could prevent them from coordinating an effective multi-front defense.

The timing of your assault will be critical. If the teams cannot maintain synchronization, the Monarchy forces could defeat them in detail by addressing each threat separately. The honor-based combat protocols might delay their responses, but if they adapt to the multi-front assault, your forces could face coordinated counter-attacks.

Your communication systems detect that the Monarchy is attempting to respond to your assault through formal challenge systems, but the impossibility of issuing individual challenges to multiple simultaneous attacks is creating confusion in their defensive coordination.
