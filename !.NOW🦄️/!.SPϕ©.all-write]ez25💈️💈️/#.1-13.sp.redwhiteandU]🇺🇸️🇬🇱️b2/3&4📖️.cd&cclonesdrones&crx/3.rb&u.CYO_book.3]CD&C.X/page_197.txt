Page 197: Attempting to Breach the Anchor Point

As you attempt to breach the Anchor Point where the retreat route is physically blocked by mobile tunnel sections, your weapons engage with the most sophisticated obstacle in the escape path. The anchor point controls the physical structure of the tunnel itself, using living tunnel systems that can be sealed or opened as needed.

Three breach outcomes emerge: Tunnel Structure Destabilization, where attempting to breach the anchor point may cause structural collapse that affects the entire tunnel section; Mobile Section Disruption, revealing how the tunnel segments are connected and how disrupting one affects the others; and Emergency Seal Override, demonstrating how the anchor point is connected to emergency sealing systems throughout the tunnel network.

As the anchor point breach is attempted, you observe the sophisticated nature of the living tunnel system - its ability to reconfigure itself, its integration of biological and mechanical systems, and its capacity for autonomous response to threats.

Your breach attempt reveals the advanced nature of the tunnel modification technology, while also showing how the escape route obstacles are part of a larger, self-regulating tunnel system that responds to threats automatically.

The anchor point breach provides the potential to clear the physical barrier blocking the escape route, but also risks structural collapse that could trap the transport or trigger emergency sealing that blocks all nearby exits.
