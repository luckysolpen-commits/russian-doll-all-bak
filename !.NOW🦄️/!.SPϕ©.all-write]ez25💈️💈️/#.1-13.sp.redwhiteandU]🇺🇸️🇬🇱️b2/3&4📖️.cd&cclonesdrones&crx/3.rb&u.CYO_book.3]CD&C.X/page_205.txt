Page 205: Attempting the Parallel Shaft Detour

As you attempt the Parallel Shaft Detour following the natural tunnel formation to create a side route, your demolition charges reshape the natural geology to create a new path that runs parallel to the original tunnel but bypasses the collapse zone. This approach works with the existing structure rather than creating entirely new passages.

Three detour outcomes emerge: Geometric Alignment Challenges, where following the natural tunnel formations requires precise calculations to maintain connection with the original route; Structural Integration Requirements, revealing how the new route must connect properly with existing tunnel systems; and Natural Formation Adaptation, demonstrating how the route must accommodate the existing geological patterns and features.

As the parallel shaft detour is attempted, you observe how the natural tunnel formations provide both opportunities and constraints - their stability and existing infrastructure, but also their specific shapes and directions that may not align perfectly with your needs.

Your detour attempt reveals how the natural geology of Mars provides a foundation for the entire tunnel system, while showing how working with natural formations can be more efficient than creating completely artificial routes.

The parallel shaft detour provides a route that works with natural formations, but also requires precise engineering to maintain the necessary geometries and connections while following the constraints imposed by the natural tunnel structure.
