CYOD#3: Clones, Drones and Carcazods - Detailed Story Structure

**Act 1: Awakening on Mars (Pages 1-30)**
- Introduction to Mars' underground tunnel system and surface hubs
- Player wakes up/arrives with initial identity (clone/droner)
- Introduces the four main governments: Solar Empire, Blood Queen Monarchy, Red African Union, Robot State
- Discovery of the clown hunting world and carcazod mythology
- First major choice: Loyalty, Rogue, Spy, or Transformation

**Act 2: The Martian Underground (Pages 31-90)**
- Branch 1: Government Loyalist Path
  - Missions serving your assigned faction
  - Interactions with other clones/drones
  - Discovering internal conflicts and corruption
  - Opportunities to rise in rank or make moral compromises

- Branch 2: Clown Hunter Path
  - Learning to track and hunt other clowns
  - Understanding the underground network
  - Moral dilemmas about the nature of clowns vs. clones
  - Encounters with other hunters and prey

- Branch 3: Spy Network Path
  - Training in espionage and disguise
  - Information gathering missions across factions
  - Double-crossing and deception challenges
  - Risk of discovery and betrayal

- Branch 4: Transformation Path
  - Researching the path to carcazod status
  - Dangerous experiments and mutations
  - Physical and mental changes
  - Confronting the ethical implications of metamorphosis

**Act 3: The Carcazod Threshold (Pages 91-150)**
- Convergence points where different paths begin to cross
- Major conflicts involving all factions
- Ultimate choices leading to various endings
- Incorporation of Moon base elements if relevant to the path
- The SS. SOL concept and the honorable death for clones

**Key Story Elements Throughout:**
- Real Mars geography integrated with fictional tunnel networks
- Drone piloting sequences and underground navigation
- Mutations and psychological transformations
- Honor codes and faction loyalty conflicts
- The philosophical question of identity (clone, clown, carcazod)

**Potential Convergence Scenarios:**
- All paths eventually lead to a confrontation about the nature of Mars' society
- The truth about the relationship between clones, clowns, and carcazods
- A choice that determines the fate of the Martian governments
- The possibility of transcending assigned roles through the carcazod transformation
