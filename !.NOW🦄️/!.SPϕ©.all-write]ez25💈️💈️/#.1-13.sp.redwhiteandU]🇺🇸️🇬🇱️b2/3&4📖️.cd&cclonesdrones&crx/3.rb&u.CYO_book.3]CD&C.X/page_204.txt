Page 204: Creating the Lower Tunnel Bypass

As you create the Lower Tunnel Bypass going beneath the collapse through older geological layers, your demolition charges open a route through geological formations that predate the current tunnel system. This deeper route presents unique challenges but offers a path that bypasses the artificial collapse entirely.

Three bypass outcomes emerge: Geological Stability Assessment, where the ancient rock layers may be more or less stable than the artificial tunnel system and require different safety considerations; Deep Tunnel Navigation Challenges, revealing how the lower passages may have different environmental conditions and unknown obstacles; and Route Accessibility Verification, demonstrating whether the new route is suitable for the transport convoy's size and capabilities.

As the lower tunnel bypass is created, you observe the geological complexity of the deep layers - their different composition, their connection to underground water systems, and their potential to contain ancient structures or systems from Mars' early history.

Your bypass creation reveals the multi-layered nature of Mars' underground, while also showing how deeper routes may connect to systems and resources unknown to the surface civilizations.

The lower tunnel bypass provides a route completely separate from the collapsed area, but also requires navigation through unknown geological conditions that may pose new risks or contain ancient systems that could be dangerous or beneficial.
