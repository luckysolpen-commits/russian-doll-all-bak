Page 61: Exploring the Mutation Acceleration Zone

The mutation acceleration zone uses a combination of electromagnetic fields, chemical catalysts, and quantum resonance to speed up the transformation process that normally takes months or years to occur over mere hours or days. Your drone's sensors detect that this facility was designed to mass-produce carcazods, though the process appears to have been stopped centuries ago.

Three distinct acceleration methods are present: The Electromagnetic Induction System, which uses precisely calibrated fields to trigger rapid genetic changes; The Chemical Catalyst Chamber, where transformation-enhancing compounds accelerate biological modifications; and The Quantum Acceleration Chamber, where time dilation effects speed up the transformation at the molecular level.

As you navigate the zone, you realize that the rapid acceleration processes caused instability in many subjects, leading to the facility's shutdown. The walls show scorch marks and other signs of accidents caused by beings whose bodies couldn't handle the accelerated transformation pace.

Your drone detects that some of the acceleration chambers still contain partially transformed beings who are caught in ongoing transformation processes. The rapid acceleration methods didn't just speed up the process - they also increased the likelihood of catastrophic failure or incomplete transformation.

The ancient researchers who operated this facility eventually realized that carcazod transformation requires a gradual integration process that can't be rushed without significant risks. However, the knowledge of how to accelerate the process could be invaluable, if the risks can be properly managed.
