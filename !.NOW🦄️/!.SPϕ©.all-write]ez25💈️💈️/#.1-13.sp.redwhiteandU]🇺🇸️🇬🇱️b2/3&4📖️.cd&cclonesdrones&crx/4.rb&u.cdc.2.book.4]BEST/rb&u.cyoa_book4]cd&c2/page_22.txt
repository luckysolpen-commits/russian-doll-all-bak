Sacrifice is for fools. You didn't survive the training vats just to die in a forgotten tunnel.

You slam the eject button. The cockpit canopy blows, and your seat rockets you into the darkness of the tunnel. You land hard, the impact jarring your bones despite the suit's shock absorbers. Behind you, your Digger erupts in a massive fireball, the concussion wave tearing past you down the tunnel. The clowns who were shooting at you are now silhouettes in a cloud of fire and shrapnel.

You are alive, but you are in trouble. You're on foot, with only a standard medkit, a sidearm with two spare clips, and a single nutrition bar. You are days, maybe weeks, of travel from the nearest friendly outpost. And you are in clown territory.

You check your bearings. The tunnel stretches into darkness in both directions. You have no map of this area. Your only guide is your instinct. One direction feels like it leads deeper into the unmapped spurs, while the other feels like it heads back toward the more established, patrolled main tunnels.
