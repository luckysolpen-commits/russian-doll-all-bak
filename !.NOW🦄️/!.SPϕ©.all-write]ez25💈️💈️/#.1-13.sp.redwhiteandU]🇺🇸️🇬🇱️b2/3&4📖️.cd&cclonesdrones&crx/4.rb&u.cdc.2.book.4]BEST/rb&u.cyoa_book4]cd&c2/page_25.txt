"Words are cheap," you broadcast, your weapons still hot. "I'm not risking my neck on a story. Show me."

The clown, qynx, is silent for a moment. The tension hangs heavy in the air. "Alright, hotshot," she says. "Your funeral. Open a secure channel. I'm sending you a file."

A data packet arrives. You run it through three different virus scans before opening it. It's a lab report. Stamped with the official seal of Redplanet Labs. And it's horrifying.

It details "Project Chimera," a plan to use a genetically engineered fungus to create a telepathic hive mind that would ensure clone loyalty. It talks about "acceptable losses" and "repurposing failed subjects." Then you get to the addendum. It's a video file.

The footage is from a security camera inside the lab. You see scientists in RPL coats strapping a clown—a captured rebel—to a table. You watch as they inject him with the fungus. You watch him scream and convulse. You watch as his body twists and mutates into something barely human.

You feel a wave of nausea. This is what you serve. This is what you protect.

"Satisfied?" qynx's voice crackles in your ear, dripping with sarcasm. "Now, are you going to let us go, or are you going to help them cover this up?"

The choice is no longer just about this moment. It's about what side you're on.
