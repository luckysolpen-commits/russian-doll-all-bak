You are a soldier. You see a tactical problem, and you solve it. Scraptown is a disaster waiting to happen.

You go to Gus and the woman who runs the bar, whose name you learn is Sal. "This town is a target," you tell them. "Sooner or later, the Empire will find it. You're not ready."

"We've survived this long," Gus grumbles.

"Surviving isn't winning," you counter. "Let me train you. Let me turn this mob into a militia."

They are reluctant, but they see the logic in your words. You begin a grueling training regimen. You teach the clowns how to shoot, how to set up ambushes, how to fortify their positions. You turn Scraptown from a refugee camp into a fortress.

Your efforts are not a moment too soon. A few weeks later, a full Imperial platoon attacks the town. It's a brutal, bloody fight. But Scraptown is ready. The clowns fight with a discipline and a ferocity that the Imperials do not expect.

Thanks to your training, they hold their ground. The Imperials are repelled, forced to retreat. Scraptown has won. And you are its hero. You are no longer an outsider. You are the commander of the Scraptown militia.
