Solitude has its charms, but curiosity is a powerful motivator. You have to know.

You spend months repairing the derelict alien ship, using your ingenuity and the resources of the asteroid belt to bring its strange, organic systems back to life. It is a labor of love, a puzzle that consumes you.

Finally, the day comes. You leave your old shuttle behind and set out in the alien vessel, following the starmap into the deep, unknown. The journey is long and strange. The ship seems to sing to you, its systems resonating with your own thoughts. You are becoming one with the vessel, a true ghost in the machine.

You arrive at your destination: a colossal space station, adrift in the void between galaxies. It is a city of light and crystal, a place of breathtaking beauty and impossible technology. A message is broadcast to your ship, in a language you have never heard but somehow understand. "Welcome, traveler," it says. "Welcome to the Library."

You have stumbled upon a repository of galactic knowledge, a place where ancient, god-like beings have stored the history of a thousand civilizations. You have a choice. You can stay, and spend an eternity learning the secrets of the universe. Or you can take what you have learned and return to the solar system, to change the course of human history.
