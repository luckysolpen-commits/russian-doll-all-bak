The drone is the immediate threat. Its high-caliber rounds are tearing through your Digger's hull plating like it's paper.

You ignore the crippled mining vessel and pour all your remaining firepower into the drone. It's agile, juking and weaving through your shots. You switch to manual targeting, leading the drone, anticipating its movements. You fire a concentrated burst from your primary cannon. The shot connects, blowing the drone into a thousand pieces of scrap metal.

The silence that follows is deafening. But it doesn't last. The crew from the disabled clown ship has used the time you gave them to abandon their vessel. They've scrambled into the rocks and are now taking potshots at you with handheld plasma cutters. Your Digger is heavily damaged, your shields are depleted, and you're now in a ground firefight you can't win.

You have one last option. The Digger's core is becoming unstable from the damage. If you overload it, you can create a massive explosion. It would take out the clowns, the evidence, and you. A noble sacrifice. Alternatively, you could try to eject and make a run for it into the dark tunnels, but you'd be on foot, hunted, and with no supplies.
