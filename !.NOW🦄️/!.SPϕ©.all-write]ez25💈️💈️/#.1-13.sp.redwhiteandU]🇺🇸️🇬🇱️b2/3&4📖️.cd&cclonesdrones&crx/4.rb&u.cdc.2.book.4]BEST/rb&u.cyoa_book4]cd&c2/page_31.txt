The wilderness is not for you. You are a product of civilization, even if you are currently on the wrong side of it. You turn back, hoping to intercept a friendly patrol.

Your suit's life support is rated for 72 hours. You spend 48 of them walking. The hunger is a physical pain, and the thirst is worse. Just as you are about to give up hope, you hear the hum of a patrol craft.

A standard Digger-class vehicle rounds the bend and its searchlight falls on you. You raise your hands in surrender. The vehicle lands, and two clones in standard Imperial gear emerge, weapons raised.

"Designation?" one of them barks.

"700," you reply, your voice hoarse. "My ship was ambushed. I was the sole survivor."

They look at each other. Their protocol is clear: rescue any loyal clone. But your story has holes. You are far from your patrol sector, and your uniform is ragged. They might believe you, or they might decide you are a deserter and execute you on the spot. Everything depends on their disposition.

One of them, the leader, steps forward. "We'll see about that," he says, his voice cold. "Put him in restraints."
