"I was stationed on a Star Destroyer," you say, puffing out your chest. "We were tasked with hunting down a group of clown insurgents. But when I saw their bravery, their love of freedom, I knew I was on the wrong side. I sabotaged the ship's reactor and escaped in the confusion."

Gus, the big clone, throws his head back and laughs, a loud, booming sound. "A Star Destroyer, you say? By yourself? You're either the best damn clown in the system, or you're a terrible liar."

He claps you on the shoulder again, hard enough to make you stumble. "I'm going with 'terrible liar.' But I like your spirit. Anyone who hates the Empire that much is welcome in Scraptown."

You've been accepted, but you are also a known liar. The other clowns are friendly, but they don't trust you. You are an outsider, a curiosity. You will have to work twice as hard to earn their respect.

You are given the worst jobs in town: cleaning the recycler units, hauling waste, guarding the perimeter in the dead of night. It is a hard, thankless life. But it is a free life.

One day, while on guard duty, you spot something in the distance. A dust trail. A fast-moving vehicle, heading straight for Scraptown. It's an Imperial scout car.
