The truth is a liability. Your survival depends on a simple, clean story.

In the debriefing, you stick to your fabricated report. You engaged two hostile ships, destroyed them both, and your vessel was critically damaged in the fight. You are a hero, a model soldier who overcame the odds. You mention nothing of dying words, data chips, or secret projects. If you have any incriminating evidence, you dispose of it before the rescue team arrives.

Your commander commends you for your bravery and your efficiency. You are given a new Digger-class patrol craft and a promotion. You are, on paper, a perfect clone.

But you are living a lie. Every time you see a Redplanet Labs transport, or hear a whisper about the Carcazod project, you feel a cold knot of dread in your stomach. You have information that could change the course of the war, but you are too afraid to use it. You are a spy with no one to report to, a rebel trapped in a loyalist's uniform.

The days turn into weeks. Your secret festers. You have to do something.
