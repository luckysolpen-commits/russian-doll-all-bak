You are a soldier, but you are not an assassin. You cannot kill a clone in cold blood, regardless of his allegiance.

"I can't do it," you say, shaking your head. "I'm a fighter, not a murderer. I can't kill one of my own."

Zi looks at you, her expression hardening. "Then you are useless to us, 700," she says. "We don't have room for sentimentality in this war. You're either with us, or you're against us."

Ranch, the old smuggler, puts a hand on Zi's arm. "Easy, Zi. He's still green. Give him a chance." He turns to you. "Look, kid. We need information. You've got access to the Imperial networks. That's your strength. We've got another mission. A less bloody one. A data heist."

He slides another data chip across the table. "Martian Markets is developing a new surveillance system. We need the schematics. It's heavily encrypted, but you've got the skills to crack it. Prove your worth that way."

You have a choice. You can take this mission, a chance to prove your loyalty without resorting to murder. Or you can walk away from the rebellion, back into the dangerous anonymity of the Martian underworld.
