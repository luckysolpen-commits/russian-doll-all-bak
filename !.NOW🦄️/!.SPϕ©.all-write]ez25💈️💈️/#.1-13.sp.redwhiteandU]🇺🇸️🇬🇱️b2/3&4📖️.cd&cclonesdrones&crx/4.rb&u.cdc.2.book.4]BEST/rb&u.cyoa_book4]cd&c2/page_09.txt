You look at the thrashing creature on the display, and you don't see power. You see a cage. A biological prison that will chew away at your mind until only a monster remains. A cold dread washes over you, more terrifying than any battlefield. You want to serve the Empire, but not like this. Not by becoming a thing.

"Proctor," you say, choosing your words with extreme care. "My loyalty is absolute. I believe I can serve the Empire more effectively in my current form. My skills as a pilot and operative—"

"Your skills are a resource," The Proctor interrupts, his voice losing its warmth. "And we will decide how that resource is best utilized. To refuse this... gift... is to question our judgment. It is a sign of potential instability." He deactivates the display. "I am disappointed, 700. Very."

You are dismissed. The air in the corridor outside feels heavy. You have been marked. Clones who show "instability" have a tendency to disappear. They are reassigned to hazardous duty, sent on impossible missions, or simply... recycled. Your every move is now watched. Your position here on the moon has become a prison of paranoia.

You need to get out. You could try to steal a ship and flee, but where would you go? The entire system is crawling with Empire patrols. Or you could try to find a flaw in the system, a way to prove your loyalty beyond a shadow of a doubt, even if it means putting yourself in immense danger.
