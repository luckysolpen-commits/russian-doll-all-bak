Patience is a virtue for those with time to spare. You don't. The images from the lab, the things qynx said, burn in your mind. You have to find her.

You start dropping hooks into the seedy underbelly of the Martian tunnel system. You use your off-duty hours to frequent dive bars in the lower levels, places where disgruntled clones and shady information brokers mingle. You flash a little bit of credit, you let slip a few choice anti-Empire sentiments. You make yourself noticeable.

It's a dangerous game. For every potential rebel contact, there are three Imperial informants. You can feel their eyes on you. But you persist. Finally, you get a bite. A grizzled old clone with a nasty scar across his face tells you about a place called the "Underground Xircus," a hidden network for clowns. He gives you a location for a meeting.

It could be your ticket into the rebellion. It could also be a trap set by Imperial intelligence.

The meeting is in a disused cargo bay in a remote sector. As you arrive, you see two figures waiting in the shadows.
