If you are to survive, it must be as a loyal clone. You must erase the stain of your insubordination with an act of undeniable devotion.

You go to The Proctor. "I was wrong to question your judgment," you say, your voice a perfect mix of contrition and resolve. "My fear made me weak. Allow me to prove my loyalty. Give me the most hazardous mission you have."

The Proctor studies you, his ancient eyes searching for any hint of deception. "Hazardous duty is easily found, 700," he says slowly. "We have a situation on Mars. A Clown-operated illegal broadcasting station near the polar ice caps is spreading seditious propaganda. The problem is, it's protected by a new generation of drone technology we haven't been able to crack. They're... adaptive."

He smirks. "It seems my new favorite 'Transformer' drones, as you so amusingly call them, are being copied by the enemy. Funny, isn't it? My own little AI children, turned against me." He leans forward. "The mission is simple. Go to Mars. Find the station. Infiltrate it, and plant a virus in their drone command server. You will be alone. If you are captured, we will deny your existence."

It's a suicide mission. But it's also the only path back into the Empire's good graces.
