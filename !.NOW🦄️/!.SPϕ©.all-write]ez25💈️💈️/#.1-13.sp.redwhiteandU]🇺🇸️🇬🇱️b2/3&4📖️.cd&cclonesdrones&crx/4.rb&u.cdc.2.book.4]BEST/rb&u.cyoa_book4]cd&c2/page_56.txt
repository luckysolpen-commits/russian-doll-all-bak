Mars is chaos. Mars is freedom.

You point your stolen, battered shuttle towards the Red Planet. The journey is tense. You are flying a ship that is broadcasting a stolen identity, and every Imperial patrol is a potential threat. You use your knowledge of military protocols to bluff your way through two checkpoints, but it's a nerve-wracking experience.

You make planetfall in the "Farland" region, a sparsely populated area known for its independent settlements and criminal activity. You ditch the shuttle in a deep canyon and set out on foot. You are a fugitive on the biggest, wildest planet in the system.

You have no allies, no resources, and no name. You are just another ghost in the Martian machine. Your first priority is to survive. You need food, water, and a place to lie low. After that, you can think about your next move. Do you try to find the rebellion? Or do you simply disappear?
