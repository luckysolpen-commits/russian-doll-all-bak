You walk into the bar. It's the center of the town's social life. The woman you saw earlier is behind the counter, cleaning a glass with a rag. She looks up as you approach, her eyes sharp and intelligent.

"What do you want?" she asks, her voice wary.

You place your Imperial-issue sidearm on the counter. "I'm looking for food and a place to stay," you say. "This is all I have to offer."

She inspects the pistol, her movements precise. "A fine piece of hardware," she says. "Worth a few weeks' room and board. But it also tells me you're a recent deserter. Which means you might be bringing trouble."

She leans forward. "I'm an excellent judge of character, clone. I've survived this long by being one. So you tell me, are you worth the trouble you're undoubtedly going to cause?"

This is a negotiation, but not for the pistol. It's for your life.
