Curiosity is a dangerous impulse, but the clown's words were too compelling to ignore. You have to know.

You step out of your Digger and enter the ravaged Redplanet Labs outpost. The air is thick with smoke and the acrid smell of burnt electronics. Emergency lights cast long, dancing shadows. Bodies of scientists in white lab coats are strewn about, victims of the firefight.

You make your way to the deeper labs, following the signs of the clowns' raid. They were clearly looking for something specific. You find what you assume is the main research lab. And you see it.

In the center of the room is a large containment field, now shattered. A viscous, pulsating fungal growth covers the floor, and in the center of it, you see bodies. Clone bodies. But they are twisted, their limbs elongated, their faces frozen in silent screams. Wires run from their heads to a central computer console.

The console is still active. On the screen is a project title: "Project Chimera." You access the logs. It's exactly what qynx said. A program to create a telepathic fungus that would enforce loyalty, turning clones into little more than puppets. The most recent logs speak of a breakthrough, and a problem. The fungus was... evolving. It was developing a consciousness of its own, and it was hostile. The scientists were losing control.

You've found the truth. And the truth is horrifying. As you turn to leave, you hear a sound from a dark corner of the lab. A wet, skittering sound.
