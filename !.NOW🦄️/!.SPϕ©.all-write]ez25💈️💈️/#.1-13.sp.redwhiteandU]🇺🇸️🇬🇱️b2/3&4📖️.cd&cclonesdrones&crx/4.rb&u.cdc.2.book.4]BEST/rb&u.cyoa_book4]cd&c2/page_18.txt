The beast within you is not a silent partner. It is a screaming, hungry passenger in your mind. During the trip back to Mars, you are plagued by nightmares of blood and bone. You meditate, you follow the discipline exercises The Proctor taught you, but the primal hunger of the Carcazod is a constant, gnawing presence. Can you truly control it? Or will it control you?

You take on your new identity as a security officer, but the fear gnaws at you. You watch your target, Cassian, and you see not just a traitor, but a man. He laughs with his colleagues. He drinks his coffee too fast. He has a picture of a family on his desk. The beast in your head simply whispers, "Flesh. Bone. Meat."

The night of the mission arrives. You are in position. Your handler gives the signal to transform and strike. But you hesitate. The faces of the scientists in the Lunar lab, their expressions a mix of awe and terror, flash in your mind. You think of the monster on the display. This is your one chance to turn back. You can abort the mission, contact your handler, and claim a systems malfunction. It would mean the end of your career, and likely your life. Or you can push the fear down and become the monster they made you.
