"I was on patrol," you say, keeping your voice low. "My ship was ambushed and destroyed by Imperial forces. They thought I was a clown. I guess I am now."

The big clone with the painted mustache looks you up and down. He grunts. "Imperial forces ambushing their own? Sounds about right. The whole system's eating itself. The name's Gus."

He takes a swig of whatever is in his flask and offers it to you. "You're welcome in Scraptown, brother. As long as you pull your weight. We're all deserters and runaways here. All we've got is each other."

You have found a home, of sorts. A place where you are not judged for your past. You spend the next few weeks working as a mechanic, repairing the town's aging equipment. You learn the stories of the other clowns, their reasons for leaving the Empire. You start to feel a sense of belonging, of camaraderie.

But Scraptown is not safe. An Imperial patrol is bound to find it eventually. And you are a trained soldier in a town of mechanics and farmers. You could help them prepare, help them fight. Or you could just enjoy the peace while it lasts.
