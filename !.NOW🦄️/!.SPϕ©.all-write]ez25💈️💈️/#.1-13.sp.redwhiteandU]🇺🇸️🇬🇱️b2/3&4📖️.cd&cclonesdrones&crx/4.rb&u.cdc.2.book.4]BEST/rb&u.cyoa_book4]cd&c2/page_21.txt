There is no victory in survival. Only duty. Only sacrifice.

You close your eyes and reroute the Digger's remaining power to the core containment system, then command it to fail. A high-pitched whine begins to fill the cockpit, a sound that drills into your skull. You have thirty seconds.

You spend them thinking of the Red Queen, of the immaculate halls of the training barracks, of the perfect, unwavering loyalty you were designed for. This is the ultimate expression of that loyalty. A clean slate. No witnesses, no survivors, no questions. Just a crater in a forgotten tunnel.

Your last thought is one of pride. You have not failed.

The world outside your cockpit vanishes in a flash of pure, white light.

THE END
