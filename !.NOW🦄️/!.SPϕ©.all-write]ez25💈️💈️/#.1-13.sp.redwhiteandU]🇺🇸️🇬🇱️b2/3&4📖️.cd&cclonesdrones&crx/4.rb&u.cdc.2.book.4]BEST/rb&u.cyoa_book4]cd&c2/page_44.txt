Hiding is for cowards.

You slam the "Ignition" button. The shuttle's engines roar to life, shaking the small craft violently. "Unauthorized launch!" a voice screams over the hangar comms. "Security team, disable that vehicle!"

You don't have time for a pre-flight check. You see the security team raising their plasma rifles. You firewall the throttle. The shuttle rockets forward, directly towards the main hangar doors. They're not open.

"Warning: Impact imminent," the shuttle's computer chirps calmly.

You brace yourself. The shuttle hits the massive doors with the force of a small meteor. Metal screams and tears. The shuttle's nose cone crumples, and the cockpit is a shower of sparks and broken glass. But you're through.

You've made it into the main launch tube, but your ship is critically damaged. Alarms are blaring. The outer doors are still closed. You are a sitting duck. A moment later, a magnetic clamp latches onto your ship, holding it in place. Your escape attempt has failed.

You are dragged from the wreckage and brought before The Proctor. He looks at you not with anger, but with a kind of weary disappointment.

"You have become a liability, 700," he says softly. "A loose end. And we are very, very good at tying up loose ends."

He nods to the guards. They raise their weapons. You have failed.

THE END
