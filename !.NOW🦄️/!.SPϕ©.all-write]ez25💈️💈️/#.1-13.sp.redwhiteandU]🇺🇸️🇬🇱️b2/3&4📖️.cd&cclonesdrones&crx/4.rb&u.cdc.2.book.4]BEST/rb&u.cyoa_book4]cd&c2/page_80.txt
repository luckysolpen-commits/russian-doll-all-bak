You turn your back on civilization, on the war, on the endless cycle of violence and betrayal. You walk out into the vast, empty desert of Mars, and you do not look back.

You become a creature of the wilderness. You learn to find water in the deep, hidden caves. You learn to hunt the strange, resilient creatures that live beneath the red sands. You learn to read the weather in the patterns of the dust storms.

You are no longer a clone. You are a hermit, a mystic, a ghost. You have shed your old identity like a skin. You have found a kind of peace, a solitude that is both liberating and terrifying.

Years pass. You lose track of time. The war on Mars, the conflict between the Empire and the clowns, it all fades into a distant memory, a story you once heard.

One day, you find a strange object half-buried in the sand. It is a drone, ancient and of a design you do not recognize. As you approach, it whirs to life. A holographic message is projected into the air. It is a distress signal, a plea for help from a ship that crashed on this planet centuries ago. The message contains coordinates, a map to a place of great power, a place that could change the destiny of Mars forever.
