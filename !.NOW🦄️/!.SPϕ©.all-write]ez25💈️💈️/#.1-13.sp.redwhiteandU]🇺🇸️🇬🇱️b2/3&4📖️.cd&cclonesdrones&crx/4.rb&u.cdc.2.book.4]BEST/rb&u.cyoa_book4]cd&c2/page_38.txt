You are a clone of the Empire. Your duty is to report the truth.

In the debriefing room aboard the rescue vessel, you tell your commanding officer everything. You tell him about the dying clown's last words, about the Carcazod project, about Project Chimera, about the Underground Xircus. You hand over the data chip you found, if you have it. You lay it all bare.

The commander listens in silence, his face an unreadable mask. When you are finished, he simply says, "Thank you, 700. Your honesty is noted."

You are taken to a private quarter to await your next assignment. You have no idea if you've just signed your own death warrant or if you've stumbled into a promotion. You are a pawn in a game you don't understand, and you have just given a king a full report on the enemy's position.

A day later, a high-ranking official from the Blood Queen Monarchy arrives. She enters your quarters alone. "You have caused quite a stir, Clone 700," she says, her voice like silk and steel. "You have confirmed our worst fears about certain... unauthorized research projects. You have also proven yourself to be a clone of unusual integrity."

She smiles. "We have a use for a clone like you. A special project. One that requires a clone who understands the enemy, and who is not afraid of the truth."
