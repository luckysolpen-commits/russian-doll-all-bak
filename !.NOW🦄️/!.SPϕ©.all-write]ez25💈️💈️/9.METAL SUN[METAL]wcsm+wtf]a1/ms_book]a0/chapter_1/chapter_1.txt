Chapter 1: Lucky Returns, Sol's Obsession

Lucky stepped out of the Metal Sun's gates, a deep sigh escaping his lips as the warm, artificial sunlight washed over him. The familiar hum of machinery and the distant chatter of the inhabitants were a comforting symphony after his time away. It was good to be back on familiar ground, a place where the dangers and uncertainties of the outside world seemed to melt away. Here, within the colossal, orbiting anomaly that was the Metal Sun, he was simply Lucky, a citizen among many.

He made his way to his quarters, a sense of relief settling over him. The Metal Sun, a celestial marvel, pulsed with energy as it orbited the solar system. It wasn't a sun in the traditional sense; its exterior was a deceptive facade, a maze of interconnected cityscapes. But the true life, the vibrant society, thrived *inside*. Towering skyscrapers and levitating habitats coalesced into a futuristic society, mostly hidden from the searing external proximity to the actual sun.

Inside, the air was crisp and clean, filled with the gentle hum of zero-point energy and the soft glow of luminescent plants. Citizens, many augmented with cybernetic limbs and organs, moved through a tapestry of interconnected habitats: floating gardens, crystal forests, and iridescent oceans. The extreme quantum fusion that powered their world kept the interior at a chilling -200°C, a stark contrast to the inferno outside.

Slipping into his apartment, Lucky shed his outerwear. Astra, his partner, greeted him with a warm smile from the kitchenette, her eyes twinkling.

"Hey, handsome. Welcome back," she winked, her voice a soothing balm.

Lucky chuckled, ambled over to her, and wrapped his arms around her waist, pulling her close. "Thanks, I needed this break," he murmured, leaning into her embrace. Astra leaned into him, her eyes sparkling with affection.

"I'm glad you're home safe," she said, her tone shifting slightly. "But you know Gem's been acting strange lately..."

Lucky's brow furrowed in concern. "What do you mean?"

"She's just... different. She won't talk about it, but I can sense something's bothering her." Astra's words resonated with a growing unease in Lucky's gut. He made a mental note to keep an eye on Gem, to try and figure out what was wrong.

Just then, his comms device beeped. Lucky answered, and a holographic projection of Gem shimmered into existence before them. Her expression was serious, a mixture of fear and determination.

"Lucky, I need to talk to you. It's about Sol..."

Lucky's eyes narrowed as he listened to Gem's words, a cold dread beginning to coil in his stomach. Astra stood beside him, her expression mirroring his concern.

"What's going on? You look... serious," Lucky pressed, his voice tight.

Gem's holographic form seemed to waver slightly, her gaze darting nervously around before focusing on Lucky again. "Sol has created a new line of clone sons. And one of them... it's mine."

Astra's eyes widened in surprise, a gasp escaping her lips. Lucky's face turned to stone. "What do you mean? Which one is it?"

"Riley," Gem whispered, her voice barely audible even through the projection. "He's grown faster than any of Sol's previous sons. And I think... I think he might be the key to stopping Sol's madness."

Lucky processed Gem's words, the horrifying implications sinking in. Astra placed a hand on his arm, her touch grounding him.

"Gem, we can't just rush in. This is Sol we're talking about. He's the master of manipulation," Astra warned, her voice laced with caution.

Lucky nodded in agreement. "She's right. We need to gather more information before we move. And that means getting our hands on some classified intel."

Gem nodded, a calculating glint in her holographic eye. "I've got just the thing. My contact in the Shadow Net can get us the data we need."

The conversation ended, leaving Lucky and Astra in the quiet of their quarters, the weight of Gem's revelation hanging heavy in the air. Sol's obsession with his clone sons had always been disturbing, but this new development, the existence of Riley, a clone son of Gem, changed everything. The game had just become infinitely more dangerous.
