Edge's laughter filled the comm unit, a cruel, mocking sound that grated on Lucky's frayed nerves.
It was the sound of triumph, of a man who had chosen the winning side.
"I'm thriving down here, Lucky. The Metal Mafia is stronger than ever."

Lucky could almost see him, leaning back in some opulent underground bunker,
surrounded by his cronies, a smug look on his face.
Edge had always been a survivor, a man who knew how to adapt.
But this was a new low, even for him.
He had embraced the very tyranny they had once fought against.

"You're a traitor, Edge," Lucky spat into the comm unit, his voice raw with contempt.
"You sold out humanity for a few scraps from the Architect's table."

"Scraps?" Edge scoffed, a hint of genuine amusement in his voice.
"I'm running the show down here, Lucky. The Architect needs me.
He needs someone to manage the... less savory elements of his new world.
And I'm happy to oblige. For a price, of course."

Lucky heard the distant, rhythmic hum of World Police Bots,
their presence a constant, oppressive threat.
They were closer now, their metallic footsteps echoing in the tunnels.
Edge was using them, using the Architect's own enforcers, to drive Lucky deeper.

"You're just a glorified prison guard, Edge," Lucky sneered.
"A dog on a leash."

"A very well-fed dog, Lucky," Edge retorted, his voice hardening.
"And you? You're just digging your own grave."

He heard a click, and the comm unit went dead.
Edge had cut him off, leaving him alone in the oppressive silence of the tunnels.
Lucky slammed his fist against the cold rock wall, a wave of impotent fury washing over him.

He closed his eyes, trying to control his breathing.
He could feel the vibrations of the bots' footsteps, growing closer.
They were hunting him, relentlessly, inexorably.
Edge was enjoying this, the slow, agonizing chase.

He thought of Astra, of her fierce spirit, her unwavering belief in freedom.
He wondered where she was, if she was safe.
He hoped she had escaped the Icarus, that she hadn't fallen into the Architect's hands.
He hoped she was still fighting.

He pushed himself to his feet, his body aching, his muscles screaming in protest.
He was tired. So tired.
But he couldn't give up. Not now.
Not when Edge was watching, enjoying his misery.

He looked at the faint, almost imperceptible throb of his modified Mark on his arm.
It was a constant reminder of the Architect's control, of the world that had been stolen from them.
But it was also a reminder of his own defiance, his own refusal to yield.

He would not let Edge win. He would not let the Architect win.
He would find a way to fight back. He would find a way to survive.
Even if it meant going deeper, into the very heart of the earth.

He began to move, his footsteps echoing in the darkness.
He was driven by a burning desire for retribution, a cold, hard anger that fueled his every step.
Revenge. That's all that mattered now.
It was the only thing that kept him moving, one weary step after another,
into the unknown depths.
