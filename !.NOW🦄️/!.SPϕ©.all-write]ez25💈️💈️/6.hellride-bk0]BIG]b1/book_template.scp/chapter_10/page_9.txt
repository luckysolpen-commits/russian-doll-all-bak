Just as unconsciousness threatened to consume him entirely, a faint hum grew louder, a sound of salvation.
It was not the metallic thrum of the bots, but a different kind of hum, a more organic, almost musical vibration.
A shaft of brilliant light pierced the gloom, cutting through the dust-filled air, illuminating the wreckage.

Lucky, barely conscious, felt a strange sensation, a pulling, a fading.
His vision blurred, the light from above becoming a hazy halo.
He heard the distant wail of sirens, the metallic clang of bots, growing closer.

Then, a figure descended, her sleek form silhouetted against the light, an almost angelic figure in the chaos.
She landed gracefully amidst the rubble, her gaze sharp, assessing the wreckage and Lucky's dire state with a cool, professional detachment.
Her armor, a dark, iridescent material, seemed to absorb the light, making her appear as if she were made of shadows.
Her helmet, with its single, glowing blue visor, gave her an otherworldly appearance.

"Well, well, Lucky," her voice, a low, melodic hum, cut through the silence.
It was a voice he knew, a voice he had heard only once before, in a fleeting, enigmatic encounter on the surface.
The Blue Bird Bounty Hunter.

Kael, Rhys, and Silas, battered and bruised, stared at her, their faces a mixture of awe and apprehension.
They had heard the legends, the whispers of the Blue Bird, a phantom of the underworld, a mercenary with a code.

Lucky, barely able to speak, held up the mutagen container, his hand trembling with effort.
It was cracked, its iridescent contents seeping into the ground, but it was still there.
"Mutagen... for rescue... Avanta.C..." His voice was hoarse, weak, barely a whisper.

The Blue Bird's eyes narrowed, a calculating glint in their depths.
She looked at the mutagen, then back at Lucky, her mind rapidly assessing the value of what he held,
the desperate plea in his eyes.
"Avanta.C, huh? You're desperate. And you're offering me a fortune."

She extended a hand, not to help him, but to take the mutagen.
Lucky, with his last ounce of strength, pushed the container towards her.
She snatched it, her grip firm, her movements swift and decisive.

"Alright, Lucky. You've got a deal. Anything to get off this rock, right? It's a wasteland of robot wars now."
As she spoke, she activated a small device on her wrist.
A faint, almost imperceptible hum filled the air, and Lucky felt a strange sensation, a pulling, a fading.

His vision blurred, the light from above becoming a hazy halo.
He collapsed, unconscious, as the Blue Bird Bounty Hunter, mutagen in hand, prepared to extract her prize and him from the collapsing nest,
her mission accomplished.

Kael, Rhys, and Silas watched in stunned silence as the Blue Bird, with an almost effortless grace,
scooped up Lucky's unconscious form.
She moved with a speed that defied her size, a blur of motion, disappearing into the shaft of light from which she had come.

"What just happened?" Kael whispered, her voice filled with a mixture of awe and confusion.

"We just witnessed a legend," Rhys said, his voice filled with a quiet reverence.
"The Blue Bird. She always gets her prize."

Silas, meanwhile, was already trying to make sense of the energy signature she had left behind.
"Incredible," he muttered, his fingers flying across his salvaged datapad.
"Her technology... it's beyond anything I've ever seen."

They were battered, bruised, but they were alive.
And Lucky was gone. Taken by the Blue Bird.
They had sent the message. They had struck a blow against the Architect.
But the war was far from over.
And now, their leader was in the hands of a mercenary.
