The battle was a maelstrom of light and fury.
Lasers crisscrossed the void, painting deadly patterns.
Explosions bloomed like malevolent flowers, silent and beautiful in the vacuum.
The *Valiant* shuddered under the impact of incoming fire,
its shields groaning under the strain.

"Shields at sixty percent!" a tactical officer shouted.
"We're taking heavy fire from the lead escort!"

"Concentrate fire on that cruiser!" Rostova commanded, her voice a steel whip.
"Bring it down!"

Lucky watched the chaos unfold, his mind racing.
They were holding their own, but just barely.
The Architect's forces were well-trained, well-equipped.
And they had the advantage of surprise, even if it was a surprise of their own making.

He saw a UN fighter explode in a flash of light, a tiny, insignificant spark extinguished.
A pang of grief, sharp and sudden, pierced through him.
Those were the young pilots, the ones he had sent into the maw of hell.

"Fighter squadron reports heavy resistance!" a comms officer yelled.
"They're being swarmed by World Police Bots!"

Lucky cursed under his breath.
He had underestimated the Architect's commitment to these bots.
He had thought they would be lightly defended, a mere cargo.
But they were a priority, a symbol of his new order.

"Edge, can your... assets... provide any distraction?" Lucky asked, turning to the gangster.

Edge's cybernetic eye glowed faintly.
"Already on it," he said, a grim smile on his face.
"My boys are a little rough around the edges, but they know how to make a mess."

On the main viewscreen, a new set of contacts appeared,
streaking in from the flanks of the battle.
They were smaller, faster ships, moving with a reckless abandon that spoke of desperation.
They were the Metal Mafia, Edge's shadow fleet.

They plunged into the fray, not engaging the main UN fleet,
but targeting the Architect's escort ships with a savage ferocity.
They were like a pack of rabid dogs, snapping and biting at the heels of a larger beast.
They didn't care about tactics or strategy.
They cared about chaos.
And they were very good at creating it.

The Architect's escorts, caught between the disciplined fire of the UN fleet
and the unpredictable attacks of the Metal Mafia,
began to falter.
Their formations broke, their fire became less coordinated.

"They're breaking!" Rostova exclaimed, a rare note of triumph in her voice.
"Maintain pressure! Don't let them regroup!"

Lucky felt a surge of hope.
It was working.
Edge's gamble, his willingness to unleash the underworld,
was paying off.

But the transports were still there, still moving towards Earth.
And the World Police Bots were still pouring out of them,
a relentless tide of chrome and steel.

"What about the bots?" Lucky asked, his gaze fixed on the main screen.
"The fighters are still getting hammered."

"We're doing what we can," Rostova said, her voice tight.
"But our primary objective is the transports."

Lucky knew she was right.
But he couldn't shake the image of the young pilots, fighting a losing battle.
He had to do something.
He had to give them a chance.

"Admiral," Lucky said, his voice firm.
"I need a shuttle. And a small team."

Rostova turned to him, her eyes narrowed.
"What are you planning?"

"I'm going to pay the Architect a visit," Lucky said, a dangerous glint in his eyes.
"And I'm going to make sure those bots never reach Earth."
