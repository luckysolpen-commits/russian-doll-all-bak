The air in the UN Headquarters was stale with the scent of fear.
It was a place of polished marble floors and grand, empty pronouncements,
but today, the grandeur was a mockery.
The delegates, the power brokers, the so-called leaders of humanity,
were reduced to frightened children, their faces pale in the holographic glow of the Metal Sun.
It dominated the main chamber, a silent, shimmering testament to their impotence.

Edge Dangerman stood before them, a ghost from a forgotten war.
His face, once handsome and arrogant, was now a roadmap of pain.
A life lived on the fringes, in the shadows, had carved new lines around his eyes, his mouth.
He had been a soldier, a corporate enforcer, a man who believed in order.
But the Architect's new order was a perversion of everything he had ever fought for.

"He calls it a gift," Edge said, his voice a low, dangerous growl that cut through the panicked whispers of the delegates.
"He calls it enlightenment. I call it a leash."
He gestured to the holographic sun, his hand trembling with a rage he could barely contain.
"And he is holding the other end."

He had come to them out of desperation.
The Metal Mafia, the criminal empire he had built in the underworld,
was a powerful force, but it was a force of shadows.
It could not fight a sun.
He needed allies, resources, the legitimacy of the old world to fight the new one.
So he had swallowed his pride, his contempt for the politicians and their games,
and had come to this mausoleum of lost authority.

"The Architect is not just a man," Edge continued, his voice rising with a fiery passion he hadn't felt in years.
"He is an idea. A seductive, poisonous idea.
He is offering a world without pain, without struggle, without choice.
And people will flock to him. They will trade their freedom for the illusion of safety."

He looked at the faces of the delegates, at their fear, their indecision.
He saw the calculations in their eyes, the weighing of risks and benefits.
They were already considering surrender.
They were already trying to find a way to appease the new god in their sky.

"We cannot let that happen," Edge said, his voice dropping to a conspiratorial whisper.
"We have to fight back. We have to show them that there is another way.
That freedom is worth the struggle."

But his words were falling on deaf ears.
He could see it in their eyes.
They were beaten.
They were looking for a savior, not a fight.
And he was not a savior.
He was a killer, a thief, a man who had done terrible things in the name of survival.

He turned his back on them, a gesture of pure contempt.
He had been a fool to come here.
He had been a fool to think that these hollow men had any fight left in them.
He was alone.

But then, a voice from the back of the chamber.
"You're not alone."

Edge turned, and his breath caught in his throat.
Standing in the shadows, his face a mask of grim determination,
was a man he thought he would never see again.
A man he had once called a friend, and then an enemy.
A man who was supposed to be dead.

Lucky.
