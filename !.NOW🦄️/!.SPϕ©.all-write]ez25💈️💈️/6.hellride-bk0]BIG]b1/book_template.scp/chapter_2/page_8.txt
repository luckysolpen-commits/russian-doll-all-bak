The final countdown echoed through the war room, a disembodied voice ticking away the seconds.
"Ten... nine... eight..."
Lucky stood on the bridge of the *UNS Valiant*,
a heavy cruiser that smelled of recycled air and old ambition.
Admiral Rostova was at his side, her face a study in controlled tension.
Edge was a few paces away, his cybernetic eye fixed on the main viewscreen,
which showed the vast, inky blackness of space.

"Seven... six... five..."

Lucky felt a strange calm settle over him.
It was the calm of a man who had faced death countless times,
who knew that fear was a luxury he couldn't afford.
His hand instinctively went to the worn grip of his blaster,
a familiar weight against his hip.

"Four... three... two..."

On the viewscreen, tiny pinpricks of light began to appear,
blossoming from the distant Icarus.
They were the Architect's transport ships,
sleek, black, and utterly devoid of any identifying marks.
They moved with a chilling precision, a silent, inexorable tide.

"One... Mark!"

"All ships, engage!" Rostova's voice, sharp and clear, cut through the silence.

The *Valiant* shuddered as its engines roared to life,
propelling it forward into the void.
Around them, the UN fleet, a formidable array of cruisers, destroyers, and frigates,
sprang into action, a swarm of angry bees descending on the Architect's transports.

Laser fire erupted, painting streaks of crimson and emerald across the black canvas of space.
The transports, caught off guard, scattered,
their evasive maneuvers clumsy and uncoordinated.
They were not built for combat, but for delivery.
And they were delivering the instruments of humanity's enslavement.

"Fighter squadrons, deploy!" Rostova commanded.

From the *Valiant*'s hangar bays, a swarm of sleek, agile fighters launched,
streaking towards the transports like angry wasps.
Lucky watched them go, his heart in his throat.
Those were the young pilots he had briefed, the ones who had looked at him with such hope.
He prayed they would make it back.

"Incoming!" a sensor officer shouted.

On the viewscreen, a new wave of ships appeared,
larger, more heavily armed.
They were the Architect's escort fleet,
and they were moving with a terrifying speed.

"They're faster than anticipated!" Rostova muttered, her jaw tight.

"He's not as arrogant as we thought," Edge said, a grim note in his voice.
"He sent a proper escort."

Lucky felt a cold knot of dread in his stomach.
This wasn't going to be easy.
This wasn't going to be a surgical strike.
This was going to be a bloodbath.

"All weapons, target the escorts!" Rostova barked.
"Protect the fighters!"

The *Valiant* unleashed a volley of plasma torpedoes,
streaking towards the enemy ships.
Explosions blossomed in the void, silent and beautiful.
But for every enemy ship that fell, another seemed to take its place.

The battle had begun.
And the fate of Earth hung in the balance.
