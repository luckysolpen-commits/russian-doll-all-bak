Lucky watched, mesmerized, as the ship expertly navigated a treacherous asteroid field,
dodging massive chunks of rock with practiced ease.
The Blue Bird's movements were fluid, precise, a testament to her skill.
She was a master pilot, her hands dancing across the controls with an almost intuitive grace.

"They're always watching. The Empire. Looking for illegals. We'll have to be careful. Blend in."
Her voice was calm, even, devoid of any emotion, but Lucky could feel the underlying tension.
They were entering hostile territory, a place where a single mistake could mean capture, or worse.

He thought of the Architect, sitting on his throne, confident in his victory.
He thought of the Metal Sun, its cold, unblinking eye watching over a world of slaves.
He thought of Astra, of her fierce spirit, her unwavering belief in freedom.
He wondered if she had escaped the Icarus, if she was still fighting.

He felt a pang of longing, a deep ache for connection, for companionship.
He was alone, utterly alone, in this vast, silent void.
But he was not defeated.
Not yet.

He looked at the faint, almost imperceptible throb of his modified Mark on his arm.
It was a constant reminder of the Architect's control, of the world that had been stolen from them.
But it was also a reminder of his own defiance, his own refusal to yield.

He was free. And he would use that freedom to fight.
To fight for a world where no one would ever have to wear the Mark again.
He had found his allies. He had found his purpose.
And the war for humanity's soul was far from over.

He turned to the Blue Bird, his voice hoarse.
"Why are you helping me?"

The Blue Bird didn't turn, her gaze fixed on the approaching Ikarus.
"You had something I wanted," she said, her voice flat.
"And you offered a fair price."

"Avanta.C," Lucky said, the name a whisper.

"A virtual world," the Blue Bird confirmed.
"A place where the rules are different. Where anything is possible."

"Why do you want to go there?" Lucky asked.

"Information," the Blue Bird replied, her voice devoid of emotion.
"Knowledge is power, Lucky. And in this new world, power is everything."

Lucky felt a chill run down his spine.
He had heard whispers of Avanta.C, a fabled virtual world, a place where people could escape the harsh realities of their lives.
But he had always dismissed it as a myth, a fantasy.
Now, it was his destination.

He wondered what kind of information the Blue Bird was seeking.
What kind of power she hoped to gain.
He knew that mercenaries operated on their own code, their own morality.
He couldn't trust her, not completely.
But he had no choice.
She was his only way to Ikarus, his only way to Avanta.C.

He looked at the approaching space station, its lights twinkling like a thousand scattered diamonds.
It was a beacon of hope, but also a place of danger.
He was entering a new world, a world where the lines between reality and illusion were blurred.
And he was about to find out what kind of secrets it held.
