Chapter 12: The Blue Bird Bounty Hunter's Rescue

Dust and debris rained down, a suffocating curtain as the cavern ceiling groaned, then collapsed with a deafening roar. Lucky, the precious mutagen container clutched tightly in his hand, was caught in the cascading torrent of rock and earth. He cried out, a raw, desperate sound, as the immense weight buried him, pressing the air from his lungs.

Silence. A heavy, oppressive silence followed the thunderous collapse. Then, a faint, desperate scraping sound. Lucky, barely conscious, struggled to free himself, every muscle screaming in protest. His body, already pushed beyond its limits, was weak and trembling. He was trapped, going weaker by the second, the darkness threatening to consume him.

Through the haze of pain and fading consciousness, he remembered the Blue Bird Bounty Hunter, the fleeting, enigmatic encounter on the surface. She was his only hope, a desperate gamble. With immense, agonizing effort, he found a small, exposed piece of metal from a collapsed pipe. He began to tap, a slow, agonizing rhythm, a desperate plea echoing in the subterranean gloom: S.O.S.

Minutes stretched into an eternity, each second a battle against the encroaching darkness. Just as unconsciousness threatened to consume him entirely, a faint hum grew louder, a sound of salvation. A shaft of brilliant light pierced the gloom, cutting through the dust-filled air, illuminating the wreckage.

The Blue Bird Bounty Hunter descended, her sleek form silhouetted against the light, an almost angelic figure in the chaos. She landed gracefully amidst the rubble, her gaze sharp, assessing the wreckage and Lucky's dire state with a cool, professional detachment.

"Well, well, Lucky," her voice, a low, melodic hum, cut through the silence. "Looks like you've found yourself in quite the pickle."

Lucky, barely able to speak, held up the mutagen container, his hand trembling with effort. "Mutagen... for rescue... Avanta.C..." His voice was hoarse, weak, barely a whisper.

The Blue Bird's eyes narrowed, a calculating glint in their depths. She looked at the mutagen, then back at Lucky, her mind rapidly assessing the value of what he held, the desperate plea in his eyes. "Avanta.C, huh? You're desperate. And you're offering me a fortune."

She extended a hand, not to help him, but to take the mutagen. Lucky, with his last ounce of strength, pushed the container towards her. She snatched it, her grip firm, her movements swift and decisive.

"Alright, Lucky. You've got a deal. Anything to get off this rock, right? It's a wasteland of robot wars now." As she spoke, she activated a small device on her wrist. A faint, almost imperceptible hum filled the air, and Lucky felt a strange sensation, a pulling, a fading.

His vision blurred, the light from above becoming a hazy halo. He collapsed, unconscious, as the Blue Bird Bounty Hunter, mutagen in hand, prepared to extract her prize and him from the collapsing nest, her mission accomplished.
