The sky above Earth, once a serene expanse of blue, was now a canvas of metallic storms.
From the distant Icarus, a relentless tide of World Police Bots descended,
their sleek, black forms blotting out the sun.
Each bot was a harbinger of a new order, a chilling testament to the Architect's absolute power.

On the ground, chaos reigned.
Cities that had once hummed with life were now battlegrounds,
the air thick with the acrid smell of ozone and burning metal.
Explosions rocked the earth, each concussive blast a testament to the bots' destructive efficiency.
Buildings crumbled, their proud facades reduced to rubble,
monuments to a world that was rapidly ceasing to exist.

Lucky, his face grim and streaked with grime, moved through the urban wreckage.
He was a ghost in the storm, his movements fluid and precise,
a lifetime of dodging authority honed into an art form.
He had seen wars before, but nothing like this.
This wasn't a conflict between armies; it was an extermination.

He saw a family, huddled in the ruins of what was once a park,
their faces etched with terror.
A World Police Bot, its single red eye glowing with an eerie luminescence,
approached them, its metallic limbs moving with a chilling, deliberate grace.
Lucky raised his blaster, but he knew it was futile.
One man, one blaster, against an army of machines.

He watched in horror as the bot extended a metallic arm,
its digits unfolding to reveal a glowing symbol.
It pressed the symbol against the wrist of the father,
a searing brand that seared itself onto his skin.
The Mark of the Beast.
A glowing, pulsating symbol that seemed to drain the life, the defiance, from the man's eyes.
He became compliant, his shoulders slumping, his gaze vacant.

Lucky felt a cold rage build within him.
This wasn't just conquest; it was soul-crushing.
It was the ultimate act of dehumanization.

He saw others, their faces pale with fear, willingly accepting the Mark.
They were trading their freedom for a false sense of security,
for the promise of not being crushed beneath the metallic treads of the bots.
He couldn't blame them. Survival was a powerful instinct.
But it was a survival that came at a terrible cost.

From the distant heavens, the ominous silhouettes of Mars and Lunar Colony ships provided a chilling backdrop.
They hung in the sky like silent, predatory birds,
their presence a stark reminder of the Architect's widespread support.
They were complicit in this, these colonies that had once been beacons of human expansion.
They had chosen the side of tyranny.

Lucky felt a profound sense of despair.
He had fought, he had planned, he had risked everything.
But it wasn't enough.
The tide was too strong.
The Architect's vision was too powerful.

He saw a group of bots herding a crowd of gaunt, defeated humans into a gaping maw in the earth.
Underground.
The new prisons.
The new world.

He knew his time above ground was running out.
He had to make a choice.
Fight a losing battle, or go underground and live to fight another day.
He looked at the sky, at the endless stream of bots descending.
He looked at the faces of the marked, their eyes empty.
He made his choice.
