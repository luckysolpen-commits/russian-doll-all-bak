celebration. Madame Dupont, in her infinite wisdom and generosity,
declared a holiday. She threw a party, a grand,

been a house of mystery and suspense, was now

had been so consumed by their own personal dramas,
now came together as a community, a family, a
tribe. They celebrated their victory, their cleverness, their
shared adventure. They were no longer just a group
of strangers; they were a team of detectives, a
band of brothers and sisters, a fellowship of the
ring.

the chef, outdid himself, preparing a banquet that was
a symphony of flavors, a masterpiece of culinary art.

victories of the human intellect over the forces of

he had written for the occasion, a poem that

of the Chateau Tivoli.

Dupont, the woman who had been the catalyst for

a quiet grace, her heart filled with a deep
and abiding sense of gratitude. She looked at her

joyous and raucous affair that was a testament
