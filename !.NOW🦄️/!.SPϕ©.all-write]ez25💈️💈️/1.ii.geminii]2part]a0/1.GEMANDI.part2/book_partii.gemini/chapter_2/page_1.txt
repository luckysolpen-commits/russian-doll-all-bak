The acrid scent of decay hung heavy in the air, a constant
companion since the world had fractured. Dr. Lucky, ever
the pragmatist, adjusted the worn bandana over his nose
and mouth. His eyes, sharp and observant, scanned the
dilapidated facade of what was once a bustling supermarket.
"Alright, team," he murmured, his voice a low rumble,
"This is it. Our next meal ticket, hopefully."

Astra, her usually pristine lab coat now stained with
grime and patched with salvaged fabric, nodded grimly.
Her gaze, usually fixed on complex equations, now meticulously
assessed the structural integrity of the building. "The
front entrance looks compromised," she stated, pointing
to a gaping hole where automatic doors once stood. "Possible
collapse risk, and a clear invitation for anything
unpleasant to wander in."

Gem, ever the vibrant splash of color in their muted
apocalyptic palette, twirled a salvaged paint brush
between her fingers. Her bright, inquisitive eyes darted
around, not just for danger, but for inspiration. "Think
of the artistic potential," she mused, a faint smile
touching her lips. "A still life of canned goods, a
sculpture of forgotten dreams."

Lucky sighed, a puff of dust escaping his bandana. "Less
art, more sustenance, Gem. We're running on fumes here."
He gestured towards the side of the building. "Let's try
the loading dock. It might be less obvious, less...
Carcazoid-friendly."

They moved with practiced caution, their footsteps
muffled by the debris-strewn pavement. The silence of
the abandoned city was punctuated only by the distant,
unsettling shriek of a Carcazoid, a sound that had become
as common as birdsong once was. Lucky led the way, his
makeshift crowbar held ready. Astra followed, a modified
stun gun clutched in her hand, its hum a faint reassurance.
Gem, surprisingly agile despite her artistic inclinations,
brought up the rear, her eyes constantly sweeping their
surroundings.

The loading dock was indeed less conspicuous, though
the heavy metal door was rusted shut. "Stand back," Lucky
grunted, bracing himself. With a powerful heave, he
levered the crowbar into the gap, straining against the
corroded metal. The screech of protesting hinges echoed
through the desolate street, a sound that made their
skin crawl.

Finally, with a groan of tortured metal, the door gave
way, revealing a cavernous, dimly lit interior. The air
inside was stale, thick with the scent of dust and decay,
but also, faintly, the ghost of forgotten produce. "Jackpot,"
Gem whispered, her voice filled with a mixture of awe
and trepidation.

The supermarket was a skeletal remains of its former
self. Shelves were overturned, aisles were choked with
debris, and the fluorescent lights hung like broken bones
from the ceiling. Yet, amidst the chaos, there were still
treasures to be found. Canned goods, though coated in
dust, lined some shelves. Bottled water, surprisingly
intact, gleamed in the gloom.

"Split up," Lucky instructed, his voice low. "Astra,
you take the medical supplies. Gem, look for anything
non-perishable, and keep an eye out for anything...
unusual. I'll focus on water and anything else we can
carry."

They fanned out, their movements swift and efficient.
Astra, with her scientific precision, quickly located
the pharmacy section, her eyes scanning for antibiotics,
bandages, and pain relievers. Gem, drawn by a glint of
color, found a forgotten display of children's art supplies,
a small, bittersweet smile touching her lips as she
tucked a box of crayons into her pack.

Lucky, meanwhile, navigated the treacherous aisles,
his senses heightened. He found a pallet of bottled water,
miraculously untouched, and began to fill their empty
containers. The silence of the store was unnerving,
broken only by the rustle of their movements and the
occasional creak of the building settling.

Suddenly, a low growl echoed from the back of the store.
Lucky froze, his hand instinctively going to his crowbar.
Astra looked up, her stun gun raised. Gem's eyes widened,
her artistic curiosity replaced by a primal fear. The
growl intensified, closer now, accompanied by the shuffling
sound of heavy footsteps.

"Carcazoid," Lucky breathed, his voice barely a whisper.
"Multiple."

The hunt was on. They had stumbled into a nest, and the
scavengers had become the prey. The faint hope of a
peaceful resupply vanished, replaced by the cold, hard
reality of their existence. Survival, once again, was
their only objective. They braced themselves, ready to
fight for every last can of food, every drop of water,
every breath they took in this desolate, unforgiving world.
The supermarket, once a symbol of abundance, had become
a battleground. They were ready. They had to be.
