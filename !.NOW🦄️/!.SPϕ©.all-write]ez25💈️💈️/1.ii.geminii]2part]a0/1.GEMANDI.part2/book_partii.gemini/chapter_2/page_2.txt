The first Carcazoid emerged from the gloom, a grotesque
hybrid of human and insect, its chitinous exoskeleton
gleaming dully in the faint light. Its multifaceted eyes
swiveled, locking onto Lucky. A guttural shriek tore
through the silence, a sound that promised pain and
dismemberment. Behind it, two more lumbered into view,
their movements jerky and unnatural.

"Formation!" Lucky barked, his voice cutting through
the fear. He positioned himself in front, crowbar held
high, a grim determination etched on his face. Astra
flanked him, her stun gun humming louder now, a blue
glow emanating from its barrel. Gem, surprisingly, didn't
hesitate. She grabbed a discarded shopping cart, its
wheels squealing in protest, and positioned it as a makeshift
barrier.

The lead Carcazoid lunged, its razor-sharp claws extended.
Lucky met it head-on, the crowbar connecting with a sickening
crunch against its armored plating. The creature recoiled,
a high-pitched squeal escaping its mandibles. Astra seized
the opportunity, firing her stun gun. A bolt of crackling
energy slammed into the Carcazoid, sending spasms through
its body. It thrashed wildly, its movements slowing,
before collapsing with a wet thud.

"One down!" Gem yelled, a surge of adrenaline coursing
through her. But her triumph was short-lived. The other
two Carcazoids, enraged by the fall of their comrade,
charged. One veered towards Astra, its focus solely on
the source of the painful energy. The other, larger and
more aggressive, bore down on Lucky.

Lucky parried a swipe from the creature's claw, the impact
jarring his arm. He ducked under another attack, the wind
of its passage ruffling his hair. He needed an opening,
a weakness. His eyes darted around, searching for anything
that could give him an advantage in this confined space.

Astra, meanwhile, was locked in a desperate struggle.
The Carcazoid was relentless, its attacks a blur of claws
and snapping mandibles. She fired her stun gun repeatedly,
but the creature seemed to be adapting, its movements
becoming more erratic, harder to predict. She knew the
charge wouldn't last forever. She needed to incapacitate
it, and fast.

Gem, seeing Astra in peril, acted on instinct. With a
scream of defiance, she shoved the shopping cart forward,
sending it careening into the Carcazoid attacking Astra.
The creature stumbled, momentarily disoriented. Astra
seized the chance, delivering a powerful, close-range
blast that sent the Carcazoid reeling, its limbs twitching
uncontrollably before it finally went still.

"Nice one, Gem!" Astra gasped, catching her breath.

Lucky, emboldened by their success, pressed his attack.
The remaining Carcazoid was formidable, but it was also
predictable. He feigned a retreat, drawing the creature
forward, then spun, bringing the crowbar down with all
his might onto its exposed neck joint. The Carcazoid
let out a final, piercing shriek, its body convulsing
before it crashed to the floor, motionless.

The silence that followed was deafening, broken only
by their ragged breathing. They stood amidst the fallen
monsters, their bodies aching, their hearts pounding.
They had survived. Again. But the encounter had drained
them, both physically and emotionally. The supermarket,
once a beacon of hope, now felt like a tomb.

"We need to move," Lucky said, his voice hoarse. "Now."

They quickly gathered what they could, their earlier
meticulousness replaced by a desperate urgency. Canned
goods were tossed haphazardly into their packs. Water
bottles were grabbed without a second thought. The medical
supplies Astra had found were crammed into a separate
bag. They didn't linger, didn't look back. The scent of
Carcazoid blood was too strong, a grim reminder of the
danger that lurked in every shadow.

As they slipped out of the loading dock and back into
the desolate street, the sun was beginning to set, casting
long, eerie shadows across the ruined city. The distant
shrieks of Carcazoids seemed closer now, more numerous.
They knew they had to find a safe place before nightfall,
a place where they could rest, regroup, and plan their
next move. The road ahead was uncertain, filled with
unknown dangers, but they had each other. And in this
broken world, that was all that mattered.
