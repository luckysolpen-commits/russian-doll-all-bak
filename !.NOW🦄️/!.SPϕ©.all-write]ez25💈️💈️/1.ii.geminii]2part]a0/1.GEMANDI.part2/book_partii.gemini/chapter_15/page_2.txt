The Architect scoffed, a hollow, defeated sound that lacked
his previous arrogance. "My personal vessel?" he sneered,
his eyes, though dulled by defeat, still holding a flicker
of disdain. "It's not a taxi, child. And even if you could
pilot it, the journey is perilous. The solar system is
not as empty as you think. There are... forces at play.
Entities that predate your petty squabbles. You would
be consumed." He gestured vaguely towards the vastness
of space, a chilling warning that seemed to hang in the
sterile air of the hangar. Lucky felt a prickle of unease.
He had faced monsters on Earth, but the thought of unknown
horrors in the void of space was a new, unsettling prospect.

"We've faced worse," Lucky retorted, his gaze unwavering,
a defiant challenge in his eyes. "Show us the way." His
voice was firm, leaving no room for argument, a silent
promise that they would not be deterred by his veiled
threats. He knew the Architect was trying to intimidate
them, to cling to some semblance of control, but Lucky
had seen too much, fought too hard, to be swayed by empty
words. He stepped closer to the sleek, futuristic spacecraft,
its polished hull reflecting his determined face, a silent
pledge to the unknown journey ahead. Astra, meanwhile,
was already pulling out her tablet, her fingers flying
across the screen, attempting to access the spacecraft's
systems, to find any schematics, any operational manuals.

Defeated, but still defiant, The Architect led them to
a hidden hangar, a vast, cavernous space beneath The Nexus.
The air here was colder, filled with the faint scent of
ozone and something metallic, something that spoke of advanced
technology. There, bathed in the soft, ethereal glow of
emergency lights, sat a spacecraft. It was sleek, futuristic,
unlike anything they had ever seen on Earth. Its hull
gleamed with an otherworldly sheen, a dark, polished surface
that seemed to absorb the light. Its engines, though silent,
exuded a sense of immense power, a dormant beast waiting
to be unleashed. Gem gasped, her eyes wide with a mixture
of awe and wonder. It was a work of art, a testament
to human ingenuity, even if it was built by a madman.

"The 'Ares I'," The Architect announced, a flicker of
pride in his eyes despite his defeat, a brief resurgence
of his former arrogance. "My personal transport. Designed
for interstellar travel. It's fully automated, but requires
specific biometric authorization for launch. And a complex
series of navigational codes, of course. Without them,
you'll be lost in the void." He watched them, a faint
smile playing on his lips, a silent challenge in his eyes.
He still believed he held the upper hand, that they were
merely pawns in his grand design. Lucky felt a surge of
frustration. The man was a genius, but his ego was insufferable.

"Which you will provide," Lucky stated, his voice leaving
no room for argument, a cold, hard edge to his tone. He
stepped closer to The Architect, his presence a silent
threat, a promise of pain if he resisted. The Architect
flushed, his eyes narrowing, but he knew he was beaten.
Under Lucky's watchful eye, The Architect reluctantly
initiated the launch sequence, his fingers hovering over
a holographic console, his movements slow and deliberate.
Astra, meanwhile, began to download all the data she could
from The Architect's systems, including detailed schematics
of the Mutagen and its processing requirements, her fingers
flying across her tablet, a silent race against time. Gem,
ever practical, began to load their essential supplies
from the Steel Caravan into the spacecraft's cargo hold,
her movements swift and efficient, her mind already envisioning
the vastness of space, the new canvas that awaited them.
