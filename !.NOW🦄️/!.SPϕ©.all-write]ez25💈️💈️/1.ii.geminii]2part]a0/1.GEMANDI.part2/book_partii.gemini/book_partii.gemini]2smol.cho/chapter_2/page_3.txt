They walked for what felt like hours, the setting sun
casting long, distorted shadows that danced like specters
around them. Every creak of metal, every rustle of wind
through shattered windows, sent a jolt of fear through
their weary bodies. The city, once a vibrant tapestry
of life, was now a mausoleum, its silence broken only
by the echoes of their own footsteps and the distant,
haunting cries of the Carcazoids.

"We need to find shelter," Astra whispered, her voice
raspy. "Somewhere defensible. We can't spend the night
out in the open."

Lucky nodded, his eyes constantly scanning their surroundings.
"I know a place," he said, his voice grim. "An old auto
repair shop, a few blocks from here. It's got a reinforced
garage door, and maybe, just maybe, some tools we can
use."

Hope, a fragile flicker in the encroaching darkness,
ignited within them. They quickened their pace, their
fatigue momentarily forgotten in the desperate need for
safety. The repair shop loomed ahead, a dark, hulking
shape against the bruised twilight sky. Its windows were
boarded up, its entrance barricaded with rusted metal
sheets.

"Looks promising," Gem said, a hint of her usual optimism
returning. "Like a fortress."

Lucky approached the main door cautiously, his crowbar
ready. The barricades were old, but sturdy. It took all
three of them, working in strained silence, to pry open
a small gap, just wide enough for them to slip through.
Inside, the air was thick with the smell of oil and stale
metal. Moonlight filtered through cracks in the roof,
illuminating dust motes dancing in the air.

They secured the entrance as best they could, piling
scrap metal and old tires against the makeshift opening.
Then, exhausted, they collapsed onto a pile of discarded
tarps, their bodies aching, their minds reeling from the
day's events. The silence inside the garage was a welcome
relief, a stark contrast to the constant threat of the
outside world.

"We made it," Astra murmured, her head resting against
a cold metal beam. "For now."

Lucky looked at his companions, their faces streaked
with dirt and exhaustion, but their eyes still held a
spark of defiance. They were survivors, each in their
own way. And as long as they had each other, they had
a chance. The thought of a mobile base, a true sanctuary
on wheels, began to form in his mind. This repair shop,
he realized, might just be the first step towards that
dream.

Sleep came fitfully, punctuated by the distant howls
of Carcazoids and the creaks and groans of the old building.
But for the first time in days, they were safe. And in
the morning, a new chapter would begin. A chapter of
steel, and wheels, and the desperate hope for a future
on the road.
