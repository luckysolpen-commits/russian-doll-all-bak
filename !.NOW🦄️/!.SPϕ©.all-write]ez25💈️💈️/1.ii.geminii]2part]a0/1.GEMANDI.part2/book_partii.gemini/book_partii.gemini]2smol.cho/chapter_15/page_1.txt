The Architect, defeated and humiliated, led them through
a labyrinthine series of pristine corridors, deeper into
the heart of The Nexus. The air grew colder, more sterile,
and the faint hum of advanced machinery filled the silence.
Finally, they arrived at a massive, reinforced vault door,
its surface gleaming under the harsh fluorescent lights.

"The Mutagen," The Architect stated, his voice devoid
of its previous arrogance. "It's in there. But as I said,
it's useless to you. Earth's technology is far too primitive
to process it. Only the advanced labs on Mars possess
the capabilities."

Lucky, Astra, and Gem exchanged a look. Mars. The word
hung in the air, a distant, almost mythical concept. Yet,
here it was, a tangible link to their desperate hope.

Astra, ever the scientist, stepped forward, her eyes scanning
the vault door. "He's right," she confirmed, her voice
thoughtful. "The energy signatures emanating from within
indicate a highly unstable compound. It would require
precise atmospheric and gravitational controls for safe
manipulation."

"So, we go to Mars," Gem declared, her voice firm, a new
sense of purpose in her eyes. "We take his spacecraft."

The Architect scoffed. "My personal vessel? It's not a
taxi, child. And even if you could pilot it, the journey
is perilous. The solar system is not as empty as you
think."

"We've faced worse," Lucky retorted, his gaze unwavering.
"Show us the way."

Defeated, The Architect led them to a hidden hangar, a
vast, cavernous space beneath The Nexus. There, bathed
in the soft glow of emergency lights, sat a spacecraft.
It was sleek, futuristic, unlike anything they had ever
seen. Its hull gleamed with an otherworldly sheen, and
its engines, though silent, exuded a sense of immense
power.

"The 'Ares I'," The Architect announced, a flicker of
pride in his eyes despite his defeat. "My personal transport.
Designed for interstellar travel. It's fully automated,
but requires specific biometric authorization for launch."

"Which you will provide," Lucky stated, his voice leaving
no room for argument.

Under Lucky's watchful eye, The Architect reluctantly
initiated the launch sequence. Astra, meanwhile, began
to download all the data she could from The Architect's
systems, including detailed schematics of the Mutagen
and its processing requirements. Gem, ever practical,
began to load their essential supplies from the Steel
Caravan into the spacecraft's cargo hold.

As the countdown began, the hangar doors slowly opened,
revealing the bruised, smoke-filled sky of Earth. The
clown infestation, they knew, was far from over. Earth
was too corrupted, too far gone. Their mission was no
longer to save it, but to find a way to prevent the same
fate from befalling other worlds.

The spacecraft vibrated, a low hum building into a powerful
roar. The G-forces pressed them back into their seats
as the Ares I ascended, piercing through the atmosphere,
leaving the dying world behind. Below them, Earth shrank,
a swirling marble of blue and green, scarred by the red
wounds of the apocalypse.

They were on their way to Mars, a new, uncertain battle
awaiting them. The Architect's final words echoed in
their minds: "The daughters of the Black Queen hold the
key to the future." A new mystery, a new challenge. But
they were ready. They had faced the worst of humanity,
and the worst of its creations. Now, they would face
the unknown. The red planet beckoned, a beacon of hope
and a new beginning.
