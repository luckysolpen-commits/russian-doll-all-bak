**GEM&I: Martian Gambit**

**Scene 13**

**EXT. SPACE**

The Seraph's Folly, a silver needle against the black velvet of space. Below, the Earth is a scarred jewel, a swirl of white clouds and brown continents, marred by the grey-brown patches of the Carcazoid infestation.

INT. SERAPH'S FOLLY (COCKPIT)

LUCKY and GEM are looking at the Earth through the main viewport. The roar of the launch has been replaced by the silent hum of the ship's systems.

<center>GEM</center>
> It looks so... peaceful from up here.

<center>LUCKY</center>
> It's not. It's dying.

A moment of silence passes between them. The weight of their mission, of what they have left behind, settles upon them.

Suddenly, a com channel opens. A familiar voice, tinny and distorted, comes through the speakers.

<center>ASTRA (V.O.)</center>
> You didn't think I'd miss the trip, did you?

Lucky and Gem look at each other, their faces a mixture of shock and relief.

<center>LUCKY</center>
> Astra! Where are you?

<center>ASTRA (V.O.)</center>
> In the cargo hold. There was a maintenance shaft that led from the control center to the launch tower. I figured it was a good back-up plan.

A hatch in the back of the cockpit opens, and ASTRA emerges. She is bruised and covered in grime, but she is alive.

<center>GEM</center>
> You're a sneaky one.

<center>ASTRA</center>
> I try.

She looks at the Earth, her expression a mixture of sadness and determination.

<center>ASTRA</center>
> We have a long way to go.

<center>LUCKY</center>
> We'll get there.

He looks at his two companions, his family forged in the crucible of the apocalypse. For the first time in a long time, he feels a flicker of hope.

<center>LUCKY</center>
> We'll get there.

**FADE OUT.**
