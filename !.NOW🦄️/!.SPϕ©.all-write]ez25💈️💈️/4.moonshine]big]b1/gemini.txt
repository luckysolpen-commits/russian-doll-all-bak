**CHAPTER 50: THE ARCHITECT'S INVITATION**

The cryptic summons from The Architect arrived on the lunar base's main communication channel, a stark, unadorned text message that simply read: "Your presence is requested at the African mine camp. Earth security. Immediate."

Lucky, Gem, Astra, Jin, Min, and Yoon gathered around the console, their faces a mixture of confusion and apprehension. "Earth security?" Lucky mused, rubbing his chin. "After everything with The Diabolus, I suppose it makes sense they'd want a debrief. But why us, and why so urgent?"

Astra, ever perceptive, felt a prickle of unease. "It's vague. And why specifically the mine camp? Why not a secure UN facility?"

Gem, always pragmatic, shrugged. "Maybe it's a more discreet location. We did just deal with a literal demon. Perhaps they want to keep this under wraps."

The Korean tear-it users, while curious, deferred to Lucky's judgment. Jin, however, pointed out, "The message doesn't specify *all* of us. Just 'your presence'."

Lucky reread the message, a frown deepening on his face. "It's addressed to the team, collectively. Still, something feels off." He tried to contact Saoirse, but the communication lines to Earth seemed unusually restricted, allowing only outbound messages to the Architect's designated contact.

Despite their lingering doubts, the weight of "Earth security" was a powerful motivator. The memory of Loona's sacrifice, and the potential for another cosmic threat, spurred them to action. They prepared for the journey, leaving the Hollow Moon with a sense of duty, yet an underlying current of suspicion.

As they descended through Earth's atmosphere, heading towards the coordinates provided, a new, more specific message arrived, this one directly to Astra's personal comms. It was from The Architect's assistant, a polite but firm directive: "Upon arrival, Mr. Lucky will be escorted to a separate holding area for preliminary security protocols. The rest of the team will proceed directly to the main briefing room."

Astra's eyes met Lucky's, a silent alarm passing between them. The Architect's true agenda was beginning to reveal itself. This wasn't just about Earth security; it was about isolating Lucky, and perhaps, exploiting the very knowledge that had cost Loona her life. The game had changed.
