Chapter 9: The UN Deal

The temporary UN conference room on the lunar base was stark and functional, a long, polished table dominating the space. The air was thick with hushed, tense conversation, a stark contrast to the usual boisterousness of the base. Dr. Patel, a sharp, no-nonsense UN representative in her fifties, sat at one end, flanked by two other UN officials. Opposite them, alone, sat Edge Danger Man. His usual flamboyant attire had been replaced by a more subdued, yet still stylish, dark suit, a calculated move to project an image of seriousness.

"Mr. Danger Man," Dr. Patel began, her voice crisp and devoid of any warmth. "Let's be clear. The United Nations is facing an unprecedented global crisis. We are not here for theatrics."

Edge offered a charming, confident smile, a practiced gesture that usually disarmed his audience. "And I, Dr. Patel, am here for solutions. Practical, effective solutions. My team and I have been at the forefront of deep-space exploration for months. We have intimate knowledge of the asteroid belt, and more importantly, of 'The Diabolus' itself."

One of the UN officials, a skeptical man with a furrowed brow, interjected, "Your team's initial reports on Asteroid-6 were... less than comprehensive. In fact, they were quite dismissive."

Edge didn't flinch. He met the official's gaze with a smooth, unperturbed demeanor. "Early days. Preliminary data. We've since conducted more in-depth analysis. We understand its unique properties. Its trajectory. Its... potential."

Dr. Patel leaned forward, her gaze piercing, attempting to see through Edge's carefully constructed facade. "And what do you propose, Mr. Danger Man? We have our own teams, our own resources. Why should we entrust this critical mission to you?"

Edge leaned back in his chair, a calculated confidence radiating from him. "Because we're already here. We have the infrastructure, the experience, and frankly, the audacity to get the job done. You send in your teams, you waste precious time and resources. We offer a streamlined, efficient approach. And in return..."

He paused, letting the silence hang in the air, allowing the weight of his unspoken demand to settle upon them.

"...we require full operational autonomy," Edge continued, his voice dropping slightly, but no less firm. "And... immunity. From any and all past inquiries. A clean slate, Dr. Patel. For services rendered in saving your planet."

Dr. Patel's eyes narrowed. She exchanged a quick, meaningful glance with her officials. The audacity of his demand was breathtaking, yet the offer was undeniably tempting. Time was of the essence, and Edge's team was already on site, possessing firsthand knowledge of the threat.

"Immunity for what, precisely, Mr. Danger Man?" Dr. Patel asked, her voice laced with suspicion.

Edge gave a slight shrug, a gesture of feigned nonchalance. "Let's just say... my past is colorful. And entirely irrelevant to the current crisis. Focus on the future, Doctor. The very immediate future of Earth."

Dr. Patel considered him for a long moment, then slowly nodded. The decision was made, driven by desperation and the ticking clock. "If your team can present a viable plan to deflect or destroy 'The Diabolus,' and execute it successfully... we will grant your request for immunity. And provide additional resources as needed. But failure, Mr. Danger Man, is not an option."

Edge's grin returned, wider and more triumphant than before. He extended a hand across the table. "Deal, Dr. Patel. You won't regret it. We're about to make history."

Dr. Patel shook his hand, her expression still stern, but with a hint of reluctant acceptance. Edge had gotten his way, securing his freedom and a leading role in humanity's desperate fight for survival.
