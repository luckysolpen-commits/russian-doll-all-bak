Edge leaned back in his chair, a calculated confidence radiating from
him, a subtle smirk playing on his lips. He knew he had them, hooked
by their desperation, reeled in by his audacious claims. "Because we're
already here," he stated, his voice calm and assured, a subtle challenge
in his tone. "We have the infrastructure, the experience, and frankly,
the audacity to get the job done. You send in your teams, you waste
precious time and resources. Time, Dr. Patel, that Earth simply doesn't
have. Every second we spend debating, every moment we delay, brings
that... thing... closer to your planet. We offer a streamlined, efficient
approach. A surgical strike, if you will. And in return..." He paused,
letting the silence hang in the air, allowing the weight of his unspoken
demand to settle upon them, a subtle power play that he had perfected
over years of high-stakes negotiations. He watched their faces, searching
for any sign of belief, any flicker of hope in their grim expressions.
The air in the room, devoid of any personal touches, felt cold and
impersonal, a perfect setting for the high-stakes negotiations about
to unfold. The faint hum of the lunar base's life support systems
provided a low, constant background drone, a reminder of their fragile
existence on this alien rock. Every detail, from the subtle vibrations
of the ground to the distant glint of metal on the horizon, seemed
to heighten the sense of anticipation, the feeling that something extraordinary
was about to unfold. He was a man who lived for the spotlight, and
the lunar landing pad, with its stark lighting and captive audience,
was his stage. He felt a surge of adrenaline, a familiar rush that
had once accompanied him into battle, but now fueled by a different
kind of ambition. This was a fight for a better future, a fight he
was more than ready to lead. He looked at Luna, who was watching him
with a subtle, almost imperceptible nod of approval, a silent acknowledgment
of his leadership. He knew that their partnership, though born of necessity,
had the potential to be truly transformative. The future, he realized,
was not just about survival; it was about creation, about building
something new, something lasting, something that would benefit all
of humanity. And he, Lucky, was at the forefront of it all.
