Edge waved a dismissive hand, not taking his eyes off the swirling chaos
outside, his face alight with a mischievous grin. "Mission parameters
are for rookies, Lucky! We're making history here! We're not just
mining rocks; we're carving out a new destiny for humanity! And speaking
of history, behold! Asteroid Alpha-1!" He pointed to a relatively
small, rocky asteroid ahead, its surface pockmarked with countless
impact craters, a silent testament to its long, tumultuous journey
through the cosmos. It was unremarkable in appearance, a dull gray
against the blackness of space, yet to Edge, it was a canvas for his
next masterpiece. With a flourish, he activated the vessel's gravity
beam, a shimmering blue light extending from the front of the Drifter,
its ethereal glow illuminating the asteroid's surface. With practiced
ease, he began to draw in smaller rocks from the asteroid's surface,
pulling them into the vessel's cargo hold with a precision that belied
his earlier recklessness. The rocks, ranging in size from pebbles to
small boulders, floated effortlessly into the Drifter's maw, their
surfaces glinting with unseen minerals. "See?" Edge crowed, his voice
filled with triumph. "Child's play! Just a little cosmic vacuuming!
And watch this!" He performed a series of unnecessary, acrobatic maneuvers
with the gravity beam, twirling the collected rocks before depositing
them, a dazzling display of skill and showmanship. Jin, Min, and Yoon
cheered from the back, their comms crackling with excitement as they
recorded every move, their faces alight with a childlike wonder. "Amazing,
Edge!" Jin exclaimed. "Do it again!" Edge grinned, basking in the
adoration, his ego swelling with every cheer. He glanced at Lucky,
a triumphant smirk on his face. "Still think your drone skills compare,
Lucky? This is real piloting! This is art!" He leaned back in his
chair, a picture of smug satisfaction, clearly enjoying Lucky's discomfort.

Lucky clenched his jaw, a muscle twitching along his cheekbone. He
knew Edge was good, infuriatingly good, but his showboating was grating
on his nerves. He forced himself to focus on the data screens, trying
to ignore the spectacle unfolding before him, the constant stream of
Edge's self-congratulatory commentary. He saw the raw data, the mineral
composition, the projected yield, and it was impressive, undeniably
so. But he also saw the unnecessary risks, the wasted energy, the sheer
inefficiency of Edge's methods. He was a man of precision, of calculated
moves, and Edge's reckless abandon went against every fiber of his
being. He took a deep breath, trying to calm the rising tide of irritation.
"Just... get the job done, Edge," Lucky said, his voice tight with
suppressed frustration, a thinly veiled command that he hoped Edge
would heed. He knew that such immense profit often came with equally
immense risks, and his instincts, honed by years of navigating treacherous
situations, were screaming caution. But with Lucky at the helm, he
felt a cautious optimism, a belief that they could overcome any obstacle.
He was ready to embrace the unknown, to build something truly extraordinary.
The silence that followed was not empty, but filled with the unspoken
understanding, the shared commitment to this audacious new dream. The
war was over, but their journey had just begun. And for the first
time in a long time, Lucky felt truly alive. He imagined the vast,
unexplored reaches of the asteroid belt, the untold riches waiting
to be discovered, the scientific breakthroughs that lay just beyond
their grasp. This was a chance to be pioneers, to write their names
in the stars, not with the blood of war, but with the sweat of innovation
and the triumph of human ingenuity. He felt a surge of adrenaline,
a familiar rush that had once accompanied him into battle, but now
fueled by a different kind of ambition. This was a fight for a better
future, a fight he was more than ready to lead. He looked at Luna,
who was watching him with a subtle, almost imperceptible nod of approval,
a silent acknowledgment of his leadership. He knew that their partnership,
though born of necessity, had the potential to be truly transformative.
The future, he realized, was not just about survival; it was about
creation, about building something new, something lasting, something
that would benefit all of humanity. And he, Lucky, was at the forefront
of it all.
