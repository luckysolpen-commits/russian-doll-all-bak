Chapter 7: Analysis and Celebration

The research lab on the lunar base buzzed with a controlled chaos, a symphony of scientific equipment humming and soft, rhythmic beeps. Asteroid Alpha-2 and Asteroid Gamma-3, now significantly smaller after initial processing, were secured on examination tables, their surfaces illuminated by the harsh, focused beams of various scanners. Holographic displays shimmered with intricate molecular structures and swirling energy readings, painting a complex picture of their hidden compositions.

Astra, Gem, Jin, Min, and Yoon moved with practiced efficiency, a well-oiled machine of scientific inquiry. Astra, her brow furrowed in concentration, operated a quantum computer, its crystalline core glowing softly, casting an ethereal light on her face. Gem, ever the pragmatist, meticulously swept a nuclear scanner over the asteroid's surface, his eyes glued to the fluctuating readouts.

"Readings confirm high concentrations of platinum and iridium!" Astra exclaimed, her voice tinged with excitement. "Even trace amounts of... antimatter? That's unprecedented for a natural formation of this size!"

Gem let out a low whistle, his eyes wide with disbelief. "Antimatter? Luna wasn't kidding about the potential. This is beyond anything we projected."

Jin and Min, their movements precise and deliberate, operated advanced extraction arms, carefully siphoning off refined minerals into containment units. Yoon, ever the meticulous one, was already cataloging the findings, her fingers flying across a data pad.

"Gamma-3 shows an unusual crystalline structure," Yoon murmured to herself, a hint of wonder in her voice. "High potential for new rare earth elements. Edge was right about this one."

As if on cue, Edge himself entered the lab, still in his jumpsuit, but now with a pair of stylish, futuristic goggles pushed up onto his forehead. He surveyed the scene with a proprietary air, a wide grin spreading across his face.

"See?" Edge crowed, his voice echoing slightly in the sterile environment. "Told you these rocks had personality! And profit!"

Lucky entered, carrying a data pad, his gaze drawn to the glowing displays. A sense of wonder, almost childlike, spread across his face. "This is incredible, Astra," he said, his voice hushed. "We're literally unlocking the secrets of the universe."

Astra, tired but radiating a quiet satisfaction, smiled. "And the secrets of a very lucrative market, Lucky. Mr. Kim is going to be very pleased."

Edge clapped Lucky on the back, his enthusiasm infectious. "Damn right he is! We're not just miners, boss. We're cosmic prospectors! And we're about to hit the motherlode!"

The air in the lab crackled with a palpable sense of triumph. The initial investment, the long journey, the risks – it was all paying off. The promise of immense wealth, coupled with the thrill of groundbreaking discovery, filled the moon base with an intoxicating energy. A celebration was undoubtedly in order.
