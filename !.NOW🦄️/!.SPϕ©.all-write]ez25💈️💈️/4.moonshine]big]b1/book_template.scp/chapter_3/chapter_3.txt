Chapter 3: The Chaebol's Journey

The lunar shuttle launchpad hummed with the deep thrum of powerful engines spooling up, a symphony of raw power and anticipation. Bathed in the stark glow of floodlights, a massive, sleek lunar shuttle stood poised for departure, a metallic titan against the inky blackness of space. Lucky, Astra, Gem, and the Korean tear-it users – Jin, Min, and Yoon – stood ready, clad in pristine new space suits. Their faces, visible through their visors, were a mixture of excitement for the journey ahead and a quiet determination.

Mr. Kim, accompanied by a small, impeccably dressed entourage of his own, approached Lucky, extending a hand. His grip was firm, his gaze unwavering. "My investment is significant, Mr. Lucky. I trust your team will prove worthy of it."

Lucky, a confident smile playing on his lips, met Mr. Kim's gaze. "We won't disappoint, Mr. Kim. We're calling ourselves "The Chaebol" now. A new era."

A rare, slight smile touched Mr. Kim's lips. He then turned to Jin, Min, and Yoon, his expression becoming more serious. "Ensure our interests are protected. And learn everything you can."

Jin, ever respectful, bowed slightly. "Yes, sir."

As they boarded the shuttle, Astra paused at the ramp, looking back at the distant, shimmering lights of Dorchester, a pang of nostalgia echoing in her heart. "Goodbye, Dorchester," she murmured to Lucky, her voice barely a whisper. "For now."

Lucky placed a reassuring hand on her shoulder, his eyes fixed on the growing crescent of the moon ahead. "Hello, Moon. And beyond."

Inside the lunar shuttle, luxury enveloped them, designed for comfort during the long journey. Lucky, Astra, and Gem settled into their seats together, while the Koreans, ever diligent, were already engrossed in their advanced tablets, no doubt reviewing mission parameters or perhaps even indulging in a quick game.

Gem, unable to contain his excitement, grinned widely. "Ten trillion won. Can you believe it? We're going to be rich!"

Astra, ever the pragmatist, offered a thoughtful counterpoint. "It's not just about the money, Gem. It's about what we can do with it. The possibilities."

Lucky gazed out the viewport, watching as Earth, a beautiful, fragile marble, receded into the vastness. The moon, a luminous crescent, grew larger with every passing moment, beckoning them forward.

"It's about a new adventure," Lucky said quietly, his voice filled with a sense of wonder. "A new frontier."
