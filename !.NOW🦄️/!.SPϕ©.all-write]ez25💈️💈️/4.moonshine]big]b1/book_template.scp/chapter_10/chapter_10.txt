Chapter 10: The Backup Plan

The secure conference room on the lunar base was a crucible of hushed, urgent conversation. Lucky, Astra, Gem, Jin, Min, and Yoon were gathered around a holographic table, their faces grim. In the corner, Edge's absurd presentation to the UN played silently on a smaller screen, its ridiculousness amplified by the gravity of their own discussion.

Loona, her holographic form shimmering softly, spoke, her voice a low, concerned whisper. "His plan is... inefficient. And dangerous. It will not work."

"'Inefficient' is an understatement, Luna," Gem burst out, frustration lacing his voice. "It's a cosmic clown show! We're wasting precious time and resources on vintage laser cannons!"

Astra paced, her movements agitated. "And the UN is buying it. They're desperate. But this isn't just about deflecting an asteroid anymore. It's about... something else. Something Loona Twilight warned me about."

Lucky slammed his fist on the table, a spark of fierce determination igniting in his eyes. "Then we do it ourselves. We come up with a real plan. A backup plan."

Jin, Min, and Yoon, the Korean tear-it users, exchanged glances. They had been quietly observing, their expertise in energy manipulation a potential, albeit terrifying, game-changer.

"We've been researching new applications for Tear-It technology," Jin began hesitantly. "High-powered energy sources. Beyond anything currently deployed."

Min nodded, his expression serious. "If we could harness enough raw Tear-It power... a focused blast... it could vaporize The Diabolus."

Gem's eyes widened. "Vaporize? You mean... completely? No fragments? No ice age?"

Yoon nodded, her voice quiet. "Potentially. But the energy required... it's immense. Unprecedented."

Astra looked at them, a dawning horror in her eyes, her voice trembling. "But... the research. The latest Tear-It applications. They involve... clones. Sacrificing clones to generate that kind of power."

Silence descended upon the room, heavy and suffocating. The ethical implications of their words hung in the air, enormous and terrifying. Loona's holographic form flickered, her expression unreadable.

Lucky's voice was low, but firm, cutting through the silence. "Think about it, Astra. The alternative. Earth. An ice age. Billions of lives. If it means sacrificing some clones... to save humanity..."

He looked at each of them, his gaze unwavering. Gem looked conflicted, his usual exuberance replaced by a somber thoughtfulness. But the Koreans nodded slowly, their faces grim but resolute. They understood the stakes, the terrible calculus of their situation.

"This is... a terrible choice," Astra whispered, her voice barely audible.

Lucky nodded, his own heart heavy. "It is. But sometimes, terrible choices are the only ones we have left. We need to explore this. We need to know if it's even possible."

The weight of their decision settled upon them, a burden they would carry, knowing that the fate of billions rested on their willingness to make the most unthinkable of sacrifices.
