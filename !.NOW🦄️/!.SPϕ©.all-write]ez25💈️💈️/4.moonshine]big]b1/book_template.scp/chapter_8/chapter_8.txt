Chapter 8: Loona's Warning

Astra lay asleep in her bunk, the soft glow of the lunar night filtering through her viewport, casting long, gentle shadows across the room. The rhythmic sound of her own breathing was the only disturbance in the quiet solitude. Suddenly, a shimmering, ethereal light began to fill the space, growing in intensity until it bathed the room in an otherworldly luminescence. Astra's eyes fluttered open, startled.

Standing at the foot of her bed was a figure of pure light and shifting color – Loona Twilight. Ageless and regal, her form rippled like water, accompanied by two smaller, silent holographic figures that hovered beside her. Astra, groggy at first, then jolted awake with a jolt of recognition, pushed herself up.

"Twilight?" she whispered, her voice laced with disbelief. "What... what are you doing here?"

Loona Twilight's voice was a melodic whisper, echoing slightly as if from a great distance. "Forgive the intrusion, Astra. But there is a matter of grave importance. Something we have observed from the deeper currents of the cosmos."

A chill ran down Astra's spine. Loona Twilight's visits were never casual, never without significant purpose. She sat up fully, her mind racing. "What is it? Is it about the asteroid belt?"

Loona Twilight nodded, her form rippling in affirmation. "Indeed. One of the celestial bodies you have been studying. You call it... Asteroid-6."

Astra frowned. Asteroid-6. It was one of the less remarkable ones they'd scanned, mostly rocky, nothing of significant value. She remembered dismissing a faint anomaly in its initial readings as a sensor glitch. "Asteroid-6? What about it?"

Loona Twilight's voice grew more somber, her luminous form flickering slightly. "It is not what it appears. It moves with an unnatural velocity, far exceeding its gravitational parameters. And its energy signature... it is unlike anything we have ever encountered. It is hollow, Astra. And it emanates a dark energy."

Astra's eyes widened, a cold dread beginning to seep into her. "Hollow? A dark energy? What do you mean?"

"We believe it is not a natural formation," Loona Twilight continued, her voice now laced with urgency. "It is... a vessel. And it is accelerating. Towards Earth."

Astra gasped, her mind struggling to comprehend the implications. A vessel? Heading for Earth? This was beyond anything they had prepared for, beyond any threat they had ever imagined. "A spacecraft? From... from where? What is it?"

Loona Twilight's form began to flicker more intensely, as if under immense strain, the smaller holographic figures dissolving around her. "Its true nature is veiled. But the dark energy... it is malevolent. Ancient. Whatever lies within, it is not benign. And if it reaches Earth..."

Her voice trailed off, her form dissolving completely, leaving Astra alone in the dim light of her quarters. The chilling words echoed in her mind, a terrifying prophecy. The soft hum of the lunar base, once a comforting sound, now felt ominous, a prelude to an unimaginable disaster.
