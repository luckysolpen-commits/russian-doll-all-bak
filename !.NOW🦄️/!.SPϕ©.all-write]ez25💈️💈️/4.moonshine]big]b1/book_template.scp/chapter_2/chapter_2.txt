Chapter 2: Luna's Proposition

The soft, ambient music that filled Luna's luxury habitat on the lunar base was a stark contrast to the recent cacophony of war. The space itself was a testament to futuristic elegance, with panoramic views of Earth hanging like a serene blue marble against the inky blackness of space. Luna, in her thirties, exuded confidence, her elegant, flowing attire complementing the sleek, modern design of her surroundings. She stood before a holographic display, which projected intricate diagrams of asteroid belts and glowing mineral compositions.

Lucky, Astra, and Gem were seated at a polished table, their attention fixed on Luna. Beside them sat Mr. Kim, a shrewd Korean representative in his fifties, impeccably dressed, his presence radiating quiet authority. Jin, Min, and Yoon, the Korean tear-it users, were also present, observing with keen interest, their faces betraying a mix of curiosity and skepticism.

Luna's smile was warm, inviting. "Gentlemen, and ladies," she began, her voice smooth and persuasive, "thank you for joining me. I believe I have an opportunity that will redefine your understanding of wealth."

Gem, ever the opportunist, leaned forward, his eyes gleaming with anticipation. "We're listening, Luna. Always open to redefining wealth."

Luna gestured gracefully to the holographic display, which shimmered with celestial bodies. "For years, the focus has been on terrestrial resources. But the true treasure, the untapped fortune, lies out there. In the newly discovered asteroid belt."

The display shifted, highlighting specific asteroids, their projected mineral content glowing with an almost ethereal light.

"We're talking rare earth elements – neodymium, dysprosium – crucial for our burgeoning electric vehicle and renewable energy sectors," Luna continued, her voice gaining a quiet intensity. "And precious metals: platinum, palladium, rhodium. The demand is skyrocketing."

Mr. Kim, who had been silently observing, finally spoke, his voice calm but authoritative, cutting through the air of excitement. "And the investment? The projected returns?"

Luna's confidence was unwavering. "An initial investment of $500 million. This covers establishing a lunar base, state-of-the-art extraction equipment, and a world-class team. As for returns... we're projecting at least 500% within the first five years. Dividends distributed quarterly."

Astra exchanged a look with Lucky, her eyebrows slightly raised. The numbers were staggering, almost unbelievable.

"That's... ambitious," Astra said, her voice tinged with a hint of skepticism. "What about the logistics? Extraction, refining, shipping?"

Luna's smile remained. "We have a comprehensive plan. The lunar base will be our hub. Missions to select asteroids, resources transported back here for refining, then shipped to Earth. We've already secured preliminary agreements with several major corporations."

Gem let out a low whistle, his initial skepticism replaced by awe. "Five hundred percent... that's not just redefining wealth, Luna. That's rewriting the rulebook."

Lucky, who had been quiet, absorbing every detail, finally spoke, his voice firm and decisive. "We're in. We want in on this opportunity."

Luna's smile widened, a genuine warmth radiating from her. "Excellent. I'll send over the detailed financial projections and business plan for your review. We can discuss the specifics further."

Mr. Kim, however, held up a hand, his gaze fixed on Lucky. "My investment, Mr. Lucky, will be substantial. Ten trillion won. I will require... due diligence. A personal inspection of the lunar operations. My team will accompany yours."

Lucky nodded, understanding the weight of the request. It was a significant commitment, and with it came significant expectations. "Understood, Mr. Kim. We welcome your oversight."
