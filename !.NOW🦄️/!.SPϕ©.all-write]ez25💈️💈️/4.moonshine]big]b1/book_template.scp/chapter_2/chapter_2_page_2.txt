Beside them sat Mr. Kim, a shrewd Korean representative in his fifties,
impeccably dressed in a tailored suit of deep charcoal gray, its fabric
so fine it seemed to ripple with his slightest movement. His presence
radiated quiet authority, a subtle power that was far more potent
than any overt display. His eyes, dark and intelligent, missed nothing,
observing every nuance of the room, every flicker of expression on
the faces of Lucky's team. He held a small, antique-looking fan, which
he occasionally unfurled with a soft snap, fanning himself with a
deliberate, almost meditative rhythm. The faint scent of sandalwood
and old paper emanated from him, a stark contrast to the floral notes
of Luna's habitat. Jin, Min, and Yoon, the Korean tear-it users, were
also present, standing respectfully a few paces behind Mr. Kim. Their
faces, usually stoic and unreadable, betrayed a mix of curiosity and
skepticism as they took in the opulent surroundings. Jin, the most
expressive of the three, occasionally exchanged a quick, almost imperceptible
glance with Min, a silent communication that spoke volumes about their
shared thoughts. Yoon, ever the quiet observer, had her hands clasped
behind her back, her gaze fixed on Luna, as if trying to decipher
the secrets hidden behind the entrepreneur's confident smile. The
air in the room, already thick with unspoken expectations, seemed
to hum with the subtle tension between the two groups, the seasoned
warriors and the shrewd investors, all brought together by Luna's
audacious proposition.

Luna's smile was warm, inviting, a carefully crafted expression that
managed to convey both genuine welcome and an underlying sense of
control. Her voice, smooth and persuasive, filled the habitat, its
melodic tones perfectly modulated to command attention without being
overbearing. "Gentlemen, and ladies," she began, her gaze sweeping
over each of them, lingering for a moment on Lucky, then Astra, then
Mr. Kim, a silent acknowledgment of their individual importance. "Thank
you for joining me. I believe I have an opportunity that will redefine
your understanding of wealth." Her words hung in the air, pregnant
with promise, a subtle challenge to their preconceived notions of
success and prosperity. She gestured gracefully with one hand, her
fingers adorned with delicate, almost invisible rings that seemed
to shimmer with an inner light, towards the holographic display, which
shimmered with celestial bodies, each one a potential treasure trove.
Her other hand rested lightly on the polished surface of the table,
her fingertips barely touching the cool, smooth obsidian. She watched
their reactions, a faint, almost imperceptible flicker of satisfaction
in her eyes as she saw the subtle shifts in their expressions, the
dawning realization of the immense potential she was offering. The
ambient music seemed to swell slightly, a subtle underscore to her
presentation, enhancing the sense of grandeur and importance. She
was a master of her craft, a conductor of desires, and her audience
was captivated.

Gem, ever the opportunist, leaned forward, his eyes gleaming with anticipation,
like a predator spotting its prey. His usual restless energy seemed
to coalesce into a laser-like focus, his entire being radiating eagerness.
"We're listening, Luna," he said, his voice a little too eager, a
little too loud in the refined quiet of the habitat. He caught Astra's
eye, and she gave him a subtle, almost imperceptible shake of her
head, a silent warning to temper his enthusiasm. But Gem was too caught
up in the moment, too enthralled by the promise of immense wealth.
"Always open to redefining wealth," he added, a wide, almost manic
grin spreading across his face. He imagined the possibilities, the
endless stream of credits, the luxurious life, the freedom from the
constant struggle for survival. His mind, always quick to calculate
profit margins and potential returns, was already running through
complex equations, envisioning a future where money was no object.
He fidgeted in his seat, unable to sit still, his excitement almost
palpable. He was a man who lived for the thrill of the deal, the rush
of the big score, and Luna's words were like a siren song, beckoning
him towards a future filled with unimaginable riches. He could almost
taste the success, the sweet, intoxicating flavor of victory. The faint
scent of the floral air freshener seemed to intensify, a subtle reminder
of the luxurious world he was about to enter.
