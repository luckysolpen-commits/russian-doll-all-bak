The day of the competition arrived, and
the Great Hall was buzzing with an
electric energy. The air was thick with
anticipation, with excitement, with the collective creative
energy of the entire school. The five
Houses were gathered, each in their designated
section, their colors on full display. The
students were a sea of silver, of
rainbow, of blue, of black, of pink.
The rivalry was palpable, but it was
a friendly rivalry, a rivalry born of
a shared passion for creation. The judges
were seated at a long table at
the front of the hall, their faces
a mixture of seriousness and excitement. The
headmaster, with his long, white beard and
his twinkling eyes. Professor Aurora, with her
radiant smile and her golden hair. Zephyr,
with his wild, gray hair and his
mischievous grin. And Lyra, with her sad,
knowing eyes and her ethereal beauty. They
were a formidable panel, a group of
beings who had seen it all, who
had created it all, who had judged
it all. And they were not easily
impressed. The competition began, and the teams
from each House presented their creations. The
team from Cirrope, the House of the
Rainbow Fish, created a spectacular light show,
a dazzling display of color and sound
that was both beautiful and utterly meaningless.
The team from Aetheria, the House of
the Blue Oyster, created a perfectly balanced
mobile, a delicate dance of shapes and
colors that was technically brilliant but lacked
soul. The team from Khthonoi, the House
of the Piranhas, created a dark and
brooding sculpture that was both terrifying and
captivating. It was a powerful piece, but
it was also a little one-note. The
team from Lethari, the House of the
Prawns, created a strange and wonderful contraption
that seemed to have no purpose at
all, but was endlessly fascinating to watch.
It was a work of pure, unadulterated
creativity, a testament to the power of
the weird. And then, it was Luminari's
turn. It was Lucky and Astra's turn.
They stepped forward, their hearts pounding with
a mixture of excitement and anxiety. They
placed their creation on the presentation table.
It was a simple, leather-bound book. The
cover was blank, with no title, no
author, no indication of what was inside.
The hall fell silent, a wave of
confusion and disappointment washing over the crowd.
A book? That was it? That was
the great creation from the House of
the gifted? They had expected something spectacular,
something dazzling, something... more. But Lucky and
Astra were not discouraged. They knew that
the true magic of their creation was
not on the outside, but on the
inside. They opened the book. And the
world held its breath. The story began.
