While Maria danced her elegant and tragic
ballet with the void, Flash made his
entrance. And it was, in true Flash
fashion, anything but subtle. His ship, 'The
Glitterbomb', was a chaotic explosion of light
and sound, a disco ball of a
spaceship that seemed to be powered by
pure, unadulterated joy. He did not try
to lure The Nothing with memories of
lost worlds. He tried to annoy it
to death. He blasted it with his
signature brand of off-key disco music, the
sound so jarring, so utterly devoid of
harmony, that it seemed to cause The
Nothing physical pain. He created giant, holographic
clowns that juggled exploding rubber chickens, their
silly and nonsensical antics a stark contrast
to the grim and serious business of
ending the universe. He created swarms of
sparkly, rainbow-colored space-poodles that yapped and
flew in circles around the void, their
chaotic and unpredictable movements a constant source
of irritation. It was a performance of
pure, unadulterated chaos. A symphony of silliness.
A masterpiece of creative annoyance. And it
was working. The Nothing, which had been
mesmerized by Maria's beautiful and tragic memories,
was now being pulled in a different
direction. It was being annoyed, irritated, confused.
It was like a great, cosmic beast
being tormented by a small, sparkly, and
incredibly annoying fly. It tried to consume
Flash's creations, but they were too fast,
too chaotic, too utterly devoid of any
redeeming artistic value. They were, in essence,
creative junk food. And The Nothing, which
was used to feasting on the rich, complex
creations of entire worlds, seemed to have
no appetite for this kind of sparkly,
silly nonsense. It was a brilliant and
utterly insane strategy. And it was a
strategy that only Flash could have come
up with. He was the jester in
the court of oblivion, the clown in
the face of annihilation. And his laughter
was a weapon. A powerful and unexpected
weapon against the darkness. He was not
just buying them time. He was buying
them hope. He was reminding them that
even in the darkest of times, there
is still room for laughter, for joy,
for a little bit of sparkle. He
was reminding them that the opposite of
nothingness is not just somethingness, but also
a whole lot of silly, sparkly, and
utterly ridiculous fun. And that was a
lesson that was just as important as
any they had learned in their long
and arduous journey. The cosmic bullfight was
in full swing. The matadors were a
perfect team. The beautiful and the silly.
The tragic and the comedic. The elegant
and the chaotic. And they were holding
the line. They were dancing with the
void. And they were winning. For now.
