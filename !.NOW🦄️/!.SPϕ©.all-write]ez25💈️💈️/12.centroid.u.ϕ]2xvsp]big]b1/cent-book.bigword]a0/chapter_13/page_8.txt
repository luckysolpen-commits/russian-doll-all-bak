And so, the new era of The
Centroid, and of the multiverse itself, continued
in a state of peaceful, creative, and
joyful harmony. The great cosmic drama was
over. The epic battle had been won.
The universe was safe. And the heroes
of the story, the small band of
teenage artists who had saved the world,
they settled into a life of quiet
and contented creation. They were no longer
the saviors of the universe. They were
just... happy. Lucky and Astra, their love
story now a cornerstone of the new
reality, continued to create their beautiful and
profound games, their stories a source of
inspiration and enlightenment for all who played
them. Pax, the gentle diplomat, continued his
journeys throughout the multiverse, spreading the gospel
of balance and harmony to all who
would listen. Nyx, the poet of the
void, continued her silent communion with Nexus,
their strange and beautiful friendship a testament
to the power of understanding and acceptance.
Maria, the artist of life, continued to
create her wondrous and beautiful ecosystems, her
worlds a living, breathing work of art.
And Flash, the court jester of the
universe, continued to throw the most amazing,
the most chaotic, and the most utterly
ridiculous parties the multiverse had ever seen.
They were a family. A strange, dysfunctional,
and incredibly loving family. And they were
content. They had found their purpose. They
had found their place. They had found
their peace. But the universe is a
big place. And a story is never
truly over. There are always new
challenges to face, new adventures to be
had, new songs to be sung. And
even in a universe of perfect balance,
there is always the potential for a
new kind of chaos. A new kind
of darkness. A new kind of nothing.
The story of Lucky and Astra and
their strange and beautiful family was far
from over. It was just beginning. The
thirteenth chapter, the chapter of the new
era, was a chapter of peace and
harmony. But it was also a chapter
of a new and subtle kind of
tension. A tension that was born not
of conflict, but of a deep and
abiding love. A tension that would lead
them on their next great adventure. An
adventure that would be even more strange,
more wonderful, and more dangerous than the
last. An adventure that would test their
love, their friendship, their very understanding of
what it means to be a family.
An adventure that would take them to
the farthest reaches of their own hearts.
And beyond. The future of The Centroid,
and of the multiverse itself, was bright.
But it was not without its shadows.
And in those shadows, a new story
was waiting to be told.
