Solus, his pride wounded, his philosophy challenged,
resorted to a new and desperate tactic.
If he could not win with the beauty
of his creation, he would win with
the power of it. He began to
create an army. An army of perfect,
soulless soldiers, their bodies made of polished
obsidian and their eyes glowing with a
cold, blue light. They were a reflection
of their creator. Beings of immense power
and immense emptiness. He sent his army
across the dimensional barrier, a wave of
perfect, silent, and deadly order that was
designed to crush the chaotic and imperfect
life of the team's world. The battle
had turned from a creative duel to
a literal war. The team was not
prepared for this. They had been so
focused on creating a world of life,
of beauty, of peace. They had not
prepared for war. They had no army,
no weapons, no defenses. They were artists,
not soldiers. And they were about to
be annihilated. The obsidian soldiers marched into
the team's world, their silent and orderly
advance a stark and terrifying contrast to
the vibrant and chaotic life of the
world they were invading. They were a
wave of death, of order, of a
cold and perfect emptiness. And they were
unstoppable. The beautiful and chaotic creatures of
the team's world, the singing fish, the
story-hoarding dragons, the wise and gentle
wizards, they were no match for the
soulless killing machines of Solus. The battle
was a massacre. The vibrant and beautiful
world of the team was being turned
into a silent and orderly graveyard. It
was a heartbreaking sight. A testament to
the destructive power of a single, unchecked
ego. The team watched in horror, their
hearts breaking as their beautiful creation was
being destroyed. They had been so naive.
They had believed that beauty, that life,
that art, would be enough. They had
not understood that sometimes, you have to
fight for the things you love. You
have to defend your creations. You have
to be willing to be a warrior
as well as an artist. It was
a hard lesson. A painful lesson. A
lesson that was costing them everything. But
it was a lesson they were about
to learn. For the children of The
Centroid were not just artists. They were
heroes. They were the saviors of the
multiverse. And they were not about to
let their world die without a fight.
The battle for the soul of creation
was not over. It had just entered
a new and more dangerous phase. A
phase where the artists would have to
become soldiers. A phase where the dreamers
would have to become warriors. A phase
where the lovers would have to become
fighters. And they were ready for it.
They were ready to fight for their
world. For their philosophy. For their way
of life. They were ready to fight
for the beautiful and chaotic messiness of
reality itself.
