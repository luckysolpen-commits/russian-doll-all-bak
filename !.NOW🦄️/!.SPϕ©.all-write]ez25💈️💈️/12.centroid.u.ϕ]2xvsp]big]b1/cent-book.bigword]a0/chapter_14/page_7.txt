Solus watched from his dimension of perfect
order as his army of soulless soldiers
was transformed into an army of soulful
artists. He watched as his perfect and
orderly world was invaded by the chaotic
and beautiful creations of his former minions.
He watched as his philosophy of the
self was being dismantled, deconstructed, and utterly
defeated by the power of collaboration, of
friendship, of love. And he felt something
he had not felt in a very
long time. Rage. A pure, white-hot, and
all-consuming rage. It was the rage of
a god whose creations had defied him.
The rage of a tyrant whose subjects
had rebelled. The rage of a lonely
ego that had been confronted with the
terrible and beautiful truth of its own
emptiness. He would not be defeated. He
would not be proven wrong. He would
not be made to feel so... small.
He gathered all of his power, all
of his skill, all of his rage,
into a single, final, and desperate act
of creation. He did not create an
army. He did not create a weapon.
He created himself. A giant, monstrous version
of himself, his body made of polished
obsidian and his eyes glowing with a
cold, blue light. He was a god
of order, of logic, of a cold
and terrible beauty. And he was going
to restore his world to its proper,
silent, and orderly state. He stepped across
the dimensional barrier, his giant form casting
a long and terrible shadow over the
team's vibrant and chaotic world. He was
a force of nature, a storm of
pure, concentrated ego. And he was going
to erase everything that was not a
perfect reflection of himself. The final confrontation
had begun. It was no longer a
battle of armies, or a battle of
ideas. It was a battle of gods.
A battle between the god of the
self and the gods of the selfless.
A battle between the philosophy of the
solo and the philosophy of the symphony.
A battle for the very soul of
creation. The team stood before the giant
Solus, their small forms a stark and
defiant contrast to his immense and terrible
power. They were outmatched. They were outgunned.
They were, by all logical measures, doomed.
But they were not afraid. They had
faced the void and had won. They
had faced their own inner darkness and
had emerged stronger. They had faced the
end of the universe and had created
a new beginning. They were not about
to be defeated by a giant, angry
ego with a god complex. They stood
together, a small band of heroes against
a single, monstrous god. And they were
ready to fight. Not with force, but
with art. Not with power, but with
love. Not with the weapons of war,
but with the tools of creation. They
were ready to show Solus the true
meaning of power. The true meaning of
beauty. The true meaning of being a
creator. And they were ready to do
it together. As a team. As a
family. As a symphony of souls.
