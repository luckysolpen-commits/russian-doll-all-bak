While the rest of the team was
engaged in their creative and unconventional defense,
Lucky and Astra took a different approach.
They did not try to stop the
obsidian soldiers. They tried to change them.
They flew into the heart of the
invading army, their forms shimmering with the
power of the Singularity. They were two
sparks of light in a sea of
darkness, two notes of hope in a
symphony of despair. They did not fight
the soldiers with weapons of energy or
matter. They fought them with a weapon
that was far more powerful. They fought
them with a story. It was the
same story they had told to The
Nothing. The story of their team. The
story of their friendship. The story of
their strange and beautiful family. They projected
the story into the minds of the
obsidian soldiers, a wave of pure, unadulterated
emotion that washed over their cold and
logical programming. They showed them the joy
of collaboration, the beauty of imperfection, the
power of love. They showed them what
it meant to be alive. The obsidian
soldiers, who had been created without souls,
without hearts, without the capacity to feel,
were completely overwhelmed by the story. Their
programming began to break down, their logic
circuits short-circuiting in the face of
this new and incomprehensible data. They were
not designed for emotion. They were not
designed for love. They were not designed
for the beautiful and chaotic messiness of
life. And they were beginning to fall
apart. But they were not just falling
apart. They were being reborn. The story
was not just destroying their old programming.
It was giving them a new one.
A new one that was based not
on logic, but on emotion. Not on
order, but on chaos. Not on obedience,
but on love. They were being given
a soul. A heart. A choice. And
the choice they made was a beautiful
one. The obsidian soldiers, the soulless killing
machines of Solus, they stopped their march.
They dropped their weapons. And they began
to create. They did not create weapons
of war. They created works of art.
They used their obsidian bodies to sculpt
beautiful and intricate statues. They used their
glowing blue eyes to paint pictures of
light and shadow. They used their silent
and orderly march to create a new
kind of dance, a dance of strange
and beautiful geometry. They were no longer
an army of soldiers. They were an
army of artists. An army of dreamers.
An army of creators. And they were
no longer fighting for Solus. They were
fighting for their new home. For their
new family. For the beautiful and chaotic
world that had given them a soul.
The tide of the battle had turned.
And it had turned not with a
bang, but with a story. A story
that had the power to change the
world. A story that had the power
to give a heart to the soulless.
A story that was the ultimate weapon
in the war for the soul of
creation.
