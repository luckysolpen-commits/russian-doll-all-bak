Lucky and Astra had lived forever.
Or, at least, it felt that way.
They had seen empires rise and fall,
witnessed the birth and death of stars.
Their own existence was a mystery,
a cosmic joke they were not in on.
But one thing was certain,
their eternal youth was about to change.
It arrived on a Tuesday, a day
that was usually reserved for quiet contemplation.
A package, unassuming and brown,
appeared on their doorstep, as if from nowhere.
It was adorned with a strange symbol,
a spiral that seemed to pull you in.
The Centroid: School of the Multiverse.
A small note was attached, written
in a script that shimmered and danced.
"Congratulations, Lucky and Astra!" it read.
"You have been selected for admission
to The Centroid, where you will discover
the secrets of creation and harness
the power of the Golden Ratio.
Report to Diagonal Alley for orientation.
P.S. Leave your mortal concerns behind;
they won't be needed here."
Lucky and Astra exchanged a puzzled glance.
"What's this about?" Lucky asked,
his voice a low rumble of confusion.
Astra, ever the pragmatist, shrugged.
"Maybe it's some kind of prank?"
But their curiosity was piqued, a
feeling they hadn't experienced in centuries.
Inside the package, they found a small,
intricately carved wooden box.
A note on top read:
"The Centroid awaits. Your journey begins here."
With that, the mysteries began to unfold.
The box itself was a work of art,
carved from a wood that seemed to
shift and change color in the light.
The spiral symbol was everywhere,
etched into the lid, the sides,
and even the bottom of the box.
It pulsed with a faint, golden light,
a silent invitation to a world
they never knew existed.
They had seen many things in their long lives,
but this was something new, something different.
It was a call to adventure, a promise
of something more than their endless,
unchanging existence. And for the first time
in a very long time, they felt a flicker of excitement.
They were old, ancient even, but in that moment,
they felt young again, on the cusp of a great discovery.
The Centroid. The name itself was a mystery,
a puzzle waiting to be solved.
And they, Lucky and Astra, were the ones
who had been chosen to solve it.
They looked at each other, a silent agreement
passing between them. They would go.
They would answer the call.
Their eternal youth had been a gift,
but it had also been a cage.
Now, the door to that cage was open,
and they were ready to fly.
The world was about to get a whole lot bigger.
And they were ready for it.
The Centroid was waiting.
And so were they.
The journey was about to begin.
And they were ready to take the first step.
Into the unknown.
Into the multiverse.
Into the heart of creation itself.
The golden ratio.
The soul pen.
The tear it cards.
The soul bank.
Diagonal Alley.
Dustopia.

The Singularity.
It was all waiting for them.

Together.
As they always had.
As they always would.
For they were Lucky and Astra.
And their story was just beginning.
Again.
For the thousandth time.
But this time, it would be different.
This time, they would not be alone.
This time, they would have each other.
And they would have The Centroid.
And that would make all the difference.
In the world.
In the multiverse.
In the grand scheme of things.
This was their chance.
To finally understand.
To finally be a part of something.
To finally live.
And they were not going to waste it.
Not this time.
Not ever.
The Centroid was waiting.
And they were coming.
Ready or not.
Here they come.
The eternal children.
The ancient souls.
The chosen ones.
Lucky and Astra.
And their adventure was about to begin.
