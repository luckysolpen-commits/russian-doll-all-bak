The sixteenth and final chapter of this
story is a chapter of endings and
beginnings. It is the end of the
epic battles, the cosmic crises, the world-saving
adventures. But it is the beginning of
a new and more profound kind of
story. A story of a life well-lived.
A story of a love that is
strong enough to embrace freedom. A story
of a friendship that is a testament
to the power of unity in diversity.
A story of a universe that has
found its balance, its harmony, its soul.
The final confrontation was not with a
monster, or a god, or a void.
It was with the simple and profound
challenge of what to do after you've
already saved the universe. And the answer,
the team discovered, was to live in
it. To embrace its beauty, its chaos,
its joy, its sorrow. To be not
just its saviors, but its students. Its
artists. Its lovers. Its friends. The legacy
of their story is not a monument,
or a philosophy, or a new and
better world. It is a new and
better way of being. A way of
being that is based on balance, on
harmony, on love, and on a deep
and abiding appreciation for the beautiful and
chaotic messiness of reality. It is a
way of being that is available to
all of us. Every day. In every
moment. The story of The Centroid is
not over. It is always beginning. It
is a story that is being written
and rewritten every day, by every creator
in the multiverse. A story that is
a testament to the power of art
to change the world. A story that
is, in the end, a love story.
A love story between something and nothing,
between light and shadow, between the symphony
and the rubber chicken. A love story
that is the heart of the universe.
And it is a love story that
will never end.
