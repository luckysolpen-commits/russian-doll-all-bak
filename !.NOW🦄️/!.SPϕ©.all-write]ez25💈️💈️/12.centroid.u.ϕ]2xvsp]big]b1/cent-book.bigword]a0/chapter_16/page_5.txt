And so, the story of our heroes
comes to a close. Or rather, it
finds its new and more sustainable beginning.
They had saved the universe, not once,
but three times. They had faced the
void, the ego, and the chaos, and
had emerged victorious. They had achieved a
level of power and wisdom that was
beyond the comprehension of most mortals. And
then, they had made the bravest choice
of all. They had chosen to be
ordinary. They had chosen to live a
life of quiet and contented creation, of
friendship, of love, of the simple joy
of being alive. They had chosen to
be happy. And in doing so, they
had become the greatest heroes of all.
For the ultimate act of creation is
not the creation of a universe, or
a masterpiece, or a new and better
world. It is the creation of a
happy and meaningful life. And that is
a masterpiece that is within the reach
of everyone. The final confrontation was not
a battle, but a choice. A choice
to embrace the beautiful and chaotic messiness
of reality. A choice to find the
joy in the ordinary. A choice to
live a life of purpose, of passion,
of a deep and abiding love for
all things. And it is a choice
that is available to all of us.
Every day. In every moment. The story
of Lucky and Astra and their strange
and beautiful family is not just a
story about saving the universe. It is
a story about how to live in
it. It is a story about the
power of art, of love, of friendship,
to heal, to transform, to make the
world a more beautiful, and a more
compassionate, place. And it is a story
that has no end. For the story
of a life, of a love, of
a friendship, it is a story that
is always being written. A story that
is always new. A story that is
always beautiful. A story that is, in
the end, the only story that truly
matters. The final chapter of this book
has been written. But the story of
The Centroid, and of the beautiful and
chaotic universe it inhabits, it goes on.
Forever. And ever. Amen.
