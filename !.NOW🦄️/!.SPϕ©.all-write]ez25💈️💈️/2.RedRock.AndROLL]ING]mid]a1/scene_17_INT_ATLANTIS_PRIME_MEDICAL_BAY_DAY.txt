**SCENE 17**

**INT. ATLANTIS PRIME - MEDICAL BAY - DAY**

SOUND of a heart monitor, the quiet hum of medical equipment

The medical bay is sterile and white. A team of UN SCIENTISTS are huddled around a holographic display, looking at the two mutagen samples.

<center>SCIENTIST 1</center>
> The regenerative properties are... astounding. It could regrow entire ecosystems in a matter of months.

<center>SCIENTIST 2</center>
> But the cellular decay... it's unstable. Without the proper bonding agent, it would cause catastrophic mutations.

One of the scientists, a younger, more observant one named DR. ARIS, zooms in on a section of the DNA strand.

<center>DR. ARIS</center>
> Wait a minute... what is that?

<center>SCIENTIST 1</center>
> What is it?

<center>DR. ARIS</center>
> There's a third element. A bio-marker. It's not human. And it's not Martian.

He isolates the bio-marker. It's a complex, alien-looking protein chain.

<center>SCIENTIST 2</center>
> I've never seen anything like it. Where did it come from?

Dr. Aris runs a cross-reference through the genetic database. The computer searches, its lights blinking.

After a moment, a match appears on the screen. It's a partial match, but it's unmistakable.

<center>DR. ARIS</center>
> My god...

<center>SCIENTIST 1</center>
> What?

Dr. Aris looks at his colleagues, his face pale.

<center>DR. ARIS</center>
> It's the Carcazods. The mutagen isn't just a cure. It's a control agent. It's made from them.

The scientists look at the screen in horror. The implications are staggering.

**FADE OUT.**
