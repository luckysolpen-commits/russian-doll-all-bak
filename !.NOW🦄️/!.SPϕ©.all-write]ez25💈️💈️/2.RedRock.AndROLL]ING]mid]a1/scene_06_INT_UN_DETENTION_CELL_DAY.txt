**SCENE 6**

**INT. UN DETENTION CELL - DAY**

SOUND of a low, sterile hum

The cell is small, white, and featureless. A single bench runs along one wall.

Lucky, Gem, and Astra are sitting on the bench. They have been here for hours.

<center>LUCKY</center>
> I hope they at least have a decent wine selection in the commissary. This is doing terrible things for my complexion.

<center>ASTRA</center>
> You're worried about your complexion? We're being held as potential bio-terrorists.

<center>GEM</center>
> They're not holding us. They're observing us. The pilot was carrying a message for the Queen. They want to know what we know.

<center>LUCKY</center>
> And what do we know?

<center>GEM</center>
> We know that Zara is making a move. We know the Queen is on Earth. And we know that whatever was on that ship was important enough to die for.

The door to the cell slides open. Director Vance stands there, flanked by two armed guards.

<center>VANCE</center>
> We've analyzed the pilot's logs. The message was a warning. Zara has accelerated her plans. She intends to frame the Queen for a full-scale invasion of Earth.

<center>LUCKY</center>
> And where is the Queen in all this?

<center>VANCE</center>
> She's here. In protective custody. She's been asking to see you.

<center>ASTRA</center>
> Us? Why?

<center>VANCE</center>
> She seems to think you can help her.

<center>GEM</center>
> We need to see her.

Vance studies them for a long moment.

<center>VANCE</center>
> The situation is... delicate. A war between the Martian factions could spill over to Earth. We need to control the narrative. We need to know who to trust.

<center>GEM</center>
> Then you need to let us talk to her.

Vance gives a curt nod to the guards.

<center>VANCE</center>
> Take them.

The guards escort the trio out of the cell.

**FADE OUT.**
