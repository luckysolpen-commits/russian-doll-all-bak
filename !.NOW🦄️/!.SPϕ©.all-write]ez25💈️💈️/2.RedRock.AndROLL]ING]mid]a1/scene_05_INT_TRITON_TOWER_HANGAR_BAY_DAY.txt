**SCENE 5**

**INT. TRITON TOWER - HANGAR BAY - DAY**

SOUND of alarms, hiss of hydraulics

The hangar bay is a flurry of activity. UN security forces in full gear surround a battered Martian transport ship. The ship is under quarantine, its ramp sealed.

LUCKY is there, looking annoyed, his business day interrupted. GEM is also present, observing the situation with a professional, calculating eye. ASTRA and her merc crew are nearby, drawn by the commotion.

<center>LUCKY</center>
> (to a UN officer)
> I have a shipment of bio-filters on that vessel. My entire quarterly projection depends on them.

<center>UN OFFICER</center>
> The vessel is under quarantine, Mr. Starr. No one and nothing gets off until we clear it.

<center>GEM</center>
> (to herself)
> Martian transport... bad timing.

Astra approaches Gem.

<center>ASTRA</center>
> You know something about this?

<center>GEM</center>
> I know that ship belongs to the Queen. And I know she's not on it.

Suddenly, the ship's ramp begins to lower. The UN soldiers raise their weapons.

A single, unarmed Martian pilot stumbles out, his face a mask of terror. He collapses on the ramp.

<center>PILOT</center>
> (gasping)
> A message... for the Queen... Zara... she's... she's...

He convulses and dies.

The UN officer turns to his team.

<center>UN OFFICER</center>
> Full bio-hazard protocol. Lock this entire sector down. No one leaves.

Lucky, Gem, and Astra exchange a look. They are all trapped in the hangar bay together.

<center>LUCKY</center>
> Well... this is inconvenient.

<center>ASTRA</center>
> Something big is happening.

<center>GEM</center>
> It already has.

They look at the dead pilot, then at the Martian ship, then at each other. The three of them, from different worlds, now linked by a single, unfolding crisis.

**FADE OUT.**
