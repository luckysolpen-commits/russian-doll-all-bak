The city’s defense systems, once the pride of the UN, were
-failing. The Carcazod, with its evolving intelligence, was
-finding weaknesses, exploiting vulnerabilities. It was a
-master tactician, a brilliant strategist. It was as if it
-knew their playbook, their every move.

The UN council, their faces pale with fear, watched the
-destruction on the main viewscreen. They were powerless to
-stop it. Their fleets were too far away, their weapons
-ineffective against this new, evolved threat.

They were trapped. They were facing annihilation. And they
-knew it.

In the midst of the chaos, a single voice of calm, of
-reason, emerged. It was Lucky.

“We can’t fight it,” he said, his voice low, but firm.
“Not like this. We need to think differently. We need to
-outsmart it.”

He turned to Zara, her face a mask of horror and disbelief.
“You created this thing,” he said, his voice cold, hard.
“Now, you’re going to help us to destroy it.”
