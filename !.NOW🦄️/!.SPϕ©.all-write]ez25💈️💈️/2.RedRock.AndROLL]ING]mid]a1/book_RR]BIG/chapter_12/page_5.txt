The UN scientists, their initial shock giving way to a
-desperate urgency, began to work with renewed fervor. They
-had to understand the bio-marker, had to find a way to
-counter the mutagen’s effects. The fate of Atlantis Prime,
and perhaps all of humanity, now rested on their ability to
-unravel the Architect’s terrifying plan.

Lucky looked at Gem and Astra. Their faces, usually so
-composed, were etched with a mixture of fear and grim
-determination. They had faced countless threats, but this
was different. This was an enemy that evolved, that learned,
that adapted. An enemy that had been unleashed by their own
-kind. The abyss was indeed gazing back, and it was far more
-intelligent, far more dangerous, than they had ever imagined.

They were no longer just fighting a war against a corporate
-tyrant. They were fighting a war against a god. A god who
-saw them as nothing more than insects, to be experimented
-on, to be perfected, to be destroyed.

And they were losing.
