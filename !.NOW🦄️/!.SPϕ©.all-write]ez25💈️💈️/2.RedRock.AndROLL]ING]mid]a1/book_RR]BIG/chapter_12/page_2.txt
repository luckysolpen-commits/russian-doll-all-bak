Lucky, Gem, and Astra stood by, listening to the scientists’
-increasingly frantic discussion. The implications were
-chilling. Zara wasn’t just trying to kill populations; she
was trying to enslave them. To turn them into puppets.

“And then there’s this,” Thorne continued, his finger
-hovering over a specific segment of the genetic code. “A
-hidden bio-marker. It’s… unlike anything I’ve ever seen.
Not human, not Martian. It’s… alien.”

Gem’s cybernetic eye whirred, processing the data. “Can you
-identify its origin?”

Thorne shook his head, his brow furrowed in concentration.
“No. It’s too complex, too… elegant. It’s almost as if it
was designed by a mind far superior to our own. But it’s
-definitely a signature. A unique biological fingerprint.”

Suddenly, an alarm blared, cutting through the tense
-atmosphere of the medical bay. A red light flashed on a
-nearby monitor, displaying a tactical map of Atlantis Prime.
A large, red blip, moving with alarming speed, was
-approaching the city’s perimeter.
