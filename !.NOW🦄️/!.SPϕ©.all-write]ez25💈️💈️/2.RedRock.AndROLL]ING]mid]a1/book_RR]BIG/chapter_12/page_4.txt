“The Architect,” Lucky breathed, the name a chilling whisper
in the suddenly silent medical bay. The shadowy figure, the
-unseen hand that had manipulated events from behind the
-scenes, the one who had sent him that cryptic message. The
Architect.

Outside, the city’s perimeter defenses, once thought
-impregnable, began to flicker. Energy shields wavered, their
-generators straining under an unseen assault. The Carcazod,
-larger and more intelligent than any seen before, was not
-just testing the defenses; it was dismantling them. It was
-learning. It was adapting. It was evolving.

The medical bay’s monitors flashed with images from the
-perimeter. The monstrous creature, its chitinous hide
-shimmering with an unnatural luminescence, moved with a
-terrifying grace. Its eyes, once thought to be mindless,
now held a chilling intelligence. It was no longer just a
-beast. It was a general. A vanguard.
