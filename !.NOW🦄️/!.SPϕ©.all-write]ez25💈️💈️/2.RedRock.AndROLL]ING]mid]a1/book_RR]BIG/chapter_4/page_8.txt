Days turned into weeks. The situation on Mars grew more
-dire. The ‘Vampire’ riots became more frequent, more violent.
Zara’s corporate forces, under the guise of ‘peacekeeping,’
-tightened their grip on the planet. The Queen was a prisoner
in her own palace, her authority all but gone.

She waited. She watched. She listened to the whispers of
her spies, the fragmented reports from her loyalists. She
-gathered her resources, what little she had left. She
-prepared for a war she knew she could not win on her own.

And then, one day, her advisor returned. He was pale,
-exhausted, but his eyes held a spark of triumph.

“I have found him,” he said, his voice a hoarse whisper.
“He is alive. And he is… not what we expected.”

The Queen leaned forward, her heart pounding in her chest.
“Where is he? What is he doing?”

“He is on Earth,” the advisor replied. “In Atlantis Prime.
He is a businessman. A very successful one. He calls himself
Lucky Starr.”

The Queen stared at him, a mixture of disbelief and dawning
-hope on her face. Lucky Starr. The renegade mercenary. The
-ghost from the shadows. A businessman. It was almost too
-perfect.

“He has a corporation,” the advisor continued. “Triton
-Biotech. It is at the forefront of deep-sea agriculture.
He is a respected member of the community. A pillar of the
-establishment.”

The Queen laughed, a genuine, heartfelt laugh that echoed
through the silent throne room. It was a sound of pure,
-unadulterated joy. Of course. Of course he was. It was
the perfect cover. The perfect disguise. The perfect way
to hide in plain sight.
