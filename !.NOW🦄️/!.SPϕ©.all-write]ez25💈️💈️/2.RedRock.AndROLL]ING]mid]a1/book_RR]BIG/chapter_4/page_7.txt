She summoned her most trusted advisor, an old, wizened man
who had served her family for generations. He was a man of
-secrets, a man who knew where all the bodies were buried.

“I need to find someone,” she said, her voice low, urgent.
“A man who doesn’t exist. A ghost.”

The advisor’s eyes, ancient and knowing, widened slightly.
“You speak of the one they called Starr.”

The Queen nodded. “He is our only hope.”

The advisor bowed his head. “It will be difficult. Dangerous.
But not impossible.”

He disappeared into the shadows, leaving the Queen alone
with her thoughts. She had made her choice. She had cast
the dice. And now, all she could do was wait, and pray
that she had not just unleashed a greater evil upon the
-universe.

She knew she was playing with fire. But sometimes, the
-only way to fight a fire is with a bigger fire. And Lucky
Starr was a conflagration waiting to happen.

She looked at her reflection in the polished surface of
the throne. She saw a woman who was no longer a queen, but
a gambler. A woman who was betting everything on a single,
-desperate hand.

And she smiled. For the first time in a long time, she
felt a flicker of hope. The game was not over. It had
just begun.
