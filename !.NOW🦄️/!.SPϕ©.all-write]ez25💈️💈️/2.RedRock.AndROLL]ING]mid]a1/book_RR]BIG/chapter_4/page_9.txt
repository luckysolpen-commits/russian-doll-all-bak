“Get me a ship,” the Queen said, her voice firm, resolute.
“A fast one. And a small, loyal crew. I am going to Earth.”

The advisor’s eyes widened in alarm. “Your Majesty, it is
-too dangerous. The UN will never allow it. You will be
-arrested. Or worse.”

“I am not going as a queen,” she replied, a cunning smile
-playing on her lips. “I am going as a desperate refugee,
-seeking asylum. I will be a pawn in their game. And then,
I will find my own game to play.”

She knew it was a reckless, desperate plan. But it was the
-only plan she had. She would go to Earth. She would find
Lucky Starr. And she would make him an offer he couldn’t
-refuse.

She would offer him a chance to be a hero again. A chance
to fight for something more than just credits. A chance to
-reclaim his own soul.

And she would offer him a chance to destroy Zara. The one
-enemy they both had in common. The one person who could
-unite them.

It was a gamble. A huge, dangerous gamble. But the Queen
was a gambler. And she was betting everything on the ghost
from the shadows.
