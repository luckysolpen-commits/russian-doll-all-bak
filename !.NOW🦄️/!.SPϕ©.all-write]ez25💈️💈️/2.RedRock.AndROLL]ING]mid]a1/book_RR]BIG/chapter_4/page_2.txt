“Your Majesty,” Zara’s voice, smooth as polished chrome,
dripped with a false, cloying politeness. “I trust your…
internal affairs are in order? The reports of ‘unrest’
among your… loyal subjects are quite concerning.”

The Queen’s jaw tightened. The words were a deliberate,
-calculated insult, a reminder of her own powerlessness.
“My affairs are precisely that, Zara. Mine. And my subjects
are loyal. Their… needs are being addressed.” The lie tasted
like ash in her mouth. Her “Vampire” guards, the genetically
-engineered clones whose very existence depended on a steady
-supply of synthesized blood, were growing restless. The
-supply lines from Earth had dwindled to a trickle, a
-deliberate act of economic warfare by Zara’s corporate
-empire.

“Needs?” Zara’s perfectly sculpted eyebrow arched. “Or
-demands? My intelligence suggests your ‘loyal subjects’ are
-quite… hungry. And hunger, as we both know, can lead to…
-regrettable actions.”

The Queen’s gaze flickered to the two hulking figures
-standing silently behind Zara’s projection. Her own former
-guards, now clad in Zara’s corporate livery. Traitors. The
-sight of them was a fresh wound, a physical pain in her
-chest. They had been her most trusted protectors, her
-shadows. And now, they served her enemy. It was a betrayal
that cut deeper than any knife.

She remembered their faces, their names. She had known them
-since they were created, had watched them grow, had trained
them herself. They had been more than just guards; they had
been her family. And now, they stood as silent symbols of
her failure, of her inability to protect her own.

She felt a wave of despair wash over her, so intense it
-almost made her gasp. She was losing. She was losing her
-people, her power, her legacy. And there was nothing she
could do to stop it.
