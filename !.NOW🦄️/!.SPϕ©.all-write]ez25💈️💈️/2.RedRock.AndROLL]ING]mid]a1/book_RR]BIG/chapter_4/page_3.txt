“You seek to destabilize my rule, Zara,” the Queen stated,
her voice devoid of emotion, a mask of regal composure that
she did not feel. “You always have. But Mars will not fall
to your corporate greed.”

“Greed?” Zara laughed, a brittle, humorless sound that
-grated on the Queen’s nerves. “I seek order, Your Majesty.
Stability. Something you seem incapable of providing. Your
‘Vampires’ are a liability, a ticking time bomb. They are a
threat to all of us, not just your precarious throne.”

“They are my people!” the Queen retorted, a flash of her
-old fire in her eyes. “They were created to serve, to
-protect. Their current… condition is a direct result of
your machinations. Your blockade. Your sabotage.”

“A convenient excuse,” Zara purred, her voice a silken
-threat. “The fact remains, their desperation is a problem.
A problem I am prepared to solve. Permanently, if necessary.”

The unspoken threat hung heavy in the air. A final solution.
A purge. The Queen knew Zara was capable of it. She had seen
the depths of her cruelty, the ruthlessness of her ambition.
She would not hesitate to exterminate the ‘Vampires’ if it
-suited her purposes.

The Queen’s own forces, depleted and demoralized, were
-barely holding the line against the growing unrest. The
‘Vampires’ were becoming more desperate, more violent. There
-had been riots, looting. The fragile peace on Mars was
-shattering.

She was trapped. Caught between her duty to her people and
the cold, hard reality of her situation. To fight Zara would
be to condemn her people to a bloody, unwinnable war. To
-surrender would be to betray everything her ancestors had
-fought for.

She felt a sense of suffocating claustrophobia. The throne
-room, once a symbol of her power, now felt like a tomb.
She was a queen in name only, a prisoner of her own legacy.
