But as she stood there, staring out at the desolate beauty
of her world, a new feeling began to stir within her. A
-flicker of something she had not felt in a long, long time.
Anger. A cold, hard, righteous anger.

She would not let Zara win. She would not let her legacy
be one of failure and surrender. She would fight. Not with
-armies and fleets, but with cunning and guile. She would
-play Zara at her own game.

She knew she needed a miracle. Or perhaps, something far
-more dangerous. She needed an ally, someone who understood
the true nature of power, someone willing to play a game
with stakes higher than any she had ever known.

Her eyes scanned the horizon, searching for a sign, any
-sign, that hope was not yet lost. But all she saw was the
-endless, unforgiving red of Mars, and the encroaching
-shadow of Zara’s ambition.

She thought of the stories her grandmother used to tell
her. Stories of the old world, of Earth. Stories of a
-renegade mercenary, a man who had been a legend in the
-shadows. A man who had disappeared, presumed dead, years
ago. A man named Lucky Starr.

It was a desperate thought, a fool’s hope. But it was the
-only hope she had. She would find him. She would offer
him a deal he couldn’t refuse. She would unleash a monster
to fight a monster.

She walked back to her throne, her steps now firm, resolute.
She was no longer a victim. She was a player. And she was
about to make her first move.
