“This isn’t about the past, Astra,” Gem said, her voice
-firm, insistent. “This is about the future. A future that
is in serious jeopardy. Zara is planning something. Something
-big. Something that could destroy us all.”

The name hung in the air between them, a cold, heavy weight.
Zara. The corporate tyrant. The monster who had been the
-architect of so much of their pain. The one enemy they
both despised with a passion.

Astra’s grip on her rifle tightened. “What about Zara?”

Gem quickly, concisely, laid it all out. The ‘Vampire’
-problem on Mars. The Red Queen’s weakening grip. Zara’s
-propaganda campaign. And the whispers of a new mutagen.
A biological weapon that could change everything.

Astra listened, her anger slowly being replaced by a cold,
hard fury. This was it. The real fight. The one she had
-been waiting for. A chance to take down the woman who had
-haunted her nightmares for years.

“And what makes you think we can stop her?” Astra asked,
her voice a low, dangerous whisper. “We’re a handful of
-mercenaries against a corporate empire.”

“Because we’re not alone,” Gem replied. “I’ve also contacted
Lucky.”

Astra felt a jolt, as if she had been struck by lightning.
Lucky. The name was a curse, a prayer, a ghost that had
-haunted her for years. The man she had loved, the man who
had betrayed her. The man she had thought she would never
see again.

“You what?” she whispered, her voice trembling with a mixture
of rage and disbelief.

“He has the resources, the connections,” Gem said, her voice
-soft, pleading. “We need him, Astra. We can’t do this
-without him.”

Astra stared at her, her mind reeling. To work with Lucky
-again… it was unthinkable. The wounds were too deep, the
-betrayal too raw. But the thought of Zara, of the mutagen…
it was a threat that could not be ignored.
