“The Martian factions are getting bolder,” Astra finally said,
her voice low, cutting through the grumbling of her crew.
Her words had an immediate effect. The room fell silent.
They all turned to look at her, their eyes filled with a
-renewed attention. She was their leader, and when she spoke,
they listened.

“The Red Queen’s ‘Vampire’ guards are a liability,” she
-continued, her eyes still fixed on the news feed. “And
Zara’s exploiting it. There’s a power vacuum, and someone’s
-going to fill it. And when they do, there’s going to be
a war. A real war. Not some back-alley skirmish over a
-few credits.”

“And it won’t be us, stuck here babysitting glorified space
-trucks,” Jax scoffed, though his voice held a new note of
-interest. “Unless, of course, someone decides to hire us
for a *real* job. Like, say, a little regime change on
Mars?” He looked at Astra, a glint in his eye. The thought
of real combat, of a worthy opponent, was a powerful lure.

Astra considered it. The thought of action, of putting her
-skills to use again, sent a jolt of adrenaline through her.
To be on the front lines again, to feel the familiar thrill
of the fight. It was a tempting thought. But the risks were
-immense. The UN wouldn’t tolerate any interference in Martian
-affairs, not with the delicate peace they had brokered. They
-would come down hard on any mercenary crew that got involved.

“Too hot,” she said, shaking her head. “Not yet, anyway.
We need to be smart about this. Wait for the right opportunity.
We go in now, we’ll be walking into a political minefield.
We’d have the UN and Zara’s corporate fleet on our backs.
The odds would be against us.”

“Opportunity? We’ll be collecting dust by then!” Kael
-exclaimed, his frustration boiling over. “The universe is
-moving on, Astra. And we’re stuck in neutral. We’re becoming
-relics, dinosaurs. I didn’t sign up to be a museum piece.”

His words stung, because they were true. They were becoming
-obsolete. The universe was changing, and they were being
-left behind. She felt a surge of anger, at the UN, at the
-peace, at her own inaction. She was a warrior, and she had
-forgotten how to fight.
