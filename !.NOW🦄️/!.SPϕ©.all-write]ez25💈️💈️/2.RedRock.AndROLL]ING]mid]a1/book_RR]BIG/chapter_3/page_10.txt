As they made their way to the rendezvous point with Lucky,
Astra’s mind was a whirlwind of conflicting thoughts and
-emotions. She was a soldier preparing for battle, her senses
-sharp, her focus absolute. But beneath the surface, she was
a woman preparing to face the ghosts of her past.

She thought about Lucky. The man who had taught her how to
-fight, how to survive. The man who had seen the warrior
-within her and had helped her to unleash it. The man who
had broken her heart.

She remembered their last conversation, the bitter words,
the accusations, the pain. She had sworn she would never
-forgive him. And yet, here she was, walking back into his
life, back into the fire.

She knew it was a mistake. A dangerous, reckless mistake.
But it was a mistake she had to make. The threat of Zara,
of the mutagen, was too great to ignore. And she would not,
-could not, stand by and do nothing.

She was a mercenary. A soldier of fortune. But she was also
a woman with a conscience. A woman who believed in fighting
for something more than just money. And this was a fight
-worth fighting.

She looked at her crew, at their determined faces, their
-eyes shining with a renewed sense of purpose. They were
-her family. Her pack. And she would do whatever it took
to protect them.

She took a deep breath, steeling herself for the confrontation
to come. She was ready. Ready to face Lucky. Ready to face
Zara. Ready to face whatever the universe threw at her.

She was Astra. And she was a warrior. And the universe was
about to be reminded of that fact.
