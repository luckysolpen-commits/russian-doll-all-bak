Suddenly, the comm unit on the table chirped, a secure,
-encrypted signal. It was a sound they hadn’t heard in
-months. Not the standard, open-channel hail of a potential
-client, but a tight-beam, military-grade transmission.
A ghost from their past.

Astra raised an eyebrow. This wasn’t a standard contract
-offer. This was something different. Something… interesting.
She walked to the table, her movements deliberate, her heart
-beating a little faster. She tapped the screen, and a
-distorted voice, familiar yet unplaceable, spoke.

“Astra. We have a situation. Mars. Urgent. Meet me at the
Triton Tower Hangar Bay. Discreetly. And bring your best.”

The line went dead. A heavy silence fell over the room.
The crew looked at each other, then at Astra, their faces
a mixture of confusion and dawning excitement. Triton Tower.
That was UN territory. The heart of the beast. And the voice…
it had a familiar cadence, a ghost of a memory.

“Who the hell was that?” Jax asked, his voice a low growl.

Astra didn’t answer. Her mind was racing. The voice… she
-knew it. It was from her past, from a life she had tried
to forget. A life of shadows and secrets, of missions that
-were never officially sanctioned. A life that had been far
-more profitable, and far more dangerous, than their current
-existence.

She looked at her crew, a slow, predatory smile spreading
-across her face. The kind of smile she hadn’t worn in a
-long, long time. The kind of smile that meant the hunt was
on.

“Looks like the universe just sent us an invitation,” she
-said, her voice a low, dangerous purr. She picked up her
-rifle, the weight of it a familiar, comforting presence.
“Pack your bags, boys. We’re going to Mars.”

Jax and Kael exchanged glances, their restlessness replaced
by a spark of anticipation. This was it. The chance they
-had been waiting for. The chance to be mercenaries again.
The chance to feel alive.
