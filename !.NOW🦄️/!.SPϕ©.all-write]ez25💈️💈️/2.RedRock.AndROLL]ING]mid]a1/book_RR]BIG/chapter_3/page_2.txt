“Another day, another dollar… not,” grumbled Jax, a hulking
-brute with a cybernetic arm and a perpetually sour expression.
He was a mountain of a man, his muscles straining against the
-confines of his worn, synth-leather jacket. He tossed a
-data-pad onto a dented metal table, the screen displaying a
-paltry contract for escorting a cargo freighter through a
-supposedly safe sector. “This is what we’ve been reduced to?
Glorified bouncers? I was built for war, not for babysitting
-space trucks.”

Astra didn’t respond, her gaze fixed on the flickering news
-feed on the communal screen. Martian unrest. The Red Queen’s
-grip weakening. Zara’s corporate forces making aggressive
-moves. It was a powder keg, and they were sitting on the
-sidelines, watching it burn. The frustration was a physical
-ache in her chest.

“Remember the good old days, Astra?” chimed in Kael, a wiry
-scout with eyes that missed nothing. He was perched on the
-arm of a couch, his movements quick, bird-like. “When we
-were actually *doing* something? When there were real stakes,
real fights? Remember that raid on the Cygnus X-1 outpost?
Now *that* was a job.”

She remembered. The adrenaline, the precision of a well-
-executed raid, the thrill of outsmarting an enemy. The
-feeling of her sniper rifle recoiling against her shoulder,
the satisfying hiss of a plasma bolt finding its mark. That
was living. This… this was just existing. A slow, agonizing
-decay of skills and spirit.

She thought of her crew. They were the best. Jax, the heavy
-weapons expert, a one-man army. Kael, the scout, the ghost
who could infiltrate any facility. And the others, a dozen
-more, each a specialist in their own right. They were a
-family, forged in the crucible of combat. And she was their
-leader. Their alpha. And she was failing them. She was
-letting them rot in this forgotten corner of the galaxy.

She felt the weight of their restlessness, their boredom,
their desperation. It was a heavy burden, a constant pressure.
She had to find them something. Something real. Something
-worthy of their skills. Before they all went crazy. Or worse,
before they started fighting each other.
