Lucky considered her words. It was a devil’s bargain, but
it was also their only play. Zara was a common enemy, a
threat to all of them. And the Queen, for all her flaws,
seemed genuinely desperate, genuinely willing to make a deal.

“What do you need us to do?” he asked, his voice firm.

The Queen’s eyes met his, a spark of hope rekindled within
them. “First, we need to understand the full scope of Zara’s
plan. The mutagen… its properties, its delivery method.
Then, we need to stop her. Before it’s too late.” She
-extended a hand, a gesture of trust. “Do we have a deal?”

Lucky looked at Gem and Astra. Gem gave a subtle nod. She
-had already calculated the odds, and this was their best
-shot. Astra, after a moment’s hesitation, shrugged. “Better
than sitting in a cell.”

Lucky took the Queen’s hand. “We have a deal, Your Majesty.
But understand this: we work on our terms. And if you betray
us, there will be consequences.”

The Queen’s grip was surprisingly strong. “Understood, Mr.
Starr. Now, let us begin. The fate of two worlds rests on
our shoulders.”

And so, the unlikely alliance was forged. A renegade
-mercenary, a disgraced queen, a hot-headed warrior, and a
-renegade clone. They were a dysfunctional family, a team
of misfits and outcasts. But they were united by a common
-enemy, a common purpose.
