Astra listened, her expression unreadable. When he was
-finished, she was silent for a long time. Then, she looked
at him, her eyes filled with a mixture of anger and pain.

“You were a coward,” she said, her voice a low whisper.
“You ran away. You left us to die.”

“I know,” he replied, his voice thick with emotion. “And I
have regretted it every day since.”

He saw a flicker of something in her eyes. A hint of the
-old affection, the old loyalty. It was a small thing, but
it was enough.

“We have a war to win,” she said, her voice firm, resolute.
“We can talk about the past later. Right now, we need to
-focus on the future.”

It was not forgiveness. But it was a start.

He found Gem in the command center, her fingers flying
-across the keyboard, her cybernetic eye glowing in the
-darkness. She didn’t look up as he entered.

“I know why you did it,” she said, her voice soft. “You
-thought you were protecting us.”

Lucky was stunned. “How did you know?”

“I’m a clone,” she replied, a faint smile on her lips.
“We’re designed to understand our creators. And you, Lucky,
are a very predictable creature.”

She turned to look at him, her eyes filled with a wisdom
-beyond her years. “I have already forgiven you. Now, you
-need to forgive yourself.”

And with those words, a weight was lifted from his soul.
The ghosts of his past were still there. But they no longer
had the power to haunt him.
