
Chapter 6: Detained Diplomacy & Unlikely Alliance

The UN detention cell was a study in minimalist discomfort. Polished durasteel
walls, a single, unyielding bench, and a faint, antiseptic scent that did little
to mask the underlying tension. Lucky, Gem, and Astra sat in silence, the echoes
of the Martian envoy’s desperate warning still ringing in their ears. A plague.
Zara’s twisted ambition. It was a nightmare unfolding in slow motion.

A UN guard, impassive and heavily armed, stood sentinel outside the transparent
force field that served as their cell door. Lucky tried to engage him, to glean
any information, but the guard remained stoic, a wall of official silence.

“This is a waste of time,” Astra finally grumbled, pacing the small confines.
“We should be out there, doing something. Not sitting here like common criminals.”

“We are common criminals, Astra,” Gem reminded her, her voice flat. “At least,
in the eyes of the UN. Unregistered mercenaries, a rogue biotech entrepreneur,
and a… well, a clone with a questionable past. Not exactly a shining example of
civic responsibility.”

Lucky sighed. “She’s right. Our best bet is to play by their rules, for now.
We need to see the Queen. She’s the key to understanding Zara’s true intentions.”

Hours crawled by, marked only by the changing shifts of the guards. Finally, the
force field shimmered and retracted. A different UN officer, this one with a
slightly less rigid posture, gestured for them to follow. “The Queen will see you.”

They were led through a labyrinth of sterile corridors, past heavily armed patrols,
until they arrived at a suite of rooms that, despite their temporary nature, exuded
an aura of regal authority. The Red Queen stood by a panoramic window, gazing out
at the shimmering cityscape of Atlantis Prime. Her crimson robes, though travel-worn,
still commanded respect. She turned as they entered, her eyes, once sharp and
commanding, now held a flicker of desperation.

“You are the ones who arrived on the *Red Dust*,” she stated, her voice surprisingly
soft. “The envoy… he spoke of a plague.”

Lucky stepped forward. “Your Majesty, Zara has weaponized the mutagen. She plans
to unleash it on Earth, framing you, and then seize control of both Martian and
Earth forces.”

The Queen’s face, already pale, seemed to drain of all color. “I… I had suspected
something was amiss. Her communications have been… evasive. But a plague… I never
imagined she would stoop so low.” She walked to a small table, pouring herself a
glass of water with a trembling hand. “My original plan… it was a desperate gamble.
A show of force, yes, but never… never this.”

“Your ‘Vampire’ guards are growing restless, Your Majesty,” Gem interjected.
“Ratchet, my contact, says they’re on the verge of open rebellion. Zara’s exploiting
their desperation.”

The Queen nodded, her gaze distant. “They are my children, in a way. Created to
serve, to protect. But the lack of resources… it has driven them to the brink.
Zara offers them a false hope, a promise of salvation that will only lead to their
destruction, and ours.” She turned back to them, her eyes hardening with resolve.
“I need your help. All of you. My forces on Mars are stretched thin. My loyalists
are fighting a losing battle. If Zara succeeds, not only will Earth fall, but Mars
will be enslaved.”

Astra scoffed. “And what’s in it for us, Your Majesty? We’re not exactly known
for our altruism.”

The Queen’s lips curved into a faint, bitter smile. “Survival. And perhaps… a
chance to reclaim what was lost. I have technology, knowledge, resources that
would be invaluable to Earth. If we stop Zara, if we stabilize my rule, I will
share it all. A true alliance. Not one built on fear, but on mutual respect and
the shared goal of survival.”

Lucky considered her words. It was a devil’s bargain, but it was also their only
play. Zara was a common enemy, a threat to all of them. And the Queen, for all
her flaws, seemed genuinely desperate, genuinely willing to make a deal.

“What do you need us to do?” he asked, his voice firm.

The Queen’s eyes met his, a spark of hope rekindled within them. “First, we need
to understand the full scope of Zara’s plan. The mutagen… its properties, its
delivery method. Then, we need to stop her. Before it’s too late.” She extended
a hand, a gesture of trust. “Do we have a deal?”

Lucky looked at Gem and Astra. Gem gave a subtle nod. Astra, after a moment’s
hesitation, shrugged. “Better than sitting in a cell.”

Lucky took the Queen’s hand. “We have a deal, Your Majesty. But understand this:
we work on our terms. And if you betray us, there will be consequences.”

The Queen’s grip was surprisingly strong. “Understood, Mr. Starr. Now, let us
begin. The fate of two worlds rests on our shoulders.”
