As they worked, the old dynamics of their team began to
-re-emerge. The easy camaraderie, the unspoken understanding,
the shared sense of purpose. It was like they had never
-been apart.

But the ghosts of their past were still there, lurking in
the shadows. The betrayal, the lies, the regrets. They were
wounds that had not yet healed. And they threatened to tear
the team apart before they even had a chance to fight.

Lucky knew he had to address the elephant in the room. He
had to talk to Astra, to Gem. He had to explain his choices,
to ask for their forgiveness. It was a conversation he had
-been dreading for years. But it was a conversation they
-needed to have.

He found Astra in the training room, her body a blur of
-motion as she sparred with one of the Martian soldiers.
She was a force of nature, a whirlwind of controlled violence.
He waited until she was finished, her chest heaving, her
-face flushed with exertion.

“We need to talk,” he said, his voice soft.

Astra’s eyes narrowed. “I have nothing to say to you.”

“Please,” he said, his voice filled with a genuine remorse.
“Just listen.”

And so, he told her everything. About the mission that had
-gone wrong, about the choices he had made, about the
-betrayal that had haunted him for years. He didn’t make
-excuses. He didn’t ask for forgiveness. He just told her
the truth.
