She thought of Lucky. The charming, ruthless entrepreneur with
a past he tried so hard to bury. He had the resources, the
-connections, the brilliant, strategic mind. He was also a
-master of deception, a man who could play Zara at her own
game. But could he be trusted? He was a man driven by his
own complex motivations, his own personal demons. He was a
-wild card, a dangerous ally.

Then she thought of Astra. The hot-headed, impulsive mercenary
with a fierce loyalty to her crew and a deep-seated hatred
of corporate tyrants like Zara. She was a blunt instrument,
a force of nature. But she was also a brilliant tactician,
a warrior who had survived countless battles. She would be
-eager for a fight, for a chance to make a real difference.
But her recklessness could be a liability.

They were a dysfunctional, volatile trio. A rogue biotech
-entrepreneur, a renegade clone with a state-of-the-art
-cybernetic eye, and a mercenary with a penchant for blowing
-things up. They were not the kind of people you sent on a
-diplomatic mission. They were the kind of people you sent
when all other options had failed.

And as Gem looked around the crowded, chaotic club, at the
-faces of the desperate and the damned, she knew, with a
-sinking feeling, that all other options had indeed failed.
The UN was too slow, too cautious. The Red Queen was too
-weak, too compromised. They were the only ones who could
-stop Zara. The only ones who were willing to break the
-rules, to fight fire with fire.

She made her decision. She stood up, leaving her half-finished
-drink on the bar. She moved through the crowd, a ghost in
the machine, her mind already racing, formulating a plan.
She would contact Lucky first. He would be the most difficult
to convince, the most resistant to getting involved. But she
-knew his weaknesses. She knew which buttons to push.

She stepped out of the club, the cool, recycled air of the
-city’s corridors a welcome relief from the humid, claustrophobic
-atmosphere of the Nova. The bass still throbbed in her ears,
a faint, distant echo of the chaos she was leaving behind.
But she knew that the real chaos was yet to come. The whispers
in the Nova had been a warning. A call to arms. And she had
-answered.
