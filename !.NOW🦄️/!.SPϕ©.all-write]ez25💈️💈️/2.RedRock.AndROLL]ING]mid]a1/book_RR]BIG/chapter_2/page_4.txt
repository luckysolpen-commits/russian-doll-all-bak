“And the Queen?” Gem pressed, her voice tight. “Is she really
that weak? I remember her as a formidable opponent.”

Ratchet let out a short, humorless laugh. “Formidable, yes.
But she’s a product of a different era. She believes in honor,
in loyalty. She’s playing by a set of rules that Zara doesn’t
even acknowledge. She’s trying to hold onto power, but her own
‘Vampire’ guards are a liability. They’re loyal, but they’re
hungry. And hunger makes people do… regrettable things.”

He paused, his gaze meeting hers. His organic eye was filled
with a weary sadness, the look of a man who had seen too many
of his own kind fall. “They’re dying, Gem. The blood supply
from Earth is a trickle. Zara’s corporate blockade is choking
them. The Queen is trying to find a synthetic substitute, but
it’s not happening fast enough. The ‘Vampires’ are getting
-desperate. There are whispers of… unsanctioned blood drives.
Of clones preying on other clones.”

Gem felt a wave of nausea. The image was horrifying. The very
-soldiers created to protect the Queen, now a threat to their
own people. It was a slow, agonizing decay, a society eating
itself from the inside out. And Zara was there, pouring salt
in the wounds, fanning the flames of desperation.

“There’s more,” Ratchet said, his voice dropping to a near-
-inaudible whisper. “There’s talk of a new mutagen. Something
Zara’s developing in a secret lab. Something that could change
-everything. Not just a cure for the blood dependency, but
-something… more. Something that could turn the clones into…
-something else.”

Gem’s mind raced, her cybernetic eye scrolling through data
-streams, searching for any mention of a new mutagen. Nothing.
Zara was keeping this one close to her chest. A mutagen. That
-sounded like Zara. Always pushing the boundaries of bio-
-engineering, always seeking an advantage, no matter the cost.
This was more than just political tension. This was a powder
-keg, and Zara was holding the match.

She knew, with a certainty that chilled her to the bone, that
this was the real threat. Not the starving clones, not the
-political maneuvering. It was the mutagen. A biological weapon
in the hands of a woman with no conscience. The possibilities
were terrifying. A plague that targeted specific genetic markers?
A way to control the clones, to turn them into an army of puppets?
The implications were staggering.
