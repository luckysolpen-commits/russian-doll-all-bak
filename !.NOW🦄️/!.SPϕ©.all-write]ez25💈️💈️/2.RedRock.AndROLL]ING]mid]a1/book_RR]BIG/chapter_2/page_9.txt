A soft chime from her terminal broke her concentration.
A reply. From Astra. It was a single word: “Where?”

Gem smiled. She had been right. Astra was in. She sent
-back a set of coordinates, a rendezvous point in a deserted
-cargo bay in the lower levels of the city. A place where
they could meet without being seen, without being heard.

Another chime. This one from Lucky. It was not a word,
but a question. A single, cryptic symbol. A test. To see
if she was who she claimed to be. She replied with the
-countersign, a code they had not used in years. A ghost
from a shared past.

A moment later, a new message appeared. “My office. One
hour.”

Gem leaned back in her chair, a sense of satisfaction
-washing over her. The pieces were in motion. The trio was
-reuniting. It was a dangerous, desperate gamble, but it
was the only one they had.

She thought about the last time they had worked together.
A disastrous mission on a remote mining colony. A job that
had gone sideways, that had ended in betrayal and bloodshed.
They had all gone their separate ways after that, their
-found-family shattered. The wounds were still there, buried
-beneath years of silence and regret.

Could they trust each other again? Could they put aside
their past, their personal demons, for the sake of a common
-cause? She didn’t know. But she knew that they were stronger
-together than they were apart. They were a force to be
-reckoned with, a combination of skills and talents that was
-greater than the sum of its parts.

She stood up, stretching her stiff muscles. It was time
to face the ghosts of her past. Time to meet with the man
who had been her commander, her mentor, and, for a brief
-time, something more. Time to see if the man she remembered
was still there, buried beneath the layers of wealth and
-respectability.

She walked to the window, looking out at the gleaming city.
It was a beautiful, fragile thing, a testament to humanity’s
-resilience. And it was in danger. The whispers in the Nova
had been a warning. A storm was coming. And she, along with
a rogue entrepreneur and a hot-headed mercenary, were the
-only ones who could stop it.
