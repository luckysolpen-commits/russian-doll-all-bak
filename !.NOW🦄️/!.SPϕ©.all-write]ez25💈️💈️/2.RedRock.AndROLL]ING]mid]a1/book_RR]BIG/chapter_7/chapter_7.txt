
Chapter 7: Playing the Game & Covert Operations

The Abyssal Biotech Lab, usually a sanctuary of sterile precision, now buzzed
with a different kind of energy. The Queen’s offer, a desperate plea for an
alliance, had been accepted. Now, the trio stood amidst holographic projections
of Martian topography and complex genetic sequences, discussing their next move.

“It’s a trap,” Astra stated, her arms crossed, her expression grim. “She’s using
us. We get her out of this mess, and then what? We’re disposable.”

“Perhaps,” Lucky conceded, adjusting a projection of a mutated DNA strand. “But
it’s a trap we can exploit. Zara is the immediate threat. The mutagen… that’s
the real danger. If we can get intel on her operations, on the mutagen itself,
we might be able to turn this around.”

Gem, her cybernetic eye whirring softly as she analyzed data, nodded. “He’s right.
Zara’s network is vast, but it’s not impenetrable. If we play along, we gain
access. We become part of the system, and then we can dismantle it from within.”

“So, we become spies?” Astra scoffed. “I prefer direct action. A well-placed
explosive, a strategic raid…”

“And get ourselves killed before we even know what we’re fighting?” Lucky countered.
“No. This is a different kind of war, Astra. Information is our weapon. Precision
is our strategy.”

Reluctantly, Astra agreed. The plan began to take shape. The Queen, desperate
for any advantage, granted them unprecedented access to her intelligence network
and resources. It was a risky gamble, but the alternative was unthinkable.

Gem, with Ratchet’s invaluable assistance, became the eyes and ears of their
operation. Ratchet, with his deep connections in the Martian underworld and his
uncanny ability to blend into any crowd, was a ghost. He moved through the shadows
of Martian cities, gathering whispers, intercepting encrypted communications,
and piecing together the fragmented puzzle of Zara’s movements. Gem, from the
relative safety of Atlantis Prime, used her advanced cybernetic implants to
process the raw data, cross-referencing it with UN intelligence, identifying
patterns, and pinpointing key targets.

Lucky, leveraging his legitimate business empire, began to track Zara’s supply
lines. He delved into the intricate web of corporate shell companies, offshore
accounts, and black market dealings that fueled Zara’s operations. He discovered
shipments of rare chemicals, specialized equipment, and even human resources
being funneled to clandestine labs on Mars. Each transaction, each anomaly,
was a breadcrumb leading them closer to the heart of Zara’s mutagen project.
He used his charm, his connections, and a healthy dose of corporate espionage
to extract information from unsuspecting executives and disgruntled employees.

Astra, meanwhile, found herself in an unfamiliar role: trainer. The Queen, in
an act of surprising trust, had assigned her a small squad of loyalist Martian
mercenaries. They were rough around the edges, fiercely loyal to their Queen,
but lacking the tactical finesse Astra had honed over years of combat. She pushed
them hard, drilling them in close-quarters combat, stealth infiltration, and
urban warfare. She taught them how to move like shadows, how to strike with
precision, and how to survive when the odds were stacked against them. It was
frustrating at times, but she saw the potential in their raw determination.
They would be her scalpel, ready to strike when the time was right.

Days bled into weeks. The trio worked tirelessly, their individual skills weaving
together into a formidable intelligence-gathering machine. They uncovered the
location of Zara’s primary mutagen production facility, a heavily fortified
complex hidden deep within a volcanic caldera on Mars. They identified key
personnel, their movements, and their vulnerabilities. They even managed to
intercept fragments of Zara’s internal communications, revealing her chilling
intentions.

Zara wasn’t just developing a mutagen; she was perfecting it. A bio-weapon
designed not to kill outright, but to transform. To control. To turn populations
into compliant, docile drones. And her target wasn’t just Earth. It was Mars.
She planned to use the mutagen to solidify her control over the Martian clones,
turning them into her personal army, and then unleash the perfected plague on
Earth, forcing humanity to submit to her will.

The stakes had never been higher. The game they were playing was far more
dangerous than any they had encountered before. But they were in it now,
deep. And as the pieces of Zara’s horrifying plan fell into place, the trio
knew one thing for certain: they had to stop her. No matter the cost.
