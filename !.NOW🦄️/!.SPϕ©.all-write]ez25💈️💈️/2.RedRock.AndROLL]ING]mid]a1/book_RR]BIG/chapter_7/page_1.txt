The Abyssal Biotech Lab, usually a sanctuary of sterile
-precision, now buzzed with a different kind of energy. It
was the energy of a war room, of a clandestine operation
in full swing. The Queen’s offer, a desperate plea for an
-alliance, had been accepted. Now, the trio stood amidst
-holographic projections of Martian topography and complex
-genetic sequences, discussing their next move.

“It’s a trap,” Astra stated, her arms crossed, her expression
-grim. She was a warrior, and she trusted her instincts. And
her instincts were screaming at her that they were walking
-into a trap. “She’s using us. We get her out of this mess,
and then what? We’re disposable.”

“Perhaps,” Lucky conceded, adjusting a projection of a
-mutated DNA strand. He was a gambler, and he knew that
-sometimes, you had to walk into a trap to get to the prize.
“But it’s a trap we can exploit. Zara is the immediate
-threat. The mutagen… that’s the real danger. If we can get
intel on her operations, on the mutagen itself, we might be
able to turn this around.”

Gem, her cybernetic eye whirring softly as she analyzed
-data, nodded. She was the strategist, the one who saw the
-patterns in the chaos. “He’s right. Zara’s network is vast,
but it’s not impenetrable. If we play along, we gain access.
We become part of the system, and then we can dismantle it
from within.”

“So, we become spies?” Astra scoffed. “I prefer direct
-action. A well-placed explosive, a strategic raid…”

“And get ourselves killed before we even know what we’re
-fighting?” Lucky countered. “No. This is a different kind
of war, Astra. Information is our weapon. Precision is our
-strategy.”
