Astra, at the controls of the *Starlight Serenade*, gritted
her teeth, her hands flying across the console. The ship
-bucked and shuddered as near misses sent shockwaves through
its hull. Warning lights flashed across the dashboard, a
-symphony of impending disaster. “They’re getting too close!”
she yelled, her voice strained.

“Evasive maneuvers, Astra!” Gem’s voice, calm and steady,
-came through the comms. “I’m plotting a course through the
-debris field. It’s risky, but it’s our only shot.”

Lucky, strapped into the co-pilot’s seat, clutched the
-small, heavily shielded casing containing the true mutagen.
It felt impossibly heavy, the weight of two worlds resting
in his hands. He watched the tactical display, the tiny
-green dot that represented the *Serenade* weaving a perilous
-path through a storm of red and blue enemy contacts.

“Incoming!” Astra shouted, yanking the stick hard to port.
A laser beam, thick as a tree trunk, lanced past their
-viewport, missing them by mere meters. The ship spun, its
-thrusters screaming in protest.
