The Martian skirmish continued to rage below, a fiery
-testament to the sacrifices made. And in the silent, deadly
-ballet of orbital chaos, the Queen’s fleet fought on, buying

Ratchet watched the rocket disappear into the clouds, a
-flicker of hope in his heart. He had done his part. He had
-given them a chance. Now, it was up to them.

He turned to his men, a grim smile on his face. “Alright,
you bastards,” he said, his voice filled with a newfound
-confidence. “Let’s give them hell.”

They fought with a renewed vigor, a suicidal bravery. They
-were no longer just fighting for their own survival. They
were fighting for a cause. They were fighting for a future.

They were the forgotten, the disposable, the dregs of
-society. But on this day, they were heroes.
