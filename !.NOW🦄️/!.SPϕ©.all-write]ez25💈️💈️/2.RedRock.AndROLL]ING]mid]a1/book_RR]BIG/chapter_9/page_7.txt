The Queen watched the battle from the bridge of her flagship,
her heart a cold, hard knot in her chest. She saw her ships
-explode, her people die. And she felt a surge of helpless
-rage.

This was her fault. Her ambition, her pride, her foolish
-gamble. It had all led to this. To this senseless slaughter.

She wanted to scream, to cry, to lash out. But she was a
-queen. And a queen did not show weakness.

She stood tall, her face a mask of calm, regal composure.
She gave her orders with a steady, unwavering voice. She was
the rock in the storm, the beacon of hope for her people.

But inside, she was crumbling. She was a woman watching her
-world burn. And there was nothing she could do to stop it.
