“We’re almost through!” Gem’s voice was a beacon of hope.
“Just a little further!”

On Mars, Ratchet watched as the last of his men secured the
-launchpad. The cargo rocket, a hastily prepared vessel
-carrying the unweaponized mutagen sample, stood ready.
“Launch it!” he roared, his voice hoarse from shouting.
“Now!”

The rocket ignited, a pillar of fire and smoke against the
-red Martian sky, slowly ascending into the chaos above. It
was a desperate gamble, a tiny needle in a haystack of
-destruction. But it was their only hope.

In orbit, the *Starlight Serenade* burst free of the debris
-field, a ghost in the machine, leaving the raging battle
-behind. Astra let out a shaky breath, her knuckles white on
the controls. “We made it,” she whispered, almost
-disbelievingly.

“Not yet,” Lucky said, his eyes fixed on the distant,
-pristine white dot that was the UN envoy ship. “The hardest
part is still ahead.”
