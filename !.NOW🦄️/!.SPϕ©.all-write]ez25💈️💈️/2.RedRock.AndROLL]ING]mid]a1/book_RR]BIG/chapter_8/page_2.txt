A holographic projection of the UN envoy ship, a pristine
-white vessel, appeared in the center of the room. “Our agents
within the UN council have already laid the groundwork. They
-will present our ‘evidence’ of the Queen’s treachery, her
-attempt to unleash a bio-weapon on Earth. The mutagen, once
-released, will confirm their fears. The Queen will be
-discredited, her influence shattered. And Earth, in its
-panic, will turn to us for salvation.”

“And the Carcazods, Madam?” one of her commanders ventured,
a hint of unease in his voice. “What of the real threat?”

Zara’s smile widened, a predatory glint in her eyes. “The
Carcazods are a useful distraction. A convenient scapegoat.
Once Earth is under our control, once the mutagen has done
its work, we will deal with the Carcazods. Or, perhaps, we
-will find a way to… integrate them into our new order.” The
-implication was clear: Zara saw even the monstrous Carcazods
as potential tools.

She was a master of manipulation, a player in a game where
-entire species were just pawns on a chessboard. She was a
-monster, a woman who would sacrifice entire populations for
her own twisted vision of order.
