The debate raged for hours. Zara’s propaganda, meticulously
-crafted and widely disseminated, clashed with the raw,
-undeniable evidence presented by the trio. The Red Queen,
-though still defensive of her initial invasion plans, found
-herself in the unusual position of being an unlikely ally,
her desperate pleas for justice resonating with some of the
-more empathetic council members.

Lucky, with his practiced charm and sharp intellect,
-systematically dismantled Zara’s arguments, exposing the
-inconsistencies in her narrative, highlighting the
-manipulation of data. Gem, with her cybernetic precision,
-provided irrefutable proof of Zara’s infiltration of UN
-systems. And Astra, with her blunt, no-nonsense approach,
-cut through the diplomatic niceties, laying bare the brutal
-reality of Zara’s ambition.

The council was torn. The weight of Zara’s corporate
-influence was immense, her propaganda deeply ingrained. But
the evidence presented by the trio, raw and unfiltered, was
-equally compelling. The image of the unweaponized mutagen,
-sitting innocently on the table, was a silent, powerful
-testament to Zara’s deception.

As the debate reached a fever pitch, a sudden, urgent
-message flashed across the main viewscreen. An emergency
-alert. A deep-space sensor anomaly. A Carcazod. Not just
any Carcazod. A massive, intelligent one. And it was heading
-directly for Atlantis Prime.

The room fell silent. The petty squabbles, the political
-maneuvering, all faded into insignificance in the face of
this new, terrifying threat. The Carcazods were supposed to
be contained, confined to the surface world. Their appearance
in deep space, heading for humanity’s last bastion, was an
-impossible, horrifying development.

Lucky’s eyes met Gem’s, then Astra’s. They knew. The bio-
-marker. The hidden component in the mutagen. It wasn’t just
a weapon. It was a key. A key to understanding the
Carcazods. And Zara, in her ruthless pursuit of power, had
-unwittingly revealed a far greater, far more ancient threat.
The Architect.
