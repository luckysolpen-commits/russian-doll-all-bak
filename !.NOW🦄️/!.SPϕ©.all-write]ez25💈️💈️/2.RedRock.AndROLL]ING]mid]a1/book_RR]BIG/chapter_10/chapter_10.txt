
Chapter 10: The Desperate Run

The *Starlight Serenade* was a blur of motion, a silver dart against the inky
blackness of space. Inside the cockpit, the air was thick with the scent of
burnt ozone and the adrenaline of a desperate gamble. Astra, her face a mask
of intense concentration, wrestled with the controls, coaxing every last ounce
of speed from the sleek vessel. The distant battle around Mars was a fading,
fiery nebula in their rearview, a testament to the sacrifices made to get them
this far.

“Approaching UN envoy ship, *Diplomat One*,” Gem’s voice, calm and precise,
cut through the hum of the engines. Her fingers danced across the navigation
console, a flurry of calculated movements. “They’re hailing us. Standard
identification protocols.”

Lucky, still clutching the heavily shielded container, felt a knot tighten in
his stomach. This was it. The moment of truth. The diplomatic standoff. He
looked at the container, the weight of its contents a tangible presence in his
hands. The fate of two worlds, perhaps even three, rested on this fragile
package.

“Patch them through, Gem,” Lucky said, his voice steady despite the tremor in
his hands. He took a deep breath, preparing for the inevitable interrogation.

The main viewscreen flickered, resolving into the stern, unyielding face of
UN Commander Eva Rostova. Her eyes, sharp and suspicious, immediately fixed
on Lucky. “This is *Diplomat One*. Identify yourselves. You are operating
without clearance in a restricted zone.”

“Commander Rostova, this is Lucky Starr,” he began, his voice smooth, practiced.
“We are on an urgent mission, authorized by the Red Queen of Mars. We carry
critical intelligence regarding Zara’s true intentions and the mutagen she
plans to unleash.”

Rostova’s expression remained impassive. “We have received intelligence regarding
a Martian bio-weapon, Mr. Starr. And it implicates the Red Queen.” Her gaze
darted to the container in his hands. “Is that it? The plague?”

“It is *a* mutagen, Commander,” Lucky corrected, his voice firm. “But not the
one Zara intends to deliver. This is the unweaponized sample, extracted from
her own labs. It proves her deception. The Queen has also sent a peace agreement,
a genuine offer of alliance against Zara’s aggression.”

“A likely story,” Rostova scoffed. “We have been warned of Martian deception.
And your presence here, a known mercenary and a… biotech entrepreneur with a
questionable past, does little to inspire confidence.”

“Commander, with all due respect, Zara’s propaganda is designed to sow distrust,”
Gem interjected, her voice cutting through the tension. “We have evidence, hard
data, that proves Zara’s agents have infiltrated your council. They are feeding
you false information, preparing you for a false flag operation.”

Rostova’s eyes narrowed. “Infiltrated? That’s a serious accusation, clone.”

“One we can prove,” Lucky said, stepping forward. “But we need to be on board.
We need to present our evidence directly to the council. Before Zara’s package
arrives.”

Astra, her eyes still glued to the external sensors, suddenly barked, “Commander,
we have an incoming vessel. High speed. Unidentified. Matching Zara’s signature
energy readings.”

Rostova’s face paled. “Zara’s ship? But… our intelligence indicated it was still
hours away.”

“That’s Zara for you,” Lucky said grimly. “Always ahead of the curve. She’s not
just sending a package, Commander. She’s sending a message. A very deadly one.”

The *Diplomat One*’s internal alarms began to blare, a shrill, piercing sound
that echoed through the cockpit. “They’re locking on!” Astra yelled. “She’s not
just delivering the mutagen, she’s going to fire it!”

“Open the bay doors, Commander!” Lucky pleaded, his voice urgent. “You have to
trust us! If Zara’s mutagen reaches your ship, it’s over. For all of us.”

Rostova hesitated, her gaze flicking between the incoming threat on her tactical
display and the desperate faces of the trio. The weight of her decision was
palpable. Trust a known mercenary, a renegade clone, and a former corporate
shark? Or risk the destruction of her ship and the potential annihilation of
Earth?

Finally, with a grim set to her jaw, she made her choice. “Open the bay doors!
And prepare for immediate docking. All security to the hangar bay! And someone
get me a direct line to the UN Council. Now!”

Astra expertly maneuvered the *Starlight Serenade* into the yawning maw of the
*Diplomat One*’s hangar bay. The ship shuddered as it docked, the clamps locking
into place with a resounding thud. As the ramp extended, Lucky, Gem, and Astra
stood ready, the container held firmly in Lucky’s grasp. They had made it this
far. The desperate run was over. But the real battle, the battle for truth and
the future of humanity, was just beginning.
