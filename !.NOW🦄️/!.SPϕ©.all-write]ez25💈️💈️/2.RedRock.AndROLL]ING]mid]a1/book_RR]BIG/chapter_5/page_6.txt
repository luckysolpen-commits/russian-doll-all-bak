The UN commander hesitated, her gaze flicking between Lucky,
the desperate Martian envoy, and the heavily armed guards
-surrounding them. She was a woman caught in an impossible
-situation, forced to make a decision that could have
-catastrophic consequences.

Lucky pressed his advantage. “Commander,” he said, his
-voice a low, conspiratorial whisper. “We are running out
of time. Every moment we waste here is a moment that Zara
-gets closer to her goal. You need to trust me.”

He saw the conflict in her eyes. Her training, her instincts,
-were telling her to follow protocol, to lock them all down,
to wait for orders from her superiors. But her gut, her
-intuition, was telling her that Lucky was right. That they
were in a race against time.

Finally, with a grim set to her jaw, she made her choice.
“Fine,” she said, her voice tight with a mixture of anger
and resignation. “You can speak with the envoy. But my
-guards will be present. And if you try anything, anything
at all, I will not hesitate to use lethal force.”

Lucky smiled, a genuine, heartfelt smile that did not
-quite reach his eyes. “Understood, Commander. You have my
word.”

It was a lie, of course. But it was a convincing one.

He turned to Astra and Gem, a silent message passing
-between them. The first hurdle was cleared. Now, the real
work began.

They were led to a small, sterile interrogation room. The
-Martian envoy, his face pale with exhaustion and fear, sat
-slumped in a chair. Lucky sat down opposite him, his
-expression a mask of calm, professional concern.

“Tell me everything,” he said, his voice soft, reassuring.
“From the beginning.”

And so, the story came pouring out. A story of betrayal,
of ambition, of a queen’s desperate gamble and a tyrant’s
-ruthless ambition. A story of a mutagen, a plague, and a
-plan to cleanse the universe.

Lucky listened, his mind racing, processing the information,
-formulating a plan. The pieces of the puzzle were falling
into place, and the picture they were forming was more
-terrifying than he could have imagined.
