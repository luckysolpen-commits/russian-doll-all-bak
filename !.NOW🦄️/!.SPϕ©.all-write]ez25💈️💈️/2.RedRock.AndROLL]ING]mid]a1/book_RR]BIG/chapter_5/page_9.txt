And so, the unlikely alliance was forged. A renegade
-mercenary, a disgraced queen, a hot-headed warrior, and a
-renegade clone. They were a dysfunctional family, a team
of misfits and outcasts. But they were united by a common
-enemy, a common purpose.

They set up a command center in a secure, off-the-books
-facility in the lower levels of Triton Tower. A place
-where they could work without being seen, without being
-heard. A place where they could plan their war.

Gem, with her cybernetic eye and her unparalleled hacking
-skills, became their eyes and ears. She delved deep into
Zara’s corporate network, intercepting communications,
-stealing data, building a profile of their enemy.

Astra, with her tactical genius and her combat experience,
became their sword. She trained a small, elite squad of
-loyalist Martian soldiers, turning them into a deadly,
-effective fighting force.

And Lucky… Lucky was the puppet master. The strategist.
The one who pulled the strings. He used his charm, his
-connections, his corporate empire, to manipulate events
from behind the scenes. He was a ghost in the machine, a
-shadowy figure who was always one step ahead of his enemies.

They were a formidable team. A force to be reckoned with.
But they were up against a powerful, ruthless enemy. An
-enemy who would stop at nothing to achieve her goals.

It was a race against time. A desperate gamble. A war
-fought in the shadows. And the fate of two worlds hung in
the balance.
