
Chapter 5: A Desperate Arrival

The Triton Tower Hangar Bay was a cavernous space, a symphony of industry and
controlled chaos. The air, usually thick with the tang of ozone and hydraulic
fluid, now carried a faint, unsettling metallic scent. Lucky Starr, his usual
composure a thin veneer over a growing unease, watched as the Martian transport
ship, the *Red Dust*, settled onto its landing pad with a hiss of compressed
air. Its hull, scarred and pitted, spoke of a long, arduous journey.

UN security forces, clad in their distinctive blue and silver armor, swarmed
the perimeter, their energy rifles held at the ready. A quarantine. That was
the official word. A routine health check for a vessel arriving from a known
contaminant zone. But the tension in the air was palpable, a silent alarm
ringing in Lucky’s gut.

He’d received an urgent, encrypted message, a summons from an unknown source,
instructing him to be here. The message had been brief, cryptic, and had hinted
at a situation far more dire than a simple quarantine. He scanned the faces in
the crowd, looking for any sign of the sender, but found only the usual mix of
hangar personnel and curious onlookers.

Then he saw her. Astra. Her mercenary gear, though toned down for a civilian
setting, was unmistakable. Her eyes, sharp and assessing, met his across the
bustling hangar. A flicker of recognition, then a subtle nod. She was here for
the same reason. And then, a few paces behind her, Gem. Her cybernetic eye
gleamed faintly in the hangar’s artificial light, her posture radiating a quiet
alertness. The trio, brought together by an unseen hand, stood on the precipice
of something significant.

A ramp extended from the *Red Dust*, and a small contingent of Martian guards,
the Red Queen’s elite, descended. They looked haggard, their crimson uniforms
dusted with red Martian soil, their faces etched with exhaustion and a desperate
urgency. Behind them, a single figure, cloaked and hooded, was carefully escorted
down the ramp.

“That’s the Queen’s envoy,” a voice whispered beside Lucky. He turned to see
Ratchet, his clone contact, his face grim. “Came in hot. No official clearance.
Something’s seriously wrong.”

The UN security commander, a stern-faced woman with a no-nonsense demeanor,
stepped forward. “Commander, your vessel is under quarantine. State your purpose.”

The Martian envoy, a tall, gaunt man with haunted eyes, pushed back his hood.
“A message for the Queen. Urgent. From Mars. Zara… Zara has accelerated the plan.
She’s twisted it. It’s no longer an invasion. It’s… a plague.” His voice was a
hoarse whisper, filled with a chilling dread.

The word hung in the air, a cold, heavy stone. Plague. Lucky felt a jolt of
recognition, a memory of the Carcazod plague that had ravaged the surface world.
This was different. This was man-made. Zara. The ruthless corporate warlord.

“What plan?” the UN commander demanded, her hand instinctively going to her sidearm.

“The Queen’s original plan… to reclaim Earth,” the envoy gasped, his eyes wide
with terror. “Zara… she’s weaponized the mutagen. She’s going to unleash it.
Not to conquer, but to… cleanse.”

Lucky’s mind reeled. The Queen’s invasion plan. A long-standing threat, but one
that had always seemed distant, a political maneuver. Now, Zara had taken it,
perverted it, turned it into something far more sinister. A plague. A cleansing.

He exchanged a quick, meaningful glance with Astra and Gem. Their faces mirrored
his own shock and dawning horror. They were no longer just curious onlookers.
They were involved. The *Red Dust* had brought more than just a message from Mars.
It had brought a new, terrifying chapter in humanity’s struggle for survival.

The UN commander, her face pale, barked orders into her comm unit. The hangar
bay doors began to seal, the heavy durasteel groaning into place. They were
trapped. Trapped with a desperate envoy, a terrifying message, and the chilling
realization that the cold war between factions was about to ignite into a full-
blown catastrophe. The gilded cage of Atlantis Prime was about to become a very
real prison.
