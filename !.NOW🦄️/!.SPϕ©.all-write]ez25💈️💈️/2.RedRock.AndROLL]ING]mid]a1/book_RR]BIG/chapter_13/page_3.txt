The realization hung heavy in the air. Humanity’s petty
-conflicts, the cold war between Earth and Mars, the corporate
-machinations of Zara – all of it had been a distraction, a
-prelude to a far greater threat. The Carcazods, once a
-mindless plague, were now a sentient, evolving force, guided
by an unseen hand.

Commander Rostova, her face etched with grim determination,
-slammed her fist on the table. “Enough! The time for blame
is over. We have a common enemy. An enemy that threatens all
life. Queen, Zara, Mr. Starr, Ms. Gem, Ms. Astra… we must
-unite. Now. Or we will all fall.”

It was an uneasy alliance, forged in the crucible of shared
-terror. The Red Queen, her pride swallowed, agreed to share
her remaining Martian forces and technology. Zara, her empire
-crumbling around her, offered her vast corporate resources
and her knowledge of the mutagen, however incomplete. Lucky,
Gem, and Astra, the unlikely heroes, found themselves at the
-forefront of a desperate, last-ditch effort to save humanity.
