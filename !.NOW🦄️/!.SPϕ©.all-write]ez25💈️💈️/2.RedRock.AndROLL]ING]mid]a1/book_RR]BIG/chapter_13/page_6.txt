He looked at the faces of his new allies, reflected in the
-darkened window of his office. The Red Queen, her eyes
-burning with a fierce, new determination. Zara, her face a
-mask of cold, calculating fury. Rostova, her expression a
-mixture of fear and resolve. Gem, her cybernetic eye glowing
with a quiet, focused intensity. Astra, her hand resting on
the hilt of her plasma rifle, her eyes scanning the darkness
-for a threat she could not yet see.

They were a strange, unlikely collection of heroes. A queen,
a tyrant, a soldier, a clone, a mercenary. They were enemies,
-rivals, lovers, friends. They were a dysfunctional family,
-forged in the crucible of a common threat.

And they were ready to fight.

“So,” Astra said, her voice breaking the silence. “What’s the
-plan?”

Lucky smiled, a genuine, heartfelt smile that reached his
-eyes. “The plan,” he said, “is to save the universe.”
