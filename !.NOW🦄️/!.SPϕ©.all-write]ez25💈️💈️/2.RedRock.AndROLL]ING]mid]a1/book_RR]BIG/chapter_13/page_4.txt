Hours later, in the quiet solitude of his Triton Tower office,
Lucky Starr stood by the panoramic window. The city lights of
Atlantis Prime, usually a comforting glow, now seemed fragile,
-vulnerable. The abyss outside, once a symbol of humanity’s
-safety, now felt like a gaping maw, ready to swallow them
-whole. The Carcazod was out there, evolving, learning,
-waiting.

He activated his private comm unit, a secure channel he
-rarely used. He stared at the blank screen, a sense of
-foreboding settling over him. He knew, deep down, that this
was just the beginning. The Architect had revealed itself,
not as a person, but as a force, a manipulator of life
-itself.

Then, a single word appeared on the screen, stark white
-against the blackness.

“Hello.”

Lucky’s grip tightened on the comm unit. The game had indeed
-changed. And the Architect was ready to play.
