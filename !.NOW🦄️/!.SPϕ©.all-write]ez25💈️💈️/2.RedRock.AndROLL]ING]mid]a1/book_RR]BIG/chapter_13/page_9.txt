Lucky looked at the faces of his allies, his friends, his
-family. He saw the fear, the exhaustion, the grim
-determination. But he also saw the hope, the courage, the
-unwavering resolve.

He knew that they would not all survive this war. There would
be losses. There would be sacrifices. There would be pain.

But he also knew that they would not give up. They would
-fight to the very end. They would fight for each other.
They would fight for a future where life was not just a
-game to be played by a capricious god.

He raised his glass, a silent toast to the fallen, to the
-living, to the ones who were yet to come.

“To the future,” he said, his voice a low, steady whisper.

And they all raised their glasses, a silent chorus of
-agreement.

To the future. Whatever it may hold.
