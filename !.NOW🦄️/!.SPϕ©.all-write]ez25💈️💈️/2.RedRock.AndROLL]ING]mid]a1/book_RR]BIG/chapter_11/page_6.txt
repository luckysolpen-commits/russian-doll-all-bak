The Architect. The name hung in the air, a chilling whisper
of a threat they did not yet understand. A threat that made
Zara’s ambitions seem petty, insignificant.

Zara’s face was a mask of confusion, of dawning horror. She
had been so focused on her own game, her own power plays,
that she had failed to see the bigger picture. She had been a
-pawn in a much larger game, a game played by a master she
-did not even know existed.

Her corporate empire, her political influence, her carefully
-crafted plans. It all meant nothing now. She was just another
-player on a board that was about to be wiped clean.

The council members, their faces pale with shock, looked at
-each other, their petty squabbles forgotten. They were united
-now, not by choice, but by a common, terrifying enemy.

Commander Rostova, her face grim, turned to Lucky. “What is
the Architect?”

“We don’t know,” Lucky replied, his voice low, serious. “But
we’re about to find out.”
