The UN envoy ship’s conference room was a sterile, brightly
-lit arena, designed for diplomacy, not confrontation. Yet,
the air crackled with an almost palpable tension. At the head
of the polished chrome table sat Commander Rostova, her face
a mask of weary resolve. To her right, the Red Queen, regal
-despite her circumstances, her crimson robes a stark contrast
to the room’s muted tones. To her left, Zara, impeccably
-dressed, her smile a predatory slash across her face, flanked
by her own contingent of corporate enforcers.

Lucky, Gem, and Astra stood before the table, the focus of
-all eyes. The heavily shielded container, now open, revealed
the unweaponized mutagen sample, a clear, viscous liquid in a
-reinforced vial. Beside it, the Queen’s peace agreement, a
-document of fragile hope.

“Commander Rostova, members of the UN Council,” Lucky began,
his voice resonating with a quiet authority. “We have brought
-you the truth. This is the mutagen Zara intends to unleash
-upon Earth, disguised as a bio-weapon from Mars. But this is
-not the weapon. This is the raw, uncorrupted sample, extracted
from Zara’s own laboratories.”
