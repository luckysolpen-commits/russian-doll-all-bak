With a sigh, he deactivated the photo, the image of his smiling,
grime-covered face dissolving into darkness. The memory was too
painful, too sharp a contrast to his current reality. He placed
the frame back in the drawer, burying it once again beneath a layer
of carefully constructed denial. Out of sight, out of mind. But the
image was burned into his memory, a constant reminder of the man
he had abandoned.

He drained the last of the whiskey, the harsh liquid doing little
to numb the ache in his soul. He walked back to the window, looking
out at the city he had helped create. The fragile bubble of
civilization in the crushing deep. The Carcazods were contained,
yes, but he knew, with a chilling certainty, that the real threat
was not the monsters without, but the monsters within. The greed,
the ambition, the lust for power that had driven humanity to the
brink of extinction once before, and would do so again.

He thought of the ‘gardener’ metaphor he so often used in his
speeches. He was the gardener, tending the fragile flower of
humanity in the abyss. But it was a lie. He was not a gardener;
he was a man who had planted a beautiful, but poisonous, flower,
and was now trying to convince everyone it was a rose. His ‘Abyssal
Bloom’ project, for all its potential, was just another tool, another
lever of power. A way to control the city’s food supply, and thus,
the city itself.

The weight of his lies, his past, and the future of this city
settled on him like the crushing pressure of the ocean outside.
He was sitting on a powder keg, pretending to be a gardener. But
he was not just sitting on it; he was the one who had lit the fuse.
And he knew, with a cold, sinking feeling, that it was only a matter
of time before it all blew up in his face.

The game he was playing was far from over. It was just beginning.
And as he stared out at the silent, beautiful, vulnerable city, he
wondered if he had what it took to win. Or if, in the end, he would
be consumed by the very demons he had sought to escape. The abyss
was indeed gazing back, and it had his face.
