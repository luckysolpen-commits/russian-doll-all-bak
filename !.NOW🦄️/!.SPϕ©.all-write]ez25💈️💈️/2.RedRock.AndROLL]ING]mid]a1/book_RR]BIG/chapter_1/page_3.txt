The hologram flickered, stabilizing into a life-sized projection
of UN Director Kaito. The man was impeccably dressed in a crisp,
gray uniform, his silver hair cut to regulation length. The lines
around his eyes spoke of long hours spent staring at data streams
and budget reports. He held himself with a rigid formality that
Lucky found both amusing and useful.

“Mr. Starr,” Kaito began, his voice a dry, official monotone.
“We are here to discuss your proposal for the ‘Abyssal Bloom’
initiative. I must confess, the projected yields are… ambitious.”
He stressed the word, loading it with skepticism. “The resource
allocation you’re requesting is substantial, exceeding the initial
budget for deep-sea agriculture by a considerable margin.”

Lucky offered a reassuring smile, a carefully crafted expression
of earnest enthusiasm. He leaned forward, his hands clasped on
the polished surface of his desk. “Director Kaito, thank you for
your time. I understand your concerns. ‘Ambitious’ is the perfect
word. But in these times, ambition is not a luxury; it is a
necessity. The methods we’re proposing are innovative, yes, but
they are based on sound, proven science.”

He subtly mirrored Kaito’s posture, a classic negotiation tactic
designed to build subconscious rapport. He kept his voice calm,
measured, the voice of a reasonable man presenting a reasonable
plan. “The current hydroponic farms are inefficient, Director.
They are a drain on our energy resources, and their yields are
plateauing. ‘Abyssal Bloom’ represents a paradigm shift. A self-
sustaining, energy-positive food source that will not only feed
Atlantis Prime, but also provide a surplus for… future expansion.”

Kaito’s expression remained impassive, but Lucky caught a flicker
of something in his eyes. Interest? Or just a bureaucrat’s
annoyance at having his routine disrupted? “Future expansion,
Mr. Starr? A bold sentiment, considering our current… situation.
The UN’s primary focus is on maintaining stability, not on
financing speculative ventures. We have regulations, protocols…
Your proposal seems to bend several of them.”

Lucky’s smile didn’t waver. “Innovation, Director, often requires
a certain… flexibility in the interpretation of existing protocols.
We are not bending the rules; we are creating a new framework.
A framework for survival.” He let the word hang in the air, a
subtle shift in the tone of the conversation. This was no longer
just about budgets and regulations. It was about something far
more primal.

He watched Kaito’s hand, a small, almost imperceptible movement
as he adjusted the collar of his uniform. A classic tell. The
man was uncomfortable. Good. Now the real performance could begin.
