INT. MOON BASE - TRAINING ROOM / RESEARCH LAB - DAY

SOUND of focused work, the hum of advanced machinery, occasional clangs

Months have passed. The Moon Base is a hive of activity. Lucky, now fully integrated, moves with purpose and authority. He’s overseeing the construction of massive, intricate devices using the SolPen, his movements precise and confident. Astra is by his side, her scientific mind invaluable in refining the designs.

<center>LUCKY</center>
> The energy conduits need to be reinforced. We can’t afford any power fluctuations when the Silent Shadow hits.

<center>ASTRA</center>
> Agreed. I’m running simulations on the optimal frequency for the counter-pulse. It’s a delicate balance; too much, and we risk destabilizing the lunar crust.

Clone Soldiers are everywhere, working tirelessly, their synchronized movements a testament to their efficiency. They are building, assembling, and testing components for the grand defense.

Despite the progress, a subtle undercurrent of doubt begins to surface. Lucky, in a rare moment of quiet, looks out at the Earth from a viewing port.

<center>LUCKY</center>
> Do you think they know, Astra? The people down there? What’s coming?

<center>ASTRA</center>
> (Softly) 
> Sol believes ignorance is bliss, for now. Panic would only hinder our efforts. Besides, what could they do? Their technology is centuries behind.

<center>LUCKY</center>
> It still feels… wrong. Like we’re playing God with their lives.

<center>ASTRA</center>
> We’re trying to save them, Lucky. That’s a different kind of playing God.

Meanwhile, GEM is seen observing Lucky and Astra from a distance. Her expression is still unreadable, but there’s a new intensity in her gaze. She’s making subtle adjustments to a small, handheld device, almost imperceptibly.

Later, Lucky is in a private chamber, reviewing schematics on his HALO HUD. He’s tired, the weight of the impending threat pressing down on him. He rubs his temples.

Suddenly, a soft BEEP from his HUD. An incoming, encrypted message. It’s from an unknown source, but the encryption signature is familiar – one he designed himself, years ago, for highly sensitive, off-the-books communications.

He opens it. A single line of text:

<center>MESSAGE (V.O.)</center>
> *He’s lying to you, Lucky. About everything.* 

Lucky’s eyes narrow. He looks around the empty chamber, a sudden chill running down his spine. He tries to trace the message, but it vanishes as quickly as it appeared, leaving no trace.

FADE OUT.
