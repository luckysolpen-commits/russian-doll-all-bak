EXT. LUCKY'S RANCH - DAY

SOUND of crickets, a gentle breeze, distant birdsong

Years later. The sprawling, rustic ranch is a stark contrast to the glittering casino. Rolling hills, ancient oak trees, and a clear, azure sky dominate the landscape. This is a place of quiet solitude, a deliberate escape.

LUCKY SHEPHERD (50s), his hair now streaked with grey at the temples, wears worn jeans and a comfortable, faded flannel shirt. He's tending to a small vegetable garden, his hands calloused, his movements deliberate and unhurried. The lines on his face tell a story of past battles, but his eyes hold a newfound peace.

ASTRA (40s), vibrant and energetic, approaches him, carrying two mugs of steaming coffee. She wears practical work clothes, her hair pulled back in a messy bun. She's not just a friend; she's a partner in this quiet life.

<center>ASTRA</center>
> Still checking on your empire, old man?

Lucky chuckles, wiping dirt from his hands.

<center>LUCKY</center>
> Just making sure the tomatoes are behaving. More reliable than quarterly reports, I'll tell you that.

He takes a mug, their fingers brushing. A comfortable silence settles between them.

<center>ASTRA</center>
> You know, you haven't looked at that thing in weeks.

She gestures subtly towards a small, sleek device on a nearby patio table – a high-tech monitor, a relic of his past, still capable of streaming live feeds from his casino.

<center>LUCKY</center>
> Old habits die hard. But honestly, I barely think about it anymore. This… this is real.

He gestures to the garden, the vast expanse of the ranch.

<center>ASTRA</center>
> Good. Because you deserve this, Lucky. All of it. Peace.

She sips her coffee, her gaze warm. Lucky looks at the device, then back at Astra, a decision forming in his eyes. He walks over to the table, picks up the monitor, and with a decisive click, switches it off. The screen goes black.

<center>LUCKY</center>
> There. Truly disconnected.

Astra smiles, a genuine, heartfelt expression.

<center>ASTRA</center>
> Welcome home, Lucky.

They stand together, watching the sun begin its slow descent, painting the sky in hues of orange and purple. The silence is profound, unbroken.

FADE TO BLACK.
