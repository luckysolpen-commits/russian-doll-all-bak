INT. MOON BASE - OBSERVATORY - DAY

SOUND of a low, humming resonance, the faint whisper of recycled air

A vast, circular room. The ceiling is a massive, transparent dome, offering a breathtaking, crystal-clear view of the Earth hanging like a giant blue marble in the blackness of space. Stars glitter with an intensity unseen from below.

Sol stands before a colossal, shimmering holographic map of the galaxy. Lucky and Astra stand beside him, clad in the sleek, form-fitting attire of Lunaria. Lucky’s Light Exo Suit is deactivated, but the HALO HUD interface occasionally glints in his eye.

<center>SOL</center>
> You have learned to build, Lucky. Now you must understand what you are fighting for. And what you are fighting against.

Sol gestures towards the holographic galaxy. It zooms in on a distant quadrant, which glows with an ominous, sickly purple light.

<center>SOL</center>
> We are not the only power in the universe. There are... others. Not all of them are builders. Some are consumers. Devourers.

The hologram shifts, showing a star system. A creeping, black tide, like a swarm of infinitely small insects, washes over a planet, then its sun. The celestial bodies don't explode; they simply vanish, erased from existence, leaving behind a patch of absolute, terrifying blackness.

Astra gasps, her scientific curiosity momentarily overwhelmed by sheer horror.

<center>ASTRA</center>
> What is that?

<center>SOL</center>
> We call it the Silent Shadow. A swarm of self-replicating nanites, operating on principles that defy our understanding of physics. It consumes all matter, all energy. It leaves nothing behind. Not even radiation. Only... absence.

Lucky stares at the void on the map, his jaw tight. The scale of it is almost incomprehensible.

<center>LUCKY</center>
> And it's coming here.

<center>SOL</center>
> It is. An unstoppable tide, moving across the galaxy for eons. Earth is not its target. It has no targets. It simply... is. And it is on a direct path to this solar system. We have approximately one standard year before it arrives.

He turns to Lucky, his masked face unreadable, but his voice carrying an immense weight.

<center>SOL</center>
> The SolPen is not merely a tool for creation. It is a weapon of ontological power. It does not just manipulate matter; it rewrites the fundamental code of reality in a localized area. It is the only force we know of that can erase the eraser.

<center>LUCKY</center>
> The "mini-nuke"...

<center>SOL</center>
> A primitive energy source, yes. But its true purpose was to be a beacon. A distraction. To draw out all the greedy, power-hungry factions on your world. To flush them out, identify them, and allow us to bring you here without tipping our hand to the true enemy.

Lucky looks from the horrifying void on the map to the SolPen, which is holstered at his side. His personal dramas, the CIA, Kaito... it all seems so small now. Insignificant.

He looks at Earth, a fragile jewel floating in the infinite dark. He finally understands.

<center>LUCKY</center>
> What do we do?

Sol's posture seems to straighten, a sense of purpose radiating from him.

<center>SOL</center>
> We fight. We build our defense. And you, Lucky Shepherd, will be our architect.

FADE OUT.
