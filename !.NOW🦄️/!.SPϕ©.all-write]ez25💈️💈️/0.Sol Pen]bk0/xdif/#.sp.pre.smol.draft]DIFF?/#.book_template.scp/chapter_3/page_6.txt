“Oh, I think you do,” Thompson purred, her voice a low, dangerous rumble. “These are your files, Lucky. The ones you thought were buried deep. Lost forever. But nothing is truly lost to us. Not when we have the resources to dig.” Her eyes, cold and unwavering, held his, a silent challenge. Lucky’s jaw tightened, a muscle twitching in his cheek. He recognized the files, the dates, the specific details. The secrets he’d worked so hard to erase, the digital breadcrumbs he’d meticulously swept away, were now laid bare. The leverage she held was immense, absolute.

“This is blackmail,” Lucky stated, the words a flat accusation, but even as he spoke them, he knew they were meaningless. He was caught.

“Call it what you will,” Thompson replied, a shrug of her shoulders that conveyed utter indifference. “We call it insurance. You’re a very special individual, Lucky. A genius, a visionary, a man who understands systems and how to exploit them. And we believe you can be of great service to us. We have a mission that requires your unique skills and expertise. A mission that only someone with your… particular talents… can accomplish.”
