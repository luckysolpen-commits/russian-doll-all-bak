Weeks bled into months within the sterile, yet strangely inspiring, confines of Lunaria’s training facilities. Lucky Shepherd, once a titan of earthly technology, was now a diligent student, his mind absorbing the intricacies of the SolPen with a voracious appetite. The initial awe had matured into a profound understanding, and with it, a growing sense of responsibility. He was no longer merely creating objects; he was shaping reality, bending the very fabric of existence to his will, and the power was intoxicating, yet humbling.

His days were a relentless cycle of practice and theoretical study. He began with increasingly complex objects, moving beyond simple geometric shapes to intricate gears that meshed with perfect precision, delicate machinery that hummed with unseen energy, and even small, functional drones that buzzed around the room, their miniature rotors whirring softly. He learned to manipulate existing matter, reshaping and reconfiguring it with a flick of the wrist, a mere thought. A pile of scrap metal could be transformed into a gleaming, functional tool; a broken circuit board could be re-woven into a new, more efficient design. The possibilities seemed endless, limited only by his imagination and the subtle, almost imperceptible, drain on the collective consciousness that fueled the device.
