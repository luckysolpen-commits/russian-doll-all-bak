They frisked Lucky with practiced efficiency, their hands moving over his tailored suit, searching for hidden weapons, for any sign of threat. He allowed it, his expression bored, as if this was a routine inconvenience. They found nothing. Their eyes, however, lingered on his briefcase.

“The briefcase stays,” Guard 1 grunted, his voice a low growl.

“Not a chance,” Lucky countered, a slight smirk playing on his lips. “What’s inside is for Kaito’s eyes only. Unless you want to explain to him why you delayed a potential multi-million dollar investment?” He knew Kaito’s reputation, his insatiable hunger for profit. The guards exchanged a look, a silent communication passing between them. They had clearly been briefed on Kaito’s priorities, and delaying a potential windfall was not an option. Reluctantly, Guard 1 nodded.

“Follow us.”

They led Lucky through a maze of dimly lit corridors, the air growing colder, the sounds of the city fading, replaced by the low hum of unseen machinery. This was the heart of the Shadow Syndicate, a hidden world beneath the bustling metropolis, a place where illicit deals were struck and fortunes were made and lost in the blink of an eye.

They arrived at a large, open chamber. Rows of computers hummed, their screens displaying complex data, lines of code scrolling endlessly, intricate networks mapping out global transactions. Technicians, pale and intense, tapped away at keyboards, their faces illuminated by the cold glow of the monitors. In the center, overseeing it all, was KAITO, a man in his forties, of sharp angles and colder eyes. He exuded an aura of quiet, dangerous power, a predator in his natural habitat.
