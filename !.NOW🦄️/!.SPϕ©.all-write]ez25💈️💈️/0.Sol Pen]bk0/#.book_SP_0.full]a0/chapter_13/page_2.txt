Meanwhile, Astra, with her insatiable scientific curiosity, delved deeper into the SSOL’s research lab and medical bay. These were not the sterile, impersonal labs she was accustomed to on Earth; they were teeming with life, with experiments, and with a chilling undercurrent of ethical ambiguity. Loona Twilight, ever present and seemingly omniscient, continued to be her guide, patiently answering Astra’s endless stream of questions, her knowledge of the SSOL’s inner workings seemingly limitless. Astra’s initial fascination with the ship’s advanced technology soon gave way to a growing unease as she uncovered the true nature of Sol’s biological advancements.

She discovered more about the cloning process, not just in theory, but in practice. Vats of nutrient-rich fluid bubbled gently, nurturing nascent forms, while advanced genetic sequencers hummed with quiet efficiency. The sheer scale of the operation was staggering, a testament to Sol’s ambition and his disregard for conventional morality. Astra learned about the “clone souls,” the very essence that fueled the SolPen. It wasn’t a metaphorical concept; it was a literal transfer of consciousness, a harvesting of nascent sentience to power a device of immense capability. The realization sent a chill down her spine. The clones, she realized, were not merely biological constructs; they were living batteries, their lives a means to an end, their individuality sacrificed for the greater good, as defined by Sol.
