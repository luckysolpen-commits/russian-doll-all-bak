Amidst the frantic preparations, a new horror unfolded. Gem, who had been working tirelessly on tactical analysis, was caught in a sudden, brutal attack. A rogue Carcazod, a grotesque mutation with razor-sharp claws and eyes that burned with malevolent intelligence, had somehow infiltrated their section of the SSOL. The creature moved with a terrifying speed, its chitinous body a blur of motion. Gem, despite her training, was caught off guard. The Carcazod lunged, its claws tearing through her Light Exo Suit, injecting her with a bio-weapon, a virulent strain of mutagenic toxins. She collapsed, her body wracked with convulsions, her skin beginning to ripple and distort, the horrifying transformation into a Carcazod already beginning.

Lucky, who had been nearby, reacted with lightning speed. He unleashed a concentrated blast from his SolPen, vaporizing the attacking Carcazod into shimmering dust. But the damage was done. Gem lay writhing, her life force ebbing, the mutagen rapidly consuming her. Astra, her face pale with shock, rushed to her side, her medical training kicking in. “She’s infected!” Astra cried, her voice strained. “The mutagen is spreading too fast! I can’t stop it here!”

Lucky’s mind raced. He had seen the horrors of the experimentation lab on Mars, the grotesque mutations, the agonizing transformations. He would not let that happen to Gem. He scooped her up, her body surprisingly light despite the exo-suit, and raced towards the SSOL’s medical bay. The journey was a blur of frantic motion, the low hum of the ship a terrifying counterpoint to Gem’s ragged breaths. He laid her on a diagnostic bed, its sterile surface a stark contrast to the vibrant chaos of her life.
