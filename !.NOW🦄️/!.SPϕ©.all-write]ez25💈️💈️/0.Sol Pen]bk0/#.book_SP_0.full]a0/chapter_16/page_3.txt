Astra worked with a desperate intensity, her fingers flying across holographic interfaces, analyzing Gem’s rapidly deteriorating biological systems. “Her heart… it’s failing!” Astra cried, her voice thick with despair. “The mutagen is attacking her cardiovascular system!”

Lucky’s gaze fell upon the SolPen in his hand. He remembered Sol’s words: “The SolPen draws its power from… unique sources. The collective consciousness of the clones. Their very essence.” He also remembered the woman in his vision, the one who had told him he needed to collect three souls to unlock the SolPen’s secrets. He had used the SolPen to absorb the life force of the Red Vipers, to fuel its power. Could he use it to *heal*? To reverse the effects of the mutagen? It was a desperate gamble, a leap of faith into the unknown.

He placed the SolPen over Gem’s chest, his mind focusing, not on destruction, but on creation, on healing, on the very essence of life. He visualized her heart, strong and vibrant, beating with a steady rhythm. He poured his will, his intent, his very being into the SolPen, drawing upon the vast, unseen energy that flowed through the ship, through the collective consciousness of the clones. A shimmering, golden light emanated from the SolPen, enveloping Gem’s body, pushing back against the encroaching darkness of the mutagen. Her convulsions subsided, her breathing steadied, and the grotesque distortions on her skin began to recede, replaced by the smooth, unblemished surface of her Light Exo Suit.
