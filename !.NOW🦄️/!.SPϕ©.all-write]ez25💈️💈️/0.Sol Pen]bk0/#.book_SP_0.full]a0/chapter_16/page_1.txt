The SSOL, a silent behemoth of advanced technology, cut through the void, its destination Earth. But the journey was far from a triumphant homecoming. News of the Carcazod and clown situation on Earth had reached them, a chilling cascade of reports that painted a grim picture of a world teetering on the brink of collapse. The US, once a bastion of global power, was buckling under the relentless force of the clowns, who were slowly but inexorably taking over the South American continent, Mexico, and now, with terrifying proximity, threatening to breach Texas. The reports were filled with images of once-vibrant cities transformed into grotesque carnivals of chaos, their streets swarming with painted faces and the monstrous forms of Carcazods.

Panic had gripped the lunar base. Everyone on Mars was in a desperate race, chasing the clowns to the space docks, a frantic effort to stop them before they could leave for Earth with more modified Carcazods and their next terrifying target: Africa. The implications were clear: if the clowns succeeded in establishing a foothold on another continent, the battle for Earth would be all but lost. The SSOL, once a symbol of hope, now felt like a desperate last resort, a fragile vessel carrying humanity’s last hope.
