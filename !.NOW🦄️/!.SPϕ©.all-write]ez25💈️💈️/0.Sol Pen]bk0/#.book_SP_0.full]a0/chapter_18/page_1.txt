The mini-ship, a sleek, silver dart against the bruised twilight sky, landed softly on the outskirts of New York City, its occupants gazing out at the familiar landscape with a mix of emotions. Lucky, Astra, Gem, Lauren, and her clones had been away for what felt like an eternity, but in reality, it was only a few short months. Yet, in that brief span, the world had irrevocably changed. The city, once a vibrant tapestry of human ambition, now bore the scars of the clown invasion, its iconic skyline punctuated by garish, oversized inflatables and the unsettling presence of Carcazods, their chitinous forms scuttling across rooftops and through the darkened streets.

As they stepped out onto solid ground, the air, thick with the metallic tang of ozone and the cloying sweetness of cotton candy, assaulted their senses. They were greeted not by cheering crowds, but by a sea of grim faces from the World Health Organization (WHO). The agency’s representatives, dressed in their crisp white coats, looked at them with a mixture of curiosity and suspicion, their eyes betraying the weariness of a world under siege. Dr. Patel, the lead WHO representative, a woman whose face was etched with the profound fatigue of countless sleepless nights, stepped forward. “Welcome back, Lucky,” she said with a nod, her voice raspy. “We’ve been expecting you. You have some… enlightening information to share with us, I presume?”

Lucky nodded, his eyes scanning the group before him, assessing their capabilities, their vulnerabilities. “We do indeed, Doctor. But first, we need to assess the situation on the ground. The reports we received on the SSOL were… disturbing. We need to see it for ourselves.” He knew that seeing was believing, and the true horror of the clown invasion could only be understood firsthand.
