Lucky’s face remained impassive, a mask he had perfected over decades of high-stakes negotiations. But internally, a flicker of genuine surprise, quickly suppressed, crossed his mind. She knew more than he thought. Far more. The Monaco incident, in particular, was a ghost he had believed he’d laid to rest years ago, a digital phantom erased from every server, every backup. How could she possibly know?

“I don’t know what you’re talking about,” Lucky said, his voice even, betraying nothing. It was a reflex, a practiced denial, but even as he spoke, he knew it was futile.

Thompson smiled, a humorless, predatory baring of teeth that sent a chill down Lucky’s spine. She turned the tablet to him, displaying a series of damning documents and photographs – irrefutable evidence of his past. Bank statements from offshore accounts he’d used to funnel illicit funds, corporate registries detailing shell companies he’d created to obscure his dealings, schematics of experimental tech he’d developed off-book, and finally, grainy surveillance footage from a high-stakes casino in Monaco, showing a younger, more reckless Lucky, a ghost in the machine, orchestrating a data heist that had nearly brought down a global financial institution. The images were clear, undeniable. His carefully constructed anonymity, his peaceful retirement, shattered in an instant.
