Suddenly, a sharp, authoritative *KNOCK* at the front door. It wasn’t a friendly knock, not the casual rap of a neighbor or a delivery person. It was a demand, a percussive assertion of presence that vibrated through the very foundations of the house. Lucky and Astra exchanged a wary glance, their eyes wide with a shared premonition.

“Who could that be?” Astra whispered, her hand instinctively reaching for Lucky’s arm.

Lucky rose, his movements slow, deliberate. Every instinct screamed at him to ignore it, to pretend they weren’t home, but he knew, with a chilling certainty, that whoever was on the other side wouldn’t be deterred. He moved to the door, Astra close behind him, her presence a silent, unwavering support.

He opened it to reveal AGENT THOMPSON, a woman in her forties, impeccably dressed in a sharp, charcoal grey suit that seemed to absorb all light. Her expression was unreadable, her features chiseled, and her eyes – cold and calculating – swept over Lucky with an unnerving intensity. Two equally stern-faced AGENTS, built like brick walls, stood behind her.

“Lucky Shepherd, I presume?” Thompson’s voice was crisp, devoid of warmth, a statement rather than a question.
