Hours later, the opulent private office within the Shadow Syndicate’s Tokyo headquarters felt like a world away from the bustling, neon-drenched streets outside. The distant city hum was a muffled whisper, and the low, industrial thrum of the building’s unseen machinery provided a constant, almost hypnotic backdrop to the clinking of ice in expensive whiskey glasses. Kaito, a man whose every movement exuded controlled power, poured two generous measures of amber liquid, the rich aroma filling the air.

“So, the CIA believes this device is a weapon,” Kaito mused, his voice a low, gravelly rumble, as he handed Lucky a glass. “A mini-nuke, perhaps?” His eyes, sharp and assessing, watched Lucky’s reaction.

Lucky took a sip, the burn of the whiskey a welcome distraction from the gnawing tension in his gut. “Something like that. They’re vague on the details, which tells me they’re either incompetent or hiding something. My bet’s on the latter.” He met Kaito’s gaze, a silent challenge passing between them. He had to convince Kaito that his interests aligned with the Syndicate’s, that he wasn’t merely a pawn in the CIA’s game.

“And you believe this ‘sensitive equipment’ is something else entirely,” Kaito prompted, a hint of intrigue in his tone. He was a man who appreciated a good mystery, especially one that promised profit.

“I believe it’s a piece of advanced technology, far beyond anything the CIA is letting on,” Lucky confirmed, his voice firm. “And I believe it’s connected to something much larger. Something that could change the world. Something they want to control, not just retrieve.” He was playing on Kaito’s ambition, his desire for ultimate power.
