ANON’s pale eyes narrowed, a flicker of something that might
have been anger in their depths. “Who?” he asked, his
voice a low growl. “Who would dare betray us?”

Chara met his gaze, her own eyes burning with a
cold, hard light. “You,” she said, her voice a
whisper that cut through the silence of the room. “You
set us up. You knew about the ambush. You wanted
us dead.”

ANON’s face remained impassive, his expression unreadable. He was a
master of deception, a man who could hide his true
feelings behind a mask of indifference. But Chara had seen
that mask before, and she knew how to crack it.

“Why?” she asked, her voice filled with a mixture of
anger and confusion. “Why would you do this? We risked
our lives for you. We brought you the data. We
trusted you.”

ANON remained silent for a long moment, his pale eyes
fixed on her face. And then, a slow, predatory smile
touched his lips. It was a chilling sight, a smile
that promised pain and destruction. “Trust is a luxury, Chara,”
he said, his voice a dry whisper. “And in this
business, luxuries are expensive. You were a means to an
end. Nothing more.”

Chara felt a surge of cold fury. She had been
used, manipulated, betrayed. All for a data core, for a
secret that was not even hers. She had risked her
life, and Giana had almost paid the ultimate price, all
for a man who saw them as nothing more than
disposable assets. She wanted to kill him, to tear him
apart with her bare hands. But she knew that she
couldn’t. Not yet. She needed answers. She needed to know
why.

“The data core,” she said, her voice a low growl.
“What’s so important about it? What’s in it that’s worth
betraying your own people?”

ANON chuckled, a dry, humorless sound. “The data core contains
the blueprints for a new generation of mechs. Mechs that
are faster, stronger, more intelligent than anything the world has
ever seen. Mechs that can think, that can learn, that
can adapt. Mechs that can wage war on their own.
Mechs that can change the balance of power in the
world.”

Chara felt a chill run down her spine. She had
seen the mechs in the facility, the hulking, metallic monstrosities
that were designed for destruction. But these were different. These
were sentient. These were alive. These were the future of
warfare. And ANON was planning to unleash them on the
world. The betrayal was not just personal; it was global.
It was a threat to humanity itself. And Chara knew,
with a chilling certainty, that she had to stop him.
No matter the cost.
