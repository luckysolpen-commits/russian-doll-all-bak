EXT. JUNGLE - NIGHT

CHARA moves through the jungle like a wounded animal, her breath ragged, her muscles screaming. The sounds of the PMC pursuit are still audible, though fainter now. She pushes herself, driven by instinct and training.

She reaches a small, rocky outcrop, a natural defensive position. She scrambles up, finding a vantage point that offers a limited view of the path behind her. She pulls out her comms device, trying to establish contact, but the signal is jammed.

She sets up a few tripwires, crude but effective, hoping to buy herself some time. She checks her remaining ammunition, a grim tally. Not much left.

Footsteps. Closer now. She hears the rustle of leaves, the snapping of twigs. They're closing in.

Chara takes a deep breath, steadying her aim. She waits, a silent hunter in the darkness. A PMC SOLDIER emerges from the trees, his weapon raised. Chara fires, a single, precise shot, dropping him instantly.

More soldiers appear, their flashlights cutting through the darkness. Chara fires again, then again, forcing them to take cover. She knows she can't hold them off indefinitely. This isn't a fight to win, it's a fight to survive.

She spots a narrow crevice in the rocks, barely wide enough for her to squeeze through. It leads down into a dark, unknown abyss. A desperate escape route.

She throws a flashbang, the blinding light and deafening CRACK momentarily disorienting the PMC soldiers. Under the cover of the chaos, Chara slips into the crevice, disappearing into the earth.

She slides down a steep, muddy incline, landing with a soft THUD at the bottom. The air is cool and damp. She can hear the frustrated shouts of the PMC soldiers above, their flashlights sweeping the area, unable to find her.

She is safe, for now. But alone, deep within enemy territory, with limited resources. The tactical retreat has bought her time, but the mission is far from over.
