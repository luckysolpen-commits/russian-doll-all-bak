EXT. MOTHRA BASE - NIGHT

The helipad of Mothra Base. The air is cool, carrying the scent of salt and distant rain. The rhythmic LAP of waves against the oil strut is the only sound. A helicopter, sleek and dark, waits, its rotors still.

CHARA stands with GIANA NGUYEN and AGENT KLONDIKE. The usual easy camaraderie is replaced by a quiet solemnity. This is goodbye.

<center>GIANA</center>
> So, this is it. Langley. Try not to get too bored with the paperwork.

Chara manages a weak smile. Her eyes, usually so sharp, are a little softer tonight.

<center>CHARA</center>
> I'll miss the jungle. And the company.

Klondike, ever the man of few words, steps forward. He extends a hand, and Chara takes it. His grip is firm, a silent promise of continued support.

<center>KLONDIKE</center>
> Stay safe, Chara. And don't forget to call.

<center>CHARA</center>
> You too, Klondike. Both of you.

She turns to Giana. Giana pulls her into a tight embrace, a rare display of affection from the usually reserved superior.

<center>GIANA</center>
> You did good, kid. Really good. Don't let them bury you in a cubicle.

<center>CHARA</center>
> I won't. And you... try not to get shot again.

They both chuckle, a fragile sound in the night. Chara pulls back, her eyes lingering on her friends. These are the people who had her back, who fought alongside her, who understood the silent language of their dangerous world.

She takes a deep breath, the weight of the moment settling on her. She turns and walks towards the helicopter, her silhouette stark against the dim lights of the base. She doesn't look back. It's harder that way.

Giana and Klondike watch her go, standing side-by-side, a silent vigil. The helicopter doors slide shut. The rotors begin to WHIRR, slowly at first, then building to a powerful roar.

The helicopter lifts off, ascending into the night sky, a dark shape disappearing into the vastness. Giana and Klondike remain on the helipad for a long moment, watching until the sound of the rotors fades completely. The oil strut feels a little emptier now.
