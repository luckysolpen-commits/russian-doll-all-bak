SCENE 20: INT. LANGLEY HEADQUARTERS - CARA'S OFFICE - DAY

SOUND of a sterile, almost oppressive silence, broken only by the faint hum of the building's HVAC system

INT. LANGLEY HEADQUARTERS - CARA'S OFFICE - DAY

The office is pristine, almost unnervingly so. The panoramic window offers a clear, unobstructed view of the city, but Cara sees none of its beauty. She sits at her desk, her posture rigid, her face pale and drawn. The framed photograph of Anon is gone, replaced by a generic landscape print. The tablet is dark, devoid of any cryptic messages.

Two stern-faced, impeccably dressed figures, a MAN (50s, sharp, unyielding) and a WOMAN (40s, cold, analytical), stand before her desk. They are high-ranking officials, faces Cara has seen in hushed corridors and on classified documents. Their presence alone speaks volumes.

MAN
(Voice devoid of warmth)
Director Cara. We trust you've had time to… reflect on your recent activities. Your unauthorized excursions. Your unsanctioned contacts.

Cara says nothing, her gaze fixed on a point beyond them, as if seeing through the walls, through the building, to a truth they can never comprehend.

WOMAN
(Her voice like ice)
Your position, Director, is contingent upon your unwavering loyalty to this agency, and to the security of this nation. Your recent actions have demonstrated a… concerning deviation from that principle. The information you have acquired, the individuals you have associated with… they pose a significant threat to our operations, and to the stability we strive to maintain.

Cara finally looks at them, her eyes hollow, but with a flicker of defiance.

CARA
Stability? You call this stability? A world built on lies, on manipulation, on suppressing the truth?

MAN
(Stepping closer, his voice dropping to a menacing whisper)
We understand your… idealism, Director. But the world is a complex place. Some truths are best left buried. For the greater good. For order. For control. You will cease all investigations into Project Nightingale. You will destroy all data, all contacts, all memories of your… recent experiences. And you will publicly denounce any claims of extraterrestrial involvement as baseless conspiracy theories. Your career, Director, depends on it. Your freedom, perhaps even your life.

Cara closes her eyes, a single tear tracing a path down her cheek. The weight of their words, the crushing reality of her situation, settles upon her. She thinks of Victor, of Ariana, of the impossible truths they revealed. And then, she thinks of Anon. His warnings. His unwavering belief in her. His love.

But the faces before her are unyielding. The threat is real. She has fought battles she couldn't win before. This one… this one would cost her everything.

CARA
(Voice barely a whisper, broken)
I understand.

The Man and Woman exchange a brief, satisfied glance. They turn to leave, their mission accomplished.

As the door clicks shut, Cara reaches for her secure phone. Her fingers tremble as she dials Anon's number, the sequence now a painful memory. The line rings once, twice, then a click. His modulated voice, so familiar, so comforting, answers.

ANON (V.O.)
(Voice modulated, calm)
Cara. I was beginning to worry.

CARA
(Voice choked with tears, barely audible)
Anon… it's over. I… I can't. They know. They're going to silence me. They're going to make me destroy everything. It's not worth it. None of it. I… I have to let you go.

A long, agonizing silence stretches between them. Cara can almost feel his presence, his pain, his understanding.

ANON (V.O.)
(A deep, sorrowful sigh)
Cara…

CARA
(Sobbing now, the words tumbling out)
I love you, Anon. I always will. But I can't… I can't risk you. I can't risk anyone else. This is goodbye.

She hears a faint, almost imperceptible sound on the other end, a sound that could be a gasp, a choked sob, or simply the static of a dying connection. Then, the line goes dead. The silence in the office is absolute, profound.

Cara stares at the phone, then slowly lowers it to her desk. Her hand lingers on the cold plastic, as if trying to hold onto the last vestiges of a connection that has been severed. She has chosen. She has sacrificed everything. Her integrity, her mission, her love. The truth remains buried, and she is left alone, in the sterile silence of her office, a ghost in her own life.

FADE TO BLACK.
