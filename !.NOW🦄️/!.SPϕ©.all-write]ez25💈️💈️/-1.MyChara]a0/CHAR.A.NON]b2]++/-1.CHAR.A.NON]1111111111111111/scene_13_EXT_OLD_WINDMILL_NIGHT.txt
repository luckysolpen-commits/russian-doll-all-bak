SCENE 13: EXT. OLD WINDMILL - NIGHT

SOUND of wind whistling, distant crickets, the faint creak of old wood

EXT. OLD WINDMILL - NIGHT

The night is still, almost unnaturally so, after the earlier downpour. A sliver of moon hangs in the inky sky, casting long, distorted shadows across a desolate, windswept field. In the distance, the silhouette of an ancient windmill stands stark against the horizon, its broken sails like skeletal arms reaching for the heavens.

Cara's government vehicle, now a familiar sight in the darkness, is parked discreetly a quarter-mile away, hidden by a cluster of gnarled oak trees. She sits inside, the engine off, the silence amplifying the frantic beat of her own heart. The data from LaGraine's lab, the horrifying truth of the global conspiracy, still swirls in her mind.

Her secure phone, which had been silent since LaGraine's last message, suddenly buzzes. It's an unknown number, encrypted, even more deeply cloaked than LaGraine's initial contact. Cara's hand hovers over the screen, a sense of foreboding washing over her. "Trust no one," Anon's voice echoes in her memory.

She answers, her voice a low, cautious whisper.

CARA
(Into phone)
This is Cara.

The voice on the other end is distorted, a strange, almost ethereal quality to it. It's neither male nor female, and carries an unsettling resonance.

DISTORTED VOICE (V.O.)
(Whispering, with an odd cadence)
Director. You seek answers. The truth you have uncovered… it is but a fragment. A single thread in a tapestry far older, far more complex. Come to the windmill. Come alone. The next piece awaits.

Cara's eyes narrow. This isn't LaGraine. The voice is different, the tone more ancient, more… alien. A cold shiver traces its way down her spine. She glances at the windmill, its dark form now seeming to pulse with an unseen energy.

CARA
(Firmly)
Who is this? How do you know about…

DISTORTED VOICE (V.O.)
(Cutting her off, a hint of impatience)
Questions will be answered. But only when you are here. Alone. The path is clear. The choice is yours.

The line goes dead. Cara stares at the phone, then at the windmill. Every instinct screams danger. This feels like a trap, a lure into something even more profound and terrifying than LaGraine's revelations.

She thinks of Anon, his warnings, his cryptic pronouncements. He had spoken of ancient forces, of secrets that did not wish to be disturbed. Is this what he meant? Is this the "unknown" he warned her to prepare for?

Despite the gnawing fear, a powerful surge of curiosity, of desperate need for answers, propels her forward. She has come too far, seen too much, to turn back now. The truth, no matter how unsettling, calls to her.

Cara grabs her compact tactical pistol from the glove compartment, checks the clip, and holsters it securely. She steps out of the car, the wind immediately whipping around her, carrying the faint, metallic scent of something unidentifiable. She begins her solitary walk towards the looming silhouette of the old windmill, a lone figure against the vast, silent night.

FADE OUT.
