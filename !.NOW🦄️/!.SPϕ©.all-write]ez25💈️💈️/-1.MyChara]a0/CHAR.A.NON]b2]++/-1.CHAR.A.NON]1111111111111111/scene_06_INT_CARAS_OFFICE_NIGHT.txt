SCENE 6: INT. CARA'S OFFICE - NIGHT

SOUND of the rain, now a steady drumming against the window

INT. CARA'S OFFICE - NIGHT

Hours have passed since Victor LaGraine's call. The rain outside has settled into a relentless rhythm, mirroring the turmoil in Cara's mind. Her office is a war room of information: screens display complex algorithms, encrypted data streams, and news feeds detailing the escalating cyber crisis. But her focus is solely on the single, encrypted file LaGraine sent.

She had run every possible decryption protocol, every known algorithm. Nothing. It was a dead end. Until she remembered a forgotten, almost archaic, decryption key she had developed years ago, a personal project, never officially sanctioned. A key designed for the most obscure, the most deeply buried secrets.

With a surge of adrenaline, she inputs the sequence. The screen flickers, then the file opens. It's a short video clip, grainy and distorted, but undeniably real. It shows a clandestine meeting in a dimly lit room. Figures in shadow, their faces obscured, but their voices clear. They are discussing the cyber attacks, not as a threat, but as a tool. A tool to manipulate, to control, to divert attention.

And then, a single, chilling phrase from one of the shadowy figures:

SHADOWY FIGURE 1 (V.O.)
(Distorted, but clear)
…the Project Nightingale data will be the perfect smokescreen. They'll be too busy chasing ghosts to see the real operation.

Cara gasps, her hand flying to her mouth. It's not just a data breach. It's a deliberate, calculated deception. The UFO connection, the cyber attacks – all a diversion. But for what?

Just as the video ends, a new message flashes on her screen. It's from LaGraine.

VICTOR LAGRAINE (O.S.)
(Text on screen)
"Convinced, Director? The truth is rarely convenient. Meet me at the old warehouse on 5th and Main at midnight. Come alone. We have much to discuss."

Cara stares at the message, her heart pounding. Every fiber of her being screams danger. This man is playing a game, a dangerous one, and she's just been drawn into it. But the video… the chilling confirmation of a deeper conspiracy… she can't ignore it.

She glances at the framed photograph of Anon. "Trust no one," he had said. But what if trusting no one means letting the real perpetrators get away? What if LaGraine, for all his unsettling methods, is the only one who can lead her to the truth?

She closes her laptop, the decision made. The rain continues its relentless drumming, a soundtrack to the perilous path she's about to embark on.

FADE OUT.
