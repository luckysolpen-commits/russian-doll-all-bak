Against her better judgment, against every rule in the book, against the silent warnings echoing in her mind from Anon, Cara agreed to a meeting. It was a reckless, dangerous move, a gamble with stakes she couldn't yet fully comprehend, but the allure of the truth was a siren song she couldn't resist. "I want proof," she said, her voice firm, a desperate attempt to regain some semblance of control. "Proof that you have what you say you have. Something tangible, Mr. Lagraine. Something that can't be faked."

A moment later, her phone buzzed with an incoming text message. It wasn't a file, or a link, or a photo. It was a single sentence, stark and chilling, a cryptic invitation to a dance with the devil: "Meet me at the old warehouse on 5th and Main at midnight. Come alone. And bring your curiosity, Director. You'll need it."

The message vanished as quickly as it appeared, leaving Cara with a cold dread that settled deep in her bones. The anonymity of the text, the precise location, the chilling instruction to come alone – it all screamed danger. Yet, a strange, almost perverse excitement flickered within her. She was stepping into the unknown, into a world where the rules she knew no longer applied. And for a woman who had always thrived on control, the prospect was both terrifying and exhilarating.
