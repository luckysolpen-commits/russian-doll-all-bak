Victor's story was a dizzying descent into a rabbit hole of paranoia and conspiracy, yet delivered with such conviction, such raw, unbridled passion, that Cara found herself believing every impossible word. He spoke of a secret cabal within the government, a shadow organization so deeply entrenched, so powerful, that their tendrils reached into every corner of power, manipulating world events for decades. He claimed to have been one of them, a high-ranking operative in their clandestine army, privy to their darkest secrets. But he had seen too much, learned too much, witnessed atrocities committed in the name of 'national security' that had shattered his faith. He had seen the truth, and it had broken him, transforming him from a loyal servant into a rogue agent, a truth-seeker.

"They're not just hiding the truth about UFOs, Cara," he said, his voice trembling with a mixture of fear and rage, his hands clenching into fists. "They're hiding the truth about everything. About who we are, where we come from, and what we're truly capable of. They've been lying to us for centuries, feeding us carefully curated narratives, keeping us in the dark, controlling our evolution. They fear our potential, our true heritage. And I, for one, am tired of it. I'm tired of the lies, the manipulation, the endless suppression of knowledge. It's time for humanity to wake up."

He paced the small lab, his shadow dancing wildly in the pulsating light of the Luminis Fragment. "They call themselves the 'Guardians of the Veil.' A self-appointed elite, deciding what humanity is ready for. But they are not guardians, Cara. They are jailers. And I intend to break us free."
