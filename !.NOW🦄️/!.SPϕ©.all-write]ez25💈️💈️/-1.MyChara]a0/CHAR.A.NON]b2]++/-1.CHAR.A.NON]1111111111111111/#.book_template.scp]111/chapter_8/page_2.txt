At the very center of the lab, bathed in the soft, pulsating glow of the crystals, lay a piece of metal on a makeshift altar. It wasn't just metal; it was a living thing, impossibly light, impossibly strong, and it hummed with a faint, internal energy that seemed to resonate with the very core of her being, a silent song that vibrated in her teeth. It was, Ariana claimed, a fragment from an extraterrestrial craft, a gift from the stars, a piece of the 'Luminis Fragment' Victor had spoken of, but far more potent, far more alive. Its surface shimmered with iridescent colors, shifting and swirling like liquid light, and strange, intricate glyphs, alien yet somehow familiar, pulsed with a soft, inner luminescence.

Cara stared at it, her mind reeling, her world turned upside down. Was this a lab, or a shrine? A scientific discovery, or the heart of a cult that worshipped beings from another world? The questions swirled in her mind, a dizzying vortex of confusion and wonder, battling with the ingrained skepticism of her training. The air grew thick, the hum of the lab intensifying, and a strange, almost hypnotic warmth spread through her limbs. As she reached out, her fingers trembling, to touch the strange, pulsating metal, a wave of pure, unadulterated energy washed over her, a torrent of images and sensations flooding her mind. Her vision tunneled, the vibrant colors of the lab blurring into an indistinct swirl, and the world around her faded to black, consumed by the overwhelming influx of alien knowledge.
