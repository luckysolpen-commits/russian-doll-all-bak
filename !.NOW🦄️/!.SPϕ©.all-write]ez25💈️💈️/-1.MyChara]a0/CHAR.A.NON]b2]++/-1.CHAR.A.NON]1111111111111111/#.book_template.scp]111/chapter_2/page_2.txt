The next morning, the sterile hum of the CIA building felt oppressive. Thompson stood in her office, his face pale, a thick, classified file clutched in his hand as if it were a lifeline. "You're not going to believe this, Director," he said, his voice barely a whisper, his eyes wide with a mixture of fear and awe. "The research project...the one all the hacked corporations were involved in...it was a front. A cover for something much bigger, something… otherworldly."

He opened the file, and Cara's blood ran cold. It wasn't just reports and eyewitness accounts. There were grainy photographs, not of blurry lights in the sky, but of impossible craft, sleek and silent, defying every known law of physics. There were redacted transcripts of hushed conversations, cryptic diagrams, and even what appeared to be schematics for propulsion systems that looked like they belonged in a science fiction novel. UFOs. The stuff of conspiracy theories and late-night talk shows, now laid bare in her hands as cold, hard fact.

"This is insane," she breathed, her skepticism battling with the undeniable, terrifying evidence in front of her. Her mind, trained for logic and reason, struggled to reconcile the impossible.

"Is it?" Thompson countered, his eyes dark with a truth he had clearly already accepted, a truth that had clearly shaken him to his core. "The data doesn't lie, Cara. And the data is telling us that something is out there. Something that has been here for a very long time. Something the government has been hiding, not just from the public, but from its own agencies, for decades." He paused, letting the weight of his words sink in, letting the silence fill with the unspoken implications. "I think it's time we stopped calling it the 'UFO Connection' and started calling it what it is: the truth."

Cara stared at the file, the images of impossible craft burned into her mind. Anon's warning echoed in her ears. *They will use you. They will destroy you.* She was already in too deep to turn back.
