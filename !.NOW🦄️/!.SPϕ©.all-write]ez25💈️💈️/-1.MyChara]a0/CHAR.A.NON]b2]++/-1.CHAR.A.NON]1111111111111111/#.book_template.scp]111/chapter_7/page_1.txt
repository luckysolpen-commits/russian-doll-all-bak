The call came a few nights later, a distorted, garbled voice on the other end of the line, a whisper that seemed to crawl under her skin and settle in her bones. It was a voice that spoke of ancient secrets and forgotten truths, of a destiny she couldn't escape. It was a voice that summoned her to an old windmill on the outskirts of town, a place she hadn't thought of since childhood, a place shrouded in local legends of strange lights and disappearances. "Come alone," the voice had whispered, a silken command, and then the line went dead, leaving behind an unsettling silence.

Victor had pleaded with her not to go, his face etched with worry, his usual bravado replaced by a raw fear. "It's a trap, Cara!" he had insisted, pacing their makeshift lab like a caged animal. "They know we're onto them. They're trying to lure you into a trap, to neutralize you before you can expose them." His words were logical, rational, everything her training screamed at her to heed. But the voice, that strange, compelling voice, had spoken to a part of her that she had long suppressed, a part that yearned for answers, for a truth that lay beyond the veil of the ordinary world. A truth that even Victor, with all his knowledge, couldn't provide. She had to go. She had to know. The pull was irresistible, a cosmic current dragging her towards an unknown shore.
