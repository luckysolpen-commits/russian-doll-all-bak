Ariana was waiting for her under the sprawling branches of the old oak tree, a sentinel of ancient wisdom in the heart of the bustling town square. Her eyes, which Cara remembered as a warm, welcoming brown, now glowed with an unnatural, golden light, like embers from a hidden fire. Her words were a torrent of cryptic pronouncements, a river of prophecies and portents that flowed around Cara like a dream, bypassing her conscious mind and settling deep within her subconscious. She spoke of a new era for humanity, of a great awakening, of a truth that would shatter the illusions of the world and usher in a new age of enlightenment. And as she spoke, Cara felt a strange sense of peace settle over her, a warm, inviting glow that seemed to emanate from Ariana herself, a comforting embrace that promised an end to all her struggles. It was a feeling of surrender, of letting go, of dissolving into something larger and more beautiful than herself. She was being pulled into a trance, a willing captive to Ariana's strange, hypnotic power, her own will slowly, inexorably, fading into the golden light.
