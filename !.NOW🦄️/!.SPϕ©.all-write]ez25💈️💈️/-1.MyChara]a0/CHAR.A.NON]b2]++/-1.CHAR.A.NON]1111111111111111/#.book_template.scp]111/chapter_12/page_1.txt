The next day, the summons came. A terse, unyielding email from the Director's office, its subject line a stark, ominous pronouncement: "Urgent Meeting. Director Evans. Now." The meeting itself was short, brutal, and to the point, held in a windowless conference room that felt more like an interrogation chamber. Her superiors, their faces grim, their expressions unreadable, laid out the evidence with chilling precision: leaked documents, unauthorized access, a meticulously constructed trail of digital breadcrumbs leading directly to her. They knew. They knew everything. The conspiracy, the windmill, Victor, Ariana – it was all laid bare, exposed to the harsh fluorescent light. They threatened her career, her freedom, her very life, painting a grim picture of a future spent in a federal prison, stripped of everything she had ever worked for. "Drop the case, Director Evans," the Director said, his voice cold and unyielding, devoid of any warmth or empathy. "Destroy the evidence. Forget what you have seen. For your sake, and for the sake of this agency, forget it all. This is not a request, Director. This is an order."
