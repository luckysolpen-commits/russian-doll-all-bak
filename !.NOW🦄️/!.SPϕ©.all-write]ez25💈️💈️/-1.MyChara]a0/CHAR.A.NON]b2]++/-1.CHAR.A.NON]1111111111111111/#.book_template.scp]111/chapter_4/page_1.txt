The old warehouse stood silhouetted against the bruised purple of the night sky, a decaying monument to a forgotten era of industry. Its skeletal frame, a lattice of rusted girders and broken windows, seemed to sigh with the weight of untold stories. The air was thick with the ghosts of yesterday, the scent of rust and rot and rain-soaked concrete, a pungent cocktail that clung to her clothes and hair. Cara moved through the shadows, a ghost in her own right, her hand resting on the cool, reassuring weight of her Glock, its familiar contours a small comfort in the vast, unsettling darkness. Every nerve ending was on fire, every sense screaming danger, a primal instinct honed by years in the field. The silence was a living thing, a predatory beast that watched her with unseen eyes, its breath a cold whisper on the back of her neck. She checked her watch. Midnight. Punctual, at least. A small mercy in a night that promised anything but.
