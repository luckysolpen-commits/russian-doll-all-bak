She awoke with a gasp, sitting bolt upright in her office chair at Langley, the sterile scent of disinfectant and stale coffee assaulting her senses. The morning sun streamed through the window, just as it had days—or was it weeks?—ago, painting the familiar room in a deceptive glow of normalcy. There was no sign of the windmill, no lingering scent of ozone and bioluminescent plants, no memory of how she had returned. It was as if the entire experience had been a vivid, terrifying dream, a hallucination brought on by stress and lack of sleep. But then her gaze fell upon her desk.

A single, folded note lay there, stark against the polished wood. It was from Ariana. "Meet me at the old oak tree in town square. Alone." The note was written in a graceful, flowing script, and as her fingers brushed against the paper, she felt a faint, almost imperceptible hum, a resonance that echoed the strange energy of the metal in the lab. Cara's head throbbed, a dull, persistent ache behind her eyes, a physical manifestation of the mental whiplash she was experiencing. She felt as though she had been dreaming, but the note in her hand was undeniably real, a tangible link to a world she was no longer sure she understood. The lines between reality and nightmare had blurred, and she was left adrift in a sea of uncertainty, clutching a piece of paper that held the key to her next, terrifying step.
