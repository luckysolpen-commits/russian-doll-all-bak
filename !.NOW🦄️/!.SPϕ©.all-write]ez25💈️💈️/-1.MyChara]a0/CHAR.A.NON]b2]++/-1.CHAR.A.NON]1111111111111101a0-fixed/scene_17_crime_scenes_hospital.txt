EXT. SUBURBAN HOUSE - DAY

SOUND of police sirens, news helicopters overhead

Chara, dressed in plain clothes, stands behind a police barricade, observing a suburban house. Crime scene tape crisscrosses the yard. Uniformed officers and forensic teams move with grim efficiency. News vans are parked down the street, their satellite dishes pointed at the house.

MILLER (O.S.)
(On Chara's earpiece)
This is the first one. Five victims. All college-aged. All… headless.

Chara’s gaze is fixed on the house. She can almost feel the lingering malevolence. She notices a few neighbors, huddled together, whispering, their faces pale with fear and morbid curiosity. She catches snippets of their conversation: "…strange lights… chanting… never thought it would happen here…"

She manages to slip past a distracted officer, flashing a discreet badge. Inside, the stench of death is overwhelming, a cloying, metallic odor. The basement is a nightmare. Dried blood stains the floor. Melted candle wax forms grotesque puddles. Symbols, crudely etched into the walls, glow faintly under the forensic lights. And the outlines of five bodies, where the heads were cleanly severed.

Chara’s stomach churns, but she forces herself to focus. This isn't just murder; it's ritual. The same patterns she saw in the video. The same chilling precision.

EXT. ABANDONED WAREHOUSE - DAY

SOUND of wind whistling through broken windows

The second crime scene is an abandoned warehouse, even more desolate. The air is thick with dust and the lingering scent of decay. More headless victims, more crude symbols. The scale of the operation here suggests a larger gathering, a more elaborate ritual.

Chara finds a discarded, singed piece of paper. On it, a partial symbol, identical to one she saw in the monastery's server room. A direct link. The Order of Providence is behind this.

INT. HOSPITAL ROOM - DAY

SOUND of medical equipment beeping softly

Chara stands at the doorway of a hospital room. Inside, a young man, pale and gaunt, lies in a bed, hooked up to various machines. This is the sole survivor from one of the seances, found barely alive, traumatized. His eyes are wide, vacant, staring at the ceiling.

Chara enters quietly. The survivor flinches, his eyes darting to her, filled with a raw, primal fear. He tries to speak, but only a choked gasp escapes his lips.

CHARA
(Softly, reassuringly)
It's okay. You're safe now. I'm Agent Chara. I just want to understand what happened.

The young man shakes his head violently, tears welling in his eyes. He pulls the blanket tighter around him, turning his face to the wall. He doesn't want to talk. He can't.

Chara sighs. She knows pushing him will only make it worse. She decides to stake him out. If he won't talk, maybe someone else will.

She settles into a chair in the waiting area, pretending to read a magazine, but her eyes are constantly on the hospital room door.

Hours pass. Then, two men in dark suits arrive. They move with an air of authority, their faces grim. They flash badges at the nurse's station, then enter the survivor's room. Chara recognizes the subtle, almost imperceptible, corporate logo on their lapel pins: OMNICORP.

Chara’s eyes narrow. Omicorp. Here? Visiting a survivor of a ritualistic murder? It doesn't make sense. A multi-billion dollar biotech firm involved with serial killings? It seems absurd, yet the pieces are starting to fit in a terrifying way.

She watches as the Omicorp agents emerge from the room, their expressions frustrated. They speak in hushed tones, glancing back at the survivor's door. They clearly got nothing from him either.

As they walk past Chara, one of them pulls out a phone, speaking in a low voice.

OMICORP AGENT 1 (O.S.)
(Into phone)
…still nothing. He's completely catatonic. We need to consider… alternative methods.

Chara’s blood runs cold. Alternative methods. The phrase hangs in the air, chilling her to the bone. Omicorp's involvement is no longer a suspicion; it's a terrifying reality. But why? What is their connection to these gruesome rituals? And what are these "alternative methods"?

FADE OUT.
