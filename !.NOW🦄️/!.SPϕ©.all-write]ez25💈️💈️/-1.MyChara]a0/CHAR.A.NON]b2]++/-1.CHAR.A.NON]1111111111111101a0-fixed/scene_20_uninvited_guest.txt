INT. CIA HEADQUARTERS - LANGLEY - NIGHT

SOUND of distant chatter, fluorescent hum

The government-issued sedan glides silently through the secure gates of Langley. Chara sits in the back, exhausted but wired. The adrenaline from the chase is slowly receding, replaced by a cold, hard resolve. Maya's words echo in her mind: "Omicorp is just the tip of the iceberg… a web of deceit and corruption that spans decades."

She steps out of the car, the cool night air a welcome contrast to the humid intensity of the chase. The familiar, sterile corridors of Langley feel both comforting and suffocating. She moves with purpose, her mind already racing, planning her next moves. She needs to debrief Miller, to share everything she's learned, however unbelievable it may sound.

She navigates the labyrinthine hallways, passing other agents, analysts, and support staff, all immersed in their own late-night tasks. The air is thick with the scent of stale coffee and ozone.

She reaches her office, a spartan space designed for efficiency. She pushes open the door, the faint hum of her computer equipment a familiar sound. She tosses her bag onto her worn leather chair, the thud echoing in the quiet room.

Her gaze drifts to her desk, a cluttered landscape of reports, files, and a half-empty coffee mug. And then she sees it.

A clear, heavy-duty ZIP-LOCK BAG, resting precisely in the center of her otherwise chaotic desk. It hadn't been there when she left. A cold dread, far more potent than any she had felt in the field, begins to coil in her stomach. Her breath hitches.

Inside the bag, nestled on a bed of what looks like shredded classified documents, is a HUMAN HEAD.

It is undeniably real. The skin, though pale, has the unmistakable texture of flesh. The hair, matted and dark, clings to the scalp. But it is the face that truly freezes her. It is heavily adorned with CLOWN MAKEUP. A grotesque, painted smile stretches from ear to ear, crimson lips smeared in a macabre grin. Large, exaggerated blue triangles frame the eyes, which are wide open, staring blankly at the ceiling, devoid of life. A single, painted tear trails from one eye, a cruel mockery of sorrow.

Chara feels a wave of nausea wash over her, a visceral reaction that bypasses her years of training. She has seen death in its most brutal forms, but this… this is an act of deliberate, theatrical cruelty. It is a message. A twisted, horrifying declaration.

Her hand instinctively goes to the Sig Sauer holstered at her hip, but she stops herself. What good would a weapon do against this? This isn't a physical threat in the immediate sense. This is psychological warfare. A violation of her sanctuary, a chilling demonstration of how deeply her adversaries have penetrated her world.

She takes a slow, deliberate breath, forcing herself to analyze the scene. No signs of forced entry. No alarms triggered. This means someone with high-level access, someone who could bypass Langley’s formidable security. Or, perhaps, someone *within* Langley.

Her eyes scan the room, searching for any other anomalies. Nothing. Just the head, sitting there, mocking her with its painted grin. She pulls on a pair of latex gloves from a drawer, her movements precise, almost robotic. She can't touch it directly, not yet. This is a crime scene, a message, and potentially, a trap.

She leans closer, examining the bag. It is sealed tightly, no discernible fingerprints on the outside. The shredded documents beneath the head… are they from Langley? Could they be from her own files? A quick glance at her filing cabinet confirms her worst fear: the top drawer, where she keeps her most sensitive case files, is slightly ajar. She always locks it. Always.

A cold fury begins to simmer beneath her shock. They aren't just sending a message; they are demonstrating their reach, their audacity. They have been in her office, rifled through her files, and left this grotesque calling card.

Suddenly, a faint, almost imperceptible HUM fills the air. It's the sound of a drone, small and agile, slipping through her open window. It's ANONYMOUS.

The drone, no larger than her palm, hovers silently in the air, its tiny camera lens blinking. A MODULATED VOICE, devoid of emotion, emanates from its miniature speaker.

MODULATED VOICE (O.S.)
Agent Chara. You called.

Chara looks from the drone to the severed head, her face a mask of grim determination. The game has escalated. And she is ready to play.

FADE OUT.
