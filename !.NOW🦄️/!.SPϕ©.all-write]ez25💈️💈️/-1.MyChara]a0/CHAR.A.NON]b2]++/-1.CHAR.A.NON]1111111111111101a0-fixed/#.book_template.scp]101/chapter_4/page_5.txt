The robot’s fist descended, a blur of metallic fury, aimed directly
at Chara. But she didn’t flinch. Instead, she moved, a sudden,
fluid shift of weight that carried her out of the path of the
blow. The fist slammed into the earth, sending a shockwave that
rippled through the clearing, uprooting trees and tearing chunks
of soil from the ground. Chara was already moving, circling the
behemoth, her eyes searching for a weakness, a vulnerability.
Anonymous’s voice was a steady presence in her ear, guiding her,
feeding her information. “Its energy core is located in the chest,
Chara. Heavily shielded. You’ll need to create an opening.” Chara
nodded, her mind racing. She couldn’t fight this thing head-on.
It was too big, too powerful. She had to use its own momentum
against it. The robot turned, its red eyes tracking her every
movement, its massive arms swinging in wide, sweeping arcs. Chara
dodged and weaved, a tiny, agile figure against a backdrop of
immense, destructive power. She was a matador, taunting the bull,
waiting for the opportune moment to strike. She noticed a subtle
pattern in its movements, a slight delay in its left arm’s swing.
An opening. “Anonymous, I need a distraction,” she whispered into
her comms. “Something big. Something that will draw its attention.”
“On it,” Anonymous replied, his voice filled with a new urgency.
Suddenly, a series of explosions rocked the forest, far off in
the distance. Trees splintered, and a plume of smoke rose into
the sky. The robot paused, its red eyes flickering, its massive
head turning towards the source of the disturbance. It was a momentary
distraction, but it was all Chara needed. She sprinted forward,
closing the distance between herself and the robot, her tactical
knife glinting in the nascent light. She leaped, a graceful, almost
impossible leap, landing on the robot’s massive arm. Its metallic
surface was cold and unyielding, but she found purchase, her boots
magnetically locking onto the plating. The robot roared, a sound
like grinding metal, and began to thrash, trying to dislodge her.
Chara clung on, her muscles screaming in protest, as she scrambled
up its arm, towards its chest. The energy shield around its core
was pulsating, a shimmering barrier of pure force. She had to
get through it. She raised her knife, its blade glowing faintly
with a charge Anonymous had imbued it with. This wasn’t just a
knife; it was a conduit, a weapon designed to disrupt energy fields.
She plunged it into the shield, a shower of sparks erupting around
her. The shield flickered, then began to crack, a spiderweb of
energy spreading across its surface. The robot roared again, its
movements becoming more erratic, more desperate. Chara gritted
her teeth, pushing deeper, her entire body vibrating with the
strain. This was it. The moment of truth. She had to break through.
She had to win.
