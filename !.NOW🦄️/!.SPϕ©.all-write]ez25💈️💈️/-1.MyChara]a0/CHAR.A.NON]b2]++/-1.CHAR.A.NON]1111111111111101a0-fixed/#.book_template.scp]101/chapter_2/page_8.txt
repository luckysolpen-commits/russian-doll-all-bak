Chara knelt beside the trembling woman, her gaze fixed on the
Quinault prayer stick clutched in her hand. “Who are you?” Chara
asked, her voice gentle, reassuring. “My name is Arianna,” the
woman whispered, her voice still hoarse with fear. “I… I was one
of them. I was part of the Order.” Chara’s eyes widened. This
was a breakthrough, a direct link to the heart of the Order of
Providence. “What happened here, Arianna? What were they trying
to awaken?” Arianna shuddered, her eyes darting around the dimly
lit chamber. “They call it the ‘Whispering One.’ An ancient entity,
a being of pure energy, trapped between dimensions. The prayer
stick… it’s a key. A conduit. They believed they could control
it, harness its power.” Chara felt a cold dread creep up her spine.
This was far beyond anything she had ever encountered, far beyond
the realm of espionage and cyber warfare. This was something ancient,
something supernatural. “And did they succeed?” she asked, her
voice barely a whisper. Arianna shook her head, a tear tracing
a path through the grime on her cheek. “No. Something went wrong.
They… they lost control. It was… terrifying. The chanting stopped.
The lights flickered. And then… the screams. They were consumed.
Pulled into… into nothingness.” Chara looked around the empty
chamber, the dried blood on the altar, the scattered feathers.
It all made a horrifying kind of sense. The Order hadn’t vanished;
they had been annihilated. “And you?” Chara asked. “How did you
escape?” “I… I hid,” Arianna stammered, her voice trembling.
“I saw what was happening. I knew I had to get out. I grabbed
the stick… I don’t know why. It just felt… important.” Chara
nodded, her mind racing. This woman, Arianna, was a survivor,
a witness. And she held the key to understanding the Order’s
ultimate goal. “Arianna,” Chara said, her voice firm, resolute.
“You’re safe now. But we need to go. This place isn’t safe. The
Order… they might send others. Or worse, whatever they awakened
might still be here.” Arianna looked at her, her eyes wide with
a mixture of fear and hope. “Where will we go?” “To a place where
you’ll be safe,” Chara replied, helping her to her feet. “And
where you can tell me everything you know about the Order of
Providence. Everything.” Arianna nodded, clutching the prayer
stick tightly. “I… I know a place. A stronghold. An ancient monastery.
It’s where the Order keeps its most sacred texts, its most guarded
secrets. It’s where they planned to… to complete the awakening.”
Chara felt a surge of adrenaline. This was it. The heart of the
Order. The place where all the answers lay. “Lead the way, Arianna,”
she said, her voice filled with a grim determination. “Lead the
way.”
