SCENE 3

EXT. AFROTTA - VILLAGE OUTSKIRTS - DAY

SOUND of helicopter rotors fading, wind, distant animal calls

The helicopter hovers briefly, kicking up a storm of dust and loose sand, then descends rapidly. CHARA, along with two other operatives (generic, stoic), disembarks quickly, their boots sinking slightly into the soft earth. The helicopter immediately ascends, disappearing into the vast sky, leaving an unnerving silence in its wake.

They are on the outskirts of a small, sun-baked village. Mud-brick huts huddle together, their roofs thatched with dry grass. A few scrawny goats wander aimlessly. The air is thick with the smell of dust and something vaguely metallic.

Chara scans the perimeter, her eyes sharp, taking in every detail. The other operatives take up defensive positions, weapons at the ready.

From the shadows between two huts, a figure emerges. It’s the LOCAL CONTACT (50s, weathered face, deep-set eyes, a worn rifle slung over his shoulder). He moves with a cautious, almost predatory grace.

LOCAL CONTACT
(Gruff, low voice)
You made good time. But you brought trouble with you.

Chara steps forward, her hand subtly resting near her sidearm.

CHARA
Trouble? Explain.

LOCAL CONTACT
Mercenaries. Not local. Well-armed. They’ve been sniffing around. Heard whispers about a… valuable package. They’re looking for what you’re carrying.

Chara’s jaw tightens. This wasn't unexpected, but the speed of their appearance is concerning.

CHARA
How many? Where are they?

LOCAL CONTACT
Too many for a quiet stroll. They’re spread out, circling the village. Waiting. They know you’re here. They just don’t know where, exactly.

He gestures vaguely towards the surrounding rocky outcrops and sparse scrubland.

LOCAL CONTACT (CONT'D)
They want your asset. And they’re not known for taking prisoners.

Chara exchanges a quick, grim look with her fellow operatives. The mission, already fraught with peril, has just escalated.

CHARA
(To her team)
Fan out. Perimeter check. Stay low. We move when I say.

The operatives nod, melting into the sparse cover. Chara turns back to the Local Contact.

CHARA (CONT'D)
Any safe routes out of here? Away from their main force?

LOCAL CONTACT
(Points to a narrow, winding path leading into a rocky canyon)
That way. But it’s tight. And they’ll be watching it.

Chara studies the path, a plan already forming in her mind. The silence of the desert is suddenly heavy, pregnant with anticipation.

FADE OUT.
