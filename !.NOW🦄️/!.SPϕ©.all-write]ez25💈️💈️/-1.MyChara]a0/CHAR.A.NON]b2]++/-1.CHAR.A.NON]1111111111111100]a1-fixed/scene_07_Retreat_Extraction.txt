SCENE 7

EXT. AFROTTA - RETREAT/EXTRACTION - DAY/NIGHT

SOUND of distant gunfire, wind, the rhythmic thud of helicopter blades approaching

Darkness. Then, a searing pain. CHARA groans, her eyes fluttering open. Her head throbs, a dull, persistent ache. She's lying on rough, sandy ground. The air is cool, the sun having long set, replaced by a vast, star-dusted sky.

She pushes herself up, every muscle protesting. Her side is bandaged crudely, the fabric stained dark. Someone helped her. But who? And where is ANON? Her remaining operative?

She remembers the chaos, the overwhelming numbers, Klondike's final, defiant stand. The image of his lifeless eyes flashes in her mind, a fresh wave of grief and anger washing over her.

She's alone. Or so she thinks.

A shadow detaches itself from a nearby rock formation. It's the LOCAL CONTACT, his face grim in the moonlight.

LOCAL CONTACT
(Voice low, weary)
Thought you were a goner. Found you near the edge of the village. They left you for dead.

Chara tries to speak, but her throat is dry, raw.

LOCAL CONTACT (CONT'D)
Your friend… the woman… she got away. Ran into the desert. Fast. Smart.

Relief, a small, fragile spark, ignites within Chara. ANON is alive. But what about her operative?

CHARA
My team…?

The Local Contact shakes his head slowly, his eyes conveying the grim truth. Chara closes her eyes, a silent acknowledgment of another loss.

LOCAL CONTACT
They'll be back. The mercenaries. They'll sweep the area at first light. We need to move. Now.

He helps Chara to her feet. She leans on him heavily, her legs unsteady. They move through the night, a slow, painful trek across the desolate landscape. The Local Contact, despite his age, seems to know every rock, every shadow, every hidden path.

Hours pass. The moon begins its descent. Chara's strength wanes, but the thought of Klondike, of the mission's failure, fuels her. She won't let it be for nothing.

Then, a faint, familiar sound. The rhythmic THUD-THUD-THUD of helicopter blades. Getting closer. An extraction. But whose?

LOCAL CONTACT
(Looking up, a flicker of hope in his eyes)
Looks like someone still cares.

The helicopter, a sleek, dark silhouette against the pre-dawn sky, descends rapidly. It's a CIA bird, a familiar model. Relief floods Chara, so potent it almost brings her to her knees.

The helicopter lands, kicking up another cloud of dust. The side door slides open. GIANA NGUYEN stands there, her face etched with concern, but also a profound relief at seeing Chara alive.

GIANA
(Voice tight with emotion)
Chara! Thank God. We thought…

She doesn't finish the sentence. Chara stumbles towards the helicopter, the Local Contact supporting her. She glances back at him.

CHARA
Thank you.

LOCAL CONTACT
(A rare, small smile)
Stay safe, Chara. The desert always remembers.

Chara is helped into the helicopter. Giana immediately begins assessing her injuries, a medic already moving to her side. The door slides shut. The helicopter lifts off, leaving the Local Contact alone in the vast, silent desert.

Inside, the ride is somber. The medic works quietly. Giana watches Chara, her expression unreadable. Chara stares out the window, the landscape blurring below. The sun begins to peek over the horizon, painting the sky in hues of orange and purple. A new day. But for Chara, it feels like the end of an era.

The weight of Klondike's death, the failure of the mission, the betrayal – it all settles upon her, heavy and cold. The journey back to Mothra Base is a silent, painful descent into the aftermath.

FADE OUT.
