SCENE 2

EXT. MOTHRA BASE - HELIPAD - DAY

SOUND of rotor blades chopping the air, distant ocean waves

The helipad, a stark concrete circle, juts out from the oil strut, a metallic behemoth against the vast, blue sky. The air is thick with the smell of jet fuel and salt.

CHARA, now in tactical gear, moves with a quiet efficiency. She checks her equipment one last time: a compact assault rifle slung across her back, a sidearm holstered securely, a small, nondescript pack. Her movements are fluid, economical – every action honed by years of training.

GIANA NGUYEN stands nearby, observing. She offers a curt nod.

GIANA
Stay sharp, Chara. Afrotta bites.

Chara meets her gaze, a flicker of determination in her eyes. She doesn't speak, her focus entirely on the task ahead.

A BLACKHAWK HELICOPTER, its rotors already spinning, waits. The pilot, a stoic figure in a flight helmet, gives a thumbs-up.

Chara climbs into the helicopter, settling into her seat. The interior is cramped, utilitarian. The roar of the engines intensifies, vibrating through the floor.

Through the small window, Mothra Base shrinks below them, a complex web of steel and pipes disappearing into the shimmering expanse of the ocean.

The helicopter banks sharply, turning inland. The ocean gives way to a vast, arid landscape – the first glimpse of Afrotta. Dust devils dance in the distance. The terrain below is rugged, unforgiving, a patchwork of ochre and brown.

Chara closes her eyes for a moment, taking a deep breath. She visualizes the maps, the intel, the potential threats. Her mind is a steel trap, preparing for the unknown.

FADE OUT.
