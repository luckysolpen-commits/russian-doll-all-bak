SCENE 8

INT. HELICOPTER/PLANE - NIGHT

SOUND of steady engine hum, faint whirring of medical equipment

CHARA lies on a makeshift cot in the back of the transport helicopter, or perhaps a small, private jet. The interior is utilitarian, dimly lit by overhead lights. A medic works quietly, tending to her wounds. The pain is a dull throb now, a constant reminder of the chaos she just endured.

GIANA NGUYEN sits opposite her, a small, rugged laptop open on her knees. Her face is grim, illuminated by the screen's glow. She glances at Chara, then back at the screen, her fingers hovering over the keyboard.

GIANA
(Voice low, weary)
Start when you're ready, Chara. Just… tell me what happened. Everything.

Chara pushes herself up slightly, wincing. The medic offers her a glass of water, which she takes gratefully, her hand trembling slightly. She stares at the blank screen of Giana's laptop, a cursor blinking impatiently.

CHARA
(Voice hoarse, distant)
It was… a setup. From the beginning. ANON… she wasn't just an informant. She was the bait. And Klondike… he was the target.

Giana's eyes widen almost imperceptibly. She types a few notes, her expression hardening.

GIANA
Klondike? You saw him? He's alive?

Chara closes her eyes, the image of Klondike's lifeless face flashing behind her eyelids. A tear escapes, tracing a path through the grime on her cheek.

CHARA
(Voice breaking)
He was. He… he died. Protecting us. Protecting ANON. They overwhelmed us. Too many. They knew we were coming. They knew everything.

Giana stops typing, her gaze fixed on Chara. The silence in the cabin is heavy, punctuated only by the hum of the engines.

GIANA
(Softly)
I'm so sorry, Chara. He was… a legend.

Chara shakes her head, a bitter laugh escaping her lips.

CHARA
Legend or not, he's gone. And it was for nothing. The asset… the mission… it was all a lie. A trap.

She takes a deep, shuddering breath, trying to compose herself. The emotional weight of it all is crushing. The grief for Klondike, the anger at the betrayal, the profound sense of failure.

CHARA (CONT'D)
(Voice gaining a steely edge)
They wanted him. Or what he knew. ANON… she said he was the asset. But it was more than that. It had to be.

Giana nods slowly, her mind already working, piecing together the fragments of information. She starts typing again, faster this time, her fingers flying across the keyboard.

GIANA
We'll get to the bottom of this, Chara. I promise you. Every single detail. Who set this up. Why. And who else is involved.

Chara stares out the small window, the vast, inky blackness of the night sky reflecting her own inner turmoil. The stars are cold, indifferent. She feels a profound sense of detachment, as if she's watching herself from afar, a character in a tragic play.

But beneath the detachment, a spark ignites. A cold, hard resolve. Klondike's death won't be in vain. The truth will come out. No matter the cost.

She reaches for the laptop, her hand steady now. Giana looks at her, surprised.

CHARA
(Voice firm, resolute)
Let me. I'll write it. Every detail. Every face. Every bullet. They won't get away with this.

Giana hands her the laptop, a flicker of admiration in her eyes. Chara begins to type, the rhythmic click of the keys filling the silence. The words flow, a torrent of pain, anger, and determination. The debriefing report becomes more than just a record; it becomes a testament, a promise.

FADE OUT.
