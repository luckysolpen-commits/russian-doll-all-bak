SCENE 10

INT. CIA HEADQUARTERS - ARCHIVES/SECURE ROOM - DAY

SOUND of hushed silence, the whir of old servers, faint clicks of keyboards

The CIA archives. A labyrinth of towering metal shelves, filled with countless files, old computers, and dusty boxes. The air is cool, dry, and smells faintly of old paper and ozone. This is where secrets are kept, and sometimes, buried.

CHARA, dressed in civilian clothes, but with the same intense focus, navigates the narrow aisles. She carries a small, encrypted tablet and a stack of printouts from her debriefing report. Her movements are deliberate, almost stealthy, as if she doesn't want to disturb the sleeping giants of information around her.

She finds a secluded terminal in a secure, soundproofed room. The screen glows with a familiar agency interface. She logs in, her fingers flying across the keyboard with practiced ease. Her search parameters are precise: "Operation Negative One," "Klondike," "ANON," "Afrotta incidents, last 6 months."

Hours pass. The initial results are overwhelming: standard mission reports, redacted documents, dead ends. But Chara is looking for something else. The inconsistencies. The things that don't quite fit.

She cross-references her own debriefing with official records. The discrepancies are subtle at first: a different time stamp here, a missing detail there. Then, they become more glaring. A communication log that suddenly ends. A satellite image that seems to have been digitally altered. A mention of a secondary asset that was never briefed to her.

Her eyes narrow. This isn't just incompetence. This is deliberate.

She pulls up Klondike's old files. Decades of service, commendations, black ops. Then, the file marked "Presumed Deceased." The official report is terse, vague. No body recovered. No detailed investigation. Just a conclusion.

Chara remembers Klondike's words in the village: "They're not after us, Chara! They're after her! The asset!" And ANON's revelation: "The intel was… incomplete. A necessary deception."

She types in a new search: "Project Little Endians." The system flags it as highly classified, restricted access. But Chara has a hunch. She tries a series of old, obscure access codes, codes she learned from Klondike himself, years ago, during a training exercise.

BEEP. ACCESS GRANTED. A new set of files appears. Encrypted. She works quickly, decrypting them one by one. The content is chilling. Blueprints for a highly advanced, experimental energy device. Research notes on its potential for both power generation and weaponization. And a list of names. High-ranking officials. Corporate executives. Some within the agency.

She finds a communication log between two unknown parties, dated just days before her mission. It mentions "neutralizing the Klondike variable" and "securing the primary asset, ANON, for Project Little Endians."

Chara's blood runs cold. Klondike wasn't just a target; he was a variable. And ANON wasn't just an informant; she was central to this project. The mission was never about transport; it was about acquisition. And elimination.

She copies the critical files to her encrypted tablet. The weight of the truth settles on her, heavier than any physical wound. This isn't just a betrayal; it's a conspiracy that reaches the highest levels.

As she prepares to leave, she notices a faint, almost imperceptible GLOW from a forgotten corner of the room. A small, old-fashioned data chip, tucked away behind a loose panel in a dusty server rack. It looks out of place, almost deliberately hidden.

She picks it up. It's unmarked. A gut feeling tells her this is important. She slips it into her pocket, a new piece of the puzzle.

FADE OUT.
