SCENE 12

EXT. AFROTTA - REBEL BASE - DAY

SOUND of distant chatter, the clanking of metal, a dry, hot wind

The sun beats down mercilessly on a hidden rebel base, nestled deep within a series of natural caves and rock formations in Afrotta. It’s a makeshift but surprisingly organized encampment: tents fashioned from salvaged materials, crude workshops, and a constant flow of armed REBELS moving with purpose.

CHARA, now accompanied by ANON, navigates the dusty paths. Chara is no longer in her tactical gear, but in practical, durable clothing, blending in more with the environment. Her expression is grim, but focused. The revelations from ANON have hardened her resolve.

ANON
(Voice low)
They call themselves the 'Desert Hawks.' They've been fighting the government's encroachment for years. They know this land better than anyone. And they hate the corporations funding Project Little Endians as much as we do.

They approach a larger, central cave, guarded by two heavily armed REBEL GUARDS. The guards eye Chara suspiciously, but nod respectfully to ANON.

REBEL GUARD 1
She's here, Anon. The leader is waiting.

Inside the cave, it's cooler, but still dusty. Maps are spread across a rough-hewn table, marked with strategic points. A woman, fierce and commanding, stands over them. This is ZARA (40s, lean, scarred, with eyes that have seen too much war). She is the leader of the Desert Hawks.

Zara looks up as they enter, her gaze piercing. She studies Chara for a long moment, assessing.

ZARA
(Voice gravelly, but strong)
Anon tells me you seek allies. And that you bring a story of betrayal from the heart of the beast.

CHARA
(Direct, no preamble)
I bring proof. Proof that your government is colluding with a powerful, shadowy organization to exploit this land and its people. Proof that they killed a man I respected, and my parents, to cover it up. And proof that this 'Project Little Endians' is a threat to everyone.

Chara places her encrypted tablet on the table, activating the holographic projection. The blueprints of the energy device, the communication logs, the list of names – all float in the air between them. Zara and her lieutenants lean in, their expressions shifting from skepticism to grim understanding.

ZARA
(Tracing a finger over a name on the list)
These names… we've heard them. Whispers. But never proof like this.

ANON
This project isn't just about energy, Zara. It's about control. They plan to weaponize it. To create a new world order, built on the ashes of the old.

Zara looks at Chara, a new respect in her eyes.

ZARA
And what do you want from us, Agent?

CHARA
(Meeting her gaze)
Information. Resources. A safe haven. And your help. We need to expose them. To dismantle this project. To make them pay.

Zara walks around the table, her eyes still on the holographic display. She stops in front of Chara.

ZARA
My people have bled for this land. We have lost too much. We fight for freedom, not for the wars of foreign powers.

CHARA
This isn't a foreign war, Zara. This is your war. Their project will consume everything. Your land, your people, your future. They've already started.

She points to a section of the map, highlighting recent government incursions and unexplained resource extractions.

Chara then pulls out the small data chip Klondike left behind. She connects it to her tablet, and a new holographic image appears: Klondike's final message, his face weary but resolute.

KLONDIKE (HOLO)
(Voice echoing slightly)
…This project… it's a cancer. It will consume everything. You are the only one who can stop it. The real asset isn't a device, Chara. It's the truth. And you are the one to wield it.

The hologram fades. The silence in the cave is profound.

Zara looks at the tablet, then at Chara, then at Anon. The weight of the evidence is undeniable. The personal plea from a legendary figure, combined with the cold, hard facts, begins to sway her.

ZARA
(After a long pause)
We are the Desert Hawks. We fight for what is right. If what you say is true… then their fight is our fight.

She extends a hand to Chara. Chara takes it, a firm, unwavering grip. An uneasy alliance, forged in the heart of the desert, against a common enemy.

FADE OUT.
