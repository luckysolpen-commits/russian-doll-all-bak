The hum of the helicopter blades was a rhythmic pulse, a mechanical heartbeat against
the vast, indifferent sky. Chara sat strapped in, her gaze fixed on the blurring landscape
below. Mothra Base, a distant memory now, had receded into the shimmering heat haze.
Ahead lay Afrotta, a canvas of ochre and rust, stretching to the horizon, promising both
peril and purpose. The air inside the cabin was thick with the scent of aviation fuel
and the unspoken tension of a team on the cusp of the unknown. Her team, a small, handpicked
group of specialists, each a master of their craft, sat in stoic silence, their faces
revealing nothing but grim determination. They were a well-oiled machine, honed by countless
hours of training, ready to execute their roles with precision and efficiency.

Chara ran a mental checklist, reviewing the mission parameters, the contingency plans,
the potential threats. Every detail was etched into her memory, a testament to her rigorous
preparation. She was a creature of habit, a meticulous planner, leaving nothing to chance.
Yet, a subtle unease gnawed at her, a premonition that whispered of unforeseen complications.
The desert, she knew, was a fickle mistress, capable of both breathtaking beauty and sudden,
brutal treachery. It was a place where the rules of civilization often dissolved, replaced
by the harsh, unforgiving laws of survival. She tightened her grip on the strap, a silent
acknowledgment of the challenges that lay ahead. This was not a training exercise; this
was real, with real consequences, real dangers.

The helicopter banked sharply, a sudden lurch that momentarily pressed her against the
seat. Below, the terrain shifted, becoming more rugged, more unforgiving. Jagged rock
formations clawed at the sky, casting long, distorted shadows across the arid landscape.
This was the treacherous terrain Giana had spoken of, a natural fortress designed to deter
all but the most determined. Chara’s eyes scanned the ground, searching for any sign of
movement, any anomaly that might betray a hidden threat. Her senses were on high alert,
her instincts finely tuned, ready to react at a moment’s notice. She was a predator in
her own right, a hunter in a world where the hunted often became the hunter. The journey
was a test of endurance, a relentless assault on the senses, but Chara remained steadfast,
unwavering in her resolve. She was a professional, and professionals did not falter.

As the helicopter descended, the wind whipped up plumes of dust, obscuring the view.
Chara felt the familiar jolt of the landing gear, the sudden stillness after the roar
of the engines. They had arrived. The door hissed open, revealing a world bathed in the
harsh glare of the African sun. The heat was immediate, a suffocating blanket that pressed
in on her, stealing her breath. She took a deep breath, filling her lungs with the dry,
sandy air, and stepped out into the vast, silent expanse of Afrotta. The mission had truly
begun. The desert stretched before her, an endless expanse of sand and rock, a silent
promise of the trials to come. She was ready to face it, to embrace the unknown, to carve
her path through the shadow of the crow.
