The initial shock, a paralyzing wave of disbelief that had swept through the Soul Pen, began to recede, replaced by a surging, ugly tide of chaos and rage. The guests, their faces contorted with a terrifying blend of fear and anger, began to close in on Lucky, their movements slow, hesitant at first, then gaining a grim momentum. Queen Sophia's wails, raw and guttural, tore through the air, a heartbreaking lament that echoed off the crystalline walls as she cradled her daughter's lifeless body. Her grief, a palpable, physical force, was quickly curdling into a burning, accusatory hatred, her eyes, blazing with tears and fury, fixed on Lucky.

"We need to go. Now." Gem's voice, low and urgent, cut through the cacophony, a lifeline in the swirling madness. Her hand, firm and insistent, gripped Lucky's arm, the pressure a sharp, grounding reality that pulled him from his stupor.

Lucky's mind, still reeling from the sudden, brutal shift in reality, struggled to process the enormity of what had just transpired. The image of the young mermaid, her vibrant life snuffed out in an instant, was seared into his memory, a brand of guilt he knew he didn't deserve but couldn't escape. He was innocent, he knew that with a certainty that was absolute, but the accusation, the cold, synthesized voice, echoed in his ears, a damning verdict already delivered. He opened his mouth to protest, to scream his innocence, but the words caught in his throat, choked by the sheer, overwhelming injustice of it all.

Spike and Astra, moving with a practiced, seamless fluidity, formed a protective perimeter around him, their bodies a living shield against the encroaching, hostile crowd. "Move! Get back!" Spike's voice, sharp and commanding, a low growl that promised violence, cut through the rising hysteria. He shoved a large, aggressive-looking man who had gotten too close, sending him stumbling backward. Astra, her face a mask of cold, analytical calm, deployed a small, almost invisible drone from her wrist, its tiny propellers whirring silently as it rose to get a tactical overview of the chaos.

"Emergency evac. Rendezvous point Delta," she murmured into her comms, her fingers flying across a hidden keypad on her wrist. Her eyes, usually so calm and analytical, held a flicker of grim, deadly determination.

Lucky, jolted by Gem's urgency and the protective, unwavering stance of his friends, finally snapped out of his daze. The fog of disbelief began to clear, replaced by the cold, hard reality of their situation. He knew they were right. Staying here, trying to explain, to reason with a grief-stricken mob, would be futile. It would mean a UN holding cell, a rigged trial, or worse. He had to escape. He had to survive. He had to find out who had done this to him, and why. The need for answers, for vengeance, was a fire that began to burn away the shock.
