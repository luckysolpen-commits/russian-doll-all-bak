Spike, his arms crossed, his gaze unwavering, his massive frame a silent, intimidating presence, pressed her. "And why would you help us? What's in it for you? What's your angle?"

Dorchester's eyes, sharp and calculating, met Spike's, a silent battle of wills playing out in the space between them. "Let's just say I have a vested interest in taking down the people who are after you," she said, her voice losing its playful edge, replaced by a cold, hard resolve. "They're a faction that's been making waves in New Atlantis, a new player in the game, and I'm not a fan of their methods. They're disrupting the delicate balance of power in the deep, and that's bad for business. Besides," she added, a hint of mercenary pragmatism entering her tone, "I have a patron. A very wealthy, very discreet patron who pays well for this kind of information, for this kind of... creative disruption."

She gestured to a large, holographic screen that flickered to life, displaying a detailed, comprehensive dossier on the UN agents who had attacked the Soul Pen. Their faces, once obscured by those terrifying, featureless helmets, were now clearly visible, their names, their ranks, their service histories, all laid bare. "These are just puppets," Dorchester stated, her voice devoid of emotion, a cold, clinical assessment. "The real players, the ones pulling the strings, are in the shadows. I can help you find them. I can help you drag them into the light. But we're going to do it my way."

Lucky, despite his deep-seated reservations, his ingrained distrust of anyone who operated outside the law, felt a flicker of hope, a dangerous, seductive spark. Dorchester was dangerous, unpredictable, a force of chaos, yes, but she also possessed knowledge and resources he desperately needed. She was offering him a weapon, a chance to fight back.

"What's your way?" he asked, his voice betraying a hint of a desperate, reluctant curiosity.

Dorchester's eyes gleamed with a mischievous, predatory glint. "A game," she said, her voice dropping to a conspiratorial whisper. "A game of cat and mouse. We'll hunt the hunters. We'll turn their own tactics against them. It'll be fun."

Raven, who had been listening with wide-eyed fascination, giggled, her youthful, untainted enthusiasm a stark, almost surreal, contrast to the grim, deadly reality of their situation. "Dorchester says I need practice with lies and tricks!" she chirped, her eyes sparkling with an innocent, almost terrifying, anticipation. "She says I have a natural talent for it!"
