**Page 10**

Lucky looked at Dorchester, then at Gem, Astra, and Spike. He saw the doubt in their eyes, the same doubt that was churning in his own gut. He didn't trust her, not entirely. Her motives were still shrouded in mystery, her methods undoubtedly ruthless, her morality questionable at best. But he also knew, with a cold, hard certainty, that they were out of options. The UN was hunting them, their faces plastered on every news feed, their names synonymous with treason and murder. They were alone in a vast, unforgiving ocean, and their enemies were everywhere.

Dorchester, for all her questionable ethics, her love of chaos, her mercenary pragmatism, was offering them a path forward, a chance to fight back, a chance to seize control of their own destiny. It was a dangerous path, a path that would lead them deeper into the shadows, a path that would force them to become the very thing they were fighting against. But it was the only path they had.

"What's the first move?" Lucky asked, the words a reluctant surrender, a leap of faith into the abyss. He was a scientist, a man of logic and reason, but he was also a survivor. And he knew that sometimes, the only way to win a rigged game is to break the rules.

Dorchester's smile widened, a predatory, triumphant grin that sent a shiver down Lucky's spine. It was the smile of a shark that had just scented blood in the water. "We go to New Atlantis," she said, her voice a low, conspiratorial purr. "And we make a little noise. We rattle some cages. We let them know that the game has changed. And that we're no longer the prey. We're the hunters."
