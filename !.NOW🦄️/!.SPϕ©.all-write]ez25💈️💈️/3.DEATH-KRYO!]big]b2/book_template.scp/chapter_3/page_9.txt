**Page 9**

Carc-Zilla let out a deafening, soul-shattering roar, a sound that seemed to tear through the very fabric of the air, and lunged. Its massive claws, tipped with razor-sharp, diamond-hard blades, swiped through the space where they had just been standing. The team scattered, dodging its monstrous, lightning-fast attack, their movements a blur of desperate, panicked flight. The creature was incredibly, impossibly fast, its immense, bulky form belying its terrifying agility, and its power was immense, each swipe of its claws capable of tearing through reinforced steel as if it were paper.

"We can't fight that thing!" Spike yelled, his voice strained with effort as he narrowly, miraculously avoided a crushing, bone-shattering blow from Carc-Zilla's massive, armored tail.

"We don't have to! This way!" Dorchester shouted, already leading the way towards a maintenance tunnel, a narrow, dark opening in the wall that promised a slim, desperate chance at escape. But their path was blocked. A squad of Sub-Korea soldiers, clad in sleek, black, state-of-the-art armor, emerged from the shadows, their energy rifles raised. They opened fire, a barrage of searing, white-hot plasma bolts that illuminated the dark, narrow tunnel.

The team was pinned down, caught between the monstrous, rampaging Carc-Zilla and the relentless, disciplined onslaught of the soldiers. The air crackled with energy, the smell of ozone and burnt metal filling their nostrils. It was a desperate, hopeless situation, a trap from which there seemed no escape.

Then, Raven, who had been staying back, observing the chaos with a surprising, almost unnerving calm, acted. With a swift, practiced motion, she pulled a small, spherical device from her belt and hurled it towards the soldiers. It exploded with a soft, almost gentle hiss, engulfing the area in a thick, opaque, disorienting cloud of smoke. The soldiers, disoriented and blinded, ceased their fire, their shouts of confusion and anger echoing through the tunnel.

"Go! Now!" Dorchester yelled, seizing the opportunity. With a powerful, desperate heave, she pulled open a floor grate, revealing a dark, watery tunnel beneath. It was a maintenance conduit, a forgotten, slimy artery of the institute, leading to the unknown, to the dark, crushing depths of the ocean.
