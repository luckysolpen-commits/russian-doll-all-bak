**Page 8**

Mi-Sun, a blur of motion, engaged the Super-Soldiers in brutal hand-to-hand combat. Her movements were precise and powerful, each strike designed to incapacitate. She used Tear-It cards to enhance her physical strength, delivering blows that cracked armor and sent even the most augmented soldiers flying.

Dorchester, a whirlwind of lethal grace, danced through the fray, her energy dagger a shimmering arc of destruction. She sliced through armor, her movements fluid and deadly, and used her intimate knowledge of the complex's layout to her advantage, leading the Super-Soldiers into traps and ambushes, turning their own fortress against them.

Sáoirse watched the battle with a detached amusement, occasionally issuing commands to her remaining guards, her composure almost unnerving. She was confident, almost arrogant, in her defenses, believing her Super-Soldiers to be invincible.

One of the Super-Soldiers, larger and more heavily armored than the rest, lunged at Lucky. It was a brutal, enhanced version of the UN agents from the Soul Pen, a living weapon designed for destruction.

"Still sending your puppets, Sáoirse?" Lucky gritted, his teeth clenched, as he met the Super-Soldier's charge. He unleashed a powerful blast from his Soul Pen, augmented by a Tear-It card, the combined energy a devastating force. The Super-Soldier crashed into a console, sparks flying, its systems shorting out.

The team systematically dismantled Sáoirse's defenses. The command center, once a pristine hub of control, was now a wreck, consoles shattered, holographic displays flickering erratically, the air thick with the smell of ozone and burned metal. Only Sáoirse and a few of her elite guards remained, cornered and desperate.

Sáoirse's composure finally cracked, a snarl twisting her elegant features. "You fools!" she shrieked, her voice raw with fury. "You think this changes anything? I am the future! I am inevitable!" Her eyes, wild and desperate, scanned the ruined room.

With a sudden, desperate movement, she pressed a hidden button on her console. A section of the floor slid open, revealing a sleek, personal submarine, its form designed for rapid escape, a final, desperate gambit. It was small, compact, and built for speed.

"You may have won this battle, but the war is far from over!" Sáoirse screamed, her voice echoing through the ruined chamber. "You have no idea what I am capable of!" She leaped into the submarine, her movements surprisingly agile. The hatch hissed shut, sealing her inside. The submarine began to descend rapidly into a hidden shaft, disappearing into the depths of the complex.
