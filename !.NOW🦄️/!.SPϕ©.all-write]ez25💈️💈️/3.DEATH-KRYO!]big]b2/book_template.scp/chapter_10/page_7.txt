Inside, Sáoirse, elegant and composed even in the face of imminent invasion, sat at a large console, her back to the shattered entrance. She was surrounded by her personal guard – a dozen Augmented Super-Soldiers, their bodies rippling with enhanced musculature, their eyes glowing faintly with cybernetic implants. They were the pinnacle of UN military engineering, designed for one purpose: to protect Sáoirse at all costs.

Sáoirse turned slowly, a cold, amused smile playing on her lips. "Well, well, well. Look what the tide dragged in. Dorchester. And her little friends. I knew you couldn't be trusted." Her voice was a silken caress, yet it held an underlying current of venom, a predatory satisfaction.

"And I knew you were a monster, Sáoirse," Dorchester retorted, her voice sharp and clear. With a fluid motion, she drew a sleek, energy-bladed dagger, its edge humming with contained power. "Time to end this." Her words were a declaration of war.

The Super-Soldiers charged, a wave of enhanced muscle and gleaming armor. The command center erupted into a maelstrom of combat, a symphony of clashing metal, crackling energy, and the shouts of battle.

Lucky drew his Soul Pen Device. It pulsed with a vibrant blue light as he channeled his energy, firing concentrated blasts that sent Super-Soldiers reeling, their advanced armor smoking. He moved with fluid grace, a whirlwind of precision strikes, his movements a blur of calculated aggression. He was not just fighting; he was dancing, a deadly ballet of light and shadow.

Ji-Hyun, a force of nature unleashed, conjured torrents of water, creating slippery surfaces that sent soldiers tumbling, their heavy boots unable to find purchase. She followed with bursts of fire, searing flames that forced them back, their armor sizzling. Her elemental control was absolute, a testament to her mastery of the Tear-It cards.

Soo-Jin, nimble and quick, was a digital phantom. She used her hacking skills to disrupt the Super-Soldiers' comms and targeting systems, causing confusion in their ranks, their movements becoming erratic and uncoordinated. She threw Tear-It cards, which exploded with blinding flashes of light or disorienting sonic bursts, further disorienting the enemy.

Hye-Mi, despite her gentle nature, was a formidable force. She used her Tear-It cards to create shimmering energy shields that deflected incoming fire, the plasma bolts dissipating harmlessly against the translucent barriers. Her touch, usually reserved for healing, now delivered stunning electrical shocks, sending soldiers spasming to the ground.
