**Chapter 10: A Deadly Plot**

The Korean-style villa, usually a haven of tranquility, felt charged with a
subtle, electric tension. The soft hum of the air conditioning was the only
sound, a counterpoint to the rapid-fire clicks of holographic interfaces.
Lucky, Dorchester, Ji-Hyun, Soo-Jin, Hye-Mi, and Mi-Sun were gathered around
a shimmering, three-dimensional map of the world. Their focus, however, was
narrowed to a single, ominous point: a heavily fortified UN complex nestled
in the heart of Geneva, Switzerland.

"Sáoirse's command center," Dorchester stated, her finger hovering over a
section of the glowing complex. Her voice was low, almost a purr, but with
an underlying steel. "Heavily guarded, yes, but not impenetrable. I've been
feeding her false intelligence for weeks, making her believe I'm working on
a top-secret project for her. She trusts me." A wry, almost cynical smile
touched her lips.

Lucky, ever the pragmatist, raised an eyebrow. "She trusts a notorious pirate?"
His skepticism was evident, a natural reaction to the audacious claim.

Dorchester's smile widened, a glint of amusement in her eyes. "She trusts a
mercenary who gets results. And I've been giving her results. Small, controlled
leaks of information that make her look good, while subtly undermining her
rivals within the UN." It was a dangerous game, a high-stakes dance on the
edge of a precipice, but Dorchester thrived on such risks.

Ji-Hyun, her calm demeanor unwavering, voiced the obvious question. "So, you're
suggesting a direct assault? An assassination?" Her tone was neutral, but her
eyes, sharp and intelligent, betrayed a flicker of concern.

Dorchester nodded, her expression hardening. "It's the only way to cut the
head off the snake. Sáoirse is the driving force behind the UN's genocidal
agenda. Without her, their plans will crumble." Her conviction was absolute,
her resolve unshakeable.

Soo-Jin, her fingers dancing across the holographic map, brought up detailed
schematics of the complex. "Security is layered," she reported, her voice
professional and precise. "Biometric scans, sonic dampeners, energy shields.
And a personal guard detail that's rumored to be composed of augmented super-
soldiers." The sheer complexity of the defenses was daunting, a testament to
Sáoirse's paranoia and power.

Mi-Sun, ever the fiery one, cracked her knuckles, a mischievous grin spreading
across her face. "Sounds like fun." Her enthusiasm, though perhaps misplaced,
was infectious.

Hye-Mi, her gentle features clouded with concern, spoke softly. "This is
incredibly dangerous, Dorchester. Sáoirse is not to be underestimated." Her
words were a plea for caution, a reminder of the very real dangers they faced.

"I know," Dorchester replied, her eyes hard, devoid of their usual humor.
"But the longer she remains in power, the more innocent lives are at risk.
We have to stop her." Her voice was low, filled with a grim determination.

Lucky, his gaze fixed on the intricate schematics, began to analyze the data.
"How do we get in? Even with your intel, it's a fortress." The challenge was
immense, but his mind, ever the problem-solver, was already seeking solutions.

Dorchester's lips curved into a knowing smile, a glint in her eye. "That's
where my 'top-secret project' comes in. I've convinced her I'm developing a
new, undetectable infiltration system. She's given me access to her personal
labs within the complex. A back door, if you will." She projected a new
schematic, revealing a hidden access tunnel leading directly to Sáoirse's
private quarters, a secret passage known only to a select few.

"Clever. Very clever," Lucky admitted, a flicker of admiration in his eyes.
Dorchester's cunning was undeniable, a force to be reckoned with.

A shadow, however, crossed Dorchester's face, her earlier confidence momentarily
waning. "There's a complication. Sáoirse has been... asking about Raven. She
seems to have taken an interest in her unique abilities. I've been deflecting,
but I don't know how much longer I can keep her at bay." The unspoken fear
hung heavy in the air: Sáoirse might be planning to kidnap Raven, to exploit
her for her own nefarious purposes.

Ji-Hyun and Soo-Jin exchanged worried glances, the implication clear. The stakes
had just been raised, the personal cost of failure now immeasurable.

"We need to move fast then," Lucky declared, his voice firm, a new urgency
propelling him. "Before she makes her move."

"Agreed," Dorchester responded, a determined nod. "We strike during the next
UN security summit. Sáoirse will be present, and her personal guard will be
spread thin, overseeing the event." The plan, though dangerous, was their best
chance. They began to strategize, discussing entry points, escape routes, and
contingency plans, their voices low and grim. The mood was resolute, their
shared purpose a powerful bond.

**INT. SAOIRSE'S PRIVATE QUARTERS - GENEVA - NIGHT (SIMULTANEOUS)**

Sáoirse, elegant and ruthless, stood by the panoramic window of her private
quarters in Geneva, a glass of amber liquid swirling in her hand. The city
lights twinkled below, a glittering tapestry of power and influence, all of
which she sought to control. Her personal assistant, a nervous-looking man,
stood respectfully by, his posture stiff with apprehension.

"Madam Director, the reports on the asteroid mining venture are in," the
assistant began, his voice barely above a whisper. "Luna's projections are...
substantial." He paused, as if bracing himself for her reaction.

Sáoirse's lips curved into a cold, predatory smile. "Excellent." The word was
a silken caress, yet it held a chilling undertone. "And the girl? Raven? Has
Dorchester brought her in yet?" Her voice was casual, almost conversational,
but her eyes, sharp and calculating, betrayed her true intent.

"Not yet, Madam Director," the assistant stammered, his gaze fixed on the floor.
"Dorchester has been... evasive. She claims the girl is too young, too untrained."

Sáoirse's eyes narrowed, a dangerous glint appearing in their depths. "Untrained?
Or too valuable to share? No matter. I have my own methods. Prepare a discreet
extraction team. I want the girl. And I want her brought to me. Intact." Her
voice was now laced with an icy command, leaving no room for argument.

"Yes, Madam Director. Immediately," the assistant replied, his voice trembling
slightly as he hurried to obey. Sáoirse turned back to the window, her smile
widening, a chilling tableau of ambition and ruthlessness. She raised her glass,
a silent toast to her own dark designs.

"To new acquisitions. And to the future of the United Nations."

**FADE OUT.**
