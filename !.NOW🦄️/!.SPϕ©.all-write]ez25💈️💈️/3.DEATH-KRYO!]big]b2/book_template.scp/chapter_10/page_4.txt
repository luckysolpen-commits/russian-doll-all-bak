**INT. SAOIRSE'S PRIVATE QUARTERS - GENEVA - NIGHT (SIMULTANEOUS)**

Sáoirse, elegant and ruthless, a figure of cold, calculating power, stood by the panoramic window of her private quarters in Geneva. The city lights twinkled below, a glittering tapestry of power and influence, all of which she sought to control, to bend to her will. A glass of amber liquid, a rare, expensive vintage, swirled in her hand, its contents reflecting the distant city lights. Her personal assistant, a nervous-looking man, his face pale and drawn, stood respectfully by, his posture stiff with apprehension, his eyes darting nervously around the opulent room.

"Madam Director, the reports on the asteroid mining venture are in," the assistant began, his voice barely above a whisper, a tremor of fear in his tone. "Luna's projections are... substantial. Far beyond our initial estimates. It could fund our operations for decades." He paused, as if bracing himself for her reaction, for the inevitable outburst of temper or the chillingly calm, ruthless command.

Sáoirse's lips curved into a cold, predatory smile, a thin, cruel line that sent a shiver down the assistant's spine. "Excellent." The word was a silken caress, yet it held a chilling undertone, a promise of future dominance. "And the girl? Raven? Has Dorchester brought her in yet? I grow impatient." Her voice was casual, almost conversational, but her eyes, sharp and calculating, betrayed her true intent, her insatiable hunger for power, for control.

"Not yet, Madam Director," the assistant stammered, his gaze fixed on the polished floor, unable to meet her gaze. "Dorchester has been... evasive. She claims the girl is too young, too untrained, too wild to be brought into our fold." He swallowed hard, bracing himself for the inevitable.

Sáoirse's eyes narrowed, a dangerous glint appearing in their depths, like ice forming on a dark lake. "Untrained? Or too valuable to share? No matter. I have my own methods. More direct methods. Prepare a discreet extraction team. I want the girl. And I want her brought to me. Intact. Her unique abilities will be invaluable to our cause." Her voice was now laced with an icy, undeniable command, leaving no room for argument, no possibility of failure.

"Yes, Madam Director. Immediately," the assistant replied, his voice trembling slightly as he hurried to obey, eager to escape her chilling presence. Sáoirse turned back to the window, her smile widening, a chilling tableau of ambition and ruthlessness. She raised her glass, a silent toast to her own dark designs, to a future where she reigned supreme.

"To new acquisitions. And to the future of the United Nations. A future where we control everything." Her laughter, cold and devoid of mirth, echoed in the silent room, a chilling promise of what was to come.
