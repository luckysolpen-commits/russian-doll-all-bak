**Chapter 6: Sub-Korea's Alliance**

The winding, bioluminescent cave system, a natural wonder of the deep, offered
a temporary respite from the chaos they had just escaped. The water, here,
was impossibly clear, revealing ancient rock formations sculpted by millennia
of currents, and strange, glowing flora that pulsed with a soft, internal
light. Lucky, still wincing from a bruise he'd acquired during their frantic
escape from Carc-Zilla, ran a hand over his aching ribs. The encounter had
been a brutal reminder of the stakes involved.

"So, the UN is weaponizing Carcazod," he muttered, the words tasting bitter
in his mouth. "That's a new low, even for them." He had dedicated his life
to eradicating the plague, to bringing hope back to a world ravaged by disease.
To see his life's work twisted into a weapon of mass destruction, a tool of
genocide, was a profound betrayal.

Dorchester, ever the pragmatist, led the way, her movements fluid and confident
through the watery labyrinth. "They've been at it for years," she stated,
her voice devoid of emotion, yet carrying a chilling weight. "My intel suggests
they're trying to create a new strain, something even more virulent. And they're
using the clone-merpeople as their test subjects." Her words hung in the water,
a dark cloud of revelation.

"They're experimenting on them?" Gem's voice was sharp with horror, her eyes
wide with disbelief. The idea of such a peaceful, advanced civilization being
subjected to such atrocities was almost unbearable.

"Not directly," Dorchester clarified, her gaze fixed on the path ahead. "They're
trying to provoke a conflict. A reason to 'cleanse' the oceans. And the merpeople
are just collateral damage." Her tone was grim, a testament to the cold,
hard realities of power and control.

"But why?" Spike demanded, his frustration evident. "What's the endgame?"

"Control. Pure and simple," Dorchester replied, her voice hardening. "If they
can control the oceans, they control the world. And the clone-merpeople are
a significant power bloc down here. They have alliances, resources. They're
an obstacle to the UN's global dominion." The pieces of the puzzle, once
disparate and confusing, were slowly beginning to click into place, forming
a terrifying picture of ambition and ruthlessness.

As they spoke, the natural rock formations of the caves began to give way to
smooth, polished surfaces. The bioluminescent flora receded, replaced by sleek,
artificial lighting that cast a soft, even glow. They passed through a shimmering
energy barrier, a subtle ripple in the water that marked the transition from
the wild, untamed depths to a meticulously engineered environment. And then,
suddenly, they were in a city. A bustling, vibrant metropolis that defied
all expectations.

Sub-Korea. It was a marvel of futuristic architecture, a testament to human
ingenius and the harmonious integration of technology with the natural world.
Sleek, metallic buildings, their surfaces gleaming under artificial sunlight,
rose from the ocean floor like colossal, polished sculptures. They were
connected by transparent tubes, through which citizens traveled in silent,
efficient pods. Schools of colorful fish, seemingly unperturbed by the urban
environment, swam alongside advanced sub-vehicles, their movements a graceful
dance in the clear water. It was a city that hummed with life, a testament
to technological prowess and harmonious living.

Mer-people, with their iridescent scales and flowing hair, moved freely
alongside humans and other aquatic beings. They were not the primitive
creatures the surface world imagined, but a sophisticated civilization, their
culture rich and ancient, their technology advanced beyond anything Lucky had
ever witnessed. Their faces, serene and intelligent, held a quiet dignity
that spoke of centuries of wisdom.

"This is... incredible," Lucky breathed, his voice filled with awe. The sheer
beauty and complexity of Sub-Korea was overwhelming, a stark contrast to the
darkness and danger they had just left behind. It was a vision of what humanity
and other sentient species could achieve when they worked together, a beacon
of hope in a world teetering on the brink of war.

"Welcome to Sub-Korea," Dorchester said, a rare hint of pride in her voice.
"The heart of the New-Korea alliance. They've been fighting the UN's
encroachment for decades. And they're not alone." She led them through the
bustling streets, past markets selling exotic seafood and tech stalls displaying
holographic devices that shimmered with intricate designs. The air was filled
with the murmur of various languages, a symphony of diverse cultures, and the
soft, rhythmic hum of machinery.

They arrived at a grand, circular building, its entrance guarded by imposing,
yet elegant, New-Korea soldiers in sleek, form-fitting armor. Their presence
was a silent testament to the city's strength and its unwavering resolve.

**INT. SUB-KOREA COUNCIL CHAMBER - CONTINUOUS**

The chamber was vast and circular, its walls adorned with intricate patterns
that seemed to shift and flow with the currents. A large holographic display
occupied the center, projecting a detailed map of the ocean, dotted with
various strategic points. Around a polished, obsidian table sat several high-
ranking officials from both New-Korea and the clone-merpeople factions. Their
expressions were stern, their posture regal, their eyes holding the weight of
centuries of conflict and wisdom.

Among them was a wise-looking Mer-Elder, his scales a deep sapphire, his eyes
ancient and knowing. Beside him sat a stern, yet dignified, New-Korea General,
his uniform adorned with intricate medals that gleamed in the soft light.

Dorchester stepped forward, bowing respectfully, a gesture that surprised Lucky
with its sincerity. "Honored elders, General," she began, her voice clear and
strong. "I bring Lucky, the one the UN seeks to frame, and his companions.
They have witnessed the UN's true intentions."

The New-Korea General's eyes narrowed as he studied Lucky, a flicker of
recognition in their depths. "We have heard of you, Lucky," he stated, his
voice deep and resonant. "Your reputation precedes you. And your recent...
escape... confirms our suspicions about the UN's aggression." His gaze was
intense, assessing, but not hostile.

The Mer-Elder, his voice a deep, resonant hum that seemed to vibrate through
the water itself, spoke next. "The UN seeks to eradicate us, to pave the way
for their dominion. They call us a threat, a mutation. But we are simply...
different. And we have allies." His gaze, ancient and unwavering, met Lucky's,
a silent plea for understanding and cooperation.

Dorchester turned to Lucky, her face serious, all traces of her usual mischievous
glare gone. "I was once part of the UN's genetic engineering program," she
confessed, her voice low, almost a whisper. "I saw the atrocities they planned,
the genocidal intent behind their 'humanitarian' efforts. I defected, and I've
been working to expose them ever since. The merpeople are not a threat. They
are a peaceful, advanced civilization. The UN wants to destroy them to seize
their resources and eliminate any opposition to their global control." Her
words were a revelation, a missing piece of the puzzle that finally explained
Dorchester's motivations.

Lucky's eyes widened as the pieces clicked into place. The UN's true agenda,
the reason for the frame-up, the weaponized Carcazod, it all made a terrifying
sense. "So, the UN wants to wipe out the merpeople, and they're using Carc-Zilla
as their ultimate weapon," he stated, the realization a cold, hard knot in his
stomach.

"Precisely," the New-Korea General confirmed, his voice grim. "But we will
not allow it. We have a proposal for you, Lucky. One that could change the
course of this war." The fate of the underwater world, and perhaps even the
surface, hung in the balance, resting on the choices they would make.

**FADE OUT.**
