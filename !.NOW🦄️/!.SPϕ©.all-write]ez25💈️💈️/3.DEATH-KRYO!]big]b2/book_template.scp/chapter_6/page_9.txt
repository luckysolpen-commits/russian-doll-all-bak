They set up a training ground in a secluded part of the villa's grounds, a hidden clearing surrounded by ancient, whispering trees. Clone practice dummies, surprisingly resilient and agile, were their opponents. These were not just simple targets; they were programmed to simulate various threats, including mer-clones, forcing the team to adapt and push their limits, honing their skills against a realistic, intelligent enemy. The air was filled with the sounds of crackling energy, whooshing wind, and the thud of powerful blows.

"These dummies are tougher than they look," Lucky said, wiping sweat from his brow after a particularly intense session, his muscles aching but his spirit invigorated, a new sense of purpose filling him. "They're almost as good as the real thing."

Soo-Jin, grinning, replied, "They have to be. The real fight will be even tougher. We need to be ready. We need to be better than ready. We need to be unstoppable." Her words were a stark reminder of the grim reality that awaited them, a call to arms.

As they trained, the news played softly in the background, a constant hum of the outside world, a distant echo of the lies and manipulations that still permeated the global consciousness. Sáoirse, the ruthless leader of the UN, appeared on screen, her voice dripping with self-righteousness, condemning the recent 'natural disaster' and vowing to bring the perpetrators to justice. Her words were a venomous lie, a thinly veiled attempt to control the narrative, to twist the truth to her own nefarious ends. Her face, usually so composed, held a hint of triumph, a subtle smirk that spoke of hidden agendas.

Dorchester's eyes narrowed, a dangerous glint in their depths, a cold, simmering fury. "Justice. She wouldn't know justice if it bit her in the face. It's time we paid her a visit. A personal visit." She looked at Lucky, a plan already forming in her mind, a daring, audacious plan to assassinate Sáoirse, to cut the head off the snake. Little did she know, Sáoirse had her own plot brewing, a sinister scheme involving Dorchester's sister, Raven, a pawn in a much larger, deadlier game, a game that would soon engulf them all.
