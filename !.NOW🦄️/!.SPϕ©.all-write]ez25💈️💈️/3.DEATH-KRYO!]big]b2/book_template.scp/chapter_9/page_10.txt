**Chapter 11: The Final Confrontation**

The United Nations Command Center in Geneva, a sprawling, multi-level complex of polished chrome and flickering holographic displays, was a fortress of power and information. Even at night, it hummed with a relentless energy, its corridors patrolled by crisp-uniformed UN soldiers, their faces grim and unyielding. Global data streamed across massive screens, troop movements charted with chilling precision, and economic forecasts painted a picture of a world meticulously controlled.

But tonight, a different kind of force moved through its hallowed halls. Lucky, Dorchester, Ji-Hyun, Soo-Jin, Hye-Mi, and Mi-Sun, a phantom strike team, moved with the silent grace of ghosts. Dorchester, her lithe form disguised in a UN officer's uniform, led the way, her face a mask of professional calm, her eyes, however, burning with a dangerous fire. Lucky and the others, clad in advanced stealth suits that shimmered with adaptive camouflage, were almost invisible, their movements silent, their presence undetectable.

"Almost there," Dorchester whispered into her comms, her voice a low, steady hum. "Sáoirse's personal guard is concentrated on the main summit hall. This is our window." Her words were a signal, a silent countdown to the inevitable storm.

They reached a reinforced door, its surface a dull, metallic gray, marked with a pulsating red warning light. This was it: the entrance to Sáoirse's private command center, the heart of the snake.

"Ready?" Lucky whispered, his gaze fixed on Dorchester, a silent question in his eyes.

Dorchester's lips curved into a dangerous smile, a glint of anticipation in her eyes. "Born ready." Her hand, gloved and steady, hovered over the door's interface.

Ji-Hyun, however, stepped forward, her hands glowing faintly with an ethereal light. She channeled her elemental energy, a raw, untamed force that pulsed with the power of the earth itself. With a guttural cry, she unleashed a powerful blast of focused wind, a concentrated vortex of air that slammed into the reinforced door. With a deafening CRASH, the door tore from its hinges, crumpling inward like a discarded piece of paper. Alarms blared, a cacophony of sound that shattered the silence, announcing their arrival.
