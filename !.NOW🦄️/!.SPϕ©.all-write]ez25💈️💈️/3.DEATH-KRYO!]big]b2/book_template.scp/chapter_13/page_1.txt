**Chapter 13: Uneasy Peace and Lingering Shadows**

**Page 1**

The deep ocean, moments ago a maelstrom of violence and despair, now hung in an uneasy, almost surreal stillness. The pursuit sub, battered and scarred from its desperate charge, floated amidst the remnants of the Carc-Zilla horde, a silent testament to the battle that had just raged. Sáoirse, though momentarily disoriented by Soo-Jin's resonant frequency attack, had regained her terrifying composure, her dark energy flaring, pushing the horde back into a relentless, mindless assault. Lucky, his Soul Pen glowing with a desperate intensity, gritted his teeth, his jaw tight with effort. "We're not going to make it! There are too many! We're going to be overwhelmed!" His voice was raw, strained, a testament to the overwhelming numbers they faced, a desperate cry against the inevitable.

Ji-Hyun, her face contorted with effort, strained to channel her elemental energy, her hands glowing with a faint, ethereal light. "I can try to create a localized thermal shock! Maybe disrupt their bio-mechanics! It's a long shot, but it's all we have!" A wave of intense heat, followed by extreme cold, emanated from the sub, causing a small pocket of Carc-Zillas to convulse and momentarily freeze, their grotesque forms shimmering with ice, their movements jerky and unnatural. But the effect was fleeting, a mere ripple in the monstrous tide, and the horde quickly closed in, their red eyes burning with renewed malice, their hunger insatiable.

"It's not enough!" Dorchester yelled, her hands flying over the helm, dodging a massive Carc-Zilla claw that scraped against the sub's reinforced hull, the metal groaning in protest. "We need a bigger distraction! Something to break their formation!" Her voice was tight with frustration, her usual cunning momentarily eclipsed by the sheer impossibility of their situation, by the overwhelming odds.

Then, a series of high-frequency sonar pings echoed through the deep, cutting through the chaos like a beacon, a sudden, unexpected sound in the maelstrom of battle. They were distinct, powerful, and utterly unfamiliar, not from Sáoirse, nor from the New-Korea forces. They were from the surface, a distant, almost forgotten world, a world that had seemingly abandoned them to their fate.

"Incoming transmission!" Soo-Jin exclaimed, her eyes wide with disbelief as she stared at her console, her fingers flying across the holographic interface. "From the surface! It's... a global broadcast!" The main viewscreen flickered, overriding the tactical display, replacing the terrifying image of the Carc-Zilla horde with the stern, composed face of an anchorwoman. Her voice, crisp and authoritative, cut through the chaos, a surreal announcement amidst the ongoing battle. "...we interrupt this broadcast with breaking news. Sources confirm that a historic, albeit unexpected, truce has been signed between the United Nations and the United States of America. This unprecedented agreement comes amidst escalating global tensions, with reports of a direct threat of intervention from the People's Republic of China if a peaceful resolution was not immediately reached."
