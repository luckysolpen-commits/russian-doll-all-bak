**Chapter 9: Calm Before the Battle**

The deployment vessel, once a symbol of their audacious plan, was now a twisted, mangled wreckage, consumed by the very anomaly it had sought to unleash. On the bridge, chaos reigned. Alarms shrieked, their piercing cries echoing through the mangled metal. Lights flashed a frantic, blood-red, painting the scene in a macabre glow. The crew, a blur of desperate motion, scrambled to stabilize the ship, but it was a futile effort. Outside the viewport, the massive energy anomaly from the malfunctioning device pulsed with a terrifying, growing intensity, a vortex of dark energy that threatened to swallow everything in its path.

"I can't shut it down!" Lucky's voice, raw with desperation, tore through the cacophony. He was still at the console, his fingers flying across the unresponsive holographic interface, his mind racing to find a solution. "It's drawing power from everywhere! It's self-sustaining!" The device, once his tool, had become a monstrous, uncontrollable entity.

Dorchester, her face pale with a dawning horror, grabbed his arm, her grip like a vise. "We have to get out of here! Now! This thing is going to consume everything!" Her usual composure had shattered, replaced by a primal fear.

"All personnel, abandon ship! Repeat, abandon ship! The device is unstable!" The New-Korea General's voice, distorted by static, crackled over the comms, a desperate command that confirmed their worst fears. There was no saving the vessel, no containing the anomaly.

They scrambled towards an escape pod, the vessel groaning under the immense strain, its metal protesting with a tortured shriek. Just as they sealed the hatch, a massive wave of dark energy, a silent, consuming force, engulfed the deployment vessel, tearing it apart with an almost casual ease. The escape pod was violently ejected, tumbling through the turbulent water, a tiny speck of hope in a sea of destruction.

**EXT. OCEAN SURFACE - DAY**

The escape pod breached the surface, bobbing amidst a sea of debris, a stark contrast to the clear, azure sky above. The sun, warm and inviting, shone down, its rays dancing on the gentle ripples of the ocean. The chaos beneath the waves seemed a distant nightmare, a fading echo in the bright light of day.

They were rescued by a New-Korea patrol boat, its sleek form cutting through the water with effortless grace. The crew, their faces etched with concern, quickly ferried them to a nearby coastal city, a haven of peace after the maelstrom.
