"Our fusion reactors, powered by geothermal vents deep within the earth's crust, provide more than enough," the General replied, a genuine smile touching his lips, a rare display of warmth. "But the device requires a specific frequency of sonar signals to activate and control. A unique signature, a precise harmonic resonance." He looked directly at Lucky, his gaze piercing, assessing, a silent challenge. "Your reputation, Lucky, as a master of sonic technology, as the creator of the Soul Pen, precedes you. Your 'Soul Pen' device, while designed for other purposes, operates on similar principles. We believe you possess the unique insight and skill to interface with our device, to truly unleash its potential, to wield its power." His words were a heavy burden, a monumental responsibility.

Dorchester stepped forward, placing a hand on Lucky's shoulder, a subtle gesture of support and encouragement, a silent promise of solidarity. "This is it, Lucky. Our chance to hit them where it hurts. To turn their own weapon, their own arrogance, against them." Her voice was low, but filled with a fierce, unwavering determination.

Gem, ever cautious, ever the voice of reason, voiced her concerns. "It's a huge risk. What if it's another trap? What if they're just using us?" The memory of the Soul Pen's betrayal, of the UN's treachery, was still fresh, a raw wound.

"We just escaped one," Spike added, his arms crossed, his gaze unwavering, his massive frame a silent, immovable presence. "What makes this different? Why should we trust them?"

The Mer-Elder, with a gentle, reassuring tone, addressed their fears, his ancient eyes filled with a profound, calming wisdom. "We offer not a trap, but an alliance. Our survival, and the survival of the ocean, depends on it. We seek not to dominate, but to protect, to preserve the delicate balance of life in the deep." His words carried the weight of ancient wisdom, a promise of genuine partnership, a plea for understanding.

Lucky looked from the pulsating device to the faces of the council, then to his friends. The scale of the problem was immense, almost overwhelming, but the potential solution was equally grand, equally audacious. "And what do you want in return?" he asked, his voice steady, resolute, betraying no hint of the turmoil within him. "Beyond just deploying this device. What is your true price?"

The New-Korea General's smile widened slightly, a flicker of genuine hope in his eyes. "We want the UN's genocidal plans to end. We want peace. And we want to ensure that such a threat can never rise again. We believe your unique technological insights, combined with our resources, our unwavering resolve, could secure that future." His words were a powerful appeal, a vision of a better world.
