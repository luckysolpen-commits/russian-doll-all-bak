"This is... incredible," Lucky breathed, his voice filled with a genuine, almost childlike awe. The sheer beauty and complexity of Sub-Korea was overwhelming, a stark contrast to the darkness and danger they had just left behind. It was a vision of what humanity and other sentient species could achieve when they worked together, a beacon of hope in a world teetering on the brink of war, a testament to the power of cooperation and mutual respect.

"Welcome to Sub-Korea," Dorchester said, a rare hint of pride, almost tenderness, in her voice. "The heart of the New-Korea alliance. They've been fighting the UN's encroachment for decades. A silent, relentless war. And they're not alone." She led them through the bustling streets, past vibrant markets selling exotic seafood and gleaming tech stalls displaying holographic devices that shimmered with intricate, impossible designs. The air was filled with the murmur of various languages, a symphony of diverse cultures, and the soft, rhythmic hum of unseen machinery. The scent of freshly prepared kelp noodles mingled with the faint, sweet aroma of bioluminescent flowers.

They arrived at a grand, circular building, its entrance guarded by imposing, yet elegant, New-Korea soldiers in sleek, form-fitting armor that seemed to ripple with contained energy. Their presence was a silent testament to the city's strength and its unwavering resolve, a clear message to any who would threaten its peace. The building itself seemed to hum with a quiet power, its walls radiating a sense of ancient wisdom and modern strength.

The chamber was vast and circular, its walls adorned with intricate, swirling patterns that seemed to shift and flow with the currents, mimicking the ebb and flow of the ocean itself. Their expressions were stern, their posture regal, their eyes holding the weight of centuries of conflict and wisdom, of battles fought and sacrifices made.

Beside him sat a stern, yet dignified, New-Korea General, his uniform adorned with intricate medals that gleamed in the soft light, each one a testament to a victory hard-won. His face was etched with the lines of experience, his gaze sharp and unwavering.
