"But why?" Spike demanded, his frustration evident, his voice echoing slightly in the confined space. "What's the endgame? What do they hope to gain from all this destruction?"

"Control. Pure and simple," Dorchester replied, her voice hardening, a steely edge entering her tone. "If they can control the oceans, they control the world. And the clone-merpeople are a significant power bloc down here. They have alliances, resources, a vast, untapped wealth of knowledge and technology. They're an obstacle to the UN's global dominion, a thorn in their side that needs to be removed." The pieces of the puzzle, once disparate and confusing, were slowly, horrifyingly, beginning to click into place, forming a terrifying picture of ambition, ruthlessness, and a chilling disregard for life.

As they spoke, the natural rock formations of the caves began to give way to smooth, polished surfaces. The bioluminescent flora receded, replaced by sleek, artificial lighting that cast a soft, even glow, a stark contrast to the organic beauty they had just left. They passed through a shimmering energy barrier, a subtle ripple in the water that marked the transition from the wild, untamed depths to a meticulously engineered environment. And then, suddenly, they were in a city. A bustling, vibrant metropolis that defied all expectations, a testament to ingenuity and resilience.

Sub-Korea. It was a marvel of futuristic architecture, a testament to human ingenuity and the harmonious, almost symbiotic, integration of technology with the natural world. Sleek, metallic buildings, their surfaces gleaming under artificial sunlight, rose from the ocean floor like colossal, polished sculptures, reaching towards the unseen surface. They were connected by transparent tubes, through which citizens traveled in silent, efficient pods, their movements a blur of color and light. Schools of colorful fish, seemingly unperturbed by the urban environment, swam alongside advanced sub-vehicles, their movements a graceful, silent dance in the clear water. It was a city that hummed with life, a testament to technological prowess and harmonious living, a beacon of hope in a world teetering on the brink of war.

Mer-people, with their iridescent scales and flowing, multi-hued hair, moved freely alongside humans and other aquatic beings. They were not the primitive, mythical creatures the surface world imagined, but a sophisticated, advanced civilization, their culture rich and ancient, their technology far beyond anything Lucky had ever witnessed. Their faces, serene and intelligent, held a quiet dignity that spoke of centuries of wisdom, of a deep connection to the ocean and its mysteries.
