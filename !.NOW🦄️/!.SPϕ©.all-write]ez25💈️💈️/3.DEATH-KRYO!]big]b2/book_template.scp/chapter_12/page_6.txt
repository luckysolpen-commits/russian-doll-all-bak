It's a vulnerability!" Her fingers flew across the console, a blur of motion, her mind racing, already formulating a plan.

"Can you amplify it?" Lucky asked, a plan forming in his mind, a desperate, last-ditch effort, a flicker of hope in the overwhelming darkness. "Channel it through the sub's sonar? Can you turn their own weapon against them? Can you use their hive mind against them?"

"It'll be risky!" Soo-Jin warned, her voice tight with apprehension, her brow furrowed in concentration. "It could fry our systems! It could tear the sub apart! It could kill us all!"

"Do it!" Dorchester commanded, her voice ringing with authority, with an unwavering resolve. "We don't have a choice! It's our only chance!" Soo-Jin worked furiously, rerouting power, pushing the sub's systems to their breaking point, her fingers flying across the console, a desperate dance of life and death. A high-pitched, agonizing whine filled the sub, then blasted outwards, a wave of pure sonic energy, a deafening scream that ripped through the water, a sound that seemed to tear at the very fabric of reality.

The Carc-Zillas reacted violently. Their movements became erratic, uncoordinated, their monstrous forms thrashing in the water, their red eyes flickering with confusion and pain. Some crashed into each other, their metallic bodies grinding, sparks flying, their bio-mechanical forms tearing themselves apart. Others writhed in apparent agony, their monstrous roars turning into pained whimpers, their power diminishing. Sáoirse, on her command Carc-Zilla, clutched her head, her dark energy flickering, her control wavering, her power diminishing, her empire crumbling.

"No!" Sáoirse screamed, her voice distorted by rage, by a desperate, animalistic fury. "You will not defy me! You will not stop me! I am inevitable!" She redoubled her efforts, forcing the Carc-Zillas back into formation, but the disruption had bought the merpeople and New-Korea forces precious time. They rallied, launching a counter-attack, their tridents and energy weapons flashing in the gloom, a renewed sense of hope filling their hearts, a turning point in the battle.

"Now!" Lucky yelled to Dorchester. "Full speed! Straight for Sáoirse!" The pursuit sub, battered but determined, surged forward, a tiny spearhead aimed at the heart of the monstrous horde, a desperate gamble for the fate of the deep, for the fate of the world, for the future of humanity.
