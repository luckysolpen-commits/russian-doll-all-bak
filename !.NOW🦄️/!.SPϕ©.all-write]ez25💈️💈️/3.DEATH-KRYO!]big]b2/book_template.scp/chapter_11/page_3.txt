Soo-Jin, nimble and quick, was a digital phantom, a blur of motion and light. She used her hacking skills to disrupt the Super-Soldiers' comms and targeting systems, causing confusion in their ranks, their movements becoming erratic and uncoordinated. She threw Tear-It cards, which exploded with blinding flashes of light or disorienting sonic bursts, further disorienting the enemy, turning their own technology against them. Her laughter, a high-pitched trill, echoed through the chaos.

Hye-Mi, despite her gentle nature, was a formidable force, a silent, deadly guardian. She used her Tear-It cards to create shimmering energy shields that deflected incoming fire, the plasma bolts dissipating harmlessly against the translucent barriers. Her touch, usually reserved for healing, now delivered stunning electrical shocks, sending soldiers spasming to the ground, their bodies convulsing uncontrollably. Her movements were graceful, almost ethereal, a dance of defense and retribution.

Mi-Sun, a blur of motion, engaged the Super-Soldiers in brutal hand-to-hand combat. Her movements were precise and powerful, each strike designed to incapacitate, to break, to destroy. She used Tear-It cards to enhance her physical strength, delivering blows that cracked armor and sent even the most augmented soldiers flying, their bodies crashing into consoles, sparks flying. She was a whirlwind of controlled violence, a force of nature.

Dorchester, a whirlwind of lethal grace, danced through the fray, her energy dagger a shimmering arc of destruction. She sliced through armor, her movements fluid and deadly, and used her intimate knowledge of the complex's layout to her advantage, leading the Super-Soldiers into traps and ambushes, turning their own fortress against them. Her eyes, burning with a cold, calculating fury, missed nothing.

Sáoirse watched the battle with a detached amusement, occasionally issuing commands to her remaining guards, her composure almost unnerving, a chilling display of arrogance. She was confident, almost arrogant, in her defenses, believing her Super-Soldiers to be invincible, her victory assured. Her lips were curved in a thin, cruel smile.

One of the Super-Soldiers, larger and more heavily armored than the rest, a hulking brute of muscle and chrome, lunged at Lucky. It was a brutal, enhanced version of the UN agents from the Soul Pen, a living weapon designed for destruction, a monster unleashed.

"Still sending your puppets, Sáoirse?" Lucky gritted, his teeth clenched, as he met the Super-Soldier's charge. He unleashed a powerful blast from his Soul Pen, augmented by a Tear-It card, the combined energy a devastating force. The Super-Soldier crashed into a console, sparks flying, its systems shorting out, its body twitching uncontrollably. The air filled with the smell of burnt electronics.
