**SCENE 8**

**INT. SUB-KOREA COUNCIL CHAMBER - CONTINUOUS**

The air in the council chamber is thick with anticipation. The NEW-KOREA GENERAL stands, his posture commanding, addressing LUCKY and his team.

NEW-KOREA GENERAL
(gesturing to the holographic display, which now shows a detailed map of the ocean with various strategic points highlighted)
The United Nations believes they can control the deep. They believe their Carc-Zillas and their naval might are invincible. They are wrong.

MER-ELDER
(his voice calm but firm)
For generations, our people have lived in harmony with the ocean. We understand its currents, its depths, its power. The UN seeks to dominate it, to exploit it. This cannot stand.

NEW-KOREA GENERAL
We have developed a countermeasure. A device capable of manipulating the very currents of the ocean. With it, we can turn the tides against the UN, create maelstroms that will swallow their fleets, and expose their hidden bases.

He gestures to a sleek, crystalline device, about the size of a human torso, that rises from a pedestal in the center of the table. It pulses with a soft, internal light.

LUCKY
(eyes wide with scientific curiosity)
An oceanic current manipulator? That's... revolutionary. The energy requirements alone would be astronomical.

NEW-KOREA GENERAL
(a hint of pride in his voice)
Our fusion reactors, powered by geothermal vents, provide more than enough. But the device requires a specific frequency of sonar signals to activate and control. A unique signature.

He looks directly at Lucky.

NEW-KOREA GENERAL (CONT'D)
Your reputation, Lucky, as a master of sonic technology, precedes you. Your 'Soul Pen' device, while designed for other purposes, operates on similar principles. We believe you possess the unique insight and skill to interface with our device, to truly unleash its potential.

Dorchester steps forward, placing a hand on Lucky's shoulder.

DORCHESTER
This is it, Lucky. Our chance to hit them where it hurts. To turn their own weapon against them.

GEM
(to Lucky)
It's a huge risk. What if it's another trap?

SPIKE
We just escaped one. What makes this different?

MER-ELDER
(gently)
We offer not a trap, but an alliance. Our survival, and the survival of the ocean, depends on it. We seek not to dominate, but to protect.

LUCKY
(looking from the device to the faces of the council, then to his friends)
And what do you want in return? Beyond just deploying this device.

NEW-KOREA GENERAL
(a slight smile)
We want the UN's genocidal plans to end. We want peace. And we want to ensure that such a threat can never rise again. We believe your unique technological insights, combined with our resources, could secure that future.

Lucky considers this. The scale of the problem is immense, but the potential solution is equally grand. He looks at Dorchester, who gives him a subtle nod of encouragement.

LUCKY
(taking a deep breath)
Alright. We'll consider your proposal. But we'll need to discuss the terms. And we'll need guarantees.

NEW-KOREA GENERAL
(a genuine smile now)
Excellent. Let us move to the negotiation chamber. We are prepared to be... flexible.

**FADE OUT.**
