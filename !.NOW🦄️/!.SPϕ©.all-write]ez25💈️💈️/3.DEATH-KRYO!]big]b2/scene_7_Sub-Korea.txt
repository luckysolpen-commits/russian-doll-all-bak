**SCENE 7**

**INT. CAVE SYSTEM / TRANSITION TO SUB-KOREA - CONTINUOUS**

The team, still recovering from their encounter with Carc-Zilla, navigates the winding, bioluminescent cave system. The water here is crystal clear, revealing ancient rock formations and strange, glowing flora.

LUCKY
(wincing, checking a bruise)
So, the UN is weaponizing Carcazod. That's a new low, even for them.

DORCHESTER
(leading the way)
They've been at it for years. My intel suggests they're trying to create a new strain, something even more virulent. And they're using the clone-merpeople as their test subjects.

GEM
(horrified)
They're experimenting on them?

DORCHESTER
Not directly. They're trying to provoke a conflict. A reason to 'cleanse' the oceans. And the merpeople are just collateral damage.

SPIKE
But why? What's the endgame?

DORCHESTER
Control. Pure and simple. If they can control the oceans, they control the world. And the clone-merpeople are a significant power bloc down here. They have alliances, resources.

As they speak, the caves begin to open up. The natural rock gives way to smooth, polished surfaces. They pass through a shimmering energy barrier, and suddenly, they are in a bustling, vibrant city.

**EXT. SUB-KOREA - DAY**

SUB-KOREA is a marvel of futuristic architecture, seamlessly integrated with the natural underwater environment. Sleek, metallic buildings gleam under artificial sunlight, connected by transparent tubes where citizens travel. Schools of colorful fish swim alongside advanced sub-vehicles. It's a testament to technological prowess and harmonious living.

Mer-people, with their iridescent scales and flowing hair, move freely alongside humans and other aquatic beings. They are not the primitive creatures the surface world imagines, but a sophisticated civilization.

LUCKY
(in awe)
This is... incredible.

DORCHESTER
Welcome to Sub-Korea. The heart of the New-Korea alliance. They've been fighting the UN's encroachment for decades. And they're not alone.

She leads them through the bustling streets, past markets selling exotic seafood and tech stalls displaying holographic devices. The air is filled with the murmur of various languages and the soft hum of machinery.

They arrive at a grand, circular building, its entrance guarded by imposing, yet elegant, New-Korea soldiers in sleek, form-fitting armor.

**INT. SUB-KOREA COUNCIL CHAMBER - CONTINUOUS**

The chamber is vast and circular, with a large holographic display in the center. Around a polished, obsidian table sit several high-ranking officials from both New-Korea and the clone-merpeople factions. Their expressions are stern, their posture regal.

Among them is a wise-looking MER-ELDER, his scales a deep sapphire, and a stern, yet dignified, NEW-KOREA GENERAL, his uniform adorned with intricate medals.

Dorchester steps forward, bowing respectfully.

DORCHESTER
(to the council)
Honored elders, General. I bring Lucky, the one the UN seeks to frame, and his companions. They have witnessed the UN's true intentions.

The New-Korea General's eyes narrow as he studies Lucky.

NEW-KOREA GENERAL
We have heard of you, Lucky. Your reputation precedes you. And your recent... escape... confirms our suspicions about the UN's aggression.

MER-ELDER
(a deep, resonant voice)
The UN seeks to eradicate us, to pave the way for their dominion. They call us a threat, a mutation. But we are simply... different. And we have allies.

Dorchester turns to Lucky, a serious expression on her face.

DORCHESTER
I was once part of the UN's genetic engineering program. I saw the atrocities they planned, the genocidal intent behind their 'humanitarian' efforts. I defected, and I've been working to expose them ever since. The merpeople are not a threat. They are a peaceful, advanced civilization. The UN wants to destroy them to seize their resources and eliminate any opposition to their global control.

Lucky's eyes widen. The pieces are starting to fit together.

LUCKY
So, the UN wants to wipe out the merpeople, and they're using Carc-Zilla as their ultimate weapon.

NEW-KOREA GENERAL
Precisely. But we will not allow it. We have a proposal for you, Lucky. One that could change the course of this war.

**FADE OUT.**
