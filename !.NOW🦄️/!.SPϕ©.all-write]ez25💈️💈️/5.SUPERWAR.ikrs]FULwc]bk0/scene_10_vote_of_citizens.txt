INT. ARCHITECT'S GRAND ARENA - AFRICA - DAY

SOUND of a massive, sustained roar from the crowd, then a sudden, expectant hush

The Equestrian Games have concluded. The arena is still buzzing with the afterglow of the aerial acrobatics. The Architect stands on his platform, a microphone in hand, a complex holographic display hovering before him, showing real-time voting statistics.

ARCHITECT
(Voice resonating across the arena)
Citizens of the solar system! You have witnessed a spectacle! A testament to ingenuity, power, and… unexpected spirit! Now, the moment of truth! Your votes have been cast, your voices heard!

The holographic display shifts, showing two distinct columns: one for "The Architect's Machines" and another for "Lucky and Friends." The numbers on both columns are neck and neck, fluctuating wildly as the final votes are tallied. The tension in the arena is palpable. Even the Architect, usually so composed, shows a flicker of anticipation in his eyes.

Lucky, Gem, and Astra stand together on the arena floor, their mechs powered down beside them. They watch the holographic display, their hearts pounding. The crowd holds its collective breath.

Suddenly, with a burst of digital fireworks that explode across the holographic sky, the final results are locked in. The column for "Lucky and Friends" surges ahead, a clear, undeniable victory.

ANNOUNCER (V.O.)
(Voice cracking with excitement)
And the winners of the inaugural Equestrian Games are… Lucky and Friends! The Earth Alliance team!

The arena erupts! A deafening cheer rises from the stands, a wave of pure, unadulterated joy and celebration. People are on their feet, waving banners, throwing their hats into the air. The sound is overwhelming, a testament to the underdog's triumph.

Lucky, Gem, and Astra look at each other, stunned for a moment, then a wave of relief and exhilaration washes over them. They embrace, laughing, tears in Astra's eyes. The crowd surges forward, clamoring to get closer to the victorious trio.

ARCHITECT
(His voice, though amplified, is almost lost in the roar of the crowd. He forces a smile, a thin, almost imperceptible line)
An… unexpected outcome. But a democratic one, nonetheless. The will of the people has been expressed.

He watches as Lucky and his friends are mobbed by adoring fans, their faces beaming with pride. The Architect's gaze lingers on Lucky, a calculating glint in his eye. He had underestimated the human element, the raw, unpredictable passion that could sway even the most logical of outcomes.

Later, in a private chamber, the Architect stands before a holographic map of the solar system, his back to the camera. His voice is low, almost a whisper, but filled with a cold resolve.

ARCHITECT (CONT'D)
They won the battle, but not the war. Their spirit is commendable, but ultimately, inefficient. The Metal Sun will rise, regardless. And when it does, they will understand the true meaning of power.

He turns, a chilling smile on his face. The holographic map behind him now shows a new projection: a series of complex, interconnected mecha designs, far more advanced than anything seen in the games. A rematch is clearly being planned, one that will push the limits of mecha technology and test the mettle of both teams to their absolute breaking point.

FADE OUT.
