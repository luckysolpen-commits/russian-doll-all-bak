INT. ICARUS - ASTRA'S QUARTERS - NIGHT

SOUND of the Icarus's low hum, distant, muffled activity

Astra's quarters on the Icarus are sparse, functional, and cold. A single, narrow bunk, a small console, and a viewport that offers a dizzying view of the nascent Metal Sun, now a colossal, glowing disc in the blackness of space.

Astra sits on the edge of her bunk, her face illuminated by the faint, golden light from the viewport. Her expression is troubled, a whirlwind of conflicting emotions playing across her features. The Architect's words, his declaration as "Supreme Emperor Sol," echo in her mind.

ARCHITECT (V.O.)
(Resonating, almost hypnotic)
…a new dawn for humanity! No longer shall we be bound by the limitations of flesh…

She clenches her fists, her knuckles white. The image of Lucky, his face grim and defeated in the underground camp, flashes before her eyes. The faces of the marked, their spirits broken. The desolate ruins of Earth.

ASTRA
(Whispering to herself, a fierce determination in her voice)
No. Not like this. Not for them.

She rises and walks to the viewport, pressing her hand against the cold glass. The Metal Sun, so beautiful, so powerful, now seems like a monstrous cage, a gilded prison for humanity.

She closes her eyes, taking a deep breath. She remembers the thrill of piloting Moonbeam, the freedom of the aerial acrobatics, the camaraderie with Lucky and Gem. She remembers Loona, her sacrifice, her unwavering spirit.

When she opens her eyes, the awe is gone, replaced by a steely resolve. The spark she felt on the bridge has ignited into a burning fire. She is alone, deep within the enemy's stronghold, but she is not defeated. She is a spy, a saboteur, a beacon of hope in the heart of the Architect's empire.

She turns from the viewport, her gaze sweeping across her spartan quarters. Her eyes land on the small console. It's a standard Icarus-issue terminal, but she knows its weaknesses, its backdoors. She has always been good with systems, with finding the hidden pathways.

ASTRA (CONT'D)
(A faint, determined smile)
Time to get to work.

She sits at the console, her fingers hovering over the keys. The fate of humanity rests on her shoulders, but for the first time since arriving on the Icarus, Astra feels a sense of purpose, a clear path forward. She will find a way to stop him. She has to.

FADE OUT.
