INT. UNDERGROUND REBEL BASE - GEM'S LAB - DAY

SOUND of whirring computers, soft beeps, the clatter of tools

GEM is hunched over a makeshift workbench in a corner of the underground rebel base. Her lab is a chaotic but organized mess of salvaged electronics, flickering monitors, and intricate wiring. She wears a pair of high-tech goggles, their lenses glowing with data streams. Empty coffee cups litter the table.

She's surrounded by disassembled World Police Bot parts, their metallic shells pried open, their internal components exposed. She meticulously probes a circuit board with a fine-tipped instrument, her brow furrowed in intense concentration.

GEM
(Muttering to herself)
Sub-routine 7-gamma… a recursive loop… designed to bypass standard encryption… clever, Architect, very clever.

She pulls back, rubbing her temples. The sheer complexity of the Architect's technology is daunting. Every piece of hardware, every line of code, is designed to enforce compliance, to suppress dissent. She feels a growing frustration, a sense of being outmatched.

She glances at a small, framed photo on her workbench: a picture of Lucky, Astra, and herself, smiling, before everything changed. A pang of longing, of sadness, hits her. She misses their easy camaraderie, the days when their biggest worry was a challenging engineering problem, not the enslavement of humanity.

She remembers Astra's departure, the fierce determination in her eyes. She remembers Lucky's grim resolve as he was herded into the camp. She is the last one on the outside, the one who must find a way to fight with her mind, with her knowledge.

GEM (CONT'D)
(A sigh, then a renewed determination)
There has to be a backdoor. There always is.

She picks up a small, discarded piece of a bot's optical sensor. She turns it over in her fingers, examining its intricate design. Her eyes light up. A tiny, almost imperceptible FLAW. A manufacturing anomaly, perhaps, or a deliberate, hidden access point.

She quickly connects the sensor to her main console. Lines of code scroll rapidly across her screens. She begins to type, her fingers flying across the holographic keyboard, a flurry of commands and algorithms. She's trying to exploit the flaw, to create a vulnerability in the Architect's seemingly impenetrable network.

Hours pass. The coffee cups multiply. Her eyes are red-rimmed, but her focus is unwavering. She is in her element, a digital warrior battling a technological tyrant.

Suddenly, a series of rapid BEEPS emanates from her console. A green light flashes. She leans closer, her heart pounding. A connection. A faint, almost imperceptible signal, originating from… the Icarus.

GEM (CONT'D)
(A gasp, a whisper of disbelief)
Astra?

She quickly works to stabilize the connection, to decipher the faint data stream. It's fragmented, encrypted, but it's there. A lifeline. A message from the heart of the enemy's empire. A new hope.

She knows this is just the beginning. The Architect's network is vast, his control absolute. But now, she has a foothold. A way in. And with Astra on the inside, and Lucky fighting from below, the tide might just turn. The hum of her computers seems to take on a new, more hopeful rhythm.

FADE OUT.
