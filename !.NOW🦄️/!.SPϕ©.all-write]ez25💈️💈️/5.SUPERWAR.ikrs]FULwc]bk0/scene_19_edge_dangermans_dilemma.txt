INT. METAL SUN - LABORER'S QUARTERS - NIGHT

SOUND of distant, rhythmic hammering, the hum of massive machinery

EDGE DANGERMAN sits on a makeshift cot in a cramped, utilitarian bunkroom on the Metal Sun. The room is one of many, identical and sterile, designed for maximum efficiency and minimal comfort. Through a small, reinforced viewport, the golden glow of the Metal Sun's interior is visible, a constant, blinding reminder of their forced labor.

Edge runs a calloused hand over his face, exhaustion etched into every line. He's no longer the slick, underworld boss. He's a leader of men, but these men are prisoners, forced to build their own cage. The "Metal Mafia" is less a union and more a desperate collective of enslaved laborers.

He pulls out a small, encrypted comm device, the same one he used to contact Lucky. He stares at it, his thumb hovering over the send button. He has information, critical data about the Metal Sun's vulnerabilities, gathered by his network of disgruntled workers. But sending it is a risk, a massive one.

He glances at the other laborers in the bunkroom, some sleeping fitfully, others staring blankly at the ceiling. They are tired, broken, but a flicker of defiance remains in their eyes. He knows he owes it to them, to Lucky, to humanity.

Suddenly, a soft, almost imperceptible BEEP comes from the comm device. A new message. He opens it, his eyes scanning the text. It's from one of his contacts, deep within the Architect's inner circle. The message is short, chilling:

"Astra is here. On the Icarus. Unmarked."

Edge's eyes widen. Astra. Here? On the Architect's flagship? A wave of conflicting emotions washes over him: surprise, fear, and a desperate, fragile hope. If Astra is here, there's a chance. A slim one, but a chance nonetheless.

He looks at the comm device again, then at the glowing Metal Sun outside the viewport. The Architect's empire is vast, powerful, seemingly unassailable. But now, there's a crack. A tiny, almost invisible crack.

He takes a deep breath, his resolve hardening. He can't fight the Architect head-on. But he can be a thorn in his side. He can provide the intelligence, the support, the distraction that Lucky and Astra will need.

He begins to type on the comm device, his fingers moving with renewed purpose. The message is for Lucky, a coded warning, a hint of the new player in the game. He knows the risks. He knows the Architect's reach is long. But for the first time in a long time, Edge Dangerman feels a flicker of his old self, the cunning, resourceful leader who always found a way to survive.

FADE OUT.
