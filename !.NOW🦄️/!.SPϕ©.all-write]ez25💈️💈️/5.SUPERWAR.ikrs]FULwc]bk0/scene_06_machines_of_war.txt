INT. ARCHITECT'S GRAND ARENA - AFRICA - DAY

SOUND of rhythmic, heavy thuds, mechanical whirs

The arena floor is now clear, awaiting the grand entrance. The crowd buzzes with anticipation. Suddenly, a massive, ornate gate at one end of the arena slowly grinds open, revealing a dark, cavernous tunnel.

From the darkness, the first of the ARCHITECT'S METAL HORSES emerges. It's a magnificent, terrifying sight. Gleaming bronze armor covers its powerful, equine form. Its eyes, twin points of crimson light, scan the crowd. It moves with an unsettling, almost organic grace, its hooves striking the ground with a heavy, metallic clang. There are no riders, yet they prance and rear with an eerie sentience, their movements defying gravity as they leap and soar through the air.

More Metal Horses follow, a squadron of mechanical steeds, each one a masterpiece of engineering and intimidation. They form a disciplined formation in the center of the arena, their crimson eyes fixed forward.

A low, guttural GROWL reverberates through the arena, shaking the very ground. The crowd gasps. From the same dark tunnel, a shadow detaches itself from the gloom, growing larger, more defined.

It's the METAL DRAGON. A behemoth of a mech, easily twice the size of the Metal Horses. Its scales are jet-black, absorbing the arena lights, making it seem like a void given form. Its head, a serpentine mass of articulated metal, sports glowing red eyes that burn with an infernal light. Massive, segmented wings, like those of a dark, metallic butterfly, slowly unfold from its back, casting a long, ominous shadow over the entire arena.

The Metal Dragon lets out another, louder ROAR, a sound that is more machine than beast, yet filled with a primal power. It settles onto the arena floor, its sheer presence dominating the space. The Metal Horses shift, almost deferentially, around it.

Lucky, Gem, and Astra, still in the crowd, watch with a mixture of awe and dread. The Architect's technological prowess is undeniable, and the machines before them are truly formidable.

LUCKY
(Muttering to Gem and Astra)
He wasn't kidding. These things are… something else.

GEM
(Adjusting her glasses, a professional admiration in her voice despite the danger)
The articulation on the Dragon's wings… the energy signature from the Horses… it's beyond anything I've ever analyzed.

ASTRA
(Her hand instinctively going to a hidden compartment on her belt)
Beautiful, in a terrifying way. But beauty won't stop them.

The Architect, from his platform, surveys his creations with a look of profound satisfaction, almost adoration. The crowd erupts in thunderous applause, captivated by the sheer spectacle of the machines of war.

FADE OUT.
