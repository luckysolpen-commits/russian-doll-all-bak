INT. SCRIBE'S HOLLOW - LOWER CHAMBER - DAY

SOUND of a creak of wood, a shuffle of feet

Elara freezes, slipping the Soulpen into her sleeve and dousing the lantern. Her INKLING, perched on her shoulder, shimmers and tries to flatten itself against her cloak, sensing danger. Elara creeps to the staircase, peering into the darkness below. A shadow moves in the lower chamber, too tall to be a stray animal.

ELARA
(voice sharp, though she feels anything but)
Who's there?

No answer, but the shadow stills. Elara's hand tightens on the blade hidden in her satchel. She descends slowly, each step deliberate, her eyes fixed on the form below.

The shadow shifts, stepping into a sliver of moonlight from a cracked window. Its hood falls back, revealing TORREN (30s-40s, sharp features, eyes like chipped obsidian), cloaked in gray. Perched on his shoulder, almost invisible against his dark cloak, is a SHADOW SPRITE, a small, bat-like creature that blends seamlessly into the gloom.

TORREN
(voice low, almost a growl)
Elara Veyn. You shouldn't be here.

ELARA
(keeping the blade hidden but ready)
How do you know my name?

Torren raises his hands, showing they are empty. A faint, knowing smile plays on his lips. The Shadow Sprite twitches its ears, its tiny head cocked as if listening intently.

TORREN
The Soulpen calls to those who listen. And I've been listening a long time.
