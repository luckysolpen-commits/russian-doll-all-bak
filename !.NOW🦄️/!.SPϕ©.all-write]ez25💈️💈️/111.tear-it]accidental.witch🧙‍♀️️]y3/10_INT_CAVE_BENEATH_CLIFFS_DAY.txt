INT. CAVE BENEATH CLIFFS - DAY

Halfway down the treacherous hidden trail, Torren stops, pointing to a cave mouth barely visible in the rock face. His SHADOW SPRITE hovers near the entrance, its tiny eyes scanning the darkness.

TORREN
In there. We'll wait out the wardens. They won't think to look this far down.

Elara hesitates, then follows him into the darkness. Her INKLING, perched on her shoulder, shimmers nervously. The cave is damp, its walls slick with moss. The air is heavy, ancient.

Torren lights a small fire with practiced ease, the flickering light revealing carvings on the cave walls – runes like those in the Scribe's Hollow, but older, worn smooth by time. Elara traces one with her finger, feeling the Soulpen hum in her satchel, a faint vibration that resonates with the ancient symbols. The Inkling pulses in sync with the pen.

ELARA
What are these?

TORREN
Wards. From when the Soulpen was guarded, not hidden. This cave was a refuge for its keepers. A training ground, even.

Elara's chest tightens. A sudden realization dawns on her.

ELARA
My mother came here, didn't she?

Torren nods, his gaze distant. The Shadow Sprite settles on his shoulder, its form almost invisible against the cave wall.

TORREN
She was the last true keeper. Before she... changed things. She wrote something that shouldn't have been written. Something that broke the veil. I don't know what, but it's why she's gone—and why the Soulpen chose you. She unleashed chaos.

Elara's hand closes around the satchel, gripping the Soulpen. Its warmth is a pulse now, steady as a heartbeat. She wants to write, to demand more answers, to force the truth from the pen, but Torren's warning echoes in her mind: *Every word costs something.* She remembers the pain, the unsettling vision. The Inkling shimmers, its form darkening slightly.

Outside, the faint sounds of the wardens searching above begin to fade. Dawn is breaking, painting the cave mouth a soft gold.

Elara stands, slinging the satchel over her shoulder, her resolve hardening.

ELARA
We go to the Ashen Wastes. But I'm not destroying the pen. I'm going to master it. I'm going to understand what she did, and why.

Torren's jaw tightens, but he nods, a flicker of respect in his eyes. The Shadow Sprite flits to his hand, as if in agreement.

TORREN
Then we'd better move. The wardens won't stop looking, and neither will the ones who want the pen for themselves. And the veil... it's growing thinner. The chaos will only spread.
