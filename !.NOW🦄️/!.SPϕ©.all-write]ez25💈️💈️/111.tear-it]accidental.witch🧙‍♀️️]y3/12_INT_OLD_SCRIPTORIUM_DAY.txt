INT. OLD SCRIPTORIUM - DAY

SOUND of wind whistling through broken windows, dust motes dancing in sunbeams

Elara, Torren, and Kai finally arrive at the OLD SCRIPTORIUM, a vast, crumbling library carved into the side of a mountain. It's a ruin, but its scale is breathtaking. Shelves, once filled with countless tomes, now stand empty or toppled, their contents scattered across the dusty floor. The air is thick with the scent of ancient parchment and decay.

Elara's INKLING, perched on her shoulder, shimmers with excitement, its tendrils reaching out as if to touch the ancient magic. Elara's eyes widen in awe and reverence. This is where the Soulpen was born. She moves through the debris, her fingers tracing the faint outlines of runes on the walls, similar to those in the Scribe's Hollow, but far more intricate and powerful.

Kai, ever practical, begins to search for anything valuable, his eyes darting around the vast space. His MISCHIEF GREMLIN zips around, occasionally knocking over a dusty scroll or chittering at a cobweb. Torren, meanwhile, moves with a quiet intensity, his gaze sweeping over the architecture, as if searching for a memory. His SHADOW SPRITE flits silently from shadow to shadow, observing.

Elara finds a section of the scriptorium that seems less damaged, a small alcove where some scrolls and fragmented texts remain. She carefully begins to sift through them, the Soulpen in her satchel humming faintly in response to the ancient magic. The Inkling pulses in sync with the pen.

She discovers texts detailing the Soulpen's creation – not as a mere writing tool, but as a conduit for raw creation, a bridge between thought and reality. It was designed to record and shape the very fabric of existence, but its power was deemed too dangerous, too volatile.

Elara finds fragmented accounts of the ancient order that guarded the Soulpen, their attempts to control its immense power, and their eventual downfall. She pieces together that the pen was meant to be a tool for understanding, not for direct manipulation of the veil.

She uncovers Lirien's personal journals, hidden beneath a loose stone. They are filled with frantic, desperate entries. Lirien's research wasn't just about understanding the veil; it was about *crossing* it, about finding a way to bring something or someone back. The entries grow increasingly erratic, detailing her experiments, her growing obsession, and the unintended consequences of her actions – minor tears in the veil, strange energies leaking into the world.

Elara reads a chilling passage:

LIRIEN (V.O.)
(from the journal)
The veil thins. The ink flows. I am so close. Just one more truth... one more word to bridge the gap. They say it cannot be undone. But I will prove them wrong. I *must*.

Elara looks up from the journal, her face pale. The truth of her mother's recklessness, and the scale of the chaos she unleashed, begins to sink in. The Soulpen in her satchel feels heavier than ever, a burden of immense power and responsibility. The Inkling shimmers, its form darkening slightly, as if sensing Elara's distress.
