EXT. SHATTERVEIL CLIFFS - DAWN

SOUND of crashing waves, salt-laced wind

The sky is a bruised purple, dawn still an hour away. ELARA VEYN (19, earnest, a dreamer with a hidden strength) stands at the cliff’s edge, her simple, practical cloak snapping in the fierce, salt-streaked wind.

Perched on her shoulder is a small, shimmering creature made of living ink, her INKLING, which subtly shifts its form, mimicking the wind's currents.

Her fingers clutch a worn leather satchel, its contents a secret even to her. She stares out at the churning sea below, which roars like a beast that never sleeps.

V.O. ELARA
They say the world holds its breath at this hour. That old magics stir.

Elara reaches into her satchel and pulls out a black wooden PEN. Its nib glints like a shard of starlight. It’s heavier than it looks, warm against her calloused fingers. She holds it up, examining it with a mixture of awe and trepidation.

The Inkling on her shoulder pulses faintly, its shimmering form momentarily solidifying as it senses the Soulpen's magic.

V.O. ELARA
My mother left me this. I never dared touch it until last night. When the dreams came.

FLASHBACK - QUICK CUTS
- Ink spilling into rivers.
- Words carving mountains.
- Elara’s own hands, writing truths she doesn’t understand.
END FLASHBACK

Elara clutches the pen tightly, her knuckles white. She looks back towards the distant, sleeping village of Quillhaven, then out at the vast, indifferent ocean. A decision hardens in her eyes.
