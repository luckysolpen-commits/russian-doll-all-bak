EXT. SHATTERVEIL CLIFFS - DAY

Elara and Torren emerge from the alleys, making their way back towards the Shatterveil Cliffs. The wind is still strong, tearing at Elara's cloak. Her INKLING, now perched on her wrist, shimmers with the effort of staying put.

At the cliff's edge, Torren stops, looking out at the vast, churning sea. His SHADOW SPRITE flits around his head, agitated by the open space.

TORREN
You can't keep it, Elara. Not here. It'll consume you. Or worse, others will find you. Ones far more dangerous than Quillhaven's wardens.

Elara faces him, her expression defiant. The Inkling pulses faintly.

ELARA
I told you, I'm not giving it up. It's my mother's. I need to understand it. Understand what happened to her.

Torren studies her, a flicker of something unreadable in his eyes – perhaps a hint of understanding, or resignation. The Shadow Sprite settles on his shoulder, its tiny head cocked.

TORREN
Then you need answers. Real answers. Not just what the pen whispers.

He gestures vaguely towards the desolate horizon.

TORREN (CONT'D)
There's a place. An old scriptorium in the Ashen Wastes. It's where the Soulpen was made. If you want answers, if you want to truly control that thing, that's where they'll be. It's your only hope.

Elara looks from the Soulpen in her satchel to the forbidding landscape Torren indicated. The Ashen Wastes. Stories of barren lands, air tasting of cinders, a place no one went willingly. But the alternative was to give up the pen, to give up on her mother.

Elara, despite her deep distrust of Torren, sees the logic in his words. She has no other path.

ELARA
(a determined nod)
Lead the way.
