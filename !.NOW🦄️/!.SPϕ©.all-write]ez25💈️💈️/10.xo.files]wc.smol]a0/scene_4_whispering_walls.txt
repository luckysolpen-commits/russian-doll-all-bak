SCENE 4: THE WHISPERING WALLS

[INT. AFINK VILLAGE - VARIOUS LOCATIONS - DAY]

Days turn into nights in the Afink Village. LUCKY, still recovering from his ordeal, observes the daily life of the Afinkers. He learns their customs, their songs, their quiet reverence for the land.

But a subtle shift begins to occur. A low, almost imperceptible HUM starts to permeate the air. It's not the natural hum of the jungle, but something else, something artificial.

Lucky notices villagers whispering to each other, their eyes darting nervously towards the dense jungle that surrounds them. They cast furtive glances at the walls of their huts, as if listening to something only they can hear.

He approaches LYRA, who is meticulously weaving a colorful tapestry outside her hut. Her brow is furrowed in concentration, but her movements are slightly agitated.

LUCKY
Lyra, have you noticed anything... strange? A sound?

Lyra pauses her weaving, her fingers still on the threads. She looks around cautiously, then leans in closer.

LYRA
(Whispering)
The walls are whispering, Lucky. They have been for days. Ancient knowledge, hidden beneath the surface.

Lucky frowns. He listens intently, straining his ears. He can hear the jungle, the distant calls of creatures, the rustle of leaves. But no whispers.

LUCKY
I don't hear anything.

Lyra touches the mud-brick wall of her hut, her fingers tracing the intricate patterns etched into its surface. A faint, almost imperceptible VIBRATION emanates from it.

LYRA
It is not with your ears you hear it, Lucky. It is with your spirit. The Extrmophiles' city is calling us.

Lucky's eyes widen. The vision from the Heart of the Ink flashes in his mind – the impossible city, floating above the sea. He remembers the feeling of being drawn to it.

LUCKY
Calling you? What do you mean?

Lyra looks at him, her eyes holding a deep, ancient wisdom that belies her youth.

LYRA
They are stirring from their slumber. Their city is awakening. And we... we must be prepared for its return.

Lucky feels a shiver run down his spine. The casualness with which Lyra speaks of this awakening is unsettling. He realizes that this is not just a rumor, but a deep-seated belief, a premonition.

The low hum in the air seems to intensify, a subtle thrumming that Lucky can now feel in his bones. The whispers, though still inaudible to his human ears, feel like a tangible presence, a silent chorus emanating from the very fabric of the village.

[FADE OUT.]
