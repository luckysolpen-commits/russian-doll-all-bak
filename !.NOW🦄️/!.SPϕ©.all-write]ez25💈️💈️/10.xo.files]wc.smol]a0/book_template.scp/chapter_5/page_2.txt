They began to move, cautiously at first, then with a growing sense of urgency. The plaza was vast, stretching out to an unseen horizon, and they were exposed. They needed cover, shelter, a place to hide. They moved like shadows, their footsteps echoing in the unnerving silence. The air was cold, metallic, and carried the faint, unsettling hum of unseen machinery. It was a sound that was both constant and pervasive, a low thrum that vibrated through the very foundations of the city, through the soles of his feet, through his very bones. It was the sound of a vast, complex machine, a machine that was alive, and that was now awake. They passed by towering structures that seemed to defy gravity, their surfaces rippling with a subtle, internal light. They saw strange, crystalline sculptures that lined the deserted thoroughfares, their delicate structures catching the alien light, casting long, distorted shadows. The city was a labyrinth, a maze of impossible angles and shifting perspectives. Every turn brought a new vista, a new impossible structure, a new sense of overwhelming scale. It was a city built for giants, for beings who thought in dimensions he could not perceive. And they were tiny, insignificant specks, lost in its vast, alien embrace. They heard a sound, a low, guttural chant that seemed to emanate from the very foundations of the city. It was a sound that was both ancient and primal, a sound that spoke of power, of dominance, of… hunger. It was the sound of the Extrmophiles. And it was getting closer. They ducked into a narrow alleyway, pressing themselves against a cold, smooth wall, their hearts pounding in their chests. They were trapped. They were exposed. And they were about to meet the inhabitants of this impossible city, face to face. He felt a cold dread settle in his stomach. He had survived the jungle, the hounds, the crash. But this… this was a different kind of danger. This was a danger that was intelligent, ancient, and utterly alien. This was a danger that he was not prepared for.
