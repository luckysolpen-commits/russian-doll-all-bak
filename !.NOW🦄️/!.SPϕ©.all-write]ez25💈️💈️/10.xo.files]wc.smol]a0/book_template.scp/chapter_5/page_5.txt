After what felt like an eternity, a faint, discordant chatter reached their ears. It was a sound that was utterly alien, yet strangely familiar. It wasn’t the guttural chants of the guards, nor the rhythmic thump of unseen machinery. It was something different, something almost… mercantile. A low murmur of voices, a rustle of movement, a clinking of unseen objects. It was a sound that spoke of life, of interaction, of something other than cold, sterile oppression. Lucky felt a flicker of hope, a tiny spark in the vast, overwhelming darkness. He looked at Lyra, her eyes wide with a similar realization. “Do you hear that?” he whispered, his voice hoarse. Lyra nodded, her gaze fixed on the source of the sound. “It is… a market,” she breathed, her voice filled with a mixture of wonder and apprehension. A market. In this city of silent, predatory giants. It seemed impossible. But the sound was undeniable. It was a beacon, a promise of something different, something that might offer a way out of this suffocating labyrinth. They moved cautiously towards the sound, their footsteps silent on the obsidian-like surface. Every shadow was a potential hiding place, every alcove a temporary sanctuary. They were still in enemy territory, still surrounded by unknown dangers. But now, there was a destination. A purpose. A reason to keep moving. The chatter grew louder, more distinct. He could almost make out individual voices, though the language was still alien to him. He could smell it now, too. A strange, exotic aroma, a mixture of pungent spices and something sweet, almost floral. It was the smell of commerce, of trade, of life. And it filled him with a desperate, almost irrational hope. They were not alone. There were others. And perhaps, just perhaps, those others might not be as hostile as the guards. Perhaps they might be… allies. Or at least, not enemies. It was a gamble. But in this city of shadows, a gamble was all they had.
