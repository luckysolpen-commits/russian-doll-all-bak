Lucky glanced at Lyra, who gave him a subtle nod. It was a silent communication, a shared understanding that transcended language. She was telling him to be bold, to ask for what they needed. He took a deep breath, his heart still hammering against his ribs, but a newfound confidence blooming in his chest. “We’ll trade them for shelter and food,” he replied, his voice surprisingly steady. “And perhaps some… information about the city. Its customs. Its… inhabitants.” The Kha’Zix woman’s single eye gleamed with a calculating intensity. A slow, unsettling smile spread across her sharp, turtle-like mouth, revealing rows of needle-sharp teeth. It was not a smile of warmth or welcome, but of shrewd assessment, of a predator recognizing a valuable opportunity. “I think we can come to an arrangement,” she said, her voice a low purr. “Follow me.” She turned, her movements fluid and predatory, leading them deeper into the market. Her name, he would soon learn, was Zyra. Lucky and Lyra exchanged a look. It was a look of shared apprehension, of grim determination. They were walking into the lion’s den, into the heart of a civilization that had already proven its ruthlessness. They had no idea what awaited them, what price they would truly pay for this desperate gamble. But they had no other choice. They were hungry, exhausted, and utterly lost. And for the first time since they had arrived in this impossible city, they had a lead. A thread to follow. A chance, however slim, to survive. They followed Zyra, their footsteps echoing in the vast, alien marketplace. The scent of exotic spices, once a source of comfort, now seemed to carry a hint of danger, a premonition of the unknown. The glowing ink flowers in Lucky’s hand felt heavy, their beauty a fragile shield against the encroaching darkness. They were in. But at what cost? The answer, he knew, would soon be revealed.
