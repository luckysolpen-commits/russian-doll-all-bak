“How much will you ask for it?” Lucky inquired, his voice betraying a hint of the desperation he felt. He had nothing of value, no currency, no technology that would be of any interest to these advanced beings. He was a scientist, not a merchant. He was about to admit his lack, to apologize for his intrusion, when Zyra’s sharp, turtle-like mouth widened into a smile, revealing rows of needle-sharp teeth. It was not a smile of warmth or welcome, but of shrewd assessment, of a predator recognizing a valuable opportunity. “Ah, the price of secrets is always steep,” she purred, her single eye glinting with amusement. “But for you, I’ll offer a special deal. One ink flower.” Lucky and Lyra exchanged a look. One ink flower. It was a ridiculously low price for something so clearly powerful, so clearly ancient. It was too good to be true. A trap? Or an opportunity too good to pass up? Lucky’s mind raced, weighing the risks, calculating the odds. His scientific training screamed caution, warned him of hidden dangers, of unforeseen consequences. But Lyra’s gaze was fixed on the box, her eyes wide with a desperate, almost childlike curiosity. She nodded, a silent agreement, a silent plea. He trusted her instincts. He had to. They had no other choice. He reached into his pouch, his fingers fumbling with the familiar petals of the glowing ink flower. It felt warm, alive, a small piece of home in this alien world. He handed it to Zyra, his hand trembling slightly. Zyra took it, her eye never leaving the box. She placed the flower on the pedestal, and the box slid open with a soft, almost inaudible click. A faint, sweet scent, like ozone and burnt sugar, wafted from within. Lucky leaned closer, his heart hammering against his ribs. He was about to discover the secrets of this alien world. And he had a feeling that his life would never be the same.
