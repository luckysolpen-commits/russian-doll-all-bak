The physical transformation was complete, but the mental one was just beginning. A torrent of new sensations, new thoughts, flooded Lucky’s mind, threatening to overwhelm him. He felt a primal urge, a hunger for dominance, a cold, calculating logic that was never his own. It was the instinct of a predator, a desire to assert his will, to control, to consume. He fought it, desperately clinging to the last vestiges of his humanity, to the memories of his old self, the scientist, the man of peace. But the alien instincts were powerful, insidious, whispering promises of power, of control, of a world where he was no longer prey, but predator. He could hear Lyra’s thoughts, clear as a bell, echoing his own newfound instincts. *Power. Control. This is… exhilarating,* Lyra’s telepathic voice purred in his mind. He felt her excitement, her exhilaration, her embrace of this new, terrifying power. And then, the whispers. Not the faint hum of the village walls, but a cacophony of voices, a chorus of thoughts, all around him. He heard the Extrmophile Kings, their minds like vast, cold oceans, discussing their plans, their strategies, their contempt for the weaker races. He heard their insatiable desire for resources, for power, for dominance. He heard the thoughts of the guards, their simple, brutal loyalty, their hunger for battle. He heard the thoughts of the enslaved creatures, their dull, vacant despair, their broken wills. It was overwhelming, a constant barrage of alien thought that threatened to shatter his mind. He was a man who had always valued his privacy, his solitude, his inner world. And now, his inner world was gone, replaced by a thousand alien voices, a thousand alien thoughts. He was exposed, vulnerable, his every thought, his every emotion, laid bare for the world to see. He was no longer alone. He was a part of something vast, something ancient, something terrifying. And he was terrified.
