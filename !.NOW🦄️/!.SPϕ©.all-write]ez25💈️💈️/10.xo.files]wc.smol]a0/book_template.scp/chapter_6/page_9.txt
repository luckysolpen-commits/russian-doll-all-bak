And then, a specific thought, cold and clear, cut through the mental din, a thought that sent a shiver of ice through Lucky’s newly scaly body. It was a thought from the Extrmophile Kings, their minds like vast, cold oceans, their voices resonating with an ancient, malevolent authority. *…the Fuzzballs. Frozen. Beneath the jungle. A valuable labor force. Their minds, so easily bent to our will.* Lucky’s new, alien mind processed this information with chilling efficiency. The ancient Fuzzballs, the revered relics of the Afinkers’ lore, were not revered relics at all. They were slaves. Mind labor slaves. Their unique telepathic abilities, their buzzing energy, their very essence, were being exploited, harnessed, and bent to the will of the Extrmophile Kings. And the Extrmophiles knew exactly where they were. They had known all along. The vision from the Heart of the Ink, the one that had filled him with such awe and wonder, was not just a prophecy of a city. It was a warning. A warning that he had failed to heed. The Extrmophiles were not guardians. They were enslavers. And the ink, the very essence of the Afinkers’ culture, the source of their power and wisdom, was their tool of subjugation. He felt a profound sense of betrayal, not just by the Extrmophiles, but by the very world itself. He had been so eager to believe in the magic, in the ancient wisdom, in the destiny that awaited him. But it was all a lie. A beautiful, terrifying lie. He looked at Lyra, her single golden eye fixed on him, her thoughts echoing his own horror. *They are monsters,* her telepathic voice resonated in his mind. *And we… we are becoming them.* The thought was a cold, hard truth. They had gained power, but at what cost? Their humanity felt distant, a fading memory, a whisper from a life that was no longer theirs. They were trapped. Trapped in a hostile, advanced civilization, surrounded by beings who viewed life as a resource to be exploited. And now, they were one of them.
