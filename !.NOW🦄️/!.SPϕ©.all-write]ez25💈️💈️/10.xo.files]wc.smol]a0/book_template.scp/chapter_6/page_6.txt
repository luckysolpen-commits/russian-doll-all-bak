Inside the box, nestled on a bed of shimmering, black dust, were two small, iridescent globules. They pulsed with a faint, internal light, like captured stars, their surfaces swirling with a thousand tiny galaxies. They were beautiful, and terrifying, and utterly alien. “Touch them,” Zyra instructed, her voice a low purr. “And embrace the secrets.” Lucky hesitated, his scientific mind screaming in protest. This was madness. He had no idea what these things were, what they would do. But Lyra, her eyes wide with a desperate, almost childlike curiosity, reached in without a second thought. As her fingers brushed one of the globules, it dissolved into a shimmering mist that instantly adhered to her skin, sinking into her pores, vanishing beneath her flesh. A gasp escaped her lips, a sound of surprise, of wonder, of… something else. Lucky, seeing her reaction, and driven by a desperate, almost irrational curiosity, reached for the other. As the globule touched his skin, a strange, tingling numbness spread throughout his body. It wasn’t painful, but profoundly unsettling, like his nerves were being slowly, systematically shut down. His skin began to prickle, to itch, to burn. He felt a sensation of stretching, of pulling, of his very flesh being reshaped, remolded, reformed. He watched in horror as his hand, once so familiar, began to change. The skin thickened, darkened, becoming scaly, like ancient, hardened leather. The color deepened to a dark, almost obsidian hue, absorbing the faint light of the chamber. He tried to pull his hand away, but it was too late. The transformation had begun. And there was nothing he could do to stop it.
