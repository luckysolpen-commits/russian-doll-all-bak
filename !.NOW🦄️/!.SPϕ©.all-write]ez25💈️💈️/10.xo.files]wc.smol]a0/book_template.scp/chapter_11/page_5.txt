They reached a wide, circular chamber, its ceiling lost in shadow, its walls carved from a single, massive crystal that pulsed with a soft, internal light. In its center stood a massive, crumbling pillar, its surface etched with the ravages of time, its base a pile of ancient debris. Lyra, her single golden eye gleaming with fierce determination, gestured to it. *This will slow them. The support structure is weak,* she thought, her telepathic voice clear and resonant in Lucky’s mind. Lucky’s new, tactical mind, honed by years of scientific analysis and now enhanced by the cold, calculating logic of his Extrmophile instincts, processed the information with chilling clarity. He saw the subtle hairline fractures, the minute shifts in the energy signatures that permeated the stone. She was right. It was a trap. A perfect trap. He felt a surge of his Extrmophile strength, a raw, untamed energy that coursed through his veins. He slammed his clawed hand into a specific point on the pillar, a precise, calculated blow that targeted the weakest point in its ancient structure. CRACKS spiderwebbed across its surface, spreading like a spider’s web, a silent scream of protest from the ancient stone. The air crackled with an unseen energy, a palpable force that made the hairs on their scaly bodies stand on end. The hum of the temple intensified, a low, resonant thrum that vibrated through their very bones. They were ready. They were prepared. They were undercover. And they were coming for the Fuzzballs.
