They activated hidden mechanisms that released torrents of corrosive liquid from the walls, dissolving their pursuers with a sickening hiss and a cloud of acrid smoke. The Fuzz Balls, under Lucky’s telepathic guidance, became active participants in the trap-setting. Their buzzing energy, which had moments before been a song of liberation, now formed intricate patterns as they darted and weaved through the chaos, their tiny, telepathic voices a chorus of encouragement and tactical information. They used their energy bursts to destabilize crumbling archways, sending massive chunks of stone crashing down, blocking the pursuing enforcers. They jammed ancient doors, their buzzing energy creating an impenetrable barrier that even the powerful Extrmophile Kings could not breach. They created diversions, their iridescent forms flashing through the darkness, drawing the enforcers into dead ends, into traps, into oblivion. Lucky felt a strange sense of exhilaration, a thrill that transcended the fear and the disgust. This was what he was trained for. This was what he was good at. Problem-solving. Infiltration. Analysis. He was a scientist, and this was the ultimate experiment. He was learning to be an Extrmophile. And the more he learned, the more he realized how little he truly knew about this strange, terrifying, and utterly fascinating civilization. He was a weapon, a tool, a means to an end. And he was ready to be wielded. He looked at Lyra, her single golden eye meeting his. He felt her resolve, her determination, her fierce loyalty. They were in this together. And together, they would find a way. They had to. The temple hummed around them, a silent, predatory beast, waiting for them to make a mistake. But they were ready. They were prepared. They were undercover. And they were coming for the Fuzzballs.
