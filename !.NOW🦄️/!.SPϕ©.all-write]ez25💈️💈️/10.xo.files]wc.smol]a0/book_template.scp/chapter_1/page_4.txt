He found a small alcove in the side of a massive, moss-covered rock formation. It wasn’t much, but it was defensible. He could watch the entrance, and nothing could sneak up on him from behind. He settled in for the night, his back against the cold stone, his makeshift club held tight in his hands. He didn’t dare to sleep. He just listened. The jungle was alive with a symphony of strange and terrifying sounds. He heard the screech of a creature that sounded like a rusty hinge, the mournful howl of something that sounded impossibly large, the rhythmic clicking of a thousand tiny insects. But it was the silence that scared him the most. The moments when all the other sounds seemed to stop, holding their breath in anticipation of something truly monstrous. It was in one of these moments of silence that he heard it. A low growl, so deep it was more of a vibration than a sound. It was close. Too close. He peered out of his alcove, his heart pounding in his chest. And then he saw them. Two of them. They were hounds, but like no hounds he had ever seen. Their bodies were sleek and muscular, covered in a coat of short, black fur that seemed to absorb the light. Their eyes were a piercing, otherworldly gold, glowing with a malevolent intelligence. They moved with a predatory grace, their powerful limbs carrying them silently over the phosphorescent moss. They were hunters. And he was their prey. They circled his alcove, their golden eyes fixed on him, their low growls a constant, menacing rumble. He could smell their breath, a hot, musky odor that smelled of blood and decay. He tightened his grip on his club, his knuckles white. He was not going to die here. Not like this. He would fight. He would show them that this human was not easy prey. He would make them bleed. He would make them pay for daring to hunt him. The thought filled him with a cold, savage rage. It was an emotion he hadn’t felt in a long time. It was the emotion of a cornered animal, a creature with nothing left to lose. And it was an emotion that just might keep him alive.
