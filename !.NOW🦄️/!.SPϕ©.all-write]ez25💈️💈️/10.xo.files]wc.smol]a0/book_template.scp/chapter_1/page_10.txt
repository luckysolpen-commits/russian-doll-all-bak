He told them his story, the young girl translating his words for the others. He told them about his ship, his mission, his crash. He told them about the jungle, the strange plants, the alien hounds. He told them about his fear, his desperation, his will to survive. The villagers listened in silence, their dark eyes fixed on his face. They were a captive audience, their expressions a mixture of awe and sympathy. When he was finished, the old male said something to the girl, his voice soft and low. “He says… you have a strong spirit,” the girl translated. “You have faced the jungle and survived. You are welcome in our village. You are welcome to share our fire, our food, our homes. You are… Afink.” Lucky didn’t know what the word meant, but he understood the sentiment. He was one of them now. He was a part of their tribe. He felt a wave of gratitude so powerful it almost brought him to tears. He had been lost, and now he was found. He had been alone, and now he had a family. A strange, alien family, but a family nonetheless. He looked at the young girl, her pale skin glowing in the firelight. “Thank you,” he said, his voice thick with emotion. “Thank you for… everything.” The girl smiled, a small, shy smile that transformed her face, making her look almost human. “You are welcome, Lucky,” she said. “My name is Lyra.” And in that moment, looking at the strange, beautiful faces around the fire, Lucky knew that his journey was far from over. In fact, it was just beginning. He had survived the jungle. He had found a new home. But he had a feeling that the greatest challenges, the greatest dangers, were still to come. And he had a feeling that he would not have to face them alone.
