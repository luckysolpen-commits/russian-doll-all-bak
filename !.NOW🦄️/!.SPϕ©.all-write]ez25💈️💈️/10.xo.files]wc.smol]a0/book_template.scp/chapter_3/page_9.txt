And then, through the cacophony of alien thought, a single, clear image emerged in his mind. The floating city. It was no longer a distant, passive vision. It was here. It was now. He could see the intricate details of its impossible architecture, the soft, pulsating glow of its internal lights, the silent, graceful movement of its strange, beautiful vessels. But this time, there was something different. This time, he felt… seen. He felt a gaze upon him, a focused, intelligent, and utterly malevolent curiosity. It was as if a single eye had opened in the heart of the city, a golden, reptilian eye that was staring directly at him. The feeling of being hunted, which had subsided during his time in the village, returned with a vengeance. But this was not the simple, primal fear of a predator in the jungle. This was a deeper, more existential dread. This was the fear of an ant under a magnifying glass, of a microbe under a microscope. He was being observed. He was being studied. He was being… targeted. The city, the Extrmophiles, they knew he was here. They knew he was different. And they were curious. It was the curiosity of a scientist dissecting a new specimen, of a child pulling the wings off a fly. It was a curiosity that was devoid of empathy, of compassion, of… mercy. He felt a surge of pure, unadulterated terror. He wanted to run, to hide, to scream. But he was paralyzed, trapped in the grip of this cosmic gaze. He was a fly in a spider’s web, a mouse in a snake’s coils. And the spider was waking up. The snake was about to strike.
