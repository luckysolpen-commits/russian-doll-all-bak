Chapter 3: The Whispers Begin

Days turned into nights in the Afink Village. Lucky, still recovering from his ordeal, observed the daily life of the Afinkers. He learned their customs, their songs, their quiet reverence for the land. But a subtle shift began to occur. A low, almost imperceptible HUM started to permeate the air. It wasn't the natural hum of the jungle, but something else, something artificial.

Lucky noticed villagers whispering to each other, their eyes darting nervously towards the dense jungle that surrounded them. They cast furtive glances at the walls of their huts, as if listening to something only they could hear. He approached Lyra, who was meticulously weaving a colorful tapestry outside her hut. Her brow was furrowed in concentration, but her movements were slightly agitated.

“Lyra, have you noticed anything... strange? A sound?” Lucky asked.

Lyra paused her weaving, her fingers still on the threads. She looked around cautiously, then leaned in closer. “The walls are whispering, Lucky. They have been for days. Ancient knowledge, hidden beneath the surface.”

Lucky frowned. He listened intently, straining his ears. He could hear the jungle, the distant calls of creatures, the rustle of leaves. But no whispers. “I don't hear anything.”

Lyra touched the mud-brick wall of her hut, her fingers tracing the intricate patterns etched into its surface. A faint, almost imperceptible VIBRATION emanated from it. “It is not with your ears you hear it, Lucky. It is with your spirit. The Extrmophiles' city is calling us.”

Lucky's eyes widened. The vision from the Heart of the Ink flashed in his mind – the impossible city, floating above the sea. He remembered the feeling of being drawn to it. “Calling you? What do you mean?”

Lyra looked at him, her eyes holding a deep, ancient wisdom that belied her youth. “They are stirring from their slumber. Their city is awakening. And we... we must be prepared for its return.”

Lucky felt a shiver run down his spine. The casualness with which Lyra spoke of this awakening was unsettling. He realized that this was not just a rumor, but a deep-seated belief, a premonition. The low hum in the air seemed to intensify, a subtle thrumming that Lucky could now feel in his bones. The whispers, though still inaudible to his human ears, felt like a tangible presence, a silent chorus emanating from the very fabric of the village.
