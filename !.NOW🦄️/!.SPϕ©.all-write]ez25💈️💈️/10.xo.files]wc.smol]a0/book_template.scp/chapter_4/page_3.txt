The tendrils of ink, thick and glistening, continued their relentless growth. They snaked up the mud-brick wall, pushing through the thatched roof with a soft, tearing sound. They reached for the sky, coalescing into a massive, undulating column of dark energy, pulsing with that unsettling violet light. The air around them grew heavy, charged with an unseen force. Lucky felt an irresistible pull, a powerful, almost violent suction that lifted him off his feet. He instinctively grabbed Lyra, pulling her close, as they were both drawn upwards, enveloped by the ink tendrils. It wasn’t a gentle ascent. It was a forceful, almost brutal, propulsion, like being shot from a cannon. He heard the screams of the villagers below, their voices swallowed by the roar of the ascending column. He saw their faces, contorted in fear and awe, as they watched him and Lyra disappear into the sky. He wondered why they weren’t being pulled, why only he and Lyra were chosen, or targeted. Was this the “purpose” Gorn had spoken of? Was this their destiny? Or was it a trap? A terrifying, beautiful trap. He had no answers. Only questions. And a growing sense of dread. The column of ink rose higher and higher, piercing the crack in the sky, a dark, pulsating artery connecting their world to another. He felt a strange sensation, a feeling of being stretched, of being pulled apart at a molecular level. It was not painful, but profoundly unsettling. He was no longer in control. He was a passenger, a piece of flotsam caught in a cosmic current, being carried to an unknown destination. He closed his eyes, bracing himself for whatever came next. He had faced alien hounds, survived a crash, and witnessed impossible magic. But this… this was something else entirely. This was the unknown. And it was terrifying.
