Finally, after what felt like an eternity of silent, desperate flight, he saw it. A shimmering, pulsating vortex at the end of a long, crystalline corridor. It was a swirling maelstrom of pure, incandescent light, a kaleidoscope of colors that shifted and danced with an otherworldly grace. It hummed with an otherworldly energy, a low, resonant thrum that vibrated through the stone, through the soles of his feet, through his very bones. It was the PORTAL TO SOUL’S DESTINY. A beacon of escape. A promise of freedom. A gateway to the unknown. He felt a surge of hope so powerful it almost brought him to his knees. He was a scientist, a man of logic and reason. But here, his logic was useless. His reason was meaningless. He was a man, and he was utterly, hopelessly lost. But he was not defeated. Not yet. He had survived worse. He had to believe that. He had to believe that they would find a way. They had to. The castle hummed around them, a silent, predatory beast, waiting for them to make a mistake. But he was ready. He was prepared. He was undercover. And he was coming for the Fuzzballs.
