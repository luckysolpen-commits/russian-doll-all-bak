He plunged into the shimmering vortex, and the world dissolved into a kaleidoscope of colors and sensations, a dizzying rush of pure energy that threatened to tear him apart. He felt a sensation of falling, of being pulled apart and reassembled at a molecular level. All his senses were on fire. He saw colors that had no names, heard sounds that were beyond the range of human hearing, felt emotions that were alien and overwhelming. It was terrifying. It was exhilarating. He was no longer Lucky, the human scientist. He was a disembodied consciousness, a mote of dust in a cosmic storm. He was everything and nothing. The chaos lasted for an eternity and a microsecond. And then, as suddenly as it had begun, it stopped. Lucky, Zig, and Zack emerged, gasping, onto a strange, new landscape. The air was different, thin and crisp, carrying the scent of pine and something else, something metallic and clean. The light was unfamiliar, a soft, golden glow that seemed to emanate from everywhere at once. The portal shimmered behind them, a swirling vortex of light and color, then winked out of existence, leaving them alone in this new, alien world. Lucky looked around, his new Extrmophile eyes taking in the bizarre surroundings. Towering trees, their bark a shimmering, bioluminescent blue, reached for the sky like skeletal fingers. The ground was a carpet of phosphorescent moss, glowing with an eerie, internal light. Strange, bird-like creatures with leathery wings and razor-sharp beaks circled overhead, their cries a discordant symphony. He was no longer on Planet X. He was somewhere else entirely. *Where are we?* Zig’s thoughts questioned, a wave of confusion. *This is… new,* Zack’s thoughts added, a chorus of bewildered curiosity. Lucky, despite the lingering horror of Lyra’s betrayal, felt a surge of grim satisfaction. They had escaped. They had survived. And now, a new adventure began. “Welcome, brothers. Welcome to… Footrace Fu,” Lucky said, first to himself, then to Zig and Zack, his voice a low, guttural rumble. The journey was far from over. It had just begun. And he was ready.
