Lucky moved through the Grand Hall, a silent, scaly shadow amidst the grotesque spectacle of creation and betrayal. He passed the writhing, merging forms of Lyra and the Kings, their bodies a horrifying, organic mass that pulsed with a sickening, sweet odor. He saw the newly born Extrmophiles, their tiny, scaly bodies twitching with an eager anticipation, their single golden eyes already scanning for prey. He kept his thoughts a chaotic jumble, a mental static that made him almost invisible to Lyra’s probing mind. He was a broken radio, emitting only white noise, a desperate attempt to hide his true intentions. He was a ghost in the machine, a shadow in the light, a silent, unseen force working from within. He could hear the new Extrmophiles’ thoughts, their minds already filled with the same cold, calculating hunger as their parents. *They move! The Fuzzballs! The human!* a new Extrmophile’s thoughts cried, a wave of pure, unadulterated greed. *Their energy… a feast!* another thought, a chilling premonition of his own impending doom. The hunger in their thoughts spurred him on, a cold dread that sharpened his focus, that fueled his desperate, primal urge for freedom. He was a scientist, a man of logic and reason. But here, his logic was useless. His reason was meaningless. He was a man, and he was utterly, hopelessly lost. But he was not defeated. Not yet. He had survived worse. He had to believe that. He had to believe that they would find a way. They had to. The castle hummed around them, a silent, predatory beast, waiting for them to make a mistake. But he was ready. He was prepared. He was undercover. And he was coming for the Fuzzballs.
