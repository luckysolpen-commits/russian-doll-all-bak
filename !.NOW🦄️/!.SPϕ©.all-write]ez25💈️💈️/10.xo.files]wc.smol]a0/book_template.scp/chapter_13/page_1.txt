Lucky, his mind still reeling from Lyra’s betrayal and the horrifying spectacle of the Extrmophile Kings’ grotesque breeding, struggled against the mental paralysis that held him captive. He was a prisoner in his own mind, a silent observer to his own impending doom. He felt the insidious tendrils of Lyra’s telepathic control, a cold, suffocating grip that threatened to crush his consciousness. But even as despair threatened to consume him, a spark of his human will, fueled by desperation, flickered. It was a tiny flame in a vast, overwhelming darkness, but it was there. He focused on Zig and Zack, the two Fuzz Ball brothers, who were also subdued, their vibrant energy dimmed, their tiny, furry bodies still and silent. He pushed a desperate, silent command to them, a raw surge of his will, a desperate plea for defiance. *Fight it!* his telepathic voice resonated, a faint whisper in the chaotic symphony of alien thought. *Disguise your thoughts! We escape! Now!* He was Lucky. He was a human scientist. And he would not be a pawn. Not anymore. He would fight. He would make them pay. He would find a way. He had to. The castle hummed around them, a silent, predatory beast, waiting for them to make a mistake. But he was ready. He was prepared. He was undercover. And he was coming for the Fuzzballs.
