With a desperate lunge, Lucky grabbed Zig and Zack, tucking their small, furry forms into a pouch he had fashioned from a loose piece of his new, scaly skin. Their faint telepathic chirps of encouragement, though weak, were a lifeline, a tiny spark of hope in the overwhelming darkness. He could feel their fear, their confusion, but also their unwavering trust. He had to save them. He had to save himself. He focused on keeping his thoughts a chaotic jumble, a mental static that made him almost invisible to Lyra’s probing mind. He was a broken radio, emitting only white noise, a desperate attempt to hide his true intentions. He was a ghost in the machine, a shadow in the light, a silent, unseen force working from within. He felt a surge of grim determination. He was a scientist, a man of logic and reason. But here, his logic was useless. His reason was meaningless. He was a man, and he was utterly, hopelessly lost. But he was not defeated. Not yet. He had survived worse. He had to believe that. He had to believe that they would find a way. They had to. The castle hummed around them, a silent, predatory beast, waiting for them to make a mistake. But they were ready. They were prepared. They were undercover. And they were coming for the Fuzzballs.
