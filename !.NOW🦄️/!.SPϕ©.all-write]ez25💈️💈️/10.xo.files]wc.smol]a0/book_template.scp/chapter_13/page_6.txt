But Lyra’s response was delayed, her attention still fragmented by the grotesque breeding process. It was enough. A crucial window of opportunity, a fragile moment of vulnerability in the heart of the enemy’s stronghold. Lucky seized it, his new, scaly limbs moving with a desperate urgency. He navigated the twisting corridors of the Fuzz Castle, relying on his memory of the temple’s labyrinthine layout and the faint, almost subconscious guidance from Zig and Zack, their tiny, telepathic chirps a constant stream of directional information. He was a ghost in the machine, a shadow in the light, a silent, unseen force working from within. He felt a surge of grim determination. He was a scientist, a man of logic and reason. But here, his logic was useless. His reason was meaningless. He was a man, and he was utterly, hopelessly lost. But he was not defeated. Not yet. He had survived worse. He had to believe that. He had to believe that they would find a way. They had to. The castle hummed around them, a silent, predatory beast, waiting for them to make a mistake. But he was ready. He was prepared. He was undercover. And he was coming for the Fuzzballs.
