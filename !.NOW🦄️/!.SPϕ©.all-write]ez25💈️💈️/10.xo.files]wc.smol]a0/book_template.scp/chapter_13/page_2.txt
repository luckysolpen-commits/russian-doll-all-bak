Zig and Zack, though weakened by Lyra’s telepathic assault, responded to Lucky’s desperate plea. Their tiny forms shimmered, their vibrant energy, which had been dimmed, now flickered with a renewed intensity. Lucky felt a subtle shift in their mental presence, a blurring, a distortion that made their thoughts harder to pinpoint. They were cloaking themselves, just as he was trying to cloak his own, a desperate act of defiance against Lyra’s overwhelming power. Lyra, consumed by her grotesque breeding with the Kings, was distracted. Her attention was divided, her telepathic net stretched thin by the sheer number of new Extrmophiles being born, their minds a chaotic jumble of primal hunger and nascent intelligence. This was their chance. A small window of opportunity, a fragile moment of vulnerability in the heart of the enemy’s stronghold. Lucky felt a surge of grim determination. He was a scientist, a man of logic and reason. But here, his logic was useless. His reason was meaningless. He was a man, and he was utterly, hopelessly lost. But he was not defeated. Not yet. He had survived worse. He had to believe that. He had to believe that they would find a way. They had to. The castle hummed around them, a silent, predatory beast, waiting for them to make a mistake. But they were ready. They were prepared. They were undercover. And they were coming for the Fuzzballs.
