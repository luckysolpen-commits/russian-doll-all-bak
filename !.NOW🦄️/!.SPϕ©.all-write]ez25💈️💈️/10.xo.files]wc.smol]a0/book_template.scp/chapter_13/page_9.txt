Behind him, he heard Lyra’s telepathic roar, a wave of pure fury that crashed over him, threatening to shatter his consciousness. *NO! They will not escape! They are mine!* Her voice, usually so calm and controlled, was now a primal scream of rage and frustration. He felt her presence, a cold, suffocating grip that threatened to pull him back, to drag him down into the abyss of her betrayal. But it was too late. He was too close. With a final burst of adrenaline, a desperate, primal urge for freedom, Lucky threw himself towards the portal. His new, scaly limbs propelled him forward, a blur of dark scales and flashing claws. Zig and Zack, nestled safely in his pouch, emitted a triumphant, telepathic cheer, their tiny voices a chorus of pure joy and exhilaration. He felt a strange sense of exhilaration, a thrill that transcended the fear and the disgust. This was what he was trained for. This was what he was good at. Problem-solving. Infiltration. Analysis. He was a scientist, and this was the ultimate experiment. He was learning to be an Extrmophile. And the more he learned, the more he realized how little he truly knew about this strange, terrifying, and utterly fascinating civilization. He was a weapon, a tool, a means to an end. And he was ready to be wielded. He looked at Lyra, her single golden eye meeting his. He felt her resolve, her determination, her fierce loyalty. They were in this together. And together, they would find a way. They had to. The castle hummed around them, a silent, predatory beast, waiting for them to make a mistake. But they were ready. They were prepared. They were undercover. And they were coming for the Fuzzballs.
