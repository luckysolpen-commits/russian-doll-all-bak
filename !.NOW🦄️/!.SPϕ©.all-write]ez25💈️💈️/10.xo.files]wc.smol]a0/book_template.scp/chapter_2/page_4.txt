One evening, as he sat by the fire with Gorn and Lyra, he decided to ask the question that had been burning in his mind for weeks. “The inks,” he said, his voice low and hesitant. “Where do they come from?” Gorn looked at him, his ancient eyes filled with a wisdom that seemed as old as the jungle itself. He said something in the Afink language, his voice a low, rumbling sound. “He says… the inks are a gift,” Lyra translated. “A gift from the earth mother. A gift from the spirits of the trees.” Lucky frowned. He was a scientist. He needed a more concrete answer than that. “But… how are they made?” he pressed. “What are they made of?” Gorn chuckled, a dry, rustling sound. He said something to Lyra, his eyes twinkling with amusement. “He says… you ask too many questions, Lucky,” Lyra said, a small smile playing on her lips. “You are like a child, always wanting to know the ‘why’ of things.” Lucky felt a flush of embarrassment. He was being rude, intrusive. He was trying to apply his own worldview to a culture that was so different from his own. “I’m sorry,” he said, his voice barely a whisper. “I didn’t mean to be disrespectful.” Gorn’s expression softened. He reached out and placed a hand on Lucky’s shoulder. His touch was surprisingly warm, comforting. He said something to Lyra, his voice gentle. “He says… it is not disrespect, Lucky,” she translated. “It is… curiosity. And curiosity is a good thing. It is the first step towards wisdom.” Gorn then stood up, his tall, slender frame silhouetted against the flickering flames. He beckoned for Lucky and Lyra to follow him. “He says… he will show you,” Lyra said, her voice filled with a sense of awe and reverence. “He will show you the source of the inks. He will show you… the Heart of the Ink.”
