Chapter 2: A Village of Ink

Lucky emerged from the dense jungle, his makeshift furs tattered, his body weary. The AFINK VILLAGE, a cluster of thatched huts, was illuminated by the warm glow of a central fire pit. Villagers, their faces lit by the flickering flames, watched him with curious, unreadable expressions.

Lucky approached cautiously, his senses on high alert. He clutched his crude club, ready for anything. An ELDERLY AFINK, his skin a mosaic of intricate, colorful patterns, stepped forward. He spoke in a language Lucky didn't understand, a series of clicks and melodic hums. A YOUNG AFINK GIRL, perhaps twelve years old, with bright, intelligent eyes, moved to the Elder's side. She looked at Lucky, then back at the Elder, and then spoke to Lucky in clear, if slightly accented, English.

“You're not from around here, are you?” she asked softly.

Lucky, still processing the strangeness of this alien world, shook his head. “No. I'm... from elsewhere.”

The Elderly Afink nodded slowly, his eyes clouding with a hint of concern. “You are lucky to have made it out of the jungle alive. What brings you to our village?” Lyra translated.

Lucky hesitated. He'd been through a lot. But something in the gentle demeanor of the villagers, particularly the Young Afink Girl's earnest gaze, put him at ease. He decided to take a chance. “My ship crashed. I'm... lost. I need help.”

The Elderly Afink studied him for a long moment, then gestured towards the fire pit. “Come. Share our fire. Share our food. We will hear your story.” Lyra translated. Lucky, exhausted but relieved, nodded. He lowered his makeshift club, a silent acceptance of their hospitality. The Young Afink Girl offered a small, reassuring smile.

***

Lucky sat cross-legged on a woven mat inside Gorn's (the ELDERLY AFINK) hut. The air was warm, filled with the earthy scent of dried herbs and a faint, sweet aroma. Lyra, the YOUNG AFINK GIRL, sat beside Gorn, translating. Lucky had shared his story – his crash, his origins from a distant world, his mission to find a way home. Gorn listened intently, his ancient eyes never leaving Lucky's face.

“Your world sounds... different. Here, our lives are woven with color,” Gorn said, translated by Lyra.

Lucky looked around the hut. The walls were adorned with intricate murals, swirling patterns of vibrant hues that seemed to shift and breathe in the dim light. Even the simple utensils were etched with colorful designs. “I've noticed. The art here is... unlike anything I've ever seen.”

“It is more than art. It is life. Our inks are not merely pigments. They are... essence,” Lyra explained. She picked up a small, smooth stone, painted with a deep, shimmering blue. She held it out to Lucky. “This is Zhilak. The ink of strength. When applied, it enhances our muscles, our speed. It allows us to build, to hunt, to endure.”

Lucky took the stone, feeling a faint warmth emanating from it. He was a scientist, but this defied his understanding of biology. “Biologically enhancing? How?”

Gorn chuckled, a low, rumbling sound. “It is the wisdom of our ancestors, passed through generations. Each color holds a specific power. Korvath, the green, grants wisdom. Ember, the fiery orange, ignites courage. They are the threads of our existence,” Lyra translated. Lucky's curiosity was piqued. He asked more questions, delving into the intricacies of their ink lore. He learned how they mixed the inks with various organic compounds, creating dyes for textiles, ceramics, and even for their own skin, forming the intricate patterns he'd observed.

Later, Gorn led Lucky and Lyra to a secluded, sacred area within the village. In the center stood a large, pulsating ORB, radiating a soft, ethereal glow. It hummed with a low, resonant frequency that vibrated through the ground. “This is the Heart of the Ink. The source of our people's power and wisdom. It holds the secrets of our ancestors,” Lyra translated. Lucky approached the orb cautiously, sensing its immense energy. The air around it felt charged, almost alive. Gorn, with reverence, carefully lifted the orb. It hovered above his palm, shimmering.

“A vision will soon unfold before you,” Lyra translated. The humming intensified. The orb began to glow brighter, a blinding flash of light erupting from its core. Lucky instinctively closed his eyes. When he opened them, he was no longer in the hut. He was standing on an ethereal landscape, suspended in a shimmering void. Below him, far below, was the vast, swirling sea of Planet X. And floating above that sea, like a mirage, was an INVISIBLE NATION. A city of impossible architecture, towers of gleaming, dark material reaching into the sky, reflecting the colors of the Heart of the Ink. This was the realm of the EXTRMOPHILES.

“These are the guardians of the deep. They possess secrets and knowledge passed down through eons,” Gorn’s voice echoed in his mind, translated by Lyra. Lucky's mind reeled. The city was immense, alien, and strangely alluring. He felt an inexplicable pull towards it, a sense of destiny. As quickly as it began, the vision faded. Lucky found himself back in Gorn's hut, the Heart of the Ink resting gently in the Elder's hands. The air was still, but the hum of the orb seemed to echo in Lucky's mind.

“This is a sign. The Heart of the Ink has chosen you for a great purpose,” Lyra translated. Lucky looked at Lyra, then back at the orb. The Extrmophiles' city, a shimmering enigma, beckoned.
