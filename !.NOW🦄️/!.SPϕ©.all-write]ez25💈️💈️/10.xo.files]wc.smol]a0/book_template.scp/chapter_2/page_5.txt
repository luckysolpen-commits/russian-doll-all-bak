Gorn led them to a secluded, sacred area at the edge of the village. It was a small clearing, surrounded by a circle of ancient, gnarled trees. The air here was different, charged with a strange, palpable energy. In the center of the clearing, on a pedestal of black, volcanic rock, was a large, pulsating orb. It was about the size of a human head, and it glowed with a soft, ethereal light, a kaleidoscope of shifting colors. It hummed with a low, resonant frequency that vibrated through the ground, through the soles of Lucky’s feet, through his very bones. “The Heart of the Ink,” Lyra whispered, her voice filled with a mixture of awe and fear. Lucky stared at the orb, mesmerized. He had never seen anything like it. It was beautiful, and terrifying, and utterly alien. He felt a strange pull towards it, a sense of… recognition. As if he had seen it before, in a dream, in a forgotten memory. Gorn approached the orb with a reverence that was almost palpable. He chanted in the Afink language, his voice low and melodic. The orb seemed to respond to his voice, its light growing brighter, its humming growing louder. He reached out and placed his hands on the orb. The light intensified, and for a moment, Lucky was blinded. When he opened his eyes, Gorn was holding the orb in his hands. It was hovering above his palms, shimmering, pulsating, alive. “He says… the Heart of the Ink is the source of our power, our wisdom, our very being,” Lyra translated, her voice trembling. “It holds the memories of our ancestors, the secrets of our world, the destiny of our people.” Gorn then turned to Lucky, his ancient eyes burning with an intense, almost feverish light. He said something to Lyra, his voice a low, urgent whisper. “He says… the Heart of the Ink has a message for you, Lucky,” Lyra said, her voice barely a whisper. “It has a vision to show you. A vision of… your destiny.”
