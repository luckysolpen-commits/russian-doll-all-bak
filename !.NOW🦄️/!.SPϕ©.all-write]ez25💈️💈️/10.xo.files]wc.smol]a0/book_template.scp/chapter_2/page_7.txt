He was standing on an ethereal platform of shimmering, translucent energy, suspended in a void of absolute blackness. Below him, stretching out to an infinite horizon, was the vast, swirling ocean of Planet X. It was a maelstrom of color and motion, a living, breathing entity of immense power. But it was what floated above the ocean that stole his breath away. A city. A nation. It was a metropolis of impossible scale and breathtaking, alien beauty. Towers of a gleaming, dark material that seemed to drink the light pierced the void, their peaks vanishing into the darkness above. Bridges of woven light connected the towers, and strange, silent vessels moved between them with a fluid, effortless grace. The architecture was unlike anything he had ever imagined, a fusion of organic curves and crystalline structures that pulsed with a soft, internal light. It was a city that seemed to be alive, a single, vast organism of unimaginable complexity. And it was invisible. He knew, with a certainty that defied logic, that if he were standing on the surface of Planet X, he would see nothing but the empty sky. The city was cloaked, hidden, existing in a dimension just slightly out of sync with his own. He could feel its power, a low, resonant hum that vibrated in his bones, a telepathic pressure against his mind. It was a city of immense power, of ancient wisdom, of… something else. Something cold. Something predatory. He felt a profound sense of awe, but it was mingled with an equally profound sense of dread. The city was beautiful, but it was a terrible beauty. It was the beauty of a shark, of a black widow spider, of a supernova. It was the beauty of something that could extinguish him without a thought, without a flicker of remorse. He felt an inexplicable pull towards it, a siren’s call that promised knowledge, power, and a thousand other things he couldn’t name. It was a temptation, a test. And he knew, with a chilling certainty, that to succumb to it would be to lose himself completely.
