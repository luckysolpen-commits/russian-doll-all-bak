Lucky woke to the sound of singing. It was a soft, melodic chant, a chorus of voices that seemed to rise and fall with the rhythm of the jungle. He opened his eyes, and for a moment, he didn’t know where he was. The ceiling was a tapestry of woven leaves, the walls were a mosaic of colorful, swirling patterns. The air was filled with the scent of woodsmoke and strange, exotic flowers. And then he remembered. The jungle. The hounds. The village. Lyra. He sat up, his body stiff and sore, but his mind clear. He was safe. He was alive. He felt a sense of peace that he hadn’t felt in a long time. He got up and walked to the entrance of the hut. The village was already bustling with activity. The men were preparing for the hunt, their bodies adorned with intricate patterns of red and black ink. The women were tending to the crops, their colorful garments a stark contrast to the green of the jungle. The children were playing, their laughter echoing through the clearing. It was a scene of domestic tranquility, a stark contrast to the savage wilderness that surrounded them. He saw Lyra, sitting by the fire, weaving a colorful tapestry. She looked up and smiled, a small, shy smile that made his heart ache. She was so young, so innocent. And yet, she possessed a wisdom that seemed to belay her years. She was a paradox, a mystery. And he found himself drawn to her, to her quiet strength, her gentle spirit. He walked over to her, his heart filled with a strange mixture of hope and trepidation. He was a stranger in a strange land. But he had a feeling that as long as he had Lyra by his side, he would be okay. He had a feeling that she was the key to his survival, the key to his escape. The key to everything.
