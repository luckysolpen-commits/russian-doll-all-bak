Lucky, with Zig and Zack as his personal, telepathic guides, focused on the Kings. He knew they were the key. Their defeat would shatter the morale of their enforcers, would break the spell of their dominance. He moved with a fluid, predatory grace, his new Extrmophile form a blur of dark scales and flashing claws. He dodged a sweeping blow from a hulking enforcer, its chitinous arm ending in a razor-sharp blade, and countered with a powerful kick that sent it sprawling. Lyra, a whirlwind of claws and cunning, protected their flanks. She moved with a terrifying efficiency, her sharp mouth snapping, her single golden eye gleaming with a cold, calculating fury. She was a force of nature, a dancer of death, and the enforcers fell before her like wheat before a scythe. The Fuzz Balls, under Lucky’s telepathic guidance, became active participants in the battle. They used their energy bursts to destabilize crumbling archways, to jam ancient doors, to create diversions that drew the enforcers into dead ends. They darted and weaved through the chaos, their buzzing energy a constant source of disruption, their tiny, telepathic voices a chorus of encouragement and tactical information. Lucky felt a strange sense of exhilaration, a thrill that transcended the fear and the disgust. This was what he was trained for. This was what he was good at. Problem-solving. Infiltration. Analysis. He was a scientist, and this was the ultimate experiment. He was learning to be an Extrmophile. And the more he learned, the more he realized how little he truly knew about this strange, terrifying, and utterly fascinating civilization. He was a weapon, a tool, a means to an end. And he was ready to be wielded. He looked at Lyra, her single golden eye meeting his. He felt her resolve, her determination, her fierce loyalty. They were in this together. And together, they would find a way. They had to. The temple hummed around them, a silent, predatory beast, waiting for them to make a mistake. But they were ready. They were prepared. They were undercover. And they were coming for the Fuzzballs.
