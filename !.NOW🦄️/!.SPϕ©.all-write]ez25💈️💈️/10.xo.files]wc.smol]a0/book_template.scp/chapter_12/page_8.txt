Lyra, her eyes burning with a chilling satisfaction, turned and walked towards the Extrmophile Kings. Her movements were fluid, predatory, a silent declaration of her newfound power. As she approached them, her form seemed to shimmer, to grow, to become even more imposing, her scales darkening, her single golden eye glowing with an intense, almost blinding light. The Kings, who had moments before been so arrogant, so triumphant, now bowed their heads in deference, their massive forms shrinking in comparison to her newfound majesty. It was a silent acknowledgment of her dominance, a chilling testament to the depth of her betrayal. Lucky felt a surge of revulsion, a profound disgust for these creatures, for Lyra, for the world that had created them. He had seen the enslaved creatures in the city, their vacant eyes, their broken wills. But this… this was different. This was not just enslavement. This was a perversion, a corruption of everything he had believed in. He had fought by her side, risked his life for her, for their mission. And now… now she was betraying him. Betraying the Fuzz Balls. Betraying everything they had fought for. He tried to probe her thoughts, to understand the sudden shift, the chilling transformation. But her mind was a fortress, impenetrable, unyielding. He was a prisoner in his own mind, a silent observer to his own impending doom. He was a scientist, a man of logic and reason. But here, his logic was useless. His reason was meaningless. He was a man, and he was utterly, hopelessly lost. But he was not defeated. Not yet. He had survived worse. He had to believe that. He had to believe that they would find a way. They had to. The castle hummed around them, a silent, predatory beast, waiting for them to make a mistake. But they were ready. They were prepared. They were undercover. And they were coming for the Fuzzballs.
