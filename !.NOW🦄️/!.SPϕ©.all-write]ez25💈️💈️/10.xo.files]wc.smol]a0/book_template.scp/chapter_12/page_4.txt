Before Lucky could react, before he could even formulate a coherent thought, Lyra reached out, her clawed hand moving with impossible speed. She touched his forehead, and a wave of overwhelming, paralyzing energy flooded his mind. It was not a physical blow, but a psychic assault, a torrent of raw, untamed power that threatened to shatter his consciousness. He tried to fight it, to push back with his own telepathic strength, to erect a mental shield against the onslaught. But it was too strong. His new Extrmophile form stiffened, his muscles locking, his body becoming a rigid, unyielding statue. His thoughts, which had been so sharp, so clear, so logical, became sluggish, trapped, like a fly caught in amber. He was aware, but he was powerless. He was a prisoner in his own mind, a silent observer to his own impending doom. He felt a profound sense of helplessness, a crushing weight that threatened to suffocate him. He was a scientist, a man of logic and reason. But here, his logic was useless. His reason was meaningless. He was a man, and he was utterly, hopelessly lost. But he was not defeated. Not yet. He had survived worse. He had to believe that. He had to believe that they would find a way. They had to. The castle hummed around them, a silent, predatory beast, waiting for them to make a mistake. But they were ready. They were prepared. They were undercover. And they were coming for the Fuzzballs.
