Zha’Zix’s mandibles clicked in approval, a sound that resonated deep within Lucky’s mind. Her thoughts, a vast, complex tapestry of ancient knowledge, rippled with intrigued fascination. *Your capacity for self-improvement is… impressive. We can learn much from each other. I sense a… mutually beneficial arrangement,* Zha’Zix responded telepathically. Lucky felt a surge of triumph. He had done it. He had gained access. He had found a way in. He had found an ally, however unlikely, in the heart of the enemy’s territory. He projected a thought of gratitude, of respect, of shared ambition. He was careful to keep his true intentions hidden, to project only what Zha’Zix expected to see: a brilliant mind, eager to learn, eager to contribute, eager to advance the cause of the Extrmophiles. He was playing a dangerous game, a constant tightrope walk between his human self and his alien disguise. He felt the insidious pull of the Extrmophile instincts, the hunger for dominance, the cold logic, the predatory urges. He fought it, desperately clinging to the last vestiges of his humanity, to the memories of his old self, the scientist, the man of peace. He was Lucky. He was a human scientist. He was undercover. And he would not succumb. He would use this power, this disguise, this alien form, to achieve his mission. He would use it to find the Fuzzballs. He would use it to expose the Extrmophile Kings. He would use it to find a way home. He was a weapon, a tool, a means to an end. And he was ready to be wielded. He looked at Lyra, her single golden eye meeting his. He felt her resolve, her determination, her fierce loyalty. They were in this together. And together, they would find a way. They had to. The city hummed around them, a silent, predatory beast, waiting for them to make a mistake. But they were ready. They were prepared. They were undercover. And they were coming for the Fuzzballs.
