SCENE 2: ARRIVAL AT AFINK VILLAGE

[EXT. AFINK VILLAGE - NIGHT]

LUCKY emerges from the dense jungle, his makeshift furs tattered, his body weary. The AFINK VILLAGE, a cluster of thatched huts, is illuminated by the warm glow of a central fire pit. Villagers, their faces lit by the flickering flames, watch him with curious, unreadable expressions.

Lucky approaches cautiously, his senses on high alert. He clutches his crude club, ready for anything.

An ELDERLY AFINK, his skin a mosaic of intricate, colorful patterns, steps forward. He speaks in a language Lucky doesn't understand, a series of clicks and melodic hums.

A YOUNG AFINK GIRL, perhaps twelve years old, with bright, intelligent eyes, moves to the Elder's side. She looks at Lucky, then back at the Elder, and then speaks to Lucky in clear, if slightly accented, English.

YOUNG AFINK GIRL
(Softly)
You're not from around here, are you?

Lucky, still processing the strangeness of this alien world, shakes his head.

LUCKY
No. I'm... from elsewhere.

The Elderly Afink nods slowly, his eyes clouding with a hint of concern.

ELDERLY AFINK (O.S.)
(Translated by Young Afink Girl)
You are lucky to have made it out of the jungle alive. What brings you to our village?

Lucky hesitates. He's been through a lot. But something in the gentle demeanor of the villagers, particularly the Young Afink Girl's earnest gaze, puts him at ease. He decides to take a chance.

LUCKY
My ship crashed. I'm... lost. I need help.

The Elderly Afink studies him for a long moment, then gestures towards the fire pit.

ELDERLY AFINK (O.S.)
(Translated by Young Afink Girl)
Come. Share our fire. Share our food. We will hear your story.

Lucky, exhausted but relieved, nods. He lowers his makeshift club, a silent acceptance of their hospitality. The Young Afink Girl offers a small, reassuring smile.

[FADE OUT.]
