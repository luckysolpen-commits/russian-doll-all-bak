INT. GLITCH WORLD - INDUSTRIAL ZONE - DAY

Lucky and Kael move through the industrial zone of the Glitch World, a stark contrast to the bustling city center. The buildings here are massive, utilitarian structures of rusted metal and crumbling concrete, their surfaces constantly flickering with visual distortions. The air is thick with the phantom smell of ozone and decaying machinery. The sky above is a perpetual, sickly grey, occasionally streaked with lines of corrupted code.

This area is less populated, but the few inhabitants they encounter are more overtly glitched. A worker on a scaffold, his body elongated and compressed like a faulty video stream, endlessly hammers at a non-existent nail. A delivery truck drives in a perfect, small circle, its engine revving loudly, its driver a blurred, indistinct figure.

KAEL
(Voice low, pointing to a flickering billboard that cycles through random numbers)
This sector is heavily influenced by the Metal Mafia's processing units. The raw data streams are more exposed here. It's why the glitches are more pronounced. And why the brainwashing is more aggressive.

They pass a group of figures huddled in an alleyway. They appear to be homeless, their clothes tattered, their faces vacant. But as Lucky gets closer, he sees the subtle, rhythmic flicker in their eyes, the faint, almost imperceptible hum emanating from their bodies. They are not just brainwashed; they are actively being *processed*.

LUCKY
(A chill running down his spine)
They're... they're like the man in the library. But worse.

KAEL
(Grimly)
These are the ones who resisted too much, too openly. The system couldn't overwrite them completely, so it's trying to break them down, piece by piece. Their minds are trapped in endless, repetitive loops of despair. A form of digital torture.

Suddenly, one of the figures looks up. Its eyes, though vacant, lock onto Lucky. A low, guttural MOAN escapes its lips, and its body begins to contort, its limbs stretching and twisting in unnatural ways. It's not attacking, but its very presence is disturbing, a raw manifestation of the Glitch World's horror.

KAEL
(Pulling Lucky away)
Don't engage. Their despair is contagious. It's designed to drain your own will.

They continue their journey, navigating through abandoned factories and warehouses. The coordinates lead them deeper into the industrial wasteland. The drones here are less subtle, larger, and more overtly aggressive, their red optical sensors sweeping the desolate landscape with chilling efficiency.

Lucky tries to use his own glitch power, focusing on a distant drone. He imagines it flickering, becoming transparent. For a moment, the drone shimmers, its outline blurring, but then it snaps back, its red eye focusing directly on them. A high-pitched ALERT SOUND blares from its chassis.

LUCKY
(Frustrated)
It didn't work! It saw me!

KAEL
(Pulling him behind a rusted shipping container)
This area has higher security protocols. Your basic visual distortion won't be enough. We need to be more precise. More subtle. Or more disruptive.

He pulls out a small, sleek device from his pocket, no bigger than his thumb. He taps it, and a faint, almost inaudible HUM emanates from it.

KAEL
(Cont.)
This is a frequency scrambler. It won't hide us, but it will make their tracking less accurate. Give us a few precious seconds if they decide to pursue.

They continue, moving from cover to cover, the distant wail of the drone fading as the scrambler takes effect. The landscape becomes even more desolate, the buildings more dilapidated. The air grows colder, and a faint, metallic taste lingers on Lucky's tongue.

Finally, they arrive at their destination. It's a massive, windowless building, its concrete facade cracked and stained. The coordinates point directly to it. There are no visible entrances, no signs of life. It looks like a tomb.

KAEL
(Looking at the building, a grim satisfaction on his face)
This is it. The old data center. Offline for decades, they say. But if the man from the library was right... the truth is buried deep within its core.

He approaches the wall, running his hand over the rough concrete. He closes his eyes, concentrating. A faint, almost invisible shimmer appears on the wall, a subtle distortion in the fabric of the simulation. It's a hidden access point, masked by layers of digital camouflage.

KAEL
(Cont.)
They didn't want anyone to find this. Which means whatever's inside... is worth finding.

He pushes against the shimmering section of the wall. It gives way with a soft, almost imperceptible CLICK, revealing a dark, narrow passage. The air inside is cold, stagnant, and smells of ozone and forgotten data.

LUCKY
(Looking into the darkness)
What do you think we'll find in there?

KAEL
(A grim smile)
Either the key to our escape, Lucky. Or the final piece of the Metal Mafia's puzzle. Either way, we're about to find out.

FADE OUT.
