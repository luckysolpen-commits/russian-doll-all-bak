INT. GLITCH WORLD - OLD DATA CENTER - INTERIOR - DAY

Lucky and Kael step into the narrow passage, the hidden entrance to the old data center. The air is heavy, cold, and smells strongly of ozone and the metallic tang of ancient, decaying electronics. The passage is short, leading into a vast, cavernous space, dimly lit by the faint, pulsating glow of dormant server racks.

The interior of the data center is a labyrinth of towering, skeletal server racks, stretching into the gloom. Cables, thick as pythons, snake across the floor and up the walls, some frayed and sparking with faint, corrupted energy. Dust motes dance in the few beams of light that penetrate from unseen vents above. The silence is profound, broken only by the soft hum of residual power and the occasional, unsettling CLICK or WHIR from the dormant machinery.

LUCKY
(Whispering)
It's... huge. And dead.

KAEL
(His voice echoing slightly in the vast space)
Not dead. Dormant. The Metal Mafia wouldn't just abandon a data center. They would repurpose it, or wipe it clean. The fact that it's still here, still humming... it means something was left behind. Something they couldn't, or didn't want to, erase.

Kael pulls out a small, handheld scanner. Its screen flickers with lines of code and faint energy readings. He sweeps it across the room.

KAEL
(Cont.)
Energy signatures. Faint, but present. And... data streams. Old ones. Heavily encrypted, but they're here. This place is a digital graveyard, but the ghosts are still active.

They begin to navigate the maze of server racks. The floor is uneven, littered with discarded components and shattered data chips. Lucky occasionally kicks a loose cable, sending a shower of sparks into the darkness.

As they move deeper, the air grows colder. The faint hum of the servers becomes more pronounced, a low, rhythmic thrum that seems to vibrate in Lucky's bones. He notices that some of the server racks, deeper within the complex, are glowing with a faint, sickly green light, different from the usual blue or red of active systems.

LUCKY
(Pointing)
What's that light?

Kael stops, his scanner beeping rapidly. His face, illuminated by the green glow, is grim.

KAEL
(Voice tight)
That's not a normal system light. That's... a processing signature. And it's active. This isn't just a data center. It's a processing unit. And it's running something. Something big.

He approaches one of the glowing racks. The green light pulses, almost like a heartbeat. On a small, dusty screen embedded in the rack, a single word flickers in corrupted, pixelated text:

"P R O C E S S I N G . . ."

And beneath it, a series of rapidly changing numbers, too fast to comprehend. Lucky feels a sense of unease. This isn't just data storage; it's active manipulation.

KAEL
(Cont.)
This is where they do it. Where they process the minds. The brainwashing. The identity erasure. This is the engine of the Glitch World's control.

Suddenly, a low, guttural MOAN echoes from deeper within the data center. It's not a mechanical sound, but something organic, something filled with pain and despair. It sends a shiver down Lucky's spine.

LUCKY
(Whispering)
What was that?

Kael's eyes narrow. He sweeps his scanner towards the sound. The readings on his screen spike erratically.

KAEL
(Cont.)
Another prisoner. One who hasn't been fully processed. One who's still fighting. And they're close. Very close.

The green light from the server racks intensifies, casting long, distorted shadows. The hum of the machinery rises to a low growl. Lucky and Kael exchange a look. They've found the heart of the Glitch World's torment. And they're not alone.

FADE OUT.
