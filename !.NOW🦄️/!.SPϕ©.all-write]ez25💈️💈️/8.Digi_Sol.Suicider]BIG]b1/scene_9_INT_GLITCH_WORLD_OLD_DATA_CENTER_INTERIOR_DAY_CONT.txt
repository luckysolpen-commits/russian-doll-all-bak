INT. GLITCH WORLD - OLD DATA CENTER - INTERIOR - DAY (CONT.)

The low, guttural MOAN echoes again, closer this time, filled with a raw, desperate pain. The green light from the server racks pulses with increased intensity, casting grotesque, shifting shadows that seem to writhe on the walls. The hum of the machinery rises to a fever pitch, a cacophony of digital torment.

Kael, his face grim, moves towards the source of the sound, his scanner held high. Lucky follows, his Blade-Shield ready, a knot of dread tightening in his stomach. They navigate through a particularly dense cluster of glowing server racks, the heat radiating from them almost unbearable.

They find him. A figure, hunched and emaciated, strapped into a complex, archaic-looking chair that is wired directly into one of the glowing server racks. His skin is pale, almost translucent, and his eyes are wide, unfocused, flickering with the same green light as the servers. His body occasionally twitches, as if battling an unseen force. He is a man, but barely, a vessel for the digital torture being inflicted upon him.

LUCKY
(Whispering, horrified)
What are they doing to him?

KAEL
(His voice tight with suppressed rage)
They're processing him. Overwriting his mind. Erasing his identity. This is the Digital Soul Suicider in action. It's not just brainwashing; it's a complete annihilation of the self. They feed him false memories, distorted realities, until his own mind breaks and accepts the new programming.

He points to a small, holographic display flickering above the man's head. It shows a complex, constantly shifting neural network, with sections of it being systematically deleted and replaced by glowing, green lines of code.

KAEL
(Cont.)
His consciousness is being fragmented, then reassembled into something... compliant. He's fighting it, but he can't last much longer.

The man on the chair lets out another moan, a sound of pure agony. His body convulses, and for a brief moment, his eyes clear, focusing on Lucky and Kael. A flicker of recognition, a desperate plea, passes through them before they cloud over again.

LUCKY
(Stepping forward)
We have to help him!

KAEL
(Grabbing Lucky's arm, pulling him back)
We can't. Not directly. Any interference will trigger a system-wide alert. And these units... they're designed to self-destruct if tampered with, taking the prisoner's mind with them. It's a failsafe.

He scans the server rack the man is connected to. His scanner beeps frantically.

KAEL
(Cont.)
This particular unit is a primary processing node. It's feeding data to the entire Glitch World. If we can disrupt *this* node, we might be able to create a system-wide ripple. A vulnerability.

He points to a series of glowing conduits running from the server rack into the floor.

KAEL
(Cont.)
These are the main data conduits. They're heavily shielded, but if we can overload them... create a feedback loop... it might cause a cascade failure in the brainwashing protocols. It won't free him, not directly, but it might give others a chance. A chance to resist.

Lucky looks at the suffering man, then at the glowing conduits. The choice is agonizing. Save one, or potentially give hope to many.

LUCKY
(Determined)
How do we do it?

Kael pulls out a small, multi-tool from his belt. It extends into a series of glowing probes and wires.

KAEL
(Cont.)
We need to access the core programming of these conduits. It's a delicate process. One wrong move, and we could be fried. Or worse, become like him.

He begins to work on the conduit, his fingers moving with practiced ease, bypassing layers of digital security. The green light from the server rack pulses faster, as if sensing their intrusion. The moans from the man on the chair become more frequent, more desperate.

LUCKY
(Watching Kael, his heart pounding)
What happens if we succeed?

KAEL
(Without looking up, his voice strained)
If we succeed, we create a window. A brief moment of clarity for those trapped in the illusion. It won't last long. The system will try to correct itself. But it might be enough. Enough for some to wake up. Enough for us to find a way out.

He makes a final connection. A high-pitched WHINE fills the air. The green light from the server rack intensifies to an unbearable glare, then flickers violently. The man on the chair lets out a final, guttural SCREAM, and then goes limp. The holographic display above his head goes dark.

The entire data center shudders. The hum of the servers rises to a deafening ROAR, then abruptly cuts out. The green light vanishes, plunging the vast space into near darkness, save for the faint, corrupted glow of a few remaining dormant units.

Kael pulls his tool away, his face pale, sweat beading on his brow.

KAEL
(Panting)
It's done. The primary node is down. The Digital Soul Suicider... it's been disrupted. But it won't last. The system will reroute, rebuild. We have to move. Now.

Suddenly, a new sound. Not the hum of servers, but the distinct, metallic CLANG of heavy footsteps. And the ominous WHIR of optical sensors. From the darkness, red lights begin to appear, dozens of them, converging on their position.

LUCKY
(Drawing his Blade-Shield)
They're here. They know.

KAEL
(A grim smile)
Of course. We just hit them where it hurts. Now, we run. And we find those who are waking up. Our resistance starts now.

FADE OUT.
