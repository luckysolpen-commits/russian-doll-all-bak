INT. GLITCH WORLD - BUSTLING CITY STREET - DAY (CONT.)

Lucky stares at the flickering image of the woman on the phone, then at the car whose wheels momentarily spun in place. The seemingly normal street now appears sinister, a thin veneer over a corrupted reality. Kael's grim explanation echoes in his ears.

LUCKY
(Voice barely a whisper)
So... this isn't Earth. Not really. It's a prison. A simulation.

KAEL
(Nodding, his gaze sweeping the street, identifying subtle anomalies)
Precisely. The Metal Mafia's most insidious creation. They call it the Glitch World. A perfect, pristine version of Earth, before the EMPRIE, before the devastation. A psychological weapon. They trap their most dangerous enemies here, those they can't simply eliminate. They immerse them in this false reality, hoping to break their minds, to brainwash them into compliance.

Lucky watches a child in a nearby park. The child laughs, chasing a ball, but for a split second, the ball phases through the child's hand, then reappears. The child doesn't react, as if it's normal.

LUCKY
(Horrified)
And the people? Are they... real?

KAEL
(A sigh, heavy with resignation)
Some are projections, constructs of the simulation. Others... others are like us. Prisoners. But many have been here so long, they've succumbed. Their minds have been overwritten, their identities erased. They believe this is real. They are the brainwashed, the compliant. They serve as unwitting enforcers, their very presence reinforcing the illusion.

He points subtly to a man in a business suit, walking with a brisk pace. The man's shadow briefly detaches from his feet, stretching and contorting before snapping back into place. The man continues, oblivious.

KAEL
(Cont.)
And the glitches... they're a constant reminder. The system isn't perfect. It's a false reality, and the inherent contradictions, the strain of maintaining such a vast illusion, manifest as these distortions. They're a sign of the underlying corruption.

Lucky looks at his own hand again. He tries to make his fingers glitch, but nothing happens. He feels a surge of fear. Is he already succumbing? Is his mind being affected?

KAEL
(Noticing Lucky's attempt)
Don't worry. The glitch powers... they manifest differently for everyone. And they're a sign of resistance, not compliance. They're your mind fighting back against the illusion. The more you resist, the more pronounced they become.

Suddenly, a sleek, black DRONE, almost invisible against the blue sky, glides silently between two skyscrapers. Its single red optical sensor sweeps the street below. Then another appears, and another, forming a silent, aerial patrol.

LUCKY
(Voice tight)
Metal Mafia robots. They're here too.

KAEL
(His jaw clenching)
Of course. They maintain the illusion. They monitor the prisoners. And they eliminate any who show too much awareness, too much resistance. They're the enforcers of this false peace.

He subtly gestures towards a seemingly innocuous street vendor selling hot dogs. The vendor's eyes, for a fleeting moment, glow with a faint, metallic sheen. A subtle whirring sound emanates from beneath his cart.

KAEL
(Cont.)
They blend in. They adapt. They are everywhere. We need to be careful, Lucky. Very careful. Every face could be a monitor, every sound a trap. Our first priority is to understand the rules of this prison. And then... to break them.

Lucky looks at the bustling, beautiful, terrifying street. The sun shines, the birds sing, and beneath it all, a chilling, digital hum. He is trapped in a nightmare disguised as a dream. But with Kael, a fellow prisoner who understands the true nature of their predicament, a flicker of hope ignites.

FADE OUT.
