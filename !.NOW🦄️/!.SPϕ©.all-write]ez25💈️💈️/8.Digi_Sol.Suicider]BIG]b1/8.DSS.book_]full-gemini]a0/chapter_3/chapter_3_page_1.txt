Chapter 3: The Library’s Secret

Lucky and Kael navigated the Glitch World’s city,
now with a clear, desperate destination:
the old library. The man’s fragmented words,
his desperate plea, had ignited a new purpose
within them, a fragile hope in this digital prison.
The journey was fraught with subtle dangers,
every step a calculated risk. They moved
through seemingly normal streets, the perfect facade
of the Glitch World shimmering around them,
but their senses were heightened, hyper-alert,
constantly scanning for the tell-tale signs
of Metal Mafia enforcers—the momentary flicker
of an eye, a subtle shift in posture, the too-perfect
smile of a passerby that hinted at a hidden mechanism.
Every shadow held a potential threat, every reflection
a possible watcher, every sound a potential trap.

They passed through a district that seemed
perpetually stuck in a time loop, a chilling testament
to the system’s ability to disorient and control.
A group of children endlessly played hopscotch,
their laughter echoing, bright and innocent,
but never progressing beyond the same few squares,
a digital tableau of arrested development.
A street vendor perpetually offered the same apple,
his arm frozen in mid-air, a silent, digital tableau,
a grotesque parody of commerce. Kael, with his enhanced
perception, used his glitch powers to navigate
the repeating patterns, his mind mapping the temporal
distortions, guiding Lucky through the unsettling loops.
“Temporal loops,” Kael explained, his voice low,
a grim analysis. “A common tactic to disorient
and exhaust prisoners. They’re designed to make you
question your sanity, to break your sense of linear time,
to erode your very perception of reality.”
Lucky felt a wave of nausea as they stepped out of the loop,
the world snapping back into fluid motion with a jarring jolt.
He shook his head, trying to clear the lingering disorientation,
the unsettling feeling of lost moments, of time stolen.

They finally arrived at the OLD LIBRARY.
It was a grand, classical building, made of what appeared
to be marble, its facade gleaming in the simulated sunlight.
But its beauty was deceptive, for it was riddled
with visual glitches, like a disease eating away
at its very structure. Sections of the wall flickered
in and out of existence, revealing glimpses of raw code
beneath, a chaotic jumble of binary and hexadecimal.
Columns occasionally stretched and compressed,
like breathing giants, their ornate carvings distorting
into abstract patterns, a grotesque dance of form.
The large, ornate doors, once grand and imposing,
were slightly ajar, revealing an interior shrouded
in digital dust, a silent invitation to a forgotten past.
“This place is really struggling to maintain its illusion,”
Lucky observed, looking at the flickering walls,
a sense of grim satisfaction in his voice.
A grim smile touched Kael’s lips, a rare display
of emotion. “Good. It means the information here is important.
The system is actively trying to suppress it,
to keep its secrets hidden. We are on the right track.”

They stepped inside, the air immediately growing colder,
heavier, thick with the scent of old paper and ozone,
a strange, anachronistic blend in this digital prison.
The vast main hall was filled with towering bookshelves,
stretching up into a digitally rendered dome ceiling,
its surface shimmering with faint, corrupted light.
Thousands upon thousands of books lined the shelves,
their spines cracked and faded, their covers worn.
But many were corrupted. Their titles were garbled,
a chaotic jumble of letters and symbols, like a broken
language. Their pages, when Lucky pulled one out,
were filled with static or repeating patterns,
like ancient, broken data files, a testament
to the Metal Mafia’s pervasive censorship.

“This is… overwhelming,” Lucky whispered,
his voice echoing in the vast space, a sense of despair
threatening to engulf him. “How do we even begin
to search this? It’s a digital graveyard.”
Kael pulled out a small, handheld device, its screen
glowing with a faint, blue light, its surface smooth and cool.
“This is a data scanner. It can detect anomalies
in the data streams, hidden information, suppressed truths.
We’re looking for anything that doesn’t fit,
any data that the Metal Mafia hasn’t fully
overwritten or suppressed. Any whisper of the past.”
He swept the scanner across the shelves.
The device hummed, its screen displaying a chaotic dance
of numbers and symbols, a constant stream of data.
“The system is actively trying to obscure the truth here,”
Kael observed, his voice low, analytical. “It’s a constant
struggle. But every now and then, a signal breaks through.
A faint echo of what once was.” He ran his hand
over a shelf, and for a moment, the books on it shimmered,
revealing their true, underlying data structures,
a fleeting glimpse into the matrix, like seeing the skeleton
beneath the skin, the raw code beneath the illusion.

Lucky, meanwhile, focused on the physical books,
hoping for a more tangible clue, something that wouldn’t
flicker and distort. He pulled out a book titled
“The History of Avanta.C.” Its pages were mostly blank,
a testament to the Metal Mafia’s censorship, a deliberate
erasure of history. But a faint, almost invisible watermark
appeared on one page: a series of coordinates, barely visible
to the naked eye, a hidden message.
“Look!” Lucky exclaimed, his voice hushed with excitement,
showing Kael the faint markings. “Coordinates!
What do you think they lead to? Another prison? Another trap?”
Kael examined the coordinates, his brow furrowed
in concentration. He pulled out a small, holographic map
from his pocket and input the numbers. A red dot appeared
on the map, deep within a section of the Glitch World
they hadn’t explored – a desolate, industrial zone,
a place of decay and forgotten dreams.

“This is… unexpected,” Kael said, his voice low,
a hint of surprise in his tone. “These coordinates point
to a forgotten data center. A very old one.
It’s been offline for decades, according to the public records
of this simulation. But if the man was right,
if the truth is hidden here… if this is where they keep
the real history, the uncorrupted data…”
Suddenly, a low, rhythmic hum began to vibrate
through the very bones of the library, a deep thrum
that resonated in Lucky’s chest. The flickering glitches
on the walls intensified, becoming more frequent,
more violent, like a system in distress. The air grew heavy,
charged with a malevolent energy, a sense of impending doom.

“They know we’re here,” Kael said, his eyes darting around,
scanning the vast hall. “The system is reacting.
We’ve triggered an alarm. They don’t want us to find
what’s at those coordinates. They’re protecting something.”
From the far end of the hall, a group of figures emerged.
They were not the subtle, disguised enforcers
they’d encountered before. These were full-blown
METAL MAFIA ROBOTS, sleek and menacing,
their optical sensors glowing with an aggressive red light,
like predatory eyes in the gloom.

They moved with a chilling, synchronized precision,
their metallic footsteps echoing ominously
in the vast hall, a sound that sent a shiver
down Lucky’s spine. “Looks like they don’t want us
reading their history!” Lucky shouted, drawing his Blade-Shield,
a surge of adrenaline coursing through him, a desperate
readiness for battle. “They’re not just enforcers,”
Kael replied, his face grim, already assessing their numbers,
their weaponry. “These are combat models. Designed
for suppression. We need to move, and we need to move fast.
We cannot fight them all.”
