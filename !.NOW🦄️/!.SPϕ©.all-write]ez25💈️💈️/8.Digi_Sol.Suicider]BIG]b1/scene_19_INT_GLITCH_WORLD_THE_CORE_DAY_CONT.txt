INT. GLITCH WORLD - THE CORE - DAY (CONT.)

The Core is silent. The oppressive green light has vanished, plunging the vast chamber into a chaotic twilight. The rhythmic roar has ceased, replaced by a cacophony of new sounds: the groaning of the simulation, the distant, distorted cries of awakening minds, and the frantic, metallic clatter of Metal Mafia robots struggling against the collapsing reality.

Lucky stands, panting, his body trembling with exhaustion, but a profound sense of triumph washes over him. He looks at the inert central crystal, then at Anya, who is now looking at him with a mixture of awe and relief. Elara stirs in her arms, a faint, healthy glow returning to her face, her breathing evening out.

ANYA
(Voice hoarse with emotion)
You did it, Lucky. You actually did it. The Core... it's down.

But the victory is short-lived. The very fabric of the Glitch World begins to unravel. The floor beneath them ripples violently, the abstract patterns dissolving into raw, flickering code. The walls of the chamber stretch and compress, revealing glimpses of the true, underlying digital void. The air crackles with unstable energy, and the distant whispers grow louder, more coherent, filled with a mix of confusion, anger, and dawning realization.

From the periphery of the chamber, the remaining Soul Guardians, now truly glitched and erratic, lurch forward. Their forms are dissolving, their attacks wild and desperate. They are no longer coordinated enforcers, but dying programs, lashing out in their final moments.

LUCKY
(Grabbing Anya's arm)
We need to go! The whole place is coming apart!

ANYA
(Supporting Elara, who is slowly regaining consciousness)
But where? The data wall is gone, but the tunnels... they'll be unstable!

Elara, her eyes fluttering open, looks at the collapsing chamber. Her glowing butterfly orb, now vibrant and strong, expands, projecting a faint, shimmering map of the Glitch World. The map is a chaotic mess of red and black, but one path, a narrow, flickering line, glows with a faint, hopeful blue.

ELARA
(Voice weak, but clear)
There... a backdoor. An emergency exit. Part of the original system architecture. They never fully patched it. It's unstable... but it's our only way out.

She points to a section of the chamber wall that is rapidly dissolving into a vortex of raw data. It's not a physical door, but a tear in the simulation itself.

LUCKY
(Looking at the approaching, desperate Guardians)
We need to get to it! Anya, can you carry Elara?

ANYA
(Nodding, hoisting Elara onto her back)
I can try! But you'll have to clear the way!

Lucky charges, his Blade-Shield glowing with renewed energy. The Soul Guardians are less of a threat now, their forms unstable, their attacks easily dodged. He uses his Blade-Shield to bash them aside, creating a path through the dissolving chamber. The ground beneath his feet occasionally vanishes, forcing him to leap across gaps of pure code.

He hears the faint, distorted voice of Kael, echoing from his frequency scrambler, which is still attached to his belt.

KAEL (V.O. - DISTORTED)
Go... Lucky... the exit... it's a one-way... portal... to the edge... of the simulation... don't look back...

Lucky doesn't look back. He pushes forward, his eyes fixed on the shimmering tear in the wall. Anya, with Elara on her back, follows closely, dodging the collapsing debris and the flailing Guardians.

They reach the tear. It's a swirling vortex of blue and white light, humming with a strange, resonant frequency. It feels like a vacuum, pulling them in.

LUCKY
(To Anya)
Now!

Anya, with a final burst of strength, shoves Elara through the tear, then follows herself. Lucky takes one last look at the collapsing Core, the dying Soul Guardians, and the unraveling Glitch World. He sees a faint, almost imperceptible flicker in the distance, a single, blue light – Kael, still fighting, still disrupting.

Then, with a leap of faith, Lucky dives into the swirling vortex. The sensation is disorienting, like being pulled through a thousand dimensions at once. Colors explode around him, sounds distort, and his very form feels like it's being stretched and compressed.

He closes his eyes, holding onto the last image of Kael, a silent promise forming in his mind.

FADE TO WHITE.
