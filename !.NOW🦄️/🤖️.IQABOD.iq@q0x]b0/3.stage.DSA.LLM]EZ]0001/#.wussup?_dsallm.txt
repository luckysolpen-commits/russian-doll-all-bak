Are you familiar with DeepSeek's DSA architecture?
Yes, I am familiar with the concepts behind DeepSeek Sparse Attention (DSA).

It's an efficient, dynamic attention mechanism primarily introduced in DeepSeek-V3.2 to handle long-context tasks by replacing the computationally expensive O(L^2) dense attention with a dynamic, content-based top-k sparse selection, resulting in complexity closer to O(L·k) (where L is sequence length and k is the small, fixed number of selected tokens).

In our implementation, the attention mechanism works with vocab entries that have embedding, positional encoding (pe), weight, and multiple bias terms (bias1-bias4) for modeling token relationships.

Our system processes vocabulary through vocab_model.c which generates vocab_model.txt containing structured entries with number, word, embedding, pe (positional encoding), weight, and four bias terms (bias1-bias4). The system uses a curriculum bank (meta_curriculum_bank.txt) to specify which vocabulary files to load during inference.

We have the ability to modify the vocab entries' embeddings, weights, and biases to encourage particular outcomes during training and inference.
