Geometry explores shapes—triangles, circles, squares—measuring angles and areas, such as the Pythagorean theorem: a^2 + b^2 = c^2. Calculus tackles change, with derivatives for slopes and integrals for areas under curves. Statistics analyzes data, calculating means, medians, and probabilities.
