SELECTOR PROMPT for 3D Tactics Game

GAME MECHANICS:
1. There is a selector (yellow wireframe cube) that the player controls
2. There are game pieces (purple soldiers, blue water, etc.) on the board
3. The selector is the primary controller - it's the "soul/hand" of the player

CONTROL FLOW:
1. INITIAL STATE: Selector mode
   - Arrow keys move the selector independently on the board
   - No piece is selected

2. SELECTING A PIECE: Press Ctrl when selector is over a piece
   - The piece becomes selected
   - Arrow keys now move both the selector AND the selected piece together
   - The piece follows the selector's movements

3. DESELECTING: Press Ctrl again
   - The piece stays in its current position
   - Arrow keys move the selector independently again
   - Selector and piece are now separate

VISUAL FEEDBACK:
- Selected piece: Slightly larger scale (1.1x) and potentially different visual state
- When piece follows selector: Both move in unison

KEY PRINCIPLE:
The selector is ALWAYS the primary controller. When a piece is selected, the piece follows the selector. When no piece is selected, only the selector moves.
